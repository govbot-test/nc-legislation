GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 89
Short Title: University Vaccination Freedom Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Gable, Almond, Kidwell, and Tyson (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Health, if favorable, Higher Education, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and
Operations of the House
February 11, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT REPEALING IMMUNIZATION REQUIREMENTS FOR PERSONS WHO
3 ATTEND A COLLEGE OR UNIVERSITY IN NORTH CAROLINA.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. This act shall be known as "The University Vaccination Freedom Act."
6 SECTION 2. G.S. 130A-154(b) reads as rewritten:
7 "(b) Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, a person who received
8 immunizations in a state other than North Carolina shall present an official certificate or record
9 of immunization to the child care facility, school (K-12), or college or university. facility or
10 school (K-12) of attendance. This certificate or record shall state the person's name, address, date
11 of birth, and sex; the type and number of doses of administered vaccine; the dates of the first
12 MMR and the last DTP and polio; the name and address of the physician or local health
13 department administering the required immunization; and other relevant information required by
14 the Commission."
15 SECTION 3. G.S. 130A-157 reads as rewritten:
16 "§ 130A-157. Religious exemption.
17 If the bona fide religious beliefs of an adult or the parent, guardian or person in loco parentis
18 of a child are contrary to the immunization requirements contained in this Chapter, the adult or
19 the child shall be exempt from the requirements. Upon submission of a written statement of the
20 bona fide religious beliefs and opposition to the immunization requirements, the person may
21 attend the college, university, school or facility without presenting a certificate of immunization."
22 SECTION 4. G.S. 130A-155.1 is repealed.
23 SECTION 5. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning with
24 the 2026-2027 academic year.
