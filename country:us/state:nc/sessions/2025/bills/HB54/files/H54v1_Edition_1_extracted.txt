Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Funds for NC APSE. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Huneycutt, Howard, Potts, and Setzer (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
February 5, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE FUNDS FOR EVIDENCE-BASED SUPPORTED EMPLOYMENT
3 SERVICES FOR INDIVIDUALS WITH SERIOUS MENTAL ILLNESS,
4 INTELLECTUAL DISABILITIES, OR DEVELOPMENTAL DISABILITIES.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
7 Health and Human Services, Division of Mental Health, Developmental Disabilities, and
8 Substance Use Services, the sum of one hundred twenty-five thousand dollars ($125,000) in
9 recurring funds for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to be allocated as a grant to the
10 North Carolina Association of People Supporting Employment First (NC APSE) to develop and
11 implement training programs for the Department, including online training modules, on the
12 provision of evidence-based supported employment services for individuals in targeted
13 populations, in order to assist these individuals with preparation for, identification of, and
14 maintenance of integrated, paid, competitive employment. The Department shall make these
15 training programs available throughout the State to (i) employers that have hired or are willing
16 to hire individuals in targeted populations, (ii) service providers of local management
17 entities/managed care organizations, and (iii) any other entity the Department determines will
18 benefit from receiving this training in order to achieve improved employment outcomes for
19 individuals in targeted populations. As used in this section, "individuals in targeted populations"
20 means individuals with serious mental illness who are not in or at risk of entry into an adult care
21 home and individuals with intellectual disabilities, developmental disabilities, or both.
22 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
