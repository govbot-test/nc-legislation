GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 4
Agriculture, Energy, and Environment Committee Substitute Adopted 4/29/25
House Committee Substitute Favorable 6/24/25
Fourth Edition Engrossed 6/24/25
Short Title: Brownfields Property Reuse Act Revisions. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 25, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO AMEND THE BROWNFIELDS PROPERTY REUSE ACT AND THE
3 BROWNFIELDS PROPERTY TAX BENEFIT.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 105-277.13 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 105-277.13. Taxation of improvements on brownfields.
7 (a) Qualifying improvements on brownfields properties are designated a special class of
8 property under Article V, Sec. 2(2) of the North Carolina Constitution and shall be appraised,
9 assessed, and taxed in accordance with this section. An owner of land real property subject to a
10 brownfields agreement entered into by the Department of Environmental Quality pursuant to
11 G.S. 130A-310.32 is entitled to the partial exclusion provided by this section for the first five
12 taxable years beginning after completion of qualifying improvements made after the later of July
13 1, 2000, or the date of the brownfields agreement. 2000. After property has qualified for the
14 exclusion provided by this section, the assessor for the county in which the property is located
15 shall annually appraise the improvements made to the property during the period of time that the
16 owner is entitled to the exclusion. Subsequent qualifying improvements shall also be entitled to a
17 separate exclusionary period.
18 (b) For the purposes of this section, the terms "qualifying improvements on brownfields
19 properties" and "qualifying improvements" mean improvements made after to real property that
20 is subject to a brownfields agreement entered into by the Department of Environmental Quality
21 and the owner pursuant to G.S. 130A-310.32.provides written confirmation that the real property
22 is eligible for a brownfields agreement pursuant to the Brownfields Property Reuse Act of 1997,
23 Part 5 of Article 9 of Chapter 130A of the General Statutes, as amended, and provided that the
24 real property is or becomes subject to a brownfields agreement as required under subsection (a)
25 of this section.
26 (c) The following table establishes the percentage of the appraised value of the qualified
27 improvements that is excluded based on the taxable year:
28 Year Percent of Appraised Value Excluded
29 Year 1 90%
30 Year 2 75%
31 Year 3 50%
32 Year 4 30%
33 Year 5 10%."
34 SECTION 2. G.S. 130A-310.39 reads as rewritten:
*S387-v-4*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 "§ 130A-310.39. Fees.
2 (a) The Department shall collect the following fees:
3 (1) A prospective developer who submits an application for a proposed
4 brownfields agreement for review by the Department shall pay an initial fee
5 of two thousand dollars ($2,000).
6 (2) A prospective developer who enters into a brownfields agreement with the
7 Department shall pay pay, on a time schedule that the Department may
8 specify, a fee in an amount equal to the full cost to the Department and the
9 Department of Justice of all activities related to the brownfields agreement,
10 including but not limited to negotiation of the brownfields agreement, public
11 notice and community involvement, and monitoring the implementation of
12 and compliance with the brownfields agreement. agreement and requirements
13 of this Part regarding the Notice of Brownfields Property. The procedure by
14 which the amount of this fee is determined shall be established by agreement
15 between the prospective developer and the Department and shall be set out as
16 a part of the brownfields agreement. The fee imposed by this subdivision shall
17 be paid in two installments. The first installment shall be due at the time the
18 prospective developer and the Department enter into the brownfields
19 agreement and shall equal all costs that have been incurred by the Department
20 and the Department of Justice at that time less the amount of the initial fee
21 paid pursuant to subdivision (1) of this subsection. The Department shall not
22 enter into the brownfields agreement unless the first installment is paid in full
23 when due. The second installment shall be due at the time the prospective
24 developer submits a final report certifying completion of remediation under
25 the brownfields agreement and shall include any additional costs that have
26 been incurred by the Department and the Department of Justice, including all
27 costs of monitoring the implementation of the brownfields agreement.If, in an
28 effort to implement and monitor the brownfields agreement, it must recover
29 costs unanticipated in the agreement, the Department must provide to the
30 prospective developer or then current owner documentation supporting any
31 fee it charges a prospective developer or current owner pursuant to this
32 section.
33 (3) Any owner of property subject to a recorded Notice of Brownfields Property
34 that is out of compliance with the requirements of this Part regarding the
35 Notice shall pay a fee to the Department and the Department of Justice equal
36 to the documented costs to the State to enforce or otherwise seek to correct
37 the noncompliance.
38 (b) Fees and interest imposed under this section shall be credited to the Brownfields
39 Property Reuse Act Implementation Account.
40 (c) If a prospective developer fails to pay the full amount of any fee due under this
41 section, interest on the unpaid portion of the fee shall accrue from the time the fee is due until
42 paid at the rate established by the Secretary of Revenue pursuant to G.S. 105-241.21. A lien for
43 the amount of the unpaid fee plus interest shall attach to the real and personal property of the
44 prospective developer and to the brownfields property until the fee and interest is paid. The
45 Department may collect unpaid fees and interest in any manner that a unit of local government
46 may collect delinquent taxes."
47 SECTION 3. Section 1 of this act is effective for taxes imposed for taxable years
48 beginning on or after July 1, 2025. The remainder of this act is effective when it becomes law.
