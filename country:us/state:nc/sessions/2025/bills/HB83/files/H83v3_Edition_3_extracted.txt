37 RFI ON HUMAN TRAFFICKING REPORTING
38 SECTION 2.(a) The General Assembly finds all of the following:
39 (1) Human trafficking is a pervasive crime that often goes undetected. National
40 statistics indicate that hundreds of thousands of people are trafficked in the
41 United States each year, yet the vast majority are never identified.
42 (2) North Carolina is recognized as a leader in efforts to combat human
43 trafficking; however, frontline professionals, including law enforcement
44 officers, healthcare providers, teachers, and social workers, often lack the
45 training, tools, and resources necessary to adequately support victim-survivors
46 and hold traffickers accountable. Services remain fragmented and limited,
47 despite a strong desire for collaboration and coordination across sectors.
48 (3) Advances in the field have produced effective models for data collection,
49 needs assessment, and landscape analysis at the State, county, and regional
50 levels. These efforts help guide statewide partners in addressing a complex
51 and constantly evolving issue.

1 SECTION 2.(b) The North Carolina Human Trafficking Commission (Commission)
2 shall conduct a request for information from vendors equipped to conduct a study on human
3 trafficking reporting, including information on the development process for a statewide human
4 trafficking reporting and response system (system). After the Commission has received the
5 responses to the request for information, the Commission is encouraged to begin development of
6 a system that facilitates referral to the National Human Trafficking hotline for crisis support as
7 necessary. It is the intent of the General Assembly to establish a system that will (i) notify law
8 enforcement and child protection officials of high priority reports, (ii) provide a secure dashboard
9 for authorized personnel to view, triage, and respond to reports, and (iii) support real-time
10 communication between investigators and survivors or reporters with consent.
11 SECTION 2.(c) This section is effective when it becomes law.
