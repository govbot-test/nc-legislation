21 "§ 18D-103. Administrative penalties.
22 (a) For any violation of the tobacco retail sales laws, the Commission may take any of
23 the following actions against a permittee:
24 (1) Suspend the permittee's permit for a specified period of time not longer than
25 three years.
26 (2) Revoke the permittee's permit.
27 (3) Fine the permittee up to five hundred dollars ($500.00) for the first violation,
28 up to seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00) for the second violation within
29 three years, and up to one thousand dollars ($1,000) for the third violation
30 within three years of the first violation.
31 (4) Suspend the permittee's permit under subdivision (1) of this subsection and
32 impose a fine under subdivision (3) of this subsection.
33 (b) In any case in which the Commission is entitled to suspend or revoke a permit, the
34 Commission may accept from the permittee an offer in compromise to pay a penalty of not more
35 than five thousand dollars ($5,000). The Commission may either accept a compromise or revoke
36 a permit, but not both. The Commission may accept a compromise and suspend the permit in the
37 same case.
38 (c) The clear proceeds of fines and penalties assessed pursuant to this section shall be
39 remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with G.S. 115C-457.2.
40 (d) Suspension or revocation of a permit is grounds for suspension or revocation of any
41 related State or local revenue license.
42 (e) Unless some other disposition is ordered by the Commission for a tobacco retail sales
43 law violation, revocation or suspension of a permit under subsection (a) of this section does not
44 include automatic revocation or suspension, respectively, of any ABC permit held by the same
45 permittee for the same establishment.

17 "§ 18D-200. Tobacco retail permit required.
18 (a) It is unlawful to sell a tobacco product to a consumer in this State without a permit
19 issued by the Commission in accordance with this Chapter.

23 "§ 18D-201. Disposition of seized tobacco products.
24 (a) A law enforcement officer who seizes tobacco products as evidence of a tobacco retail
25 sales law violation shall provide for the storage of those tobacco products until the
26 commencement of the trial or administrative hearing relating to the violation, unless some other
27 disposition is authorized under this section.
28 (b) After giving notice to each defendant, to any other known owner, and to the
29 Commission, a judge may order any of the following dispositions of tobacco products seized as
30 evidence of a tobacco retail sales law violation:
31 (1) The destruction of any tobacco products except that amount needed for
32 evidence at trial.
33 (2) The sale of any tobacco products other than any tobacco products needed for
34 evidence at trial, if the trial is likely to be delayed for more than 90 days, or if
35 the quantity or nature of the tobacco products is such that storage is
36 impractical or unduly expensive.
37 (3) The destruction of the tobacco products if storage or sale is not practical.
38 (4) Continued storage of the tobacco products.
39 (c) After the criminal charge is resolved, a judge may order any of the following
40 dispositions of seized tobacco products:
41 (1) If the owner or possessor of the tobacco products is found guilty of a criminal
42 charge relating to those tobacco products, the judge may order the sale or
43 destruction of any tobacco products that were held until trial.
44 (2) If the owner or possessor of the tobacco products is found not guilty, or if
45 charges are dismissed or otherwise resolved in favor of the owner or
46 possessor, the judge shall order the tobacco products returned to that owner or
47 possessor, except as provided in subdivision (3) of this subsection.
48 (3) If the owner or possessor of the tobacco products is found not guilty, or if
49 charges are otherwise resolved in favor of the owner or possessor, but
50 possession of the tobacco products by that owner or possessor would be
51 unlawful, the judge shall order the tobacco products either sold or destroyed.

7 "§ 18D-300. Qualifications for a permit.
8 (a) To be eligible to receive and to hold a tobacco retail sales permit, a person must satisfy
9 all of the following requirements:
10 (1) Be at least 21 years old.
11 (2) Be a resident of North Carolina, unless either of the following applies:
12 a. The person is an officer, director, or stockholder of a corporate
13 applicant or permittee and is not a manager or otherwise responsible
14 for the day-to-day operation of the business.
15 b. The person has executed a power of attorney designating a qualified
16 resident of this State to serve as attorney-in-fact for the purposes of
17 receiving service of process and managing the business for which
18 permits are sought.
19 (3) Not have been convicted of a felony within three years and, if convicted of a
20 felony before then, have had his or her citizenship restored.
21 (4) Not have been convicted of a tobacco product retail sales offense pursuant to
22 this Chapter within two years.
23 (5) Not have been convicted of a misdemeanor controlled substance offense
24 within two years.
25 (6) Not have had a tobacco retail sales permit revoked within three years, except
26 where the revocation was based solely on a permittee's failure to pay the
27 annual renewal fee required in G.S. 18D-302(d).

29 taxes, interest, and penalties that are collectible under G.S. 105-241.22.

21 "§ 18D-301. Issuance of permits.
22 (a) All tobacco retail sales permits shall be issued by the Commission.
23 (b) Before issuing a permit, the Commission shall be satisfied that the applicant is a
24 suitable person to hold a tobacco retail sales permit and that the location is a suitable place to
25 hold the permit for which the applicant has applied. Factors the Commission shall consider in
26 determining whether the applicant and the business location are suitable are all of the following:
27 (1) The reputation, character, and criminal record of the applicant.
28 (2) Any other evidence that would tend to show whether the applicant would
29 comply with the tobacco retail sales laws.
30 (3) Whether the operation of the retail seller permit applicant's business at that
31 location would be detrimental to the neighborhood, including evidence
32 admissible under G.S. 150B-29(a) of any of the following:
33 a. Past revocations, suspensions, and violations of ABC laws by prior
34 permittees related to or associated with the applicant, or a business
35 with which the applicant is associated, within the immediate preceding
36 12-month period at this location.
37 b. Evidence of illegal drug activity on or about the licensed premises.
38 c. Evidence of fighting, disorderly conduct, and other dangerous
39 activities on or about the licensed premises.
40 (c) The Commission shall have the sole power, in its discretion, to determine the
41 suitability and qualifications of an applicant for a permit. The Commission shall also have the
42 authority to determine the suitability of the location to which the permit may be issued.

36 a permit, but not both. The Commission may accept a compromise and suspend the permit in the

24 (a) A law enforcement officer who seizes tobacco products as evidence of a tobacco retail
25 sales law violation shall provide for the storage of those tobacco products until the
26 commencement of the trial or administrative hearing relating to the violation, unless some other
27 disposition is authorized under this section.
28 (b) After giving notice to each defendant, to any other known owner, and to the
29 Commission, a judge may order any of the following dispositions of tobacco products seized as
30 evidence of a tobacco retail sales law violation:
31 (1) The destruction of any tobacco products except that amount needed for
32 evidence at trial.
33 (2) The sale of any tobacco products other than any tobacco products needed for
34 evidence at trial, if the trial is likely to be delayed for more than 90 days, or if
35 the quantity or nature of the tobacco products is such that storage is
36 impractical or unduly expensive.
37 (3) The destruction of the tobacco products if storage or sale is not practical.
38 (4) Continued storage of the tobacco products.
39 (c) After the criminal charge is resolved, a judge may order any of the following
40 dispositions of seized tobacco products:
41 (1) If the owner or possessor of the tobacco products is found guilty of a criminal
42 charge relating to those tobacco products, the judge may order the sale or
43 destruction of any tobacco products that were held until trial.
44 (2) If the owner or possessor of the tobacco products is found not guilty, or if
45 charges are dismissed or otherwise resolved in favor of the owner or
46 possessor, the judge shall order the tobacco products returned to that owner or
47 possessor, except as provided in subdivision (3) of this subsection.
48 (3) If the owner or possessor of the tobacco products is found not guilty, or if
49 charges are otherwise resolved in favor of the owner or possessor, but
50 possession of the tobacco products by that owner or possessor would be
51 unlawful, the judge shall order the tobacco products either sold or destroyed.
