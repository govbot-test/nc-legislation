1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROHIBIT CORPORAL PUNISHMENT IN THE PUBLIC SCHOOLS.
3 Whereas, "corporal punishment" is defined in statute as "the intentional infliction of
4 physical pain upon the body of a student as a disciplinary measure"; and
5 Whereas, more than 80 research studies confirm that corporal punishment is not
6 associated with improvement in academic outcomes, but is associated with impeding students'
7 psychological and emotional development; and
8 Whereas, 31 states have enacted statutory bans on corporal punishment in public
9 schools; and
10 Whereas, none of this State's 115 local school administrative units currently allow the
11 use of corporal punishment; and
12 Whereas, the State Board of Education, the North Carolina Parent Teacher
13 Association, and virtually all professional and advocacy organizations related to children
14 recommend the prohibition of corporal punishment in public schools; Now, therefore,
15 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
16 SECTION 1. G.S. 115C-390.4 reads as rewritten:

1 (4) The school shall maintain records of each administration of corporal
2 punishment and the reasons for its administration.
3 (5) In no event shall excessive force be used in the administration of corporal
4 punishment. Excessive force includes force that results in injury to the child
5 that requires medical attention beyond simple first aid.
6 (6) Corporal punishment shall not be administered on a student whose parent or
7 guardian has stated in writing that corporal punishment shall not be
8 administered to that student. Parents and guardians shall be given a form to
9 make such an election at the beginning of the school year or when the student
10 first enters the school during the year. The form shall advise the parent or
11 guardian that the student may be subject to suspension, among other possible
12 punishments, for offenses that would otherwise not require suspension if
13 corporal punishment were available. If the parent or guardian does not return
14 the form, corporal punishment may be administered on the student.
15 (c) Each governing body of a public school unit shall report annually to the State Board
16 of Education, in a manner prescribed by the State Board of Education, on the number of times
17 that corporal punishment was administered. The report shall be in compliance with the federal
18 Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, 20 U.S.C. § 1232g, and shall include the following:
19 (1) The number of students who received corporal punishment.
20 (2) The number of students who received corporal punishment who were also
21 students with disabilities and were eligible to receive special education and
22 related services under the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act,
23 20 U.S.C. § 1400, et seq.
24 (3) The grade level of the students who received corporal punishment.
25 (4) The race, gender, and ethnicity of the students who received corporal
26 punishment.
27 (5) The reason for the administration of the corporal punishment for each student
28 who received corporal punishment."
