1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE FUNDS FOR ADDITIONAL ALL PRO DAD CHAPTERS AND
3 CREATE A PROGRAM TO SUPPORT NORTH CAROLINA FATHERS.
4 Whereas, research affirms that children with involved fathers tend to perform better
5 socially, maintain better school attendance, have a lower risk of dropping out, and are less likely
6 to engage in the use of drugs or alcohol; and
7 Whereas, the fatherlessness crisis in America affects 1 in 5 children, or 15.7 million
8 kids, who are raised without a father (U.S. Census Bureau, 2023); and
9 Whereas, 80% of single-parent households in the United States in 2023 were headed
10 by single mothers; and
11 Whereas, abundant research demonstrates that 71% of children growing up without a
12 father figure, whether physically or emotionally absent, perform more poorly academically and
13 experience regular truancy; and
14 Whereas, teens without a father are 7 times more likely to experience teen pregnancy
15 and have a 54% likelihood of being poorer than their parents; and
16 Whereas, there is an 85% chance that fatherless children will become involved with
17 the juvenile justice system (Children's Bureau, 2023); and
18 Whereas, fathers' engagement plays a critical role in helping children overcome
19 barriers to education, including mental, emotional, and behavioral challenges; and
20 Whereas, such engagement fosters improvements in social interactions, academic
21 performance, school attendance, and decreases the likelihood of dropping out, while improving
22 student performance; and
23 Whereas, conversely, impaired behaviors exhibited by fathers can lead to insufficient
24 development of a child's emotional intelligence and mental resilience, hindering their overall
25 growth and educational success; Now, therefore,
26 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
27

6 PART II. RESPONSIBLE FATHERHOOD NC PROGRAM
7 SECTION 2.1. Article 2 of Chapter 108A of the General Statutes is amended by
8 adding a new Part to read:
9 "Part 12. Responsible Fatherhood NC Act.
10 "§ 108A-70.60. Short title.
11 This act may be cited as the "Responsible Fatherhood NC Act."
12 "§ 108A-70.61. Definitions.
13 The following definitions apply in this Part:
14 (1) Department. – Refers to the Department of Health and Human Services.
15 (2) Program. – Refers to the Responsible Fatherhood NC program.
16 "§ 108A-70.62. Creation of the Responsible Fatherhood NC program; purpose.
17 (a) The General Assembly recognizes that families are stronger when both parents act
18 responsibly in caring for their children. It is the intent of the General Assembly to recognize and
19 support the important and unique role that fathers play in ensuring the physical, emotional, and
20 economic well-being of their children and families.
21 (b) The Department shall contract for the development and implementation of the
22 Responsible Fatherhood NC program. The program shall provide an opportunity for every father
23 in the State to be able to obtain information and inspiration that will motivate and enable him to
24 enhance his abilities as a father, recognizing that some fathers have greater challenges than others
25 and would benefit from greater support.
26 (c) The entity the Department contracts with to develop, implement, and manage the
27 program, "Program Manager," shall be a nonprofit organization that satisfies all of the following
28 criteria:
29 (1) Has a history of focusing on responsible fatherhood, including providing
30 online resources to fathers, and engaging fathers, father figures, and children
31 through community-based and school-based events to encourage responsible
32 fatherhood.
33 (2) Has the organizational capacity to manage a statewide initiative and
34 successfully carry out the requirements of this section.
35 (d) The Program Manager shall be solely responsible for developing, implementing, and
36 managing the program which shall include, but not be limited to, all of the following:
37 (1) A statewide media campaign that increases the awareness and importance of
38 fathers being involved in their children's lives.
39 (2) Resources and information for fathers and father figures to increase
40 engagement and involvement in their children's lives.
41 (3) Promotion of related fatherhood programs that are provided by the
42 Department of Public Instruction, the Community College System, or The
43 University of North Carolina System.
44 (e) To most effectively use the funds appropriated to this program, the Program Manager
45 shall identify and use other existing media assets, content, and online resources it deems
46 appropriate, including any existing media assets, content, and online resources it has available,
47 to implement and execute the items required by this section.
48 "§ 108A-70.63. Grants to address the needs of fathers.
49 (a) The Department shall award grants to nonprofit community-based organizations to
50 address the needs of fathers. The Department shall award the following types of grants:

1 (1) Grants that comprehensively address the needs of fathers, such as assisting
2 them in finding employment, managing child support obligations,
3 transitioning from a period of incarceration, accessing health care,
4 understanding child development, and enhancing parenting skills. Services
5 provided shall be tailored to the needs of the father being served. Case
6 management services shall be provided to the fathers who are served by the
7 grants under this subdivision.
8 (2) Grants that provide evidence-based parenting education specifically for
9 fathers. The grants under this subdivision do not require case management
10 services.
11 (b) The Department shall prioritize applicants for a grant specified under subsection (a)
12 of this section based on all of the following:
13 (1) Need in a geographic area and the population to be served by the grant as
14 indicated by, at a minimum, all of the following:
15 a. Unemployment rates.
16 b. Incarceration rates.
17 c. Housing instability.
18 d. The number of single-parent households.
19 e. The number of public benefit recipients.
20 f. Graduation rates.
21 g. Levels of academic achievement.
22 (2) If an applicant has a primary mission of, or a history of a significant focus on
23 and effective work toward, addressing the needs of men in their role as fathers.
24 (3) Applicant current and historical involvement in the community being served.
25 (4) Applicant commitment and capability to employ competent staff who can
26 effectively engage with the fathers being served, including, at a minimum,
27 those individuals who share a similar background as the fathers being served.
28 (5) The number of individuals the applicant plans to serve through the grant and
29 the projected costs for the program.
30 (6) Applicant organizational capacity to effectively meet the requirements of the
31 grant and to deliver the programs proposed by the applicant. The Department
32 may offer technical assistance to applicants and grant recipients that have
33 lower organizational capacity as long as such organizations have, or the
34 organization's leadership has, significant experience serving fathers.
35 (c) Grants shall be awarded for no more than three years, with subsequent year funding
36 contingent on compliance with grant requirements and adequate performance. Grant recipients
37 shall submit reports to the Department in a format and at intervals prescribed by the Department."
38 SECTION 2.2. The Department has six months from the effective date of this act to
39 implement the requirements outlined in Section 1 of this act.
40 SECTION 2.3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
41 Health and Human Services the sum of three million dollars ($3,000,000) in nonrecurring funds
42 for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used to implement the provisions of Section 1 of this act. Of
43 the funds appropriated by this act, the Department shall use up to two million dollars ($2,000,000)
44 to contract for the Program Manager as authorized in G.S. 108A-70.62. The remainder of the
45 funds shall be used for the grants authorized in G.S. 108A-70.63.
46
47 PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
48 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
