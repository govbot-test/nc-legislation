33 PROVISIONS REGARDING THE RECORDING, STORAGE, AND DISCLOSURE OF
34 VIDEO FROM DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION HIGHWAY CAMERAS
35 SECTION 4.(a) G.S. 132-1.1 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
36 "(i) Highway camera video records stored by the Department of Transportation may not
37 be disclosed except as provided in G.S. 132-1.7B."
38 SECTION 4.(b) Chapter 132 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
39 section to read:
40 "§ 132-1.7B. Highway camera video records.
41 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
42 (1) Disclose or disclosure. – To make a recording available for viewing or
43 listening to by the person requesting disclosure, at a time and location chosen
44 by the custodial law enforcement agency. This term does not include the
45 release of a recording.
46 (2) Highway camera. – A Department of Transportation video or digital camera,
47 located on highway right-of-way, for the purpose of monitoring vehicle
48 traffic.
49 (3) Personal representative. – A parent, court-appointed guardian, spouse, or
50 attorney of a person whose image or voice is in the recording. If a person
51 whose image or voice is in the recording is deceased, the term also means the

2 person's surviving spouse, parent, or adult child; the deceased person's
3 attorney; or the parent or guardian of a surviving minor child of the deceased.
4 (4) Recording. – A visual, or visual and audio, recording captured by a highway
5 camera.
6 (5) Release. – To provide a copy of a recording.
7 (b) Highway cameras shall record and store incidents upon the request of law
8 enforcement agencies to assist the following types of incident investigations along the interstates
9 and highways:
10 (1) Vehicle chases.
11 (2) Hit and run crashes.
12 (3) Road rage altercations.
13 (4) Street races.
14 (5) Street takeovers.
15 (6) Any other incident investigations of which law enforcement agencies feel the
16 assistance of this technology would support and expedite.
17 (c) Public Record Classification. – Recordings under this section are not public records,
18 as defined by G.S. 132-1.
19 (d) Disclosure; General. – Except as provided under subsection (b) of this section,
20 recordings shall be disclosed only as provided by this section. A person requesting disclosure of
21 a recording must make a written request to the Department of Transportation. The request shall
22 include the name of the person making the request, date and approximate time of the activity
23 captured in the recording, or otherwise identify the activity with reasonable particularity
24 sufficient to identify the recording to which the request refers. The Department may only disclose
25 a recording to the following:
26 (1) A person whose image or voice is in the recording.
27 (2) A personal representative of an adult person whose image or voice is in the
28 recording, if the adult person has consented to the disclosure.
29 (3) A personal representative of a minor or of an adult person under lawful
30 guardianship whose image or voice is in the recording.
31 (4) A personal representative of a deceased person whose image or voice is in the
32 recording.
33 (5) A personal representative of an adult person who is incapacitated and unable
34 to provide consent to disclosure.
35 When disclosing the recording, the Department shall disclose only those portions of the
36 recording that are relevant to the person's request. A person who receives disclosure pursuant to
37 this subsection shall not record or copy the recording.
38 (e) Disclosure; Factors for Consideration. – Upon receipt of the written request for
39 disclosure under subsection (d) of this section, as promptly as possible, the Department must
40 either disclose the portion of the recording relevant to the person's request or notify the requestor
41 of the decision not to disclose the recording to the requestor. The Department may consider any
42 of the following factors in determining if a recording is disclosed:
43 (1) If the person requesting disclosure of the recording is a person authorized to
44 receive disclosure pursuant to subsection (d) of this section.
45 (2) If the recording contains information that is otherwise confidential or exempt
46 from disclosure or release under State or federal law.
47 (3) If disclosure would reveal information regarding a person that is of a highly
48 sensitive personal nature.
49 (4) If disclosure may harm the reputation or jeopardize the safety of a person.
50 (5) If disclosure would create a serious threat to the fair, impartial, and orderly
51 administration of justice.
