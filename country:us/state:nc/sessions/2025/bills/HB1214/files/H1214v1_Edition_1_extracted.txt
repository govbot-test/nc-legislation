1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO INCREASE THE NUMBER OF EMPLOYERS WHO ARE REQUIRED TO
3 PARTICIPATE IN THE FEDERAL E-VERIFY PROGRAM, REQUIRE STATE AND
4 LOCAL GOVERNMENT EMPLOYERS TO COMPLY WITH ARTICLE 2 OF CHAPTER
5 64 OF THE GENERAL STATUTES, CREATE A GOOD-FAITH SAFE HARBOR FOR
6 EMPLOYERS WHO UNKNOWINGLY RECEIVE FRAUDULENT DOCUMENTATION
7 RELATED TO E-VERIFY, ALLOW THE COMMISSIONER OF LABOR TO CONDUCT
8 RANDOM AND RISK-BASED COMPLIANCE CHECKS RELATED TO E-VERIFY,
9 ALLOW EMPLOYERS TO CURE FIRST VIOLATIONS OF E-VERIFY
10 REQUIREMENTS, INCREASE THE CIVIL PENALTIES FOR EMPLOYERS WHO FAIL
11 TO COMPLY WITH E-VERIFY REQUIREMENTS, PROTECT EMPLOYEES FROM
12 RETALIATION FOR GOOD-FAITH REPORTS OF SUSPECTED VIOLATIONS OF
13 E-VERIFY REQUIREMENTS, AND APPROPRIATE FUNDS TO THE DEPARTMENT
14 OF LABOR FOR THE ENFORCEMENT OF THIS ACT.

7 "§ 64-26. Verification of employee work authorization.
8 (a) Employers Must Use E-Verify. – Each employer, after hiring an employee to work in

10 (b) Employer Preservation of E-Verify Forms. – Each employer shall retain the record of
11 the verification of work authorization required by this section while the employee is employed
12 and for one year thereafter.
13 (c) Repealed by Session Laws 2013-418, s. 2(g), effective September 4, 2013.
14 (d) Good-Faith Compliance. – An employer shall be deemed in good-faith compliance
15 with this Article if the employer timely enrolls in and utilizes E-Verify as required by law,
16 properly completes and retains all required forms and documentation, and does not knowingly or
17 with reckless disregard accept fraudulent documentation. An employer acting in good-faith
18 compliance with this Article shall not be subject to civil penalties under this Article solely
19 because an employee submitted fraudulent information or documentation that could not
20 reasonably have been detected."

1 violation occurs when the Commissioner has not previously issued an order against the employer
2 pursuant to subsection (b) of this section or former G.S. 64-31, regardless of whether the
3 employer has previously utilized the cure period under this subsection. If the employer is entitled
4 to the cure period under this subsection, the Commissioner shall issue a written notice of
5 noncompliance to the employer. The employer shall have 30 calendar days from receipt of the
6 notice to cure the alleged violation by enrolling in E-Verify, completing required verifications,
7 and otherwise complying with G.S. 64-26. If the employer cures the alleged violation within the
8 30-day cure period and submits proof of compliance to the Commissioner, the Commissioner
9 shall not hold a hearing or issue an order against the employer under subsection (b) of this section
10 for the alleged violation.
11 (b) Hearing and Penalties. – If the employer fails to cure the alleged violation of
12 G.S. 64-26 in accordance with subsection (a) of this section, or if the employer is not entitled to
13 the cure period under subsection (a) of this section, the Commissioner shall hold a hearing to
14 determine if a violation of G.S. 64-26 has occurred and, upon a determination of a violation,
15 impose civil penalties as follows:
16 (1) First violation. – For a violation of G.S. 64-26 that occurs when the
17 Commissioner has not previously issued an order against the employer
18 pursuant to this subsection or former G.S. 64-31, the Commissioner may order
19 the employer to pay a civil penalty of up to one thousand dollars ($1,000) for
20 each required employee verification the employer failed to make.
21 (2) Second violation. – For a violation of G.S. 64-26 that occurs after the
22 Commissioner has issued an order against the employer pursuant to
23 subdivision (1) of this subsection or former G.S. 64-31, the Commissioner
24 shall order the employer to pay a civil penalty of not less than two thousand
25 five hundred dollars ($2,500) and not more than ten thousand dollars
26 ($10,000), regardless of the number of required employee verifications the
27 employer failed to make.
28 (3) Third or subsequent violations. – For a violation of G.S. 64-26 that occurs
29 after the Commissioner has issued an order against the employer pursuant to
30 subdivision (2) of this subsection or former G.S. 64-32, the Commissioner
31 shall order the employer to pay a civil penalty of not less than ten thousand
32 dollars ($10,000) and not more than twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000)
33 for each required employee verification the employer failed to make. The
34 Commissioner may also refer the matter to the Attorney General for
35 appropriate enforcement action.
36 (c) Notice to Law Enforcement. – If, during the course of a hearing pursuant to subsection
37 (b) of this section, the Commissioner concludes that there is a reasonable likelihood that an
38 employee is an unauthorized alien, the Commissioner shall notify the following entities of the
39 possible presence of an unauthorized alien:
40 (1) United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement.
41 (2) Local law enforcement agencies."
