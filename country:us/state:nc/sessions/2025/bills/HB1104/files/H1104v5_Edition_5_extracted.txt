12 IMPROVE DATA COLLECTION AND FURTHER STUDY
13 SECTION 1.(a) The North Carolina Collaboratory (Collaboratory) shall study
14 relevant statutes, judicial and clinical practices, and available technological resources to identify
15 areas for systemic improvement in the involuntary commitment (IVC) process in the State. This
16 study shall identify existing gaps in the State's current IVC process and shall provide specific
17 recommendations to address or eliminate those gaps and ensure that individuals subject to
18 involuntary commitment receive timely, data-driven, and accessible support. No later than
19 December 1, 2026, the Collaboratory shall submit a progress report on the study, and no later
20 than March 1, 2027, the Collaboratory shall submit a final report on the study, to the Joint
21 Legislative Committee on Health and Human Services on the results of the study, which shall
22 include, at a minimum, all of the following:
23 (1) A comprehensive evaluation of the legal and operational frameworks
24 governing involuntary commitment in the State to provide formal
25 recommendations for systemic improvement. This evaluation shall focus on
26 the following:
27 a. Ensuring that judicial officers receive timely clinical data from
28 examiners to make informed, legally sound decisions regarding an
29 individual's safety and treatment needs.
30 b. Parameters for training judges and magistrates on community-based
31 services, such as Treatment Accountability for Safer Communities
32 (TASC), "Community Treatment" teams, and Forensic Assertive

1 Community Treatment (FACT) teams, to bolster treatment compliance
2 and reduce recidivism.
3 c. Collaborating with the University of North Carolina School of
4 Government to develop proposed clinical workflows, transport
5 guidance, and bench cards that ensure successful referrals across all
6 agencies.
7 d. The update of electronic examination forms, affidavits, and petitions
8 to capture consistent, high-quality data statewide.

33 PLAN TO UTILIZE MOBILE CRISIS UNITS TO COMPLETE IVC FIRST
34 EXAMINATIONS
35 SECTION 3.(a) The Local Management Entities/Managed Care Organizations
36 (LME/MCOs) and the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) are directed to
37 develop a plan to use mobile crisis units to enhance the efficiency of the involuntary commitment
38 process. In developing this plan, the LME/MCOs and DHHS shall consult with relevant
39 stakeholders. The plan shall include at least all of the following:
40 (1) The development of a statewide coverage model that uses in-person clinicians
41 or on-call licensed clinicians in mobile crisis units to complete the first
42 examination for involuntary commitment.
43 (2) Recommendations to improve mobile crisis response.
44 (3) An analysis of the funding necessary to implement the plan, including costs
45 associated with training and technology.
46 (4) Any additional information that the LME/MCOs and DHHS deem relevant to
47 improving mobile crisis units.
48 SECTION 3.(b) No later than October 1, 2026, the LME/MCOs and DHHS shall
49 submit a report on the plan as required in subsection (a) of this section to the Joint Legislative
50 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services and the Fiscal Research Division.

51 improving the use and effectiveness of outpatient commitment.

23 STUDY LEGAL STANDARDS FOR INVOLUNTARY COMMITMENT AND
24 INCAPACITY TO PROCEED
25 SECTION 7.(a) The North Carolina Collaboratory (Collaboratory) shall conduct a
26 comprehensive study of the differing legal standards governing involuntary commitment and
27 incapacity to proceed to identify statutory revisions that would enhance each system's
28 effectiveness and advance public safety for all individuals involved. In developing this study, the
29 Collaboratory shall consult with relevant stakeholders. This study shall include recommendations
30 for statutory changes to address inconsistent terminology in the governing statutes and clarify
31 procedures for the transition of individuals between systems.
32 SECTION 7.(b) No later than December 1, 2026, the Collaboratory shall submit a
33 progress report on the study as required in subsection (a) of this section to the Joint Legislative
34 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services. No later than March 1, 2027, the
35 Collaboratory shall submit a final report on the study as required in subsection (a) of this section
36 to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services.

38 WORKING GROUP TO ADDRESS MENTAL HEALTH AND CRIMINAL JUSTICE
39 SYSTEMS OPERATING AS A "REVOLVING DOOR"
40 SECTION 8.(a) The North Carolina Department of Health and Human Services
41 (DHHS) shall establish a working group composed of representatives from the Administrative
42 Office of the Courts (AOC), and other stakeholders, to examine the systemic factors contributing
43 to the prevalent "revolving door" pattern in which individuals cycle repeatedly through arrest,
44 detention, or involuntary commitment, only to be released back into the community without
45 sustained stabilization or support. The purpose of the working group is to identify gaps, evaluate
46 current practices, and recommend strategies to interrupt repeated crises and reduce avoidable
47 recidivism, in addition to what has previously been examined and reported pursuant to Section
48 6.1 of S.L. 2025-27.
49 SECTION 8.(b) Beginning on January 1, 2027, and quarterly thereafter, DHHS shall
50 report on the findings and recommendations of the working group to the Joint Legislative
51 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services and the Fiscal Research Division.
