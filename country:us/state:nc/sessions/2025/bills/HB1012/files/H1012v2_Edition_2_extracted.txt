8 PART I. TITLE AND SCOPE OF ACT
9 SECTION 1.1. Title. – This act shall be known as "The Disaster Recovery Act of
10 2025 – Part II."
11 SECTION 1.2. Maximum Amounts; Effectuate Savings. – The appropriations and
12 allocations made in this act are for maximum amounts necessary to implement this act. Savings
13 shall be effected where the total amounts appropriated or allocated are not required to implement
14 this act.
15 SECTION 1.3. Scope. – Unless otherwise provided, Part II of this act applies to the
16 North Carolina counties in the affected area, as defined in Section 1.4 of this act.
17 SECTION 1.4. Definitions. – Unless otherwise provided, the following definitions
18 apply in this act:
19 (1) Affected area. – The counties designated before, on, or after the effective date
20 of this act under a major disaster declaration by the President of the United
21 States under the Stafford Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Helene.
22 (2) FEMA. – The Federal Emergency Management Agency.
23 (3) Helene Fund. – The Hurricane Helene Disaster Recovery Fund established in
24 Section 4.1 of S.L. 2024-51.
25 (4) HUD. – The United States Department of Housing and Urban Development.
26 (5) NCEM. – The Division of Emergency Management of the Department of
27 Public Safety.
28 (6) NCSBE. – The North Carolina State Board of Elections.
29 (7) OSBM. – The Office of State Budget and Management.
30 SECTION 1.5. Findings for Western North Carolina Wildfires. – The North Carolina
31 General Assembly finds that several wildfires have burned thousands of acres in Western North
32 Carolina in 2025, and efforts were hampered to extinguish those wildfires due to Hurricane
33 Helene. Dry conditions in the region and storm debris left from Hurricane Helene have
34 contributed to the many wildfires that have burned. On March 26, 2025, Governor Josh Stein
35 declared a state of emergency due to the current and anticipated impacts from those wildfires in

47 SECTION 2B.1.(a) Program; Purpose. – There is established the Hurricane Helene
48 Economic Recovery Grant Program (Program) to be administered by the Department of Revenue.
49 The Department shall adopt guidelines providing for the administration of the Program in
50 accordance with this section and shall prioritize job retention, economic viability, and
51 commercial stability when awarding grants under this section. The purpose of the Program is to
Page 6 House Bill 1012-Second Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 ensure the economic viability of Western North Carolina in the aftermath of Hurricane Helene
2 by providing a one-time grant to qualifying businesses that meet the conditions of this section.
3 SECTION 2B.1.(b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
4 (1) Department. – The Department of Revenue.
5 (2) Economic loss. – A reduction in revenue experienced by a qualifying business
6 as a result of Hurricane Helene and determined as the difference between the
7 business's average combined gross receipts for the fourth calendar quarter of
8 2022 and 2023 as compared to the business's gross receipts for the fourth
9 calendar quarter of 2024.
10 (3) Gross receipts. – North Carolina gross receipts, determined as the sum of all
11 amounts listed on line 1 of Form E-500, Sales and Use Tax Return, for sales
12 occurring during a specified time period.
13 (4) Program. – The Hurricane Helene Economic Recovery Grant Program created
14 by this section.
15 (5) Qualifying business. – A business that meets all of the following
16 requirements:
17 a. Is subject to income tax under Article 4 of Chapter 105 of the General
18 Statutes.
19 b. Experienced economic loss of at least twenty-five thousand dollars
20 ($25,000).
21 c. Has been registered to do business in the State and actively engaged
22 in business for a minimum of two years immediately preceding
23 application for grant funding under this section.
24 d. Has not filed a bankruptcy petition or otherwise initiated a bankruptcy
25 proceeding during the five years immediately preceding application
26 for grant funding under this section.
27 e. Is located in a county that qualifies, in whole or in part, for FEMA
28 Public Assistance Categories C through G.
29 SECTION 2B.1.(c) Eligibility; Verification. – A qualifying business is eligible for
30 a grant under the Program. Prior to awarding a grant to an applicant business, the Department
31 shall verify the applicant's economic loss.
32 SECTION 2B.1.(d) Applications; Supplemental Documentation. – The business
33 must apply to the Department for a grant during the time frame and on a form prescribed by the
34 Department and the business must provide all supporting documentation required by the
35 Department. The Department may accept applications until the funds available under the
36 Program have been awarded and all funds shall be awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.
37 SECTION 2B.1.(e) Grant Amount. – The grant amount to a qualifying business
38 under the Program is equal to the lesser of (i) twenty-five percent (25%) of the qualifying
39 business's economic loss or (ii) seventy-five thousand dollars ($75,000).
40 SECTION 2B.1.(f) Eligible Uses. – Grants awarded under the Program shall be used
41 by qualifying businesses for working capital and normal business expenses consistent with the
42 allowable use of loan proceeds under the Economic Injury Disaster Loan program of the Small
43 Business Administration. The Department shall adopt guidelines detailing the eligible use of
44 grant proceeds under the Program in accordance with the requirements of this subsection.
45 SECTION 2B.1.(g) Grant Program Limit. – The total of all funds granted under the
46 Program may not exceed the amount allocated to the Program under this act.
47 SECTION 2B.1.(h) Clawback. – If a business receives a grant under the Program
48 for which it is ineligible, the business forfeits the grant awarded under this section and is liable
49 for the amounts received. An award forfeited under this section shall bear interest at the rate
50 determined in accordance with G.S. 105-241.21 as of the date of receipt until repaid. Failure to
51 pay an award forfeited shall be collected by a civil action in the name of the State, and the

20 SUBPART II-C. HURRICANE HELENE LOCAL GOVERNMENT CAPITAL GRANT
21 PROGRAM
22 SECTION 2C.1.(a) Establishment. – There is established the Hurricane Helene
23 Local Government Capital Grant Program (Program) to be administered by the Office of State
24 Budget and Management. The purpose of the Program is to disburse grants to eligible recipients
25 for capital projects in the affected area in accordance with the Program requirements set forth in
26 this section.
27 SECTION 2C.1.(b) Criteria; Uses. – OSBM shall disburse grants to eligible
28 recipients under a damage per capita formula developed from FEMA's damage estimates and
29 reports in the affected area from Hurricane Helene. The grants shall be used for capital projects
30 to repair, renovate, or replace infrastructure damaged by Hurricane Helene. These capital projects
31 must be projects that have been denied eligibility for FEMA Public Assistance reimbursement.
32 SECTION 2C.1.(c) Eligible Recipients. – To be eligible for a grant under the
33 Program, a recipient shall be a unit of local government or a federally recognized tribe on lands
34 held in trust by the United States located in the affected area.
35 SECTION 2C.1.(d) Prioritization. – OSBM shall prioritize grants to an eligible
36 recipient with a population of 300,000 or fewer based on the 2023 Certified County Population
37 Estimates from the State Demographer and that qualify, in whole or in part, for FEMA Public
38 Assistance Categories C through G.
39 SECTION 2C.1.(e) Maximum Grant Awards. – OSBM shall not award more than
40 the following percentages of the funds appropriated for the Program:
41 (1) Twenty percent (20%) to one county, including grants to units of local
42 government within that county.
43 (2) Five percent (5%) per eligible recipient.
44 (3) Five percent (5%) per zip code if that zip code only qualifies for FEMA Public
45 Assistance Categories A through B.
46 SECTION 2C.1.(f) Reporting Requirement. – OSBM shall submit a report to the
47 chairs of the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on General Government and the Joint
48 Legislative Emergency Management Oversight Committee and the Fiscal Research Division no
49 later than six months after the application period for this Program opens, and every six months
50 thereafter until all funds are expended. The report shall include, at a minimum, all of the
51 following:

5 SUBPART II-D. HURRICANE HELENE FLOOD MITIGATION GRANT PROGRAM
6 SECTION 2D.1.(a) Establishment. – There is established the Hurricane Helene
7 Flood Mitigation Grant Program (Program). The funds appropriated in this act to NCEM for the
8 Disaster Relief and Mitigation Fund (DRMF) shall be used to provide funds to the Program to
9 provide flood mitigation grants to units of local government and nonprofit organizations to
10 undertake flood mitigation projects in the affected area. Units of local government may also apply
11 for engineering assistance grants.
12 SECTION 2D.1.(b) Flood Mitigation Grants. – Grants disbursed under the Program
13 for eligible flood mitigation grants must be for projects that reduce the risk of future damage
14 from flooding through structural or nonstructural measures through the following:
15 (1) Culvert or bridge retrofits or replacements.
16 (2) Stormwater and drainage system improvements.
17 (3) Relocation of at-risk infrastructure.
18 (4) Hardening of critical facilities and utilities.
19 SECTION 2D.1.(c) Engineering Assistance Grants. – Grants disbursed under the
20 Program may also be used by units of local government to identify and design shovel-ready
21 projects related to flood mitigation.
22 SECTION 2D.1.(d) Cost-Share. – There is no cost-share or matching requirement
23 by recipients to receive grants under the Program. Recipients of grants will be paid under a
24 reimbursement model for costs incurred for eligible flood mitigation or engineering assistance
25 grants.
26 SECTION 2D.1.(e) Maximum Grant Awards. – NCEM shall not award a grant
27 larger than (i) ten percent (10%) to a single recipient or (ii) twenty percent (20%) to a single
28 county, of the total amount of funds appropriated in this act for the Program.
29 SECTION 2D.1.(f) Administrative Expenses. – NCEM may retain up to one and
30 one-half percent (1.5%) of the funds appropriated for the Program created by this section for
31 administrative expenses.
32 SECTION 2D.1.(g) DRMF Requirements. – To the extent the Program requirements
33 described in this section conflict with the DRMF requirements, this section controls.

35 SUBPART II-E. FARM INFRASTRUCTURE DISASTER RECOVERY PROGRAM
36 SECTION 2E.1.(a) Establishment. – There is established the Farm Infrastructure
37 Disaster Recovery Program (Program) within the Department of Agriculture and Consumer
38 Services to repair, rebuild, and restore farm infrastructure damaged by Hurricane Helene. The
39 Department shall open an application period of six months within 30 days of this section
40 becoming law to allow persons in the affected area to apply for funds for these purposes. The
41 applicant must verify to the Department that the losses were directly caused by Hurricane Helene.
42 SECTION 2E.1.(b) Audit. – The Department may audit the financial and other
43 records of each recipient of funds in order to ensure that the funds are used in accordance with
44 the requirements of this Program. The Department may require any documentation or proof it
45 considers necessary to efficiently administer this Program, including the ownership structure of
46 each entity, the social security numbers of each owner, and any documentation of insurance
47 payments or federal funds for verified losses. In order to verify losses, the Department may
48 require the submission of dated, signed, and continuous records.
49 SECTION 2E.1.(c) Definition. – For the purposes of the Program, the term "farm
50 infrastructure" means fencing, greenhouses, barns, equipment, and farm roads, or other structures
51 or improvements used for farming purposes.

13 Program is to disburse grants to local airports and airfields in the affected area to (i) repair,
14 replace, and restore infrastructure damaged by Hurricane Helene and (ii) build and improve
15 capacity for emergency preparedness and disaster response for future natural disasters and
16 emergencies. NCEM shall consult with the Department of Transportation if grant applications
17 concern airport roads or other infrastructure, as necessary.
18 SECTION 2F.1.(b) Grants for Damaged Infrastructure. – NCEM shall disburse
19 grants to eligible recipients to repair, replace, and restore infrastructure on airport property,
20 including terminals, runways, gates, hangars, curtilage structures, airport roads, and other
21 structures damaged by Hurricane Helene. Applicants for these grants must pursue insurance
22 coverage and federal aid prior to being awarded a grant for these purposes.
23 SECTION 2F.1.(c) Grants for Capacity for Emergency Preparedness. – NCEM shall
24 disburse grants to eligible recipients to improve the capacity for emergency preparedness and
25 disaster response through capital projects and infrastructure and communication improvements
26 on airfield property. Eligible recipients must demonstrate how the project or improvement will
27 increase emergency preparedness and disaster response.
28 SECTION 2F.1.(d) Eligible Recipient. – An eligible recipient for a grant awarded
29 under this Program shall be limited to persons or units of local government that own an airport
30 that (i) is currently in operation or (ii) was in operation prior to Hurricane Helene and not
31 currently in operation but for damage from Hurricane Helene and meet the following criteria:
32 (1) Must have a fuel truck available 24 hours per day and seven days per week.
33 (2) The county in which the airport resides must enter into an agreement with the
34 State giving the State untethered access to any facilities provided under the
35 Program.
36 (3) Must have on-site aviation mechanics that offer services to the public.
37 (4) During a state of emergency, the airport must be able to be open 24 hours per
38 day and seven days per week.
39 (5) Does not offer passenger service.
40 SECTION 2F.1.(e) Prioritization. – NCEM shall prioritize applicants with facilities
41 that demonstrate adequate runway capacity, storage capacity, and personnel that can substantially
42 contribute to enhanced regional emergency and disaster readiness. These prioritized applicants
43 shall also be capable of facilitating access of various forms and sizes of aircraft providing
44 adequate takeoff and landing clearance.
45 SECTION 2F.1.(f) Maximum Grant Award. – Grants awarded under this Program
46 shall not exceed five million dollars ($5,000,000) per eligible recipient. A county in the affected
47 area shall not receive more than two grants from the Program.
