8 PART I. TITLE AND SCOPE OF ACT
9 SECTION 1.1. Title. – This act shall be known as "The Disaster Recovery Act of
10 2025 – Part II."
11 SECTION 1.2. Maximum Amounts; Effectuate Savings. – The appropriations and
12 allocations made in this act are for maximum amounts necessary to implement this act. Savings
13 shall be effected where the total amounts appropriated or allocated are not required to implement
14 this act.
15 SECTION 1.3. Scope. – Unless otherwise provided, Part II of this act applies to the
16 North Carolina counties in the affected area, as defined in Section 1.4 of this act.
17 SECTION 1.4. Definitions. – Unless otherwise provided, the following definitions
18 apply in this act:
19 (1) Affected area. – The counties designated before, on, or after the effective date
20 of this act under a major disaster declaration by the President of the United
21 States under the Stafford Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Helene.
22 (2) FEMA. – The Federal Emergency Management Agency.
23 (3) Helene Fund. – The Hurricane Helene Disaster Recovery Fund established in
24 Section 4.1 of S.L. 2024-51.
25 (4) HUD. – The United States Department of Housing and Urban Development.
26 (5) NCEM. – The Division of Emergency Management of the Department of
27 Public Safety.
28 (6) NCSBE. – The North Carolina State Board of Elections.
29 (7) OSBM. – The Office of State Budget and Management.
30 SECTION 1.5. Findings for Western North Carolina Wildfires. – The North Carolina
31 General Assembly finds that several wildfires have burned thousands of acres in Western North
32 Carolina in 2025, and efforts were hampered to extinguish those wildfires due to Hurricane
33 Helene. Dry conditions in the region and storm debris left from Hurricane Helene have
34 contributed to the many wildfires that have burned. On March 26, 2025, Governor Josh Stein
35 declared a state of emergency due to the current and anticipated impacts from those wildfires in

36 SUBPART II-B. HURRICANE HELENE ECONOMIC RECOVERY GRANT
37 PROGRAM
38 SECTION 2B.1.(a) Program; Purpose. – There is established the Hurricane Helene
39 Economic Recovery Grant Program (Program) to be administered by the Department of
40 Commerce. The Department shall adopt guidelines providing for the administration of the
41 Program in accordance with this section and shall prioritize job retention, economic viability,
42 and commercial stability within the affected area when awarding grants under this section. The
43 purpose of the Program is to ensure the economic viability of Western North Carolina in the
44 aftermath of Hurricane Helene by providing a one-time grant to qualifying businesses in the
45 affected area that meet the conditions of this section.
46 SECTION 2B.1.(b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
47 (1) Department. – The Department of Commerce.
48 (2) Economic loss. – A reduction in revenue experienced by a qualifying business
49 as a result of Hurricane Helene and determined as the difference between the
50 business's average combined gross receipts for the fourth calendar quarter of
Page 6 House Bill 1012-First Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 2022 and 2023 as compared to the business's gross receipts for the fourth
2 calendar quarter of 2024.
3 (3) Gross receipts. – North Carolina gross receipts, determined as the sum of all
4 amounts listed on line 1 of Form E-500, Sales and Use Tax Return, for sales
5 occurring during a specified time period.
6 (4) Program. – The Hurricane Helene Economic Recovery Grant Program created
7 by this section.
8 (5) Qualifying business. – A business located in the affected area that meets all of
9 the following requirements:
10 a. Is subject to income tax under Article 4 of Chapter 105 of the General
11 Statutes.
12 b. Experienced economic loss of at least twenty-five thousand dollars
13 ($25,000).
14 c. Has been registered to do business in the State and actively engaged
15 in business in the affected area for a minimum of two years
16 immediately preceding application for grant funding under this
17 section.
18 d. Has not filed a bankruptcy petition or otherwise initiated a bankruptcy
19 proceeding during the five years immediately preceding application
20 for grant funding under this section.
21 SECTION 2B.1.(c) Eligibility; Verification. – A qualifying business is eligible for
22 a grant under the Program. Prior to awarding a grant to an applicant business, the Department, in
23 consultation with the Department of Revenue, shall verify the applicant's economic loss.
24 Notwithstanding G.S. 105-259, the Department of Revenue may disclose tax information to the
25 Department for purposes of this subsection.
26 SECTION 2B.1.(d) Applications; Supplemental Documentation. – The business
27 must apply to the Department for a grant during the time frame and on a form prescribed by the
28 Department and the business must provide all supporting documentation required by the
29 Department. The Department may accept applications until the funds available under the
30 Program have been awarded and all funds shall be awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.
31 SECTION 2B.1.(e) Grant Amount. – The grant amount to a qualifying business
32 under the Program is equal to the lesser of (i) twenty-five percent (25%) of the qualifying
33 business's economic loss or (ii) seventy-five thousand dollars ($75,000).
34 SECTION 2B.1.(f) Eligible Uses. – Grants awarded under the Program shall be used
35 by qualifying businesses for working capital and normal business expenses consistent with the
36 allowable use of loan proceeds under the Economic Injury Disaster Loan program of the Small
37 Business Administration. The Department shall adopt guidelines detailing the eligible use of
38 grant proceeds under the Program in accordance with the requirements of this subsection.
39 SECTION 2B.1.(g) Grant Program Limit. – The total of all funds granted under the
40 Program may not exceed the amount allocated to the Program under this act.
41 SECTION 2B.1.(h) Clawback. – If a business receives a grant under the Program
42 for which it is ineligible, the business forfeits the grant awarded under this section and is liable
43 for the amounts received. An award forfeited under this section shall bear interest at the rate
44 determined in accordance with G.S. 105-241.21 as of the date of receipt until repaid. Failure to
45 pay an award forfeited shall be collected by a civil action in the name of the State, and the
46 recipient business shall pay the cost of the action. The Attorney General, at the request of the
47 Secretary of Commerce, shall institute the action in the proper court for the collection of the
48 award forfeited, including interest thereon. A grant under the Program authorized in this section
49 shall be conditioned on a recipient business maintaining operations for a minimum of one year
50 following receipt of the grant, and the Department shall clawback a proportionate amount of the
51 grant for any portion of the one year the recipient business does not maintain business operations.

14 SUBPART II-C. HURRICANE HELENE LOCAL GOVERNMENT CAPITAL GRANT
15 PROGRAM
16 SECTION 2C.1.(a) Establishment. – There is established the Hurricane Helene
17 Local Government Capital Grant Program (Program) to be administered by the Office of State
18 Budget and Management. The purpose of the Program is to disburse grants to eligible recipients
19 for capital projects in the affected area in accordance with the Program requirements set forth in
20 this section.
21 SECTION 2C.1.(b) Criteria; Uses. – OSBM shall disburse grants to eligible
22 recipients under a damage per capita formula developed from FEMA's damage estimates and
23 reports in the affected area from Hurricane Helene. The grants shall be used for capital projects
24 to repair, renovate, or replace infrastructure damaged by Hurricane Helene. These capital projects
25 must be projects that have been denied eligibility for FEMA Public Assistance reimbursement.
26 SECTION 2C.1.(c) Eligible Recipients. – To be eligible for a grant under the
27 Program, a recipient shall be a unit of local government or a federally recognized tribe on lands
28 held in trust by the United States located in the affected area.
29 SECTION 2C.1.(d) Prioritization. – OSBM shall prioritize grants to an eligible
30 recipient with a population of 300,000 or fewer based on the 2023 Certified County Population
31 Estimates from the State Demographer and that qualify, in whole or in part, for FEMA Public
32 Assistance Categories C through G.
33 SECTION 2C.1.(e) Maximum Grant Awards. – OSBM shall not award more than
34 the following percentages of the funds appropriated for the Program:
35 (1) Twenty percent (20%) to one county, including grants to units of local
36 government within that county.
37 (2) Five percent (5%) per eligible recipient.
38 (3) Five percent (5%) per zip code if that zip code only qualifies for FEMA Public
39 Assistance Categories A through B.
40 SECTION 2C.1.(f) Reporting Requirement. – OSBM shall submit a report to the
41 chairs of the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on General Government and the Joint
42 Legislative Emergency Management Oversight Committee and the Fiscal Research Division no
43 later than six months after the application period for this Program opens, and every six months
44 thereafter until all funds are expended. The report shall include, at a minimum, all of the
45 following:
46 (1) The number of grants disbursed and to which eligible recipients.
47 (2) The proposed uses for each grant.
48 (3) The average and median amounts of the grants disbursed under the Program.

1 SECTION 2D.1.(a) Establishment. – There is established the Hurricane Helene
2 Flood Mitigation Grant Program (Program). The funds appropriated in this act to NCEM for the
3 Disaster Relief and Mitigation Fund (DRMF) shall be used to provide funds to the Program to
4 provide flood mitigation grants to units of local government and nonprofit organizations to
5 undertake flood mitigation projects in the affected area. Units of local government may also apply
6 for engineering assistance grants.
7 SECTION 2D.1.(b) Flood Mitigation Grants. – Grants disbursed under the Program
8 for eligible flood mitigation grants must be for projects that reduce the risk of future damage
9 from flooding through structural or nonstructural measures through the following:
10 (1) Culvert or bridge retrofits or replacements.
11 (2) Stormwater and drainage system improvements.
12 (3) Relocation of at-risk infrastructure.
13 (4) Hardening of critical facilities and utilities.
14 SECTION 2D.1.(c) Engineering Assistance Grants. – Grants disbursed under the
15 Program may also be used by units of local government to identify and design shovel-ready
16 projects related to flood mitigation.
17 SECTION 2D.1.(d) Cost-Share. – There is no cost-share or matching requirement
18 by recipients to receive grants under the Program. Recipients of grants will be paid under a
19 reimbursement model for costs incurred for eligible flood mitigation or engineering assistance
20 grants.
21 SECTION 2D.1.(e) Maximum Grant Awards. – NCEM shall not award a grant
22 larger than (i) ten percent (10%) to a single recipient or (ii) twenty percent (20%) to a single
23 county, of the total amount of funds appropriated in this act for the Program.
24 SECTION 2D.1.(f) Administrative Expenses. – NCEM may retain up to one and
25 one-half percent (1.5%) of the funds appropriated for the Program created by this section for
26 administrative expenses.
27 SECTION 2D.1.(g) DRMF Requirements. – To the extent the Program requirements
28 described in this section conflict with the DRMF requirements, this section controls.

30 SUBPART II-E. FARM INFRASTRUCTURE DISASTER RECOVERY PROGRAM
31 SECTION 2E.1.(a) Establishment. – There is established the Farm Infrastructure
32 Disaster Recovery Program (Program) within the Department of Agriculture and Consumer
33 Services to repair, rebuild, and restore farm infrastructure damaged by Hurricane Helene. The
34 Department shall open an application period of six months within 30 days of this section
35 becoming law to allow persons in the affected area to apply for funds for these purposes. The
36 applicant must verify to the Department that the losses were directly caused by Hurricane Helene.
37 SECTION 2E.1.(b) Audit. – The Department may audit the financial and other
38 records of each recipient of funds in order to ensure that the funds are used in accordance with
39 the requirements of this Program. The Department may require any documentation or proof it
40 considers necessary to efficiently administer this Program, including the ownership structure of
41 each entity, the social security numbers of each owner, and any documentation of insurance
42 payments or federal funds for verified losses. In order to verify losses, the Department may
43 require the submission of dated, signed, and continuous records.
44 SECTION 2E.1.(c) Definition. – For the purposes of the Program, the term "farm
45 infrastructure" means fencing, greenhouses, barns, equipment, and farm roads, or other structures
46 or improvements used for farming purposes.
47 SECTION 2E.1.(d) Reporting Requirement. – The Department shall report to the
48 Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Agriculture and Natural and Economic Resources six
49 months after the application period opens for the Program, and every six months thereafter,
50 detailing, at a minimum, the number and type of applicants, structures covered, and average grant
51 amount.

5 SUBPART II-F. AERIAL ASSET ACCESSIBILITY GRANT PROGRAM
6 SECTION 2F.1.(a) Program; Purpose. – There is established the Aerial Asset
7 Accessibility Grant Program (Program) to be administered by NCEM. The purpose of the
8 Program is to disburse grants to local airports and airfields in the affected area to (i) repair,
9 replace, and restore infrastructure damaged by Hurricane Helene and (ii) build and improve
10 capacity for emergency preparedness and disaster response for future natural disasters and
11 emergencies. NCEM shall consult with the Department of Transportation if grant applications
12 concern airport roads or other infrastructure, as necessary.
13 SECTION 2F.1.(b) Grants for Damaged Infrastructure. – NCEM shall disburse
14 grants to eligible recipients to repair, replace, and restore infrastructure on airport property,
15 including terminals, runways, gates, hangars, curtilage structures, airport roads, and other
16 structures damaged by Hurricane Helene. Applicants for these grants must pursue insurance
17 coverage and federal aid prior to being awarded a grant for these purposes.
18 SECTION 2F.1.(c) Grants for Capacity for Emergency Preparedness. – NCEM shall
19 disburse grants to eligible recipients to improve the capacity for emergency preparedness and
20 disaster response through capital projects and infrastructure and communication improvements
21 on airfield property. Eligible recipients must demonstrate how the project or improvement will
22 increase emergency preparedness and disaster response.
23 SECTION 2F.1.(d) Eligible Recipient. – An eligible recipient for a grant awarded
24 under this Program shall be limited to persons or units of local government that own an airport
25 that (i) is currently in operation or (ii) was in operation prior to Hurricane Helene and not
26 currently in operation but for damage from Hurricane Helene and meet the following criteria:
27 (1) Must have a fuel truck available 24 hours per day and seven days per week.
28 (2) The county in which the airport resides must enter into an agreement with the
29 State giving the State untethered access to any facilities provided under the
30 Program.
31 (3) Must have on-site aviation mechanics that offer services to the public.
32 (4) During a state of emergency, the airport must be able to be open 24 hours per
33 day and seven days per week.
34 (5) Does not offer passenger service.
35 SECTION 2F.1.(e) Prioritization. – NCEM shall prioritize applicants with facilities
36 that demonstrate adequate runway capacity, storage capacity, and personnel that can substantially
37 contribute to enhanced regional emergency and disaster readiness. These prioritized applicants
38 shall also be capable of facilitating access of various forms and sizes of aircraft providing
39 adequate takeoff and landing clearance.
40 SECTION 2F.1.(f) Maximum Grant Award. – Grants awarded under this Program
41 shall not exceed five million dollars ($5,000,000) per eligible recipient. A county in the affected
42 area shall not receive more than two grants from the Program.
43 SECTION 2F.1.(g) Reporting Requirement. – NCEM shall submit a report to the
44 chairs of the Joint Legislative Emergency Management Oversight Committee and the Fiscal
45 Research Division no later than six months after the application period for this Program opens,
46 and every six months thereafter until all funds are expended. The report shall include, at a
47 minimum, all of the following:
48 (1) The number of grants disbursed and to which eligible recipients.
49 (2) The proposed uses for each grant.
50 (3) The average and median amounts of the grants disbursed under the Program.

9 SECTION 4.1.(f) Directive. – The Governor shall ensure that funds allocated in this
10 act are expended in a manner that does not adversely affect any person's or entity's eligibility for
11 federal funds that are made available, or that are anticipated to be made available, as a result of
12 natural disasters. The Governor shall also, to the extent practicable, avoid using State funds to
13 cover costs that will be, or likely will be, covered by federal funds.
14 SECTION 4.1.(g) Continuation of Allocation Reporting Requirements. – OSBM
15 shall add the appropriations and allocations provided for in this act to the reporting requirements
16 set forth in Section 4.1(g) of S.L. 2025-2.
17 SECTION 4.1.(h) Continuation of State Auditor Oversight. – The Office of the

19 S.L. 2025-2. The State Auditor shall include all funds appropriated and allocated under this act
20 in their report to the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations and include the
21 expenditure of these funds in the public dashboard as set forth in Sections 4.2(c) and (d) of S.L.
22 2025-2.
