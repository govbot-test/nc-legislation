Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/30/25
Short Title: The Vehicle Registration Convenience Act. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
April 1, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT ENACTING THE VEHICLE REGISTRATION CONVENIENCE ACT
3 AUTHORIZING VEHICLE OWNERS TO ELECT THE MONTH OF REGISTRATION
4 RENEWAL.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6
7 PART I. AUTHORIZE VEHICLE OWNERS TO ELECT MONTH OF REGISTRATION
8 RENEWAL
9 SECTION 1.(a) This act shall be known as "The Vehicle Registration Convenience
10 Act."
11 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 20-66 reads as rewritten:
12 "§ 20-66. Renewal of vehicle registration.
13 (a) Annual Renewal. – When a vehicle registration is initially issued with a plate that is
14 not a calendar-year plate, the owner shall elect a month within 12 months of issuance upon which
15 the vehicle's registration expires. The initial period of registration expires at midnight on the last
16 day of the month elected by the owner. The registration of a vehicle must otherwise be renewed
17 annually.
18 (a1) Initial Registration Fee. – The fee for an initial period of registration elected by the
19 owner pursuant to subsection (a) of this section that is less than a year in duration is one-twelfth
20 of the annual fee provided in Part 7 of this Article multiplied by the number of full months in the
21 period beginning with the first full month following the month of issuance until the date the
22 registration expires, rounded to the nearest dollar.
23 (a2) Notice. – In accordance with G.S. 105-330.5(b), upon receiving written consent from
24 the owner of the vehicle, the Division may send to an email address provided by the owner of the
25 vehicle any required notice of renewal electronically to an e-mail address provided by the owner
26 of the vehicle.renewal.
27 (a3) Renewal Application. – To renew the registration of a vehicle, the owner of the
28 vehicle must file an application with the Division and pay the required registration fee. The owner
29 of a vehicle registered in North Carolina may renew that vehicle registration by mail. The
30 Division may receive and grant an application for renewal of registration at any time before the
31 registration expires.
32 …."
33 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 20-52 reads as rewritten:
34 "§ 20-52. Application for registration and certificate of title.
35 (a) An owner of a vehicle subject to registration must apply to the Division for a
36 certificate of title, a registration plate, and a registration card for the vehicle. To apply, an owner
*H613-v-2*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 must complete an application provided by the Division. The application shall contain a preprinted
2 option that co-owners may use to title the vehicle as a joint tenancy with right of survivorship.
3 The co-owners' designation of a joint tenancy with right of survivorship on the application shall
4 be valid notwithstanding whether this designation appears on the assignment of title. The
5 application must request all of the following information and may request other information the
6 Division considers necessary:
7 …
8 (8) A statement indicating the month of registration renewal the owner elects
9 under G.S. 20-66(a).
10 …."
11 SECTION 1.(d) G.S. 105-330.5(a1) reads as rewritten:
12 "(a1) Proration. – When a new registration is obtained for a registered classified motor
13 vehicle that is registered under the annual system, the taxes are prorated for the remainder of the
14 calendar year. The amount of prorated taxes due is the product of the proration fraction and the
15 taxes computed according to subsection (a) of this section. The numerator of the proration
16 fraction is the number of full months remaining in the calendar year following the registration
17 application date and the denominator of the fraction is 12. When a new registration is obtained
18 for a registered classified motor vehicle, and the owner elects an initial period of registration that
19 is less than a year in duration under G.S. 20-66(a), the taxes are prorated for the initial period of
20 registration. The amount of prorated taxes due is the product of the proration fraction and the
21 taxes computed according to subsection (a) of this section. The numerator of the proration
22 fraction is the number of full months in the initial period of registration and the denominator of
23 the fraction is 12."
24
25 PART II. EFFECTIVE DATE
26 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective January 1, 2026, and applies to applications
27 for vehicle registrations on or after that date.
