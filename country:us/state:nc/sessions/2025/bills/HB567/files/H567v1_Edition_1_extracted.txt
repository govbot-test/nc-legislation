GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Ensure Access to Biomarker Testing. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Wheatley, Penny, Campbell, and Moss (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Health, if favorable, Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations
of the House
March 31, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ENSURE ACCESS TO AN EARLY AND ACCURATE DIAGNOSIS OF
3 DEMENTIA IN ORDER TO IMPROVE ACCESS TO CARE AND SUPPORT SERVICES
4 FOR, ENHANCE THE QUALITY OF LIFE OF, AND REDUCE THE FINANCIAL
5 IMPACT OF THE CONDITION ON NORTH CAROLINIANS.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7
8 PART I. HEALTH BENEFIT PLAN COVERAGE OF BIOMARKER TESTING
9 SECTION 1.1.(a) Article 3 of Chapter 58 of the General Statutes is amended by
10 adding a new section to read:
11 "§ 58-3-216. Coverage of biomarker testing.
12 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
13 (1) Biomarker. – A characteristic that is objectively measured and evaluated as an
14 indicator of normal biological processes, pathogenic processes, or
15 pharmacologic responses to a specific therapeutic intervention, including
16 known gene-drug interactions for medication being considered for use or
17 already being administered. This term incudes gene mutations, characteristics
18 of genes, and protein expression.
19 (2) Biomarker testing. – The analysis of a patient's tissue, blood, or other
20 biospecimen for the presence of a biomarker. This term includes
21 single-analyte tests, multi-plex panel tests, protein expression, and whole
22 exome, whole genome, and whole transcriptome sequencing.
23 (3) Consensus statement. – A statement that is developed by an independent
24 multidisciplinary panel, aimed at specific clinical circumstances, and based
25 upon the best available evidence for the purpose of optimizing the outcomes
26 of clinical care.
27 (4) FDA. – The United States Food and Drug Administration.
28 (5) Reserved for future codification purposes.
29 (6) Independent multidisciplinary panel. – A multidisciplinary panel of experts
30 that utilizes a transparent methodology and reporting structure and that has a
31 conflict of interest policy.
32 (7) Independent organization or medical professional society. – An organization
33 or medical professional society that utilizes a transparent methodology and
34 reporting structure and that has a conflict of interest policy.
*H567-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (8) Reserved for future codification purposes.
2 (9) Nationally recognized clinical practice guidelines. – Evidence-based clinical
3 practice guidelines developed by independent organizations or medical
4 professional societies that establish standards of care that are informed by a
5 systematic review of evidence and an assessment of the benefits and risks of
6 alternative care options and that include recommendations intended to
7 optimize patient care.
8 (b) A health benefit plan shall provide coverage for biomarker testing for the purposes of
9 diagnosis, treatment, appropriate care management, or ongoing monitoring of an insured's
10 disease or condition when the testing is supported by medical and scientific evidence. At a
11 minimum, any of the following shall be considered support for biomarker testing:
12 (1) Label indications for a test that has been FDA-approved or FDA-cleared.
13 (2) Indicated tests for an FDA-approved drug.
14 (3) Warnings and precautions on an FDA-approved drug label.
15 (4) National coverage determinations developed by the Centers for Medicare and
16 Medicaid Services.
17 (5) Local coverage determinations developed by a Medicare Administrative
18 Contractor.
19 (6) Nationally recognized clinical practice guidelines and consensus statements.
20 (c) Coverage required under this section shall be provided in a manner that limits
21 disruption in patient care, including the need for multiple biopsies or biospecimen samples."
22 SECTION 1.1.(b) G.S. 58-3-215, as amended by subsection (c) of this section, reads
23 as rewritten:
24 "§ 58-3-215. Genetic and biomarker information in health insurance.
25 (a) Definitions. – As used The following definitions apply in this section:
26 (1) Biomarker. – A characteristic that is objectively measured and evaluated as an
27 indicator of normal biological processes, pathogenic processes, or
28 pharmacologic responses to a specific therapeutic intervention, including
29 known gene-drug interactions for medication being considered for use or
30 already being administered. This term incudes gene mutations, characteristics
31 of genes, and protein expression.
32 (1a) "Genetic information" means information Genetic information. – Information
33 about genes, gene products, or inherited characteristics that may derive from
34 an individual or a family member. "Genetic information" does not include the
35 results of routine physical measurements, blood chemistries, blood counts,
36 urine analyses, tests for abuse of drugs, and tests for the presence of human
37 immunodeficiency virus.
38 …
39 (c) No insurer shall:shall do any of the following:
40 (1) Raise the premium or contribution rates paid by a group for a group health
41 benefit plan on the basis of genetic or biomarker information obtained about
42 an individual member of the group.
43 (2) Refuse to issue or deliver a health benefit plan because of genetic or biomarker
44 information obtained about any person to be insured by the health benefit plan.
45 (3) Charge a higher premium rate or charge for a health benefit plan because of
46 genetic or biomarker information obtained about any person to be insured by
47 the health benefit plan.
48 …."
49 SECTION 1.1.(c) G.S. 58-3-215(a)(2) and G.S. 58-3-215(a)(3) are repealed.
50 SECTION 1.1.(d) This section is effective October 1, 2025, and applies to insurance
51 contracts issued, renewed, or amended on or after that date.

41 PART II. MEDICAID COVERAGE OF BIOMARKER TESTING
42 SECTION 2.1. The Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Health
43 Benefits (DHB), shall ensure coverage for biomarker testing under the laboratory services
44 clinical coverage policies 1S-1 through 1S-13 to the same extent those services are required to
45 be covered by a health benefit plan under G.S. 58-3-216. DHB shall ensure its policies and
46 procedures for the prior authorization of any service covered under this section are in compliance
47 with Section 2.2 of this act.
48 SECTION 2.2.(a) For purposes of this section, the term "urgent prior authorization
49 request" is defined as a request for which a time line for decision longer than 72 hours could
50 seriously jeopardize the beneficiary's life, health, or ability to attain, maintain, or regain
51 maximum function, in the opinion of the beneficiary's healthcare provider.

1 SECTION 2.2.(b) If prior authorization is required for any service covered under
2 Section 2.1 of this act, the time line for completion of a review of that prior authorization request
3 shall be as follows:
4 (1) For urgent prior authorization requests, DHB shall ensure that the prior
5 authorization request is either approved or denied and notice is given to the
6 beneficiary and beneficiary's healthcare provider within 24 hours after DHB
7 receives all information needed to complete a review of the request for prior
8 authorization.
9 (2) For non-urgent prior authorization requests, DHB shall ensure that the prior
10 authorization request is either approved or denied and notice is given to the
11 beneficiary and beneficiary's healthcare provider within 72 hours after DHB
12 receives all information needed to complete a review of the request for prior
13 authorization.
