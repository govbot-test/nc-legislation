Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Prepared Food Tax - Modification. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Wheatley, F. Jackson, and Charles Smith (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Finance, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 14, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO INCREASE THE GROSS RECEIPTS THRESHOLD FOR ARTISANAL
3 BAKERIES EXEMPT FROM THE SALES TAX.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 105-164.13B reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 105-164.13B. Food exempt from tax.
7 (a) State Exemption. – Food is exempt from the taxes imposed by this Article unless the
8 food is included in one of the subdivisions in this subsection. The following food items are
9 subject to tax:
10 (1) Repealed by Session Laws 2005-276, s. 33.10, effective October 1, 2005.
11 (2) Dietary supplements.
12 (3) Food sold through a vending machine.
13 (4) Prepared food, other than bakery items sold without eating utensils by an
14 artisan bakery. The term "bakery item" includes bread, rolls, buns, biscuits,
15 bagels, croissants, pastries, donuts, danish, cakes, tortes, pies, tarts, muffins,
16 bars, cookies, and tortillas. An artisan bakery is a bakery that meets all of the
17 following requirements:
18 a. It derives over eighty percent (80%) of its gross receipts from bakery
19 items.
20 b. Its annual gross receipts, combined with the gross receipts of all
21 related persons, do not exceed one million eight hundred thousand
22 dollars ($1,800,000). two million four hundred thousand dollars
23 ($2,400,000). For purposes of this subdivision, the term "related
24 person" means a person described in one of the relationships set forth
25 in section 267(b) or 707(b) of the Code.
26 (5) Soft drinks.
27 (6) Repealed by Session Laws 2003-284, s. 45.6B, effective January 1, 2004.
28 (7) Candy.
29 (b) Administration of Local Food Tax. – The Secretary must administer local sales and
30 use taxes imposed on food as if they were imposed under this Article. This applies to local taxes
31 on food imposed under Subchapter VIII of this Chapter and under Chapter 1096 of the 1967
32 Session Laws."
33 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to sales made
34 on or after that date.
