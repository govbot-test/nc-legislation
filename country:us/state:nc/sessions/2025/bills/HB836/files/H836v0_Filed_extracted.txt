HOUSE BILL DRH40456-MCf-65
Short Title: North Carolina Sound Money Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Brody.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ENACT THE NORTH CAROLINA SOUND MONEY ACT.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. G.S. 105-153.5 reads as rewritten:
5 "§ 105-153.5. Modifications to adjusted gross income.
6 …
7 (b) Other Deductions. – In calculating North Carolina taxable income, a taxpayer may
8 deduct from the taxpayer's adjusted gross income any of the following items that are included in
9 the taxpayer's adjusted gross income:
10 …
11 (17) Gain or loss, to the extent the loss is not deducted in arriving at adjusted gross
12 income, from the disposition of investment coins and investment metal
13 bullion. For purposes of this subdivision, the following definitions apply:
14 a. Investment coins. – Numismatic coins or other forms of money and
15 legal tender manufactured of metal under the laws of the United States
16 or any foreign nation with a fair market value greater than any
17 statutory or nominal value of such coins.
18 b. Investment metal bullion. – Any elementary precious metal that has
19 been put through a process of smelting or refining and that is in such
20 state or condition that its value depends upon its content and not upon
21 its form. The term does not include fabricated precious metal that has
22 been processed or manufactured for one or more specific and
23 customary industrial, professional, or artistic uses.
24 …."
25 SECTION 2. Article 13 of Chapter 66 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
26 a new section to read:
27 "§ 66-67.6. Commerce of investment coins and investment metal bullion.
28 (a) Legal Tender. – Investment coins and investment metal bullion (i) as defined in
29 G.S. 105-153.5(b)(17), (ii) made of refined gold or silver, and (iii) stamped, marked, or imprinted
30 with its weight and purity shall be recognized as legal tender in the State.
31 (b) Commerce. – No person is required to offer or accept any recognized legal tender as
32 described in subsection (a) of this section for payment of debts, deposit, or any other purpose. In
33 addition, no person incurs liability for refusing to offer or accept such legal tender, except as
34 specifically provided for by contract or otherwise required by law."
