6 PART I. CURRITUCK COUNTY OCCUPANCY TAX CHANGES
7 SECTION 1.(a) Section 1 of Chapter 209 of the 1987 Session Laws, as amended by
8 Chapter 155 of the 1991 Session Laws, S.L. 1999-155, S.L. 2004-95, and Section 60(s) of S.L.
9 2013-414, reads as rewritten:
10 "Section 1. Occupancy tax. (a) Authorization and scope. Scope. – The Currituck County
11 Board of Commissioners may levy a room occupancy tax of three percent (3%) of the gross
12 receipts derived from the rental of any room, lodging, or similar an accommodation furnished by
13 a hotel, motel, inn, or similar place within the county that is subject to sales tax imposed by the
14 State under G.S. 105-164.4(a)(3). This tax is in addition to any State or local sales tax.
15 (a1) Additional occupancy tax. Occupancy Tax. – In addition to the tax authorized by
16 subsection (a) of this section, the Currituck County Board of Commissioners may levy a room
17 occupancy tax of one percent (1%) of the gross receipts derived from the rental of
18 accommodations taxable under subsection (a). (a) of this section. The levy, collection,
19 administration, and repeal of the tax authorized by this subsection shall be in accordance with
20 the provisions of this act. Currituck County may not levy a tax under this subsection unless it
21 also levies the tax under subsection (a).(a) of this section.
22 (a2) Second Additional Occupancy Tax. – In addition to the tax authorized by subsections
23 (a) and (a1) of this section, the Currituck County Board of Commissioners may levy a room
24 occupancy tax of up to two percent (2%) of the gross receipts derived from the rental of
25 accommodations taxable under subsection (a). (a) of this section. The levy, collection,
26 administration, and repeal of the tax authorized by this subsection shall be in accordance with
27 the provisions of this act. Currituck County may not levy a tax under this subsection unless it
28 also levies the tax under subsections (a) and (a1).(a1) of this section.
29 …
30 (e) Use of tax revenue. Tax Revenue. – Currituck County shall use the net proceeds of
31 the tax levied under subsection (a) of this section under this act as follows:
32 (1) Two-thirds only for tourism-related expenditures, including tourist-related
33 purposes, including services or programs needed due to the impact of tourism
34 and seasonal population increases, such as law enforcement, emergency
35 services, fire protection, construction and maintenance of public facilities,
36 solid waste collection and disposal, and beach nourishment. Funds under this

10 (1) Beach nourishment. – The placement of sand, from other sand sources, on a
11 beach or dune by mechanical means and other associated activities that are in
12 conformity with the North Carolina Coastal Management Program along the
13 shorelines of the Atlantic Ocean of North Carolina and connecting inlets for
14 the purpose of widening the beach to benefit public recreational use and
15 mitigating damage and erosion from storms to inland property. The term
16 includes expenditures for any of the following:
17 a. Costs directly associated with qualifying for projects either contracted
18 through the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers or otherwise permitted by
19 all appropriate federal and State agencies.
20 b. The nonfederal share of the cost required to construct these projects.
21 c. The costs associated with providing enhanced public beach access.
22 d. The costs of associated nonhardening activities such as the planting of
23 vegetation, the building of dunes, and the placement of sand fences.
24 (2) Net proceeds. – Gross proceeds less the cost to the county of administering
25 and collecting the tax, as determined by the finance officer, not to exceed three
26 percent (3%) of the first five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) of gross
27 proceeds collected each year and one percent (1%) of the remaining gross
28 receipts collected each year.
29 (3) Promote travel and tourism. – To advertise or market an area or activity,
30 publish and distribute pamphlets and other materials, conduct market research,
31 or engage in similar promotional activities that attract tourists or business
32 travelers to the area; the term includes administrative expenses incurred in
33 engaging in these activities.
34 (4) Tourism-related expenditures. – Expenditures that, in the judgment of the
35 Currituck County Board of Commissioners, are designed to increase the use
36 of lodging facilities, meeting facilities, recreational facilities, and convention
37 facilities in a county by attracting tourists or business travelers to the county.
38 The term includes tourism-related capital expenditures and beach
39 nourishment.
40 …."

42 expenditure of occupancy tax proceeds collected on or after that date.

44 PART II. NEW HANOVER BEACH TOWNS OCCUPANCY TAX CHANGES
45 SECTION 2.(a) Section 1(d) of S.L. 2002-138 reads as rewritten:
46 "SECTION 1.(d) Distribution and Use of Tax Revenue. – The county shall, on a quarterly
47 basis, remit the net proceeds of the occupancy tax levied by each beach town under this section
48 to the Tourism Development Authority for deposit to a separate account for that beach town. The
49 Authority shall use at least one-half of the funds in each beach town account to promote travel
50 and tourism in that beach town and shall use the remainder in each beach town account for
51 tourism-related expenditures in that beach town. Before spending any funds in a beach town

7 and collecting the tax, as determined by the finance officer, not to exceed three
8 percent (3%) of the first five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) of gross

18 Part VIII of Chapter 908 of the 1983 Session Laws, as amended.

20 Tourism Development Authority, are designed to increase the use of lodging
21 facilities, meeting facilities, or convention facilities in a town or to attract
22 tourists or business travelers to the town. The term includes tourism-related
23 capital expenditures.expenditures, seasonal ocean rescue and lifeguard

25 are funded by occupancy tax proceeds."
26 SECTION 2.(b) This Part is effective when it becomes law and applies to the
