1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO LIMIT THE SIZE OF AMMUNITION MAGAZINES.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
5 to read:
6 "Article 53D.
7 "Regulation of Large-Capacity Ammunition Magazines.
8 "§ 14-409.60. Definition.
9 For purposes of this Article, the term "large-capacity magazine" includes all of the following:
10 (i) a fixed or detachable magazine box, drum, feed strip, or similar device capable of accepting,
11 or that is designed to be readily converted to accept, more than 10 rounds of ammunition, (ii) a
12 fixed or detachable magazine that is capable of accepting more than eight shotgun shells, and
13 (iii) a detachable magazine, tube, box, drum, feed strip, or similar device that is capable of
14 accepting more than eight shotgun shells when combined with a fixed magazine. The term does
15 not include any of the following: (i) a feeding device that has been permanently altered so that it
16 cannot accommodate more than 10 rounds of ammunition, (ii) an attached tubular device
17 designed to accept and capable of operating only with .22 caliber rimfire ammunition, or (iii) a
18 tubular magazine that is contained in a lever-action firearm.
19 "§ 14-409.61. Large-capacity magazines prohibited; penalties; exceptions.
20 (a) It is unlawful for a person to manufacture, sell, offer to sell, purchase, transfer, or
21 possess a large-capacity magazine. A violation of this subsection is a Class 2 misdemeanor;
22 provided, however, that a second or subsequent violation of this subsection is a Class 1
23 misdemeanor.
24 (b) Any person who violates subsection (a) of this section commits a Class I felony under
25 this section if the person possessed a large-capacity magazine during the commission of a felony.
26 (c) The offense described in subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to any of the
27 following:
28 (1) An entity, or any employee thereof engaged in the employee's employment
29 duties, that manufactures large-capacity magazines within North Carolina
30 exclusively for transfer or any federally licensed gun dealer, or any employee
31 thereof engaged in his or her official employment duties, that sells
32 large-capacity magazines exclusively to any of the following:
33 a. A branch of the Armed Forces of the United States.
34 b. A department, agency, or political subdivision of the State of North
35 Carolina, any other state, or of the United States government.
