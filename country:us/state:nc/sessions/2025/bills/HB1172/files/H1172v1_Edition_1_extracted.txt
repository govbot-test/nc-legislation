1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO IMPROVE MATERNAL HEALTH OUTCOMES AND REDUCE MATERNAL
3 HEALTH DISPARITIES IN NORTH CAROLINA THROUGH THE ESTABLISHMENT
4 OF A HIGH-RISK PREGNANCY CARE NAVIGATION PROGRAM, A PREGNANCY
5 CONSULTATION HOTLINE, A CENTRALIZED CLINICAL INFORMATION HUB
6 FOR MANAGING HIGH-RISK PREGNANCIES, AND STANDARDIZED REFERRAL
7 PATHWAYS FOR HIGH-RISK PREGNANCIES; AND TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS TO
8 THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES, DIVISION OF PUBLIC
9 HEALTH, FOR THESE PURPOSES.
10 Whereas, the untimely death of Ciji Graham – a beloved mother, daughter, and friend
11 – highlights the devastating intersection of medical risk, delayed treatment, and restrictive
12 healthcare policies; and
13 Whereas, current policy created an unfortunate situation in which Ciji Graham's
14 serious underlying illness, the hesitancy of physicians due to restrictive laws, and policy-driven
15 delays prevented timely, coordinated care when minutes and days mattered; and
16 Whereas, maternal health outcomes in North Carolina remain a significant public
17 health concern, particularly among Black women and rural populations; and
18 Whereas, there is an urgent need to bolster North Carolina's healthcare system to
19 ensure access to timely and accurate information, resources, and care for high-risk pregnancies;
20 Now, therefore,
21 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
22
23 PART I. TITLE
24 SECTION 1.1. This act shall be known as "The Ciji Graham Act."
25
26 PART II. HIGH-RISK PREGNANCY CARE NAVIGATION PROGRAM
27 SECTION 2.1.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department
28 of Health and Human Services, Division of Public Health, the sum of two million three hundred
29 thousand dollars ($2,300,000) in recurring funds beginning in the 2026-2027 fiscal year to
30 establish a uniform High-Risk Pregnancy Care Navigation Program (the program) in each of the
31 six North Carolina Medicaid managed care regions to support the use of licensed nurses as care
32 navigators to assist individuals experiencing high-risk pregnancies with accessing timely,
33 equitable, and clinically appropriate care. These funds shall be allocated and used as follows:
34 (1) One million three hundred thousand dollars ($1,300,000) to establish 12
35 full-time, permanent Nurse Consultant positions within the Department of

25 SECTION 2.1.(b) Annually by September 1, beginning September 1, 2028, the
26 Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Public Health, shall report to the Joint
27 Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services and the Fiscal Research
28 Division on the establishment and operation of the High-Risk Pregnancy Care Navigation
29 Program authorized and funded by subsection (a) of this section. The report shall include at least
30 all of the following:
31 (1) An itemized list of program expenditures.
32 (2) A description of the care navigation services provided under the program,
33 including the utilization frequency of these services.
34 (3) An evaluation of the timeliness of referrals provided under the program and
35 the impact on patient access to care.
36 (4) An evaluation of the impact of the program on maternal and infant health
37 outcomes associated with high-risk pregnancies.
38 (5) Any obstacles to effective program operation and recommendations for
39 program improvement.

41 PART III. PREGNANCY CONSULTATION HOTLINE
42 SECTION 3.1.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department
43 of Health and Human Services, Division of Public Health, the sum of seven million seven
44 hundred thousand dollars ($7,700,000) in recurring funds beginning in the 2026-2027 fiscal year
45 to establish a statewide hotline that provides free, real-time telephone consultation to healthcare
46 providers serving pregnant patients and community-based organizations focused on caring for
47 pregnant women. The hotline shall be staffed Monday through Friday from 8:00 A.M. to 5:00
48 P.M. with qualified healthcare providers capable of doing all of the following:
49 (1) Providing rapid access to clinical guidance from qualified specialists,
50 including maternal-fetal medicine specialists.
51 (2) Offering immediate case-specific clinical guidance.

26 PART IV. CENTRALIZED CLINICAL INFORMATION HUB FOR MANAGING
27 HIGH-RISK PREGNANCIES
28 SECTION 4.1. The Department of Health and Human Services shall develop and
29 maintain a centralized, evidence-based digital information hub for clinicians managing patients
30 with high-risk pregnancies that includes all of the following:
31 (1) Up-to-date clinical guidelines for managing high-risk pregnancy conditions.
32 (2) A real-time, regionally organized directory of:
33 a. Maternal-fetal medicine specialists.
34 b. High-risk obstetric providers.
35 c. Facilities equipped for complex pregnancy care.
36 d. Providers of abortion care services, to the extent permitted by State
37 law.
38 (3) Standardized referral protocols to support timely and appropriate care
39 transitions.
40 (4) Decision-support tools to assist clinicians in counseling and referral for
41 patients with complex or life-threatening conditions during pregnancy.
42
43 PART V. STANDARDIZED REFERRAL PATHWAYS FOR HIGH-RISK
44 PREGNANCIES
45 SECTION 5.1. The Department of Health and Human Services, in consultation with
46 relevant stakeholders, shall establish uniform referral pathways to ensure that patients diagnosed
47 with high-risk pregnancy conditions are offered either of the following:
48 (1) For those seeking to continue their pregnancies, immediate referral to
49 appropriate high-risk obstetric or maternal-fetal medicine care.
