Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Reflexologists Right to Work Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Riddell, Warren, Belk, and Brody (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Health, if favorable, Regulatory Reform, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and
Operations of the House
February 12, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO EXEMPT CERTIFIED REFLEXOLOGISTS FROM OVERSIGHT FROM THE
3 NORTH CAROLINA BOARD OF MASSAGE AND BODYWORK THERAPY.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 90-624 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 90-624. Activities not requiring a license to practice.
7 Nothing in this Article shall be construed to prohibit or affect:
8 …
9 (9) A nationally certified reflexologist engaged in the practice of reflexology, who
10 has a current certification from the American Reflexology Certification Board
11 (ARCB) or its successor entity, or an individual who is a reflexology student
12 working to obtain certification from the ARCB or its successor entity under
13 the supervision of an ARCB-certified reflexologist. Provided, however, that
14 this exemption shall only apply to reflexology students who obtain
15 certification within 12 months of beginning the certification process. For the
16 purposes of this subdivision, "reflexology" means a protocol of manual
17 techniques, including thumb- and finger-walking, hook and backup, and
18 rotating-on-a-point, that are applied to specific reflex areas predominantly on
19 the feet and hands and that stimulate the complex neural pathways linking
20 body systems and support the body's efforts to function optimally."
21 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025.
