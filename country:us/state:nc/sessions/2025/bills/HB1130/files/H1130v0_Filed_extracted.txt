32 PART IV. EXPLORATORY SABBATICAL GRANT PROGRAM FOR TEACHERS
33 SECTION 4.(a) Article 20 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
34 adding the following new section to read:
35 "§ 115C-302.5. Exploratory sabbatical grant program for teachers.
36 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
37 (1) Department. – The Department of Public Instruction.
38 (2) Eligible teacher. – A teacher employed in a local school administrative unit
39 who meets all of the following criteria:
40 a. Has worked as a teacher in a public school unit for 10 years, regardless
41 of a break in service.
42 b. Has not taken a sabbatical pursuant to the Program in the prior 10
43 years.
44 c. Has a continuing professional teacher license.
45 d. Is not currently eligible under G.S. 135-5 for an unreduced retirement
46 allowance.
47 (3) Program. – The Exploratory Sabbatical Grant Program for Teachers
48 established pursuant to this section.
49 (b) Program. – To the extent funds are provided for this purpose, the Department of
50 Public Instruction shall establish the Exploratory Sabbatical Grant Program for Teachers. The
51 purpose of the Program is to support teacher retention by allowing eligible teachers to take
Page 4 DRH10614-MTa-173
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 sabbaticals from their current teaching positions and serve as substitute teachers in other subjects,
2 other schools located in the local school administrative unit, or both, while continuing to receive
3 their salaries as full-time teachers.
4 (c) Applications; Time Line. – No later than November 1 of each year, the Department
5 of Public Instruction shall issue a request for applications from local school administrative units
6 to participate in the Program. Local school administrative units shall submit their applications to
7 the Department by February 15 of the following year. The Department shall select local school
8 administrative units to participate in the Program by April 15 of the year applications are
9 submitted for participation in the following school year.
10 (d) Award of Funds. – The Department shall award funds for up to a total of 500 teacher
11 sabbatical positions. The Department shall allocate teacher sabbatical positions to local school
12 administrative units proportionally based on the number of eligible teachers in the unit. For each
13 teacher sabbatical position authorized by the Department under the Program, the Department
14 shall allocate one additional classroom teaching position to the local school administrative unit
15 for that school year.
16 (e) Selection of Teachers. – Local school administrative units that receive an award of
17 grant funds pursuant to subsection (d) of this section shall select eligible teachers in the unit to
18 receive teacher sabbaticals. As part of that process, the unit shall prioritize the award of
19 sabbaticals to teachers with between 10 and 19 years of experience as a teacher. Thereafter, the
20 unit shall award funds to teachers with 20 to 29 years of experience as a teacher.
21 (f) Sabbatical Requirements. – The following requirements shall apply to sabbaticals
22 taken under this section:
23 (1) A teacher selected to receive a sabbatical pursuant to subsection (e) of this
24 section shall serve as a substitute teacher in the local school administrative
25 unit where the teacher is employed for up to one school year.
26 (2) The teacher shall receive compensation equivalent to his or her full salary as
27 a teacher during the sabbatical.
28 (3) The local school administrative unit where the teacher is employed shall
29 determine the minimum number of hours the teacher is required to work as a
30 substitute teacher during the sabbatical.
31 (4) At the conclusion of the sabbatical, the local school administrative unit shall
32 offer the teacher a full-time teaching position somewhere within the unit
33 unless the teacher is dismissed or demoted for cause. The position need not be
34 in the same location or the same grade level as the position the teacher vacated
35 prior to taking the sabbatical.
36 (g) Administration. – Of the funds appropriated to the Department pursuant to this
37 section, the Department may retain up to one percent (1%) each fiscal year for administrative
38 costs associated with the Program.
39 (h) Report. – No later than March 15, 2028, and every year thereafter while grant funds
40 are awarded under the Program, the Department shall report on the Program to the Joint
41 Legislative Education Oversight Committee. The report shall include at least the following
42 information:
43 (1) The number of local school administrative units participating in the Program
44 in that school year and the identity of those units.
45 (2) The number of teachers in each participating local school administrative unit
46 that are taking sabbaticals in that school year and the average years of
47 experience of those teachers.
48 (3) The number of teachers in the prior school year who took sabbaticals and
49 remained employed as teachers in the participating local school administrative
50 units in the following year."
