35 SECTION 4.(a) Article 20 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
36 adding the following new section to read:
37 "§ 115C-302.5. Exploratory sabbatical grant program for teachers.
38 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
39 (1) Department. – The Department of Public Instruction.
40 (2) Eligible teacher. – A teacher employed in a local school administrative unit
41 who meets all of the following criteria:
42 a. Has worked as a teacher in a public school unit for 10 years, regardless
43 of a break in service.
44 b. Has not taken a sabbatical pursuant to the Program in the prior 10
45 years.
46 c. Has a continuing professional teacher license.
47 d. Is not currently eligible under G.S. 135-5 for an unreduced retirement
48 allowance.
49 (3) Program. – The Exploratory Sabbatical Grant Program for Teachers
50 established pursuant to this section.

1 (b) Program. – To the extent funds are provided for this purpose, the Department of
2 Public Instruction shall establish the Exploratory Sabbatical Grant Program for Teachers. The
3 purpose of the Program is to support teacher retention by allowing eligible teachers to take
4 sabbaticals from their current teaching positions and serve as substitute teachers in other subjects,
5 other schools located in the local school administrative unit, or both, while continuing to receive
6 their salaries as full-time teachers.
7 (c) Applications; Time Line. – No later than November 1 of each year, the Department
8 of Public Instruction shall issue a request for applications from local school administrative units
9 to participate in the Program. Local school administrative units shall submit their applications to
10 the Department by February 15 of the following year. The Department shall select local school
11 administrative units to participate in the Program by April 15 of the year applications are
12 submitted for participation in the following school year.
13 (d) Award of Funds. – The Department shall award funds for up to a total of 500 teacher
14 sabbatical positions. The Department shall allocate teacher sabbatical positions to local school
15 administrative units proportionally based on the number of eligible teachers in the unit. For each
16 teacher sabbatical position authorized by the Department under the Program, the Department
17 shall allocate one additional classroom teaching position to the local school administrative unit
18 for that school year.
19 (e) Selection of Teachers. – Local school administrative units that receive an award of
20 grant funds pursuant to subsection (d) of this section shall select eligible teachers in the unit to
21 receive teacher sabbaticals. As part of that process, the unit shall prioritize the award of
22 sabbaticals to teachers with between 10 and 19 years of experience as a teacher. Thereafter, the
23 unit shall award funds to teachers with 20 to 29 years of experience as a teacher.
24 (f) Sabbatical Requirements. – The following requirements shall apply to sabbaticals
25 taken under this section:
26 (1) A teacher selected to receive a sabbatical pursuant to subsection (e) of this
27 section shall serve as a substitute teacher in the local school administrative
28 unit where the teacher is employed for up to one school year.
29 (2) The teacher shall receive compensation equivalent to his or her full salary as
30 a teacher during the sabbatical.
31 (3) The local school administrative unit where the teacher is employed shall
32 determine the minimum number of hours the teacher is required to work as a
33 substitute teacher during the sabbatical.
34 (4) At the conclusion of the sabbatical, the local school administrative unit shall
35 offer the teacher a full-time teaching position somewhere within the unit
36 unless the teacher is dismissed or demoted for cause. The position need not be
37 in the same location or the same grade level as the position the teacher vacated
38 prior to taking the sabbatical.
39 (g) Administration. – Of the funds appropriated to the Department pursuant to this
40 section, the Department may retain up to one percent (1%) each fiscal year for administrative
41 costs associated with the Program.
42 (h) Report. – No later than March 15, 2028, and every year thereafter while grant funds
43 are awarded under the Program, the Department shall report on the Program to the Joint
44 Legislative Education Oversight Committee. The report shall include at least the following
45 information:
46 (1) The number of local school administrative units participating in the Program
47 in that school year and the identity of those units.
48 (2) The number of teachers in each participating local school administrative unit
49 that are taking sabbaticals in that school year and the average years of
50 experience of those teachers.
