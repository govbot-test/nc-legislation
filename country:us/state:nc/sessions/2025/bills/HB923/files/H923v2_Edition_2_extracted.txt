6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. Chapter 132 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
8 section to read:
9 "§ 132-20. Removal of personal identifying information from public websites; judicial
10 personnel.
11 (a) Any judicial official may request, in writing, that all personal identifying information
12 be removed from any website, online computer database, or geographical information system
13 maintained by any public agency and available to the general public, provided the request
14 includes all of the following:
15 (1) The name of the judicial official.
16 (2) Information indicating the judicial official is eligible to make the request.
17 (3) The specific personal identifying information to be removed.
18 (b) Each public agency shall develop and make available a process by which any judicial
19 official may request removal of that individual's personal identifying information from any
20 website, online computer database, or geographical information system maintained by the public
21 agency and available to the general public. The request to remove personal identifying
22 information may also include a request to remove the personal identifying information of the
23 individual's spouse.
24 (c) The public agency must remove the personal identifying information if requested in
25 accordance with this section. The personal identifying information removed in accordance with
26 this section shall not be made available online again unless the public agency receives a written
27 revocation from the judicial official who made the original request. Personal identifying
28 information removed in accordance with this section continues to be a public record if it would
29 otherwise be subject to disclosure under this Chapter.
30 (d) Neither the request to remove the personal identifying information nor the revocation
31 of the request is a public record as defined by G.S. 132-1, and the public agency shall keep the
32 request and any revocation confidential.
33 (e) A public agency and its officers, officials, employees, and agents, both past and
34 present, in their official and individual capacity, shall be immune and held harmless from liability
35 in any action brought by or on behalf of any person injured or harmed by the action or inaction,
36 in good faith, of the public agency or its officers, officials, employees, and agents in

27 SECTION 2. G.S. 132-6.1(a1) reads as rewritten:
28 "(a1) Notwithstanding G.S. 132-6.2(a), a public agency may satisfy the requirement under
29 G.S. 132-6 to provide access to public records in computer databases by making public records
30 in computer databases individually available online in a format that allows a person to view the
31 public record and print or save the public record to obtain a copy. A public agency that provides
32 access to public records under this subsection is not required to provide access to the public
33 records in the computer database in any other way; provided, however, that a a public agency
34 that provides access to public records under this subsection shall comply with G.S. 132-20 to
35 remove any personal identifying information from an online computer database upon the request
36 of a judicial official. A public agency that provides access to public records in computer databases
37 under this subsection shall also allow inspection of any of such public records that the public
38 agency also maintains in a nondigital medium."
39 SECTION 3. G.S. 132-10 reads as rewritten:
40 "§ 132-10. Qualified exception for geographical information systems.
41 (a) Geographical information systems databases and data files developed and operated
42 by counties and cities are public records within the meaning of this Chapter. The county or city
43 shall provide public access to such systems by public access terminals or other output devices.
44 Upon request, the county or city shall furnish copies, in documentary or electronic form, to
45 anyone requesting them at reasonable cost. As a condition of furnishing an electronic copy,
46 whether on magnetic tape, magnetic disk, compact disk, or photo-optical device, a county or city
47 may require that the person obtaining the copy agree in writing that the copy will not be resold
48 or otherwise used for trade or commercial purposes.
49 (b) A county or city shall comply with G.S. 132-20 to remove any personal identifying
50 information from a geographical information system database upon the request of a judicial
51 official.
