9 SECTION 1.2. G.S. 110-91 reads as rewritten:
10 "§ 110-91. Mandatory standards for a license.
11 All child care facilities shall comply with all State laws and federal laws and local ordinances
12 that pertain to child health, safety, and welfare. Except as otherwise provided in this Article, the
13 standards in this section shall be complied with by all child care facilities. However, none
14 Notwithstanding any provision of law or rule to the contrary, any building and grounds which
15 are currently approved for school occupancy and which house a public or private elementary or
16 middle school shall be deemed to have met the space and equipment, sanitation, fire, and building
17 code requirements for a licensed child care facility when the building and grounds are serving
18 the same, or a subset of the same, school-age children in an out-of-school child care program.
19 None of the standards in this section apply to the school-age children of the operator of a child
20 care facility but do apply to the preschool-age children of the operator. Children 13 years of age
21 or older may receive child care on a voluntary basis provided all applicable required standards
22 are met. The standards in this section, along with any other applicable State laws and federal laws
23 or local ordinances, shall be the required standards for the issuance of a license by the Secretary
24 under the policies and procedures of the Commission except that the Commission may, in its
25 discretion, adopt less stringent standards for the licensing of facilities which provide care on a
26 temporary, part-time, drop-in, seasonal, after-school or other than a full-time basis.

18 PART II. ESTABLISH WORKGROUP TO EXAMINE POTENTIAL FOR
19 DEVELOPMENT OF GROUP LIABILITY INSURANCE PLAN OPPORTUNITIES
20 FOR CERTAIN ENTITIES PROVIDING SERVICES FOR MINORS
21 SECTION 2.1. The Commissioner of the Department of Insurance shall establish a
22 workgroup to examine the potential for developing group liability insurance plan opportunities
23 for all child care providers and for all nongovernmental contractors that contract with the
24 Department of Health and Human Services and any county or local agency administering
25 programs of public assistance pursuant to Chapter 108A of the General Statutes for the provision
26 of services to minors which are licensed under Article 2 of Chapter 122C of the General Statutes
27 or Article 1A of Chapter 131D of the General Statutes. The workgroup shall consist of
28 representatives from all of the following:
29 (1) The Department of Insurance.
30 (2) The insurance industry.
31 (3) NC Licensed Child Care Association.
32 (4) The Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Child
33 Development and Early Education.
34 (5) The American Tort Reform Association.
35 (6) Benchmarks NC.
36 SECTION 2.2. The workgroup shall develop findings and recommendations related
37 to at least the following:
38 (1) Potential methods for creating group liability insurance plan opportunities for
39 all child care providers.
40 (2) Reforms that could reduce group liability insurance plan premiums.
41 (3) Tort reforms that could reduce the liability damages of child care providers.
42 SECTION 2.3. By January 1, 2026, the Department of Insurance shall report the
43 findings and recommendations of the workgroup to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee
44 on Health and Human Services, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on General
45 Government, and the Fiscal Research Division.
46
47 PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
48 SECTION 3.1. This act is effective when it becomes law.

22 workgroup to examine the potential for developing group liability insurance plan opportunities
23 for all child care providers and for all nongovernmental contractors that contract with the
24 Department of Health and Human Services and any county or local agency administering
25 programs of public assistance pursuant to Chapter 108A of the General Statutes for the provision
26 of services to minors which are licensed under Article 2 of Chapter 122C of the General Statutes
27 or Article 1A of Chapter 131D of the General Statutes. The workgroup shall consist of
28 representatives from all of the following:
29 (1) The Department of Insurance.
30 (2) The insurance industry.
31 (3) NC Licensed Child Care Association.
32 (4) The Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Child
33 Development and Early Education.
34 (5) The American Tort Reform Association.
35 (6) Benchmarks NC.
36 SECTION 2.2. The workgroup shall develop findings and recommendations related
37 to at least the following:
38 (1) Potential methods for creating group liability insurance plan opportunities for
39 all child care providers.
40 (2) Reforms that could reduce group liability insurance plan premiums.
41 (3) Tort reforms that could reduce the liability damages of child care providers.
42 SECTION 2.3. By January 1, 2026, the Department of Insurance shall report the
43 findings and recommendations of the workgroup to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee
44 on Health and Human Services, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on General
45 Government, and the Fiscal Research Division.
