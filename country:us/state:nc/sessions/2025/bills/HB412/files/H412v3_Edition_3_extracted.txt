Short Title: Child Care Regulatory Reforms. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:

1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO DECOUPLE THE CHILD CARE RATED LICENSE SYSTEM AND
3 SUBSIDIZED CHILD CARE SUBSIDY REIMBURSEMENT RATES AND TO MAKE
4 OTHER CHILD CARE REGULATORY REFORMS.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6
7 PART I. DECOUPLE RATED LICENSE AND SUBSIDIZED CHILD CARE
8 SECTION 1.(a) The General Assembly recognizes the need to balance maintaining
9 critical health, safety, and welfare standards for child care, as well as a well-established rating
10 system used for informational purposes, with the need to move toward maximizing State funds
11 for child care and increasing the supply of child care from State-funded sources. The General
12 Assembly further recognizes the importance of weighing the need to decrease the cost of child
13 care through deregulatory actions and at the same time maintain child care subsidy
14 reimbursement rates. The purpose of this provision, in part, is to encourage the business
15 community to partner with the State in achieving this goal.
16 SECTION 1.(b) To that end, by May 1, 2026, the Department of Health and Human
17 Services, Division of Child Development and Early Education (Division), shall develop a
18 proposed plan to separate the quality rating improvement system (QRIS) from the requirements
19 and payments for participation in the State-subsidized child care program using the market rate
20 study required by Section 1(c) of this act and make recommendations on implementation of the
21 plan while meeting the federal Child Care and Development Fund requirements. The Division
22 shall submit the proposed plan to the chairs of the House and Senate Appropriations Committees,
23 the chairs of the House and Senate Appropriations Committees on Health and Human Services,
24 the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services, and the Fiscal
25 Research Division by May 1, 2026. The current plan will stay in full force and effect until such
26 time as the General Assembly first approves and adopts the proposed plan and any amendments
27 to that plan and then the federal government approves the proposed plan and any amendments.
28 SECTION 1.(c) The Division shall complete a new market rate study by May 1,
29 2026. This market rate study shall be made available to the public by May 1, 2026. The Division
30 shall ensure that the market rate study includes potential rates that are not segmented by
31 star-rating and new market rates for the QRIS system. The Division shall not implement new
32 reimbursement rates unless approved by the federal Administration of Children and Families and
33 authorized to do so by the General Assembly.
34 SECTION 1.(d) Nothing in this section shall be construed as impacting the
35 star-rating requirements for the NC Prekindergarten (NC Pre-K) program.
