1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO DECOUPLE THE CHILD CARE RATED LICENSE SYSTEM AND

4 OTHER CHILD CARE REGULATORY REFORMS.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6
7 PART I. DECOUPLE RATED LICENSE AND SUBSIDIZED CHILD CARE
8 SECTION 1.(a) The General Assembly recognizes the need to balance maintaining
9 critical health, safety, and welfare standards for child care, as well as a well-established rating
10 system used for informational purposes, with the need to move toward maximizing State funds
11 for child care and increasing the supply of child care from State-funded sources. The General
12 Assembly further recognizes the importance of continuing the child care stabilization grants
13 funding while weighing the need to decrease the cost of child care through deregulatory actions
14 and at the same time maintain child care subsidy reimbursement rates. The purpose of this
15 provision, in part, is to encourage the business community to partner with the State in achieving
16 this goal.
17 SECTION 1.(b) To that end, by May 1, 2026, the Department of Health and Human
18 Services, Division of Child Development and Early Education (Division), shall develop a plan
19 to separate the quality rating improvement system (QRIS) from the requirements and payments
20 for participation in the State subsidized child care program using the market rate study submitted
21 in 2026. The next market rate study shall be completed and made available to the public by May
22 1, 2026. Under the plan, licensed child care centers and homes may continue to receive
23 star-ratings on a voluntary basis. However, the star-rating shall not impact the rate at which
24 licensed child care centers or homes are reimbursed for subsidized child care.
25 SECTION 1.(c) The Division of Child Development and Early Education shall
26 ensure that the next market rate study also includes recommended rates that are not segmented
27 by star-rating. The Division shall not implement new reimbursement rates unless authorized to
28 do so by the General Assembly.
