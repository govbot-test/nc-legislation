16 "Part 2. Health Care Practitioner Transparency Act.
17 "§ 90-641. Short title.
18 This Part shall be known as the "Health Care Practitioner Transparency Act."
19 "§ 90-642. Definitions.
20 The following definitions apply in this Part:
21 (1) Advertisement. – Any communication or statement that is printed, electronic,
22 or oral which names the health care practitioner in relation to their practice,
23 profession, or institution where the health care practitioner is employed,
24 volunteers, or otherwise provides health care services, including business
25 cards, letterhead, patient brochures, email, internet, audio and video, or any
26 other communication or statement used in the course of business.
27 (2) Deceptive or misleading. – Any verbal or written representation or
28 advertisement that misstates, falsely describes, or holds out in a false light the
29 profession, skills, expertise, education, board certification, or licensure of the
30 health care professional.
31 (3) Health care practitioner. – An individual who is licensed, certified, or
32 registered to engage in the practice of medicine, nursing, dentistry, pharmacy,
33 or any related occupation involving the direct provision of health care to
34 patients.

1 (4) Licensee. – A health care practitioner who holds an active license with a

4 "§ 90-643. Advertisement and representation requirements.
5 (a) An advertisement for health care services that names a health care practitioner shall
6 identify the type of license, certification, or registration held by the health care practitioner. The
7 advertisement shall not contain any deceptive or misleading information.
8 (b) A health care practitioner shall not make a representation about the health care
9 practitioner's license, certification, or registration that is deceptive or misleading.
10 (c) Any individual not licensed to practice medicine under Article 1 of this Chapter shall
11 not hold himself or herself out to the public by calling oneself a physician or any of the following
12 titles, or using any similar title or description of services with the intent to represent that the
13 individual practices medicine: "surgeon," "medical doctor," "doctor of osteopathy," "M.D.,"
14 "D.O.," "anesthesiologist," "cardiologist," "dermatologist," "endocrinologist," "family
15 medicine," "family physicians," "gastroenterologist," "general practitioner," "gynecologist,"
16 "hematologist," "hospitalist," "internist," "intensivist," "laborist," "laryngologist,"
17 "nephrologist," "neurologist," "obstetrician," "oncologist," "ophthalmologist," "orthopedic
18 surgeon," "orthopedist," "osteopath," "otologist," "otolaryngologist," "otorhinolaryngologist,"
19 "pathologist," "pediatrician," "primary care physician," "proctologist," "psychiatrist,"
20 "radiologist," "rheumatologist," "rhinologist," or "urologist." Nothing in this subsection shall be
21 construed to prevent a health care practitioner from using any title or abbreviation which is
22 authorized for such health care practitioner pursuant to licensing statutes.
23 "§ 90-644. Violations and enforcement.
24 (a) Any health care practitioner subject to this Article who does any of the following shall
25 be in violation of this Article:
26 (1) Knowingly aids, assists, procures, employs, or advises an unlicensed
27 individual or entity in practicing or engaging in acts outside of the scope of
28 the health care practitioner's degree of licensure.
29 (2) Knowingly delegates or contracts the performance of health care services to a
30 health care practitioner that is unqualified to perform those health care
31 services.
32 (3) Fails to comply with any provision of this Article.
33 (b) Any health care practitioner who violates this Article as provided under subsection
34 (a) of this section shall be guilty of unprofessional conduct and may be subject to disciplinary
35 action under the health care practitioner's licensure board or other appropriate governing
36 provisions.
37 (c) Each day of noncompliance with this Article by a health care practitioner shall
38 constitute a separate and distinct violation.
39 (d) Any health care practitioner who practices in more than one office shall be required
40 to comply with this Article in each practice setting.
41 (e) Health care practitioners that work in non-patient settings and do not have any direct
42 patient health care interactions are not subject to this Article."

13 PART II. REAGAN'S LAW
14 SECTION 2.(a) Article 3 of Chapter 58 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
15 a new section to read:
16 "§ 58-3-286. Prosthetic and orthotic devices and care.
17 (a) This section shall apply to all health benefit plans offered in this State other than those
18 regulated under Part 5 of Article 50 of this Chapter, Small Employer Group Health Insurance
19 Reform, or Article 50A of this Chapter, Multiple Employer Welfare Arrangements.
20 (b) Health benefit plan coverage shall include coverage for all prosthetic and orthotic
21 devices required to be covered by federal law or regulation under Medicare Part B, as detailed
22 under Part B of Subchapter XVIII of Chapter 7 of Title 42 of the U.S. Code and Subpart D of
23 Part 414 of Subchapter B of Chapter IX of Title 42 of the Code of Federal Regulations. Coverage
24 under this section shall include:
25 (1) All materials and components necessary to use a prosthetic or orthotic device.
26 (2) Instruction relating to the use of a prosthetic or orthotic device.
27 (3) Repair or replacement of a prosthetic or orthotic device meeting the
28 requirements of subsection (g) of this section.
29 (c) Coverage consistent with this section shall be required for all prosthetic or orthotic
30 devices, including custom devices, determined by the insured's healthcare provider to be the most
31 appropriate model to adequately meet the medical needs of the insured for completing activities
32 of daily living or essential job-related activities.
33 (d) Coverage under this section shall not be limited to one prosthetic or orthotic device.
34 In addition to coverage required under subsection (c) of this section, a health benefit plan shall
35 provide coverage for additional prosthetic or orthotic devices, including custom devices
36 determined by the insured's healthcare provider to be the most appropriate model to adequately
37 meet the medical needs of the insured for either or both of the following:
38 (1) Performing physical activities, such as running, biking, swimming, and
39 strength training.
40 (2) Maximizing the insured's whole-body health and function of one or more
41 lower or upper limb.
42 (e) Coverage for prosthetic and orthotic devices, including custom devices, is considered
43 a habilitative or rehabilitative benefit, including for the purposes of any federal requirement for
44 the coverage of essential health benefits.
45 (f) An insurer shall not deny any health benefit claim for a prosthetic or orthotic device
46 for an insured with limb loss or absence that would otherwise be covered for any insured without
47 a disability seeking medical or surgical intervention to restore or maintain the ability to perform
48 the same physical activity.
49 (g) A health benefit plan shall provide coverage for the replacement of a prosthetic or
50 orthotic device, or part of a prosthetic or orthotic device, and all of the following shall apply to
51 that coverage:
