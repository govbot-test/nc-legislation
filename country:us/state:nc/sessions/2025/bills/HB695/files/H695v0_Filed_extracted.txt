H.B. 695
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 2, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Support VIPER/Assist 911 Operations/Dispatch. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative B. Jones.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO SUPPORT THE STATE'S VIPER PUBLIC SAFETY RADIO NETWORK IN
3 ORDER TO SUPPORT 911 OPERATIONS INTEROPERABILITY AND FIRST
4 RESPONDER DISPATCH THROUGHOUT THE STATE.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6
7 ALLOCATE A PERCENTAGE OF 911 FUNDS TO SUPPORT THE STATE'S VIPER
8 RADIO SYSTEM INSOFAR AS THAT SYSTEM SUPPORTS 911 OPERATIONS IN
9 THE STATE
10 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 143B-1404(b) reads as rewritten:
11 "(b) Allocation of Revenues. – The 911 Board may deduct and retain for its administrative
12 expenses a percentage of the total service charges remitted to it under G.S. 143B-1403 for deposit
13 in the 911 Fund. The percentage may not exceed three and one-half percent (3.5%). The
14 percentage is one percent (1%) unless the 911 Board sets the percentage at a different amount.
15 The 911 Board must monitor the amount of funds required to meet its duties under this Part and
16 set the rate at an amount that enables the 911 Board to meet this commitment. The 911 Board
17 must allocate a minimum of fifteen percent (15%) of the total service charges to the Next
18 Generation 911 Reserve Fund to be administered as provided in G.S. 143B-1407. The 911 Board
19 must allocate a minimum of five percent (5%) of the total service charges to the PSAP Grant and
20 Statewide Projects Account to be administered as provided in G.S. 143B-1407. The 911 Board
21 must allocate a minimum of twenty percent (20%) of the total service charges to the State
22 Highway Patrol to be used for the support and maintenance of the State's Voice Interoperability
23 Plan for Emergency Responders (VIPER) system insofar as that system relates to the provision
24 of 911 services in the State, the provision for the interoperability of 911 systems with one another
25 and with public safety and first responder radio systems, and the dispatch of first responders in
26 response to 911 communications. The remaining revenues remitted to the 911 Board for deposit
27 in the 911 Fund are allocated for distribution to the primary PSAPs, CMRS providers, or the
28 Accounts established in G.S. 143B-1407."
29 SECTION 1.(b) This section becomes effective July 1, 2025, and applies to 911
30 Board allocations made on or after that date.
31
32 911 BOARD TO ADOPT TEMPORARY AND PERMANENT RULES
33 SECTION 2. The 911 Board shall adopt rules in accordance with this act and is
34 granted authority to adopt temporary rules to enact this act until permanent rules are adopted.
35
36 GENERAL EFFECTIVE DATE
*DRH10318-ND-33*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 3. Except as otherwise provided, this act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
