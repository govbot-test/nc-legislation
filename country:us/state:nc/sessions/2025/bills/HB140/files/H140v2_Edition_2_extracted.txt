1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO AUTHORIZE THE GULLAH GEECHEE GREENWAY/BLUEWAY
3 HERITAGE TRAIL IN BRUNSWICK COUNTY.
4 Whereas, the history of the Gullah Geechee culture is an important part of the story
5 and formation of the United States; and
6 Whereas, the Gullah Geechee peoples of coastal Africa were forcibly brought
7 centuries ago to the coastal areas and the sea islands of southeastern North Carolina, South
8 Carolina, Georgia, and Florida because of their experience in rice cultivation, and the rice
9 plantations that formerly lined the western side of the Cape Fear River in Brunswick County thus
10 became the focal point of Gullah Geechee culture in this State; and
11 Whereas, many reminders of the Gullah Geechee culture remain on the west side of
12 the Cape Fear River in Brunswick County, including Reaves Chapel, Moore's Chapel, former
13 rice plantations, and pine tar pits used to repair wooden ships; and
14 Whereas, numerous local governments and regional organizations, including
15 Brunswick County, the Towns of Navassa, Leland, and Belville, the Wilmington Area
16 Metropolitan Planning Organization, and the Cape Fear Council of Governments have partnered
17 with many local and regional nonprofit entities to delineate and sponsor sites important to Gullah
18 Geechee culture along or near the west bank of the Cape Fear River; and
19 Whereas, this partnership has led to the creation of the Gullah Geechee
20 Greenway/Blueway Heritage Trail, which will include 9 segments utilizing existing
21 rights-of-way stretching 21 miles from northern Brunswick County to the Brunswick Nature
22 Park; and
23 Whereas, the extensive planning envisions a multiuse trail for bicycle and pedestrian
24 use, as well as points of historical interest, such as the restored Reaves Chapel; and
25 Whereas, the Gullah Geechee Greenway/Blueway Heritage Trail can become a part
26 of the Gullah Geechee Cultural Heritage Corridor, a National Heritage Area established by the
27 U.S. Congress, to recognize the unique culture of the Gullah Geechee people; and
28 Whereas, benefits of the Gullah Geechee Greenway/Blueway Heritage Trail include
29 tourism, economic development, transportation mobility, and the connection of communities,
30 which are important to this traditionally economically disadvantaged area; Now, therefore,
31 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
32 SECTION 1. The General Assembly authorizes the Gullah Geechee
33 Greenway/Blueway Heritage Trail (Heritage Trail) in Brunswick County. The State shall
34 support, promote, encourage, and facilitate the establishment of trail segments on State park lands
35 and on lands of other federal, State, local, and private landowners. On segments of the Heritage
