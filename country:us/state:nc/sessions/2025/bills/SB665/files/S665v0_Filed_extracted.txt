4 SECTION 1. Article 7 of Chapter 113A of the General Statutes is amended by
5 adding a new Part to read:
6 "Part 5A. Upland Basins.
7 "§ 113A-129.10. Legislative findings; broad construction.
8 (a) The General Assembly finds that development of properly sited and planned upland
9 basins to accommodate vessels is preferable to open-water marinas for a number of reasons,
10 including all of the following:
11 (1) Increasing riparian property owner access to the State's public trust waters.
12 (2) Expanding the total area of the State's public trust waters.
13 (3) Reducing and confining potential storm debris.
14 (4) Reducing the need for additional onshore impervious surfaces associated with
15 parking.
16 (5) Promoting the use of innovative technologies.
17 (6) Promoting the collection of site-specific data designed to assure compliance
18 with applicable water quality standards.
19 (7) Maintaining or improving overall water quality when sited, designed,
20 constructed, and operated in conformance with the provisions of this Part.
21 (b) Accordingly, it is the intent of the General Assembly that this Part be broadly
22 construed to further the general purposes stated in this Part.
23 "§ 113A-129.11. Definitions.
24 As used in this Part, the following definitions apply:
25 (1) AEC or Area of Environmental Concern. – An area designated by the Coastal
26 Resources Commission pursuant to G.S. 113A-113.
27 (2) Boat slip. – Any structure adjoining, attached to, or part of a pier which has
28 the capacity to store one boat.
29 (3) Upland basin. – A marina constructed by excavating or dredging lands of
30 elevations above the current mean or ordinary high-water mark and designed
31 to accommodate more than ten vessels or boat slips. Upland basins shall be
32 considered a water dependent use for purposes of general use standards adopted
33 by the Coastal Resources Commission.
34 "§ 113A-129.12. Upland basin permitting and development.
35 (a) Unless the Director of the Division of Water Resources makes a finding, based on
36 site-specific technical information, that the applicant cannot substantially comply with one or

11 If the Department fails to act on an application for the development of an upland basin project

13 (b) The criteria with which an upland basin shall comply to qualify for permitting pursuant
14 to subsection (a) of this section are all of the following:
15 (1) The upland basin is designed to accommodate ten or more vessels, including
16 individual homeowner boat slips, boat lifts, or dry stack storage.
17 (2) The waters contained in the upland basin have a dissolved oxygen content
18 equal to or greater than the dissolved oxygen content of the water located 50
19 feet plus or minus 5 feet from the entrance to the upland basin before
20 development. As part of the application, an applicant shall provide to the
21 Division of Water Resources and the Division of Coastal Management
22 site-specific sampling data documenting pre-project dissolved oxygen levels
23 of the water located 50 feet plus or minus 5 feet from each proposed entrance
24 to the proposed upland basin. Waters located within the upland basin shall be
25 classified the same as the waters in the immediate vicinity of any entrance to
26 the upland basin.
27 (3) The project has a bond or set-aside funds for the long-term operation and
28 maintenance of any technology required to meet or exceed then-applicable
29 water quality standards. The applicant shall provide an estimate of annual
30 operation and maintenance costs and the bond or set aside funds shall be equal
31 to five years of the estimated annual operation and maintenance costs.
32 (4) Construction of the upland basin impacts or removes a total area of coastal
33 wetlands that is no more than five percent (5%) of the total area of the
34 proposed upland basin waters.
35 (5) Construction of the upland basin impacts or removes a total linear footage of
36 coastal wetlands fringe that is no more than ten percent (10%) of the total
37 linear footage of existing coastal wetlands along the shoreline of the property
38 or properties of the permit applicant.
39 (6) The proposed upland basin project complies with specific use standards
40 adopted by the Coastal Resources Commission for navigation channels, canals
41 and boat basins. An upland basin project shall not be considered a "finger
42 canal" or "finger canal system" if the project does cot contain right angle
43 corners.
44 (7) The upland basin has a 30-foot vegetated buffer or buffers along the
45 post-project shoreline, excluding any newly created inside shorelines, if
46 required by the Department.
47 (8) The proposed upland basin project complies with specific use standards
48 adopted by the Coastal Resources Commission for marinas, with the following
49 exceptions:

37 Chapter 113A of the General Statutes.
38 (e) The permit and any related authorizations or approvals issued for an upland basin
39 project pursuant to this Part shall be transferable.
40 (f) The Department shall allow an applicant for an upland basin project to use innovative
41 technology to maintain dissolved oxygen levels or improve dissolved oxygen above pre-project
42 ambient dissolved oxygen levels in the immediate vicinity of the entrance to the upland basin. If
43 the applicant proposes the use of innovative technology, the Department shall require the permit
44 holder to provide financial assurance, in the form of a bond or set-aside funds, for long-term
45 operation and maintenance of the innovative technology, in accordance with subdivision (b)(3)
46 of this section. If the applicant provides a certification from a North Carolina licensed
47 professional engineer that the proposed innovative technology is capable of attaining required
48 dissolved oxygen levels within the proposed upland basin, the Department shall not require any
49 additional information.
50 (g) In the absence of site-specific technical concerns provided to the applicant by the
51 Director of the Division of Water Resources based on information provided during the permit
