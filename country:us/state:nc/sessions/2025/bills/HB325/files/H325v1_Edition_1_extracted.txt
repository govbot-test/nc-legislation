1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ESTABLISH A PILOT PROGRAM FOR PUBLIC SCHOOL STUDENTS TO
3 ATTEND A FIELD TRIP TO THE LEGISLATIVE BUILDING.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Program; Purpose. – There is established the Legislative Building Field
6 Trip Pilot Program (Program) for the 2025-2026 and 2026-2027 school years. The purpose of the
7 Program is to encourage civic engagement and study by assisting public school units in
8 conducting one field trip per school year to tour the Legislative Building.
9 SECTION 2. Application. – No later than August 1 of each year that funds are made
10 available for the Program, the Department of Public Instruction shall develop and publish criteria
11 and guidelines for the application process for the Program in the upcoming school year.
12 Applications shall include, at a minimum, a proposed budget for the field trip for each school in
13 the public school unit. All public school units are eligible to apply. The Department shall accept
14 applications until October 1 of each year of the Program.
15 SECTION 3. Selection. – No later than November 1 of each year of the Program,
16 the Department shall select applicants for the Program. The Department shall prioritize selection
17 of public school units that are located in a county designated as a tier 1 pursuant to
18 G.S. 143B-437.08.
19 SECTION 4. Award; Use of Funds. – Each selected public school unit shall receive
20 an award of up to two thousand dollars ($2,000) per school in the public school unit, but the
21 amount received shall not be greater than the amount estimated to be needed per school in the
22 proposed budget submitted with the application. Funds may be used for transportation expenses
23 to and from the Legislative Building. Travel expenses include only bus rental, fuel, or
24 compensation for drivers.
25 SECTION 5. Report. – No later than February 15 of each year of the Program, the
26 Department of Public Instruction shall report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight
27 Committee at least the following information:
28 (1) The public school units that received a grant.
29 (2) A description of how the grants were used.
30 (3) An estimate of the number of students that toured the Legislative Building
31 through the Program.
32 (4) The number of applications for the Program that were received but not
33 awarded a grant and the total amount needed to fund those applications.
