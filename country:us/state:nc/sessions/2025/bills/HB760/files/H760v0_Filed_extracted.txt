4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 130A-283 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 130A-283. Tattooing regulated.
7 (a) Definition. – As used in this Part, the term "tattooing" means the inserting of
8 permanent markings or coloration, or the producing of scars, upon or under human skin through
9 puncturing by use of a needle or any other method.
10 (b) Prohibited Practice. Tattooing Permit Required; Exemptions. – No person shall
11 engage in tattooing without first obtaining a tattooing permit from the Department. Licensed
12 physicians, as well as physician assistants and nurse practitioners working under the supervision
13 of a licensed physician, who perform tattooing within the normal course of their professional
14 practice are exempt from the requirements of this Part.
15 (c) Application. Permit Application. – To Any person seeking to obtain a tattooing
16 permit, a person must permit shall apply to the Department. Department on forms prescribed by
17 the Department. Upon receipt of the application, the Department, acting through the local health
18 department, shall (i) verify that the applicant has completed the bloodborne pathogen training
19 required by subsection (c1) of this section and (ii) inspect the premises, instruments, utensils,
20 equipment, and procedures of the applicant to determine whether the applicant meets the
21 requirements for a tattooing permit set by the Commission. If the applicant meets these
22 requirements, the Department shall issue a permit to the applicant. A permit is valid for one year
23 and must be renewed annually by applying to the Department for a permit renewal.
24 (c1) Annual Bloodborne Pathogen Training. – Each applicant seeking an initial or renewal
25 tattooing permit under this section is required to demonstrate to the satisfaction of the Department
26 that the applicant has completed annual training in compliance with the Occupational Safety and
27 Health Association's bloodborne pathogens standard set forth in 29 C.F.R. § 1910.1030.
28 (d) Violations. – The Department may deny an application for a an initial or renewal
29 tattooing permit if an applicant fails to demonstrate successful completion of the bloodborne
30 pathogen training required by subsection (c1) of this section or does not meet the requirements
31 set by the Commission for the permit. The Department may suspend, revoke, or refuse to renew
32 a permit if it finds that tattooing is being performed in violation of this Part. In accordance with
33 G.S. 130A-24(a), Chapter 150B of the General Statutes, the Administrative Procedure Act,
34 governs appeals concerning the enforcement of this Part.
