Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Expand Emergency Judge Eligibility. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives G. Pierce, A. Jones, Morey, and Blackwell (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Judiciary 1, if favorable, State and Local Government, if favorable, Rules,
Calendar, and Operations of the House
March 10, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO EXPAND EMERGENCY JUDGE ELIGIBILITY BY ALLOWING JUDGES
3 WHO SERVED AS BOTH A SUPERIOR COURT AND DISTRICT COURT JUDGE
4 PRIOR TO RETIREMENT TO BE AN EMERGENCY JUDGE ON EITHER COURT.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. Article 8 of Chapter 7A of the General Statutes reads as rewritten:
7 "Article 8.
8 "Retirement of Judges of the Superior Court; Retirement Compensation for Superior Court
9 Judges; Recall to Emergency Service of Judges of the District and Superior Court; Disability
10 Retirement for Judges of the Superior Court.
11 "§ 7A-50. Emergency judge defined.
12 As used in this Article "emergency judge" means any judge of the superior or district court
13 who has retired subject to recall to active service for temporary duty.
14 …
15 "§ 7A-52. Retired district and superior court judges may become emergency judges subject
16 to recall to active service; compensation for emergency judges on recall.
17 (a) Judges of the district court and judges of the superior court who have not reached the
18 mandatory retirement age specified in G.S. 7A-40.1 and G.S. 7A-140.1, respectively, but who
19 have retired under the provisions of G.S. 7A-51, or under the Uniform Judicial Retirement Act
20 after having completed five years of creditable service, may apply as provided in G.S. 7A-53 to
21 become emergency judges of the court or courts from which they retired. the judge retired or on
22 which the judge previously served. From the commissioned emergency district, superior, and
23 special superior court judges, the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court shall create two lists of
24 active emergency judges and two lists of inactive emergency judges. For emergency superior and
25 special superior court judges, the active list shall be limited to a combined total of 10 emergency
26 judges; all other emergency superior and special superior court judges shall be on an inactive list.
27 For emergency district court judges, the active list shall be limited to 25 emergency judges; all
28 other emergency district court judges shall be on an inactive list. There is no limit to the number
29 of emergency judges on either inactive list. In the Chief Justice's discretion, emergency judges
30 may be added or removed from their respective active and inactive lists, as long as the respective
31 numerical limits on the active lists are observed. The Chief Justice is requested to consider
32 geographical distribution in assigning emergency judges to an active list but may utilize any
33 factor in determining which emergency judges are assigned to an active list. The Chief Justice of
34 the Supreme Court may order any emergency district, superior, or special superior court judge

1 on an active list who, in the Chief Justice's opinion, is competent to perform the duties of a judge
2 and to hold regular or special sessions of the court or courts from which the judge retired, retired
3 or on which the judge previously served, as needed. Order of assignment shall be in writing and
4 entered upon the minutes of the court or courts to which the emergency judge is assigned. An
5 emergency judge shall only be assigned in the event of a:
6 …
7 "§ 7A-53. Application to the Governor; commission as emergency judge.
8 No retired judge of the district or superior court may become an emergency judge except
9 upon the judge's written application to the Governor certifying the judge's desire and ability to
10 serve as an emergency judge. If the Governor is satisfied that the applicant qualifies under
11 G.S. 7A-52(a) to become an emergency judge and the applicant is physically and mentally able
12 to perform the official duties of an emergency judge, the Governor shall issue to the applicant a
13 commission as an emergency judge of the court or courts from which the applicant retired. retired
14 or on which the applicant previously served. The commission shall be effective upon the date of
15 its issue and shall terminate when the judge to whom it is issued reaches the maximum age for
16 judicial service under G.S. 7A-40.1 or G.S. 7A-140.1, whichever is applicable.
17 …."
18 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to emergency
19 judges serving on or after that date.
