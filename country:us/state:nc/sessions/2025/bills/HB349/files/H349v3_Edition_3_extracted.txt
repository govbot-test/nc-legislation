10 SECTION 1.1. G.S. 32A-16(3) reads as rewritten:
11 "(3) Health care power of attorney. – Except as provided in G.S. 32A-16.1, a

18 SECTION 3.2. G.S. 130A-468 reads as rewritten:
19 "§ 130A-468. Filing of documents with the registry.
20 (a) When the Secretary of State receives a hard copy of a document that may be filed
21 with the registry pursuant to this Article, the Secretary shall create a digital reproduction of that
22 document and enter the reproduced document into the registry database. When the Secretary of
23 State receives a document in electronic format that may be filed with the registry pursuant to this
24 Article, the Secretary shall enter that document into the registry database. The Secretary is not
25 required to review a document to ensure that it complies with the particular statutory
26 requirements applicable to the document. Each document entered into the registry database shall
27 be assigned a unique file number and password.
28 (b) Upon entering the a reproduced hard copy of a document into the registry database,
29 the Secretary shall return the original hard copy of the document and a wallet-size card containing
30 the document's file number and password to the person who submitted the document. Upon
31 entering into the registry database a document that was received in electronic format, the
32 Secretary shall send a wallet-size card containing the document's file number and password to
33 the person who submitted the document.
34 (c) When the Secretary of State receives a revocation of a document that is filed with the
35 registry and that document's file number and password, or a request to remove that document
36 from the registry without its revocation, the Secretary shall delete that document from the registry
37 database.
38 (c1) The Secretary of State may remove documents of deceased registrants from the
39 registry upon notification of death in writing in a form acceptable to the Secretary of State.
40 (d) The Secretary of State's entry of a document into, or removal of a document from, the
41 registry database does not do any of the following:
42 (1) Affect the validity of the document in whole or in part.
43 (2) Relate to the accuracy of information contained in the document.
44 (3) Create a presumption regarding the validity of the document, regarding the
45 accuracy of information contained in the document, or that the statutory
46 requirements for the document have been met."

48 PART IV. PARENT CHOICE IN NURSING SERVICE PROVIDERS WHEN NURSING
49 SERVICES ARE PROVIDED PURSUANT TO A CHILD'S INDIVIDUALIZED
50 EDUCATION PROGRAM
51 SECTION 4. G.S. 115C-111.2 reads as rewritten:

1 "§ 115C-111.2. Contracts with private service providers.
2 (a) Local educational agencies furnishing special education and related services to
3 children with disabilities may contract with private special education facilities or service
4 providers to furnish any of these services that the public providers are unable to furnish.services.
5 (b) As used in this subsection, "nursing services" are services that may only be provided
6 by nurses licensed in accordance with Article 9A of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes. When
7 providing nursing services pursuant to a child's IEP, local educational agencies shall make
8 available the parent's choice of nurse if all of the following apply:
9 (1) The child's IEP requires nursing services.
10 (2) The child received nursing services from the nurse (i) prior to the nursing
11 services being required by the child's IEP or (ii) prior to the child enrolling in
12 his or her current school.
13 (3) The parent's choice of nurse is employed by a nursing agency and willing to
14 provide the nursing services required by the child's IEP.
15 (4) The nursing agency employing the parent's choice of nurse meets all standard
16 contract terms required for any other nursing agency contracted by the local
17 educational agency, including licensing and liability requirements.
18 (5) The contracted rate is equal to or less than the contracted rate of other nurses
19 contracted by the local educational agency.
20 (c) This section shall not be construed to limit the local educational agency's
21 responsibility to provide a free appropriate public education."
