9 SECTION 1.1. G.S. 32A-16(3) reads as rewritten:
10 "(3) Health care power of attorney. – Except as provided in G.S. 32A-16.1, a
11 written instrument that substantially meets the requirements of this Article,

1 EXPLANATION: You have the right to name someone to make health care decisions for you
2 when you cannot make or communicate those decisions. This form may be used to create a health
3 care power of attorney, and meets the requirements of North Carolina law. However, you are
4 not required to use this form, and North Carolina law allows the use of other forms that meet
5 certain requirements. If you prepare your own health care power of attorney, you should be very
6 careful to make sure it is consistent with North Carolina law.
7
8 This document gives the person you designate as your health care agent broad powers to make
9 health care decisions for you when you cannot make the decision yourself or cannot communicate
10 your decision to other people. You should discuss your wishes concerning life-prolonging
11 measures, mental health treatment, and other health care decisions with your health care agent.
12 Except to the extent that you express specific limitations or restrictions in this form, your health
13 care agent may make any health care decision you could make yourself.
14
15 This form does not impose a duty on your health care agent to exercise granted powers, but when
16 a power is exercised, your health care agent will be obligated to use due care to act in your best
17 interests and in accordance with this document.
18
19 This Health Care Power of Attorney form is intended to be valid in any jurisdiction in which it
20 is presented, but places outside North Carolina may impose requirements that this form does not
21 meet.
22
23 If you want to use this form, you must complete it, sign it, and have your signature witnessed by
24 two qualified witnesses and or proved by a notary public. Follow the instructions about which
25 choices you can initial very carefully. Do not sign this form until two witnesses and or a notary
26 public are present to watch you sign it. You then should give a copy to your health care agent
27 and to any alternates you name. You should consider filing it with the Advance Health Care
28 Directive Registry maintained by the North Carolina Secretary of State:
29 http://www.nclifelinks.org/ahcdr/State.

32 PART II. ADVANCE HEALTH CARE DIRECTIVES
33 SECTION 2.1. G.S. 90-321(c)(3) reads as rewritten:
34 "(3) Except as provided in G.S. 90-321.1, that has been signed by the declarant in
35 the presence of a notary public or two witnesses who believe the declarant to

37 degree to the declarant or to the declarant's spouse, (ii) do not know or have a

31 PART III. ELECTRONIC FILING OF HEALTH CARE POWERS OF ATTORNEY AND
32 ADVANCE HEALTH CARE DIRECTIVES WITH THE NORTH CAROLINA
33 SECRETARY OF STATE
34 SECTION 3.1. G.S. 130A-466 reads as rewritten:
35 "§ 130A-466. Filing requirements.
36 (a) A person may submit any of the following documents and the revocations of these
37 documents to the Secretary of State in electronic or hard copy format for filing in the Advance
38 Health Care Directive Registry established pursuant to this Article:
39 (1) A health care power of attorney under Article 3 of Chapter 32A of the General
40 Statutes.
41 (2) A declaration of a desire for a natural death under Article 23 of Chapter 90 of
42 the General Statutes.
43 (3) An advance instruction for mental health treatment under Part 2 of Article 3
44 of Chapter 122C of the General Statutes.
45 (4) A declaration of an anatomical gift under Part 3A of Article 16 of Chapter
46 130A of the General Statutes.
47 (5) A Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) waiver.
48 (b) Any document and any revocation of a document submitted for filing in the registry
49 shall be notarized regardless of whether notarization is required for its validity. This subsection
50 does not apply to a declaration of an anatomical gift described in subdivision (a)(4) of this
51 section.

5 SECTION 3.2. G.S. 130A-468 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 130A-468. Filing of documents with the registry.
7 (a) When the Secretary of State receives a hard copy of a document that may be filed
8 with the registry pursuant to this Article, the Secretary shall create a digital reproduction of that
9 document and enter the reproduced document into the registry database. When the Secretary of
10 State receives a document in electronic format that may be filed with the registry pursuant to this
11 Article, the Secretary shall enter that document into the registry database. The Secretary is not
12 required to review a document to ensure that it complies with the particular statutory
13 requirements applicable to the document. Each document entered into the registry database shall
14 be assigned a unique file number and password.
15 (b) Upon entering the a reproduced hard copy of a document into the registry database,
16 the Secretary shall return the original hard copy of the document and a wallet-size card containing
17 the document's file number and password to the person who submitted the document. Upon
18 entering into the registry database a document that was received in electronic format, the
19 Secretary shall send a wallet-size card containing the document's file number and password to
20 the person who submitted the document.
21 (c) When the Secretary of State receives a revocation of a document that is filed with the
22 registry and that document's file number and password, or a request to remove that document
23 from the registry without its revocation, the Secretary shall delete that document from the registry
24 database.
25 (c1) The Secretary of State may remove documents of deceased registrants from the
26 registry upon notification of death in writing in a form acceptable to the Secretary of State.
27 (d) The Secretary of State's entry of a document into, or removal of a document from, the
28 registry database does not do any of the following:
29 (1) Affect the validity of the document in whole or in part.
30 (2) Relate to the accuracy of information contained in the document.
31 (3) Create a presumption regarding the validity of the document, regarding the
32 accuracy of information contained in the document, or that the statutory
33 requirements for the document have been met."
