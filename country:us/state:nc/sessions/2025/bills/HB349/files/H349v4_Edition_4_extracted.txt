11 HEALTH CARE AGENT IF YOU ARE COMFORTABLE GIVING THAT PERSON

15
16 EXPLANATION: You have the right to name someone to make health care decisions for you
17 when you cannot make or communicate those decisions. This form may be used to create a health
18 care power of attorney, and meets the requirements of North Carolina law. However, you are
19 not required to use this form, and North Carolina law allows the use of other forms that meet
20 certain requirements. If you prepare your own health care power of attorney, you should be very
21 careful to make sure it is consistent with North Carolina law.
22
23 This document gives the person you designate as your health care agent broad powers to make
24 health care decisions for you when you cannot make the decision yourself or cannot communicate
25 your decision to other people. You should discuss your wishes concerning life-prolonging
26 measures, mental health treatment, and other health care decisions with your health care agent.
27 Except to the extent that you express specific limitations or restrictions in this form, your health
28 care agent may make any health care decision you could make yourself.
29
30 This form does not impose a duty on your health care agent to exercise granted powers, but when
31 a power is exercised, your health care agent will be obligated to use due care to act in your best
32 interests and in accordance with this document.
33
34 This Health Care Power of Attorney form is intended to be valid in any jurisdiction in which it
35 is presented, but places outside North Carolina may impose requirements that this form does not
36 meet.
37
38 If you want to use this form, you must complete it, sign it, and have your signature witnessed by
39 two qualified witnesses and proved by a notary public. Follow the instructions about which
40 choices you can initial very carefully. Do not sign this form until two witnesses and a notary
41 public are present to watch you sign it. You then should give a copy to your health care agent
42 and to any alternates you name. You should consider filing it with the Advance Health Care
43 Directive Registry maintained by the North Carolina Secretary of State:
44 http://www.nclifelinks.org/ahcdr/State.
