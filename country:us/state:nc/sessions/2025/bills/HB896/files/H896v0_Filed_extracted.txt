4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Chapter 50 of the General Statutes is amended by adding the following
6 new Article to read:
7 "Article 6.
8 "Jesse's Law.
9 "§ 50-110. Purpose.
10 The purpose of this Article is to ensure that professional personnel involved in cases
11 containing domestic violence or child abuse allegations receive trauma-informed and culturally
12 appropriate training on the dynamics, signs, and impact of domestic violence and child abuse,
13 including child sexual abuse.
14 "§ 50-111. Definitions.
15 The following definitions apply in this Article:
16 (1) Child custody proceeding. – Any civil action brought under this Chapter
17 concerning the custody of minor children.
18 (2) Domestic violence. – As defined in G.S. 50B-1.
19 (3) Minor child. – As defined in Article 5 of this Chapter.
20 (4) Reunification treatment. – A treatment or therapy aimed at reuniting or
21 reestablishing a relationship between a child and an estranged or rejected
22 parent or other family member of the child.
23 "§ 50-112. Training requirements for court officers and personnel.
24 (a) Any judge, magistrate, or judicial officer who hears child custody proceedings, and
25 any court personnel, including guardian ad litems, mediators, parent coordinators, and any court
26 referred professionals engaging in reunification treatment must complete the training program
27 described in this section.
28 (b) The training program shall consist of an initial training session constituting at least
29 20 hours and not less than 15 hours of ongoing training every five years that includes, at a
30 minimum, all of the following:
31 (1) A focus on solely domestic and sexual violence, including child sexual abuse,
32 physical abuse, emotional abuse, coercive control, implicit and explicit bias,
33 and trauma.
34 (2) Long- and short-term impacts of domestic violence and child abuse on minor
35 children.

1 (3) Victim and perpetrator behavior patterns and relationship dynamics within the
2 cycle of violence.
3 (4) A study of scientific-based research, including, but not limited to, the Duluth
4 model, Saunders study, and Adverse Childhood Experiences study.
5 (c) The training must be provided by a professional with substantial experience in
6 assisting survivors of domestic violence or child abuse, including a victim service provider, as
7 defined in 34 U.S.C. § 12291, and, if possible, a survivor of domestic violence or child physical
8 or sexual abuse. The training must rely on evidence-based and peer-reviewed research by
9 recognized experts in the types of abuse referenced in this section and shall not include any
10 theories, concepts, or belief systems unsupported by the research described in this section.
11 (d) The training must be designed to improve the ability of those persons participating to
12 recognize and respond to child physical and sexual abuse, domestic violence, and trauma in all
13 family victims, particularly children, and assist in making child custody decisions in appropriate
14 proceedings."
15 SECTION 2. The Administrative Office of the Courts or appropriate State agency
16 shall apply to the United States Attorney General's Office for federal grant support to implement
17 the training program established in this act, and any other federal grant funds made available
18 under the Violence Against Women Act Reauthorization Act of 2022.
19 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025.
