28 ESTABLISH A VOLUNTARY DONATION PROGRAM TO SUPPORT FARMS
29 AFFECTED BY HURRICANE HELENE
30 SECTION 3.(a) Definitions. – Unless otherwise provided, the following definitions
31 apply in this section:
32 (1) Affected area. – The counties designated before, on, or after the effective date
33 of this section under a major disaster declaration by the President of the United
34 States under the Stafford Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Helene.
35 (2) Department. – The Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services.
36 (3) Foundation. – The North Carolina Foundation for Soil and Water
37 Conservation, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit corporation.
38 (4) Fund. – The Western North Carolina Farm Relief Fund established in
39 subsection (b) of this section.
40 SECTION 3.(b) There is created the Western North Carolina Farm Relief Fund as
41 an interest-bearing, nonreverting special fund in the Department of Agriculture and Consumer
42 Services. The State Treasurer shall be the custodian of the Fund and shall invest its assets in
43 accordance with the provisions of G.S. 147-69.2 and G.S. 147-69.3. The Department shall award
44 grants from the Fund to individual farmers located in the affected area with verifiable crop losses
45 or damage to farm infrastructure due to Hurricane Helene to assist with any of the following
46 priorities:
47 (1) Removal of debris from stream channels, road culverts, and bridges.
48 (2) Reshape and protect eroded banks.
49 (3) Repair damaged drainage facilities.
50 (4) Establish cover on critically eroding lands.
51 (5) Repair damaged levees and structures.

1 (6) Repair conservation practices.
2 SECTION 3.(c) The Department, in collaboration with the Foundation, shall develop
3 a voluntary plan by which retailers may offer consumers the option to round up the amount of
4 their purchases to the nearest dollar or make a greater contribution and remit the collected funds
5 to the Fund. The Department shall develop criteria for the prioritization of funds and
6 requirements for the matching of funds by grant recipients, if any. A retailer may retain one
7 percent (1%) of the funds collected by the retailer to offset the costs of participation in the
8 program. The Foundation may use up to one percent (1%) of the funds in the Fund for the
9 administration of the program and two percent (2%) of the funds in the Fund for marketing and
10 advertising of the program.
11 SECTION 3.(d) This section is effective when it becomes law and expires June 30,
12 2030. Any unexpended, unencumbered funds remaining in the Fund on June 30, 2030, shall
13 revert to the State Emergency Response and Disaster Relief Fund established in
14 G.S. 166A-19.42.

16 FARM AND FORESTRY DISASTER RELIEF GOVERNMENT EFFICIENCY AUDIT
17 SECTION 4.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
18 Agriculture and Consumer Services the amount of one million five hundred thousand dollars
19 ($1,500,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year for the Department, in
20 consultation with North Carolina State University, North Carolina A&T State University, and
21 North Carolina Cooperative Extension, in the 2025-2026 and 2026-2027 fiscal years to map
22 damage to agricultural and forestry operations in Western North Carolina using drone and
23 satellite data in order to determine the efficiency of the State's response to Hurricane Helene. The
24 Department shall take all steps necessary to establish baseline data as soon as possible in the
25 2025-2026 fiscal year. The Department shall compare the baseline data to data collected as soon
26 as possible after the beginning of the 2026-2027 fiscal year. The Department shall report its
27 findings to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Agriculture and Natural and Economic
28 Resources no later than February 1, 2027.
29 SECTION 4.(b) The funds appropriated under this section shall not revert but shall
30 remain available for purposes consistent with this section until the project is complete.

17 SECTION 4.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
