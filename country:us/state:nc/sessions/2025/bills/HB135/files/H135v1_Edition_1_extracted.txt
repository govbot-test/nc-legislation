5 "§ 105-187.101. Environmental investment assessment imposed.
6 (a) Levy and Rate. – An environmental investment assessment (EIA) of five cents (5¢)
7 is imposed on each item of an animal agriculture product or an analogue product sold at retail if
8 the article bears an identifying term on the product's label. For purposes of this Article, the term
9 "item" refers to an item that is identified by a universal product code or other identifier
10 representing the item and that is used to retrieve the sales price of the item.
11 (b) Environmental Investment Assessment – The EIA imposed by this Article is intended
12 to be passed on to the purchaser of an item to which the EIA is imposed and borne by the
13 purchaser instead of by the retailer. A retailer must collect the EIA due on an item when sold at
14 retail. A retailer must record the protein group to which any animal agriculture product or
15 analogue product belongs and report this information to the Department of Revenue when it
16 remits the collected EIAs to the Department. If the retailer cannot determine the protein group to
17 which an animal agriculture product or analogue product belongs, the retailer shall consult with
18 the trade association with which the product would be most closely identified. The trade
19 association shall make the determination of whether the product should be subject to the
20 assessment and notify the Department.
21 (c) The retailer may retain one percent (1%) of the gross proceeds of the EIAs it collects
22 for the retailer's administrative costs. The EIA is a debt from the purchaser to the retailer until
23 paid and is recoverable at law by the retailer in the same manner as other debts. A retailer is
24 considered to act as a trustee on behalf of the State when it collects the EIA from the purchaser
25 on an applicable sale. The EIA must be stated and charged separately on the invoices or other
26 documents of the retailer given to the purchaser at the time of the sale except for either of the
27 following:
28 (1) Vending machine sales.
29 (2) Where a retailer displays a statement indicating the sales price includes the
30 tax.
31 "§ 105-187.102. Administration.
32 Except as otherwise provided in this Article, the EIA imposed by this Article shall be
33 collected and administered in the same manner as the State sales and use taxes imposed by Article
34 5 of this Chapter. The provisions of Article 9 of this Chapter that are not inconsistent with this
35 Article, including administration, auditing, making returns, promulgation of rules and regulations
36 by the Secretary, additional taxes, assessments and assessment procedure, imposition and
37 collection of taxes and the lien thereof, and penalties, are made a part of this Article and shall be
38 applicable thereto.
39 "§ 105-187.103. Exemptions and refunds.
40 (a) The EIA imposed by this Article does not apply to any of the following:
41 (1) Sales the State cannot constitutionally tax.
42 (2) Products of a farm sold in their original state by the producer of the products
43 if the producer is not primarily a retail merchant.
44 (3) Prepared food.
45 (4) Products containing ingredients from more than one protein group.
46 (5) Meat food products or poultry products with a nonmeat or nonpoultry
47 component other than seasoning, brine solution, or vinegar, or analogue
48 products to such meat food products or poultry products.
49 (b) Except as otherwise provided in this section, the exemptions and refunds allowed in
50 Article 5 of this Chapter do not apply to sales of items subject to assessment under this Article.
