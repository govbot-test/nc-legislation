25 SECTION 3.(a) The Department of Military and Veterans Affairs shall select a

27 responders, and immediate family members of veterans and first responders experiencing one or
28 more of the conditions listed in subsection (b) of this section. For purposes of this act, the
29 following definitions shall apply:
30 (1) Electroencephalogram combined Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation
31 Treatment (eTMS). – Treatment in which transcranial magnetic stimulation
32 frequency pulses are tuned to the patient's physiology and biometric data.
33 (2) Immediate family. – A spouse, child, stepchild, parent, or stepparent.
34 (3) Veteran. – A person who (i) served in the Armed Forces of the United States
35 on active duty, for reasons other than training, and has been discharged under
36 other than dishonorable conditions, (ii) served in a reserve component as
37 defined in 38 U.S.C. § 101(27), and (iii) served in the National Guard of any

50 to or resulting from problems with cognition and problems maintaining
51 attention, concentration, or focus.

33 PART IV. UPDATE OPIOID ABATEMENT TREATMENT PROGRAM RULES
34 SECTION 4.(a) The Commission for Mental Health, Developmental Disabilities,
35 and Substance Use Services (Commission) shall amend the rules applicable to outpatient opioid
36 treatment programs (OTPs) in 10A NCAC 27G .3600 through 10A NCAC 27G .3605 to be more
37 consistent with 42 C.F.R. Part 8 governing medications for the treatment of opioid use disorder.
38 In amending these rules, the Commission shall make at least all of the following changes:
39 (1) Remove stability of a patient's home environment and social relationships as
40 an eligibility criterion for take-home medication.
41 (2) Require the State Opioid Treatment Authority (SOTA) to review patient
42 discharge policies related to continued substance use, missed doses, and
43 nonparticipation in ancillary services such as counseling.
44 (3) Remove structured counseling schedules to better align with federal
45 regulations that promote individualized care that is more patient-centered and
46 flexible.
47 (4) Reduce the number of required drug tests to align with the frequency required
48 by federal regulations.
49 (5) Explicitly permit OTPs to administer methadone to patients who are not
50 enrolled with the OTPs as their patients but can be verified as a patient in

22 (1) Smoke evacuation/filtering system. – Stand-alone, portable equipment
23 utilizing either an electrocautery device with a smoke removal collar or
24 assistant-held smoke evacuation device that effectively captures, filters, and
25 eliminates surgical smoke at the site of origin before the smoke makes contact
26 with the eyes or respiratory tracts of occupants in the room. This equipment is
27 not required to be interconnected to the hospital surgical ventilation or medical
28 gas system.

30 devices, including surgical plume, smoke plume, bio-aerosols, laser-generated
31 airborne contaminants, or lung-damaging dust.

33 implement policies that require the use of a smoke evacuation/filtering system during any
34 surgical procedure that is likely to generate surgical smoke.

36 action against a hospital under G.S. 131E-78 for a violation of this section."
