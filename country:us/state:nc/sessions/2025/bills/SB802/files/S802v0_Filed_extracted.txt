30 SECTION 3. Part 1 of Article 36 of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended
31 by adding a new section to read:
32 "§ 143-341.3. Disposal of surplus real property.
33 (a) Surplus Real Property Disposal. – The Department of Administration shall establish
34 a surplus real property disposal system that limits the duration that unneeded or vacant property
35 is retained by the State. As part of the system, the Department shall (i) adopt rules in accordance
36 with this section, including defining surplus State-owned real property, and (ii) establish a system
37 for continuously identifying and disposing of that property, subject to the approvals required
38 under Chapter 146 of the General Statutes, which shall take into consideration all of the
39 following:
40 (1) The value each facility or parcel of land brings to the performance of the
41 mission of the State or State agency and the fulfillment of its goals and
42 objectives.
43 (2) A general measure of the facility's condition, calculated as a ratio of repair
44 needs to replacement value.
45 (3) The degree to which the property is utilized, measured in accordance with the
46 procedures developed pursuant to G.S. 143-341.2(a)(3).
47 (4) The extent to which the property meets the purpose for which it was intended.
48 (5) The extent to which the State or State agency is likely to need to continue to
49 provide the service or function currently provided at the property.
50 (6) Consideration of the best and most cost-effective manner in which these future
51 needs can be met.

24 SECTION 4. No later than three months after the effective date of this act, the
25 Department of Administration shall submit a report to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee
26 on General Government and the Fiscal Research Division with the vacant building data required
27 under G.S. 143-341, as amended in this act.
28 SECTION 5. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
29 Administration beginning in the 2026-2027 fiscal year the sum of one hundred thousand dollars
30 ($100,000) in recurring funds to administer the surplus real property disposal system created by
31 this act.
32 SECTION 6. This act becomes effective July 1, 2026.
