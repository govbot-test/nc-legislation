35 PART II. PROFESSIONAL EVALUATION TOOLS
36 SECTION 2. No later than November 1, 2026, the State Board of Education shall
37 report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee on at least three potential
38 professional evaluation tools for teachers to provide feedback to teachers on their professional
39 development and to provide administrators with information on teacher quality. The professional
40 evaluation tools shall meet at least the following criteria:
41 (1) Be evidence-based and replicable.
42 (2) Take into account existing measures of teacher effectiveness, including
43 student performance and EVAAS student growth index scores.
44 (3) Include professional feedback, including student surveys and independent
45 performance evaluations from third-party entities.
46 (4) For at least two of the professional evaluation tools proposed by the State
47 Board, be viewable by teachers throughout the school years.
