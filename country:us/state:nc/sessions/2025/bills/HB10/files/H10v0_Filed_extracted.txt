H.B. 10
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Jan 29, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Study State Travel Allowance Reimbursements. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Warren.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ESTABLISH AN INDEPENDENT BIPARTISAN COMMITTEE TO STUDY
3 THE MODERNIZATION OF THE STATE'S CURRENT TRAVEL ALLOWANCES FOR
4 STATE OFFICERS, EMPLOYEES, TEACHERS, AND MEMBERS OF BOARDS,
5 COMMISSIONS, AND THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY BY ADJUSTING THE AMOUNTS
6 BASED ON FEDERAL GUIDELINES.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1. Establishment. – (a) The Committee shall consist of seven members,
9 appointed as follows:
10 (1) Two members appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives as
11 follows:
12 a. One member appointed from two candidates submitted for
13 consideration by the Majority Leader of the House of Representatives.
14 b. One member appointed from two candidates submitted for
15 consideration by the Minority Leader of the House of Representatives.
16 (2) Two members appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the Senate as
17 follows:
18 a. One member appointed from two candidates submitted for
19 consideration by the Majority Leader of the Senate.
20 b. One member appointed from two candidates submitted for
21 consideration by the Minority Leader of the Senate.
22 (3) Three members appointed by the Governor.
23 In the appointment of the members to the Committee, consideration may be given to
24 an individual's current or previous employment, profession, or affiliations, or knowledge or
25 expertise that may be relevant to the Committee's study.
26 (b) The Governor shall call for the first Committee meeting no later than 30 days after
27 this act is effective. The members of the Committee shall appoint a chair. The Committee shall
28 meet upon the call of the chair. A quorum of the Committee shall be a majority of the Committee
29 members.
30 SECTION 2. Duties. – The Committee shall study and make recommendations on
31 adjusting the State's current travel allowances for State officers, employees, teachers, and
32 members of boards, commissions, and the General Assembly. As part of its study, the Committee
33 shall consider the following:
34 (1) History of the State's travel allowances and per diem and mileage rates.
35 (2) Federal reimbursement rates.
36 (3) Consultations with individuals in the following fields and industries:
*DRH10015-MR-26*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 a. Hospitality industry, specifically food and lodging.
2 b. Accounting.
3 c. Human Resources.
4 d. Personnel Management.
5 e. Education.
6 (4) Any matters the Committee deems relevant and instructive.
7 The Committee may establish subcommittees to carry out the duties of the
8 Committee.
9 SECTION 3. Report. – The Committee shall report its findings and
10 recommendations to the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations no later than
11 March 15, 2026. The Committee shall terminate upon the submission of its report or on March
12 15, 2026, whichever occurs first.
13 SECTION 4. This act is effective when it becomes law.
