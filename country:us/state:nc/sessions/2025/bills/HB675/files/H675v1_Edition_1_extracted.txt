6 SECTION 1. G.S. 131E-155 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 131E-155. Definitions.
8 As used in this Article, unless otherwise specified:
9 …
10 (17) "Practical examination" means a test that complies with National Registry of
11 Emergency Medical Technicians examination standards where an applicant
12 for credentialing as an emergency medical technician, emergency medical
13 responder, advanced emergency medical technician, or paramedic

15 SECTION 2. G.S. 131E-159 reads as rewritten:
16 "§ 131E-159. Credentialing requirements.
17 (a) Individuals seeking credentials as an emergency medical technician, advanced
18 emergency medical technician, paramedic, emergency medical responder, emergency medical
19 dispatcher, or emergency medical services instructor shall apply to the Department using forms
20 prescribed by that agency. The Department's representatives shall examine the applicant by either
21 written, practical, or written and practical examination. The Department shall issue appropriate
22 credentials to the applicant who meets all the requirements set forth in this Article and the rules
23 adopted for this Article and who successfully completes the examinations required for
24 credentialing. Emergency medical technician, emergency medical responder, emergency medical
25 dispatcher, advanced emergency medical technician, paramedic, and emergency medical services
26 instructor credentials shall be valid for a period not to exceed four years and may be renewed if
27 the holder meets the requirements set forth in the rules of the Commission. The Department is
28 authorized to revoke or suspend these credentials at any time it determines that the holder no
29 longer meets the qualifications prescribed.
30 (b) The Subject to subsections (c1) and (d1) of this section, the Commission shall adopt
31 rules setting forth the qualifications required for credentialing of emergency medical responders,
32 emergency medical technicians, advanced emergency medical technicians, paramedics,
33 emergency medical dispatchers, and emergency medical services instructors.

1 (f) The Department may deny, suspend, amend, or revoke the credentials of an
2 emergency medical responder, emergency medical technician, advanced emergency medical
3 technician, paramedic, emergency medical dispatcher, or emergency medical services instructor
4 in any case in which the Department finds that there has been a substantial failure to comply with
5 the provisions of this Article or the rules issued under this Article. Prior to implementation of
6 any of the above disciplinary actions, the Department shall consider the recommendations of the
7 EMS Disciplinary Committee pursuant to G.S. 143-519. The Department's decision to deny,
8 suspend, amend, or revoke credentials may be appealed by the applicant or credentialed
9 personnel pursuant to the provisions of Article 3 of Chapter 150B of the General Statutes, the
10 Administrative Procedure Act.
11 (g) An individual who applies for EMS credentials, seeks to renew EMS credentials, or
12 holds EMS credentials is subject to a criminal background review by the Department. At the
13 request of the Department, the Emergency Medical Services Disciplinary Committee, established
14 by G.S. 143-519, shall review criminal background information and make a recommendation
15 regarding the eligibility of an individual to obtain initial EMS credentials, renew EMS
16 credentials, or maintain EMS credentials. The Department and the Emergency Medical Services
17 Disciplinary Committee shall keep all information obtained pursuant to this subsection
18 confidential. The Medical Care Commission shall adopt rules to implement the provisions of this
19 subsection, including rules to establish a reasonable fee to offset the actual costs of criminal
20 history information obtained pursuant to G.S. 143B-1209.33.
21 (g1) A person who is credentialed under this Article shall be required to keep on their
22 person a certification card that is up to date. In the event of a person losing their certification
23 card, the person shall order a duplicate. The Commission shall publish on their website a method
24 for contact to assist persons in ordering a replacement card.
25 (h) A person who is required to register as a sex offender under Article 27A of Chapter
26 14 of the General Statutes, or who was convicted of an offense which would have required
27 registration if committed at a time when such registration would have been required by law, shall
28 not be granted EMS credentials. The Department shall not renew the credentials of any person
29 who would be ineligible for EMS credentials under this subsection."
30 SECTION 3. The Medical Care Commission shall adopt temporary rules to
31 implement the provisions of this act. The temporary rules adopted shall remain in effect until
32 permanent rules are adopted to replace the temporary rules.
33 SECTION 4. This act becomes effective January 1, 2026.
