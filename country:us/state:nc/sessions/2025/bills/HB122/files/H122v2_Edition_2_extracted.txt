11 "§ 90-750.1. Title and purpose.
12 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "North Carolina Integrative
13 Therapies Act."
14 (b) The North Carolina Integrative Therapies Act is established to safeguard the public
15 health, safety, and welfare; to protect the public from being harmed by unqualified persons; to
16 assure the highest degree of professional services and conduct on the part of practitioners of the
17 integrative therapies; to provide for the establishment of licensure requirements; and to ensure
18 the availability of integrative therapies services of high quality to persons in need of such
19 services.
20 "§ 90-750.2. Definitions.
21 The following definitions apply in this Article:
22 (1) Advisory committee. – A committee charged with supervising the day-to-day
23 regulation of an integrative therapy profession. Advisory committees shall
24 screen applicants for licensure and conduct all investigations necessary to give
25 recommendations to the Board.
26 (2) Board. – The North Carolina Board of Integrative Therapies, as established
27 pursuant to G.S. 90-750.3.
28 (3) Integrative therapies. – The use of allopathic, complementary, or integrative
29 approaches to the art and science of medicine for the prevention,
30 identification, and treatment of human physical or mental conditions, diseases,
31 ailments, illnesses, infirmities, pain, defects, or injuries and the promotion and
32 restoration of health and wellness.
33 (4) Integrative therapies profession. – Any of the professions listed in
34 G.S. 90-750.8.
35 (5) North Carolina Integrative Therapies License. – A license in an integrative
36 therapy profession issued pursuant to this Article.

3 (a) Establishment. – The North Carolina Board of Integrative Therapies is hereby created.
4 The Board shall consist of the following members appointed to three-year terms as follows:

6 recommendation of the President Pro Tempore of the Senate.
7 (2) One music therapist appointed by the Speaker of the House of
8 Representatives.
9 (3) One art therapist appointed by the Governor.
10 (4) One music therapist appointed by the Governor.

12 with integrative therapies appointed by the Governor.
13 (6) One member of the public appointed by the Governor.
14 (b) Vacancies. – Any vacancies on the Board shall be filled by the appointing authority
15 as provided in subsection (a) of this section, except that any vacancy in appointments by the
16 General Assembly shall be filled in accordance with G.S. 120-122. Individuals appointed to fill
17 vacancies shall serve until the normal expiration of the term they were appointed to fill.
18 (c) Qualifications. –
19 (1) All members appointed to the Board shall be citizens of the United States and
20 residents of the State of North Carolina.
21 (2) All members appointed to the Board, except for the physicians appointed by
22 the Governor under subdivision (5) of subsection (a) of this section and the
23 public member appointed by the Governor under subdivision (6) of subsection

25 Therapies License in their respective profession and remain in good standing
26 for the duration of their terms.
27 (3) Public members of the Board shall not be:
28 a. An agent or employee trained or experienced in the practice of
29 integrative therapies.
30 b. An agent or employee of a person engaged in the practice of
31 integrative therapies.
32 c. A spouse of an individual licensed pursuant to this Article.

34 profession.

47 removed from office by the Governor for good cause shown.

49 compensation but may receive reimbursement as provided in G.S. 93B-5. The officers of the

42 (a) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, any person violating any
43 provision of this Article shall be punished as follows:
44 (1) For the first violation, the individual shall receive a written warning.
45 (2) For the second violation, the individual shall be subject to a civil penalty of
46 no more than two hundred dollars ($200.00).

44 observation and interaction of the client in music and nonmusic settings, (ii)
45 development and implementation of treatment plans, based on a client's
46 assessed needs, using music interventions, including music improvisation,
47 receptive music listening, song writing, lyric discussion, music and imagery,
48 singing, music performance, learning through music, music combined with
49 other arts, music-assisted relaxation, music-based patient education,
50 electronic music technology, adapted music intervention, and movement to

2 treatment.
3 (4) Music therapy services. – The provision of services to accomplish music
4 therapy goals, including (i) conducting an individualized assessment for the
5 purpose of collecting systematic, comprehensive, and accurate data necessary
6 to determine the course of action subsequent to the individualized treatment
7 plan, (ii) planning and developing the individualized music therapy treatment
8 plan that identifies an individual's goals, objectives, and potential treatment

11 "§ 90-750.1. Title and purpose.
12 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "North Carolina Integrative
13 Therapies Act."
14 (b) The North Carolina Integrative Therapies Act is established to safeguard the public
15 health, safety, and welfare; to protect the public from being harmed by unqualified persons; to
16 assure the highest degree of professional services and conduct on the part of practitioners of the
17 integrative therapies; to provide for the establishment of licensure requirements; and to ensure
18 the availability of integrative therapies services of high quality to persons in need of such
19 services.
20 "§ 90-750.2. Definitions.
21 The following definitions apply in this Article:
22 (1) Advisory committee. – A committee charged with supervising the day-to-day
23 regulation of an integrative therapy profession. Advisory committees shall
24 screen applicants for licensure and conduct all investigations necessary to give
25 recommendations to the Board.
26 (2) Board. – The North Carolina Board of Integrative Therapies, as established
27 pursuant to G.S. 90-750.3.
28 (3) Integrative therapies. – The use of allopathic, complementary, or integrative
29 approaches to the art and science of medicine for the prevention,
30 identification, and treatment of human physical or mental conditions, diseases,
31 ailments, illnesses, infirmities, pain, defects, or injuries and the promotion and
32 restoration of health and wellness.
33 (4) Integrative therapies profession. – Any of the professions listed in
34 G.S. 90-750.8.
35 (5) North Carolina Integrative Therapies License. – A license in an integrative
36 therapy profession issued pursuant to this Article.
