11 "§ 90-750.1. Title and purpose.
12 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "North Carolina Healing Arts
13 Act."
14 (b) The North Carolina Healing Arts Act is established to safeguard the public health,
15 safety, and welfare; to protect the public from being harmed by unqualified persons; to assure
16 the highest degree of professional services and conduct on the part of practitioners of the healing
17 arts; to provide for the establishment of licensure requirements; and to ensure the availability of
18 healing arts services of high quality to persons in need of such services.
19 "§ 90-750.2. Definitions.
20 The following definitions apply in this Article:
21 (1) Advisory committee. – A committee charged with supervising the day-to-day
22 regulation of a healing arts profession. Advisory committees shall screen
23 applicants for licensure and conduct all investigations necessary to give
24 recommendations to the Commission.
25 (2) Commission. – The North Carolina Healing Arts Commission, as established
26 pursuant to G.S. 90-750.3.
27 (3) Healing arts. – The use of allopathic, complementary, or alternative
28 approaches to the art and science of medicine for the prevention,
29 identification, and treatment of human physical or mental conditions, diseases,
30 ailments, illnesses, infirmities, pain, defects, or injuries and the promotion and
31 restoration of health and wellness.
32 (4) Healing arts profession. – Any of the professions listed in G.S. 90-750.8.
33 (5) North Carolina Healing Arts License. – A license in a healing arts profession
34 issued pursuant to this Article.

13 It is the policy of this State that the practice of reflexology shall be considered a healing arts

15 "§ 90-750.11. Definitions.
16 The following definitions apply in this Part:
17 (1) Certifying entity. – The American Reflexology Certification Board or its
18 successor.
19 (2) Committee. – The North Carolina Reflexology Advisory Committee.
20 (3) National certification. – A determination by the certifying entity that an
21 individual has passed the certifying entity's national exam and that the
22 individual has an active status with the certifying entity.
23 (4) North Carolina Licensed Reflexologist. – An individual that (i) is certified by
24 the certifying entity as a certified reflexologist and (ii) has been issued a North
25 Carolina Healing Arts License in reflexology by the Commission.
26 (5) Reflexology. – A protocol of manual techniques, including thumb- and
27 finger-walking, hook and backup, and rotating-on-a-point, that are applied to
28 specific reflex areas predominantly on the feet and hands and that stimulate
29 the complex neural pathways linking body systems and support the body's
30 efforts to function optimally. The practice of reflexology is not massage and
31 bodywork therapy as defined in Article 36 of this Chapter.

4 "§ 90-750.16. Compensation for reflexology services; prohibited acts.
5 (a) Compensation may be received for reflexology services only when those services are
6 performed by an individual holding a North Carolina Healing Arts License in reflexology.
7 Nonlicensed individuals who provide reflexology services or who engage in the practice of
8 reflexology are subject to the penalties in G.S. 90-750.6 or an injunction under G.S. 90-750.7
9 unless any of the following applies:
10 (1) The individual is a reflexology student working to obtain a certification of
11 completion from a nationally recognized reflexology education program.
12 (2) The individual holds a certification of completion from a nationally
13 recognized reflexology education program, and that program was completed
14 no more than six months prior to that individual receiving compensation for
15 reflexology services.
16 (b) Unless all reflexology services are performed by individuals holding a North Carolina
17 Healing Arts License in reflexology, a place of business where reflexology services are offered
18 for compensation shall be subject to a civil penalty of no more than five hundred dollars
19 ($500.00) for each violation and a suspension of any license to operate the business for no more
20 than 90 days. The clear proceeds of the civil penalty assessed under this section shall be remitted
21 to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with G.S. 115C-457.2. The Commission
22 shall enforce the provisions of this subsection.
23 "§ 90-750.17. License to be displayed; advertising.
24 (a) At all places of business in which compensated reflexology services are offered, a
25 valid and up-to-date license for all individuals offering compensated reflexology services shall
26 be displayed in a prominent place.
27 (b) All public advertising designed to attract clients for reflexology services shall identify
28 that the services will be performed by an individual holding a North Carolina Healing Arts
29 License in reflexology.
30 (c) No individual shall use the title "North Carolina Licensed Reflexologist" unless that
31 individual holds a valid North Carolina Healing Arts License in reflexology issued by the
32 Commission. Individuals holding themselves out as North Carolina Licensed Reflexologists shall
33 carry the identification card described in G.S. 90-750.13(7) at all times when providing
34 reflexology services. Individuals in violation of this subsection are subject to the penalties in
35 G.S. 90-750.6 or an injunction under G.S. 90-750.7.
36 "§ 90-750.18. Reciprocity.
37 The Committee may recommend that the Commission grant, upon application and payment
38 of proper fees, a North Carolina Healing Arts License in reflexology to a person who has been
39 licensed or certified to practice reflexology in another state or territory of the United States whose
40 standards of competency are substantially equivalent to the requirements for licensure provided
41 in this Article.
42 "§ 90-750.19. Exemptions.
43 This Part shall not apply to an individual who may apply pressure to the reflex areas on the
44 hands, feet, and outer ears in the regular course of that individual's work, so long as (i) the
45 individual is licensed by this State as a massage and bodywork therapist, physician, chiropractor,
46 acupuncturist, physical therapist, cosmetologist, registered nurse, or as a member of other
47 professions licensed by the State and (ii) this application of pressure to the reflex areas on the
48 hands, feet, and outer ears accounts for no more than twenty-five percent (25%) of that
49 individual's work.

3 "§ 90-750.21. Definitions.
4 The following definitions apply in this Part:
5 (1) Certifying entity. – The Certification Board for Music Therapists or its
6 successor.
7 (2) Committee. – The North Carolina Music Therapy Advisory Committee.
8 (3) Music therapy. – The clinical and evidence-based use of music interventions
9 to accomplish individualized goals within a therapeutic relationship by a
10 credentialed professional who has completed an approved music therapy
11 program, including (i) assessment of a client's emotional, physical, and
12 spiritual health; social functioning; communication abilities; and cognitive
13 skills through the client's history and observation and interaction of the client
14 in music and nonmusic settings, (ii) development and implementation of
15 treatment plans, based on a client's assessed needs, using music interventions,
16 including music improvisation, receptive music listening, song writing, lyric
17 discussion, music and imagery, music performance, learning through music,
18 and movement to music, and (iii) evaluation and documentation of the client's
19 response to treatment.
20 (4) Music therapy services. – The provision of services to accomplish music
21 therapy goals, including (i) conducting an individualized assessment for the
22 purpose of collecting systematic, comprehensive, and accurate data necessary
23 to determine the course of action subsequent to the individualized treatment
24 plan, (ii) planning and developing the individualized music therapy treatment
25 plan that identifies an individual's goals, objectives, and potential treatment
26 intervention strategies, (iii) implementing the individualized music therapy
27 treatment plan consistent with the individual's overall treatment program, (iv)
28 systematically evaluating and comparing the individual's response to the
29 individualized music therapy treatment plan and suggesting modifications, as
30 appropriate, (v) developing a discharge plan in collaboration with the
31 individual, the individual's family, treatment team, and other identified
32 support networks, when appropriate, (vi) minimizing the impact of
33 environmental constraints as a barrier to participation in least restrictive
34 environments for individuals engaging in music therapy, (vii) collaborating
35 with and educating the individual, family, caregiver, and others to foster an
36 environment responsive to the developmental needs of the individual as
37 addressed in music therapy, and (viii) consulting with groups, programs,
38 organizations, or communities to improve accessibility to music therapy
39 services.
40 (5) National certification. – A determination by the certifying entity that an
41 individual has satisfied the certifying entity's requirements and that the
42 individual has an active status with the certifying entity.
43 (6) North Carolina Licensed Music Therapist. – An individual who has been
44 issued a North Carolina Healing Arts License in music therapy by the
45 Commission.

12 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "North Carolina Healing Arts
13 Act."
14 (b) The North Carolina Healing Arts Act is established to safeguard the public health,
15 safety, and welfare; to protect the public from being harmed by unqualified persons; to assure
16 the highest degree of professional services and conduct on the part of practitioners of the healing
17 arts; to provide for the establishment of licensure requirements; and to ensure the availability of
18 healing arts services of high quality to persons in need of such services.

24 recommendations to the Commission.

26 pursuant to G.S. 90-750.3.
27 (3) Healing arts. – The use of allopathic, complementary, or alternative
28 approaches to the art and science of medicine for the prevention,
29 identification, and treatment of human physical or mental conditions, diseases,
30 ailments, illnesses, infirmities, pain, defects, or injuries and the promotion and
31 restoration of health and wellness.
32 (4) Healing arts profession. – Any of the professions listed in G.S. 90-750.8.
33 (5) North Carolina Healing Arts License. – A license in a healing arts profession
