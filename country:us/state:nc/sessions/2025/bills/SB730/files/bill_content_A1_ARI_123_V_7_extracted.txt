19 "WATER USE STANDARDS FOR DATA CENTERS
20 SECTION 2.(a) Article 38 of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended by
21 adding a new section to read:
22 "§ 143-355.5A. Water use standards for data centers.
23 (a) The Department shall adopt rules establishing water use standards for data centers to
24 ensure the protection of surface water and groundwater resources.
25 (b) The water use standards adopted by the Department: (i) may require data centers to
26 employ closed-loop or reclaimed water systems as necessary to avoid impacts to areas with water
27 availability concerns; and (ii) shall not permit a data center to use an evaporative cooling system.
28 For purposes of this subsection, an "evaporative cooling system" means any cooling system that
29 consumes water through evaporation as a primary method of heat rejection.
30 (c) For purposes of this section, the term "data center" means a facility, campus of
31 facilities, or array of interconnected facilities used by an entity or other business enterprise for
32 the primary purpose of storing, retrieving, managing, and processing digital data, which has a
33 peak monthly electricity demand of 100 megawatts or greater. The term "data center" does not
34 include a facility or portion of a facility containing electronic equipment that is incidental to and
35 used in support of a business whose primary business activity is not the storing, retrieving,
36 managing, and processing of digital data."
37 SECTION 2.(b) The Department of Environmental Quality shall adopt permanent
38 rules to implement the requirements of this section no later than September 1, 2026. The rules
39 adopted shall apply only to data centers for which no local development approvals or State
40 permits, certifications, or authorizations have been issued for siting of the data center on or before
41 the date the rules become effective.";
