Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Annex Various Military Property/Jacksonville. (Local)
Sponsors: Senator Lazzara (Primary Sponsor).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
April 22, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ANNEX INTO THE CITY OF JACKSONVILLE CERTAIN PORTIONS OF
3 MARINE CORPS BASE CAMP LEJEUNE, MARINE CORPS AIR STATION NEW
4 RIVER, AND MARINE CORPS SPECIAL OPERATIONS COMMAND AT STONES
5 BAY AND THE NEW RIVER.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. The following described territory is added to the corporate limits of
8 the City of Jacksonville:
9 Marine Corps Base Camp Lejeune
10 Beginning at the intersection of the southern right of way line of NC Highway 24 and the
11 centerline of the Camp Lejeune Railroad;
12 Thence leaving said right of way and following the centerline of said Camp Lejeune Railroad
13 in a southernly direction for approximately 3,600 feet to the intersection of the Camp Lejeune
14 Railroad and the centerline of the mainline of the Camp Lejeune Railroad to a point;
15 Thence along the mainline of the Camp Lejeune Railroad, paralleling Holcomb Boulevard in
16 a southeasterly and southerly direction for a distance of approximately 13,200 feet to the point
17 of intersection of the centerline of Sneads Ferry Road and the centerline of the Camp Lejeune
18 Railroad;
19 Thence following the centerline of the Sneads Ferry Road in a southeasterly direction
20 approximately 17,700 feet to the intersection of said road and the high water mark of southern
21 bank of Cowhead Creek;
22 Thence following the southern high water mark of Cowhead Creek approximately 5,600 feet
23 in a westerly direction to its junction with French's Creek (also known as Frenchman's Creek);
24 Thence along the southern high water mark of French's Creek in a northerly and westerly
25 direction approximately 8,300 feet to the monument known as "French" (North Carolina State
26 Plane Coordinates X = 2,498,523.47, Y = 326,128.67);
27 Thence heading in a southerly direction towards New River Inlet where Camp Lejeune
28 crosses the Atlantic Intercoastal Waterway to the southern tip of Onslow Beach where the New
29 River meets the Atlantic Ocean (New River Inlet);
30 Thence heading in a northeasterly direction for approximately 54,800 feet to Sanders
31 Channel;
32 Thence heading southwesterly and then turning northerly where the boundary crosses the
33 Atlantic Intercoastal Waterway following the western boundary of Bear Creek until it meets Bear
34 Creek Road;
35 Thence following the southern right of way boundary of Bear Creek Road until it meets NC
36 Highway 172;

1 Thence following the western right of way boundary of NC Highway 172 in a northerly
2 direction for approximately 20,350 feet until it intersects Starling Road;
3 Thence following the southern right of way of Starling Road in a westerly direction for
4 approximately 1,735 feet until this meets the southern right of way of NC Highway 24;
5 Thence following the southern right of way of NC Highway 24 in a westerly direction for
6 approximately 27,815 feet until it intersects with the beginning point and only containing those
7 parcels/land owned by the United States of America.
8 Marine Corps Base Camp LeJeune – Greater Sandy Run Training Area
9 Beginning at the southwest right of way corner at the intersection of US Highway 17 and
10 High Hill Road;
11 Thence following the western boundary of US Highway 17 in a southerly direction to NC
12 highway 50;
13 Thence following the eastern boundary of NC Highway 50 in a northwesterly direction to
14 Padgett Road;
15 Thence following the southern boundary of Padgett Road to the intersection of Haws Run
16 Road;
17 Thence following the southern boundary of Haws Run Road in an easterly direction to
18 intersection of Dawsons Cabin Road;
19 Thence following the southern right of way boundary of Dawsons Cabin Road to High Hill
20 Road;
21 Thence following the western right of way boundary of High Hill Road in a southerly
22 direction to the beginning point and only containing those parcels/land owned by the United
23 States of America.
24 Marine Corps Air Station New River, Marine Corps Special Operations Command at Stones
25 Bay and the New River
26 Beginning at the monument "Jarman" located at New River Air Station at the junction of
27 Southwest Creek with New River (NC Plane Coordinates X= 2,472,581.66, Y= 348,526.23);
28 Thence up the northern or main channel of Southwest Creek in a southerly and then
29 northwesterly direction to the junction of the said creek with Rhodes Creek at a point immediately
30 northwest of the old Atlantic Coast Line Railroad (New Bern Branch);
31 Thence up the meanders of Rhodes Creek and Richardson Branch in a northwesterly direction
32 to its intersection with the eastern right of way line of US Highway 17;
33 Thence heading in a southerly direction following the eastern right of way boundary of US
34 Highway 17 for approximately 42,970 feet to the northern corner point of PAR ID 028458 (Tax
35 map parcel 757-9);
36 Thence heading in a southerly direction for approximately 960 feet towards the southeastern
37 most corner point of PAR ID 058282 (Tax map parcel 757-9.1) which intersects with NC
38 Highway 210 Right of way;
39 Thence following in an easterly direction the northern right of way boundary of NC Highway
40 210 for approximately 14,601 feet to the northwest corner point of PAR ID 041004 (Tax map
41 parcel 765-62.18);
42 Thence following the northwestern property boundary in a northeasterly direction to
43 northeastern corner point of PAR ID 041004 where it abuts Everett Creek for approximately
44 6,090 feet;
45 Thence following Everett Creek in a southeasterly direction until it meets the New River;
46 Thence following the New River towards the NC Highway 172 Bridge in a northeasterly
47 direction;
48 Thence following the centerline of NC Highway 172 to Pollocks Point;
49 Thence following the New River in a northerly direction towards Jacksonville to the
50 monument known as "French" (North Carolina State Plane Coordinates X = 2,498,523.47, Y =
51 326,128.67);

1 Thence approximately 17,908 feet to Monument "371 USMC" located on Hadnot Point
2 (Hospital Point) (NC Plane Coordinates X = 2,487,613.10, Y = 340,329.81);
3 Thence north approximately 13,959 feet to the monument "Paradise Eccentric" located at
4 Paradise Point (NC Plane Coordinates X = 2,482,316.56, Y = 353,245.56);
5 Thence crossing New River South approximately 10,819 feet to the beginning point,
6 monument "Jarman" located at New River Air Station at the junction of Southwest Creek with
7 New River (NC Plane Coordinates X = 2,472,581.66, Y = 348,526.23).
8 SECTION 2. This act shall not create or increase any right for a city to regulate or
9 otherwise influence activities of the federal government or any activities and operations
10 occurring in or on Marine Corps Base Camp Lejeune, Marine Corps Air Station New River, or
11 Marine Corps Special Operations Command at Stones Bay and the New River.
12 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective June 30, 2026. To the extent permitted
13 under State and federal law, property in the territory described by Section 1 of this act as of
14 January 1, 2026, is subject to municipal taxes for taxes imposed for taxable years beginning on
15 or after July 1, 2026.
