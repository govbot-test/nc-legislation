1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO DIRECT THE LEGISLATIVE RESEARCH COMMISSION TO STUDY
3 AFFORDABLE HOUSING IN THE STATE, TO MAKE HOUSING DISCRIMINATION
4 ON THE BASIS OF "SOURCE OF INCOME" A VIOLATION OF THE STATE FAIR
5 HOUSING ACT, TO EXPAND WORKFORCE HOUSING BY PROVIDING CURRENT
6 AND ONGOING FUNDING FOR THE HOUSING TRUST FUND, TO PROHIBIT

11 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
12 SECTION 1.(a) The Legislative Research Commission (LRC) shall study the
13 availability of affordable housing in this State.
14 As part of its study, the LRC shall do all of the following:
15 (1) Examine the current availability of affordable housing in metropolitan and
16 rural areas of the State.
17 (2) Assess the availability of publicly owned land that could be developed into
18 affordable residential housing.
19 (3) Identify existing federal, State, and local subsidies, grants, and other
20 incentives and programs available to increase the availability of affordable
21 housing, the extent to which the subsidies, grants, and other incentives and
22 programs are utilized, and the successfulness of their usage in increasing the
23 availability of affordable housing.
24 (4) Examine the challenges facing those in need of affordable housing, including
25 financial, social, and logistical challenges.
26 (5) Assess the ability of local governments to leverage existing laws and
27 resources, including partnering with nonprofit and for-profit entities, to
28 increase the availability of affordable housing.
29 (6) Examine the best practices of other states in increasing the availability of
30 affordable housing.
31 (7) Examine any unique challenges faced by local governments across the State
32 resulting from differences in economic prosperity and considering the
33 differences between the metropolitan and rural areas of the State.
34 (8) Evaluate the potential benefits, cost-savings, and improved affordability from
35 utilizing energy efficiency standards in housing.

2 utilities, private businesses, and local governments in replacing aging housing
3 in existing programs with newer modular and mobile home units.
4 (10) Examine any costs that may be associated with government rules, regulations,
5 and ordinances.
6 (11) Any other matters the LRC deems relevant to its efforts.

5 SECTION 5. G.S. 105-228.30 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 105-228.30. Imposition of excise tax; distribution of proceeds.
7 (a) An excise tax is levied on each instrument by which any interest in real property is
8 conveyed to another person. The tax rate is one dollar ($1.00) on each five hundred dollars
9 ($500.00) or fractional part thereof of the consideration or value of the interest conveyed. The
10 transferor must pay the tax to the register of deeds of the county in which the real estate is located
11 before recording the instrument of conveyance. If the instrument transfers a parcel of real estate
12 lying in two or more counties, however, the tax must be paid to the register of deeds of the county
13 in which the greater part of the real estate with respect to value lies.
14 The excise tax on instruments imposed by this Article applies to timber deeds and contracts
15 for the sale of standing timber to the same extent as if these deeds and contracts conveyed an
16 interest in real property.
17 (b) The register of deeds of each county must remit the proceeds of the tax levied by this
18 section to the county finance officer. The finance officer of each county must credit one-half of
19 the proceeds to the county's general fund and remit the remaining one-half of the proceeds, less
20 taxes refunded and the county's allowance for administrative expenses, to the Department of
21 Revenue on a monthly basis. A county may retain two percent (2%) of the amount of tax proceeds
22 allocated for remittance to the Department of Revenue as compensation for the county's cost in
23 collecting and remitting the State's share of the tax. The Department of Revenue shall credit an
24 amount equal to thirty-three percent (33%) of the funds remitted to the Department of Revenue
25 under this subsection to the North Carolina Housing Trust Fund, established under G.S. 122E-3,
26 and shall credit the remaining funds to the General Fund."

31 "§ 75-70.1. Definitions.
32 The following definitions apply in this Article:
33 (1) Consumer. – An individual.
34 (2) Credit report. – Any written, oral, or other communication of any information
35 by a credit reporting agency bearing on a consumer's creditworthiness, credit
36 standing, credit capacity, character, general reputation, personal
37 characteristics, or mode of living which is used or expected to be used or
38 collected in whole or in part for the purpose of serving as a factor in
39 establishing the consumer's eligibility for any of the following:
40 a. Employment.
41 b. Housing, including applications for lease or mortgage.
42 c. Credit to be used primarily for personal, family, or household
43 purposes.
44 d. Any other purpose authorized under 15 U.S.C. § 168(b).
45 (3) Credit reporting agency. – Any person who, for monetary fees, dues, or on a
46 cooperative nonprofit basis, regularly engages in whole or in part in the
47 practice of assembling or evaluating consumer credit information or other
48 information on consumers for the purpose of furnishing consumer reports to
49 third parties.

9 SECTION 7.(a) Chapter 157 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
10 Article to read:
11 "Article 6.
12 "Optional Credit Reporting for Tenants of Subsidized Housing Program.
13 "§ 157-71. Definitions.
14 The following definitions apply in this Article:
15 (1) Credit reporting agency. – As defined in G.S. 75-70.1(3).
16 (2) Housing authority. – As defined in G.S. 157-3(1).
17 (3) Housing project. – As defined in G.S. 157-3(12).
18 (4) Landlord. – As defined in G.S. 42-59(7).
19 (5) Participating landlord. – A landlord who decides to participate in the Optional
20 Credit Reporting Program for Tenants of Subsidized Housing created by
21 G.S. 157-72. Any housing authority engaged in the practice of directly leasing
22 residential accommodations to tenants shall automatically be considered a
23 participating landlord for the purposes of this Article.
24 (6) Persons of low income. – As defined in G.S. 157-3(15a).
25 (7) Persons of moderate income. – As defined in G.S. 157-3(15b).
26 (8) Rent payment information. – Information concerning a tenant's timely
27 payment of rent, untimely payment of rent, or nonpayment of rent. "Rent
28 payment information" does not include information concerning a tenant's
29 payment or nonpayment of any fees other than rent.
30 (9) Residential accommodations. – Real property used for residential purposes by
31 an individual or family.
32 (10) Subsidized housing. – Residential accommodations that receive financial
33 assistance from federal, state, or local government programs aimed at
34 reducing housing costs for persons of low income and persons of moderate
35 income. This assistance may include direct subsidies, tax credits, grants, or
36 other financial mechanisms designed to make housing more affordable.
37 (11) Tenant. – As defined in G.S. 42-59(10).
38 "§ 157-72. Establishment of the Optional Credit Reporting for Tenants of Subsidized
39 Housing Program.
40 (a) Any participating landlord of subsidized housing shall offer the tenant or tenants
41 obligated on the lease of each residence the option of having the tenant's rent payment
42 information reported to at least one credit reporting agency. A tenant's election to have his or her
43 rent payment information reported under this section shall be in writing, as described in
44 subsection (c) of this section.
45 (b) For leases entered into on or after October 1, 2025, the offer to report rent payment
46 information shall be made at the time of the lease agreement and at least once annually thereafter.
47 (c) The offer to report rent payment information shall include a written election of rent
48 reporting that contains at least all of the following:
49 (1) A statement that reporting the tenant's rent payment information is optional.
50 (2) Identification of each credit reporting agency to which rental payment
51 information will be reported.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (3) A statement that all of the tenant's rental payments will be reported, regardless
2 of whether the payments are timely, late, or missed.
3 (4) The amount of any fee charged pursuant to subsection (f) of this section.
4 (5) Instructions on how to submit the written election of rent reporting to the
5 landlord by mail.
6 (6) A statement that the tenant may opt into rent reporting at any time following
7 the initial offer by the participating landlord.
8 (7) A statement that the tenant may elect to stop rent reporting at any time, but
9 that they will not be able to resume rent reporting for at least six months after
10 their election to opt out.
11 (8) Instructions on how to opt out of reporting rental payment information.
12 (9) A signature block that the tenant shall date and sign in order to accept the offer
13 of rent reporting.
14 (d) When the offer of rent reporting is made, the participating landlord shall provide the
15 tenant with a self-addressed, stamped envelope to return the written election of rent reporting.
16 The participating landlord may also accept the written election of rent reporting from the tenant
17 in person.
18 (e) The written election to begin rent reporting shall not be accepted from the tenant at
19 the time of the offer. A tenant may submit their completed written election of rent reporting at
20 any time after they receive the offer of rent reporting from the participating landlord. A tenant
21 may request and shall obtain additional copies of the written election of rent reporting form from
22 the participating landlord at any time.
23 (f) If a tenant elects to have that tenant's rental payments reported to a credit reporting
24 agency under subsection (a) of this section, the participating landlord may require that tenant to
25 pay a fee not to exceed the lesser of the actual cost to the landlord to provide the service or ten
26 dollars ($10.00) per month. The payment or nonpayment of this fee by the tenant shall not be
27 reported to a credit reporting agency.
28 (g) If a tenant fails to pay any fee required by the participating landlord pursuant to
29 subsection (f) of this section, all of the following shall apply:
30 (1) The failure to pay the fee shall not be cause for termination of the tenancy.
31 (2) The landlord shall not deduct the unpaid fee from the tenant's security deposit.
32 (3) If the fee remains unpaid for 30 days or more, the landlord may stop reporting
33 the tenant's rental payments and the tenant shall be unable to elect rent
34 reporting again for a period of six months from the date on which the fee first
35 became due.
36 (h) A tenant who elects to have rent payment information reported as described in
37 subsection (a) of this section may subsequently file a written request with their landlord to stop
38 that reporting with which the participating landlord shall comply. A tenant who elects to stop
39 reporting rent payment information shall not be allowed to elect rent reporting again for a period
40 of at least six months from the date of the tenant's written request to stop reporting.
41 (i) A tenant who elects to have rent reported does not forfeit any rights under G.S. 42-42.
42 Nothing in this section shall be construed to relieve a landlord of his or her obligation to provide
43 fit premises as required by G.S. 42-42."
