4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-202, as amended by S.L. 2025-70, reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 14-202. Secretly peeping into room occupied by another person.
7 (a) Any Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, any person who shall peep
8 secretly into any room occupied by another person shall be guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor. Any
9 person who commits a violation of this subsection in which the victim is a minor shall be guilty
10 of a Class A1 misdemeanor.
11 (a1) Unless covered by another provision of law providing greater punishment, any person
12 who secretly or surreptitiously peeps underneath or through the clothing being worn by another
13 person, through the use of a mirror or other device, for the purpose of viewing the body of, or the
14 undergarments worn by, that other person without their consent shall be guilty of a Class 1
15 misdemeanor. Any person who commits a violation of this subsection in which the victim is a
16 minor shall be guilty of a Class A1 misdemeanor.
17 (b) The following definitions apply in this section:
18 (1) Minor. – An individual who is less than 18 years of age.
19 (2) Photographic image. – Any photograph or photographic reproduction, still or
20 moving, or any videotape, motion picture, or live television transmission, or
21 any digital image of any individual.
22 (2)(3) Private area of an individual. – The naked or undergarment clad genitals,
23 pubic area, buttocks, or female breast of that individual.
24 (3)(4) Room. – Includes, but is not limited to, a bedroom, a rest room, a bathroom, a
25 shower, a dressing room, a dressing stall, a cubicle, or other similar area
26 designed to provide privacy.
27 (4)(5) Under circumstances in which that individual has a reasonable expectation of
28 privacy. – Means either of the following:
29 a. Circumstances in which a reasonable person would believe that he or
30 she could disrobe in privacy, without being concerned that a
31 photographic image of a private area of the individual was being
32 created.
33 b. Circumstances in which a reasonable person would believe that a
34 private area of the individual would not be visible to the public,
35 regardless of whether that person is in a public or private place.
