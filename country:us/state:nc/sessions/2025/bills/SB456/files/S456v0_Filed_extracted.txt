1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO IMPLEMENT THE HEALTHY START NC PROGRAM TO REDUCE
3 MATERNAL MORTALITY AND CHILDHOOD POVERTY AND TO OFFSET COSTS
4 BY MODIFYING THE CORPORATE INCOME TAX RATE SO AS TO KEEP THE
5 EFFECTIVE TAX RATE FOR CORPORATE TAXPAYERS MORE CLOSELY
6 ALIGNED WITH THAT WHICH IS REQUIRED OF INDIVIDUAL TAXPAYERS OF
7 THE STATE.
8 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
9 SECTION 1.(a) Program. – Of the funds received by the State and appropriated to
10 the Department of Health and Human Services from the Temporary Assistance for Needy
11 Families (TANF) Block Grant, the Department shall allocate to the Division of Social Services
12 (Division) the recurring sum of one hundred sixty-one million six hundred thousand dollars
13 ($161,600,000) for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to implement the Healthy Start
14 NC Program (Program). In addition, there is appropriated from the General Fund to the
15 Department the recurring sum of one hundred forty-six million three hundred thousand dollars
16 ($146,300,000) for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to be allocated to the Division of
17 Social Services for implementation of the Program. The purpose of the Program is to provide
18 nonrecurrent, short-term benefits in the form of prenatal and infant cash allowances to meet
19 pre- and post-birth needs of expecting mothers and babies.
20 SECTION 1.(b) Administration. – The Division shall develop detailed guidelines to
21 administer, and accomplish the purposes of, the Program. The Division, in administering the
22 Program, shall do all of the following:
23 (1) Study, and make any amendments necessary to, the TANF State plan,
24 including what was approved in Section 9J.1 of S.L. 2023-134, to implement
25 the Program. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the
26 Division may modify the State plan to accomplish the purposes of this act. To
27 the extent the Division identifies State laws modified pursuant to the authority
28 provided in this subsection, the Division shall make recommendations to the
29 General Assembly for legislative changes needed.
30 (2) Define childbirth as a specific crisis situation and episode of need and take
31 other steps required to use TANF funds to provide nonrecurrent, short-term
32 benefits to families who give birth to a child. In so doing, the Division shall
33 follow the guidance from the United States Department of Health and Human
34 Services, Administration of Children and Families Office of Family
35 Assistance, for funding the Program in conformity with this section.

1 (3) Adopt policies, including a compliant means-based test at the time of birth for
2 Program recipients (such as Medicaid or a percentage of the federal poverty
3 level), to identify needy families that can be supported with TANF Block
4 Grant dollars in a manner that will neither trigger time limits or work
5 requirements nor adversely affect entitlement to other State or federal aid. The
6 Division shall collect and review information relevant to the means-based
7 eligibility determination required by this section.
8 (4) Identify and partner with a nonprofit organization to administer the Program.
9 As a condition of partnering with the nonprofit organization, the Division shall
10 require that the organization (i) deliver programmatic funding to families as a
11 private gift below applicable federal maximums in such a way as to make it
12 non-taxable and protect, to the maximum extent possible, the receipt of other
13 social benefits and (ii) engage in fundraising efforts to spur the receipt of funds
14 donated through private philanthropy to expand the reach of the Program so
15 as to provide programmatic funding to families that do not qualify under
16 subdivision (3) of this subsection.
17 (5) Identify and report annually to the General Assembly on the number of
18 families receiving aid, broken down by those supported using TANF funds
19 and those supported using private funds, the total amount of private funds
20 raised through fundraising efforts, and additional needs or resources that
21 would assist the Division in maximizing private fundraising efforts.
22 SECTION 1.(c) Use. – Awards of programmatic funding shall be subject to all of
23 the following:
24 (1) A one-time award to an expecting mother during pregnancy in the amount of
25 one thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500) for needs determined by the
26 mother, including food, prenatal care, rent, and other needs.
27 (2) A monthly award for the maximum amount of time permitted under federal
28 law to qualify as nonrecurrent, short-term benefits, not to exceed the first year
29 after giving birth to the mother in the amount of five hundred dollars ($500.00)
30 per month for needs of the baby determined by the mother, including formula,
31 diapers, child care, and other needs.
32 (3) Awards utilizing TANF funds shall use, and be limited by, the means-based
33 test provided in subdivision (b)(3) of this section, other applicable federal
34 restrictions, and this section to ensure the funds are used solely to benefit
35 eligible families.
36 (4) Awards utilizing funds raised by the nonprofit organization with which the
37 Division partners pursuant to subdivision (b)(4) of this section shall be used,
38 first, to augment awards for families that qualify for TANF funds under
39 subdivision (3) of this subsection to the maximum amount permitted by this
40 section and, thereafter, to beneficially impact as many other families as
41 possible; provided that, such awards shall not exceed the amounts and
42 purposes permitted in subdivisions (1) and (2) of this subsection.
