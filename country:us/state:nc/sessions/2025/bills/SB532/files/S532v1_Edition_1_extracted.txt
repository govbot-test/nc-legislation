9 "§ 131E-214.20. Definitions.
10 The following definitions apply in this Article:
11 (1) Acquiring entity. – The person or entity that gains ownership or control of a
12 hospital entity as a result of a transaction subject to review under this Article.
13 (2) Attorney General. – The Attorney General or any employee of the Department
14 of Justice designated by the Attorney General.
15 (3) Hospital entity. – Any corporation or governmental entity licensed as a
16 hospital under Article 5 of Chapter 131E of the General Statutes, including
17 any entity affiliated with such corporation or governmental entity through
18 ownership, governance, or membership, such as a holding company or
19 subsidiary.
20 (4) Person. – Any individual, partnership, trust, estate, corporation, association,
21 joint venture, joint stock company, or other organization.
22 (5) State Auditor. – The State Auditor or any employee of the Office of the State
23 Auditor designated by the State Auditor.
24 (6) State Treasurer. – The State Treasurer or any employee of the Office of the
25 State Treasurer designated by the State Treasurer.
26 (7) Transaction. – Includes all of the following, if the value of the assets, control,
27 or governance interest equals or exceeds five million dollars ($5,000,000):
28 a. The sale, transfer, lease, exchange, optioning, conveyance, or other
29 disposition of no less than fifty percent (50%) of the assets or
30 operations of any hospital entity to any person or entity other than
31 another hospital entity that controls, is controlled by, or is under
32 common control with such hospital entity.
33 b. The transfer of control or governance of a hospital entity to a person
34 or entity other than another hospital entity that controls, is controlled
35 by, or is under common control with such hospital entity.

22 "§ 131E-214.32. Required considerations by the State Auditor, Attorney General, and State
23 Treasurer.
24 (a) The State Auditor, Attorney General, and State Treasurer shall consider all of the
25 following criteria in making a decision about any transaction subject to the provisions of this
26 Article:
27 (1) Whether the fair market value of any asset to be transferred from the hospital
28 entity to the acquiring entity has been manipulated by the actions of the parties
29 in a manner that causes the fair market value of the asset to decrease.
30 (2) Whether healthcare providers will be offered the opportunity to invest or own
31 an interest in the acquiring entity or a related party, and whether procedures
32 or safeguards are in place to avoid healthcare providers' conflicts of interest
33 with respect to patient referrals.
34 (3) Whether the terms of any management or services contract negotiated in
35 conjunction with the proposed transaction are reasonable.
36 (4) Whether the proposed transaction may have a significant effect on the cost,
37 availability, accessibility, or quality of healthcare services for any affected
38 community. In making this determination, the State Auditor, Attorney
39 General, and State Treasurer shall consider all of the following:
40 a. Whether sufficient safeguards are included to ensure that the affected
41 community will have continued access to affordable healthcare
42 services.
43 b. Whether the proposed transaction creates or has the likelihood of
44 creating an adverse effect on the cost, availability, accessibility, or
45 quality of healthcare services within the affected community.
46 c. Whether the acquiring entity has made a commitment to provide (i)
47 free care to individuals whose income is three hundred percent (300%)
48 or less of the federal poverty guidelines, (ii) free or discounted
49 healthcare to other individuals who are disadvantaged, uninsured, or
50 underinsured, and (iii) other benefits to the affected community to
51 promote improved healthcare. In determining whether the level of

14 "§ 131E-214.42. Violations; penalties; preservation of statutory and common law authority
15 of the State Auditor, Attorney General, and State Treasurer.
16 (a) Any transactions entered into in violation of this Article shall be null and void.
17 (b) Each member of the governing boards and each chief financial officer of the parties
18 to a transaction entered into in violation of this Article are subject to a civil penalty of up to fifty
19 thousand dollars ($50,000) each per transaction, unless the violation was made in wanton
20 disregard of the law, in which case the civil penalty may be up to one million dollars ($1,000,000)
21 each per transaction. The State Auditor, Attorney General, and State Treasurer shall institute
22 proceedings to impose a civil penalty authorized by this section in a court of competent
23 jurisdiction in Wake County, and the court shall determine the amount of the civil penalty to be
24 imposed under this section. The clear proceeds of civil penalties provided for in this subsection
25 shall be remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with G.S. 115C-457.2.
26 (c) The Department of Health and Human Services shall not issue a new or renewal
27 license to operate a hospital under Article 5 of Chapter 131E of the General Statutes, or any
28 applicable rules, on behalf of any hospital that is a party to a transaction entered into in violation
29 of the notice, public hearing, and review requirements of this Article.
30 (d) Nothing in this Article shall be construed to limit the statutory or common law
31 authority of the State Auditor, Attorney General, or State Treasurer to protect charitable trusts
32 and assets located in this State. The penalties and remedies set forth in this Article are in addition
33 to, and not a replacement for, any other civil or criminal actions the State Auditor, Attorney
