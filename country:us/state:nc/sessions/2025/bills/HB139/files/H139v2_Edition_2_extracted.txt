6 "§ 7B-101. Definitions.
7 As used in this Subchapter, unless the context clearly requires otherwise, the following words
8 have the listed meanings:
9 …
10 (19a) Safely surrendered infant. – An infant reasonably believed to be not more than
11 30 days of age and without signs of abuse or neglect who is voluntarily
12 delivered to an individual or placed in a newborn safety device in accordance
13 with Article 5A of this Subchapter by the infant's parent who does not express
14 an intent to return for the infant. In determining whether there are signs of
15 neglect, the act of surrendering the infant, in and of itself, does not constitute
16 neglect.
17 …."
18 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 7B-521 reads as rewritten:
19 "§ 7B-521. Persons to whom infant may be surrendered.
20 (a) The following individuals shall, without a court order, take into temporary custody
21 an infant reasonably believed to be not more than 30 days of age that is voluntarily delivered to
22 the individual by the infant's parent who does not express an intent to return for the infant:
23 (1) A health care provider, as defined under G.S. 90-21.11, who is on duty or at a
24 hospital or at a local or district health department or at a nonprofit community
25 health center.
26 (2) A first responder who is on duty, including a law enforcement officer, a
27 certified emergency medical services worker, or a firefighter.
28 (3) A social services worker who is on duty or at a local department of social
29 services.
30 (b) An employee of a qualified establishment with an installed newborn safety device as
31 provided for in G.S. 7B-521A shall, without a court order, take into temporary custody an infant
32 reasonably believed to be not more than 30 days of age that is voluntarily placed in a newborn
33 safety device by the infant's parent who does not express an intent to return for the infant."

38 SECTION 1.(e) G.S. 7B-527 reads as rewritten:
39 "§ 7B-527. Rights of surrendering parent.
40 …
41 (c) Immunity. – A parent surrendering an infant pursuant to this Article is immune from
42 any civil liability or criminal prosecution in accordance with G.S. 14-322.3 as long as the
43 surrendering parent was acting in good faith. The immunity established by this section does not
44 extend to gross negligence, wanton conduct, or intentional wrongdoing that would otherwise be
45 actionable. The act of safely surrendering an infant under this Article does not create an
46 affirmative defense to any prosecution arising from any other acts taken by the surrendering
47 parent."
