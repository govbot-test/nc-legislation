19 PART II. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE
20 SECTION 2.1. The General Assembly makes the following findings of fact:
21 (1) North Carolina is experiencing a severe opioid crisis, with fentanyl and
22 synthetic opioids contributing to record-high overdose deaths.
23 (2) The State lacks comprehensive measures to address substance use disorder
24 prevention, rehabilitation, and treatment access, particularly in rural and
25 underserved communities.
26 (3) The Controlled Substances Reporting System (CSRS) has been effective in
27 tracking prescription drug abuse, but it must be expanded to monitor
28 additional substances.
29 (4) Medical cannabis has been shown to provide relief for chronic pain, Post
30 Traumatic Stress Disorder, and seizure disorders. North Carolina should
31 implement a tightly regulated Medical Cannabis Pilot Program.
32 (5) Law enforcement lacks sufficient resources and training to handle synthetic
33 opioids safely and to combat cartel-driven drug trafficking.
34 (6) A public education campaign is needed to raise awareness about fentanyl
35 risks, treatment options, and drug prevention.

48 (5) Individual. – As defined in G.S. 105-163.1.

23 exceed fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) per taxable year.
24 (e) Substantiation. – A taxpayer allowed a credit under this section must maintain and
25 make available for inspection any information or records required by the Secretary of Revenue.

28 eligible employee has successfully completed substance use disorder counseling; provided,
29 however, that an eligible employee may disclose, but shall not be required to disclose, any
30 protected health information protected by State or federal law."
