1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ALLOW THE USE OF STATE-OWNED PROPERTY FOR THE USE OF
3 CHILDCARE FOR STATE EMPLOYEES, REQUIRE RENOVATIONS OR NEW

8 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
9
10 PART I. USE OF STATE-OWNED PROPERTY FOR ONSITE CHILDCARE CENTERS
11 FOR STATE EMPLOYEES.
12 SECTION 1.(a) The Department of Administration shall report to the Joint
13 Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services and the Joint Legislative
14 Commission on Governmental Operations, no later than March 31, 2026, on the feasibility and
15 advisability of using obsolete or underutilized State-owned buildings (available buildings) to
16 house childcare facilities giving childcare priority to State employees. The report shall include
17 the following:
18 (1) Location of each available building.
19 (2) Estimated costs for upfitting each property to meet daycare licensing
20 standards.
21 (3) Estimated costs for asbestos and lead remediation.
22 (4) Barriers to the available buildings' use as a childcare facility.
23 SECTION 1.(b) Any project undertaken by the State to build or renovate property
24 having a budget greater than five million dollars ($5,000,000) shall include a child care center or
25 adult day care center if more than 250 people would work in the building. This requirement
26 includes current projects which have not broken ground by July 1, 2025, unless one or both of
27 the following exceptions apply:
28 (1) Inclusion of either type of center would delay the project by six months or
29 more.
30 (2) Inclusion of either type of center would increase the project cost by 10% or
31 more.
32
33 PART II. CHILDCARE FOR STATE EMPLOYEES
34 SECTION 2.(a) The Division of Child Development and Early Education shall
35 establish a pilot program for onsite childcare for State employees as follows:

1 (1) The Division of Child Development and Early Education shall contract with
2 a private commercial childcare provider to establish three childcare centers
3 for State employees use to be established on State-owned property that is
4 unused or underutilized.
5 (2) Priority in contracting shall be given to commercial childcare providers who
6 currently operate five or fewer facilities and provide high quality childcare.
7 (3) The Division of Child Development and Early Education must include the
8 following terms in the contract with the commercial childcare providers:
9 a. The childcare facility must operate an apprenticeship program in
10 conjunction with a public or private university or community college
11 who operates an early childcare education program.
12 b. The childcare center must comply with the requirements established
13 by the Division of Child Development and Early Education for
14 apprenticeship program.
15 c. The childcare center must maintain the operation of the apprenticeship
16 program for so long as the childcare center is housed in state-owned
17 property and the university or community college is willing to
18 continue the partnership.
19 d. The State will provide the upfit cost of the space to meet the licensure
20 requirements at reasonable levels that are customary at the operators'
21 other facilities and those similarly situated and provide use of the space
22 rent free, notwithstanding the provisions of G.S. 146-29.1.
23 e. The lease terms for state-owned property must be approved as required
24 by G.S. 146-27.
25 SECTION 2.(b) The Division of Child Development and Early Education shall
26 create the requirements for the apprenticeship program and pair the childcare centers with a
27 university or community college early education program utilizing parameters similar to the
28 Durham Childcare Apprenticeship Program.
29 SECTION 2.(c) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
30 Health and Human Resources the sum of five million dollars ($5,000,000) for the 2025-2026
31 fiscal year for the Division of Child Development and Early Education's use in establishing the
32 childcare centers as required by Section 2(a) of this act.
33 SECTION 2.(d) If any expenses are incurred by the State for asbestos or lead
34 remediation in establishing the childcare centers required by Section 2.(a) of this act, the State
35 shall be eligible for reimbursement from the Asbestos and Lead Remediation Fund subject to the
36 rules of the fund for up to five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000).
37 SECTION 2.(e) By April 1, 2027, the Division of Child Development and Early
38 Education shall report on and make recommendations to the Joint Legislative Oversight
39 Committee on Health and Human Services, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on
40 General Government, and the Fiscal Research Division the status of the implementation of the
41 pilot program including successes, concerns, problems encountered, enrollment, and
42 expenditures.
43 SECTION 2.(f) Sections 2(c) and 2(d) of this act are effective July 1, 2025.
44
45 PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
46 SECTION 3. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
47 law.
