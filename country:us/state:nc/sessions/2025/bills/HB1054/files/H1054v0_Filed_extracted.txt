9 (2) If an employee is separated and rehired within 90 days by the same State
10 agency, previously accrued unused sick leave is reinstated.
11 (3) Permissible uses of sick leave shall include the following:
12 a. The employee's own physical or mental illness, injury, medical
13 condition, preventive care, or routine medical appointment; and
14 temporary disability due to childbirth or recovery therefrom.
15 b. Death of an immediate family member.
16 c. Donations to a member of the immediate family who is an approved
17 voluntary shared leave recipient.
18 d. Adoption of a child limited to a maximum of 30 days for each parent.
19 e. Absences related to the employee being the victim of domestic
20 violence, sexual assault, or stalking, taken for the purpose of medical
21 care, counseling, relocation, or legal proceedings.
22 f. The same uses as sub-subdivision a. of this subdivision for an
23 immediate family member.
24 (4) For the purposes of this section, an "immediate family member" means (i) a
25 spouse, domestic or civil union partner, (ii) a parent, including a biological,
26 adoptive, or stepparent; a parent-in-law; or a person who stood in loco parentis
27 to the employee, (iii) a child, including a biological, adoptive, foster, or
28 stepchild; a child-in-law; a legal ward; or a person for whom the employee
29 stands in loco parentis, (iv) a brother or sister, including adoptive, step, half,
30 and in-law relationships, (v) a grandparent, great-grandparent, grandchild, or
31 great-grandchild, including step and in-law relationships thereto, and (vi) any
32 dependent living in the employee's household.
33 (5) If an employee or the employee's spouse, parent, or child has a serious illness
34 that qualifies under the federal Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA), the
35 FMLA provisions apply. If the illness does not qualify under FMLA, the
36 provisions of the State Human Resources Act (SHRA) apply.
37 (6) A State agency may require documentation when an employee absence
38 exceeds three consecutive working days, but the agency shall not require
39 disclosure of details beyond what is reasonably necessary to verify the
40 permissible purpose of the leave. Evidence to support leave for
41 adoption-related purposes may be required. Sick leave shall be taken and
42 charged in units appropriate and consistent with agency operational needs.
43 Only scheduled work time shall be charged when calculating the amount of
44 leave taken. When exhausting leave, an employee continues to accumulate
45 leave, is entitled to holidays, and remains eligible for salary increases.
46 (7) Nothing in this section shall be construed to affect existing rules and policies
47 concerning the transferability of sick leave or how sick leave is handled upon
48 separation and reinstatement unless specifically set forth in this section.
49 SECTION 2.21.(b) Nothing in this section shall be construed to modify the treatment
50 of sick leave for purposes of retirement credit under Chapter 135 of the General Statutes.
