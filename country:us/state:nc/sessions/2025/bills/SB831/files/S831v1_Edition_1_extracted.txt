1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT ALLOWING FIRST RESPONDERS WHO ARE MEMBERS OF THE TEACHERS'
3 AND STATE EMPLOYEES' RETIREMENT SYSTEM OR THE LOCAL
4 GOVERNMENTAL EMPLOYEES' RETIREMENT SYSTEM TO RETIRE WITH
5 UNREDUCED BENEFITS AFTER ACHIEVING TWENTY-FIVE YEARS OF
6 CREDITABLE SERVICE AND MAKING CONFORMING CHANGES.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 135-1 reads as rewritten:
9 "§ 135-1. Definitions.
10 The following words and phrases as used in this Chapter, unless a different meaning is plainly
11 required by the context, shall have the following meanings:
12 …
13 (9a) "Emergency medical services personnel" or "EMS personnel" shall mean all
14 full-time employees of any State department, agency, or institution who meet
15 the definition of emergency medical services personnel under G.S. 131E-155.
16 This includes emergency medical dispatchers and the Director of EMS.
17 …
18 (11b) "Firefighter" shall mean all full-time employees of any State department,
19 agency, or institution who are actively serving in a position with assigned
20 primary duties and responsibilities for the prevention, detection, and
21 suppression of fire. This includes fire dispatchers, fire inspectors, and fire
22 marshals.
23 (11b)(11c) "Fraud investigation" means an independent review or examination by
24 Retirement Systems Division staff or authorized representatives who are
25 assisting the Retirement Systems Division staff of records, activities, actions,
26 or decisions by employers or other affiliated or associated entities having an
27 impact on the Retirement System. The purpose of a fraud investigation is to
28 help detect and prevent fraud and to ensure full accountability in the use of
29 pension funds.
30 (11c)(11d) "Law Enforcement Officer" means a full-time paid employee of an
31 employer who is actively serving in a position with assigned primary duties
32 and responsibilities for prevention and detection of crime or the general
33 enforcement of the criminal laws of the State of North Carolina or serving
34 civil processes, and who possesses the power of arrest by virtue of an oath
35 administered under the authority of the State. "Law Enforcement Officer" also
36 means a probation/parole officer as defined in this section with respect to any

1 service rendered on or after July 1, 2017. This includes police dispatchers,
2 police investigators, sheriff's deputies, bailiffs, corrections officers, detention
3 officers, and jailers.
4 …."
5 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 135-5(a) is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
6 "(4b) Any member who is a law enforcement officer, firefighter, or emergency
7 medical services personnel and has completed 25 years of creditable service
8 may retire upon electronic submission or written application to the Board of
9 Trustees setting forth at what time, as of the first day of a calendar month, not
10 less than one day nor more than 120 days subsequent to the execution and
11 filing thereof, the member desires to be retired; provided, however, that any
12 member who has met the 25 years of creditable service required by this
13 subdivision but does not retire and later becomes employed other than as the
14 member was employed while completing 25 years of creditable service shall
15 continue to have the right to commence retirement."
16 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 135-5(b21) reads as rewritten:
17 "(b21) Service Retirement Allowance of Non-First Responder Members Retiring on or After
18 July 1, 2019; and First Responder Members Retiring on or After July 1, 2019. July 1, 2019, but
19 Before January 1, 2027. – Upon retirement from service on or after July 1, 2019, in accordance
20 with subsection (a) or (a1) of this section, a member shall receive the following service retirement
21 allowance:
22 …."
23 SECTION 1.(d) G.S. 135-5 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
24 "(b22) Service Retirement Allowance of First Responders Retiring on or After January 1,
25 2027. – Upon retirement from service on or after January 1, 2027, in accordance with subsection
26 (a) or (a1) of this section and notwithstanding any other provision of this section to the contrary,
27 a member who is a law enforcement officer, eligible former law enforcement officer, firefighter,
28 eligible former firefighter, emergency medical services personnel, or eligible former medical
29 services personnel shall receive a service retirement allowance computed as follows:
30 (1) If the member's service retirement date occurs on or after the member's 55th
31 birthday and completion of five years of creditable service as a law
32 enforcement officer, as a firefighter, or as EMS personnel, or after the
33 completion of 25 years of creditable service, the allowance shall be equal to
34 one and eighty-two hundredths percent (1.82%) of the member's average final
35 compensation multiplied by the number of years of the member's creditable
36 service.
37 (2) If the member's service retirement date occurs on or after the member's 50th
38 birthday and before the member's 55th birthday with 15 or more years of
39 creditable service as a law enforcement officer, firefighter, or EMS personnel
40 and prior to the completion of 25 years of creditable service, the retirement
41 allowance shall be equal to the greater of the following amounts:
42 a. The service retirement allowance payable under subdivision (1) of this
43 subsection reduced by one-third of one percent (1/3 of 1%) thereof for
44 each month by which the retirement date precedes the first day of the
45 month coincident with or next following the month the member would
46 have attained age 55.
47 b. The service retirement allowance as computed under subdivision (1)
48 of this subsection reduced by five percent (5%) times the difference
49 between 25 years and the amount of creditable service at retirement."
