Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 3/25/25
Short Title: Expedited Removal of Unauthorized Persons. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
February 12, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CREATE AN ALTERNATIVE REMEDY FOR THE EXPEDITED REMOVAL
3 OF UNAUTHORIZED PERSONS FROM PRIVATE PROPERTY BY A LAW
4 ENFORCEMENT AGENCY.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
7 to read:
8 "Article 22D.
9 "Expedited Removal of Unauthorized Persons from Residential Property.
10 "§ 14-159.50. Definitions.
11 The following definitions apply to this Article:
12 (1) Authorized representative. – A real estate broker or other person who has
13 written legal authority to act on behalf of a property owner.
14 (2) Contract for deed. – As defined in G.S. 47H-1.
15 (3) Law enforcement agency. – A county sheriff's office, a county police
16 department, or a municipal police department.
17 (4) Real estate broker. – As defined in G.S. 93A-2(a).
18 (5) Residential property. – As defined in G.S. 42A-4.
19 (6) Tenant. – As defined in G.S. 42-59.
20 (7) Unauthorized person. – A person or persons occupying residential property
21 who has no legal claim to the property, is not entitled to occupy it under a
22 valid rental agreement or contract for deed, has not paid any rent or other form
23 of payment to the property owner or an authorized representative of the
24 property owner in connection with the occupancy of the property, and is not
25 otherwise authorized to occupy the property. This term does not include a
26 tenant who holds over after the lease term has expired under G.S. 42-26.
27 "§ 14-159.51. Requirements for removal of unauthorized persons.
28 A property owner or an authorized representative of the property owner may request from a
29 law enforcement agency with jurisdiction over where the residential property is located the
30 removal of an unauthorized person unlawfully occupying the residential property pursuant to this
31 Article if all of the following conditions are met:
32 (1) The requesting party is the property owner or the authorized representative of
33 the property owner.
34 (2) The property that is being unlawfully occupied is residential property or
35 property used in connection with or appurtenant to residential property.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (3) An unauthorized person has entered the property after the property owner
2 acquired the property and is remaining or residing unlawfully on the
3 residential property of the property owner.
4 (4) The property was not offered or intended as an accommodation for the general
5 public at the time the unauthorized person entered.
6 (5) The property owner or the authorized representative of the property owner has
7 directed the unauthorized person to leave the residential property.
8 (6) The unauthorized person is not a tenant of the property being unlawfully
9 occupied.
10 (7) The unauthorized person is not an owner of the property being unlawfully
11 occupied.
12 (8) There is no pending litigation between the property owner and the
13 unauthorized person related to the residential property.
14 (9) No other valid rental agreement or contract for deed has been entered into or
15 formed by the property owner or a former property owner and the
16 unauthorized person permitting the unauthorized person to occupy the
17 residential property.
18 (10) No rent or other form of payment has ever been demanded of or paid by the
19 unauthorized person to the property owner or to an authorized representative
20 of the property owner in connection with the occupancy of the residential
21 property.
22 "§ 14-159.52. Expedited removal affidavit.
23 (a) To request the immediate removal of an unauthorized person from residential
24 property, the property owner or an authorized representative of the property owner must appear
25 before a magistrate in the county where the property is located and complete a sworn affidavit
26 on a form created pursuant to subsection (c) of this section. The magistrate shall sign the affidavit
27 verifying that the property owner or the authorized representative of the property owner appeared
28 before the magistrate and swore under oath or affirmation to the information contained therein.
29 The property owner or the authorized representative shall pay to the magistrate a fee of
30 twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for completion of the sworn affidavit.
31 (b) The affidavit shall allege that all of the conditions and requirements for removal of
32 an unauthorized person imposed by G.S. 14-159.51 are met.
33 (c) The Administrative Office of the Courts, in consultation with the North Carolina
34 Sheriffs' Association and the North Carolina Association of Chiefs of Police, shall develop a
35 form for the affidavit required by this section. In addition to the required allegations set forth in
36 subsection (b) of this section, the form shall require the property owner to provide the property
37 address and may include space for the property owner to provide information that may assist law
38 enforcement in identifying the unauthorized person. The form shall also include clear language
39 notifying the affiant that providing false information or statements on the affidavit is perjury
40 pursuant to G.S. 14-209 and punishable as a Class F felony. The form shall further notify the
41 affiant that any person removed from the residential property as a result of the affidavit may have
42 a cause of action against the affiant for any false information or statements provided on the
43 affidavit or wrongfully using the procedures provided in this Article.
44 "§ 14-159.53. Removal of unauthorized persons.
45 Upon completion of the affidavit before the magistrate, the property owner or authorized
46 representative of the property owner shall provide the original affidavit to the law enforcement
47 agency with jurisdiction over where the residential property is located. Within 24 hours after
48 receipt of the affidavit, the law enforcement agency shall remove the unauthorized person from
49 the residential property. If appropriate, the law enforcement agency may arrest any person found
50 in the property for trespass, outstanding warrants, or any other legal cause. The property owner
51 or the authorized representative of the property owner may request that the law enforcement

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 agency stand by to keep the peace while the property owner or authorized representative of the
2 property owner changes the locks and removes the personal property of the unauthorized person
3 from the premises to or near the property line.
4 "§ 14-159.54. Immunity from liability.
5 No law enforcement agency, law enforcement officer, or magistrate may be held liable to an
6 unauthorized person or any other person or entity for compliance with or actions taken in
7 furtherance of the provisions of this Article, including for the loss or destruction of or damage to
8 personal or real property, provided that the acts or omissions are made in good faith and do not
9 constitute gross negligence, willful or wanton misconduct, or intentional wrongdoing. The
10 property owner or the authorized representative of the property owner is not liable to an
11 unauthorized person or any other person or entity for the loss or destruction of or damage to any
12 personal property that arises out of or in any way relates to the removal of an unauthorized person
13 pursuant to this Article unless the removal was wrongful.
14 "§ 14-159.55. Remedy for wrongful removal.
15 (a) A person may bring a civil cause of action against the affiant for wrongful removal
16 under this Article. A person harmed by a wrongful removal under this Article may be entitled to
17 recover possession of the property and may recover from the affiant actual costs and damages
18 incurred, statutory damages equal to triple the fair market rent of the residence, a civil penalty of
19 not less than four hundred dollars ($400.00) nor more than four thousand dollars ($4,000) as
20 determined by a court, court costs, and reasonable attorneys' fees.
21 (b) This Article does not limit the rights of a property owner or limit the authority of a
22 law enforcement officer to arrest an unauthorized person for trespassing, vandalism, theft, or
23 other crimes."
24 SECTION 2. The Administrative Office of the Courts shall develop and make
25 available the affidavit form required by G.S. 14-159.52, as enacted in Section 1 of this act, no
26 later than September 30, 2025.
27 SECTION 3. Section 1 of this act becomes effective October 1, 2025. The remainder
28 of this act is effective when it becomes law.
