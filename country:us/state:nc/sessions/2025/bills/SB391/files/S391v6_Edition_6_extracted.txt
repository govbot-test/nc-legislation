6 REGULATION OF DRIVERS EDUCATION OFFERED BY COMMERCIAL DRIVER
7 TRAINING SCHOOLS
8 SECTION 1. G.S. 20-322 reads as rewritten:
9 "§ 20-322. Licenses for schools necessary; regulations as to requirements.
10 (a) No commercial driver training school shall be established nor any such existing
11 school be continued on or after July 1, 1965, unless such school applies for and obtains from the
12 Commissioner a license in the manner and form prescribed by the Commissioner.
13 (b) Regulations adopted by the Commissioner shall state the requirements for a school
14 license, including requirements concerning location, equipment, courses of instruction,
15 instructors, financial statements, schedule of fees and charges, character and reputation of the
16 operators, insurance, bond or other security in such sum and with such provisions as the
17 Commissioner deems necessary to protect adequately the interests of the public, and such other
18 matters as the Commissioner may prescribe. A driver education course offered to prepare an
19 individual for a limited learner's permit or another provisional license must meet the requirements
20 set in G.S. 115C-215 for the program of driver education offered in the public schools.
21 (c) Regulations adopted by the Commissioner for the course of instruction to be offered
22 by commercial driver training schools to prepare an individual for a limited learner's permit or
23 another provisional license must include, but are not limited to, the curriculum requirements in
24 G.S. 115C-215(b).
25 (d) In addition to regulations adopted by the Commissioner under subsection (c) of this
26 section, commercial driver training schools providing courses to prepare an individual for a
27 limited learner's permit or another provisional license through the public schools must meet all
28 requirements set in G.S. 115C-215 for the program of driver education offered in the public
29 schools."

14 AUTHORIZE USE OF ELECTRONIC SPEED-MEASURING SYSTEMS TO DETECT
15 SPEED LIMIT VIOLATIONS IN SCHOOL ZONES
16 SECTION 13.(a) Chapter 160A of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
17 section to read:
18 "§ 160A-300.4. Use of electronic speed-measuring systems to enforce speed limits in school
19 zones.
20 (a) An electronic speed-measuring system is a mobile or fixed device consisting of an
21 automated traffic camera and sensor capable of measuring speed and producing one or more
22 digital photographs of a motor vehicle violating a posted speed limit.
23 (b) An electronic speed-measuring system shall be approved, calibrated, and tested for
24 accuracy in accordance with G.S. 8-50.4.
25 (c) Any electronic speed-measuring system installed or in use on a street or highway shall
26 be identified by appropriate advance warning signs conspicuously posted not more than 1,000
27 feet from the location of an electronic speed-measuring system. Signs shall comply with
28 G.S. 136-30. An electronic speed-measuring system installed on Department of Transportation
29 right-of-way must be approved by the Department. No approval for use of Department
30 right-of-way under this subsection shall abrogate the Department's ownership and control of the
31 right-of-way. The Department may adopt policies and rules necessary to implement this
32 subsection.
33 (d) A municipality may adopt ordinances for the civil enforcement of G.S. 20-141.1 by
34 means of an electronic speed-measuring system. Notwithstanding the provisions of
35 G.S. 20-141.1 and G.S. 20-176, in the event that a municipality adopts an ordinance pursuant to
36 this section, a violation of G.S. 20-141.1 detected by an electronic speed-measuring system shall
37 not be an infraction if a citation is issued in accordance with this subsection. An ordinance
38 authorized by this subsection shall provide that:
39 (1) The owner of a vehicle shall be responsible for a violation unless the owner
40 can furnish evidence that the vehicle was, at the time of the violation, in the
41 care, custody, or control of another person. Notification of the violation shall
42 be given in the form of a citation issued to the owner of the vehicle no more
43 than 60 days after the date of the violation. The owner of the vehicle shall not
44 be responsible for the violation if the owner of the vehicle furnishes, within
45 30 days of notification of the violation, to the officials or agents of the
46 municipality that issued the citation either of the following:
47 a. An affidavit stating the name and address of the person or company
48 who leased, rented, or otherwise had the care, custody, or control of
49 the vehicle at the time of the violation. If the owner provides an
50 affidavit under this sub-subdivision, the identified person or company
Page 6 Senate Bill 391-Sixth Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 may be issued a citation complying with the requirements of
2 subdivision (3) of this subsection.
3 b. An affidavit stating that the vehicle involved was, at the time of the
4 violation, stolen or in the care, custody, or control of some person who
5 did not have permission of the owner to use the vehicle.
6 (2) A violation detected by an electronic speed-measuring system shall be deemed
7 a noncriminal violation for which a civil penalty of two hundred fifty dollars
8 ($250.00) shall be assessed and for which no points authorized by
9 G.S. 20-16(c) or G.S. 58-36-65 shall be assigned to the owner or driver of the
10 vehicle.
11 (3) The citation shall contain all of the following:
12 a. The recorded image of the vehicle speeding.
13 b. The vehicle registration number and state of issuance.
14 c. The date, time, and location of the violation.
15 d. The recorded speed.
16 e. A copy of a certificate sworn to or affirmed by a sworn law
17 enforcement officer authorized to enforce the speed limit in the
18 applicable school zone stating that, based upon inspection of
19 photographically recorded images, the owner's vehicle was operated
20 in violation of G.S. 20-141.1.
21 f. The process for paying the civil penalty or contesting responsibility
22 for the violation.
23 (4) The citation shall be processed by officials or agents of the municipality and
24 shall be served by any method permitted for service of process pursuant to
25 G.S. 1A-1, Rule 4 of the North Carolina Rules of Civil Procedure, or by
26 first-class mail to the address of the registered owner of the vehicle. If the
27 owner fails to pay the civil penalty or to respond to the citation within 30 days
28 of receiving the citation, the owner waives the right to contest responsibility
29 for the violation and is subject to an additional penalty not to exceed fifty
30 dollars ($50.00). The municipality may establish procedures for the collection
31 of these penalties and may recover the penalties by civil action in the nature
32 of debt.
33 (5) The municipality shall provide a nonjudicial administrative hearing process to
34 review contested citations or penalties issued or assessed under this section. If
35 the decision is adverse to the person contesting the citation, the decision shall
36 contain instructions explaining the manner and the time within which the
37 decision may be appealed. A person may appeal the decision to the district
38 court in the county where the violation occurred within 30 days of notification
39 of a final decision by the municipality. Enforcement of an adverse decision
40 shall be stayed pending the outcome of a timely appeal.
41 (6) If the registered owner of a motor vehicle who receives a citation fails to pay
42 a penalty imposed under this section when due, the Division of Motor
43 Vehicles shall refuse to register the motor vehicle in accordance with
44 G.S. 20-54(14). The municipality shall coordinate and establish procedures
45 for providing appropriate notice to the Division of Motor Vehicles.
46 (e) A municipality, local board of education, and law enforcement agency may enter into
47 a local agreement pursuant to Part 1 of Article 20 of Chapter 160A of the General Statutes that
48 is necessary and proper to effectuate the purpose of this section. Any agreement entered into
49 pursuant to this subsection may include provisions on cost-sharing and reimbursement to which
50 the municipality, local board of education, or law enforcement agency freely and voluntarily
51 agree for the purposes of effectuating this section."

18 "§ 160A-300.4. Use of electronic speed-measuring systems to enforce speed limits in school
19 zones.
20 (a) An electronic speed-measuring system is a mobile or fixed device consisting of an
21 automated traffic camera and sensor capable of measuring speed and producing one or more
22 digital photographs of a motor vehicle violating a posted speed limit.
23 (b) An electronic speed-measuring system shall be approved, calibrated, and tested for
24 accuracy in accordance with G.S. 8-50.4.
25 (c) Any electronic speed-measuring system installed or in use on a street or highway shall

27 feet from the location of an electronic speed-measuring system. Signs shall comply with

29 right-of-way must be approved by the Department. No approval for use of Department
30 right-of-way under this subsection shall abrogate the Department's ownership and control of the
31 right-of-way. The Department may adopt policies and rules necessary to implement this
32 subsection.

34 means of an electronic speed-measuring system. Notwithstanding the provisions of
35 G.S. 20-141.1 and G.S. 20-176, in the event that a municipality adopts an ordinance pursuant to
36 this section, a violation of G.S. 20-141.1 detected by an electronic speed-measuring system shall
37 not be an infraction if a citation is issued in accordance with this subsection. An ordinance
38 authorized by this subsection shall provide that:
39 (1) The owner of a vehicle shall be responsible for a violation unless the owner
40 can furnish evidence that the vehicle was, at the time of the violation, in the
41 care, custody, or control of another person. Notification of the violation shall
42 be given in the form of a citation issued to the owner of the vehicle no more
43 than 60 days after the date of the violation. The owner of the vehicle shall not
44 be responsible for the violation if the owner of the vehicle furnishes, within
45 30 days of notification of the violation, to the officials or agents of the
46 municipality that issued the citation either of the following:
47 a. An affidavit stating the name and address of the person or company
48 who leased, rented, or otherwise had the care, custody, or control of
49 the vehicle at the time of the violation. If the owner provides an
50 affidavit under this sub-subdivision, the identified person or company
