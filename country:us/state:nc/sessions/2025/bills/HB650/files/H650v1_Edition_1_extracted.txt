3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Chapter 66 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
5 to read:
6 "Article 52.
7 "No Interchange Fees on Sales Tax or Gratuities.
8 "§ 66-511. Definitions.
9 In this Article, the following definitions apply:
10 (1) Acquirer bank. – A member of a payment card network that contracts with a
11 merchant for the settlement of electronic payment transactions, whether
12 directly with merchants or indirectly through a processor.
13 (2) Authorization. – The process through which a merchant requests approval for
14 an electronic payment transaction from the issuer.
15 (3) Clearance. – The process of transmitting final transaction data from a
16 merchant to an issuer for posting to the cardholder's account and the
17 calculation of fees and charges, including interchange fees, that apply to the
18 issuer and the merchant.
19 (4) Covered credit card issuer. – A credit card issuer that, together with any
20 affiliates, had consolidated worldwide banking and nonbanking assets,
21 including assets of affiliates, other than trust assets under management, of
22 more than eighty-five billion dollars ($85,000,000,000) at any point during
23 the previous calendar year.
24 (5) Credit card. – A card, plate, coupon book, or other credit device existing for
25 the purpose of obtaining money, property, labor, or services on credit.
26 (6) Debit card. – A card or other payment code or device issued or approved for
27 use through a payment card network to debit an asset account, regardless of
28 the purpose for which the account is established, whether authorization is
29 based on a signature, a personal identification number, or other means. The
30 term includes a general-use prepaid card, as defined in 15 U.S.C. § 1693l-1.
31 The term does not include paper checks.
32 (7) Electronic payment transaction. – A transaction in which a person uses a debit
33 card, a credit card, or other payment code or device issued or approved
34 through a payment card network to debit a deposit account or use a line of

33 amount of an electronic payment transaction and the tax or gratuity amount of
34 the transaction. Tax documentation may be related to a single electronic
35 payment transaction or multiple electronic payment transactions aggregated
36 over a period of time.
37 "§ 66-512. Interchange fees on taxes and gratuities prohibited.
38 (a) An issuer, a payment card network, an acquirer bank, or a processor shall not receive
39 or charge a merchant an interchange fee on the tax amount or gratuity of an electronic payment
40 transaction if the merchant informs the acquirer bank or its designee of the tax or gratuity amount
41 as part of the authorization or settlement process for the electronic payment transaction. The
42 merchant shall transmit the tax or gratuity amount data as part of the authorization or settlement
43 process to avoid being charged an interchange fee on the tax or gratuity amount of an electronic
44 payment transaction.
45 (b) A merchant that does not transmit the tax or gratuity amount data in accordance with
46 subsection (a) of this section may submit tax documentation for the electronic payment
47 transaction to the acquirer bank or its designee no later than 180 days after the date of the
48 electronic payment transaction and, within 30 days after the merchant submits the necessary tax
49 documentation, the issuer shall credit to the merchant the amount of the interchange fee charged
50 on the tax or gratuity amount of the electronic payment transaction.

1 (c) This section does not create liability for a payment card network regarding the
2 accuracy of the tax or gratuity data reported by the merchant.
3 (d) It is unlawful for an issuer, a payment card network, an acquirer bank, or a processor
4 to alter or manipulate the computation and imposition of interchange fees by increasing the rate
5 or amount of the fees applicable to or imposed upon the portion of a credit or debit card
6 transaction not attributable to taxes or gratuities to circumvent the effect of this section.
7 "§ 66-513. Penalties.
8 (a) Injunctive Relief. – The Attorney General may file suit to seek injunctive relief from
9 a covered credit card issuer or payment card network that has violated any of the prohibitions of
10 this Article.
11 (b) Civil Penalty. – The Attorney General may file suit to assess a civil penalty on an
12 issuer, payment card network, acquirer bank, or processor that has violated this Article. The
13 amount of the civil penalty shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000) per electronic payment
14 transaction conducted in violation of this Article. The clear proceeds of civil penalties provided
15 for in this section shall be remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with
16 G.S. 115C-457.2.
17 (c) Refund. – If a court finds that a person has violated this Article, the person shall refund
18 the merchant the interchange fee calculated on the tax or gratuity amount of the electronic
19 payment transaction.
20 (d) Data Use. – An entity, other than the merchant, involved in facilitating or processing
21 an electronic payment transaction, including an issuer, payment card network, acquirer bank, or
22 processor, shall not distribute, exchange, transfer, disseminate, or use the electronic payment
23 transaction data except to facilitate or process the electronic payment transaction; to monitor for,
24 detect, or prevent fraud; to support loyalty, rewards, or promotional offerings; to tailor products
25 and services to serve customer needs; or as required by law. A violation of this subsection
26 constitutes a violation of G.S. 75-1.1."
27 SECTION 2. If a provision of this act or its application to a person or circumstance
28 is held invalid, the invalidity does not affect another provision or application that can be given
29 effect without the invalid provision.
30 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to transactions
31 made on or after that date.
