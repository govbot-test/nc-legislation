Section 1:
H.B. 650
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 1, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: No Interchange Fees on Sales Tax or Tips. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Howard.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROHIBIT INTERCHANGE FEES ON SALES TAX OR GRATUITIES.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Chapter 66 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
5 to read:
6 "Article 52.
7 "No Interchange Fees on Sales Tax or Gratuities.
8 "§ 66-511. Definitions.
9 In this Article, the following definitions apply:
10 (1) Acquirer bank. – A member of a payment card network that contracts with a
11 merchant for the settlement of electronic payment transactions, whether
12 directly with merchants or indirectly through a processor.
13 (2) Authorization. – The process through which a merchant requests approval for
14 an electronic payment transaction from the issuer.
15 (3) Clearance. – The process of transmitting final transaction data from a
16 merchant to an issuer for posting to the cardholder's account and the
17 calculation of fees and charges, including interchange fees, that apply to the
18 issuer and the merchant.
19 (4) Covered credit card issuer. – A credit card issuer that, together with any
20 affiliates, had consolidated worldwide banking and nonbanking assets,
21 including assets of affiliates, other than trust assets under management, of
22 more than eighty-five billion dollars ($85,000,000,000) at any point during
23 the previous calendar year.
24 (5) Credit card. – A card, plate, coupon book, or other credit device existing for
25 the purpose of obtaining money, property, labor, or services on credit.
26 (6) Debit card. – A card or other payment code or device issued or approved for
27 use through a payment card network to debit an asset account, regardless of
28 the purpose for which the account is established, whether authorization is
29 based on a signature, a personal identification number, or other means. The
30 term includes a general-use prepaid card, as defined in 15 U.S.C. § 1693l-1.
31 The term does not include paper checks.
32 (7) Electronic payment transaction. – A transaction in which a person uses a debit
33 card, a credit card, or other payment code or device issued or approved
34 through a payment card network to debit a deposit account or use a line of
35 credit, whether authorization is based on a signature, a personal identification
36 number, or other means.

1 (8) Gratuity. – A voluntary monetary contribution to an employee from a guest,
2 patron, or customer in connection with services rendered.
3 (9) Interchange fee. – A fee established, charged, or received by a payment card
4 network for the purpose of compensating the issuer for its involvement in an
5 electronic payment transaction.
6 (10) Issuer. – A person issuing a debit card or credit card.
7 (11) Merchant. – A person that accepts electronic payment transactions and
8 collects and remits a tax.
9 (12) Payment card network. – An entity to which both of the following apply:
10 a. The entity, directly or through licensed members, processors, or
11 agents, provides the proprietary services, infrastructure, and software
12 to route information and data for the purpose of conducting electronic
13 payment transaction authorization, clearance, and settlement.
14 b. A merchant uses the entity to accept as a form of payment a brand of
15 debit card, credit card, or other device that may be used to carry out
16 electronic payment transactions.
17 (13) Person. – Any individual, firm, public or private corporation, government,
18 partnership, association, or any other organization or entity.
19 (14) Processor. – An entity that facilitates, services, processes, or manages the
20 debit or credit authorization, billing, transfer, payment procedures, or
21 settlement with respect to any electronic payment transaction.
22 (15) Settlement. – The process of transmitting sales information to the issuing bank
23 for collection and reimbursement of funds to the merchant and calculating and
24 reporting the net transaction amount to the issuer and merchant for an
25 electronic payment transaction that is cleared.
26 (16) Tax. – Any use and occupation tax or excise tax imposed by the State or a unit
27 of local government in the State.
28 (17) Tax documentation. – Documentation, such as invoices, receipts, journals,
29 ledgers, and tax returns filed with the Department of Revenue or local taxing
30 authorities, that is sufficient for a payment card network to determine the total
31 amount of an electronic payment transaction and the tax or gratuity amount of
32 the transaction. Tax documentation may be related to a single electronic
33 payment transaction or multiple electronic payment transactions aggregated
34 over a period of time.
35 "§ 66-512. Interchange fees on taxes and gratuities prohibited.
36 (a) An issuer, a payment card network, an acquirer bank, or a processor shall not receive
37 or charge a merchant an interchange fee on the tax amount or gratuity of an electronic payment
38 transaction if the merchant informs the acquirer bank or its designee of the tax or gratuity amount
39 as part of the authorization or settlement process for the electronic payment transaction. The
40 merchant shall transmit the tax or gratuity amount data as part of the authorization or settlement
41 process to avoid being charged an interchange fee on the tax or gratuity amount of an electronic
42 payment transaction.
43 (b) A merchant that does not transmit the tax or gratuity amount data in accordance with
44 subsection (a) of this section may submit tax documentation for the electronic payment
45 transaction to the acquirer bank or its designee no later than 180 days after the date of the
46 electronic payment transaction and, within 30 days after the merchant submits the necessary tax
47 documentation, the issuer shall credit to the merchant the amount of the interchange fee charged
48 on the tax or gratuity amount of the electronic payment transaction.
49 (c) This section does not create liability for a payment card network regarding the
50 accuracy of the tax or gratuity data reported by the merchant.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (d) It is unlawful for an issuer, a payment card network, an acquirer bank, or a processor
2 to alter or manipulate the computation and imposition of interchange fees by increasing the rate
3 or amount of the fees applicable to or imposed upon the portion of a credit or debit card
4 transaction not attributable to taxes or gratuities to circumvent the effect of this section.
5 "§ 66-513. Penalties.
6 (a) Injunctive Relief. – The Attorney General may file suit to seek injunctive relief from
7 a covered credit card issuer or payment card network that has violated any of the prohibitions of
8 this Article.
9 (b) Civil Penalty. – The Attorney General may file suit to assess a civil penalty on an
10 issuer, payment card network, acquirer bank, or processor that has violated this Article. The
11 amount of the civil penalty shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000) per electronic payment
12 transaction conducted in violation of this Article. The clear proceeds of civil penalties provided
13 for in this section shall be remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with
14 G.S. 115C-457.2.
15 (c) Refund. – If a court finds that a person has violated this Article, the person shall refund
16 the merchant the interchange fee calculated on the tax or gratuity amount of the electronic
17 payment transaction.
18 (d) Data Use. – An entity, other than the merchant, involved in facilitating or processing
19 an electronic payment transaction, including an issuer, payment card network, acquirer bank, or
20 processor, shall not distribute, exchange, transfer, disseminate, or use the electronic payment
21 transaction data except to facilitate or process the electronic payment transaction; to monitor for,
22 detect, or prevent fraud; to support loyalty, rewards, or promotional offerings; to tailor products
23 and services to serve customer needs; or as required by law. A violation of this subsection
24 constitutes a violation of G.S. 75-1.1."
25 SECTION 2. If a provision of this act or its application to a person or circumstance
26 is held invalid, the invalidity does not affect another provision or application that can be given
27 effect without the invalid provision.
28 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to transactions
29 made on or after that date.
