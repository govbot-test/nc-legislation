GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 3
HOUSE BILL 354
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/1/25
Third Edition Engrossed 4/9/25
Short Title: Civil Procedure/Gatekeeper Orders/Database. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 12, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT AMENDING RULE 17 OF THE NORTH CAROLINA RULES OF CIVIL
3 PROCEDURE TO PROVIDE FOR THE ISSUANCE OF GATEKEEPER ORDERS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 1A-1, Rule 17 of the Rules of Civil Procedure, is amended by
6 adding a new subsection to read:
7 "(f) Gatekeeper Order. – A person who has been adjudicated incompetent and has been
8 prohibited from filing pleadings in court may file a pleading with the court only through the
9 person's guardian or counsel. If a person has been identified by the Administrative Office of the
10 Courts as having been so adjudicated, then the court shall strike any pleading filed by the person
11 during the person's adjudicated incompetency and not filed by the person's guardian or counsel
12 and issue a gatekeeper order to prevent the person from executing filings in violation of this
13 subsection. A gatekeeper order entered under this subsection shall be served on the person's
14 guardian and counsel. Nothing in this subsection shall prohibit a person who has been adjudicated
15 incompetent from filing any pleading without the assistance of that person's guardian or counsel
16 in any of the following: (i) an action against that person's guardian or counsel, (ii) an action
17 appealing the determination of being adjudicated incompetent, (iii) an action petitioning for
18 modification of guardianship, (iv) an action to restore the person's competency, and (v) an action
19 in which the person who has been adjudicated incompetent is sued. The court shall withdraw the
20 gatekeeper order and allow the person to file pleadings with a court if the person's competency
21 has been restored."
22 SECTION 2. The Administrative Office of the Courts shall develop a functional
23 database of all persons currently adjudicated as incompetent by a North Carolina court as
24 described in Section 1 of this act. The database must be updated when a person has been
25 adjudicated incompetent, when a guardianship has been modified, and when a person's
26 competency has been restored. The database must be readily accessible to judges and the clerks
27 of superior court.
28 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to actions
29 and proceedings brought or pending on or after that date.
