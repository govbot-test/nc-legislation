9 SUBPART I-A. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND SCOPE
10 SECTION 1A.1.(a) Legislative Findings. – The General Assembly finds that on July
11 6, 2025, Tropical Storm Chantal impacted the central region of North Carolina, resulting in
12 several days of severe weather, including flooding, high winds, and tornadoes, necessitating
13 recovery efforts in the impacted area of this State. On July 25, 2025, the United States Small
14 Business Administration issued a disaster declaration for several counties in the State. On August
15 5, 2025, the Governor declared a Type I disaster in the impacted area due to the effects of Tropical
16 Storm Chantal.
17 SECTION 1A.1.(b) Applicability. – Except as otherwise provided, funds
18 appropriated in this Part apply only to the counties listed in this section.
19 SECTION 1A.1.(c) Scope. – Funds appropriated and allocated in this Part for
20 Tropical Storm Chantal shall only be expended to support disaster relief and recovery efforts in
21 the impacted area. For the purposes of this Part, the term "impacted area" means Alamance,
22 Caswell, Chatham, Durham, Granville, Moore, Orange, Person, and Wake Counties.

2 SUBPART I-D. REVERSION, LIMITATIONS ON USE OF FUNDS, AUDIT, AND
3 REPORTING OF FUNDS
4 SECTION 1D.1.(a) Reversion. – Funds appropriated under Subpart I-B of this Part
5 shall revert to the Savings Reserve if not expended or encumbered by June 30, 2031.
6 SECTION 1D.1.(b) Receipt of Allocations. – A recipient of State funds under this
7 Part shall use best efforts and take all reasonable steps to obtain alternative funds that cover the
8 losses or needs for which the State funds are provided, including funds from insurance policies
9 in effect and available federal aid. State funds paid under this Part are declared to be excess over
10 funds received by a recipient from the settlement of a claim for loss or damage covered under the
11 recipient's applicable insurance policy in effect or federal aid. Where a recipient is an institution
12 of higher education or a non-State entity, the requirement regarding alternative funds and the
13 calculation of alternative funds received under this subsection includes seeking private donations
14 to help cover the losses or needs for which State funds are provided. An agency awarding State
15 funds for disaster relief shall include a notice to the recipient of the requirements of this
16 subsection.
17 SECTION 1D.1.(c) Remittance of Funds. – If a recipient obtains alternative funds
18 pursuant to subsection (b) of this section, the recipient shall remit the funds to the State agency
19 from which the State funds were received. A recipient is not required to remit any amount in
20 excess of the State funds provided to the recipient under this Part. The State agency shall transfer
21 these funds to the Savings Reserve.
22 SECTION 1D.1.(d) Contract Requirements. – Any contract or other instrument
23 entered into by a recipient for receipt of funds under this Part shall include the requirements set
24 forth in subsections (b) and (c) of this section.
25 SECTION 1D.1.(e) Limitation on Powers of Governor. – The Governor may not use
26 the funds described in this Part to make budget adjustments under G.S. 143C-6-4 or to make
27 reallocations under G.S. 166A-19.40(c). Nothing in this Part shall be construed to prohibit the
28 Governor from exercising the Governor's authority under these statutes with respect to funds
29 other than those described in this Part.
30 SECTION 1D.1.(f) Directive. – The Governor shall ensure that funds allocated in
31 this Part are expended in a manner that does not adversely affect any person's or entity's eligibility
32 for federal funds that are made available, or that are anticipated to be made available, as a result
33 of natural disasters. The Governor shall also, to the extent practicable, avoid using State funds to
34 cover costs that will be, or likely will be, covered by federal funds.
35 SECTION 1D.1.(g) Continuation of Allocation Reporting Requirements. – The
36 Office of State Budget and Management shall add the appropriations and allocations provided
37 for in this Part to the reporting requirements set forth in Section 4.1(g) of S.L. 2025-2.
38 SECTION 1D.1.(h) Continuation of State Auditor Oversight. – The Office of the
39 Governor of North Carolina shall continue the reporting requirements set forth in Section 4.2 of
40 S.L. 2025-2 for the funds appropriated and allocated in this Part. The State Auditor shall include
41 all funds appropriated and allocated under this Part in their report to the Joint Legislative
42 Commission on Governmental Operations and include the expenditure of these funds in the
43 public dashboard as set forth in Section 4.2 of S.L. 2025-2.
