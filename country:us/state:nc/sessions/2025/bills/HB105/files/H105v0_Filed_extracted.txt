4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Section 2 of Chapter 633 of the 1977 Session Laws reads as rewritten:
6 "Sec. 2. Composition of board of education. Notwithstanding Section 115-18 of the General
7 Statutes of North Carolina, G.S. 115C-35, the board of education shall consist of nine members.
8 Two members shall be elected by the duly qualified voters of Gaston County without regard to
9 residence in any particular township and seven district. Seven members shall be elected by the
10 duly qualified voters of Gaston County from the following townships:residency districts. All
11 candidates shall be voted on by all eligible voters in the county. Members shall be elected from
12 districts as follows:
13 (1) Cherryville Township shall be entitled to one member.
14 (2) Crowders Mountain Township shall be entitled to one member.
15 (3) Dallas Township shall be entitled to one member.
16 (4) Gastonia Township shall be entitled to two members.
17 (5) Riverbend Township shall be entitled to one member.
18 (6) Southpoint Township shall be entitled to one member."
19 SECTION 2. Section 3 of Chapter 633 of the 1977 Session Laws reads as rewritten:
20 "Sec. 3. Elections. Notwithstanding Section 115-19 of the General Statutes of North Carolina,
21 at the general election to be held in the year 1978, there shall be elected by the duly qualified
22 voters of Gaston County three members of the board of education, with one member from
23 Cherryville Township, one member from Crowders Mountain Township and one member at
24 large. The term for these three members shall commence on the first Monday in December 1978,
25 and expire at the end of two years thereafter, or on the first Monday in December 1980.
26 At the general election to be held in the year 1980, there shall be elected by the duly qualified
27 voters of Gaston County six members of the board of education, with one member from
28 Cherryville Township, one member from Crowders Mountain Township, one member from
29 Gastonia Township, one member from Riverbend Township, one member from Southpoint
30 Township and one member at large. Those members elected from Cherryville Township,
31 Crowders Mountain Township, Gastonia Township and the member elected at large shall
32 compose one class, whose term of office shall commence on the first Monday in December 1980,
33 and expire at the end of four years thereafter, or on the first Monday in December 1984. The term
34 of office for those members elected from Riverbend Township and Southpoint Township shall
35 commence on the First Monday in December 1980, and expire at the end of two years thereafter,
36 or on the first Monday in December 1982.

21 SECTION 3. Section 4 of Chapter 633 of the 1977 Session Laws, as amended by
22 Chapter 331 of the 1979 Session Laws, as amended by Chapter 73 of the 1993 Session Laws,
23 reads as rewritten:
24 "Sec. 4. Ballot; notice of candidacy and residence requirements. The names of the candidates
25 shall be placed on a separate ballot bearing no party designation, and no party affiliation need be
26 indicated at the time of filing. Each candidate shall file a notice of candidacy with the board of
27 elections indicating whether he the candidate is running as a member at large or from a particular
28 township at any time after 12:00 noon on the Friday preceding the eighteenth Saturday and before
29 12:00 noon on the Friday preceding the fourteenth Saturday before the general election. A
30 candidate seeking to represent a particular township shall be a resident and qualified voter of said
31 township.district at a time consistent with deadlines for other county officers. For the district
32 seats, no person shall be eligible to file for, be elected to, or serve on the board of education
33 unless the person is a qualified voter and resident of the district in which the person seeks to be
34 elected."
35 SECTION 4.(a) Section 7 of Chapter 633 of the 1977 Session Laws reads as
36 rewritten:
37 "Sec. 7. Vacancy. Notwithstanding Section 115-24 of the General Statutes of North Carolina,
38 all All vacancies in the membership of the board of education by death, resignation, or other
39 causes shall be filled by the remaining members of the board of education by the appointment of
40 a person to serve until the next election of members of such board at which time the remaining
41 unexpired term of the office in which the vacancy occurs shall be filled by election. In the event
42 that the replacement is not selected by the board of education within a period of 90 days after the
43 vacancy occurs, such vacancy shall be filled by the board of commissioners. When one who has
44 been elected at large vacates his office, the successor may be selected without regard to the
45 location of his residence in the county. When one who has been elected from a township vacates
46 his office, the successor shall be selected from that same township.in accordance with
47 G.S. 115C-37.1. Any person appointed to fill a vacancy for a district seat shall reside in the
48 district in which the vacating member was elected to serve."
49 SECTION 4.(b) This section becomes effective December 1, 2026.
