13 The following definitions apply in this Part:
14 (1) Academic term. – A semester or summer session.
15 (2) Authority. – The State Education Assistance Authority.
16 (3) Dean. – The Dean of the School of Health Sciences at WSSU.

18 a. Received a forgivable loan.
19 b. Graduated within 10 years from at least one of the following at WSSU,
20 excluding any authorized deferment for extenuating circumstances:
21 1. A Bachelor of Science in Nursing program.
22 2. A Master of Science in Nursing Education program.
23 c. Is licensed as a registered nurse in this State.
24 (5) Forgivable loan. – A forgivable loan made under the Program.
25 (6) Program. – The Nursing Fellows Program.
26 (7) Qualifying nurse. – An eligible nurse who meets all of the following criteria:
27 a. Holds a Bachelor of Science degree in Nursing from WSSU.
28 b. Is employed as a nurse in this State.
29 (8) Qualifying nurse instructor. – An eligible nurse who meets all of the following
30 criteria:
31 a. Holds a Bachelor of Science degree in Nursing from WSSU and a
32 Master of Science degree in Nursing Education from WSSU.
33 b. Is employed as an instructor in a qualifying nursing program.
34 (9) Qualifying nursing program. – A nursing program at one of the following that
35 prepares students to earn a degree in nursing and become a licensed practical
36 nurse as defined in Article 9A of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes:

10 "§ 116-209.65. North Carolina Nursing Fellows Program established; administration.
11 (a) Program. – There is established the Nursing Fellows Program. The purpose of the
12 Program is to recruit, prepare, and support eligible nursing students at WSSU for preparation as
13 highly effective nurses and instructors in qualifying nursing programs. The Program shall be used
14 to provide forgivable loans to nursing students who are (i) enrolled in degree programs at WSSU
15 to receive a Bachelor of Science in Nursing or a Master of Science in Nursing Education and (ii)
16 interested in preparing to become nurses in the State or instructors in qualifying nursing
17 programs.
18 (b) Program Administration; Dean. – The Authority shall administer the Program in
19 cooperation with the Dean. The Dean shall determine forgivable loan recipient selection criteria
20 and selection procedures and shall select the recipients to receive forgivable loans under the
21 Program in accordance with the requirements of this Part. The Dean shall appoint any needed
22 staff of the Program and shall be responsible for recruitment and coordination of the Program,
23 including proactive, aggressive, and strategic recruitment of potential recipients. Recruitment
24 activities shall include (i) targeting regions of the State with the greatest need for nurses and
25 nursing instructors in qualifying nursing programs, (ii) actively engaging with registered nurses,
26 business leaders, experts in human resources, elected officials, and other community leaders
27 throughout the State, and (iii) attracting candidates to the Program. WSSU shall provide office
28 space and clerical support staff, as necessary, to the Dean for the Program.
29 (c) Trust Fund. – There is established the Nursing Fellows Program Trust Fund to be
30 administered by the Authority, in conjunction with the Dean. All funds (i) appropriated to, or
31 otherwise received by, the Program for forgivable loans and other Program purposes, (ii) received
32 as repayment of forgivable loans, and (iii) earned as interest on these funds shall be placed in the
33 Trust Fund. The purpose of the Trust Fund is to provide financial assistance to qualified students
34 to fill needed positions for nurses in the State and instructors in qualifying nursing programs
35 through completion of a Bachelor of Science degree in Nursing or both a Bachelor of Science
36 degree in Nursing and a Master of Science degree in Nursing Education.
37 (d) Uses of Monies in the Trust Fund. – The monies in the Trust Fund may be used only
38 for (i) forgivable loans granted under the Program, (ii) administrative costs associated with the
39 Program, including recruitment and recovery of funds advanced under the Program, (iii)
40 mentoring and coaching support to forgivable loan recipients, and (iv) extracurricular
41 enhancement activities of the Program in accordance with the following:
42 (1) The Authority shall transfer ten percent (10%) of the available funds from the
43 Trust Fund to WSSU at the beginning of each fiscal year for the following
44 purposes:
45 a. The Program's administrative costs.
46 b. Extracurricular enhancement activities of the Program.
47 c. Mentoring and coaching support to forgivable loan recipients in an
48 amount of up to two thousand two hundred dollars ($2,200) for each
49 Program recipient.

4 (e) Student Selection Criteria for Forgivable Loans. – The Dean, in consultation with the
5 Associate Dean of Nursing at WSSU, shall adopt stringent standards for awarding forgivable
6 loans based on multiple measures to ensure that only the strongest applicants receive them,
7 including the following:
8 (1) Grade point averages.
9 (2) Performance on relevant assessments.
10 (3) Experience, accomplishments, and other criteria demonstrating qualities
11 positively correlated with highly effective nurses and instructors in qualifying
12 nursing programs, including excellent verbal and communication skills.
13 (4) Demonstrated commitment to serve in North Carolina.
14 (f) Awards of Forgivable Loans. – The Program shall provide forgivable loans to selected
15 students who are initially enrolled in a Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree program at WSSU.
16 Loan payments shall be provided each year for completion of the Bachelor of Science in Nursing
17 program and, if a loan recipient seeks to become a qualifying nurse instructor, for a Master of
18 Science in Nursing Education program. The student shall be eligible to receive loan payments
19 for the Master of Science in Nursing Education program as long as the student enrolls within two
20 years of graduation from the Bachelor of Science in Nursing program. Forgivable loans may be
21 used for tuition, fees, the cost of books, and expenses related to completing a Bachelor of Science
22 degree in Nursing and a Master of Science degree in Nursing Education. Forgivable loans shall
23 be awarded per academic term in amounts of up to five thousand dollars ($5,000) per semester
24 or two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500) per summer session, as follows:
25 (1) For students pursuing careers as qualifying nurses, for up to five semesters.
26 (2) For persons pursuing careers as qualifying nurse instructors, for up to nine
27 semesters.
28 (g) Administration of Forgivable Loan Awards. – Upon the naming of recipients of the
29 forgivable loans by the Dean, the Dean shall transfer to the Authority its decisions. The Authority,
30 in coordination with the Dean, shall perform all of the administrative functions necessary to
31 implement this Part, which functions shall include rulemaking, disseminating information, acting
32 as a liaison with participating institutions of higher education, implementing forgivable loan
33 agreements, loan monitoring, loan canceling through service and collection, determining the
34 acceptability of service repayment agreements, enforcing the agreements, and all other functions
35 necessary for the execution, payment, and enforcement of promissory notes required under this
36 Part.
37 (h) Annual Report. – The Dean, in coordination with the Authority, shall report no later
38 than January 1, 2027, and annually thereafter, to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight
39 Committee regarding the following:
40 (1) Forgivable loans awarded from the Trust Fund, including the following:
41 a. Demographic information regarding recipients.
42 b. Number of recipients by institution of higher education.
43 (2) Placement and repayment rates, including the following:
44 a. Number of graduates who have been employed as qualifying nurses in
45 the State and qualifying nurse instructors in qualifying nursing
46 programs within two years of graduation.
47 b. Number of graduates who have elected to do loan repayment and their
48 years of service, if any, prior to beginning loan repayment.
49 c. Graduation rates of associate degree students taught by instructors in
50 qualifying nursing programs who are recipients of loans under the
51 Program.

8 "§ 116-209.66. Terms of forgivable loans; receipt and disbursement of funds.
9 (a) Notes. – All forgivable loans shall be evidenced by notes made payable to the
10 Authority that bear interest at a rate not to exceed ten percent (10%) per year as set by the
11 Authority and beginning on the first day of September after completion of the applicable degree
12 program or 90 days after graduation, whichever is later. If a forgivable loan is terminated, the
13 note shall be made payable to the Authority 90 days after termination of the forgivable loan. The
14 forgivable loan may be terminated upon the recipient's withdrawal from the Program or by the
15 recipient's failure to meet the standards set by the Dean.
16 (b) Forgiveness. – For every year a qualifying nurse remains a qualifying nurse or a
17 qualifying nurse instructor remains a qualifying nurse instructor, the Authority shall forgive
18 one-third of the total loan amount received over the course of enrollment in the applicable degree
19 program and any interest accrued on that amount. The Authority shall also forgive the loan if it
20 finds that it is impossible for the recipient to work for up to three years, within 10 years after
21 completion of the Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree program for persons pursuing
22 forgiveness as qualifying nurses or of the Master of Science in Nursing Education degree
23 program for persons pursuing forgiveness as qualifying nurse instructors, because of the death or
24 permanent disability of the recipient. If the recipient repays the forgivable loan by cash payments,
25 all indebtedness shall be repaid within 10 years after completion of the latest applicable degree
26 program supported by the forgivable loan. If the recipient completes the applicable degree
27 program, payment of principal and interest shall begin no later than the first day of September
28 after the completion of the program. Should a recipient present extenuating circumstances, the
29 Authority may extend the period to repay the loan in cash to no more than a total of 12 years."
