1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REENACT THE CHILD TAX CREDIT.
3 Whereas, North Carolina is a pro-family, pro-child State; and
4 Whereas, decades of research documents the benefits of a refundable child tax credit,
5 including improved health, educational outcomes, and even higher future earnings potential for
6 children in claiming families; and
7 Whereas, the cost of childcare is prohibitively expensive for low- and
8 moderate-income families, forcing a choice between working to afford basics or caring for
9 children; and
10 Whereas, the cost of raising a child is causing many families to opt out of having
11 children altogether; and
12 Whereas, no family or individual should ever have to sacrifice parenthood because it
13 is too expensive; and
14 Whereas, the State's birth rate is falling, and it is in the economic interest of the State
15 to incentivize raising children for those who want this option; and
16 Whereas, the State of North Carolina has over 388,000 children living in poverty; and
17 Whereas, the expansion of the child tax credit during the COVID-19 pandemic was a
18 significant force in reducing child poverty to a historic low; Now, therefore,
19 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
20 SECTION 1. G.S. 105-153.10 is reenacted as it existed immediately before its
21 expiration and reads as rewritten:
22 "§ 105-153.10. Credit for children.
