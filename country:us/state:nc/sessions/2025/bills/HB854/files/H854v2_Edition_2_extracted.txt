GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 5/6/25
Short Title: Require Licensure of Educational Interpreters. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
April 10, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REMOVE THE EXEMPTION FOR EDUCATIONAL INTERPRETERS OR
3 TRANSLITERATORS FROM THE LICENSURE REQUIREMENTS OF THE NORTH
4 CAROLINA INTERPRETER AND TRANSLITERATOR LICENSURE ACT.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 90D-3 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 90D-3. Definitions.
8 The following definitions apply in this Chapter:
9 …
10 (3) Educational interpreter or educational transliterator. – A person who provides
11 accessible communication, using the most understandable language model, to
12 individuals in prekindergarten through grade 12 or in any institution of higher
13 education.
14 (4) Interpreter. – A person who practices the act of interpreting as defined in this
15 section. The term includes an educational interpreter as defined in subdivision
16 (3) of this section.
17 …
18 (9) Transliterator. – A person who practices the art of transliterating as defined in
19 this section. The term includes an educational transliterator as defined in
20 subdivision (3) of this section."
21 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 90D-4(b) reads as rewritten:
22 "(b) The provisions of this Chapter do not apply to:
23 …
24 (5) Educational interpreters or transliterators.
25 …."
26 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 90D-7 reads as rewritten:
27 "§ 90D-7. Requirements for licensure.
28 (a) Upon application to the Board and the payment of the required fees, an applicant may
29 be licensed as an interpreter or transliterator if the applicant meets all of the following
30 qualifications:
31 (1) Is 18 years of age or older.
32 (2) Is of good moral character as determined by the Board.
33 (3) Meets one of the following criteria:
34 a. Repealed by Session Laws 2023-137, s. 45(a), effective December 1,
35 2023, and applicable to licenses and provisional licenses issued or

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 renewed by the North Carolina Interpreter and Transliterator
2 Licensing Board after that date.
3 b. Is nationally certified by the Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf, Inc.,
4 (RID), or another nationally recognized body that issues certificates or
5 assessments for interpreting approved by the Board by rule.
6 c. Holds a valid Testing, Evaluation and Certification Unit, Inc.,
7 (TECUnit) national certification in cued language transliteration.
8 d. Repealed by Session Laws 2023-137, s. 45(a), effective December 1,
9 2023, and applicable to licenses and provisional licenses issued or
10 renewed by the North Carolina Interpreter and Transliterator
11 Licensing Board after that date.
12 e. Holds a current Cued Language Transliterator State Level Assessment
13 (CLTSLA) level 3 or above classification.
14 f. Holds a current Educational Interpreter Performance Assessment
15 (EIPA) level 4.0 or above classification and passed the EIPA written
16 test.
17 (b) Repealed by Session Laws 2014-115, s. 42(b), effective August 11, 2014.
18 (c) The Department of Public Safety may provide a criminal record check to the Board
19 for a person who has applied for a new, provisional, or renewal license through the Board. The
20 Board shall provide to the Department of Public Safety, along with the request, the fingerprints
21 of the applicant, any additional information required by the Department of Public Safety, and a
22 form signed by the applicant consenting to the check of the criminal record and to the use of the
23 fingerprints and other identifying information required by the State or national repositories. The
24 applicant's fingerprints shall be forwarded to the State Bureau of Investigation for a search of the
25 State's criminal history record file, and the State Bureau of Investigation shall forward a set of
26 the fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation for a national criminal history check. The
27 Board shall keep all information pursuant to this subdivision privileged, in accordance with
28 applicable State law and federal guidelines, and the information shall be confidential and shall
29 not be a public record under Chapter 132 of the General Statutes.
30 The Department of Public Safety may charge each applicant a fee for conducting the checks
31 of criminal history records authorized by this subsection."
32 SECTION 1.(d) G.S. 90D-8 reads as rewritten:
33 "§ 90D-8. Provisional license.
34 (a) Upon application to the Board and the payment of the required fees, an applicant may
35 be issued a one-time provisional license as an interpreter or transliterator if the applicant meets
36 all of the following qualifications:
37 (1) Is at least 18 years of age.
38 (2) Is of good moral character as determined by the Board.
39 (3) Completes two continuing education units approved by the Board. These units
40 must be completed for each renewable year.
41 (4) Holds at least a two-year associate degree in interpreting from an accredited
42 institution and satisfies one of the following:
43 a. Holds a quality assurance North Carolina Interpreter Classification
44 System (NCICS) level C classification.
45 b. Holds a valid National Association of the Deaf (NAD) level 2 or 3
46 certification.
47 c. Holds a current Educational Interpreter Performance Assessment
48 (EIPA) level 3.5 or above classification.classification and passed the
49 EIPA written test.
50 d. Repealed by Session Laws 2005-299, s. 2, effective August 22, 2005.

1 e. Repealed by Session Laws 2023-137, s. 45(b), effective October 10,
2 2023.
3 f. Holds any other certificate or assessment issued by a nationally
4 recognized body approved by the Board by rule.
5 (a1) Upon application to the Board, payment of the required fees, and meeting the
6 requirements for a provisional license under subdivisions (1) and (2) of subsection (a) of this
7 section, the Board may also issue a provisional license to any of the following categories of
8 persons seeking a provisional license:
9 (1) A deaf interpreter who completes 16 hours of training in interpreting
10 coursework or workshops, including role and function or ethics, and 20 hours
11 in the 12 months immediately preceding the date of application in the
12 provision of interpreting services.
13 (2) An oral interpreter who completes a total of 40 hours of training in interpreting
14 coursework or workshops related to oral interpreting.
15 (3) A cued language transliterator who holds a current TECUnit Cued Language
16 Transliterator State Level Assessment (CLTSLA) level 2 or above
17 classification.
18 (4) A person providing interpreting or transliterating services who has a
19 recognized credential from another state in the field of interpreting or
20 transliterating.
21 (5) An interpreter or transliterator who meets both of the following:
22 a. has Has accumulated 200 hours per year in the provision of
23 interpreting or transliterating services, in this State or another state,
24 totaling 400 hours for the two years immediately preceding the date of
25 application. An applicant must provide documentation of hours when
26 applying for a provisional license under this category, subject to
27 verification by the Board.
28 b. Holds any certificate or assessment issued by a nationally recognized
29 body approved by the Board by rule.
30 (b) A provisional license issued under this section shall be valid for one year. Upon
31 expiration, a provisional license may be renewed for an additional one-year period in the
32 discretion of the Board. However, a provisional license shall not be renewed more than three two
33 times. The Board may, in its discretion, grant an extension after the third second time the
34 provisional license has been renewed under circumstances to be established in rules adopted by
35 the Board.
36 (c) Repealed by Session Laws 2014-115, s. 42(b), effective August 11, 2014."
37 SECTION 1.(e) G.S. 115C-110.2 reads as rewritten:
38 "§ 115C-110.2. Interpreters/transliterators.
39 Each interpreter or transliterator employed by a local educational agency to provide services
40 to hearing-impaired students must annually complete 15 hours of job-related training that has
41 been approved by the local educational agency. Continuing education hours completed in
42 compliance with licensure renewal requirements adopted by the North Carolina Interpreter and
43 Transliterator Licensing Board pursuant to G.S. 90D-11 may be applied toward the 15 hours of
44 job-related training, to the extent those hours are relevant to the interpreter's or transliterator's
45 job-based duties and approved by the local educational agency."
46 SECTION 2.(a) For an educational interpreter or educational transliterator who
47 was issued a provisional license pursuant to G.S. 90D-8 and the provisional license expired prior
48 to the effective date of this act, if the educational interpreter or educational transliterator
49 continues to qualify for a provisional license pursuant to G.S. 90D-8, as amended by Section 1(d)
50 of this act, then the North Carolina Interpreter and Transliterator Licensing Board shall issue a
51 new initial provisional license upon application to the Board and the payment of the required fee
House Bill 854-Second Edition Page 3
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 for a provisional license. Notwithstanding G.S. 90D-8(b), the Board shall not grant an extension
2 after a second renewal to a provisional license issued pursuant to this section.
3 SECTION 2.(b) This section expires on September 30, 2027.
4 SECTION 3. The North Carolina Interpreter and Transliterator Licensing Board and
5 the State Board of Education may adopt rules to implement the provisions of this act.
6 SECTION 4. This act becomes effective October 1, 2026.
