Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Gen. Contractor Lic'g Bd. Mods. (Public)
Sponsors: Senator Jarvis (Primary Sponsor).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MODIFY THE DISCIPLINARY AUTHORITY OF THE NORTH CAROLINA
3 LICENSING BOARD FOR GENERAL CONTRACTORS, TO CLARIFY THE
4 COMPLAINT PROCESS, AND TO REQUIRE PAYMENT OF COURT AWARDS PRIOR
5 TO EXAMINATION ELIGIBILITY.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 87-11 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 87-11. Revocation of license; charges of fraud, negligence, incompetency, etc.; hearing
9 thereon; reissuance of certificate. Disciplinary action; complaints; hearings.
10 (a) The Board shall have the power to refuse to issue or renew or revoke, suspend, or
11 restrict a certificate of license or to issue a reprimand or take other disciplinary action if a general
12 contractor licensed under this Article is found guilty of any fraud or deceit in obtaining a license,
13 or gross negligence, incompetency, or misconduct in the practice of his or her profession, or
14 willful violation of any provision of this Article. The Board shall also have the power to revoke,
15 suspend, or otherwise restrict the ability of any person to act as a qualifying party for a license to
16 practice general contracting, as provided in G.S. 87-10(c), for any copartnership, corporation or
17 any other organization or combination, if that person committed any act in violation of the
18 provisions of this section and the Board may take disciplinary action against the individual
19 license held by that person.
20 (a1) Any person may prefer charges of fraud, deceit, negligence, or misconduct against
21 any general contractor licensed under this Article. The charges shall be in writing and sworn to
22 by the complainant and submitted to the Board. The charges, unless dismissed without hearing
23 by the Board as unfounded or trivial, shall be heard and determined by the Board in accordance
24 with the provisions of Chapter 150B of the General Statutes.
25 (b) The Board shall adopt and publish guidelines, consistent with the provisions of this
26 Article, governing the suspension and revocation of licenses.
27 (c) The Board shall establish and maintain a system whereby detailed records are kept
28 regarding complaints against each licensee. This record shall include, for each licensee, the date
29 and nature of each complaint, investigatory action taken by the Board, any findings by the Board,
30 and the disposition of the matter.
31 (d) The Board may reissue a license to any person, firm or corporation whose license has
32 been revoked: Provided, five or more members of the Board vote in favor of such reissuance for
33 reasons the Board may deem sufficient.
34 The Board shall immediately notify the Secretary of State of its findings in the case of the
35 revocation of a license or of the reissuance of a revoked license.

1 A certificate of license to replace any certificate lost, destroyed or mutilated may be issued
2 subject to the rules and regulations of the Board.
3 (e) The Board shall be entitled to recover its reasonable administrative costs associated
4 with the investigation and prosecution of a violation of this Article or rules or regulations of the
5 Board up to a maximum of five thousand dollars ($5,000) for any licensee or qualifying party
6 found to have committed any of the following:
7 (1) Fraud or deceit in obtaining a license.
8 (2) Gross negligence, incompetency, or misconduct in the practice of general
9 contracting.
10 (3) Willful violation of any provision of this Article.
11 (a) The Board shall have the power to refuse to issue or renew or revoke, suspend, or
12 restrict a certificate of license or to issue a reprimand or take other disciplinary action if a general
13 licensee, qualifier, or applicant does any of the following:
14 (1) Employs the use of fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation in obtaining or
15 attempting to obtain a license or the renewal of a license.
16 (2) Commits an act of gross negligence, incompetency, or misconduct in the
17 practice of general contracting.
18 (3) Willfully violates any provision of this Article, any rules adopted by the
19 Board, or any order of the Board.
20 (b) The Board shall also have the power to revoke, suspend, or otherwise restrict the
21 ability of any person to act as a qualifying party for a license to practice general contracting, as
22 provided in G.S. 87-10(c), for any copartnership, corporation or any other organization or
23 combination, if that person committed any act in violation of the provisions of this section. The
24 Board may also take disciplinary action against the individual license held by that person.
25 (c) The Board shall initiate an investigation upon receipt of information about any act
26 that might violate any provision of this Article, or any rule promulgated by the Board. All
27 complaints shall be in writing and affirmed to by the complainant. The charges, unless dismissed
28 by the Board, shall be heard and determined by the Board in accordance with the provisions of
29 Chapter 150B of the General Statutes. The Board will not accept anonymous complaints.
30 (d) The Board shall adopt and publish rules consistent with the provisions of this Article,
31 governing the discipline of licensees and qualifiers.
32 (e) The Board shall be entitled to recover its reasonable administrative costs associated
33 with the investigation and prosecution of a violation of this Article or rules or regulations of the
34 Board up to a maximum of five thousand dollars ($5,000) per violation for any licensee or
35 qualifying party found to have committed any of the following:
36 (1) Fraud or deceit in obtaining a license.
37 (2) Gross negligence, incompetency, or misconduct in the practice of general
38 contracting.
39 (3) Willful violation of any provision of this Article."
40 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 87-13.1 reads as rewritten:
41 "§ 87-13.1. Board may seek injunctive relief; attorneys' fee.
42 Whenever the Board determines that any person, firm or corporation has violated or is
43 violating any of the provisions of this Article or rules and regulations of the Board promulgated
44 under this Article, the Board may apply to the superior court for a restraining order and injunction
45 to restrain the violation; and the superior courts have jurisdiction to grant the requested relief,
46 irrespective of whether or not criminal prosecution has been instituted or administrative sanctions
47 imposed by reason of the violation. When the Board prevails in actions brought under this
48 section, the court shall award the Board its reasonable attorneys' fee not to exceed five thousand
49 dollars ($5,000) plus the costs associated with obtaining the relief and the investigation and
50 prosecution of the violation. Examination applicants who have failed to pay a court award
Page 2 Senate Bill 755-First Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 pursuant to this section shall not be allowed to take any examination offered by the Board until
2 the award has been satisfied."
3 SECTION 1.(c) The North Carolina Licensing Board for General Contractors may
4 adopt rules to implement the provisions of subsections (a) and (b) of this section.
5 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to violations
6 that occur on or after that date.
