13 "§ 58-3-271. Coverage for diagnostic, screening, and supplemental examinations for
14 breast cancer, including mammograms and other imaging, and cervical cancer
15 screening.
16 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
17 (1) Breast magnetic resonance imaging. – A diagnostic tool that uses a powerful
18 magnetic field, radio waves, and a computer to produce detailed pictures of
19 the structures within the breast.
20 (2) Breast ultrasound. – A noninvasive diagnostic tool that uses high-frequency
21 sound waves to produce detailed images of the breast.
22 (3) Cost-sharing. – A deductible, coinsurance, copayment, and any maximum
23 limitation on the application of a deductible, coinsurance, copayment, or
24 similar out-of-pocket expense.
25 (4) Diagnostic examination for breast cancer. – An examination for breast cancer
26 that is determined by the healthcare provider treating the patient to be
27 medically necessary and appropriate and that may include breast magnetic
28 resonance imaging, breast ultrasound, and diagnostic low-dose
29 mammography to evaluate the abnormality in the breast that meets one of the
30 following criteria:
31 a. Is seen or suspected from a screening examination for breast cancer.
32 b. Is detected by another means of examination.
33 (5) High-deductible health plan. – As defined under the Internal Revenue Code.

24 (a)(a1) Every policy or contract of accident or health insurance, and every preferred provider
25 benefit plan under G.S. 58-50-56, that is issued, renewed, or amended on or after January 1, 1992,

28 screening mammography. The same deductibles, coinsurance, and other limitations as apply to
29 similar services covered under the policy, contract, or plan shall apply to coverage for
30 examinations and laboratory tests for the screening for the early detection of cervical cancer and
31 low-dose screening mammography.
32 (a1) As used in this section, "examinations and laboratory tests for the screening for the
33 early detection of cervical cancer" means conventional PAP smear screening, liquid-based
34 cytology, and human papilloma virus (HPV) detection methods for women with equivocal
35 findings on cervical cytologic analysis that are subject to the approval of and have been approved
36 by the United States Food and Drug Administration.
37 (b) As used in this section, "low-dose screening mammography" means a radiologic
38 procedure for the early detection of breast cancer provided to an asymptomatic woman using
39 equipment dedicated specifically for mammography, including a physician's interpretation of the
40 results of the procedure.
41 (b1) Every health benefit plan offered by an insurer that provides benefits for a diagnostic
42 or supplemental examination for breast cancer shall ensure that the cost-sharing requirements
43 applicable to a diagnostic or supplemental examination for breast cancer are no less favorable
44 than the cost-sharing requirements applicable to low-dose screening mammography for breast
45 cancer.
46 (b2) An insurer shall not be required to reimburse a healthcare provider that is not a
47 contracted provider in the provider network of a health benefit plan offered by the insurer any
48 reimbursement rate more than the rate paid to a provider that has contracted with the insurer to
49 participate in the provider network of the health benefit plan for any of the following services:
50 (1) Diagnostic, screening, or supplemental examination for breast cancer.
51 (2) Low-dose mammography.
