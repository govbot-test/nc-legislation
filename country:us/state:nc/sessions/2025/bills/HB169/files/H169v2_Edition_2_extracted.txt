4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Part V of Chapter 908 of the 1983 Session Laws, as amended by
6 Chapter 942 of the 1985 Session Laws (Regular Session 1986), Chapter 48 of the 1987 Session
7 Laws, Chapter 540 of the 1995 Session Laws, and S.L. 2007-337, reads as rewritten:
8 "Part V. Haywood Occupancy Tax.
9 "Sec. 10. Occupancy Tax. (a) Authorization and Scope. – The Haywood County Board of
10 Commissioners may levy a room occupancy and tourism development tax of two percent (2%)
11 up to six percent (6%) of the gross receipts derived from the rental of any room, lodging, or
12 similar an accommodation furnished by any hotel, motel, inn, tourist camp, or other similar place
13 within the county that is subject to sales tax imposed by the State under G.S. 105-164.4(a)(3).
14 This tax is in addition to any State or local sales tax. This tax does not apply to accommodations
15 furnished by nonprofit charitable, educational, benevolent, or religious organizations.
16 "Sec. 10.1. Additional One Percent (1%) Occupancy Tax. – In addition to the tax authorized
17 by Section 10 of this Part, the Haywood County Board of Commissioners may levy a room
18 occupancy and tourism development tax of one percent (1%) of the gross receipts derived from
19 the rental of accommodations taxable under that section. The levy, collection, administration,
20 and repeal of the tax authorized by this section, and the use of tax revenue from a tax levied under
21 this section, shall be in accordance with this Part. Haywood County may not levy a tax under this
22 section unless it also levies a tax under Section 10 of this Part.
23 "Sec. 10.2. Additional One Percent (1%) Occupancy Tax. – In addition to the tax authorized
24 by Sections 10 and 10.1 of this Part, the Haywood County Board of Commissioners may levy a
25 room occupancy and tourism development tax of one percent (1%) of the gross receipts derived
26 from the rental of accommodations taxable under Sections 10 and 10.1 of this Part. The levy,
27 collection, administration, and repeal of the tax authorized by this section and the use of tax
28 revenue from a tax levied under this section shall be in accordance with this Part. Haywood
29 County may not levy a tax under this section unless it also levies the tax authorized under
30 Sections 10 and 10.1 of this Part.
31 "Sec. 12. Administration. – A tax levied under this Part shall be levied, administered,
32 collected, and repealed as provided in G.S. 153A-155. The penalties provided in G.S. 153A-155
33 apply to a tax levied under this Part.
34 "Sec. 13. Repealed by S.L. 2007-337.
35 "Sec. 14. Distribution and Use of the First Three Percent Occupancy Tax. Tax Revenue. –
36 Haywood County shall, on a monthly quarterly basis, remit the net proceeds of the room

15 travelers to the area. The term includes administrative expenses incurred in

17 (3) Tourism-related expenditures. – Expenditures that, in the judgment of the
18 Tourism Development Authority, are designed to increase the use of lodging
19 facilities, meeting facilities, or convention facilities in a county or to attract
20 tourists or business travelers to the county. The term includes tourism-related
21 capital expenditures.
22 "Sec. 14.1. Distribution and Use of the Additional One Percent Occupancy Tax. – Haywood
23 County shall, on a monthly basis, remit the net proceeds of the room occupancy and tourism
24 development tax levied under Section 10.2 of this Part to the Haywood County Tourism
25 Development Authority. The Authority must segregate the net proceeds received under this
26 section into five separate accounts based on the collection area from which the proceeds were
27 collected. Net proceeds collected under this section from accommodations located in the 28716
28 zip code area must be credited to the Canton Area Account. Net proceeds collected under this
29 section from accommodations located in the 28721 zip code area must be credited to the Clyde
30 Area Account. Net proceeds collected under this section from accommodations located in 28745
31 zip code area must be credited to the Lake Junaluska Area Account. Net proceeds collected under
32 this section from accommodations located in the 28751 zip code area must be credited to the
33 Maggie Valley Area Account. Net proceeds collected under this section from accommodations
34 located in the 28785 and the 28786 zip code areas must be credited to the Waynesville Area
35 Account. Based on recommendations from and in consultation with each of the five collection
36 areas, the Authority shall use at least two-thirds of the funds in each account to promote travel
37 and tourism and the remainder for tourist-related expenditures in each of the collection areas.

39 Haywood County Board of Commissioners adopts a resolution levying a room occupancy and
40 tourism development tax pursuant to this Part, it shall also adopt a resolution creating the
41 Haywood County Tourism Development Authority, which shall be a public authority under the
42 Local Government Budget and Fiscal Control Act. The resolution shall provide that the Authority
43 is composed of the following 15 eight members:
44 (1) Three Two members who own or operate hotels, motels, or other
45 accommodations with more than 20 rental units. Of these members, one
46 member must be from the 28751, 28785, or 28786 zip code, and one member
47 must be from the 28716, 28721, or 28745 zip code.
48 (2) Three Two members who own or operate hotels, motels, or other
49 accommodations with 20 or fewer rental units. Of these members, one
50 member must be from the 28751, 28785, or 28786 zip code, and one member
51 must be from the 28716, 28721, or 28745 zip code.

41 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law. When the Haywood County
42 Board of Commissioners adopts a resolution levying an increase of the room occupancy tax as
43 authorized under this act, it shall adopt a resolution modifying the composition of the Haywood
44 County Tourism Development Authority in accordance with this act.
