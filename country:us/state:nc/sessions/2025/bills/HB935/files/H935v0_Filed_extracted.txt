Section 1:
H.B. 935
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 10, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Fire/Emergency Agency Property Voting Limits. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Greene.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO EXCLUDE FIRE OR EMERGENCY RESPONSE AGENCY PROPERTY FROM
3 ELECTION USE IF THE USE WILL INHIBIT THE ABILITY TO CARRY OUT
4 EMERGENCY RESPONSE.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 163-129(a) reads as rewritten:
7 "(a) At the voting place in each precinct established under the provisions of G.S. 163-128,
8 the county board of elections shall provide or procure by lease or otherwise a suitable structure
9 or part of a structure in which registration and voting may be conducted. To this end, the county
10 board of elections shall be entitled to demand and use any school or other State, county, or
11 municipal building, or a part thereof, or any other building, or a part thereof, which is supported
12 or maintained, in whole or in part by or through tax revenues provided, however, that this section
13 shall not be construed to permit any board of elections to demand and use any tax exempt church
14 property for such purposes without the express consent of the individual church involved,
15 revenues, for the purpose of conducting registration and voting for any primary or election, and
16 it may require that the requisitioned premises, or a part thereof, be vacated for these purposes.
17 This section shall not be construed to permit any board of elections to demand and use any
18 tax-exempt church property for such purposes without the express consent of the individual
19 church involved. Fire or emergency response agency property shall be exempted for demand and
20 use under this subsection if (i) the fire chief responsible for the property determines such
21 utilization of the property will inhibit the ability to carry out emergency response, (ii) the fire
22 chief makes a timely appeal to the board of county commissioners against the demand and use
23 of the property by the board of elections, and (iii) the board of county commissioners makes a
24 final determination in favor of the fire chief's appeal."
25 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
