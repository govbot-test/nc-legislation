9 "§ 18E-100. Definitions.
10 Unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply in this Chapter:
11 (1) ALE Division. – The Alcohol Law Enforcement Division of the Department
12 of Public Safety.
13 (2) Batch. – The kratom product produced during a period of time under similar
14 conditions and identified by a specific code that allows traceability.
15 (3) Distributor. – A person or entity that delivers or sells kratom products for the
16 purpose of distribution in commerce.
17 (4) Exit package. – An opaque bag or other similar opaque covering provided at
18 the point of sale that satisfies the child-resistant effectiveness standards under
19 16 C.F.R. § 1700.15(b)(1) when tested in accordance with the requirements
20 of 16 C.F.R. § 1700.20 in which kratom products are placed by a seller after
21 being sold to the ultimate consumer of the product.
22 (5) Independent testing laboratory. – A laboratory that meets all of the following
23 conditions:
24 a. Holds an ISO 17025 accreditation or is registered with the Drug
25 Enforcement Administration (DEA) in accordance with 21 C.F.R. §
26 1301.13.
27 b. Does not have a direct or indirect interest in the entity whose product
28 is being tested.
29 c. Does not have a direct or indirect interest in a facility that cultivates,
30 processes, distributes, dispenses, or sells kratom products in this State
31 or any other jurisdiction.
32 d. Has entered into a compliance agreement with the ALE Division to
33 conduct kratom product testing.

29 "§ 18E-101. Sales restrictions on kratom products.
30 (a) Restriction. – No person shall do any of the following:
31 (1) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell a kratom product to a person who
32 is under 21 years of age. Any retail dealer of kratom products shall demand
33 proof of age from a prospective purchaser of kratom products before the
34 kratom products are released to the purchaser if the retail dealer has reasonable
35 grounds to believe that the prospective purchaser is under 30 years of age.
36 Any retail dealer that sells a kratom product on an internet website shall verify
37 the age of any prospective purchaser and shall use a method of delivery that
38 requires the signature of a person at least 21 years of age before the kratom
39 product is released.
40 (2) Knowingly, or having reason to know, distribute samples of kratom products
41 in or on a public street, sidewalk, park, or public building.
42 (3) Engage in the business of selling a kratom product without a valid license
43 issued in accordance with this Chapter.
44 (4) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell at retail a kratom product that
45 violates the provisions of G.S. 18E-104.
46 (5) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell a kratom product that is not
47 contained in an exit package or a child proof package.
48 (6) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell at retail or on an internet website
49 offering delivery in this State, a kratom product that is not in compliance with
50 G.S. 18E-105.

24 of the overall alkaloid composition of the product.
25 (5) A kratom product containing any synthetic alkaloids, including synthetic
26 mitragynine, synthetic 7-hydroxymitragynine, or any other synthetically
27 derived compounds of the kratom plant.
28 (6) A kratom product that does not provide adequate labeling directions necessary
29 for safe use by consumers, including a recommended serving size, the
30 recommended number of servings per day, and the number of servings in the
31 package that is sold.
32 (7) A kratom product in any form that is combustible, intended to be used for

24 "§ 18E-201. Licensing requirements; qualifications; duration.
25 (a) Requirement. – Prior to the commencement of business or by July 1, 2026, whichever

45 taxes, interest, and penalties collectable pursuant to G.S. 105-241.22.
