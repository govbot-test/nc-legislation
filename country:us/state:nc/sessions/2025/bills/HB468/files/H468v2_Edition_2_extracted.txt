9 "§ 18E-100. Definitions.
10 Unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply in this Chapter:
11 (1) ALE Division. – The Alcohol Law Enforcement Division of the Department
12 of Public Safety.
13 (2) Batch. – The kratom product produced during a period of time under similar
14 conditions and identified by a specific code that allows traceability.
15 (3) Distributor. – A person or entity that delivers or sells kratom products for the
16 purpose of distribution in commerce.
17 (4) Independent testing laboratory. – A laboratory that meets all of the following
18 conditions:
19 a. Holds an ISO 17025 accreditation or is registered with the Drug
20 Enforcement Administration (DEA) in accordance with 21 C.F.R. §
21 1301.13.
22 b. Does not have a direct or indirect interest in the entity whose product
23 is being tested.
24 c. Does not have a direct or indirect interest in a facility that cultivates,
25 processes, distributes, dispenses, or sells kratom products in this State
26 or any other jurisdiction.
27 d. Has entered into a compliance agreement with the ALE Division to
28 conduct kratom product testing.
29 (5) Ingestion. – The process of consuming kratom through the mouth, by
30 swallowing into the gastrointestinal system or through tissue absorption.
31 (6) Inhalation. – The process of consuming kratom into the respiratory system
32 through the mouth or nasal passages.
33 (7) Kratom product. – Any consumer commodity containing any quantity of
34 mitragynine or 7-hydroxymytragynine or both, extracted from the leaf of the
35 plant Mitragyna speciosa. This definition includes a synthesized kratom
36 product.

21 "§ 18E-101. Sales restrictions on kratom products.
22 (a) Restriction. – No person shall do any of the following:
23 (1) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell a kratom product to a person who
24 is under 21 years of age. Any retail dealer of kratom products shall demand
25 proof of age from a prospective purchaser of kratom products before the
26 kratom products are released to the purchaser if the retail dealer has reasonable
27 grounds to believe that the prospective purchaser is under 30 years of age.
28 Any retail dealer that sells a kratom product on an internet website shall verify
29 the age of any prospective purchaser or shall use a method of delivery that
30 requires the signature of a person at least 21 years of age before the kratom
31 product is released.
32 (2) Knowingly, or having reason to know, distribute samples of kratom products
33 in or on a public street, sidewalk, park, or public building.
34 (3) Engage in the business of selling a kratom product without a valid license
35 issued in accordance with this Chapter.
36 (4) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell at retail a kratom product that
37 violates the provisions of G.S. 18E-104.
38 (5) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell at retail or on an internet website
39 offering delivery in this State, a kratom product that is not in compliance with
40 G.S. 18E-105.
41 (b) Civil Penalties. – For any violation of this section, the ALE Division may take any of
42 the following actions against a retail dealer:
43 (1) For the first violation, the ALE Division may impose a civil penalty of no
44 more than five hundred dollars ($500.00).
45 (2) For the second violation within three years, the ALE Division may impose a
46 civil penalty of no more than seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00).
47 (3) For the third violation within three years of the first violation, the ALE
48 Division shall impose a civil penalty of no more than one thousand dollars
49 ($1,000) and suspend the retail dealer's license for one year.

20 products by a person under 21 years of age.
21 (a) It is unlawful for any person to give a kratom product to anyone less than 21 years
22 old without the consent of the underaged person's parent or legal guardian.
23 (b) It is unlawful for a person less than 21 years old to purchase or attempt to purchase a
24 kratom product.

26 are sold or consumed, or to obtain or attempt to obtain kratom products, or to obtain or attempt
27 to obtain permission to purchase kratom products, in violation of subsection (b) of this section,
28 by using or attempting to use any of the following:
29 (1) A fraudulent or altered drivers license.
30 (2) A fraudulent or altered identification document other than a drivers license.
31 (3) A drivers license issued to another person.
32 (4) An identification document other than a drivers license issued to another
33 person.

35 person is not prohibited from purchasing or possessing a kratom product under
36 this section.

38 other form of identification of any kind issued or given to the person by any other person who
39 violates or attempts to violate subsection (b) of this section.

41 (1) Any person less than 21 years old who violates this section is guilty of a Class
42 2 misdemeanor.
43 (2) Any person at least 21 years old who violates this section is guilty of a Class
44 1 misdemeanor.
45 (3) Aiding or abetting a violation of this section shall be punished as provided in
46 subdivisions (1) and (2) of this subsection, and all other provisions of this
47 section shall apply to that offense.

7 "§ 18E-201. Licensing requirements; qualifications; duration.
8 (a) Requirement. – Prior to the commencement of business or by July 1, 2026, whichever

12 this section:
13 (1) Manufacturing kratom products.
14 (2) Distributing kratom products.
15 (3) Selling kratom products.
16 (b) Qualifications. – In order to obtain and maintain a license under subsection (a) of this
17 section, a person shall meet all of the following criteria:
18 (1) Be at least 21 years old.
19 (2) Submit to the ALE Division any information determined by the ALE Division
20 to be necessary for the efficient enforcement of this Chapter.
21 (3) Have not been convicted of a felony relating to a controlled substance within
22 10 years in any state or federal jurisdiction.
23 (4) Consent to reasonable inspection by the ALE Division of the inventory of
24 products regulated by this Chapter to ensure compliance with this Chapter and
25 the taking of samples found to not be in compliance with the packaging,
26 labeling, and testing requirements of this section.

28 taxes, interest, and penalties collectable pursuant to G.S. 105-241.22.

34 entity applying for a license for more than one type of business listed in subsection (a) of this
35 section shall pay a single fee as provided in G.S. 18E-202(c).
