GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE RESOLUTION 777
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Federal Relations and American Indian Affairs, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and
Operations of the House
April 7, 2025
1 A HOUSE RESOLUTION URGING THE UNITED STATES CONGRESS TO WORK MORE
2 CLOSELY WITH FRANCE ON TRADE ISSUES AND FOREIGN INVESTMENT,
3 EXPRESSING SUPPORT FOR THE UNITED STATES-FRANCE ALLIANCE,
4 CELEBRATING CENTURIES OF FRIENDSHIP BETWEEN NORTH CAROLINA AND
5 FRANCE, STRENGTHENING FRIENDSHIP AND ECONOMIC TIES BETWEEN THE
6 STATE OF NORTH CAROLINA AND FRANCE, ACKNOWLEDGING THE IMPACT
7 OF FRENCH CULTURE AND CENTURIES OF FRENCH IMMIGRATION ON NORTH
8 CAROLINA, AND REAFFIRMING THE BONDS OF FRIENDSHIP AND
9 COOPERATION BETWEEN THE STATE OF NORTH CAROLINA AND THE FRENCH
10 REPUBLIC.
11 Whereas, North Carolina and France have enjoyed decades of friendly and enduring
12 relations, marked by robust economic partnerships, vibrant cultural ties, and shared values that
13 have strengthened the relations between the two regions; and
14 Whereas, North Carolina has been enriched by French cultural influences, as
15 evidenced by towns such as Bath, Beaufort, New Bern, and La Grange, which bear the legacy of
16 French Huguenots and settlers from the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, and by the city of
17 Fayetteville, named in honor of General Marie-Joseph Paul Yves Roch Gilbert du Motier de La
18 Fayette, known to Americans as the Marquis de Lafayette, a French volunteer and ally in the
19 American Revolution, a steadfast friend of General Washington and America, and recognized as
20 an honorary citizen of the United States of America by the U.S. Congress in 2002; and
21 Whereas, France stands as an important originator of foreign direct investment in
22 North Carolina, with over $429 million invested into North Carolina's economy since 2013; and
23 Whereas, France has played a vital role in job creation in North Carolina across
24 various sectors, with 84 French firms employing over 15,500 North Carolinians, accounting for
25 more than 5% of job creation by foreign firms in North Carolina, thereby contributing
26 significantly to the State's employment and economy; and
27 Whereas, North Carolina has emerged as a premier destination for French investment,
28 with French firms achieving incredible success, marked by 32% growth over the past ten years,
29 reflecting the State's favorable business environment, developed infrastructure, and skilled
30 workforce; and
31 Whereas, trade with France plays an instrumental role in North Carolina's economic
32 activity, enhancing prosperity and growth, and fostering mutually beneficial exchanges of goods,
33 services, and technologies, thereby solidifying France as a vital trading partner and ally; and
*H777-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 Whereas, France is one of North Carolina's largest trading partners, and fourth largest
2 export destination, with more than $3 billion in combined trade annually, with a 34% increase
3 from 2011 to 2021; and
4 Whereas, over 4,565 French immigrants live in North Carolina; and
5 Whereas, France and the United States maintain one of the oldest alliances in history,
6 with France providing decisive support for the United States' independence, negotiated with King
7 Louis XVI and his foreign minister, Charles Gravier, the Comte de Vergennes, by Benjamin
8 Franklin on behalf of the Continental Congress; and
9 Whereas, the United States provided support to France in World War I and World
10 War II against the forces of tyranny, with 2,501 American soldiers dying on the beaches of
11 Normandy on June 6, 1944, alone, 39 of whom were North Carolinians; and
12 Whereas, France and the United States' alliance, from its inception 247 years ago, the
13 longest such relationship between two republics, has been a wellspring for democracy, liberty,
14 and the rights of mankind for the entire world, and a steadfast partnership, contributing to
15 international and regional peace, stability, and prosperity; and
16 Whereas, these democratic principles, upheld by both France and the United States,
17 serving, as they do, as the essential foundations for international order and cooperation, form the
18 bedrock of the enduring friendship between North Carolina and France; and
19 Whereas, North Carolina honors the contributions of French forces, including the
20 Marquis de Lafayette's role, in securing independence for the United States of America, as a
21 foundational part of the U.S.-France alliance; and
22 Whereas, North Carolina remains committed to its friendship with France and to the
23 U.S.-France alliance and peaceful trade between the nations; Now, therefore,
24 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives:
25 SECTION 1. The House of Representatives recognizes the historic significance of
26 the friendship and alliance between the United States and France.
27 SECTION 2. The House of Representatives commends the people of France for their
28 remarkable achievements in promoting democracy, economic prosperity, and international
29 cooperation.
30 SECTION 3. The House of Representatives applauds France for its continued
31 partnership and economic cooperation with the State of North Carolina.
32 SECTION 4. The House of Representatives affirms the steadfast and enduring
33 friendship and cooperation between France and the State of North Carolina and expresses
34 commitment to further strengthening these ties.
35 SECTION 5. The House of Representatives urges Congress to work more closely
36 with France on mutually beneficial trade relations to encourage increased investment and
37 collaboration between French and American companies in the economy of both the United States
38 and France, respectively.
39 SECTION 6. The House of Representatives expresses to Congress full support for
40 the longstanding alliance between the United States of America and France, which remains
41 essential for global security and prosperity.
42 SECTION 7. The Principal Clerk shall transmit a copy of this resolution to the
43 Embassy of France in Washington, D.C., the Honorary Consuls of France to the U.S. Southeast
44 in Atlanta, Georgia, each member of North Carolina's Congressional Delegation, the Clerk of the
45 United States House of Representatives and the Secretary of the United States Senate, and the
46 President of the United States.
47 SECTION 8. This resolution is effective upon adoption.
