11 "§ 90-750. Short title; findings.
12 (a) This Article may be cited as the "Patient Safety in Medical Imaging Act."
13 (b) The General Assembly finds and declares that citizens in this State are entitled to the
14 highest quality of care and maximum protection practicable from the harmful effects of
15 improperly performed medical imaging and radiation therapy procedures and that protection and
16 quality can be increased by requiring appropriate education and licensure of persons operating
17 equipment used for medical imaging and radiation therapy procedures. Therefore, it is essential
18 to establish standards of education for these professionals and to provide for appropriate
19 examination and licensure.

23 "§ 90-752. License required; exemptions.
24 (a) License Required. – Except as provided in this section, only a person currently
25 licensed under this Article may perform or offer to perform radiologic imaging or radiation
26 therapy procedures on humans for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes or otherwise indicate or
27 imply that the person is licensed to perform radiologic imaging or radiation therapy procedures.
28 (b) License Required for Employment. – No person or business entity shall knowingly
29 employ a person who does not hold a license, or is not exempt under this Article, to perform
30 radiologic imaging or radiation therapy procedures on humans for diagnostic or therapeutic
31 purposes.
32 (c) No Limitation or Enlargement of Practice. – Nothing in this Article shall limit or
33 enlarge the practice of a licensed practitioner.
34 (d) Exemptions. – The provisions of this Article shall not apply to the following:
35 (1) A licensed practitioner performing medical imaging procedures or
36 administering radiation therapy.
37 (2) A registered dental hygienist (RDH).
38 (3) A dental assistant I or II who has satisfactorily completed one of the following:
39 a. The North Carolina radiography equivalency examination.
40 b. Dental assisting school through an American Dental
41 Association-accredited program.
42 c. The Dental Assisting National Board examination.
43 d. Classification as a dental assistant II in this State under the laws
44 regulating the practice of dentistry.
45 (4) A resident physician licensed under Article 1 of this Chapter.
46 (5) A student enrolled in and attending a school or college of medicine or medical
47 imaging or radiation therapy who performs medical imaging or radiation
48 therapy procedures on humans while under the supervision of a licensed
49 practitioner, or an individual holding a license under this Article.
50 (6) A person administering radiologic imaging or radiation procedures as an
51 employee of the United States government.

28 "§ 90-754. Medical Imaging and Radiation Therapy Board of Examiners.
29 (a) Composition of Board. – The North Carolina Medical Imaging and Radiation Therapy
30 Board of Examiners is hereby created. The Board shall consist of a maximum of nine members
31 appointed by the Governor and shall serve at the pleasure of the Governor. The initial Board shall
32 be appointed within 90 days following the effective date of this section. The members of the
33 Board shall include:
34 (1) One licensed nuclear medicine technologist.
35 (2) One licensed practitioner who is a radiologist and supervises medical imaging
36 or radiation therapy professionals.
37 (3) One certified radiological or therapeutic physicist.
38 (4) One licensed radiation therapist.
39 (5) One licensed computed tomography technologist or radiographer.
40 (6) One licensed magnetic resonance imaging technologist or licensed
41 sonographer.
42 (7) One radiologist assistant.
43 (8) One retired medical imaging professional as public member.
44 (9) One licensed radiography educator.
45 (b) Board Member Qualifications for Appointment. – In addition to the requirements of
46 subsection (a) of this section, Board members shall meet the following qualifications:
47 (1) Each Board member shall be a resident of the State of North Carolina.
48 (2) The public member shall be at least 21 years of age.
49 (3) Except for the public member and the licensed practitioners, each Board
50 member shall have at least two years of experience performing medical

23 constitutes a quorum.

27 of a family member.
28 (i) Quorum. – A majority of the voting members of the Board shall constitute a quorum.
29 No action may be taken by the Board except by affirmative vote of the majority of those present
30 and voting.

31 "§ 90-759. Educational programs approved by the Board.

33 or radiation therapy shall (i) be offered by a medical facility or educational institution and (ii) be
34 affiliated with one or more hospitals or clinics to provide the requisite clinical education.

36 be no less stringent than the standards approved by the Joint Review Committee on Education in
37 Radiologic Technology, the Joint Review Committee on Education in Nuclear Medicine
38 Technology, the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs, or other
39 educational accreditation agency approved by the Board.
40 "§ 90-760. License renewal; reciprocity.
41 (a) Every license issued under this Article shall expire on the last day of the licensee's
42 month of birth in the second year following the year of issuance. A licensee who fails to renew a
43 license prior to the expiration date shall submit an application for reinstatement to obtain
44 licensure. The license shall be renewed upon the payment of a renewal fee, as provided in
45 G.S. 90-761, if, at the time of application for renewal, the applicant is not in violation of this
46 Article and has complied with any continuing education requirements pursuant to rules adopted
47 by the Board.
48 (b) The Board shall notify a licensee at least 60 days in advance of the expiration of his
49 or her license. The licensee shall inform the Board of any change of the licensee's address. Each
50 licensee is responsible for renewing his or her license before the expiration date. Licenses that
51 are not renewed automatically lapse.

14 (a) Issuance of License. – The Board shall issue a license to an applicant who meets the
15 requirements for licensure specified in this Article and the regulations adopted by the Board,
16 verified by oath or affirmation, upon payment of a fee as provided in G.S. 90-761.

31 "§ 90-759. Educational programs approved by the Board.
32 (a) In order to obtain approval by the Board, educational programs in medical imaging
33 or radiation therapy shall (i) be offered by a medical facility or educational institution and (ii) be

40 "§ 90-760. License renewal; reciprocity.
41 (a) Every license issued under this Article shall expire on the last day of the licensee's
42 month of birth in the second year following the year of issuance. A licensee who fails to renew a
43 license prior to the expiration date shall submit an application for reinstatement to obtain
44 licensure. The license shall be renewed upon the payment of a renewal fee, as provided in
45 G.S. 90-761, if, at the time of application for renewal, the applicant is not in violation of this
46 Article and has complied with any continuing education requirements pursuant to rules adopted
47 by the Board.
48 (b) The Board shall notify a licensee at least 60 days in advance of the expiration of his
49 or her license. The licensee shall inform the Board of any change of the licensee's address. Each
50 licensee is responsible for renewing his or her license before the expiration date. Licenses that
51 are not renewed automatically lapse.

23 "§ 90-762. Disciplinary authority.
24 (a) The Board may deny, suspend, revoke, or refuse to renew a license or impose
25 probationary conditions on a license if the licensee or applicant for licensure has engaged in any
26 of the following conduct:
27 (1) Obtaining a license by means of fraud, misrepresentation, or concealment of
28 material facts.
29 (2) Engaging in unprofessional conduct pursuant to rules adopted by the Board.
30 (3) Having been convicted of or pleaded guilty or nolo contendere to a crime
31 involving moral turpitude or any crime that indicates that the licensee or
32 applicant is unfit or incompetent to administer medical imaging or radiation
33 therapy procedures or that the licensee or applicant has deceived or defrauded
34 the public.
35 (4) Engaging in any act or practice in violation of any of the provisions of this
36 Article or any rule adopted by the Board or aiding, abetting, or assisting any
37 person in a violation.
38 (5) Committing an act or acts of malpractice, gross negligence, or incompetence
39 in administering medical imaging or radiation therapy procedures.
40 (6) Practicing as a person licensed to administer medical imaging or radiation
41 therapy procedures without a current license.
42 (7) Engaging in conduct that could result in harm or injury to the public.
43 (8) Being unfit or incompetent to administer medical imaging or radiation therapy
44 services by reason of deliberate or negligent acts or omissions, regardless of
45 whether actual injury to a patient is established.
46 (b) The denial, refusal to renew, suspension, revocation, or imposition of probationary
47 conditions upon a license may be ordered by the Board after a hearing held in accordance with
48 Chapter 150B of the General Statutes and rules adopted by the Board. An application may be
49 made to the Board for reinstatement of a revoked license if the revocation has been in effect for
50 at least two years after the date of the Board's order revoking the license.
