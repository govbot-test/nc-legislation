17 SECTION 3. Part 1 of Article 36 of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended
18 by adding a new section to read:
19 "§ 143-341.3. Disposal of surplus real property.
20 (a) Surplus Real Property Disposal. – The Department of Administration shall establish
21 a surplus real property disposal system that limits the duration that unneeded or vacant property
22 is retained by the State. As part of the system, the Department shall (i) adopt rules in accordance
23 with this section, including defining surplus State-owned real property, and (ii) establish a system
24 for continuously identifying and disposing of that property, subject to the approvals required
25 under Chapter 146 of the General Statutes, which shall take into consideration all of the
26 following:
27 (1) The value each facility or parcel of land brings to the performance of the
28 mission of the State or State agency and the fulfillment of its goals and
29 objectives.
30 (2) A general measure of the facility's condition calculated as a ratio of repair
31 needs to replacement value.
32 (3) The degree to which the property is utilized, measured in accordance with the
33 procedures developed pursuant to G.S. 143-341.2(a)(3).
34 (4) The extent to which the property meets the purpose for which it was intended.
35 (5) The extent to which the State or State agency is likely to need to continue to
36 provide the service or function currently provided at the property.
37 (6) Consideration of the best and most cost-effective manner in which these future
38 needs can be met.
39 (7) Whether the property has been vacant for one year or longer.
40 (8) An assessment of private or public interest in the property.
41 (b) Method for Disposal. – For properties identified as surplus pursuant to this section,
42 the Department of Administration shall, subject to the approvals required under Chapter 146 of
43 the General Statutes, dispose of the surplus real property by sale. If the Department of
44 Administration is unable to dispose of the real property by sale for a period of six months, the
45 Department shall dispose of the property by auction or by transfer of title to a city or county in
46 this State, provided that the city or county presents evidence satisfactory to the Department of an
47 economic development use for the property.
48 (c) Proceeds of Sale. – Notwithstanding G.S. 146-15, G.S. 146-30, or any other provision
49 of law, the gross proceeds of the sale of surplus real property under this section shall be handled
50 in accordance with the following priority:

2 allowed under rules and regulations adopted by the Governor and approved
