1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REINSTATE EDUCATION-BASED SALARY SUPPLEMENTS FOR

7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8
9 PART I. REINSTATE EDUCATION-BASED SALARY SUPPLEMENTS FOR
10 TEACHERS AND INSTRUCTIONAL SUPPORT PERSONNEL, INCLUDING SCHOOL
11 SOCIAL WORKERS
12 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 115C-302.10 is repealed.
13 SECTION 1.(b) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, for the 2025-2026
14 fiscal year, State Board of Education policy TCP-A-006, as it was in effect on June 30, 2013,
15 shall be used to determine (i) whether teachers and instructional support personnel, including
16 school social workers, are paid on the "M" salary schedule and (ii) whether they receive a salary
17 supplement for academic preparation at the six-year or doctoral degree level.
18 SECTION 1.(c) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
19 Public Instruction the sum of eight million dollars ($8,000,000) in recurring funds for the
20 2025-2026 fiscal year to reinstate education-based salary supplements for teachers and
21 instructional support personnel, including school social workers, in accordance with this section.
22
23 PART II. PUBLICIZE MINIMUM SALARY SCHEDULES FOR OCCUPATIONAL
24 THERAPISTS AND PHYSICAL THERAPISTS
25 SECTION 2. G.S. 115C-316(b1) reads as rewritten:
26 "(b1) Every local board of education shall adopt a minimum salary schedule for
27 occupational therapists and physical therapists employed in full-time, permanent positions. The
28 minimum salary schedule shall apply to positions paid from State, local, or federal funds. In
29 accordance with the noncertified salary grades and ranges adopted by the State Board of
30 Education, the minimum salary schedule shall differentiate salaries based on years of experience,
31 but experience-based intervals shall be no greater than five years. Local boards of education may
32 compensate occupational therapists and physical therapists above the minimum salary schedule
33 provided all State-funded salaries are within the noncertified salary grades and ranges adopted
34 by the State Board of Education. No later than October 15 of each year, every local board of
