8 PART I. JOINT LEGISLATIVE STUDY COMMITTEE ON INFRASTRUCTURE AT
9 HISTORICALLY BLACK COLLEGES AND UNIVERSITIES
10 SECTION 1.(a) There is created the Joint Legislative Study Committee on
11 Infrastructure (Committee) at Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs).
12 SECTION 1.(b) The Committee shall consist of nine members of the Senate
13 appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the Senate and nine members of the House of
14 Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives. The President Pro
15 Tempore of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall each appoint a
16 cochair of the Committee from among its membership. These appointments shall be made no
17 later than September 1, 2026.
18 It is expected that the makeup of the Committee reflect geographic and urban/rural
19 diversity. At least one member of the House of Representatives and at least one member of the
20 Senate shall be from the minority party of their respective chambers.
21 SECTION 1.(c) In consultation with the Board of Governors of The University of
22 North Carolina and the president or equivalent officer of each HBCU in this State, the Committee
23 shall study all of the following related to buildings on HBCU campuses in the State:
24 (1) Threats posed to HBCUs by the proximity of certain facilities to toxic sites.
25 (2) Vulnerability of facilities to natural disasters and environmental risks.
26 (3) Age of facilities.
27 (4) Student enrollment in relation to classroom capacity.
28 (5) The condition of major systems, including heating, ventilation, air
29 conditioning, electrical, water, and sewer.
30 (6) The condition of exterior components of facilities, including roofing,
31 windows, and doors.
32 (7) Critical health of and other safety conditions of campus buildings.
33 (8) Facilities in significant disrepair and any deferred maintenance on those
34 facilities.
35 (9) Any other issue the Committee considers relevant.

26 "§ 116-44.9. Completion Assistance Programs.
27 (a) Definition. – For purposes of this section, the term "eligible constituent institutions"
28 refers to the following constituent institutions of The University of North Carolina:
29 (1) Elizabeth City State University.

38 criteria:
39 (1) A student enrolled in a Program established by this section may receive up to

49 d. Has completed or is on track to complete at least 60 academic credit
50 hours by the end of the semester in which the funds are provided.

15 "§ 116-209.67. Terms of forgivable loans; receipt and disbursement of funds.
16 (a) Notes. – All forgivable loans shall be evidenced by notes made payable to the
17 Authority that bear interest at a rate not to exceed ten percent (10%) per year as set by the
18 Authority and beginning on the first day of September after completion of the eligible nursing
19 program or 90 days after graduation, whichever is later. If a forgivable loan is terminated, the
20 note shall be made payable to the Authority 90 days after termination of the forgivable loan. The
21 forgivable loan may be terminated upon the recipient's withdrawal from the Program or by the
22 recipient's failure to meet the standards set by the Commission.
23 (b) Forgiveness. – For every year a qualifying nurse remains a qualifying nurse, the
24 Authority shall forgive the loan amount received over one year of enrollment in an eligible
25 nursing program and any interest accrued on that amount. The Authority shall also forgive the
26 loan if it finds that it is impossible for the recipient to work for up to eight years, within 10 years
27 after completion of the eligible nursing program, as a nurse in the State because of the death or
28 permanent disability of the recipient. If the recipient repays the forgivable loan by cash payments,
29 all indebtedness shall be repaid within 10 years after completion of the eligible nursing program
30 supported by the forgivable loan. If the recipient completes an eligible nursing program, payment
31 of principal and interest shall begin no later than the first day of September after the completion
32 of the program. Should a recipient present extenuating circumstances, the Authority may extend
33 the period to repay the loan in cash to no more than a total of 12 years."

17 SECTION 11. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Office of State
18 Budget and Management the following sums in nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year
19 to be allocated to Bennett College:
20 (1) The sum of one million dollars ($1,000,000) as a directed grant for deferred
21 facility maintenance needs, including maintenance of residence halls.
22 (2) The sum of one million five hundred thousand dollars ($1,500,000) to support
23 the education degree program at that private postsecondary institution.

25 PART XII. FUNDS FOR LIVINGSTONE COLLEGE
26 SECTION 12. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Office of State
27 Budget and Management the following sums in nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year
28 to be allocated to Livingstone College:
29 (1) Two million dollars ($2,000,000) to support the college.

33 PART XIII. FUNDS FOR SAINT AUGUSTINE'S UNIVERSITY
34 SECTION 13.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Office of State
35 Budget and Management the following sums in nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year
36 to be allocated to Saint Augustine's University:
37 (1) Ten million dollars ($10,000,000) for a completion assistance grant program
38 to address gaps in degree completion rates for students.
39 (2) Twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) for the construction of a new
40 academic building for collaborative learning and innovation.
41 SECTION 13.(b) It is the intent of the General Assembly to annually appropriate a
42 portion of available funds in the Education Lottery Fund in G.S. 18C-164(a) to support
43 scholarships for needy students enrolled at Saint Augustine's University.

45 PART XIV. EFFECTIVE DATE
