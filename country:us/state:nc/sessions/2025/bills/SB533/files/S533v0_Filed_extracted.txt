18 SECTION 3.2.(a) From the tax proceeds credited to the Department of Health and
19 Human Services pursuant to G.S. 105-113.128, as amended by this act, the Department of Health
20 and Human Services shall do all of the following:
21 (1) Develop a statewide public awareness campaign about gambling addiction. In
22 developing this campaign, the Department shall consult with addiction
23 specialists and individuals who have lived experience with gambling
24 addiction to (i) develop materials and initiatives to raise public awareness
25 about the risks, potential harms, and addictive nature of gambling and (ii)
26 promote resources for the prevention and treatment of gambling addiction.
27 The statewide public awareness campaign shall include at least all of the
28 following components:
29 a. Factual, evidence-based information about gambling addiction risks
30 and warning signs.
31 b. Specific messaging about youth gambling and digital gambling
32 platforms.
33 c. Real stories and testimonials from North Carolinians affected by
34 gambling addiction.
35 d. Clear information about how to access treatment and support services
36 for gambling addiction.
37 e. Availability of information in multiple languages reflecting North
38 Carolina's diverse population.
39 f. Targeted messaging for high-risk populations as identified by current
40 research.
41 g. Utilization of multiple media channels, including social media,
42 television, radio, and community-based outreach.
43 (2) Establish and administer a grant program that awards directed grants on a
44 competitive basis to nonprofit organizations to implement or expand
45 evidence-based gambling prevention and treatment programs. The
46 Department of Health and Human Services shall develop an application
47 process and eligibility criteria for the grant program authorized by this
48 subdivision.
49 (3) Expand access to treatment programs and services for individuals and families
50 affected by gambling addiction.
