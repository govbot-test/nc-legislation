7 SECTION 1.(a) Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
8 adding a new Part to read:
9 "Part 1C. Mathematics Instruction.
10 "§ 115C-83.20. North Carolina mathematics.
11 The General Assembly intends that all students become proficient in grade-level mathematics
12 as defined by the standard course of study in order to prepare to take and pass NC Math 1 and
13 the related end-of-course assessment and ensure they are college and career ready. To attain these
14 goals, all students should have access to high-quality systems of mathematics instruction that
15 include mathematics facts, procedural fluency, conceptual learning, and problem solving. These
16 systems should be based on the best evidence on how students learn mathematics and include
17 core instructional materials and professional development.
18 "§ 115C-83.22. Definitions.
19 The following definitions apply in this Part:
20 (1) Explicit instruction. – A form of high-quality instruction in which, for some
21 portion of a lesson or intervention, the teacher provides clear modeling,
22 think-alouds, worked examples, practice, and timely corrective feedback
23 directly to students.
24 (2) Mathematical deficiency. – When a student does not meet the minimum
25 mathematical skills for their grade level as defined by the standard course of
26 study. These skills include at least: one-to-one correspondence, cardinality,
27 number sense, counting, and the four basic operations.
28 (3) Mathematical discourse. – Opportunities for students to reason
29 mathematically and discuss with their peers and teacher how they are thinking
30 about mathematics, including use of math-specific vocabulary, procedural
31 steps, concepts, and problem-solving strategies.
32 (4) Mathematical proficiency. – A demonstrated understanding of a mathematical
33 standard, which serves as foundational prior knowledge on which to build new
34 learning.

1 (5) Responsive feedback. – Immediate, interactive feedback made possible by
2 technology that shows students the mathematical meaning of their thinking
3 and guides them to develop stronger conceptual understanding, procedural

8 instruction to systematically build on students' prior knowledge by combining
9 conceptual understanding, procedural fluency, and application in a
10 pedagogically coherent sequence.
11 "§ 115C-83.24. Department of Public Instruction mathematics supports.
12 The Department shall provide a system of support based on high-quality mathematics
13 instructional systems and a structured approach to problem-based learning for all students in
14 kindergarten through grade eight and teachers of students in those grades. The system shall,
15 where appropriate and reasonable, leverage technology to engage students and provide them with
16 responsive feedback, while also providing teachers with actionable instructional insights. The
17 system of support shall include the following:
18 (1) An approved list of one or more high-quality mathematics assessment and
19 support systems that meet all of the following:
20 a. Include assessments that are valid and reliable.
21 b. Measure a number of age-appropriate skills that include one-to-one
22 correspondence, cardinality, number sense, counting, the four basic
23 operations, addition and multiplication facts, measurement, fractions,
24 and geometry.
25 c. Identify students who have a mathematical deficiency, including
26 identifying students with characteristics of dyscalculia.
27 d. Identify the areas of mathematical proficiency that each student has so
28 teachers can build on what students already understand to inform
29 differentiated instruction and appropriate interventions.
30 e. Include a system of parent or guardian notifications that will describe,
31 in understandable language to the parent or guardian, the nature of a
32 student's mathematics deficiency and areas of mathematical
33 proficiency on which to build no later than 15 calendar days after the
34 identification of this deficiency. The notification will include the
35 current services being provided to the student, proposed interventions
36 and materials to address the deficiency, and strategies for parents or
37 guardians to use at home.
38 f. Are supported by research that has demonstrated positive student
39 outcomes.
40 (2) An approved list of one or more high-quality mathematics instructional
41 systems that meet all of the following:
42 a. Include both core curriculum and supplemental materials.
43 b. Are based on the best evidence of how students learn mathematics and
44 are supported by research that has demonstrated positive student
45 outcomes.
46 c. Use a coherent progression of topics, skills, and approaches to learning
47 mathematics which include a mix of explicit instruction, practice,
48 conceptual reasoning, and problem solving. These strategies and
49 materials simultaneously develop students' conceptual understanding,
50 procedural fluency, ability to apply concepts, and problem-solving
51 skills.

26 "§ 115C-83.26. Local school administrative unit requirements.
27 Local school administrative units shall select and implement all of the following from the
28 approved lists adopted by the Department of Public Instruction pursuant to this Part:
29 (1) A high-quality mathematics assessment and support system for screening and
30 progress monitoring toward grade-level mathematics.
31 (2) A high-quality mathematics instructional system to be used for mathematics
32 support. Each student in kindergarten through grade eight shall be provided
33 an appropriate mathematics intervention to address their specific needs.
34 (3) High-quality professional learning offerings for mathematics teachers in
35 kindergarten through grade eight."
36 SECTION 1.(b) The Department of Public Instruction shall report to the Joint
37 Legislative Education Oversight Committee prior to the implementation of the first mathematics
38 standards adopted by the State Board of Education after the effective date of this act. The report
39 shall include the following:
40 (1) A summary of the mathematics standards adopted by the State Board.
41 (2) Mathematics supports to be provided by the Department pursuant to
42 G.S. 115C-83.24, as enacted by this act.
43 (3) Any other information the Department deems relevant.
44 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning with
45 the 2026-2027 school year.
