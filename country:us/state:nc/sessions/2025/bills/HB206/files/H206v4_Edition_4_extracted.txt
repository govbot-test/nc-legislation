16 ADJUTANT GENERAL CONFIRMATION
17 SECTION 8.(a) G.S. 127A-19 reads as rewritten:
18 "§ 127A-19. Adjutant General.
19 (a) The military head of the militia shall be the Adjutant General who shall hold the rank
20 of major general with federal recognition at time of appointment or attain the rank of major
21 general pursuant to this section. The Adjutant General shall be appointed by the Governor in the
22 Governor's capacity as commander in chief of the militia, in consultation with the Secretary of
23 Public Safety, and shall be subject to confirmation by the General Assembly by joint resolution.
24 The Governor shall submit the name of the person to be appointed, for confirmation by the
25 General Assembly, to the General Assembly by May 1 of the year in which the Adjutant General
26 is to be appointed. If the Governor does not submit the name by that date, the President Pro
27 Tempore of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall submit a name to
28 the General Assembly on or before May 15 of the same year. The appointment shall then be made
29 by enactment of a bill. The bill shall state the name of the person being appointed, the office to
30 which the appointment is being made, the residence of the appointee, and that the appointment
31 is being made upon the joint recommendation of the Speaker of the House of Representatives
32 and the President Pro Tempore of the Senate. If there is no vacancy in the office of the Adjutant
33 General and a bill that would confirm the appointment of the person as Adjutant General fails a
34 reading in either chamber of the General Assembly, then the Governor shall submit a new name
35 within 30 days.
36 Following appointment pursuant to this section, the Adjutant General shall serve at the
37 pleasure of the Governor. The Adjutant General, while holding this office, shall be a member of
38 the active North Carolina National Guard. If an appointed Adjutant General does not attain the
39 rank of major general with federal recognition within a reasonable period of time from the date
40 of appointment, the Governor shall replace the Adjutant General with an appointee who meets
41 the criteria in in, and is appointed in accordance with, this section. A "reasonable period of time"
42 shall take into account time in grade requirements for promotion or promotions and
43 administrative periods necessary to complete the promotion process.
44 (a1) In case of a vacancy in the office of the Adjutant General, the name of the Adjutant
45 General's successor shall be submitted by the Governor to the General Assembly not later than
46 60 days after the vacancy arises. If a vacancy arises in the office when the General Assembly is
47 not in session, an acting Adjutant General shall be appointed by the Governor to serve pending
48 confirmation by the General Assembly. However, in no event shall an acting Adjutant General
49 serve (i) for more than 12 months without General Assembly confirmation or (ii) after a bill that
50 would confirm the appointment of the person as Adjutant General fails a reading in either
51 chamber of the General Assembly.
