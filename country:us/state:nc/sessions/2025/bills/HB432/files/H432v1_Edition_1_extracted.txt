15 SECTION 3. The Department of Revenue (Department) shall study ways to abolish
16 the statutory framework for the listing, appraisal, and assessment of real property under Chapter
17 105 of the General Statutes and shall develop a framework to eliminate property taxes on real
18 property in this State. The Department shall develop a framework to replace property tax
19 revenues through State and local budget reductions, sales-based consumption taxes, and locally
20 determined consumption taxes. The study must include, at a minimum, the following
21 information:
22 (1) An analysis of the potential impact of eliminating property taxes on public
23 services, including education, infrastructure, and emergency services.
24 (2) An assessment of potential housing market fluctuations, including changes in
25 homeownership rates and property values.
26 (3) An evaluation of whether a shift to consumption-based taxes would make
27 North Carolina more attractive to businesses compared to other states.
28 (4) An analysis of the potential impact of eliminating property taxes on overall
29 economic stability, consumer behavior, and long-term economic growth.
30 The Department may consult with any other relevant State, local, or private entity in
31 conducting the study required by this section. The Department shall report its findings to the Joint
32 Legislative Oversight Committee on General Government by February 1, 2026.

34 beginning on or after July 1, 2026. Section 2 of this act is effective beginning October 1, 2025,
35 and applies to judgments entered against a debtor on or after that date. The remainder of this act
36 is effective when it becomes law.
