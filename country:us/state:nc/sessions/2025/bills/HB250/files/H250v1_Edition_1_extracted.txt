GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 250
Short Title: Waive Annual Report/Fee For Deployed Military. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Carver and Campbell (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Commerce and Economic Development, if favorable, Finance, if favorable, Rules,
Calendar, and Operations of the House
March 3, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO WAIVE THE ANNUAL REPORT REQUIREMENT AND FEE FOR BUSINESS
3 ENTITIES OWNED BY DEPLOYED MEMBERS OF THE ARMED FORCES.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 55-16-22(a) reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 55-16-22. Annual report.
7 (a) Requirement. – Except as provided in G.S. 55-16-22.3 and in subsections (a1) and
8 (a2) of this section, each domestic corporation and each foreign corporation authorized to transact
9 business in this State shall deliver an annual report directly to the Secretary of State in electronic
10 form or in paper form as prescribed by the Secretary of State under this section."
11 SECTION 1.(b) Article 16 of Chapter 55 of the General Statutes is amended by
12 adding a new section to read:
13 "§ 55-16-22.3. Exemptions for corporations owned by deployed members of the Armed
14 Forces.
15 (a) For the purposes of this section, a deployed member of the Armed Forces is a member
16 of the United States Air Force, Army, Coast Guard, Marine Corps, or Navy, or a member of any
17 reserve component, who is removed from his or her county of residence pursuant to an official
18 order for a deployment period that ends on or after the ninetieth day preceding the due date of
19 the annual report required by G.S. 55-16-22.
20 (b) Notwithstanding G.S. 55-16-22, an annual report is deemed timely filed if it is filed
21 by a domestic or foreign corporation (i) in which more than fifty percent (50%) of the ownership
22 interest is owned by one or more deployed members of the Armed Forces and (ii) within 90 days
23 of the end of the deployment period. The following provisions apply:
24 (1) Prior to the start of the deployment, the corporation shall file notice of
25 deployment with the Secretary of State in electronic or paper form as
26 prescribed by the Secretary of State. The notice shall include the following:
27 a. The name of the corporation and the state under whose law it is
28 incorporated.
29 b. A copy of the orders for deployment or written verification from the
30 commanding officer.
31 c. A statement either certifying that the information contained in the most
32 recently filed annual report has not changed or setting forth the
33 updated information required by G.S. 55-16-22(a3)(2) through (5).

1 (2) The due date of the corporation's next annual report is the ninetieth business
2 day following the end of the deployment. If the deployment is extended, the
3 corporation's annual report is due the ninetieth business day following the end
4 of the extension period. At the time of filing, the corporation may submit a
5 copy of orders or written verification from the commanding officer of the
6 extension period, and the report is retroactively deemed timely filed.
7 (3) The grounds for dissolution under G.S. 55-14-20 apply to corporations that
8 are subject to this section only if the period of delinquency for the applicable
9 ground is 180 days or more past the end of the deployment period indicated
10 in the documentation provided to the Secretary of State under this section.
11 (c) Any fees required by G.S. 55-1-22 for documents filed under subdivision (b)(1) of
12 this section are waived. Additionally, the fee required by G.S. 55-1-22 for the annual report due
13 on the ninetieth business day following the end of deployment under subdivision (b)(2) of this
14 section is waived."

44 SECTION 2.(b) Article 2 of Chapter 57D of the General Statutes is amended by
45 adding a new section to read:
46 "§ 57D-2-26. Exemptions for LLCs owned by deployed members of the Armed Forces.
47 (a) For the purposes of this section, a deployed member of the Armed Forces is a member
48 of the United States Air Force, Army, Coast Guard, Marine Corps, or Navy, or a member of any
49 reserve component, who is removed from his or her county of residence pursuant to an official
50 order for a deployment period that ends on or after January 15 of the year in which the annual
51 report is due under G.S. 57D-2-24.

1 (b) Notwithstanding G.S. 57D-2-24, an annual report is deemed timely filed if it is filed
2 by an LLC or foreign LLC (i) in which more than fifty percent (50%) of the ownership interest
3 is owned by one or more deployed members of the Armed Forces and (ii) by April 15 of the year
4 immediately following the end of the deployment period. The following provisions apply:
5 (1) Prior to the start of the deployment, the LLC or foreign LLC shall file notice
6 of deployment with the Secretary of State in electronic or paper form as
7 prescribed by the Secretary of State. The notice shall include the following:
8 a. The name of the LLC or foreign LLC and, for a foreign LLC, any
9 different name under which it is authorized to transact business in this

11 b. A copy of the orders for deployment or written verification from the

14 recently filed annual report has not changed or setting forth the
15 updated information required by G.S. 57D-2-24.
16 (2) The due date of the LLC's or foreign LLC's next annual report is April 15 of
17 the year immediately following the end of the deployment.
18 (3) If the extension of a deployment results in the annual report being deemed
19 delinquent, the LLC or foreign LLC may submit a copy of orders or written
20 verification from the commanding officer of the original deployment and
21 extension period at the time the annual report is submitted. The report is
22 retroactively deemed timely filed, and any adverse action taken by the
23 Secretary of State shall be reversed.

28 section.

30 this section are waived. Additionally, the fee required by G.S. 57D-1-22 for the annual report
31 due April 15 of the year immediately following the end of deployment under subdivision (b)(2)
32 of this section is waived."

2 SECTION 3.(b) Article 3B of Chapter 59 of the General Statutes is amended by
3 adding a new section to read:
4 "§ 59-84.6. Exemptions for limited liability partnerships owned by deployed members of
5 the Armed Forces.
6 (a) For the purposes of this section, a deployed member of the Armed Forces is a member
7 of the United States Air Force, Army, Coast Guard, Marine Corps, or Navy, or a member of any
8 reserve component, who is removed from his or her county of residence pursuant to an official
9 order for a deployment period that ends on or after the ninetieth day preceding the due date of
10 the annual report required by G.S. 59-84.4.
11 (b) Notwithstanding G.S. 59-84.4, an annual report is deemed timely filed if it is filed by
12 a registered or foreign limited liability partnership (i) in which more than fifty percent (50%) of
13 the ownership interest is owned by one or more deployed members of the Armed Forces and (ii)
14 within 90 days of the end of the deployment period. The following provisions apply:
15 (1) Prior to the start of the deployment, the registered or foreign limited liability
16 partnership shall file notice of deployment with the Secretary of State in
17 electronic or paper form as prescribed by the Secretary of State. The notice
18 shall include the following:
19 a. The name of the registered or foreign limited liability partnership and
20 the state under whose law it is incorporated.
21 b. A copy of the orders for deployment or written verification from the
22 commanding officer.
23 c. A statement either certifying that the information contained in the most
24 recently filed annual report has not changed or setting forth the
25 updated information required by G.S. 59-84.4(a)(2) through (5).
26 (2) The due date of the registered or foreign limited liability partnership's next
27 annual report is the ninetieth business day following the end of the
28 deployment. If the deployment is extended, the corporation's annual report is
29 due the ninetieth business day following the end of the extension period. At
30 the time of filing, the corporation may submit a copy of orders or written
31 verification from the commanding officer of the extension period, and the
32 annual report is retroactively deemed timely filed.
33 (3) The grounds for revocation of registration under G.S. 59-84.4(f) apply to
34 registered and foreign limited liability partnerships that are subject to this
35 section only if the period of delinquency for the applicable ground is 180 days
36 or more past the end of the deployment period indicated in the documentation
37 provided to the Secretary of State under this section.
38 (c) Any fees required by G.S. 59-35.2 for documents filed under subdivision (b)(1) of
39 this section are waived. Additionally, the fee required by G.S. 59-35.2 for the annual report due
40 on the ninetieth business day following the end of deployment under subdivision (b)(2) of this
41 section is waived."
42 SECTION 4. The Secretary of State shall make available the form or forms needed
43 for the notice of deployment required by this act and shall take any other action necessary to
44 allow business entities to begin filing pursuant to this act on October 1, 2025.
45 SECTION 5. Sections 1 through 3 of this act become effective October 1, 2025. The
46 remainder of this act is effective when it becomes law.
