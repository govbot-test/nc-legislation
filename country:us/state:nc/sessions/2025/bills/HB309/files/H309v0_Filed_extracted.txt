1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO DIRECT THE RESIDENTIAL CODE COUNCIL AND BUILDING CODE
3 COUNCIL TO CREATE A FAMILY CHILD CARE HOME OCCUPANCY
4 CLASSIFICATION WITHIN THE NORTH CAROLINA STATE BUILDING CODE.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) Definitions. – For the purposes of this section, the following
7 definitions apply:
8 (1) Code. – The North Carolina State Building Code, and amendments to the
9 Code, as adopted by the Councils.
10 (2) Councils. – The Residential Code Council and the Building Code Council.
11 (3) Family child care home. – As described in G.S. 110-86(3)b.
12 SECTION 1.(b) Family Child Care Home. – Until the effective date of the rules to
13 create a family child care home occupancy classification within a dwelling subject to the North
14 Carolina Residential Code, the Office of the State Fire Marshal, the Councils, and State and local
15 governments enforcing the Code shall adhere to family child care home requirements as provided
16 in subsection (c) of this section.
17 SECTION 1.(c) Implementation. – Notwithstanding Section 310, Residential Group
18 R, of the North Carolina Building Code, and Section 203.10, Residential Group R, of the North
19 Carolina Fire Code, a family child care home located within a dwelling subject to the North
20 Carolina Residential Code shall be treated as a Residential Group R-3 occupancy. The building
21 where the family child care home is located shall be permitted to comply with the North Carolina
22 Residential Code, provided that a family child care home must meet only the following additional
23 requirements:
24 (1) Rooms and areas within a family child care home where occupants receive
25 care shall be on the same level of exit discharge.
26 (2) Rooms and areas within a family child care home where occupants receive
27 care shall be located on the same level with, and within a maximum of 40 feet
28 travel distance to, at least one 2A:10B:C fire extinguisher.
29 (3) A family child care home shall have and maintain a Fire Safety, Evacuation,
30 and Lockdown Plan compliant with Section 404 of the North Carolina Fire
31 Code.
32 (4) A family child care home shall have carbon monoxide alarm and detection
33 systems compliant with Section R315 of the North Carolina Residential Code.
34 (5) A family child care home shall have smoke alarms compliant with Section
35 R314 of the North Carolina Residential Code.
