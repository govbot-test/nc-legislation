7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8
9 PART I. DISASTER FOCUSED CONSTITUENT PORTAL
10 SECTION 1.1. The Department of Information Technology shall issue a request for
11 proposals for the development and creation of a disaster relief portal, focused on constituent
12 engagement, that will operate as a central platform for relevant updates and constituent services
13 in response to natural disasters occurring in the State. The disaster focused constituent
14 engagement portal shall be designed to consolidate critical information and programs from all
15 relevant State agency websites into a single, centralized portal where affected citizens can engage
16 with State agencies, find resources, and receive real-time information.
17 SECTION 1.2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
18 Information Technology the sum of five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) in nonrecurring
19 funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used for the purposes described in this Part.
20 SECTION 1.3. This Part becomes effective July 1, 2025.
21
22 PART II. CONTRACTOR LIABILITY LIMITS/STATE CONTRACTS
23 SECTION 2.1. Article 3 of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended by
24 adding a new section to read:
25 "§ 143-49.2. Contractor liability in State contracts.
26 (a) All contracts subject to the provisions of this Article and Article 8 of this Chapter
27 shall include a limitation on the contractor's liability to the State for damages. Except as otherwise
28 provided in this section, the limitation of liability shall be for damages arising from any cause
29 whatsoever, regardless of the form of action. The amount of liability shall be determined based
30 on the nature of the goods, services, or construction work covered by the contract; however, there
31 shall be a presumptive limitation of no more than two times the value of the contract. Limitation
32 of liability pursuant to this section shall specifically include, but not be limited to, the contractor's
33 liability for damages. The amount of liability for damages may be raised to no more than three
34 times the value of the contract if all of the following apply:

1 (1) The Secretary or the Secretary's designee completes a risk assessment prior to
2 the bid solicitation or request for proposals that determines an increase in the
3 liability amount is necessary to protect the State's best interests.
4 (2) The bid solicitation or request for proposals indicates that increased liability
5 will be required for the resulting contract.
6 (b) Prior to entering into any contract subject to the provisions of this Article or Article
7 8 of this Chapter, the Secretary or the Secretary's designee shall reasonably determine that the
8 contractor possesses sufficient financial resources, either independently or through third-party
9 sources, such as insurance, to satisfy the agreed upon limitation of liability. The limitation of
10 liability required by this section shall not apply to liability of the contractor for intentional or
11 willful misconduct, damage to tangible personal property, or physical injuries to persons.
12 Nothing in this subsection limits the contractor's liability directly to third parties or affects the
13 rights and obligations related to contribution among joint tortfeasors established by Chapter 1B
14 of the General Statutes and other applicable law.
15 (c) Not later than March 1 of each year, the Secretary shall report annually to the Joint
16 Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations and the Joint Legislative Oversight
17 Committee on General Government regarding the contracts containing liability amounts of more
18 than two times the value of the contract."
19 SECTION 2.2. This Part is effective when it becomes law and applies to contracts
20 awarded, renewed, or amended on or after that date.
21
22 PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
23 SECTION 3.1. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
24 law.
