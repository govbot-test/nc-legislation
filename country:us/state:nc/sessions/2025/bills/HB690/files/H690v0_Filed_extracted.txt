1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT DIRECTING THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES, THE
3 DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, THE HOUSING FINANCE AGENCY, AND LOCAL
4 HOUSING AUTHORITIES TO DEVELOP, IMPLEMENT, AND REPORT ON A PLAN
5 TO ENSURE THAT STATE-FUNDED BENEFITS PROVIDED BY OR THROUGH
6 THESE STATE AND LOCAL AGENCIES ARE AVAILABLE ONLY TO UNITED
7 STATES CITIZENS AND NONCITIZENS RESIDING IN THE UNITED STATES WITH
8 LEGAL PERMISSION; DIRECTING STATE-FUNDED INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER
9 EDUCATION TO ADOPT AND IMPLEMENT A POLICY TO VERIFY THAT ALL
10 APPLICANTS ARE LEGALLY AUTHORIZED TO RESIDE IN THE UNITED STATES;
11 AND DIRECTING THE DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, DIVISION OF
12 EMPLOYMENT SECURITY, TO ADOPT AND IMPLEMENT A POLICY TO VERIFY
13 THAT ALL APPLICANTS FOR BENEFITS ARE LEGALLY AUTHORIZED TO RESIDE
14 IN THE UNITED STATES.
15 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
16
17 PART I. DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
18 SECTION 1. To the extent permitted by federal law, the Department of Health and
19 Human Services (DHHS) shall take all steps necessary to cease providing State-funded benefits
20 to noncitizens residing in the United States without legal permission.
21 SECTION 2. To the extent permitted by federal law, the DHHS shall develop and
22 implement a plan to review and update the eligibility criteria for all State-funded benefits to
23 ensure that noncitizens determined to be residing in the United States without legal permission
24 are ineligible to receive State-funded benefits. As a part of this plan, the DHHS shall develop a
25 method for verifying the immigration status of applicants for State-funded benefits who are
26 noncitizens prior to the initiation of benefits.
27 SECTION 3. By January 15, 2026, the DHHS shall report to the Joint Legislative
28 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services and the Fiscal Research Division all of the
29 following:
30 (1) The steps taken to implement Section 1 of this act and the resulting success of
31 those steps.
32 (2) A list of State-funded benefits for which the DHHS is prohibited from
33 changing.
34 (3) The plan developed and implemented pursuant to Section 2 of this act, which
35 shall include a citation to each federal law or regulation that prohibits the

24 PART II. DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, HOUSING FINANCE AGENCY, AND
25 LOCAL HOUSING AUTHORITIES
26 SECTION 5. To the extent permitted by federal law, the Department of Commerce,
27 the Housing Finance Agency, and all local housing authorities established under Chapter 157 of
28 the General Statutes shall take all steps necessary to cease providing publicly funded housing
29 benefits to noncitizens residing in the United States without legal permission.
30 SECTION 6. To the extent permitted by federal law, the Department of Commerce,
31 the Housing Finance Agency, and all local housing authorities established under Chapter 157 of
32 the General Statutes shall each develop and implement a plan to review and update the eligibility
33 criteria for all publicly funded housing benefits to ensure that noncitizens determined to be
34 residing in the United States without legal permission are ineligible to receive publicly funded
35 housing benefits. As a part of this plan, the Department of Commerce, the Housing Finance
36 Agency, and all local housing authorities established under Chapter 157 of the General Statutes
37 shall develop a method for verifying the immigration status of applicants for publicly funded
38 housing benefits who are noncitizens prior to the initiation of benefits.
39 SECTION 7. By January 15, 2026, the Department of Commerce, the Housing
40 Finance Agency, and all local housing authorities established under Chapter 157 of the General
41 Statutes shall report to the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations and the
42 Fiscal Research Division on both of the following:
43 (1) The steps taken to implement Section 5 of this act and the resulting success of
44 those steps.
45 (2) The plan developed and implemented pursuant to Section 6 of this act, which
46 shall include a citation to each federal law or regulation that prohibits the
47 Department, Office, Agency, or local government unit from denying
48 eligibility for publicly funded benefits to noncitizens determined to be
49 residing in the United States without legal permission.
50 SECTION 8. As used in this Part, "publicly funded housing benefits" means any of
51 the following programs or assistance administered by or through a contract with the Department

18 PART III. STATE-FUNDED INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER EDUCATION
19 SECTION 9. Notwithstanding G.S. 116-143.1 or any other provision of law, the
20 governing bodies of all institutions of higher education in North Carolina receiving State funds
21 shall adopt and implement a policy to verify, for tuition and educational benefits purposes, that
22 all applicants are legally authorized to reside in the United States.
23
24 PART IV. UNEMPLOYMENT COMPENSATION
25 SECTION 10. Notwithstanding any provision of State law and to the extent
26 permitted by federal law, the Department of Commerce, Division of Employment Security, shall
27 adopt and implement a policy to verify, for unemployment benefits purposes, that all applicants
28 are legally authorized to reside in the United States prior to the first payment of benefits.

30 PART V. EFFECTIVE DATE
