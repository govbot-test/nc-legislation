5 REQUIRE LABELING OF MANUFACTURED-PROTEIN MEAT PRODUCTS
6 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 106-549.15 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 106-549.15. Definitions.
8 As used in this Article, except as otherwise specified, the following terms shall have the
9 meanings stated below:
10 …
11 (1a) "Agricultural food animal" means a domesticated animal belonging to the
12 bovine, caprine, ovine, or porcine species.
13 …
14 (5a) "Cell-cultured food product" means a food product having one or more
15 sensory attributes that resemble a type of tissue originating from an
16 agricultural food animal but that, in lieu of being derived from meat
17 processing, is derived from manufacturing cells, in which one or more stem
18 cells are initially isolated from an agricultural food animal, are grown in vitro,
19 and may be manipulated, as part of a manufacturing operation.
20 (5b) "Close proximity" means any of the following:
21 a. Immediately before or after the name of the product.
22 b. In the line of the label immediately before or after the line containing
23 the name of the product.
24 c. Within the same phrase or sentence containing the name of the
25 product.
26 …
27 (9a) "Identifying meat term" means any word or phrase that states, indicates,
28 suggests, or describes a meat product, regardless of whether the word or
29 phrase is used individually, as a portmanteau, or as a compound word. This
30 term includes all of the following:
31 a. A common name for the species of the agricultural food animal subject
32 to slaughter and processing, including a calf or cow, goat or kid, hog
33 or pig, lamb, or sheep.

1 b. A common name for a characteristic of a species of the agricultural
2 food animal subject to slaughter and processing based on age, breed,
3 or sex.
4 c. Meat; beef or veal; cabrito or chevon; lamb or mutton; or pork.
5 d. A common name used to describe a major cut of a meat of an
6 agricultural food animal slaughtered and processed, including a major
7 meat cut specified in 9 C.F.R. § 317.344, or the common name of an
8 organ or offal, including heart, liver, kidney, or tongue.
9 e. Any other common name that a reasonable purchaser would
10 immediately and exclusively associate with a meat product prepared
11 for sale in normal commercial channels such as bacon, baloney,
12 bologna, bone, brat or bratwurst, brisket, burger or hamburger, butt,
13 chop, chuck, cold cut, cutlet, filet, flat iron, frank or frankfurter, ham,
14 hock, hot dog, jerky, liverwurst, loin, London broil, lunch meat, New
15 York strip, pepperoni, porterhouse, ribeye, roast, rib or sparerib,
16 salami, sausage, shank, sirloin, tenderloin, or a comparable word or
17 phrase.
18 (9b) "Insect-protein food product" means a food product having one or more
19 sensory attributes that resemble a type of tissue originating from an
20 agricultural food animal but that, in lieu of being derived from meat
21 processing, is manufactured from insect parts.
22 …
23 (12a) "Manufactured-protein food product" means a cell-cultured food product or
24 insect-protein food product.
25 …
26 (14) "Meat food product" means any product capable of use as human food that is
27 made wholly or in part from any meat or other portion of the carcass of any
28 cattle, sheep, swine, goats, bison, fallow deer, or red deer, or any cell-cultured
29 food product made from cells of those animals, excepting products that
30 contain meat or other portions of such carcasses only in a relatively small
31 proportion or historically have not been considered by consumers as products
32 of the meat food industry, and that are exempted from definition as a meat
33 food product by the Board under such conditions as it may prescribe to assure
34 that the meat or other portions of such carcasses contained in such product are
35 not adulterated and that such products are not represented as meat food
36 products. This term as applied to food products of equines shall have a
37 meaning comparable to that provided in this subdivision with respect to cattle,
38 sheep, swine, goats, and bison.
39 (15) "Misbranded" shall apply to any carcass, part thereof, meat or meat food
40 product under one or more of the following circumstances:
41 a. If its labeling is false or misleading in any particular;
42 b. If it is offered for sale under the name of another food;
43 c. If it is imitation of another food, unless its label bears, in type of
44 uniform size and prominence, the word "imitation" and immediately
45 thereafter, the name of the food imitated;
46 …
47 m. The product is a cell-cultured food product and it is not labeled in
48 accordance with G.S. 106-549.28A.
49 …
50 (21a) "Qualifying term" means a word, compound word, or phrase that would
51 clearly disclose to a reasonable purchaser of meat products that a food product

5 cannot attach, such as an ostrich, an emu, and a rhea. These birds are subject
6 to the provisions of this Article and Article 49C to the same extent as any other
7 meat food product.

9 SECTION 1.(b) Article 49B of Chapter 106 of the General Statutes is amended by
10 adding a new section to read:
11 "§ 106-549.28A. Labeling of manufactured-protein food products.
12 (a) The label of any manufactured-protein food product that contains an identifying meat
13 term shall also contain an appropriate qualifying term in prominent type and in close proximity
14 to the name of the product.
15 (b) A manufactured-protein food product that does not meet the requirements of this
16 section shall be deemed misbranded for purposes of this Article, provided that the Commissioner
17 shall not enforce the prohibition set forth in G.S. 106-549.23(3) with respect to a product deemed
18 misbranded under this section against any person other than the manufacturer of the
19 manufactured-protein food product."
20 SECTION 1.(c) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to
21 products sold or offered for sale on or after that date.

33 (2) The menu listing for any item containing or consisting of a
34 manufactured-protein food product shall include a parenthetical or bracketed

38 cell-cultured components by using language such as "(Contains

40 "(Lab-Grown)," or another qualifying term appropriate to the listed
41 menu item that would clearly convey to a reasonable consumer that
42 the product is not derived from traditional meat or poultry.
