5 REQUIRE LABELING OF MANUFACTURED-PROTEIN MEAT PRODUCTS
6 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 106-549.15 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 106-549.15. Definitions.
8 As used in this Article, except as otherwise specified, the following terms shall have the
9 meanings stated below:
10 …
11 (1a) "Agricultural food animal" means a domesticated animal belonging to the
12 bovine, caprine, ovine, or porcine species.
13 …
14 (5a) "Cell-cultured food product" means a food product having one or more
15 sensory attributes that resemble a type of tissue originating from an
16 agricultural food animal but that, in lieu of being derived from meat
17 processing, is derived from manufacturing cells, in which one or more stem
18 cells are initially isolated from an agricultural food animal, are grown in vitro,
19 and may be manipulated, as part of a manufacturing operation.
20 (5b) "Close proximity" means any of the following:
21 a. Immediately before or after the name of the product.
22 b. In the line of the label immediately before or after the line containing
23 the name of the product.
24 c. Within the same phrase or sentence containing the name of the
25 product.
26 …
27 (9a) "Identifying meat term" means any word or phrase that states, indicates,
28 suggests, or describes a meat product, regardless of whether the word or
29 phrase is used individually, as a portmanteau, or as a compound word. This
30 term includes all of the following:
31 a. A common name for the species of the agricultural food animal subject
32 to slaughter and processing, including a calf or cow, goat or kid, hog
33 or pig, lamb, or sheep.
34 b. A common name for a characteristic of a species of the agricultural
35 food animal subject to slaughter and processing based on age, breed,
36 or sex.

1 c. Meat; beef or veal; cabrito or chevon; lamb or mutton; or pork.
2 d. A common name used to describe a major cut of a meat of an
3 agricultural food animal slaughtered and processed, including a major
4 meat cut specified in 9 C.F.R. § 317.344, or the common name of an
5 organ or offal, including heart, liver, kidney, or tongue.
6 e. Any other common name that a reasonable purchaser would
7 immediately and exclusively associate with a meat product prepared
8 for sale in normal commercial channels such as bacon, baloney,
9 bologna, bone, brat or bratwurst, brisket, burger or hamburger, butt,
10 chop, chuck, cold cut, cutlet, filet, flat iron, frank or frankfurter, ham,
11 hock, hot dog, jerky, liverwurst, loin, London broil, lunch meat, New
12 York strip, pepperoni, porterhouse, ribeye, roast, rib or sparerib,
13 salami, sausage, shank, sirloin, tenderloin, or a comparable word or
14 phrase.
15 (9b) "Insect-protein food product" means a food product having one or more
16 sensory attributes that resemble a type of tissue originating from an
17 agricultural food animal but that, in lieu of being derived from meat
18 processing, is manufactured from insect parts.
19 …
20 (12a) "Manufactured-protein food product" means a cell-cultured food product or
21 insect-protein food product.
22 …
23 (14) "Meat food product" means any product capable of use as human food that is
24 made wholly or in part from any meat or other portion of the carcass of any
25 cattle, sheep, swine, goats, bison, fallow deer, or red deer, or any cell-cultured
26 food product made from cells of those animals, excepting products that
27 contain meat or other portions of such carcasses only in a relatively small
28 proportion or historically have not been considered by consumers as products
29 of the meat food industry, and that are exempted from definition as a meat
30 food product by the Board under such conditions as it may prescribe to assure
31 that the meat or other portions of such carcasses contained in such product are
32 not adulterated and that such products are not represented as meat food
33 products. This term as applied to food products of equines shall have a
34 meaning comparable to that provided in this subdivision with respect to cattle,
35 sheep, swine, goats, and bison.
36 (15) "Misbranded" shall apply to any carcass, part thereof, meat or meat food
37 product under one or more of the following circumstances:
38 a. If its labeling is false or misleading in any particular;
39 b. If it is offered for sale under the name of another food;
40 c. If it is imitation of another food, unless its label bears, in type of
41 uniform size and prominence, the word "imitation" and immediately
42 thereafter, the name of the food imitated;
43 …
44 m. The product is a cell-cultured food product and it is not labeled in
45 accordance with G.S. 106-549.28A.
46 …
47 (21a) "Qualifying term" means a word, compound word, or phrase that would
48 clearly disclose to a reasonable purchaser of meat products that a food product
49 is not a meat product. This includes the following terms: "cell-cultured,"
50 "cultivated," "cell-cultivated," "fake," "grown in a lab," "insect,"
51 "insect-based," "insect-protein," "lab-created," or "lab-grown."

1 (21b) "Ratite" means a bird whose breastbone is smooth so that flight muscles
2 cannot attach, such as an ostrich, an emu, and a rhea. These birds are subject
3 to the provisions of this Article and Article 49C to the same extent as any other
4 meat food product.
5 …."
6 SECTION 1.(b) Article 49B of Chapter 106 of the General Statutes is amended by
7 adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 106-549.28A. Labeling of manufactured-protein food products.
9 (a) The label of any manufactured-protein food product that contains an identifying meat
10 term shall also contain an appropriate qualifying term in prominent type and in close proximity
11 to the name of the product.
12 (b) A manufactured-protein food product that does not meet the requirements of this
13 section shall be deemed misbranded for purposes of this Article, provided that the Commissioner
14 shall not enforce the prohibition set forth in G.S. 106-549.23(3) with respect to a product deemed
15 misbranded under this section against any person other than the manufacturer of the
16 manufactured-protein food product."
17 SECTION 1.(c) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to
18 products sold or offered for sale on or after that date.

4 "§ 130A-248. Regulation of food and lodging establishments.
5 (a) For the protection of the public health, the Commission shall adopt rules governing
6 the sanitation of establishments that prepare or serve drink or food for pay and establishments
7 that prepare and sell meat food products or poultry products. However, any establishment that
8 prepares or serves food or drink to the public, regardless of pay, shall be subject to the provisions
9 of this Article if the establishment that prepares or serves food or drink holds an ABC permit, as
10 defined in G.S. 18B-101, meets any of the definitions in G.S. 18B-1000, and does not meet the
11 definition set forth in G.S. 130A-247 for a brewery, distillery, bar, private club, or winery.
12 …
13 (c4) Every establishment that prepares or serves food, as defined in G.S. 130A-247(7),
14 that offers for sale or consumption of a food item containing or consisting of a
15 manufactured-protein food product shall clearly and conspicuously disclose that fact on its menu
16 in accordance with all of the following:
17 (1) For purposes of this subsection, the following definitions apply:
18 a. "Manufactured-protein food product" means a cell-cultured food
19 product, or an insect-protein food product. This term includes:
20 1. A "cell-cultured food product" as defined in
21 G.S. 106-549.15(5a) for products originating from agricultural
22 food animals and in G.S. 106-549.51(4a) for products
23 originating from poultry.
24 2. An "insect-protein food product" as defined in
25 G.S. 106-549.15(9b) for products resembling the meat of an
26 agricultural food animal and in G.S. 106-549.51(11a) for
27 products resembling poultry.
28 b. "Qualifying term" means a term as defined in G.S. 106-549.15(21a)
29 for products marketed as a substitute or imitation of the meat of an
30 agricultural food animal or G.S. 106-549.51(28a) for products
31 marketed as a substitute or imitation of poultry.
32 (2) The menu listing for any item containing or consisting of a
33 manufactured-protein food product shall include a parenthetical or bracketed
34 statement immediately following the item's name. This statement shall:
35 a. Be in a font size no smaller than the menu item name.
36 b. Clearly indicate that the item is derived from artificial, imitation, or
37 cell-cultured components by using language such as "(Contains
38 Artificial/Cell-Cultured Food Product)," "(Imitation)," or
39 "(Lab-Grown)," or another qualifying term appropriate to the listed
40 menu item that would clearly convey to a reasonable consumer that
41 the product is not derived from traditional meat or poultry.
42 (3) The Department and local health departments shall enforce the menu
43 disclosure requirements of this subsection as part of their food establishment
44 inspection and permitting duties under this Article. Compliance with the menu
45 disclosure requirements under this subsection shall be a condition of receiving
46 or renewing a permit pursuant to subsection (b) of this section.
47 (4) The Commission shall adopt rules to implement the menu disclosure
48 requirements provided by this subsection.
49 …."
50 SECTION 3.(b) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to food
51 items sold, offered for sale, or made available for consumption on or after that date.
