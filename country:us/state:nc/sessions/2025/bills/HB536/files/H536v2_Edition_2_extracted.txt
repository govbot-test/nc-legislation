10 …
11 (4) "Physical therapy" means the evaluation or treatment of any person by the use
12 of physical, chemical, or other properties of heat, light, water, electricity,
13 sound, massage, or therapeutic exercise, or other rehabilitative procedures,
14 with or without assistive devices, for the purposes of preventing, correcting,
15 or alleviating a physical or mental disability. Physical therapy includes the
16 performance of specialized tests of neuromuscular function, administration of
17 specialized therapeutic procedures, interpretation and implementation of
18 referrals from licensed medical doctors or dentists, and establishment and
19 modification of physical therapy programs for patients. Evaluation and
20 treatment of patients may involve physical measures, methods, or procedures
21 as are (i) found commensurate with physical therapy education and training
22 training, (ii) the standards of acceptable and prevailing physical therapy
23 practice, and (iii) generally or specifically authorized by regulations of the
24 Board. Physical therapy education and training shall include study of the
25 skeletal manifestations of systemic disease. Physical therapy does not include
26 the application of roentgen rays or radioactive materials, surgery, the practice
27 of chiropractic, as defined by G.S. 90-143, or medical diagnosis of disease.
