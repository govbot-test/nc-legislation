20 PART IV. WATER AND SEWER ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM
21 SECTION 4.(a) Award Authorized. – Pursuant to Part 2H of Article 10 of Chapter
22 143B of the General Statutes, the Department of Commerce may commit in a Governor's Letter
23 to reimburse, over a period of time not less than 10 years, a business, as defined in
24 G.S. 143B-437.51, for the costs incurred for an eligible project. For purposes of this section, an
25 eligible project is the construction of, or improvements to, new or existing water or sewer lines,
26 facilities, or equipment for new, proposed, or existing buildings by the business, and can include
27 water reuse and water reclamation projects, if the business commits, in a company performance
28 agreement, to the requirements of subsection (c) of this section.
29 SECTION 4.(b) Award Amount. – The total amount that may be committed for
30 reimbursement under this section is equal to the lesser of (i) fifty million dollars ($50,000,000)
31 or (ii) fifty percent (50%) of the cost of the eligible project. The maximum amount that can be
32 paid out in a single calendar year for all but the final year of the award term is the lesser of
33 one-tenth of the amount committed or one-half of the costs incurred and verified by the business
34 for that calendar year.
35 SECTION 4.(c) Company Performance Agreement. – For a project to be eligible
36 under this section, a business must commit to the following requirements in addition to those
37 contained in G.S. 143B-437.72(b):
38 (1) Invest at least two billion dollars ($2,000,000,000) of private funds in the
39 development or expansion of the manufacturing site served by or located
40 within the same county as the eligible project.
41 (2) For the greater of the term of the agreement or 10 years, create at least 500
42 new jobs having an overall average compensation that exceeds the average
43 wage for all insured private employers in the county in which the eligible
44 project is located and maintain both the new and existing jobs of the business
45 in that county.
46 (3) Annually verify costs incurred for the eligible project.
47 (4) Ensure that the eligible project will supply at least sixty percent (60%) of the
48 total aggregate water and wastewater needs required by the manufacturing site
49 as developed or expanded by the investment made under subdivision (1) of
50 this subsection.

14 PART V. LIFE SCIENCE AND BIOMANUFACTURING TECHNOLOGIES RESERVE
15 FUND
16 SECTION 5.(a) Initial Capitalization Transfer. – There is transferred from the
17 General Fund to the Life Science and Biomanufacturing Technologies Reserve Fund established
18 in subsection (b) of this section the sum of twenty million dollars ($20,000,000) in nonrecurring
19 funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year.
20 SECTION 5.(b) Fund Established; Purpose. – There is created the Life Science and
21 Biomanufacturing Technologies Reserve Fund (the Reserve) to be administered by the Office of
22 State Budget and Management. Funds in the Reserve shall be available only for appropriation by
23 the General Assembly and shall be used to provide State matching funds, cost-share
24 contributions, and other financial support for joint federal, State, local government, and industry
25 initiatives involving emerging life science or biomanufacturing technologies.
26 SECTION 5.(c) Eligible Uses. – Eligible uses of funds from the Reserve include:
27 (1) State matching funds for federal grants or cooperative agreements related to
28 life science research, development, or manufacturing.
29 (2) Cost-share contributions required for competitive federal programs, including
30 programs administered by the National Institutes of Health, the Biomedical
31 Advanced Research and Development Authority (BARDA), the Advanced
32 Research Projects Agency for Health (ARPA-H), or the Department of
33 Commerce Economic Development Administration.
34 (3) Financial for public-private partnerships involving life science or
35 biomanufacturing facility development, workforce development, or
36 technology transfer initiatives.
37 (4) Seed funding for multigovernmental consortia or regional innovation hubs
38 focused on biomanufacturing technologies, cell and gene therapy
39 manufacturing, advanced pharmaceutical manufacturing, or related fields.
40 SECTION 5.(d) Appropriation Required. – Funds in the Reserve shall not be
41 expended except upon an act of appropriation by the General Assembly designating the specific
42 initiative, purpose, and amount to be disbursed from the Reserve.
43 SECTION 5.(e) Reporting. – The Director of the Budget shall report to the Joint
44 Legislative Economic Development and Global Engagement Oversight Committee and the Fiscal
45 Research Division no later than March 1 of each fiscal year that funds are appropriated from the
46 Reserve. The report required under this subsection shall include, at a minimum, the balance of
47 the Reserve, any proposals received for use of Reserve funds, and recommendations regarding
48 capitalization of the Reserve for the ensuing biennium.
