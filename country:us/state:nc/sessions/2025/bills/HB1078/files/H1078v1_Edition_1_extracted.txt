22 PART IV. WATER AND SEWER ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM
23 SECTION 4.(a) Award Authorized. – Pursuant to Part 2H of Article 10 of Chapter
24 143B of the General Statutes, the Department of Commerce may commit in a Governor's Letter
25 to reimburse, over a period of time not less than 10 years, a business, as defined in
26 G.S. 143B-437.51, for the costs incurred for an eligible project. For purposes of this section, an
27 eligible project is the construction of, or improvements to, new or existing water or sewer lines,
28 facilities, or equipment for new, proposed, or existing buildings by the business, and can include
29 water reuse and water reclamation projects, if the business commits, in a company performance
30 agreement, to the requirements of subsection (c) of this section.
31 SECTION 4.(b) Award Amount. – The total amount that may be committed for
32 reimbursement under this section is equal to the lesser of (i) fifty million dollars ($50,000,000)
33 or (ii) fifty percent (50%) of the cost of the eligible project. The maximum amount that can be
34 paid out in a single calendar year for all but the final year of the award term is the lesser of
35 one-tenth of the amount committed or one-half of the costs incurred and verified by the business
36 for that calendar year.
37 SECTION 4.(c) Company Performance Agreement. – For a project to be eligible
38 under this section, a business must commit to the following requirements in addition to those
39 contained in G.S. 143B-437.72(b):
40 (1) Invest at least two billion dollars ($2,000,000,000) of private funds in the
41 development or expansion of the manufacturing site served by or located
42 within the same county as the eligible project.
43 (2) For the greater of the term of the agreement or 10 years, create at least 500
44 new jobs having an overall average compensation that exceeds the average
45 wage for all insured private employers in the county in which the eligible
46 project is located and maintain both the new and existing jobs of the business
47 in that county.
48 (3) Annually verify costs incurred for the eligible project.
49 (4) Ensure that the eligible project will supply at least sixty percent (60%) of the
50 total aggregate water and wastewater needs required by the manufacturing site

1 as developed or expanded by the investment made under subdivision (1) of
2 this subsection.
3 (5) Not seek or accept any other grant from the State, including a grant pursuant
4 to Part 2G of Article 10 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes, for any jobs
5 created as part of the company performance agreement.
6 SECTION 4.(d) One NC Commitment Exclusion. – The amount committed pursuant

16 PART V. LIFE SCIENCE AND BIOMANUFACTURING TECHNOLOGIES RESERVE
17 FUND
18 SECTION 5.(a) Initial Capitalization Transfer. – There is transferred from the
19 General Fund to the Life Science and Biomanufacturing Technologies Reserve Fund established
20 in subsection (b) of this section the sum of twenty million dollars ($20,000,000) in nonrecurring
21 funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year.
22 SECTION 5.(b) Fund Established; Purpose. – There is created the Life Science and
23 Biomanufacturing Technologies Reserve Fund (the Reserve) to be administered by the Office of
24 State Budget and Management. Funds in the Reserve shall be available only for appropriation by
25 the General Assembly and shall be used to provide State matching funds, cost-share
26 contributions, and other financial support for joint federal, State, local government, and industry
27 initiatives involving emerging life science or biomanufacturing technologies.
28 SECTION 5.(c) Eligible Uses. – Eligible uses of funds from the Reserve include:
29 (1) State matching funds for federal grants or cooperative agreements related to
30 life science research, development, or manufacturing.
31 (2) Cost-share contributions required for competitive federal programs, including
32 programs administered by the National Institutes of Health, the Biomedical
33 Advanced Research and Development Authority (BARDA), the Advanced
34 Research Projects Agency for Health (ARPA-H), or the Department of
35 Commerce Economic Development Administration.
36 (3) Financial for public-private partnerships involving life science or
37 biomanufacturing facility development, workforce development, or
38 technology transfer initiatives.
39 (4) Seed funding for multigovernmental consortia or regional innovation hubs
40 focused on biomanufacturing technologies, cell and gene therapy
41 manufacturing, advanced pharmaceutical manufacturing, or related fields.
42 SECTION 5.(d) Appropriation Required. – Funds in the Reserve shall not be
43 expended except upon an act of appropriation by the General Assembly designating the specific
44 initiative, purpose, and amount to be disbursed from the Reserve.
45 SECTION 5.(e) Reporting. – The Director of the Budget shall report to the Joint
46 Legislative Economic Development and Global Engagement Oversight Committee and the Fiscal
47 Research Division no later than March 1 of each fiscal year that funds are appropriated from the
48 Reserve. The report required under this subsection shall include, at a minimum, the balance of
49 the Reserve, any proposals received for use of Reserve funds, and recommendations regarding
50 capitalization of the Reserve for the ensuing biennium.

16 PART V. LIFE SCIENCE AND BIOMANUFACTURING TECHNOLOGIES RESERVE
17 FUND
18 SECTION 5.(a) Initial Capitalization Transfer. – There is transferred from the
19 General Fund to the Life Science and Biomanufacturing Technologies Reserve Fund established
20 in subsection (b) of this section the sum of twenty million dollars ($20,000,000) in nonrecurring
21 funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year.
22 SECTION 5.(b) Fund Established; Purpose. – There is created the Life Science and
23 Biomanufacturing Technologies Reserve Fund (the Reserve) to be administered by the Office of
24 State Budget and Management. Funds in the Reserve shall be available only for appropriation by
25 the General Assembly and shall be used to provide State matching funds, cost-share
26 contributions, and other financial support for joint federal, State, local government, and industry
27 initiatives involving emerging life science or biomanufacturing technologies.
28 SECTION 5.(c) Eligible Uses. – Eligible uses of funds from the Reserve include:
29 (1) State matching funds for federal grants or cooperative agreements related to
30 life science research, development, or manufacturing.
31 (2) Cost-share contributions required for competitive federal programs, including
32 programs administered by the National Institutes of Health, the Biomedical
33 Advanced Research and Development Authority (BARDA), the Advanced
34 Research Projects Agency for Health (ARPA-H), or the Department of
35 Commerce Economic Development Administration.
36 (3) Financial for public-private partnerships involving life science or
37 biomanufacturing facility development, workforce development, or
38 technology transfer initiatives.
39 (4) Seed funding for multigovernmental consortia or regional innovation hubs
40 focused on biomanufacturing technologies, cell and gene therapy
41 manufacturing, advanced pharmaceutical manufacturing, or related fields.
42 SECTION 5.(d) Appropriation Required. – Funds in the Reserve shall not be
43 expended except upon an act of appropriation by the General Assembly designating the specific
44 initiative, purpose, and amount to be disbursed from the Reserve.
45 SECTION 5.(e) Reporting. – The Director of the Budget shall report to the Joint
46 Legislative Economic Development and Global Engagement Oversight Committee and the Fiscal
47 Research Division no later than March 1 of each fiscal year that funds are appropriated from the
48 Reserve. The report required under this subsection shall include, at a minimum, the balance of
49 the Reserve, any proposals received for use of Reserve funds, and recommendations regarding
50 capitalization of the Reserve for the ensuing biennium.
