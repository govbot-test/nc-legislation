Section 1:
H.B. 239
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Feb 26, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Change to EC Funding Formula. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Scott.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MODIFY THE CHILDREN WITH DISABILITIES FUNDING FORMULA.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 115C-111.05 reads as rewritten:
5 "§ 115C-111.05. Funding for children with disabilities.
6 To the extent funds are made available for this purpose, the State Board shall allocate funds
7 for children with disabilities to each local school administrative unit public school unit, except
8 those operating under Article 9C of this Chapter, on a per child basis. Each local school
9 administrative unit public school unit receiving funds pursuant to this section shall receive funds
10 for the lesser of (i) all children who are identified as children with disabilities or (ii) thirteen
11 percent (13%) of its the sum of the allocated average daily membership in the public school units
12 co-located within the boundaries of a local school administrative unit for the current school
13 year.year, plus the number of students that received scholarship grants pursuant to Part 2A of
14 Article 39 of this Chapter for the most recent school year that data is available and reside within
15 the boundaries of the local school administrative unit."
16 SECTION 1.(b) The State Education Assistance Authority shall provide to the
17 Department of Public Instruction by March 15 of each year the number of students that received
18 scholarship grants pursuant to Part 2A of Article 39 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes that
19 resided within the boundaries of each local school administrative unit during the most recent
20 school year.
21 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
22 Public Instruction the sum of twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) in recurring funds for the
23 2025-2026 fiscal year to be allocated to public school units for children with disabilities funding.
24 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025, and applies beginning with
25 the funding allocations for the 2025-2026 fiscal year.
*DRH10094-NGa-19*
