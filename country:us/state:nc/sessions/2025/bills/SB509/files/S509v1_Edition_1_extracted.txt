10 This act Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Statewide Health Information

12 "§ 90-414.2. Purpose.
13 This Article is intended to improve the quality of health care delivery within this State by
14 facilitating and regulating the use of a voluntary, statewide health information exchange network
15 for the secure electronic transmission of individually identifiable health information among
16 health care providers, health plans, and health care clearinghouses clearinghouses, and the State
17 in a manner that is consistent with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act,
18 Privacy Rule and Security Rule, 45 C.F.R. §§ 160, 164.

4 (a) Findings. – The General Assembly makes the following findings:
5 (1) That controlling escalating health care costs of the Medicaid program and
6 other State-funded health care services is of significant importance to the
7 State, its taxpayers, its Medicaid recipients, and other recipients beneficiaries
8 of State-funded health care services.care.
9 (2) That the State and covered entities in North Carolina need timely access to
10 certain demographic and clinical information pertaining to services rendered
11 to Medicaid and other beneficiaries of State-funded health care program
12 beneficiaries and paid for with Medicaid or other State-funded State health
13 care funds in order to assess performance, improve health care outcomes,
14 pinpoint medical expense trends, identify beneficiary health risks, and
15 evaluate how the State is spending money on Medicaid and other State-funded
16 health care services. The care. To that end, the Department of Information
17 Technology, the Department of State Treasurer, State Health Plan Division,
18 and the Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Health
19 Benefits, have an affirmative duty to facilitate and support participation by
20 covered entities in the statewide health information exchange network.
21 (3) That making demographic and clinical information available to the State and
22 covered entities in North Carolina by secure electronic means as set forth in
23 subsection (b) of this section will improve care coordination within and across
24 health systems, increase care quality for such beneficiaries, beneficiaries of
25 State-funded health care, enable more effective population health
26 management, reduce duplication of medical services, augment syndromic
27 surveillance, allow more accurate measurement of care services and
28 outcomes, increase strategic knowledge about the health of the population,
29 and facilitate health care cost containment.

13 (a) Creation. – There is hereby established the North Carolina Health Information
14 Exchange Authority to oversee and administer the HIE Network in accordance with this Article.
15 The Authority shall be located within the Department of Information Technology and shall be
16 under the supervision, direction, and control of the State CIO. The State CIO shall employ an
17 Authority Director and may delegate to the Authority Director all powers and duties associated
18 with the daily operation of the Authority, its staff, and the performance of the powers and duties
19 set forth in subsection (b) of this section. In making this delegation, however, the State CIO
20 maintains the responsibility for the performance of these powers and duties.
21 (b) Powers and Duties. – The Authority has the following powers and duties:
22 (1) Oversee and administer the HIE Network in a manner that ensures all of the
23 following:
24 a. Compliance with this Article.

27 c. Compliance with the terms of any participation agreement, business
28 associate agreement, or other agreement the Authority or qualified
29 organization or other person or entity enters into with a covered entity
30 participating in submission of data through or accessing the HIE
31 Network.
32 d. Notice to the patient by the healthcare provider or other person or
33 entity about the HIE Network, including information and education
34 about the right of individuals on a continuing basis to opt out or rescind
35 a decision to opt out.
36 e. Opportunity for all individuals whose data has been submitted to the
37 HIE Network to exercise on a continuing basis the right to opt out or
38 rescind a decision to opt out.
39 f. Nondiscriminatory treatment by covered entities of individuals who
40 exercise the right to opt out.
41 g. Facilitation of HIE Network interoperability with electronic health
42 record systems of all covered entities listed in G.S. 90-414.4(b).
43 h. Minimization of the amount of data required to be submitted under
44 G.S. 90-414.4(b) and any use or disclosure of such data to what is
45 determined by the Authority to be required in order to advance the
46 purposes set forth in G.S. 90-414.2 and G.S. 90-414.4(a).

21 "§ 90-414.11. Construction and applicability.
22 (a) Nothing in this Article shall be construed to do any of the following:

27 c. The right of access to inspect and obtain copies.
28 d. The right to request amendment.
29 e. The right to request confidential forms of communication.
30 f. The right to receive an accounting of disclosures.
31 (2) Authorize the disclosure of protected health information through the HIE
32 Network to the extent that the disclosure is restricted by federal laws or
33 regulations, including the federal drug and alcohol confidentiality regulations
34 set forth in 42 C.F.R. Part 2.

36 Network for public health purposes or research purposes, so long as disclosure
37 is permitted by both HIPAA and State law.

39 from maintaining in the Authority's or qualified organization's computer

42 organization does not access, use, or disclose the individual's protected health
43 information for any purpose other than for necessary system maintenance or
44 as required by federal or State law.

46 the HIE Network, including disclosures made within qualified organizations. It does not apply to

12 "§ 90-414.2. Purpose.
13 This Article is intended to improve the quality of health care delivery within this State by
14 facilitating and regulating the use of a voluntary, statewide health information exchange network
15 for the secure electronic transmission of individually identifiable health information among
16 health care providers, health plans, and health care clearinghouses clearinghouses, and the State

20 Authority shall send these determinations, in writing, to providers and entities via certified mail,
21 return receipt requested, and via email, if known to the Authority. If a provider or entity disagrees
22 with the Authority's determination, it shall deliver a petition for appeal to the Department of
23 Information Technology's registered agent via certified mail, return receipt requested, within 30
24 calendar days after receipt of the Authority's written determination. The petition for appeal shall
25 include an explanation of the specific reasons the provider or entity disagrees with the Authority's
26 determination and shall be supported by documentation and affidavits regarding the petitioner's
27 compliance with this Article along with any other supporting documentation the petitioner deems
28 relevant to the appeal. The Authority shall develop and make available on its website the form to
29 be used by any provider or entity seeking to appeal the Authority's determination.
30 (b) Untimely Appeals. – A petitioner's failure to submit a timely petition for appeal shall
31 result in the dismissal of the appeal with prejudice. The Department of Information Technology
32 shall notify the provider or entity of such dismissal in writing.
33 (c) Review by the State CIO or the State CIO's Designee. – The State CIO or the State
34 CIO's designee shall review all timely petitions for appeal under this section. The State CIO or
35 the State CIO's designee may render a decision on the petition without meeting with the
36 petitioner. If the State CIO or State CIO's designee renders a decision without meeting with the
37 petitioner, then the State CIO or the State CIO's designee shall notify the petitioner of his or her
38 decision, in writing, within 30 calendar days after the date the petition was received by the
39 Department of Information Technology. If the State CIO or the State CIO's designee determines
40 it is necessary to meet with the petitioner prior to rendering a decision, the State CIO or the State
41 CIO's designee and petitioner shall schedule a meeting within 30 calendar days after the date the
42 petition was received by the Department of Information Technology, or as soon as reasonably
43 practical thereafter, or as agreed upon by the parties. Within 30 calendar days after the date of
44 the meeting, the State CIO or the State CIO's designee shall submit a decision, in writing, to the
45 petitioner by certified mail, return receipt requested, and via email, if known.
