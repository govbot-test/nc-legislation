26 PART VII. SIGNATURE VERIFICATION PILOT PROGRAM
27 SECTION 7.(a) The State Board of Elections shall study and report on the
28 feasibility, costs, and technical considerations of using signature verification technology. The
29 State Board of Elections may seek feedback from other stakeholders, including the county boards
30 of elections, and the study shall evaluate all of the following:
31 (1) The use of signature verification software in other states for the purposes of
32 voter registration, absentee ballot signatures, and other uses in elections
33 administration. This may include information about error rates in other states
34 across potential vendors, any discontinuation of vendor contracts in other
35 states, and any cost-saving measures implemented by other states.
36 (2) The cost and logistics of implementing a signature verification component for
37 absentee ballot verification, including any training requirements, equipment
38 needs, or costs incurred by county boards of elections.
39 (3) Any foreseen challenges related to voter signature retention under the current
40 elections management infrastructure and possible technology solutions to
41 minimize error rates under a statewide signature verification program.
42 SECTION 7.(b) The State Board of Elections shall report its findings, including any
43 recommendations or proposed legislation, to the Joint Legislative Elections Oversight Committee
44 on or before May 1, 2027.
