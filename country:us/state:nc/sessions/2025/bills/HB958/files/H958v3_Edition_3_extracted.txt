17 PART VI. SIGNATURE VERIFICATION PILOT PROGRAM
18 SECTION 6.(a) The State Board of Elections shall select 10 counties in the State in
19 which to conduct a pilot program during the primary held in 2026 for signature verification on
20 executed absentee ballots. In selecting the 10 counties for the pilot, the State Board of Elections
21 shall seek diversity of population size, regional location, and demographic composition, and may
22 use the same 10 counties that were selected for the pilot program in accordance with S.L.
23 2023-140. The pilot program shall consist of county boards of elections using signature
24 verification software to check the signatures of voters noted on all executed absentee ballots
25 received by the county boards of elections in the 2026 primary. The State Board of Elections
26 shall select the signature verification software and ensure that the software is available for all 10
27 counties to use in the 2026 primary. The State Board of Elections shall assist the selected county
28 boards of elections in implementing the signature verification software, including assisting the
29 selected county boards of elections in any training needed on how the software is to be used for
30 signature matching on executed absentee ballots.
31 SECTION 6.(b) The State Board of Elections shall closely monitor the pilot program
32 established in this section. The selected county boards of elections shall report to the State Board
33 of Elections its findings on the use of the signature verification software during the 2026 primary,
34 including all of the following:
35 (1) Whether the signature verification software was used for all executed absentee
36 ballots, and what the voter signature on the executed absentee ballot was
37 matched against.
38 (2) How many executed absentee ballots were counted by the county board of
39 elections in the 2026 primary.
40 (3) How many executed absentee ballots were flagged by the signature
41 verification software, and any information known on how close of a match the
42 signatures must be for the signature match software to not flag the voter's
43 signature.
44 (4) Information on how the signature matching software flagged an executed
45 absentee ballot with a signature that did not match the signature on file for the
46 voter, including any known information on the rate of error in the software.
47 SECTION 6.(c) In implementing the pilot program established in this section, no
48 executed absentee ballot shall be rejected by the county board of elections for failing any
49 signature verification. All executed absentee ballots that are otherwise eligible to be counted in
50 accordance with Chapter 163 of the General Statutes shall be counted.
