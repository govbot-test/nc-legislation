Short Title: Workforce Credential Grant Program/CCP. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Willis.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ESTABLISH A CAREER AND COLLEGE PATHWAYS INNOVATION
3 CHALLENGE GRANT PROGRAM.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Chapter 115D of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
6 Article to read:
7 "Article 10.
8 "Workforce Grant Programs.
9 "§ 115D-150. Program established.
10 There is established the Career and College Pathways Innovation Challenge Grant Program
11 (Program) to meet statewide educational attainment and workforce development goals by
12 developing local and regional partnerships that foster innovations to accomplish the following:
13 (1) Increase postsecondary enrollment and completion for both students enrolling
14 directly from high school and adults returning to education.
15 (2) Ensure alignment between educational options provided and labor market
16 needs.
17 (3) Eliminate educational opportunity gaps where they exist.
18 "§ 115D-151. Definitions.
19 The following definitions apply in this Article:
20 (1) Approved grant award. – Awards granted to participating community colleges
21 upon approval of a Program application by the State Board.
22 (2) Participating community college. – A community college that submitted an
23 application for the Program that was approved by the State Board.
24 "§ 115D-155. Grant program administration.
25 (a) The State Board of Community Colleges shall adopt necessary rules and policies to
26 administer the Program, including for the following purposes:
27 (1) The administration and awarding of grant funds, based on a competitive grant
28 process, to local and regional partnerships that represent cross-sector
29 collaborations among the Department of Public Instruction, the Community
30 Colleges System Office, The University of North Carolina System Office,
31 local governments, community-based organizations, employers, and other
32 local entities. The design of the Program is to be developed in consultation
33 with the following entities:
34 a. The Division of Workforce Solutions of the Department of Commerce.
35 b. The North Carolina Community Colleges System Office.
36 c. The North Carolina Independent Colleges and Universities.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 d. The University of North Carolina System Office.
2 e. The leadership of MyFutureNC.
3 (2) The use of funds and reporting of expenditures by participating community
4 colleges. In awarding grant funds to participating community colleges, the
5 State Board shall consider applications that propose to do the following:
6 a. Plan and pilot innovative initiatives to raise educational attainment and
7 decrease opportunity gaps.
8 b. Engage community-based organizations and resources.
9 c. Expand the use of work-based learning.
10 d. Provide financial support to cover (i) expenses beyond educational
11 tuition and fees and (ii) other services and supports to encourage
12 students to enroll and to complete education and training.
13 e. Provide local matching funds.
14 (3) The monitoring and control of spending grant funds, including support and
15 technical assistance to participating community colleges.
16 (b) Up to four percent (4%) of the funds appropriated to the Community Colleges System
17 Office for implementation of the Program in each fiscal year may be used for administrative
18 costs.
19 (c) Up to ten percent (10%) of the funds appropriated to the Community Colleges System
20 Office for implementation of the Program in each fiscal year may be used for marketing and
21 communications, including contracting with non-State entities for marketing and
22 communications.
23 "§ 115D-160. Grant amounts.
24 (a) To the extent funds are made available for the Program, the State Board of
25 Community Colleges shall award grants to participating community colleges in an amount up to
26 the lesser of (i) the actual cost associated with an application by a community college or (ii) two
27 hundred thousand dollars ($200,000).
28 (b) In the event that the funds appropriated for the Program are insufficient to award all
29 approved grant awards, each award shall be reduced by an equivalent percentage to ensure that
30 funding is awarded to all participating community colleges.
31 "§ 115D-165. Reporting requirement.
32 The State Board, in collaboration with the Department of Commerce, shall analyze data
33 pertaining to the Program and shall report annually by July 15 to the Joint Legislative Education
34 Oversight Committee on the following information from the prior academic year:
35 (1) The total dollar amount of grant funds awarded.
36 (2) The total number of participating community colleges.
37 (3) The progress of each local and regional partnership that partnered with a
38 participating community college, as reflected in high school graduation rates,
39 postsecondary enrollment and completion, job growth, and labor market
40 alignment, with data being disaggregated by income, race, ethnicity, and other
41 demographic characteristics, where available."
42 SECTION 2.(a) Notwithstanding G.S. 115D-160, as enacted by this act, for the
43 2025-2026 and 2026-2027 fiscal years, the State Board of Community Colleges shall allocate a
44 total of two million dollars ($2,000,000) to be directed to participating community colleges in
45 the following areas affected by Hurricane Helene:
46 (1) Alexander County.
47 (2) Alleghany County.
48 (3) Ashe County.
49 (4) Avery County.
50 (5) Buncombe County.
51 (6) Burke County.
