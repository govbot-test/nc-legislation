Short Title: A PTA for Every School. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Chitlik and Chaudhuri (Primary Sponsors).

1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ESTABLISH THE PARENT-TEACHER ASSOCIATION LAUNCH PILOT
3 GRANT PROGRAM.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) Definitions. – As used in this section, the following definitions
6 apply:
7 (1) Department. – The Department of Public Instruction.
8 (2) Parent-Teacher Association (PTA). – A group of public school teachers and
9 parents of students enrolled in the public school that work together for the
10 improvement of the school and benefit of the students.
11 (3) Public school. – A school within a local school administrative unit.
12 SECTION 1.(b) Program; Purpose. – The Department shall establish the A PTA for
13 Every School Grant Program (Program) for the 2026-2027 fiscal year to provide supplemental
14 funds to assist public schools with establishing PTAs. The goal of the Program is to promote
15 having an active PTA in every public school in the State.
16 SECTION 1.(c) Application. – The Department shall establish an application
17 process for the Program. Any local school administrative unit that contains schools without an
18 existing PTA may apply for the Program on behalf of those schools. As part of the application
19 process, the local school administrative unit shall identify the source of matching funds.
20 SECTION 1.(d) Award; Required Match. – Local school administrative units are
21 eligible to receive grants for one year in the amount of two thousand dollars ($2,000) per public
22 school within the unit that does not have a PTA, up to a maximum of thirty thousand dollars
23 ($30,000) total per local school administrative unit. The local school administrative unit shall
24 provide certification to the Department that the grant received under the Program shall be
25 matched on the basis of two dollars ($2.00) in grant funds for every one dollar ($1.00) in nongrant
26 funds. Matching funds shall not include other State funds.
27 SECTION 1.(e) Local Allocation. – Recipient local school administrative units have
28 discretion to allocate grant funds among public schools in the unit that do not have an existing
29 PTA; however, the school shall certify that one PTA was created for every two thousand dollars
30 ($2,000) received. For every PTA not established, the local school administrative unit shall revert
31 two thousand dollars ($2,000) but shall not revert a total amount greater than the total award
32 received by the local school administrative unit.
33 SECTION 1.(f) Report. – No later than April 1, 2027, the Department shall report
34 to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee on (i) the number of local school
35 administrative units that applied for the Program, (ii) the number of local school administrative
36 units that received an award under the Program, (iii) the number of schools that established a

8 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2026.
