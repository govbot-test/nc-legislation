26 SECTION 3.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the North Carolina
27 Association of County Commissioners (Association) the nonrecurring sum of twenty million
28 dollars ($20,000,000) for the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be used by the Association to provide
29 grants to local governments for the purpose of transitioning those governments to shortened
30 reappraisal cycles and thereby ensuring more frequent and accurate property valuations and to
31 educate the general public on property taxes and property tax relief programs offered by the State.
32 Grant funds provided under this section shall be used by local governments for (i) technical
33 assistance, (ii) public education regarding the property tax system and property tax relief
34 programs, and (iii) one-time capital investments in technological infrastructure that improve local
35 capacity and efficiency in conducting reappraisals, including the acquisition of specialized
36 software, data migration, and the implementation of digital systems. In awarding grants under
37 this section, the Association shall prioritize awarding grants to local governments operating on a
38 reappraisal cycle of more than four years. For purposes of this section, "local governments"
39 means counties, cities, or towns conducting reappraisals of property under Subchapter II of
40 Chapter 105 of the General Statutes as of the effective date of this section.
41 SECTION 3.(b) This section becomes effective July 1, 2026.
42 SECTION 4.(a) Subchapter I of Chapter 105 of the General Statutes is amended by
43 adding a new Article to read:
44 "Article 8F.
45 "Excise Tax on Controlling Interest Transfers.
46 "§ 105-228.40. Purpose and scope.
47 The tax imposed by this Article is intended to ensure parity in the taxation of real property
48 transfers, whether such transfer is accomplished by the recordation of a deed or by the transfer
49 of a controlling interest in an entity that owns an interest in real property in this State.
50 "§ 105-228.41. Definitions.
51 The following definitions apply in this Article:

1 (1) Controlling interest. – Either of the following:
2 a. In the case of a corporation, either more than fifty percent (50%) of
3 the total combined voting power of all classes of stock of such
4 corporation, or more than fifty percent (50%) of the capital, profits, or
5 beneficial interest in the voting stock of such corporation.
6 b. In the case of a partnership, association, trust, or other entity, more
7 than fifty percent (50%) of the capital, profits, or beneficial interest in
8 such partnership, association, trust, or other entity.
9 (2) Entity. – A corporation, partnership, association, trust, limited liability
10 company, or other organization.
11 (3) Real property interest. – Any interest in real property located in this State.
12 (4) Value. – The fair market value, including any stated consideration
13 corresponding to thereto, of the real property interest owned by the entity.
14 There is a rebuttable presumption that the value is the higher of (i) the total
15 assessed ad valorem tax value of the property at the time of the transfer or (ii)
16 the prior purchasing price of the property if the purchase occurred within five
17 years of the transfer.
18 "§ 105-228.42. Imposition of excise tax; rate.
19 (a) Imposition. – An excise tax is levied on the sale or transfer, by one or more persons
20 or by one or more transactions, within any 36-month period, of a controlling interest in an entity
21 that possesses, directly or indirectly, a real property interest in the State. This tax applies if the
22 value of the real property interest exceeds one hundred dollars ($100.00).
23 (b) Rate. – The tax rate is one dollar ($1.00) on each five hundred dollars ($500.00), or
24 fractional part thereof, of the value of the real property interest.
25 "§ 105-228.43. Reporting and payment; duties of Register of Deeds.
26 (a) Report and Payment. – Every person who acquires a controlling interest in an entity
27 subject to this Article shall, within 30 days of the transfer, file a return with the Register of Deeds
28 in each county where the entity holds a real property interest. The tax must be paid at the time
29 the return is filed.
30 (b) Recording. – Upon payment of the tax, the Register of Deeds shall record a "Notice
31 of Controlling Interest Transfer" in the consolidated real property index. This notice shall not be
32 required to be a deed but shall serve as public notice of the satisfaction of the tax liability under
33 this Article.
34 "§ 105-228.44. Exemptions.
35 The tax imposed by this Article does not apply to the following:
36 (1) Transfers of an interest in an entity that is traded on a public stock exchange.
37 (2) Transfers resulting from death, whether by will or statutory succession.
38 (3) Transfers between spouses.
39 (4) Transfers that are exempt under G.S. 105-228.29 except transfers by "merger"
40 under subdivision (7) of that section.
41 "§ 105-228.45. Penalties.
42 (a) Failure to File. – Any person required to file a return under this Article who fails to
43 do so within the time required shall be subject to a penalty equal to twenty-five percent (25%) of
44 the tax due.
45 (b) Fraud. – Any person who willfully provides false information regarding the value of
46 the property or the percentage of interest transferred is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor."
47 SECTION 4.(b) This section becomes effective January 1, 2027, and applies to
