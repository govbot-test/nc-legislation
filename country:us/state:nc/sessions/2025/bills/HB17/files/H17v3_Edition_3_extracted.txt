Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 3
Committee Substitute Favorable 3/4/25
Senate Elections Committee Substitute Adopted 5/20/25
Short Title: Various Local Election Changes. (Local)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
January 30, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO EXTEND THE TERM OF OFFICE OF THE MAYOR OF THE TOWN OF
3 MADISON FROM TWO YEARS TO FOUR YEARS; TO PROVIDE THAT REGULAR
4 MUNICIPAL ELECTIONS FOR THE TOWN OF FAITH SHALL BE HELD IN
5 EVEN-NUMBERED YEARS AND TO EXTEND THE TERMS OF OFFICE FOR
6 MEMBERS OF THE BOARD OF ALDERMEN IN THE TOWN OF FAITH FROM TWO
7 YEARS TO FOUR YEARS; AND TO PROVIDE THAT REGULAR MUNICIPAL
8 ELECTIONS IN THE TOWN OF CHINA GROVE SHALL BE HELD IN
9 EVEN-NUMBERED YEARS.
10 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
11 SECTION 1.(a) Section 3.3(a) of the Charter of the Town of Madison, being Chapter
12 289 of the 1973 Session Laws, as amended by S.L. 2004-54, reads as rewritten:
13 "(a) The members of the Board of Aldermen shall serve for staggered terms of four years,
14 and the Mayor shall serve for a term of two years, beginning the day and hour of the
15 organizational meeting following their election, as established by ordinance in accordance with
16 this Charter; provided, they shall serve until their successors are elected and qualify. In the 2005
17 municipal election, six members shall be elected. The three persons receiving the highest
18 numbers of votes shall receive four-year terms, and the three persons receiving the next highest
19 numbers of votes shall receive two-year terms. In 2007 and biennially thereafter, three members
20 shall be elected for four-year terms.four years."
21 SECTION 1.(b) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies to
22 elections held on or after that date.
23 SECTION 2.(a) Section 4 of the Charter of the Town of Faith, being Chapter 363 of
24 the Private Laws of 1903, as rewritten by Chapter 159 of the 1961 Session Laws and Chapter
25 1111 of the 1979 Session Laws, reads as rewritten:
26 "Sec. 4. Elections in the Town of Faith shall be held at the time of the general election in each
27 even-numbered year. Except as otherwise provided, the election shall be conducted in accordance
28 with Subchapter IX of the uniform municipal election laws in Chapter 163 of the General
29 Statutes."
30 SECTION 2.(b) Section 3 of the Charter of the Town of Faith, being Chapter 363 of
31 the Private Laws of 1903, as rewritten by Chapter 159 of the 1961 Session Laws, reads as
32 rewritten:
33 "Sec. 3. That the officers of said Town shall consist of five aldermen, elected to serve a
34 four-year term who shall elect one of their number as mayor. The mayor and aldermen when
*H17-v-3*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 qualified as required by law, may elect a town clerk, a tax collector, a treasurer, such policemen
2 and other officers necessary to the preservation and regulation of the Town's best interests."
3 SECTION 2.(c) No municipal elections shall be conducted in the Town of Faith in
4 2025. The terms of office for the aldermen in the Town of Faith serving on the effective date of
5 this act shall be extended by one year. Regular municipal elections shall be conducted in the
6 Town of Faith every four years beginning in 2026. In 2026 and quadrennially thereafter, five
7 aldermen shall be elected to serve four-year terms.
8 SECTION 2.(d) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies to
9 elections held on or after that date.
10 SECTION 3.(a) Sections 3.1, 3.2, and 3.3 of the Charter of the Town of China
11 Grove, being S.L. 2002-42, read as rewritten:
12 "Section 3.1. Regular Municipal Elections. Regular municipal elections shall be held at the
13 time of the general election in each odd-numbered year. even-numbered year. Except as
14 otherwise provided, the election shall be conducted in accordance with the uniform municipal
15 election laws of North Carolina. Elections shall be conducted on a nonpartisan plurality basis and
16 the results determined using the nonpartisan plurality method as provided in G.S. 163-292.
17 "Section 3.2. Election of Aldermen. The Town shall continue to have staggered terms for its
18 Aldermen. Two Aldermen shall be elected at the regular municipal election in 2003 to four-year
19 terms and quadrennially thereafter. Three Aldermen shall be elected at the regular municipal
20 election in 2005 to four-year terms and quadrennially thereafter.for staggered four-year terms.
21 "Section 3.3. Election of Mayor. A Mayor shall be elected in the regular municipal election
22 in 2003 and quadrennially thereafter.for a four-year term."
23 SECTION 3.(b) No municipal elections shall be conducted in the Town of China
24 Grove in 2025. The terms of office for the three Aldermen serving on the effective date of this
25 section whose terms are set to expire in 2025 shall be extended by one year. The terms of office
26 for the two Aldermen and the Mayor serving on the effective date of this section whose terms are
27 set to expire in 2027 are extended by one year. Regular municipal elections in the Town of China
28 Grove shall resume in even-numbered years beginning in 2026 with the same staggering of terms
29 as when elections were conducted in the odd-numbered years.
30 SECTION 3.(c) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies to
31 elections held on or after that date.
32 SECTION 4. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
33 law.
