10 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
11
12 PART I. REDIRECT CRISIS PREGNANCY CENTER FUNDS TO EVIDENCE-BASED
13 MATERNAL AND INFANT HEALTH PROGRAMS
14 SECTION 1.(a) Section 9H.11 of S.L. 2023-134 is repealed.
15 SECTION 1.(b) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
16 Health and Human Services, Division of Public Health, the sum of six million two hundred fifty
17 thousand dollars ($6,250,000) in recurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year and the sum of six
18 million two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($6,250,000) in recurring funds for the 2026-2027
19 fiscal year to be used to support the expansion of evidence-based maternal and infant health
20 programs and services administered by the Department of Health and Human Services.
21 SECTION 1.(c) This Part becomes effective July 1, 2025.
22
23 PART II. REQUIRE THE STATE AUDITOR TO CONDUCT AN AUDIT OF
24 CAROLINA PREGNANCY CARE FELLOWSHIP
25 SECTION 2. By March 1, 2026, the State Auditor shall conduct a financial statement
26 audit of Carolina Pregnancy Care Fellowship, a nonprofit corporation. The State Auditor shall
27 report the results of the audit to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human
28 Services and the Fiscal Research Division.
29
30 PART III. ESTABLISH ADDITIONAL REPORTING REQUIREMENTS FOR CRISIS
31 PREGNANCY CENTERS THAT RECEIVE STATE FUNDS
32 SECTION 3.(a) Chapter 131E of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
33 section to read:
34 "§ 131E-269.5. Crisis pregnancy centers; reporting requirements.
35 (a) Definitions. – As used in this section, the following terms have the following
36 meanings:

1 (1) Crisis pregnancy center. – Any nonprofit organization that holds itself out as
2 providing services to pregnant women through pregnancy, birth, and the
3 postpartum period, including a birthing center, clinic, community-based
4 organization, or faith-based organization that holds itself out as providing
5 pregnancy counseling services, pregnancy testing, and other nonmedical
6 pregnancy services. The term does not include a hospital, as defined in
7 G.S. 131E-76, or an ambulatory surgical facility, as defined in G.S. 131E-106.
8 (2) Fiscal year. – The annual operating year of the crisis pregnancy center.
9 (3) State financial assistance. – State funds disbursed to a crisis pregnancy center
10 as a grant, cooperative agreement, noncash contribution, or direct
11 appropriation to a crisis pregnancy center.
12 (4) State funds. – As defined in G.S. 143C-1-1(d)(25).
13 (b) Reporting Requirements. – In addition to the reporting requirements specified in 09
14 NCAC 03M .0205, each crisis pregnancy center that is a recipient or subrecipient of any amount
15 of State financial assistance shall submit a report to the Department of Health and Human
16 Services, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services, and the
17 Fiscal Research Division within 60 days after the end of the fiscal year in which the crisis
18 pregnancy center received State financial assistance. The report shall include all of the following
19 information with respect to the fiscal year in which the State financial assistance was received:
20 (1) The name of the center and each location where the center conducts business
21 or provides services.
22 (2) The total amount of State funds, federal funds, tax subsidies, or other
23 subsidies, if any, the center received, directly or indirectly, broken down by
24 location if the center conducts business or provides services at more than one
25 location.
26 (3) An explanation of whether the center is part of a larger umbrella organization
27 that operates within the State and, if so, the total amount of State and federal
28 funds or taxes or other subsidies, if any, the umbrella organization received,
29 directly or indirectly, broken down by location if the umbrella organization
30 conducts business or provides services at more than one location.
31 (4) An explanation of all services provided at the center and what services were
32 most frequently sought at the center, broken down by location if the center
33 conducts business or provides services at more than one location.
34 (5) The number of women who accessed services provided at the center during
35 the fiscal year, the geographic regions of the State where these women reside,
36 the distance from the center to the nearest licensed health care facility that
37 provides the same services offered at the center, the price of each service
38 offered at the center, and basic demographic information about each of the
39 women who accessed services provided at the center, including their race, age,
40 and marital status. Basic demographic information included in any report
41 required by this subdivision shall be published in the aggregate without any
42 personal identifying information.
43 (6) An explanation of whether the center holds itself out to the public, either in
44 person, through community participation or events, or through advertising or
45 websites, as a medical facility, health care facility, or other entity that provides
46 comprehensive counseling about all pregnancy options.
47 (7) An explanation of whether women seeking or accessing services at the center
48 are also seeking comprehensive counseling services about pregnancy options
49 or pregnancy services at a licensed health care facility and, if so, whether these
50 women have experienced a delay in receiving health care, including abortion
51 or the initiation of prenatal care, due to a visit to a center.
