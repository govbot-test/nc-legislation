Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Modernize STI Transit Funding. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Belk, Morey, T. Brown, and Lopez (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 3, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MODERNIZE STI TRANSIT FUNDING.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. G.S. 136-189.10(2)e. reads as rewritten:
5 "e. Public transportation service not included in subdivision (3) or (4) of
6 this section. This sub-subdivision includes commuter rail, intercity
7 rail, and light rail. Nothing in this sub-subdivision shall be construed
8 as authorizing total State funding in excess of the maximum
9 established in sub-subdivision g. of subdivision (3) of this section for
10 commuter rail and light rail projects."
11 SECTION 2. G.S. 136-189.10(3)g. reads as rewritten:
12 "g. Public transportation service that spans two or more counties and that
13 serves more than one municipality. Programmed funds pursuant to this
14 sub-subdivision shall not exceed ten percent (10%) of any distribution
15 region allocation. This sub-subdivision includes commuter rail,
16 intercity rail, and light rail. Total State funding for a commuter rail or
17 light rail project shall not exceed the lesser of ten percent (10%) of the
18 distribution region allocation or ten percent (10%) of the estimated
19 total project costs used during the prioritization scoring process. The
20 State shall not be responsible or liable for any project costs in excess
21 of the maximum established under this sub-subdivision. Any
22 agreement entered into by the State to fund a commuter rail or light
23 rail project shall include language setting out the limitations set forth
24 in this sub-subdivision."
25 SECTION 3. G.S. 136-189.10(4) is amended by adding a new sub-subdivision to
26 read:
27 "i. Public transportation service that spans four or more counties and that
28 serves more than three municipalities."
29 SECTION 4. G.S. 136-189.11(d1) is repealed.
30 SECTION 5. This act is effective when it becomes law.
