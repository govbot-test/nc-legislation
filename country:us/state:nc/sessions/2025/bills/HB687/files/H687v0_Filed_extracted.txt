H.B. 687
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 1, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Clarify Laws Regulating Public Swimming Pools. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Ager.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT CLARIFYING THE SCOPE OF PUBLIC SWIMMING POOL LAWS TO EXEMPT
3 FLOATATION OR SENSORY DEPRIVATION SYSTEMS THAT MEET CERTAIN
4 SAFETY STANDARDS AND MAKING OTHER TECHNICAL AND CLARIFYING
5 CHANGES.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 130A-280, as amended by Section 4.51 of S.L. 2024-49, reads as
8 rewritten:
9 "§ 130A-280. Scope.Scope, definitions, and exclusions.
10 (a) Scope. – This Article provides for the regulation of public swimming pools in the
11 State as they may affect the public health and safety.
12 (b) As used in this Article, the term "public swimming pool" means any Definitions. –
13 The following definitions apply in this Article:
14 (1) Artificial swimming lagoon. – Any body of water used for recreational
15 purposes with more than 20,000 square feet of surface area, an artificial line,
16 and a method of disinfectant that results in a disinfectant residual in the
17 swimming zone that is protective of public health.
18 (2) Public swimming pool. – Any structure, chamber, or tank containing an
19 artificial body of water used by the public for swimming, diving, wading,
20 recreation, or therapy, together with buildings, appurtenances, and equipment
21 used in connection with the body of water, regardless of whether a fee is
22 charged for its use. The term includes municipal, school, hotel, motel,
23 apartment, boarding house, athletic club, or other membership facility pools
24 and spas, spas operating for display at temporary events, and artificial
25 swimming lagoons. As used in this Article, an "artificial swimming lagoon"
26 means any body of water used for recreational purposes with more than 20,000
27 square feet of surface area, an artificial liner, and a method of disinfectant that
28 results in a disinfectant residual in the swimming zone that is protective of the
29 public health.
30 (c) Exclusions. – This Article does not apply to any of the following:
31 (1) A private pool serving a single family dwelling and used only by the residents
32 of the dwelling and their guests.
33 (2) A private pool serving a single family dwelling meeting the minimum
34 requirements of this subdivision which is offered to, and used by, individuals
35 on a temporary basis utilizing a sharing economy platform. For the purposes
36 of this subdivision, a sharing economy platform means an online platform
*DRH30061-MG-59*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 used to facilitate peer-to-peer transactions to acquire, provide, or share access
2 to goods and services. For the purposes of this subdivision, a pool must meet
3 all of the following minimum requirements:
4 a. Pools must have proper fencing and barriers to prevent unsupervised
5 access, especially by children. The fence should be at least 4 feet high
6 with a self-latching gate.
7 b. Pools must have clear and conspicuous signage posted around the pool
8 area specifying pool rules, depth markers, and any potential hazards.
9 c. Pools must be equipped with basic lifesaving equipment, including life
10 rings and reaching poles.
11 d. Pool decks and surrounding areas must have non-slip surfaces.
12 e. Pools must have properly fitted covers for all submerged suction
13 outlets.
14 f. Pools must be well-maintained with proper chemical balance and
15 cleanliness to ensure safe and healthy swimming conditions.
16 (3) Therapeutic pools used in physical therapy programs operated by medical
17 facilities licensed by the Department or operated by a licensed physical
18 therapist, nor to therapeutic chambers drained, cleaned, and refilled after each
19 individual use.
20 (4) Floatation or sensory deprivation systems certified by the National Sanitation
21 Foundation to meet the most current version of Standard 50 of the National
22 Sanitation Foundation/American National Standards Institute."
23 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
