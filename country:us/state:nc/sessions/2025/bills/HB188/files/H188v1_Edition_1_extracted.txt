Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Automatic Renewal of Contracts. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Stevens and Kidwell (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Judiciary 2, if favorable, State and Local Government, if favorable, Commerce
and Economic Development, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the
House
February 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO AMEND THE REQUIREMENTS FOR CERTAIN AUTOMATICALLY
3 RENEWING CONSUMER CONTRACTS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 75-41 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 75-41. Contracts with automatic renewal clauses.
7 (a) Any person engaged in commerce that sells, leases, or offers to sell or lease, any
8 products or services to a consumer pursuant to a contract where the contract automatically renews
9 unless the consumer cancels the contract, that contains an automatic renewal provision for a term
10 of more than one month shall do all of the following:
11 (1) Disclose the automatic renewal clause clearly and conspicuously in the
12 contract or contract offer.Provide a disclosure statement that provides notice
13 of all of the following clearly and conspicuously in at least 12-point type and
14 in bold print:
15 a. That the contract will be automatically renewed unless the consumer
16 gives notice to the seller of the consumer's intention to terminate the
17 contract prior to the renewal date. There shall be a space beside this
18 disclosure to be initialed by the consumer acknowledging the
19 consumer's specific consent to this provision of the contract.
20 b. The length of the initial term of the contract and the length of each
21 renewal period under the contract.
22 c. The amount to be charged to the consumer for the initial term of the
23 contract and the amount to be charged to the consumer for any renewal
24 periods, if known.
25 d. If any terms of the contract will change upon contract renewal, a list
26 and explanation of those terms.
27 e. An electronic mail address, mailing address, toll-free telephone
28 number, or another cost-effective, timely, and easy-to-use mechanism
29 that the consumer may use to terminate the automatic renewal. A
30 consumer who enters into a contract online shall be permitted to cancel
31 the contract online.
*H188-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (1a) Impose no charge for an automatic renewal if the consumer did not initial the
2 space provided beside the disclosure required in sub-subdivision (a)(1)a. of
3 this section.
4 (2) Disclose clearly and conspicuously how to cancel the contract in the initial
5 contract, contract offer, or with delivery of products or services.
6 (3) For any automatic renewal exceeding 60 days, of six months or more, provide
7 written notice to the consumer by personal delivery, electronic mail, or
8 first-class mail, or any other form of notice agreed to by the consumer, at least
9 15 days but no earlier than 45 60 days before the date the contract is to be
10 automatically renewed, stating the date on which the contract is scheduled to
11 automatically renew and notifying the consumer that the contract will
12 automatically renew unless it is cancelled by the consumer prior to that date.
13 (4) If the terms of the contract will change upon the automatic renewal of the
14 contract, disclose the changing terms of the contract clearly and conspicuously
15 on the notification in at least 12 point type and in bold print.
16 …
17 (d) This section does not apply to insurers licensed under Chapter 58 of the General
18 Statutes, or to banks, trust companies, savings and loan associations, savings banks, or credit
19 unions licensed or organized under the laws of any state or the United States, or any foreign bank
20 maintaining a branch or agency licensed under the laws of the United States, or any subsidiary
21 or affiliate thereof, nor does this section apply to any entity subject to regulation by the Federal
22 Communications Commission under Title 47 of the United States Code or by the North Carolina
23 Utilities Commission under Chapter 62 of the General Statutes, or to any entity doing business
24 directly or through an affiliate pursuant to a franchise, license, certificate, or other authorization
25 issued by a political subdivision of the State or an agency thereof.
26 …
27 (f) For purposes of this section, the term "automatic renewal provision" means a
28 provision under which a contract is automatically renewed at the end of a definite term for a
29 subsequent term of more than one month. Such renewal is effective unless the consumer gives
30 notice to the seller of the consumer's intention to terminate the contract."
31 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective January 1, 2026, and applies to contracts
32 entered into on or after that date.
