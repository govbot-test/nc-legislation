17 "§ 93D-7.1. Disclosure of locked hearing aid software; additional disclosures and record
18 keeping.
19 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
20 (1) Locked hearing aid. – A hearing aid that uses either proprietary programming
21 software or locked, nonproprietary programming software that restricts
22 programming or servicing of the device to specific facilities or providers.
23 (2) Locked, nonproprietary programming software. – Software that any provider
24 or seller can render inaccessible to other hearing aid programmers.
25 (3) Proprietary programming software. – Software used to program hearing aids
26 that is supplied by a hearing aid distributor or manufacturer for exclusive use
27 by affiliated providers or sellers. This software is locked and inaccessible to
28 nonaffiliated providers or sellers.
29 (b) Disclosure of Locked Programming Software. – To the extent not inconsistent with
30 federal law, any person licensed under this Chapter who sells locked hearing aids shall, before
31 consummating the sale of any locked hearing aid, provide the purchaser with a written notice, in
32 12-point type or larger, stating:
33 "The hearing aid being purchased uses proprietary or locked programming software and can
34 only be serviced or programmed at specific facilities or locations."
35 The purchaser shall sign the notice prior to sale completion. The seller shall retain a copy of
36 the signed notice for at least seven years, subject to the conditions of subsection (d) of this section.
37 (c) Written Receipt of Sale. – Upon consummation of a sale of a locked hearing aid, in
38 addition to complying with G.S. 93D-7, the licensee shall deliver to the purchaser a written
39 receipt signed by or on behalf of the licensee, containing all of the following information:
40 (1) The date of consummation of the sale.
41 (2) The make, model number, and serial number of the hearing aid sold.
42 (3) Whether the hearing aid is new, used, or reconditioned.
43 (4) The licensee's name and license number, and the name and license number of
44 any other hearing aid dispenser, apprentice, temporary licensee, or trainee
45 licensee who provided any recommendation or consultation regarding the
46 purchase.
47 (5) The address of the principal place of business of the licensee, and the address
48 and office hours at which the licensee shall be available for fitting or
49 post-fitting adjustments and servicing of the hearing aid sold.
50 (6) The terms of any guarantee or written warranty made to the purchaser with
51 respect to the hearing aid.
