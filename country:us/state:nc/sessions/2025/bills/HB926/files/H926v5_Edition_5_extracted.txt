3 SECTION 5.(a) Chapter 93D of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
4 section to read:
5 "§ 93D-7.1. Disclosure of locked hearing aid software; additional disclosures and record
6 keeping.
7 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
8 (1) Locked hearing aid. – A hearing aid that uses either proprietary programming
9 software or locked, nonproprietary programming software that restricts
10 programming or servicing of the device to specific facilities or providers.
11 (2) Locked, nonproprietary programming software. – Software that any provider
12 or seller can render inaccessible to other hearing aid programmers.
13 (3) Proprietary programming software. – Software used to program hearing aids
14 that is supplied by a hearing aid distributor or manufacturer for exclusive use
15 by affiliated providers or sellers. This software is locked and inaccessible to
16 nonaffiliated providers or sellers.
17 (b) Disclosure of Locked Programming Software. – To the extent not inconsistent with
18 federal law, any person licensed under this Chapter who sells locked hearing aids shall, before
19 consummating the sale of any locked hearing aid, provide the purchaser with a written notice, in
20 12-point type or larger, stating:
21 "The hearing aid being purchased uses proprietary or locked programming software and can
22 only be serviced or programmed at specific facilities or locations."
23 The purchaser shall sign the notice prior to sale completion. The seller shall retain a copy of
24 the signed notice for at least seven years, subject to the conditions of subsection (d) of this section.
25 (c) Written Receipt of Sale. – Upon consummation of a sale of a locked hearing aid, in
26 addition to complying with G.S. 93D-7, the licensee shall deliver to the purchaser a written
27 receipt signed by or on behalf of the licensee, containing all of the following information:
28 (1) The date of consummation of the sale.
29 (2) The make, model number, and serial number of the hearing aid sold.
30 (3) Whether the hearing aid is new, used, or reconditioned.
31 (4) The licensee's name and license number, and the name and license number of
32 any other hearing aid dispenser, apprentice, temporary licensee, or trainee
33 licensee who provided any recommendation or consultation regarding the
34 purchase.
35 (5) The address of the principal place of business of the licensee, and the address
36 and office hours at which the licensee shall be available for fitting or
37 post-fitting adjustments and servicing of the hearing aid sold.
38 (6) The terms of any guarantee or written warranty made to the purchaser with
39 respect to the hearing aid.
40 If multiple locked hearing aids are sold in a single transaction, a single written notice under
41 subsection (b) of this section and a single written receipt under this subsection may be used to
42 satisfy the requirements of this section, provided that the required information for each hearing
43 aid sold is clearly documented.
44 (d) Record Keeping. – The licensee shall maintain, for a period of at least seven years
45 after the sale, the following records for each hearing aid sold:
46 (1) A copy of the written notice described in subsection (b) of this section as
47 signed by the purchaser.
48 (2) A copy of the written receipt described in subsection (c) of this section.
49 (3) The results of any audiologic tests or measurements performed as part of the
50 fitting and dispensing of the hearing aid or aids.

41 as a taxing district. Its jurisdiction consists of only that part of Rocky Mount that is located within
42 Nash County. Rocky Mount District R is a body politic and corporate and has the power to carry

45 governing body of the district. A simple majority of the governing body constitutes a quorum,
46 and approval by a majority of those present is sufficient to determine any matter before the
47 governing body, if a quorum is present.

30 "Racing Facility and Racetrack Nuisance Immunity.
31 "§ 99E-90. Racing facility nuisance immunity.
32 (a) For purposes of this Article, the following definitions apply:
33 (1) Area of the racing facility. – Within a 3-mile radius of the perimeter of the
34 property or contiguous group of properties where a racing facility is located.
35 (2) Racing facility. – A designated area where competitive vehicle and motorsport
36 races are conducted. The term includes the track, spectator areas, garages, and
37 any associated grounds, buildings, or appurtenances used to operate the races.
38 (b) A racing facility shall not be subject to any action brought by a surrounding property
39 owner under any nuisance or taking cause of action if the developer of the racing facility obtained
40 all permits required for construction of the racing facility and established a vested right in the
41 development of the property or contiguous group of properties where the racing facility is located
42 before the surrounding property owner either purchased the real property or constructed any
43 building in the area of the racing facility."

33 ENCOURAGE ARTICLE 3A AGENCIES TO NEGOTIATE INFORMALLY
34 SECTION 30. G.S. 150B-22 reads as rewritten:
35 "§ 150B-22. Settlement; contested case.
36 (a) It is the policy of this State that any dispute between an agency and another person
37 that involves the person's rights, duties, or privileges, including licensing or the levy of a
38 monetary penalty, should be settled through informal procedures. In trying to reach a settlement
39 through informal procedures, the agency may not conduct a proceeding at which sworn testimony
40 is taken and witnesses may be cross-examined.
41 (b) If the agency and the other person do not agree to a resolution of the dispute through
42 informal procedures, either the agency or the person may commence an administrative
43 proceeding to determine the person's rights, duties, or privileges, at which time the dispute
44 becomes a "contested case." A party or person aggrieved shall not be required to petition an
45 agency for rule making or to seek or obtain a declaratory ruling before commencing a contested
46 case pursuant to G.S. 150B-23.
47 (c) This section applies to agencies covered under both this Article and Article 3A of this
48 Chapter."
