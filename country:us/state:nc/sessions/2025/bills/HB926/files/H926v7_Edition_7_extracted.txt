1 SECTION 2.(b) Article 22B of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by
2 adding a new section to read:
3 "§ 14-159.15. Limited right of entry by professional land surveyors.
4 (a) For the purposes of this section, the following terms are defined as follows:
5 (1) Critical infrastructure. – As defined in 42 U.S.C. § 5195c.
6 (2) Landowner. – The owner, lessee, or occupant of a parcel of land.
7 (3) Practice of land surveying. – As defined in G.S. 89C-3.
8 (4) Professional land surveyor. – As defined in G.S. 89C-3. For purposes of this
9 section, this term includes any agents, employees, or personnel under the
10 supervision of a professional land surveyor.
11 (b) A professional land surveyor shall have the right to enter upon the lands of others, if
12 necessary to perform surveys for the practice of land surveying, including the location of property
13 corners, boundary lines, rights-of-way, and easements, and may carry with them their customary
14 equipment and vehicles. An entry by a professional land surveyor to perform the practice of land
15 surveying under this section shall not constitute trespass under this Article or Article 22A of this
16 Chapter and shall not cause the professional land surveyor to be subject to arrest or a civil action
17 by reason of the entry.
18 (c) Nothing in this section shall be construed as giving authority to a professional land
19 surveyor to destroy, injure, damage, or move anything on the lands of another without the written
20 permission of the landowner, and nothing in this section shall be construed as removing civil
21 liability for such damage.
22 (d) No professional land surveyor shall have a civil cause of action against a landowner
23 for personal injury or property damage incurred while on the land for purposes consistent with
24 those described in subsection (b) of this section, except when such damages and injury were
25 willfully or deliberately caused by the landowner.
26 (e) Nothing in this section shall be construed as giving authority to a professional land
27 surveyor to do the following:
28 (1) Enter lands traversed by an operating railroad or properties owned, held, used,
29 or operated by a railroad or their subsidiaries.
30 (2) Enter lands containing critical infrastructure."
31 SECTION 2.(c) This section applies to acts on or after its effective date.

24 "§ 93D-7.1. Disclosure of locked hearing aid software; additional disclosures and record
25 keeping.
26 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
27 (1) Locked hearing aid. – A prescription hearing aid or an over-the-counter
28 hearing aid that uses either proprietary programming software or locked,
29 nonproprietary programming software that restricts programming or servicing
30 of the device to specific facilities or providers.
31 (2) Locked, nonproprietary programming software. – Software that any provider
32 or seller can render inaccessible to other hearing aid programmers.
33 (3) Proprietary programming software. – Software used to program hearing aids
34 that is supplied by a hearing aid distributor or manufacturer for exclusive use
35 by affiliated providers or sellers. This software is locked and inaccessible to
36 nonaffiliated providers or sellers.
37 (b) Disclosure of Locked, Nonproprietary or Proprietary Programming Software. – To
38 the extent not inconsistent with federal law, any person licensed under this Chapter who engages
39 in fitting or selling of locked hearing aids shall, at the time of purchase of any locked hearing aid,
40 provide the purchasing patient with a written notice, in 12-point type or larger, stating:
41 "The locked hearing aid being purchased uses locked, nonproprietary or proprietary
42 programming software and can only be serviced or programmed at specific facilities or
43 locations."
44 The purchasing patient shall sign the notice at the time of purchase with physical or electronic
45 signature. The licensee shall retain a copy of the signed notice in the patient's file for at least
46 three years, subject to the conditions of subsection (d) of this section.
47 (c) Written Receipt of Sale. – Upon consummation of a sale of a locked hearing aid, in
48 addition to complying with G.S. 93D-7, the licensee shall give to the purchasing patient a written
49 receipt signed, with physical or electronic signature, by or on behalf of the licensee and the
50 patient, containing all of the following information:
51 (1) The date of consummation of the sale.
