21 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
22 (1) Locked hearing aid. – A hearing aid that uses either proprietary programming
23 software or locked, nonproprietary programming software that restricts
24 programming or servicing of the device to specific facilities or providers.
25 (2) Locked, nonproprietary programming software. – Software that any provider
26 or seller can render inaccessible to other hearing aid programmers.
27 (3) Proprietary programming software. – Software used to program hearing aids
28 that is supplied by a hearing aid distributor or manufacturer for exclusive use
29 by affiliated providers or sellers. This software is locked and inaccessible to
30 nonaffiliated providers or sellers.

32 federal law, any person licensed under this Chapter who sells locked hearing aids shall, before
33 consummating the sale of any locked hearing aid, provide the purchaser with a written notice, in
34 12-point type or larger, stating:
35 "The hearing aid being purchased uses proprietary or locked programming software and can
36 only be serviced or programmed at specific facilities or locations."
37 The purchaser shall sign the notice prior to sale completion. The seller shall retain a copy of
38 the signed notice for at least seven years, subject to the conditions of subsection (d) of this section.

40 addition to complying with G.S. 93D-7, the licensee shall deliver to the purchaser a written

42 (1) The date of consummation of the sale.
43 (2) The make, model number, and serial number of the hearing aid sold.
44 (3) Whether the hearing aid is new, used, or reconditioned.
45 (4) The licensee's name and license number, and the name and license number of
46 any other hearing aid dispenser, apprentice, temporary licensee, or trainee
47 licensee who provided any recommendation or consultation regarding the
48 purchase.

5 (1) Dimension lumber. – Lumber that has not been grade-stamped under the

7 (2) Small mill. – A sawmill that mills less than 1,000,000 board feet of lumber
8 per year.
9 SECTION 10.(b) The North Carolina Residential Code Council shall amend the
10 North Carolina Residential Code in order to permit dimension lumber that has not been
11 grade-stamped under the authority of a lumber grading bureau to be used in the construction of
12 one- and two-family dwellings, when that use meets all of the following requirements:
13 (1) The lumber is sold directly by the owner or employee of the sawmill that
14 milled the lumber to the owner of the dwelling to be constructed or that
15 person's authorized representative.
16 (2) The dimension lumber meets or exceeds the requirements of the North
17 Carolina Residential Code other than the requirements that only
18 grade-stamped lumber be used in residential construction.
19 (3) The operator of the sawmill has a certificate from a State-approved lumber
20 grading training program, certifies that the lumber conforms with product and
21 inspection standards under American Softwood Lumber Standard PS20, and
22 marks the lumber with (i) the mill number, name, or abbreviation, (ii) the
23 species or combination of species of the lumber, (iii) whether the lumber was
24 dry or green when manufactured as required by American Softwood Lumber
25 Standard PS20, and (iv) whether the lumber conforms with PS20 standards.
26 (4) The appropriate code enforcement official reviews the framing of the dwelling
27 to ensure that it meets the requirements of the North Carolina Residential
28 Code in all respects other than the requirements that only grade-stamped
29 lumber be used in residential construction. The code enforcement official shall
30 not be liable for any structural failure that occurs as a result of the use of
31 dimension lumber rather than grade-stamped lumber.
