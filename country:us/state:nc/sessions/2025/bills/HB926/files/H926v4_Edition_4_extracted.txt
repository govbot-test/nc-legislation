4 SECTION 5.(a) Chapter 93D of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
5 section to read:
6 "§ 93D-7.1. Disclosure of locked hearing aid software; additional disclosures and record
7 keeping.
8 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
9 (1) Locked hearing aid. – A hearing aid that uses either proprietary programming
10 software or locked, nonproprietary programming software that restricts
11 programming or servicing of the device to specific facilities or providers.
12 (2) Locked, nonproprietary programming software. – Software that any provider
13 or seller can render inaccessible to other hearing aid programmers.
14 (3) Proprietary programming software. – Software used to program hearing aids
15 that is supplied by a hearing aid distributor or manufacturer for exclusive use
16 by affiliated providers or sellers. This software is locked and inaccessible to
17 nonaffiliated providers or sellers.
18 (b) Disclosure of Locked Programming Software. – To the extent not inconsistent with
19 federal law, any person licensed under this Chapter who sells locked hearing aids shall, before
20 consummating the sale of any locked hearing aid, provide the purchaser with a written notice, in
21 12-point type or larger, stating:
22 "The hearing aid being purchased uses proprietary or locked programming software and can
23 only be serviced or programmed at specific facilities or locations."
24 The purchaser shall sign the notice prior to sale completion. The seller shall retain a copy of
25 the signed notice for at least seven years, subject to the conditions of subsection (d) of this section.
26 (c) Written Receipt of Sale. – Upon consummation of a sale of a locked hearing aid, in
27 addition to complying with G.S. 93D-7, the licensee shall deliver to the purchaser a written
28 receipt signed by or on behalf of the licensee, containing all of the following information:
29 (1) The date of consummation of the sale.
30 (2) The make, model number, and serial number of the hearing aid sold.
31 (3) Whether the hearing aid is new, used, or reconditioned.
32 (4) The licensee's name and license number, and the name and license number of
33 any other hearing aid dispenser, apprentice, temporary licensee, or trainee
34 licensee who provided any recommendation or consultation regarding the
35 purchase.
36 (5) The address of the principal place of business of the licensee, and the address
37 and office hours at which the licensee shall be available for fitting or
38 post-fitting adjustments and servicing of the hearing aid sold.
39 (6) The terms of any guarantee or written warranty made to the purchaser with
40 respect to the hearing aid.
41 If multiple locked hearing aids are sold in a single transaction, a single written notice under
42 subsection (b) of this section and a single written receipt under this subsection may be used to
43 satisfy the requirements of this section, provided that the required information for each hearing
44 aid sold is clearly documented.
45 (d) Record Keeping. – The licensee shall maintain, for a period of at least seven years
46 after the sale, the following records for each hearing aid sold:
47 (1) A copy of the written notice described in subsection (b) of this section as
48 signed by the purchaser.
49 (2) A copy of the written receipt described in subsection (c) of this section.
50 (3) The results of any audiologic tests or measurements performed as part of the
51 fitting and dispensing of the hearing aid or aids.
