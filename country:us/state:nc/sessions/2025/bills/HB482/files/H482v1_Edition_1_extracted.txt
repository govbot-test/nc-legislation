SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Reauthorize & Revise Teacher Bonuses/Military. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives F. Jackson, Loftis, and Lofton (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Education - K-12, if favorable, Homeland Security and Military and Veterans
Affairs, if favorable, Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and
Operations of the House
March 25, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REAUTHORIZE BONUSES FOR TEACHERS FOR THE 2025-2027 FISCAL
3 BIENNIUM AND TO REVISE THE PROGRAM TO PERMIT TEACHERS TO RECEIVE
4 THE BONUSES WHO ARE UNABLE TO CONTINUE TEACHING IN THE SAME
5 PUBLIC SCHOOL UNIT BECAUSE OF MILITARY ORDERS.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1.(a) Establish Consolidated Bonus Program. – The State Board of
8 Education shall establish a consolidated teacher bonus program for the 2025-2027 fiscal
9 biennium to reward teacher performance and encourage student learning and improvement. To
10 attain this goal, the Department of Public Instruction shall administer bonus pay to qualifying
11 teachers whose salaries are supported from State funds in January of 2026 and 2027, based on
12 data from the 2024-2025 and 2025-2026 school years, respectively, in accordance with this
13 section.
14 SECTION 1.(b) Definitions. – For purposes of this section, the following definitions
15 shall apply:
16 (1) Eligible advanced course teacher. – A teacher of Advanced Placement
17 courses, International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme courses, or the
18 Cambridge Advanced International Certificate of Education (AICE) program
19 who meets the following criteria:
20 a. Is employed by, or retired having last held a position at, one or more
21 of the following:
22 1. A qualifying public school unit.
23 2. The North Carolina Virtual Public School program.
24 b. Taught one or more students who received a score listed in subsection
25 (c) of this section.
26 (2) Eligible career and technical education (CTE) teacher. – A teacher who meets
27 the following criteria:
28 a. Is employed by, or retired having last held a position at, a qualifying
29 public school unit.
30 b. Taught one or more students who attained approved industry
31 certifications or credentials consistent with G.S. 115C-156.2.
32 (3) Eligible growth teacher. – A teacher who meets at least one of the following
33 criteria:

1 a. Is employed by, or retired having last held a position at, a qualifying
2 public school unit and meets one of the following criteria:
3 1. Is in the top twenty-five percent (25%) of teachers in the State
4 according to the EVAAS student growth index score for third
5 grade reading from the previous school year.
6 2. Is in the top twenty-five percent (25%) of teachers in the State
7 according to the EVAAS student growth index score for fourth
8 or fifth grade reading from the previous school year.
9 3. Is in the top twenty-five percent (25%) of teachers in the State
10 according to the EVAAS student growth index score for fourth,
11 fifth, sixth, seventh, or eighth grade mathematics from the
12 previous school year.
13 b. Is employed by, or retired having last held a position at, a local school
14 administrative unit and meets one of the following criteria:
15 1. Is in the top twenty-five percent (25%) of teachers in the
16 teacher's respective local school administrative unit according
17 to the EVAAS student growth index score for third grade
18 reading from the previous school year.
19 2. Is in the top twenty-five percent (25%) of teachers in the
20 teacher's respective local school administrative unit according
21 to the EVAAS student growth index score for fourth or fifth
22 grade reading from the previous school year.
23 3. Is in the top twenty-five percent (25%) of teachers in the
24 teacher's respective local school administrative unit according
25 to the EVAAS student growth index score for fourth, fifth,
26 sixth, seventh, or eighth grade mathematics from the previous
27 school year.
28 c. Was employed by a local school administrative unit that employed in
29 the previous school year three or fewer total teachers in that teacher's
30 grade level as long as the teacher has an EVAAS student growth index
31 score from the previous school year of exceeded expected growth in
32 one of the following subject areas:
33 1. Third grade reading.
34 2. Fourth or fifth grade reading.
35 3. Fourth, fifth, sixth, seventh, or eighth grade mathematics.
36 (4) EVAAS. – The Education Value-Added Assessment System.
37 (5) Qualifying public school unit. – Any of the following:
38 a. A local school administrative unit.
39 b. A charter school.
40 c. A regional school.
41 d. A school providing elementary or secondary instruction operated by
42 The University of North Carolina under Article 29A of Chapter 116 of
43 the General Statutes.
44 (6) Qualifying teacher. – An eligible teacher who meets one of the following
45 criteria:
46 a. Remains employed teaching in the same qualifying public school unit
47 or, if an eligible advanced course teacher is only employed by the
48 North Carolina Virtual Public School program, remains employed
49 teaching in that program, at least from the school year the data is
50 collected until January 1 of the corresponding school year that the
51 bonus is paid.

1 b. Retired, between the last day of the school year in which the data is
2 collected and January 1 of the corresponding school year in which the
3 bonus is paid, after attaining one of the following:
4 1. The age of at least 65 with five years of creditable service.
5 2. The age of at least 60 with 25 years of creditable service.
6 3. Thirty years of creditable service.
7 c. Left employment with the qualifying public school unit after the last
8 day of the school year in which the data is collected due to the transfer
9 or pending transfer of a spouse pursuant to an official military order.
10 In order to receive a bonus pursuant to this sub-subdivision, a
11 qualifying teacher shall provide the following information to the
12 qualifying public school unit by January 1 of the school year in which
13 the bonus is to be paid:
14 1. Notice of the transfer.
15 2. An updated mailing address for the teacher.
16 SECTION 1.(c) Advanced Course Bonuses. – A bonus in the amount of fifty dollars
17 ($50.00) shall be provided to qualifying advanced course teachers for each student taught in each
18 advanced course who receives the following score:
19 (1) For Advanced Placement courses, a score of three or higher on the College
20 Board Advanced Placement Examination.
21 (2) For International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme courses, a score of four
22 or higher on the International Baccalaureate course examination.
23 (3) For the Cambridge AICE program, a score of "E" or higher on the Cambridge
24 AICE program examinations.
25 SECTION 1.(d) CTE Bonuses. – For qualifying career and technical education
26 teachers, bonuses shall be provided in the following amounts:
27 (1) A bonus in the amount of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for each student taught
28 by a teacher who provided instruction in a course that led to the attainment of
29 an industry certification or credential with a twenty-five dollar ($25.00) value
30 ranking as determined under subsection (e) of this section.
31 (2) A bonus in the amount of fifty dollars ($50.00) for each student taught by a
32 teacher who provided instruction in a course that led to the attainment of an
33 industry certification or credential with a fifty dollar ($50.00) value ranking
34 as determined under subsection (e) of this section.
35 SECTION 1.(e) CTE Course Value Ranking. – The Department of Commerce, in
36 consultation with the State Board, shall assign a value ranking for each industry certification and
37 credential based on academic rigor and employment value in accordance with this subsection.
38 Fifty percent (50%) of the ranking shall be based on academic rigor and the remaining fifty
39 percent (50%) on employment value. Academic rigor and employment value shall be based on
40 the following elements:
41 (1) Academic rigor shall be based on the number of instructional hours, including
42 work experience or internship hours, required to earn the industry certification
43 or credential, with extra weight given for coursework that also provides
44 community college credit.
45 (2) Employment value shall be based on the entry wage, growth rate in
46 employment for each occupational category, and average annual openings for
47 the primary occupation linked with the industry certification or credential.
48 SECTION 1.(f) Statewide Growth Bonuses. – Of the funds appropriated in this act
49 for the program, bonuses shall be provided to qualifying teachers who are eligible teachers under
50 sub-subdivision a. of subdivision (3) of subsection (b) of this section, as follows:

13 subdivision (3) of subsection (b) of this section, as follows:
14 (1) The sum of five million dollars ($5,000,000) shall be allocated for bonuses to

17 proportionally based on average daily membership in third grade for each

24 each qualifying teacher who is an eligible teacher under sub-sub-subdivision
25 b.3. or c.3. of subdivision (3) of subsection (b) of this section.
26 SECTION 1.(h) Limitations and Other Criteria. – The following additional
27 limitations and other criteria shall apply to the program:
28 (1) Bonus funds awarded to a teacher pursuant to subsection (c), subsection (d),
29 subdivision (1) of subsection (f), and subdivision (1) of subsection (g) of this
30 section shall not exceed three thousand five hundred dollars ($3,500) per
31 subsection or subdivision in any given school year.
32 (2) A qualifying teacher who is an eligible teacher under sub-sub-subdivision a.1.,
33 b.1., or c.1. of subdivision (3) of subsection (b) of this section may receive a
34 bonus under both subdivision (1) of subsection (f) and subdivision (1) of
35 subsection (g) of this section but shall not receive more than seven thousand
36 dollars ($7,000) pursuant to subdivision (1) of subsection (f) and subdivision
37 (1) of subsection (g) of this section in any given school year.
38 (3) A qualifying teacher who is an eligible teacher under sub-sub-subdivision a.2.,
39 b.2., or c.2. of subdivision (3) of subsection (b) of this section may receive a
40 bonus under both subdivision (2) of subsection (f) and subdivision (2) of
41 subsection (g) of this section but shall not receive more than two bonuses
42 pursuant to subdivision (2) of subsection (f) and subdivision (2) of subsection
43 (g) of this section in any given school year.
44 (4) A qualifying teacher who is an eligible teacher under sub-sub-subdivision a.3.,
45 b.3., or c.3. of subdivision (3) of subsection (b) of this section may receive a
46 bonus under both subdivision (3) of subsection (f) and subdivision (3) of
47 subsection (g) of this section but shall not receive more than two bonuses
48 pursuant to subdivision (3) of subsection (f) and subdivision (3) of subsection
49 (g) of this section in any given school year.
50 SECTION 1.(i) Bonuses Not Compensation. – Bonuses awarded to a teacher
51 pursuant to this section shall be in addition to any regular wage or other bonus the teacher receives
