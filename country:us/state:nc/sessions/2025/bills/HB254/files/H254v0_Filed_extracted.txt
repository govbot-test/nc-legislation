H.B. 254
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Feb 27, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
HOUSE BILL DRH40149-LG-86
Short Title: Adopt Tuskegee Airmen Day. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Price.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO DESIGNATE THE FOURTH THURSDAY OF MARCH OF EVERY YEAR AS
3 TUSKEGEE AIRMEN COMMEMORATION DAY IN NORTH CAROLINA.
4 Whereas, in 1941 the federal government established the 66th Air Force Flying
5 School at Tuskegee Institute to train African-American pilots for the war; and
6 Whereas, from 1941 through 1946, almost 1,000 African-Americans trained at the
7 Tuskegee Army Air Field in Tuskegee, Alabama; and
8 Whereas, 450 of the men who completed training at the Tuskegee Army Air Field
9 were sent overseas for combat duty; and
10 Whereas, pilots trained at Tuskegee Institute were known as "Tuskegee Airmen,"
11 although this phrase also later included navigators, bombardiers, instructors, and maintenance
12 and support staff; and
13 Whereas, the 99th Fighter Squadron, the first class trained at Tuskegee Institute, was
14 sent to North Africa in the spring of 1943 for combat duty and in 1944, they were joined by other
15 African-American squadrons to form the 332nd Fighter Squadron, which flew missions over
16 Sicily, the Mediterranean, and North Africa; and
17 Whereas, the Tuskegee Airmen compiled an outstanding record, which included
18 completing more than 1,500 missions, destroying 260 enemy aircraft, sinking an enemy
19 destroyer, and demolishing other enemy installation areas; and
20 Whereas, the Tuskegee Airmen achieved an exceptional record for escorting bomber
21 crews and earned the respect of their fellow bomber crews and their military leaders; and
22 Whereas, on March 27, 2007, President George W. Bush awarded the Tuskegee
23 Airmen the Congressional Gold, the highest medal awarded by the United States Congress; and
24 Whereas, it is fitting that the State of North Carolina designate the fourth Thursday
25 of March, of each year, to honor and perpetuate the legacy of the men and women who
26 participated in the Tuskegee Experience from January 1941 through July 1949; Now, therefore,
27 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
28 SECTION 1. Chapter 103 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
29 section to read:
30 "§ 103-19. Tuskegee Airmen Commemoration Day.
31 The fourth Thursday of March is designated as Tuskegee Airmen Commemoration Day in
32 North Carolina."
33 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
