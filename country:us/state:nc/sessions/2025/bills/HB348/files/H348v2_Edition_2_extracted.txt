Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/30/25
Short Title: Annexation of PUV Land/School Capacity. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 11, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE THAT PRIOR TO ANNEXING CERTAIN PRESENT-USE VALUE
3 PROPERTY, A CITY MUST OBTAIN APPROVAL FROM THE BOARD OF COUNTY
4 COMMISSIONERS.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 160A-58.2 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 160A-58.2. Public hearing.
8 (a) Upon receipt of a petition for annexation under this Part, the city council shall cause
9 the city clerk to investigate the petition, and to certify the results of his investigation. If the clerk
10 certifies that upon investigation the petition appears to be valid, the council shall fix a date for a
11 public hearing on the annexation. Notice of the hearing shall be published once at least 10 days
12 before the date of hearing.
13 (b) At the hearing, any person residing in or owning property in the area proposed for
14 annexation and any resident of the annexing city may appear and be heard on the questions of
15 the sufficiency of the petition and the desirability of the annexation. If the council then finds and
16 determines that (i) the area described in the petition meets all of the standards set out in
17 G.S. 160A-58.1(b), (ii) the petition bears the signatures of all of the owners of real property
18 within the area proposed for annexation (except those not required to sign by G.S. 160A-58.1(a)),
19 (iii) the petition is otherwise valid, and (iv) the public health, safety and welfare of the inhabitants
20 of the city and of the area proposed for annexation will be best served by the annexation, the
21 council may adopt an ordinance annexing the area described in the petition. The ordinance may
22 be made effective immediately or on any specified date within six months from the date of
23 passage.
24 (c) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (b) of this section, prior to the public
25 hearing, the city council shall direct the city planning department to consult with the planning
26 department of the county with jurisdiction over the area proposed for annexation to determine
27 whether zoning the area for residential use will increase the number of students attending public
28 school in the county to more than one hundred percent (100%) of the county's current capacity,
29 if the area meets all of the following:
30 (1) Is agricultural land, forestland, or horticultural land, as defined in
31 G.S. 105-277.2.
32 (2) Is not contiguous to the city's primary corporate limits.
33 (3) Is not within the city's extraterritorial planning jurisdiction.
34 (d) If the planning department finds that zoning the area for residential use will increase
35 the number of students as provided in subsection (c) of this section, the board of county
36 commissioners with jurisdiction over the area must approve the annexation prior to the city
*H348-v-2*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 council adopting the annexation ordinance. If the board of county commissioners does not
2 approve the annexation, the city council may not proceed with the adoption of the annexation
3 ordinance unless it agrees to pay the county the amount necessary to come back into compliance
4 with school capacity."
5 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025, and applies to petitions for
6 annexation received on or after that date.
