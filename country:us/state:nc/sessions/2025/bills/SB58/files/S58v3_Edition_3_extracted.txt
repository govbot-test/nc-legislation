Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 3
Judiciary Committee Substitute Adopted 2/26/25
Third Edition Engrossed 3/11/25
Short Title: AG/Restrict Challenge to Presidential EOs. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
February 6, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO LIMIT THE ATTORNEY GENERAL'S PARTICIPATION IN LITIGATION
3 THAT WOULD RESULT IN THE INVALIDATION OF ANY EXECUTIVE ORDER
4 ISSUED BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES AND TO REQUIRE THE
5 ATTORNEY GENERAL TO SUBMIT A REPORT REGARDING VIOLATIONS OF
6 G.S. 14-277.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1. G.S. 114-2.8, as enacted by Section 3D.1 of S.L. 2024-57, reads as
9 rewritten:
10 "§ 114-2.8. Limitation on participation in foreign certain litigation.
11 (a) The Attorney General shall not, as a party, amicus, or any other participant in an
12 action pending before a state or federal court in another state, advance any argument that would
13 result in the invalidation of any statute enacted by the General Assembly.
14 (b) The Attorney General shall not, as a party, amicus, or any other participant, do either
15 of the following in any state or federal court:
16 (1) File an action that would result in the invalidation of an executive order issued
17 by the President of the United States.
18 (2) Advance any argument in a pending action that would result in the
19 invalidation of any executive order issued by the President of the United
20 States."
21 SECTION 2. No later than June 1, 2025, the Attorney General shall submit a report
22 to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Justice and Public Safety containing the
23 following information to the extent the information is available:
24 (1) How many convictions for violations of G.S. 14-277 have been obtained from
25 January 1, 2021 through March 1, 2025.
26 (2) How many convictions for violations of G.S. 14-277 obtained from January
27 1, 2021 through March 1, 2025 were based on the defendant's impersonation
28 of an agent of the Immigration and Customs Enforcement of the United States
29 Department of Homeland Security.
30 The Attorney General shall request the required information and coordinate the
31 required response by requesting information from all necessary parties, including but not limited
32 to the North Carolina Administrative Office of the Courts, the North Carolina Conference of
33 District Attorneys, and any elected district attorney.
34 SECTION 3. Section 1 is effective when it becomes law and applies to actions filed
35 or commenced on or after that date. The remainder of this act is effective when it becomes law.
*S58-v-3*
