Section 1:
H.B. 579
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Mar 31, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: NC House Public Access Act of 2025. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Cervania.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO FACILITATE CIVIC ENGAGEMENT WITH AND TRANSPARENCY OF
3 LEGISLATIVE PROCEEDINGS BY EQUIPPING ADDITIONAL AREAS OF THE
4 GENERAL ASSEMBLY COMPLEX WITH EQUIPMENT TO ALLOW FOR
5 REAL-TIME AND TIME-SHIFTED REMOTE VIEWING.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the General Assembly
8 the nonrecurring sum of ninety-one thousand dollars ($91,000) and the recurring sum of four
9 thousand dollars ($4,000) for the 2025-2026 fiscal year for the purpose of obtaining and
10 installing, as soon as practicable, and maintaining equipment in committee meeting rooms and
11 the chamber of the House of Representatives to allow for recordation of and viewing by the
12 public via the internet of committee meetings and chamber proceedings to make possible (i)
13 real-time access via the internet to the audio and the video of committee meetings and chamber
14 proceedings as provided herein and (ii) archival of and time-shifted viewing access via the
15 internet to the audio and video of committee meetings and chamber proceedings as provided
16 herein for a period of 10 years. It is the intent of the General Assembly by this act to increase the
17 availability of real-time access and archiving for time-shifted viewing of committee meetings
18 and chamber proceedings. The use of equipment and degree of availability funded by the
19 appropriation in this act will be determined with greater specificity by the rules of the House of
20 Representatives adopted for each Regular Session of the General Assembly.
21 SECTION 2. Unless otherwise prohibited by State law or applicable rules adopted
22 by one or both chambers of the General Assembly, the presiding chair of a legislative committee
23 may, in the chair's discretion, decide not to make a video recordation of a committee meeting,
24 provided that the chair (i) provides notice of the decision not to record prior to the start of the
25 meeting and (ii) ensures an official audio recording and written minutes are maintained in
26 accordance with applicable public records laws.
27 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
