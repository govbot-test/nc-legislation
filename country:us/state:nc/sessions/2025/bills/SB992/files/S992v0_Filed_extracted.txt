1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO IMPLEMENT TRUTH IN TAXATION PROCEDURES WHEN A GOVERNING
3 BODY OF A TAXING UNIT INTENDS TO ADOPT A TAX RATE EXCEEDING THE
4 REVENUE-NEUTRAL TAX RATE IN ANY YEAR A GENERAL REAPPRAISAL IS
5 CONDUCTED AND TO INCREASE THE FEE FOR REINSTATING AN EXPIRED
6 REAL ESTATE APPRAISER TRAINEE REGISTRATION, LICENSE, OR
7 CERTIFICATE.
8 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
9 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 159-13(b) is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
10 "(7a) In the year in which a general reappraisal of real property has been conducted,
11 the governing board shall follow the procedure required under G.S. 159-13.3
12 in order to adopt a property tax rate exceeding the revenue-neutral property
13 tax rate determined under G.S. 159-11(e)."
14 SECTION 1.(b) Chapter 159 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
15 section to read:
16 "§ 159-13.3. Adoption of tax rate exceeding revenue-neutral tax rate.
17 (a) Rate Prohibition. – In each year in which a general reappraisal of property is
18 conducted, the governing body of a taxing jurisdiction may not adopt a property tax rate
19 exceeding the revenue-neutral tax rate determined under G.S. 159-11(e) unless a resolution or
20 ordinance has been approved by the governing body as provided under this section.
21 (b) General Notice of Intent. – The governing body shall publish notice of its intent to
22 exceed the revenue-neutral tax rate in the following formats:
23 (1) In a newspaper of general circulation in the taxing jurisdiction at least one
24 week prior to the public hearing required under subsection (d) of this section.
25 (2) On the website of the governing body, if applicable, at least 10 days prior to
26 the public hearing required under subsection (d) of this section.
27 (c) Specific Notice of Intent. – At least 30 days prior to the public hearing required under
28 subsection (d) of this section, the governing body shall notify the county clerk of its proposed
29 intent to exceed the revenue-neutral tax rate and shall provide the clerk the information required
30 to be included in the notice sent to taxpayers in this subsection. The clerk shall send notice to
31 each taxpayer with property in the taxing jurisdiction, by first-class mail at the taxpayer's last
32 known address, of the governing body's intent to exceed the revenue-neutral tax rate at least 10
33 days prior to the public hearing. Alternatively, if the taxpayer has consented to receive notice by
34 electronic means, the clerk may send notice to the taxpayer by such means at least 10 days prior
35 to the public hearing. The taxing unit shall bear the costs of providing the notice required under
36 this subsection. The county clerk shall consolidate the information required under this subsection

1 for all applicable taxing units associated with the taxpayer's property into one notice. The notice
2 shall include, at a minimum, the following information specific to each taxing unit:
3 (1) The proposed amount of property tax revenue needed to balance the taxing
4 unit's budget.
5 (2) The revenue-neutral tax rate determined under G.S. 159-11(e).
6 (3) The proposed tax rate for the upcoming tax year.
7 (4) The amount of tax due on the property for the current tax year.
8 (5) The estimated amount of tax due on the property for the upcoming tax year if
9 the revenue-neutral rate is adopted.
10 (6) The estimated amount of tax due on the property for the upcoming tax year if
11 the proposed tax rate is adopted.
12 (7) The date, time, and location of the public hearing.
13 (d) Public Hearing. – The governing body shall hold a public hearing on the issue of
14 exceeding the revenue-neutral property tax rate. The public hearing may be held at or before the
15 public hearing required under G.S. 159-12; however, if the public hearing occurs at the same
16 time as the public hearing required under G.S. 159-12, the governing body shall dedicate a
17 portion of the hearing to the issue of exceeding the revenue-neutral property tax rate at which
18 time any persons who wish to be heard on the subject may appear.
19 (e) Majority Approval. – The governing body shall vote on the matter of exceeding the
20 revenue-neutral property tax rate at the hearing required under subsection (d) of this section. The
21 governing body shall adopt a resolution or ordinance to exceed the revenue-neutral property tax
22 rate by majority vote which shall occur prior to the adoption of the budget ordinance.
23 (f) Tax Refund. – A governing body that fails to comply with this section shall refund
24 any amount of property taxes collected in excess of the amount that would have been collected
25 under the revenue-neutral property tax rate."
26 SECTION 1.(c) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies to budget
27 ordinances adopted for fiscal years beginning on or after July 1, 2027.
