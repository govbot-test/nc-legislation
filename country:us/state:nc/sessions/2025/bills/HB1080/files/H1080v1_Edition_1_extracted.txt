14 Whereas, the General Assembly recognizes that chronic and historic underfunding
15 and workforce instability in critical State institutions have contributed to a public safety crisis
16 where courts and jail systems are overburdened, weakening timely responses and increasing the
17 risk of harm to the public, victims, and frontline personnel; and
18 Whereas, the General Assembly finds that high-volume judicial districts require
19 sufficient clerk and prosecutorial capacity to ensure the timely administration of justice and
20 maintain public confidence in the courts; and
21 Whereas, the General Assembly finds that safe and reliable operations in correctional
22 institutions, juvenile justice facilities, law enforcement agencies, forensic laboratories, and public
23 transportation systems depend upon the recruitment and retention of qualified personnel and the
24 modernization of critical safety infrastructure; and
25 Whereas, the General Assembly finds that targeted investments in staffing,
26 compensation, workplace safety, and operational modernization are necessary to restore capacity
27 and strengthen public safety outcomes statewide; Now, therefore,
28 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

30 PUBLIC TRANSIT SAFETY MODERNIZATION
31 SECTION 1.(a) Article 2B of Chapter 136 of the General Statutes is amended by
32 adding a new section to read:
33 "§ 136-44.28. Emergency trauma kits and first aid training.
34 (a) All public transportation operators receiving State funds, including any metropolitan
35 transit authority, regional transit authority, or municipal transit system operating bus, rail, light

2 shall equip every revenue service vehicle, including each bus and each individual train car, with
3 an emergency trauma response kit.
4 (b) Each emergency trauma response kit shall include, at minimum, all of the following
5 items:
6 (1) Commercially manufactured tourniquets.

14 shall complete emergency first aid and bleeding control training consistent with nationally
15 recognized standards, including training in the proper use of tourniquets and trauma response
16 kits. Such training shall be completed within six months of hire and refreshed at least once every
17 two years. An employee who renders emergency aid in good faith pursuant to this section shall
18 be afforded the protections of G.S. 90-21.14.
19 (d) The Department of Health and Human Services, in consultation with the Department
20 of Transportation and the Department of Public Safety, shall adopt minimum implementation
21 standards to ensure consistency across public transportation systems."
22 SECTION 1.(b) Article 2B of Chapter 136 of the General Statutes is amended by
23 adding a new section to read:
24 "§ 136‑44.29. Fare enforcement and anti-fare-dodging measures.
25 (a) All public transportation operators receiving State funds shall implement reasonable
26 and effective measures to deter and prevent fare evasion. Such measures shall include all of the
27 following:
28 (1) Installation and use of physical access control systems, including turnstiles or
29 gated entry systems where operationally and structurally practicable based on
30 facility design.
31 (2) Use of electronic validation systems, including barcode scanners, mobile
32 ticket validation, or contactless fare verification technology.
33 (3) In-person fare inspection programs, including uniformed or plainclothes fare
34 inspectors.
35 (4) Randomized fare verification protocols.
36 (5) Any other technology or enforcement practice determined appropriate by the
37 governing board of the transit authority.
38 (b) Each public transportation operator shall adopt written fare enforcement policies that
39 balance revenue protection, public safety, and equitable enforcement and shall make such
40 policies publicly available."
41 SECTION 1.(c) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
42 Transportation the sum of ten million dollars ($10,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the
43 2026-2027 fiscal year to implement the provisions of G.S. 136‑44.28 and G.S. 136‑44.29, as
44 enacted in this section. Funds appropriated in this section shall be distributed by the Department
45 of Transportation to public transportation operators receiving State funds through a
46 formula-based allocation consistent with existing public transportation funding formulas
47 administered by the Department. Funds may be used for:
48 (1) Purchase and installation of emergency trauma response kits.
49 (2) Training of transit personnel in first aid and bleeding control.
50 (3) Capital expenditures associated with fare enforcement modernization,
51 including access control systems and electronic fare validation technology.

23 COURT SYSTEM STAFFING FOR WAKE AND MECKLENBURG COUNTIES
24 SECTION 2.(a) Beginning in the 2026-2027 fiscal year, there is appropriated from
25 the General Fund to the Administrative Office of the Courts (AOC) the sum of thirty million
26 dollars ($30,000,000) in recurring funds to be used to hire in Wake and Mecklenburg Counties

30 Division on (i) the number of positions allocated to each county and (ii) the reasoning used in
31 determining those numbers.

33 the General Fund to the AOC, Budget Fund 100072, the sum of thirty million dollars
34 ($30,000,000) in recurring funds to be used to hire in Wake and Mecklenburg Counties assistant
35 district attorney positions. Notwithstanding G.S. 7A-60, the AOC shall determine the appropriate
36 allocation of positions between the counties and, no later than 60 days after the allocation,
37 provide a report to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Justice and Public Safety and
38 the Fiscal Research Division on (i) the number of positions allocated to each county and (ii) the
39 reasoning used in determining those numbers.
40 SECTION 2.(c) No later than July 1, 2027, the Department of Health and Human
41 Services, Division of Mental Health, Developmental Disabilities, and Substance Use Services,
42 in coordination with the Administrative Office of the Courts, shall provide a report to the Joint
43 Legislative Oversight Committee on Justice and Public Safety, the Joint Legislative Oversight
44 Committee on Health and Human Services, and the Fiscal Research Division on the status of the
45 appropriation contained in this section, including (i) cost-savings to the court system, (ii)
46 reductions in case loads, (iii) reductions in the State's incarcerated population, and (iv) any
47 impacts on recidivism. An updated report shall be provided no later than December 1, 2027, and
48 annually thereafter.
