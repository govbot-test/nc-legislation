Short Title: Conscientious Objections to Vaccine Mandates. (Public)

Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A CONSCIENTIOUS OBJECTION EXEMPTION TO NORTH
3 CAROLINA'S STUDENT VACCINATION REQUIREMENTS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 130A-155(c) reads as rewritten:
6 "(c) The school shall file an annual immunization report with the Department by
7 November 1. The child care facility shall file an immunization report annually with the
8 Department. The report shall be filed on forms prepared by the Department and shall state the
9 number of children attending the school or facility, the number of children who had not obtained
10 the required immunization within 30 days of their first attendance, the number of children who
11 received a medical exemption and the number of children who received a religious or
12 conscientious objection exemption."
13 SECTION 2. G.S. 130A-155.1(c) reads as rewritten:
14 "(c) Within 60 calendar days after the commencement of a new school year, the college
15 or university shall file an immunization report with the Department. The report shall be filed on
16 forms prepared by the Department and shall state the number of students attending the school or
17 facility, the number of students who had not obtained the required immunization within 30 days
18 of their first attendance, the number of students who received a medical exemption and the
19 number of students who received a religious or conscientious objection exemption."
20 SECTION 3. G.S. 130A-157 reads as rewritten:
21 "§ 130A-157. Religious or conscientious objection exemption.
22 If the bona fide religious beliefs of an adult or the parent, guardian or person in loco parentis
23 of a child has religious beliefs or reasons of conscience that are contrary to the immunization
24 requirements contained in prescribed by this Chapter, the adult or and the child shall be are
25 exempt from the these requirements. Upon submission of a written statement of the bona fide
26 religious beliefs or reasons of conscience and opposition to the immunization requirements, the
27 person may attend the college, university, school or facility without presenting a certificate of
28 immunization."
29 SECTION 4. G.S. 115C-76.25(a) reads as rewritten:
30 "§ 115C-76.25. Parent legal rights for their child's education.
31 (a) Parents have legal rights with regards to their child's education, including the
32 following:
33 …
34 (2) The right to seek a medical or religious medical, religious, or conscientious
35 objection exemption from immunization requirements, consistent with the
36 requirements of G.S. 130A-156 and G.S. 130A-157.
