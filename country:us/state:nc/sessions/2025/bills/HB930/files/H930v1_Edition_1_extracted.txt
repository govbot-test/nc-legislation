6 PART I. FIRST RESPONDER APPRECIATION BONUS PROGRAM
7 SECTION 1.1. The following definitions apply in this Part:
8 (1) Appreciation bonus payment or bonus. – A one-time supplemental payment
9 to a first responder that does not become part of the first responder's base
10 salary.
11 (2) Base salary. – The regular, recurring annual compensation of a first responder,
12 excluding overtime pay, shift differentials, longevity pay, or any other
13 supplemental forms of compensation.
14 (3) Employing authority. – A State agency or a city, county, or other local
15 political subdivision of this State that employs first responders.
16 (4) First responder. – A public employee whose primary duties involve the
17 protection of life, property, or public safety during emergency situations,
18 including specifically:
19 a. Law enforcement officers.
20 b. Firefighters.
21 c. Emergency medical services personnel.
22 d. Public safety telecommunicators or dispatchers.
23 e. Nurses employed by public hospitals, health departments, correctional
24 facilities, or other government healthcare facilities.
25 (5) Public employee. – A person employed by an employing authority.
26 (6) Years of service. – The total number of years that a first responder has been
27 employed in a qualifying position by any employing authority within the
28 State.
29 SECTION 1.2. Appropriation for First Responders' and Teachers' Appreciation
30 Bonus Payments. – There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of Public
31 Safety the sum of one hundred thirty million dollars ($130,000,000) in each year of the
32 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to provide one-time bonus payments to eligible first responders as
33 defined in this act. The Department of Public Safety shall distribute these funds to employing
34 authorities based on the number of eligible first responders employed by each authority. Each
35 eligible first responder shall receive a bonus payment of one thousand five hundred dollars

1 ($1,500) in each fiscal year. Funds distributed under this section shall be used exclusively for
2 bonus payments to eligible first responders and may not be used for administrative costs or other
3 purposes. Bonus payments made pursuant to this section shall be:
4 (1) Paid in addition to any other wages or benefits the first responder may be
5 entitled and are not part of the first responder's base salary;
6 (2) Treated as exempt from retirement deductions; and
7 (3) Subject to all applicable federal and State withholding taxes.
8 SECTION 1.3. Administration. – The Office of State Budget and Management shall
9 coordinate with the Department of Public Safety to distribute funds to employing authorities by
10 no later than November 1 of each fiscal year within the 2025-2027 biennium. Employing
11 authorities shall distribute bonus payments to eligible first responders no later than December 31
12 of each fiscal year. To be eligible for the bonus payment, a first responder must be employed by
13 an employing authority on the first day of October of the fiscal year in which the bonus is paid.
14 First responders who work less than full time shall receive a prorated bonus based on the
15 percentage of full-time equivalent hours worked. The Office of State Budget and Management
16 shall develop simplified procedures for the allocation and distribution of bonus funds and shall
17 provide necessary guidance to the Department of Public Safety and employing authorities. The
18 Department of Public Instruction shall assist in coordinating with local school administrative
19 units, charter schools, and regional schools. Employing authorities shall certify to the Department
20 of Public Safety the number of eligible first responders by October 15 of each fiscal year. By
21 February 15 of each fiscal year, employing authorities shall submit a verification that all bonus
22 payments have been distributed to eligible first responders. Any funds not expended for the
23 purposes specified in this section by the end of each respective fiscal year shall revert to the
24 General Fund.

26 PART II. TEACHERS' APPRECIATION BONUS PROGRAM
27 SECTION 2.1. Definition. – For purposes of this Part, the term "teacher" means a
28 classroom teacher, instructional support personnel, principal, or assistant principal employed by
29 any of the following: (i) a local school administrative unit, (ii) a charter school, (iii) a regional
30 school, (iv) a school operated under Article 9C of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes, or (v) a
31 school operated under Article 29A of Chapter 116 of the General Statutes.
32 SECTION 2.2. Appropriation. – There is appropriated from the General Fund to the
33 Department of Public Instruction the sum of one hundred sixty-five million dollars
34 ($165,000,000) in each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to provide one-time bonus
35 payments to teachers. The Department of Public Instruction shall distribute these funds to local
36 school administrative units, charter schools, and regional schools based on the number of eligible
37 teachers employed by each entity. Each eligible teacher shall receive a bonus payment of one
38 thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500) in each fiscal year. Funds distributed under this section
39 shall be used exclusively for bonus payments to eligible teachers and may not be used for
40 administrative costs or other purposes. Bonus payments made pursuant to this section shall be:
41 (1) Paid in addition to any other wages or benefits the first responder may be
42 entitled and are not part of the first responder's base salary;
43 (2) Treated as exempt from retirement deductions; and
44 (3) Subject to all applicable federal and State withholding taxes.
45 SECTION 2.3. Administration. – The State Board of Education and the Department
46 of Public Instruction shall establish procedures and guidelines for the administration of the
47 teacher bonus program consistent with this section. The Department of Public Instruction shall
48 distribute these funds to local school administrative units, charter schools, and regional schools
49 no later than November 1 of each fiscal year within the 2025-2027 biennium. To be eligible for
50 the bonus payment, a teacher must be employed by a local school administrative unit, charter
51 school, or regional school on the first day of October of the fiscal year in which the bonus is paid.
