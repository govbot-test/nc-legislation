H.B. 106
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Feb 11, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Revive High-Need Retired Teachers Program. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Carver.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REVIVE AND EXPAND THE PROGRAM TO ALLOW RETIRED
3 EDUCATORS TO RETURN TO WORK IN HIGH-NEED SCHOOLS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 115C-302.4 is reenacted as it existed immediately prior to its
6 expiration.
7 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 115C-302.4(a)(1), as reenacted by this act, reads as rewritten:
8 "(1) High-need retired teacher. – A beneficiary of the Teachers' and State
9 Employees' Retirement System of North Carolina who meets both of the
10 following requirements:
11 a. Retired on or before February 1, 2019, at least two months prior to the
12 date on which the beneficiary is scheduled to be reemployed pursuant
13 to this section after attaining (i) the age of at least 65 with five years
14 of creditable service, (ii) the age of at least 60 with 25 years of
15 creditable service, or (iii) 30 years of creditable service.
16 b. Is reemployed by a local board of education to provide classroom
17 instruction as a teacher, as defined in G.S. 135-1(25), employed on an
18 annual contract to provide classroom instruction exclusively at a
19 high-need school or schools."
20 SECTION 2.(a) G.S. 135-3(a)(8) is amended by adding a new sub-subdivision to
21 read:
22 "i. Notwithstanding sub-subdivisions c. and d. of this subdivision, the
23 computation of postretirement earnings under this subdivision shall
24 not include earnings while a beneficiary is employed as a high-need
25 retired teacher as defined under G.S. 115C-302.4(a) and the
26 beneficiary shall not be restored to service as a teacher or employee.
27 The Department of Public Instruction shall certify to the Retirement
28 System that a beneficiary is employed by a local board of education as
29 a high-need retired teacher. No later than September 15 annually, a
30 local board of education shall inform the Retirement System if it will
31 not employ any high-need retired teachers for that school year. The
32 retirement allowance of a beneficiary who retired on an early or
33 service retirement shall not cease due to reemployment as a high-need
34 retired teacher. A beneficiary reemployed as a high-need retired
35 teacher is not entitled to any benefits otherwise provided under this
36 Chapter as a result of this period of employment."

11 "(10) "Employee" shall mean all full-time employees, agents or officers of the State

15 University of North Carolina Health Care System who are not eligible for
16 participation under G.S. 135-5.6, employees of the East Carolina University
17 School of Medicine or Dental School of Medicine who are not eligible for
18 participation under G.S. 135-5.7, any person who is a member of the
19 Consolidated Judicial Retirement System, any member of the General
20 Assembly or Assembly, any high-need retired teacher as defined under
21 G.S. 115C-302.4(a), or any part-time or temporary employee.
22 Notwithstanding any other provision of law, "employee" shall include all
23 employees of the General Assembly except participants in the Legislative
24 Intern Program, pages, and beneficiaries in receipt of a monthly retirement
25 allowance under this Chapter who are reemployed on a temporary basis.
26 "Employee" also includes any participant whose employment is interrupted
27 by reason of service in the Uniformed Services, as that term is defined in
28 section 4303(16) of the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment
29 Rights Act, Public Law 103-353, if that participant was an employee at the
30 time of the interruption; if the participant does not return immediately after
31 that service to employment with a covered employer in this System, then the
32 participant shall be deemed "in service" until the date on which the participant
33 was first eligible to be separated or released from his or her involuntary
34 military service. In all cases of doubt, the Board of Trustees shall determine
35 whether any person is an employee as defined in this Chapter. "Employee"
36 shall also mean every full-time civilian employee of the North Carolina
37 National Guard who is employed pursuant to section 709 of Title 32 of the
38 United States Code and paid from federal appropriated funds, but held by the
39 federal authorities not to be a federal employee: Provided, however, that the
40 authority or agency paying the salaries of such employees shall deduct or
41 cause to be deducted from each employee's salary the employee's contribution
42 in accordance with applicable provisions of G.S. 135-8 and remit the same,
43 either directly or indirectly, to the Retirement System; coverage of employees
44 described in this sentence shall commence upon the first day of the calendar
45 year or fiscal year, whichever is earlier, next following the date of execution
46 of an agreement between the Secretary of Defense of the United States and
47 the Adjutant General of the State acting for the Governor in behalf of the State,
48 but no credit shall be allowed pursuant to this sentence for any service
49 previously rendered in the above-described capacity as a civilian employee of
50 the North Carolina National Guard: Provided, further, that the Adjutant
51 General, in the Adjutant General's discretion, may terminate the Retirement

18 from the definition of "employee" solely because the person holds a temporary
19 or time-limited visa."
20 SECTION 2.(d) G.S. 135-1(20) reads as rewritten:
21 "(20) "Retirement" under this Chapter, except as otherwise provided, means the
22 commencement of monthly retirement benefits along with termination of
23 employment and the complete separation from active service with no intent or
24 agreement, express or implied, to return to service. A retirement allowance
25 under the provisions of this Chapter may only be granted upon retirement of
26 a member. In order for a member's retirement to become effective in any
27 month, the member must perform no work for an employer, including
28 part-time, temporary, substitute, or contractor work, at any time during the six
29 months immediately following the effective date of retirement. retirement,
30 except that in the case of a high-need retired teacher, as defined under
31 G.S. 115C-302.4(a), the period of separation required is two months. A
32 member who is a full-time faculty member of The University of North
33 Carolina may effect a retirement allowance under this Chapter,
34 notwithstanding the six-month requirement above, provided the member
35 immediately enters the University's Phased Retirement Program for Tenured
36 Faculty as that program existed on May 25, 2011. For purposes of this
37 subdivision, all of the following shall not be considered service or work:
38 …."
39 SECTION 2.(e) G.S. 135-48.40(b)(1a) reads as rewritten:
40 "(1a) All retirees who (i) are meet all of the following criteria:
41 a. The retiree is employed by an employing unit that elects to be covered
42 by this subdivision, (ii) do subdivision.
43 b. The retiree does not qualify for coverage under subdivision (1) of this
44 subsection, and (iii) are subsection.
45 c. The retiree is either a high-need retired teacher under G.S. 115C-302.4
46 or is determined to be "full-time" by their employing unit in
47 accordance with section 4980H of the Internal Revenue Code and the
48 applicable regulations, as amended.
49 The employing unit shall pay the employer premiums for retirees who enroll
50 under this subdivision."

1 SECTION 2.(f) The State Treasurer shall seek a private letter ruling from the Internal
2 Revenue Service to determine if any provisions of this section jeopardize the status of the
3 Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System.
4 SECTION 2.(g) If the Internal Revenue Service determines that any provision of
5 this section jeopardizes the status of the Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System of
6 North Carolina under the Internal Revenue Code, then this section is repealed on the last day of
7 the month following the month of receipt of that determination by the State Treasurer. Upon
8 receipt of that determination, the State Treasurer shall notify the Revisor of Statutes of the
9 determination and the date of receipt. Within three business days of receipt of the determination,
10 the State Treasurer shall notify all local school administrative units of the repeal of this section
11 and shall publicly notice the receipt of this information on the Department of State Treasurer's
12 website. Within three business days of receipt of the notice from the State Treasurer, a local
13 school administrative unit shall notify all high-need retired teachers employed by its local board
14 of education of the repeal of this section.
15 SECTION 2.(h) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, in order
16 to pay costs associated with the administration of the provisions of this section, the Retirement
17 Systems Division of the Department of State Treasurer may increase receipts from the retirement
18 assets of the Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System or pay costs associated with the
19 administration directly from the retirement assets.
20 SECTION 2.(i) Any beneficiary that is employed by a local board of education as a
21 high-need retired teacher, as defined in G.S. 115C-302.4(a), shall not be eligible to elect into a
22 position that would lead the beneficiary to be eligible to accrue any additional benefits under
23 G.S. 135-3(a)(8). Any failure of a local board of education or a beneficiary to comply with the
24 foregoing shall be corrected by the Executive Director of the Retirement System as may be
25 appropriate under State and federal law. Any costs of the correction, as determined by the
26 Executive Director of the Retirement System, shall be the sole responsibility of the local board
27 of education and shall be transferred to the Pension Accumulation Fund under G.S. 135-8 under
28 rules adopted by the Board of Trustees.
29 SECTION 2.(j) Effective July 1, 2025, there is appropriated from the General Fund
30 to the Department of State Treasurer the sum of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) in
31 nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used to obtain the private letter ruling
32 under subsection (f) of this section.
33 SECTION 2.(k) Effective July 1, 2025, there is appropriated from the General Fund
34 to the Department of State Treasurer the sum of ten million dollars ($10,000,000) in recurring
35 funds for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to be used to address the increased cost to
36 the Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System as a result of the implementation of this
37 section.
38 SECTION 3. This act is effective when it becomes law. G.S. 115C-302.4, as
39 reenacted by this act, applies beginning with the 2025-2026 school year, and this act shall not
40 expire except by subsequent enactment of the General Assembly.
