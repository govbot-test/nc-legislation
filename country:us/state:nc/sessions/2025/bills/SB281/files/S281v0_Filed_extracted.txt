Section 1:
FILED SENATE
Mar 13, 2025
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
S.B. 281
SESSION 2025
PRINCIPAL CLERK
S D
Short Title: Essential Relief for Child Care Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Chitlik, Robinson, and Bradley (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CONTINUE FUNDING FOR CHILD CARE STABILIZATION GRANTS.
3 Whereas, accessible child care is essential for the continued growth of North
4 Carolina's economy; and
5 Whereas, there is bipartisan consensus that child care must be a focus of the
6 2025-2027 legislative biennium; and
7 Whereas, one in five employers cite child care issues as a barrier to hiring; and
8 Whereas, the cost of operating a child care facility continues to rise even as parents'
9 ability to afford it declines; and
10 Whereas, child care providers and the families they serve have not recovered from
11 the impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic; and
12 Whereas, the failure to continue to provide for child care stabilization grants will lead
13 to the closure of an estimated 20% of all child care facilities in our State within the next year;
14 and
15 Whereas, we affirm that State investment in our child care providers is an investment
16 in the economic development of current and future generations; Now, therefore,
17 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
18 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
19 Health and Human Services, Division of Child Development and Early Education (Division), the
20 sum of fifty million dollars ($50,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2024-2025 fiscal year to
21 continue the compensation grants portion of the child care stabilization grants. The Division shall
22 continue the compensation grants portion of the child care stabilization grants through the fourth
23 quarter at the current 2024-2025 fiscal year level.
24 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
*DRS45148-MCa-125*
