Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Commercial Vehicle & Cargo Protection. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Branson, Loftis, Schietzelt, and Hawkins (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Transportation, if favorable, Judiciary 1, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and
Operations of the House
March 24, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ENACT THE COMMERCIAL VEHICLE AND CARGO PROTECTION ACT.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Article 7 of Chapter 20 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
5 a new section to read:
6 "§ 20-219.3A. Commercial booting.
7 A commercial motor vehicle shall not be immobilized using a device such as a boot or any
8 other device. Using an immobilization device on a commercial vehicle will be deemed a
9 misdemeanor."
10 SECTION 2. Article 7A of Chapter 20 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
11 a new section to read:
12 "§ 20-219.15. Return of commercial cargo.
13 Notwithstanding G.S. 20-219.10(a) to the contrary, a tower of a nonconsensual tow or
14 government-initiated tow shall promptly return any commercial cargo towed by the tower to the
15 owner of the commercial cargo or to a designee of the owner. In the case of a trailer containing
16 commercial cargo, the tower shall allow the trailer containing the commercial cargo to be
17 exchanged with a different trailer that is in equal or better condition."
18 SECTION 3. Section 1 of this act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies
19 to offenses charged on or after that date. The remainder of this act is effective when it becomes
20 law.
