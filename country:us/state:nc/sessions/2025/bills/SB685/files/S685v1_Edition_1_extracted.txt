1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO IMPLEMENT MAINTENANCE AND ACCEPTANCE GUARANTEES FOR
3 SUBDIVISION DEVELOPMENTS TO PREVENT THE CREATION OF
4 UNMAINTAINED PUBLICLY DEDICATED ROADS.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. Article 8 of Chapter 160D of the General Statutes is amended by
7 adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 160D-804.2. Maintenance and acceptance guarantees for subdivision streets.
9 (a) To ensure proper maintenance of publicly dedicated subdivision roadways and
10 compliance with G.S. 160D-804 and other development regulation requirements, a county may
11 require posting of a maintenance guarantee, by the developer, for any publicly dedicated roadway
12 to be transferred for public maintenance under G.S. 136-102.6 or under any applicable municipal
13 ordinance.
14 (b) For purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
15 (1) Administering entity. – The Board of Transportation or a municipality,
16 whichever is applicable.
17 (2) Roadway system. – The State highway system or a municipal street system,
18 as each is defined in G.S. 136-66.1, whichever is applicable.
19 (c) For purposes of this section, all of the following apply with respect to maintenance
20 and acceptance guarantees:
21 (1) Type. – The type of maintenance and acceptance guarantee shall be at the
22 election of the developer. The term "maintenance and acceptance guarantee"
23 includes any of the following forms of guarantee:
24 a. Surety bond issued by any company authorized to do business in this
25 State.
26 b. Letter of credit issued by any financial institution licensed to do
27 business in this State.
28 c. Other forms of guarantee that provide equivalent security to a surety
29 bond or letter of credit.
30 (2) Use. – Maintenance and acceptance guarantees shall only be used to ensure
31 that a road or street meets the minimum standards set by the administering
32 entity prior to acceptance to the applicable roadway system. Once the road or
33 street meets all applicable requirements set by the administering entity, the
34 developer may utilize the maintenance and acceptance guarantee to meet
35 minimum standards for acceptance into the roadway system. If the developer
36 is unable, as determined by the county, to ensure the road meets minimum

1 standards for acceptance, the county may utilize the maintenance and
2 acceptance guarantee and bid, pursuant to Chapter 143 of the General Statutes,
3 for the repair of the roadway using funds from the maintenance and
4 acceptance guarantee in order to meet such minimum standards.
5 (3) Required acceptance. – If the road or street has not been accepted into the
6 applicable roadway system for maintenance for a period of four years after the
7 plat has been recorded in the office of the appropriate register of deeds, the
8 administering entity shall automatically accept the road or street into its
9 respective roadway system for maintenance.
10 (4) Petition. – Once the maintenance and acceptance guarantee has been used to
11 ensure the road or street meets the minimum standards of the respective
12 administering entity, the county shall submit a petition to the administering
13 entity for road addition to its roadway system within five days of completed
14 construction, in compliance with G.S. 136-102.6(d).
15 (5) Release. – The following apply to the return or release of any maintenance
16 and acceptance guarantee established under this section:
17 a. The maintenance and acceptance guarantee shall be returned or
18 released, as appropriate, including any funds not utilized, once the road
19 or street has been accepted for maintenance by the administering
20 entity, within 30 days of acceptance.
21 b. If the road or street is accepted pursuant to subdivision (3) of this
22 subsection, the maintenance and acceptance guarantee shall be
23 released by the county to the respective administering entity in an
24 amount commensurate with the reasonable costs necessary for the road
25 or street to meet minimum standards for acceptance into the respective
26 roadway system. Any funds released under this sub-subdivision may
27 be utilized absent any occupancy requirement.
28 (6) Amount. – The posted amount of the maintenance and acceptance guarantee
29 shall be set by the county but shall not exceed twenty percent (20%) of the
30 reasonably estimated total cost of construction for the road or street secured
31 by the maintenance and acceptance guarantee, as determined by the county,
32 of the transportation improvements at the time the guarantee is issued. If the
33 maintenance and acceptance guarantee is not sufficient to cover the total costs
34 to ensure that the road meets the standards set by the administering entity, the
35 county or administering entity may use other funds to supplement the cost.
36 When calculating the estimated total cost of construction for the road or street,
37 the county shall include costs for labor and materials. Where applicable, the
38 costs shall be based on unit pricing.
39 (7) Timing. – A county, at its discretion, shall require the maintenance and
40 acceptance guarantee to be posted either at the time the plat is recorded or
41 prior to recordation of the plat.
42 (8) Legal responsibilities. – No person shall claim any rights under or to any
43 maintenance and acceptance guarantee provided pursuant to this section or in
44 the proceeds of any such maintenance and acceptance guarantee other than the
45 following:
46 a. The county for which the maintenance and acceptance guarantee is
47 provided.
48 b. The developer at whose request or for whose benefit the maintenance
49 and acceptance guarantee is given.
