Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Optometrists Telehealth Services. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Campbell, Huneycutt, Almond, and Lowery (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Health, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 2, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ALLOW LICENSED OPTOMETRISTS FLEXIBILITY TO PROVIDE
3 TELEHEALTH SERVICES.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Article 6 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
6 a new section to read:
7 "§ 90-118.5A. Telemedicine practice.
8 (a) A licensed optometrist may perform the practice of optometry, as defined by this
9 Article, to treat patients located in this State through the method of telemedicine, as long as the
10 licensed optometrist has a valid and active license in this State. The licensed optometrist shall
11 not be required to be located physically in this State or to have a license in the other state in which
12 the licensed optometrist is located physically while providing care to a patient in this State.
13 (b) Nothing in this section shall be construed to limit the jurisdiction of the Board to
14 pursue violations of this Article against any licensed optometrist that uses telemedicine to
15 practice optometry under this section. Further, the use of telemedicine does not alter the scope of
16 the practice of optometry, and the licensed optometrist is subject to all laws and Board rules while
17 treating patients in other states."
18 SECTION 2. The North Carolina Board of Examiners in Optometry shall adopt rules
19 to implement the provisions of this act, including rules on the licensee-patient relationship,
20 examination, evaluation, and diagnosis of a patient, patient record keeping, prescribing,
21 telemedicine standard of care, and display of licensure and credentials, to the extent that these
22 rules are not contrary to G.S. 90-118.5A, as enacted by Section 1 of this act, and in alignment
23 with the Board's Telemedicine Position Statement.
24 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025.
