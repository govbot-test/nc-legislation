1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE THAT CERTAIN EMPLOYEES AND VOLUNTEERS AT
3 NONPUBLIC SCHOOLS MAY CARRY CERTAIN WEAPONS ON EDUCATIONAL
4 PROPERTY WHEN AUTHORIZED BY THE SCHOOL BOARD OF TRUSTEES OR
5 SCHOOL ADMINISTRATIVE DIRECTOR, TO PROVIDE THAT A PERSON MAY
6 CARRY A CONCEALED HANDGUN ON EDUCATIONAL PROPERTY THAT IS THE
7 LOCATION OF BOTH A SCHOOL AND A PLACE OF RELIGIOUS WORSHIP WHEN
8 ATTENDING WORSHIP SERVICES AND OTHER SACERDOTAL FUNCTIONS AT
9 THE PLACE OF RELIGIOUS WORSHIP, TO INCREASE THE PUNISHMENT
10 IMPOSED FOR AN ASSAULT OR THREAT AGAINST AN EXECUTIVE OFFICER,
11 LEGISLATIVE OFFICER, COURT OFFICER, OR LOCAL ELECTED OFFICER, TO

17 LAW ENFORCEMENT SHOOTING RANGES.
18 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
19 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-269.2(a) is amended by adding the following new subdivisions
20 to read:
21 "(1d) School administrative director. – Any individual authorized by the school
22 board of trustees to act on behalf of the nonpublic school.
23 (1e) School board of trustees. – The governing body of any nonpublic school. For
24 purposes of parochial schools, the school board of trustees may be the board
25 of deacons, the board of elders, or any other designated board which oversees
26 the general affairs of the church, synagogue, temple, or other place of religious
27 worship that is affiliated with the nonpublic school."

29 to read:
30 "(8) The person is an employee or volunteer of a nonpublic school who meets all
31 of the following criteria:
32 a. The person has written authorization from the school board of trustees
33 or the school administrative director to possess and carry a firearm or
34 stun gun on the educational property that is owned, used, or operated
35 by the nonpublic school.

19 SECTION 3. G.S. 14-269.2(k1) reads as rewritten:
20 "(k1) For the purposes of this subsection, property owned by a local board of education or
21 county commission shall not be construed as a building that is a place of religious worship as
22 defined in G.S. 14-54.1. The provisions of this section shall not apply to a person who has a
23 concealed handgun permit that is valid under Article 54B of this Chapter, or who is exempt from
24 obtaining a permit pursuant to that Article, if all of the following conditions apply:
25 (1) The person possesses and carries a handgun on educational property other than
26 an institution of higher education as defined by G.S. 116-143.1 or a nonpublic,
27 postsecondary educational institution.
28 (2) The educational property is the location of both a school and a building that is
29 a place of religious worship as defined in G.S. 14-54.1.
30 (3) The weapon is a handgun.
31 (4) The handgun is only possessed and carried on educational property outside in
32 one of the following circumstances:
33 a. Outside of the school operating hours.
34 b. At any time, in a building that is a place of religious worship while the
35 person is attending worship services, funeral services, wedding
36 ceremonies, Christenings, religious fellowships, and any other
37 sacerdotal functions in the building. For purposes of this subdivision,
38 the term "attending" includes ingress and egress between the building
39 and the designated parking area for the place of religious worship.
40 (5) The person or persons in legal possession or control of the premises have not
41 posted a conspicuous notice prohibiting the carrying of a concealed handgun
42 on the premises in accordance with G.S. 14-415.11(c)."
