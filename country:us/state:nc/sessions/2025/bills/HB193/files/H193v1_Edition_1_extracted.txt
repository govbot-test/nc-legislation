10 PART I. ALLOW PERSON WITH CONCEALED HANDGUN PERMIT TO CARRY A
11 CONCEALED HANDGUN ON PRIVATE SCHOOL PROPERTY
12 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 14-269.2 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
13 "(k2) The provisions of this section shall not apply to a person who has a concealed handgun
14 permit that is valid under Article 54B of this Chapter, or who is exempt from obtaining a permit
15 pursuant to that Article, if all of the following conditions apply:
16 (1) The person possesses and carries a handgun on educational property that is
17 owned, used, or operated by a private school. Nothing in this subdivision shall
18 be construed as applying to a nonpublic postsecondary educational institution.
19 (2) The weapon is a handgun.
20 (3) The person or persons in legal possession or control of the educational
21 property have not posted a conspicuous notice prohibiting the carrying of a
22 concealed handgun on the property in accordance with G.S. 14-415.11(c).
23 (4) The person or persons in legal possession or control of the educational
24 property has or have provided written permission authorizing the person to
25 possess and carry a handgun on the property. The permission required by this
26 subdivision shall be signed by the person or persons in legal possession or
27 control of the property and shall specify the dates of issuance and expiration."
28 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 14-415.11(c)(1) reads as rewritten:
29 "(1) Areas prohibited by G.S. 14-269.2, except as allowed under
30 G.S. 14-269.2(k1).subsection (k1) or (k2) of G.S. 14-269.2."
31 SECTION 1.(c) This Part becomes effective December 1, 2025.
32

1 "Article 53C.
2 "Sport Shooting Range Protection Act of 1997.
3 "§ 14-409.45. Definitions.
4 The following definitions apply in this Article:
5 (1) Law enforcement organization. – A State or national organization whose
6 membership is composed of sworn law enforcement officers, including a
7 regional or local chapter of such a State or national organization.
8 (2) Law enforcement shooting range. – A sport shooting range owned or operated
9 by a State, federal, or local law enforcement agency, or by a law enforcement
10 organization.
11 (1)(3) Person. – An individual, proprietorship, partnership, corporation, club, or
12 other legal entity.
13 (2)(4) Sport shooting range or range. – An area designed and operated for the use of
14 rifles, shotguns, pistols, silhouettes, skeet, trap, black powder, or any other
15 similar sport shooting.
16 (3)(5) Substantial change in use. – The current primary use of the range no longer
17 represents the activity previously engaged in at the range.
18 "§ 14-409.46. Sport shooting range protection.
19 (a) Notwithstanding Except as otherwise provided in this Article, notwithstanding any
20 other provision of law, a person who owns, operates, or uses a sport shooting range in this State
21 shall not be subject to civil liability or criminal prosecution in any matter relating to noise or
22 noise pollution resulting from the operation or use of the range if the range is in compliance with
23 any noise control laws or ordinances that applied to the range and its operation at the time the
24 range began operation.
25 (b) A person who owns, operates, or uses a sport shooting range is not subject to an action
26 for nuisance on the basis of noise or noise pollution, and a State court shall not enjoin the use or
27 operation of a range on the basis of noise or noise pollution, if the range is in compliance with
28 any noise control laws or ordinances that applied to the range and its operation at the time the
29 range began operation.
30 (c) Rules adopted by any State department or agency for limiting levels of noise in terms
31 of decibel level that may occur in the outdoor atmosphere shall not apply to a sport shooting
32 range that was in operation prior to the adoption of the rule.
33 (d) A person who acquires title to real property adversely affected by the use of property
34 with a permanently located and improved sport shooting range constructed and initially operated
35 prior to the time the person acquires title shall not maintain a nuisance action on the basis of noise
36 or noise pollution against the person who owns the range to restrain, enjoin, or impede the use of
37 the range. If there is a substantial change in use of the range after the person acquires title, the
38 person may maintain a nuisance action if the action is brought within one year of the date of a
39 substantial change in use. This section does not prohibit actions for negligence or recklessness in
40 the operation of the range or by a person using the range.
41 (e) A sport shooting range that is operated and is not in violation of existing law at the
42 time of the enactment of an ordinance shall be permitted to continue in operation even if the
43 operation of the sport shooting range at a later date does not conform to the new ordinance or an
44 amendment to an existing ordinance, provided there has been no substantial change in use.
45 "§ 14-409.46A. Additional protection for relocated law enforcement shooting range.
46 Notwithstanding any provision of law or any other provision of this Article, for any law
47 enforcement shooting range that operates in the same location for at least 25 years, relocates to a
48 new location within the same county, and has no substantial change in use, the following shall
49 apply:
50 (1) The provisions of this Article shall be applied to the law enforcement shooting
51 range based on the date the range began operation in the original location.
