Short Title: Funds for Madison County/Hurricane Helene. (Public)

1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS FOR VARIOUS REPAIRS NEEDED IN MADISON
3 COUNTY AS A RESULT OF HURRICANE HELENE.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Office of State
6 Budget and Management the sum of eighty-eight million three hundred ninety-five thousand
7 dollars ($88,395,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to provide a directed
8 grant to Madison County to be used as follows:
9 (1) Twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) for repairs at the Marshall
10 Wastewater Treatment Plant.
11 (2) Six million dollars ($6,000,000) for repairs to the Marshall Town Hall.
12 (3) One million six hundred thousand dollars ($1,600,000) to rebuild the Marshall
13 Housing Authority Apartments.
14 (4) One million five hundred thousand dollars ($1,500,000) for repairs to the
15 Marshall Volunteer Fire Department.
16 (5) Four hundred thousand dollars ($400,000) for repairs to the Hot Springs Town
17 Hall.
18 (6) Twelve million dollars ($12,000,000) to build a new facility for the Hot
19 Springs Wastewater Treatment Plant.
20 (7) Six hundred thousand dollars ($600,000) for repairs to the Hot Springs
21 Community Center.
22 (8) Two hundred seventy thousand dollars ($270,000) for repairs to the Hot
23 Springs Library.
24 (9) Three million dollars ($3,000,000) for temporary relocation of Town
25 departments housed in the Sprinkle Shelton Building.
26 (10) Four hundred thousand dollars ($400,000) for repairs to Barnard Park.
27 (11) Two hundred twenty-five thousand dollars ($225,000) for repairs to the Hot
28 Springs Housing Authority Wastewater Treatment Facility.
29 (12) Twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) for repairs to the French Broad
30 River, Ivy River, and Additional County Tributaries and Waterways,
31 including debris removal.
32 (13) Four hundred thousand dollars ($400,000) for repairs to the County's
33 Communications Tower at Duckett Top.
34 (14) Nine hundred thousand dollars ($900,000) to complete the upgrades to the NC
35 Emergency Communication Towers.
