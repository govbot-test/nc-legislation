7 SECTION 2. Article 7 of Chapter 113A of the General Statutes is amended by
8 adding a new Part to read:
9 "Part 5A. Upland Basin Marinas.
10 "§ 113A-129.10. Legislative findings; broad construction.
11 (a) The General Assembly finds that development of properly sited and planned upland
12 basin marinas to accommodate vessels promotes the public welfare for a number of reasons,
13 including all of the following:
14 (1) Increasing riparian property owner access to the State's public trust waters.
15 (2) Expanding the total area of the State's public trust waters.
16 (3) Reducing and confining potential storm debris.
17 (4) Reducing the need for additional onshore impervious surfaces associated with
18 parking.
19 (5) Promoting the use of innovative technologies.
20 (6) Promoting the collection of site-specific data designed to assure compliance
21 with applicable water quality standards.
22 (7) Maintaining or improving overall water quality when sited, designed,
23 constructed, and operated in conformance with the provisions of this Part.
24 (b) Accordingly, it is the intent of the General Assembly that this Part be broadly
25 construed to further the general purposes stated in this Part.
26 "§ 113A-129.11. Definitions.
27 As used in this Part, the following definitions apply:
28 (1) AEC or Area of Environmental Concern. – An area designated by the Coastal
29 Resources Commission pursuant to G.S. 113A-113.
30 (2) Boat slip. – Any structure adjoining, attached to, or part of a pier which has
31 the capacity to store one boat.
32 (3) Upland basin marina. – A marina constructed by excavating or dredging lands
33 of elevations above the current mean or ordinary high-water mark and
34 designed to accommodate more than 10 vessels or boat slips. Upland basin
35 marinas shall be considered a water-dependent use for purposes of general use
36 standards adopted by the Coastal Resources Commission.
37 "§ 113A-129.12. Upland basin marina permitting and development.
38 (a) Unless the Director of the Division of Water Resources or the Director of the Division
39 of Coastal Management makes a finding, based on site-specific technical information, that the
40 applicant cannot comply with one or more of the criteria listed in subsection (b) of this section,
41 the Division of Water Resources and the Division of Coastal Management shall approve an
42 application for the development of an upland basin marina project, within 60 days after the
43 applicant submits a completed application or supplemental information requested by the
44 Department to demonstrate compliance with all of the criteria set forth in subsection (b) of this
45 section. The Department may make one written request for any supplemental information
46 necessary to make its finding within 30 days after the applicant submits a complete application,
47 and the permit may be conditioned upon measures that are necessary to ensure that the applicant
48 complies with all of the criteria set forth in subsection (b) of this section. When the Department
49 requests supplemental information, the 60-day review period restarts upon receipt of the
50 supplemental information requested by the Department. If the Department fails to act on an

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 application for the development of an upland basin marina project within the 60-day review period,
2 the application shall be deemed approved.
3 (b) The criteria with which an upland basin marina shall comply to qualify for permitting
4 pursuant to subsection (a) of this section are all of the following:
5 (1) The upland basin marina is designed to accommodate 10 or more vessels,
6 including individual homeowner boat slips, boat lifts, or dry stack storage.
7 (2) The waters contained in the upland basin marina have a dissolved oxygen
8 content equal to or greater than the dissolved oxygen content of the water
9 located 50 feet plus or minus 5 feet from the entrance to the upland basin
10 marina before development. As part of the application, an applicant shall
11 provide to the Division of Water Resources and the Division of Coastal
12 Management site-specific sampling data documenting pre-project dissolved
13 oxygen levels of the water located 50 feet plus or minus 5 feet from each
14 proposed entrance to the proposed upland basin marina. Waters located within
15 the upland basin marina shall be classified the same as the waters in the
16 immediate vicinity of any entrance to the upland basin marina.
17 (3) The project has a bond or set-aside funds for the long-term operation and
18 maintenance of any technology required to meet or exceed then-applicable
19 water quality standards. The applicant shall provide an estimate of annual
20 operation and maintenance costs, and the bond or set-aside funds shall be equal
21 to five years of the estimated annual operation and maintenance costs.
22 (4) Construction of the upland basin marina impacts or removes a total area of
23 coastal wetlands that is no more than five percent (5%) of the total area of the
24 proposed upland basin marina waters.
25 (5) Construction of the upland basin marina impacts or removes a total linear
26 footage of coastal wetlands fringe that is no more than ten percent (10%) of
27 the total linear footage of existing coastal wetlands along the shoreline of the
28 property or properties of the permit applicant.
29 (6) The proposed upland basin marina project complies with specific use
30 standards adopted by the Coastal Resources Commission for navigation
31 channels, canals, and boat basins. An upland basin marina project shall not be
32 considered a "finger canal" or "finger canal system" if the project does not
33 contain right angle corners.
34 (7) The upland basin marina has a 30-foot vegetated buffer or buffers along the
35 post-project shoreline, excluding any newly created inside shorelines, if
36 required by the Department.
37 (8) The proposed upland basin marina project complies with specific use
38 standards adopted by the Coastal Resources Commission for marinas, except
39 that a stormwater management system may be located within the 30-foot
40 buffer area described in subdivision (7) of this subsection.
41 (9) The project includes mitigation or has mitigation credits for wetland impacts
42 caused by excavation or construction of entrances, exits, and upland vessel
43 accommodation areas, where such entrances, exits, and upland vessel
44 accommodation areas exceed 125 linear feet of shoreline in total.
45 (10) The upland basin marina is sited and designed to avoid significant adverse
46 impacts to the productivity and biologic integrity of coastal wetlands, shellfish
47 beds, submerged aquatic vegetation, water quality, and spawning and primary
48 nursery areas. Compliance with subdivisions (1) through (10) of this
49 subsection shall create a presumption that the project has avoided significant
50 adverse impacts to the productivity and biologic integrity of coastal wetlands,
51 non-coastal wetlands, shellfish beds, submerged aquatic vegetation, water

1 quality, spawning areas, and primary nursery areas and to have complied with
2 State water quality antidegradation requirements. Compliance constitutes
3 appropriate avoidance of significant adverse environmental impacts,
4 including those identified in this Part.
5 (c) The submission of a Major Development Coastal Area Management Act (CAMA)
6 permit application for an upland basin marina project shall constitute a complete application for
7 purposes of water quality certification review by the Division of Water Resources. Unless the
8 Director of the Division of Water Resources or the Director of the Division of Coastal
9 Management objects in writing or requests additional information necessary to evaluate
10 compliance with water quality standards as provided in subsection (a) of this section, no further
11 information shall be required by the Department for purposes of evaluating whether the upland
12 basin marina project will comply with water quality standards or CAMA use standards referenced
13 in this Part. Compliance with the Major Development CAMA permitting process shall also
14 constitute compliance with all relevant provisions of Article 1 of this Chapter.
15 (d) The Department shall allow an applicant for an upland basin marina project to use
16 innovative technology to maintain dissolved oxygen levels or improve dissolved oxygen above
17 pre-project ambient dissolved oxygen levels in the immediate vicinity of the entrance to the
18 upland basin marina. If the applicant proposes the use of innovative technology, the Department
19 shall require the permit holder to provide financial assurance, in the form of a bond or set-aside
20 funds, for long-term operation and maintenance of the innovative technology, in accordance with
21 subdivision (b)(3) of this section. If the applicant provides a certification from a North Carolina
22 licensed professional engineer that the proposed innovative technology is capable of attaining
23 required dissolved oxygen levels within the proposed upland basin marina, the Department shall
24 not require any additional information.
25 (e) In the absence of site-specific technical concerns provided to the applicant by the
26 Director of the Division of Water Resources or the Director of the Division of Coastal
27 Management based on information provided during the permit review process, an upland basin
28 marina project that satisfies the criteria provided in subsection (b) of this section shall be deemed
29 to satisfy all of the following:
30 (1) CAMA management objectives and relevant use standards of the estuarine
31 and ocean system AECs.
32 (2) CAMA management objectives and use standards of the coastal wetlands
33 AEC.
34 (3) The policy goals stated in G.S. 113-102(b).
35 (f) Nothing in this section shall be construed to abrogate the Department's authority to
36 ensure long-term compliance with applicable water quality standards in light of the goals and
37 requirements set forth in this Part. If data collected as part of the requirements of this Part, or
38 otherwise, indicate noncompliance with applicable water quality standards in the waters within
39 the upland basin marina or adjacent waters within the vicinity of each entrance to the upland
40 basin marina, the Department may require the owner of the upland basin marina development at
41 the time of the noncompliance to bring the waters within the upland basin marina and adjacent
42 waters into compliance with applicable water quality standards, in light of the overall goals and
43 policies set forth in this Part to promote the construction and use of upland basin marinas.
44 (g) Nothing in this section shall obviate the need for an applicant to obtain all relevant
45 federal permits required for the upland basin marina project."
