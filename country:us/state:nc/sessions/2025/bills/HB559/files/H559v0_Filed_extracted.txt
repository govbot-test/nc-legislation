Section 1:
H.B. 559
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Mar 27, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Make Elevators Great Again.-AB (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Howard.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MAKE VARIOUS FEE MODIFICATIONS TO THE STATE LABOR
3 REGULATIONS.
4 Whereas, the North Carolina Department of Labor's Elevator and Amusement Device
5 Bureau is a fully receipt-supported operation that receives no State appropriations and is currently
6 facing a backlog of over 3,000 overdue inspections and critical staffing shortages, particularly in
7 North Carolina's fast-growing urban regions; and
8 Whereas, outdated statutory fee caps have constrained the Department's ability to
9 modernize operations, attract qualified inspectors, and deliver timely inspections; and
10 Whereas, authorizing modest, common-sense fee adjustments will empower the
11 Department to eliminate the inspection backlog, raise inspector pay to market rates, and launch
12 an in-house apprenticeship program to train the next generation of safety professionals; and
13 Whereas, this Act ensures that all fee increases remain capped, transparent, and tied
14 to inflation, while keeping the Elevator and Amusement Device Bureau fully receipt-supported,
15 free from new taxpayer burdens, and operated with the accountability and efficiency of a small
16 business; and
17 Whereas, it is in North Carolina's best interest to modernize the Department in a way
18 that is accountable, fiscally responsible, and tailored to the needs of our fast-growing State; Now,
19 therefore,
20 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
21
22 PART I. FEE MODIFICATIONS
23 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 95-107 reads as rewritten:
24 "§ 95-107. Assessment and collection of fees; certificates of safe operation.
25 The assessment of the fees adopted by the Commissioner pursuant to G.S. 95-69.11,
26 95-110.5, 95-111.4 95-110.5A, 95-111.4A, and 95-120 95-120A shall be made against the owner
27 or operator of the equipment and may be collected at the time of inspection. If the fees are not
28 collected at the time of inspection, the Department must bill the owner or operator of the
29 equipment for the amount of the fee assessed for the inspection of the equipment and the amount
30 assessed is payable by the owner or operator of the equipment upon receipt of the bill. Certificates
31 of safe operation may be withheld by the Department of Labor until such time as the assessed
32 fees are collected."
33 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 95-108 reads as rewritten:
34 "§ 95-108. Disposition of fees.
35 All fees collected by the Department of Labor pursuant to G.S. 95-69.11, 95-110.5, 95-111.4
36 95-110.5A, 95-111.4A, and 95-120 95-120A shall be deposited with the State Treasurer and shall
*DRH40317-NI-115A*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 be used exclusively for inspection inspection, permitting, and certification purposes. Fees
2 deposited pursuant to this section that have not been expended or encumbered at the end of the
3 fiscal year shall not revert but shall remain available for uses consistent with this section."
4 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 95-110.5(20) is repealed.
5 SECTION 1.(d) Article 14A of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by
6 adding a new section to read:
7 "§ 95-110.5A. Fees.
8 (a) Beginning July 1, 2025, the Department shall charge fees not to exceed the following:
9 Special Inspection Fee – Expedited $1,000
10 Temporary Limited Certificate for Construction Use Only; Less than 10 Floors $200.00
11 Temporary Limited Certificate for Construction Use Only; 10 or More Floors $300.00
12 Reinspection Fee of Failed New and Repair/Alteration Inspections $1,000
13 Routine/Annual Elevator Inspections; Less than 10 Floors $200.00
14 Routine/Annual Elevator Inspections; 10 or More Floors $300.00
15 Routine/Annual Wheelchair Lift and Dumbwaiter Inspections $100.00
16 Routine/Annual Escalator and Moving Walk Inspections $500.00
17 (b) Upon application to the Department for a new or alteration construction permit for a
18 device subject to this section, an applicant shall submit a permit application fee. The amount of
19 the permit application fee shall be the greater of (i) two hundred dollars ($200.00) or (ii) one
20 percent (1%) of the contract price for the alteration or installation of the device being permitted.
21 (c) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, for fiscal years beginning on or
22 after July 1, 2026, the Department shall adjust the fee amounts listed in subsection (a) of this
23 section in accordance with the Consumer Price Index computed by the Bureau of Labor Statistics
24 using the most recent 12-month period for which data is available. The adjustment for fees under
25 this subsection shall be rounded to the nearest dollar ($1.00), and the Commissioner shall publish
26 any increase in fees under this subsection in the North Carolina Register and on the Department's
27 website at least 60 days prior to any increase."
28 SECTION 1.(e) G.S. 95-111.4(19) is repealed.
29 SECTION 1.(f) Article 14B of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by
30 adding a new section to read:
31 "§ 95-111.4A. Fees.
32 (a) Beginning July 1, 2025, the Department shall charge fees not to exceed the following:
33 Advance Location Notice (ALN) Application Fee $25.00
34 Special Inspection Fee – Expedited $1,000
35 Amusement Major Ride Inspections $250.00
36 Amusement Return Trip Inspections $500.00
37 Holiday/Weekend Inspections $500.00
38 Kiddie Ride Inspections $100.00
39 Go Kart Inspections (per cart) $50.00
40 Go Kart Track Inspections $200.00
41 Amusement Rock Wall Inspections $100.00
42 Roller Coaster (permanent and portable) Inspections $500.00
43 Simulators $100.00
44 Bungee Trampoline Inspections $100.00
45 Water Slide Inspections $300.00
46 Train Inspections $250.00
47 (b) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, for fiscal years beginning on or
48 after July 1, 2026, the Department shall adjust the fee amounts listed in subsection (a) of this
49 section in accordance with the Consumer Price Index computed by the Bureau of Labor Statistics
50 using the most recent 12-month period for which data is available. The adjustment for fees under
51 this subsection shall be rounded to the nearest dollar ($1.00), and the Commissioner shall publish
Page 2 DRH40317-NI-115A
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 any increase in fees under this subsection in the North Carolina Register and on the Department's
2 website at least 60 days prior to any increase."
3 SECTION 1.(g) G.S. 95-120(9) is repealed.
4 SECTION 1.(h) Article 15 of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by
5 adding a new section to read:
6 "§ 95-120A. Fees.
7 (a) Beginning July 1, 2025, the Department shall charge fees not to exceed the following:
8 Gondolas, Chairlifts, Inclined Railroad Inspections $500.00
9 J or T Bars and Conveyors Inspections $300.00
10 Rope Tow Inspections $200.00
11 (b) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, for fiscal years beginning on or
12 after July 1, 2026, the Department shall adjust the fee amounts listed in subsection (a) of this
13 section in accordance with the Consumer Price Index computed by the Bureau of Labor Statistics
14 using the most recent 12-month period for which data is available. The adjustment for fees under
15 this subsection shall be rounded to the nearest dollar ($1.00), and the Commissioner shall publish
16 any increase in fees under this subsection in the North Carolina Register and on the Department's
17 website at least 60 days prior to any increase."
18 SECTION 1.(i) The Commissioner shall publish notice of the changes in fees created
19 by this section in the North Carolina Register and on the Department's website no later than 30
20 days after the effective date of this act.
21
22 PART II. REGULATORY FLEXIBILITY MODIFICATIONS
23 SECTION 2.(a) G.S. 95-110.5(13) reads as rewritten:
24 "(13) To adopt, modify or revoke rules and regulations governing the qualifications
25 of inspectors;inspectors. The Commissioner may waive or amend the
26 American National Safety Standards from the American National Standards
27 Institute as those standards relate to the qualifications of inspectors in this
28 State if the Commissioner sets alternative standards that are reasonably
29 equivalent, as determined by the Commissioner;"
30 SECTION 2.(b) G.S. 95-111.4(13) reads as rewritten:
31 "(13) To adopt, modify or revoke rules and regulations governing the qualifications
32 of inspectors. The Commissioner may waive or amend the American National
33 Safety Standards from the American National Standards Institute as those
34 standards relate to the qualifications of inspectors in this State if the
35 Commissioner sets alternative standards that are reasonably equivalent, as
36 determined by the Commissioner."
37
38 PART III. MISCELLANEOUS
39 SECTION 3. The Department of Labor (Department) shall consult with the North
40 Carolina Community College System to develop an in-house training and apprenticeship
41 program (Program) for elevator inspectors. The Department shall utilize the Program to fill
42 vacancies in receipt-supported inspector positions within the Elevator and Amusement Device
43 Division of the Department.
44
45 PART IV. EFFECTIVE DATE
46 SECTION 4. This act is effective when it becomes law.
