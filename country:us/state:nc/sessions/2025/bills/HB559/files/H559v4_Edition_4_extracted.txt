1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MAKE VARIOUS FEE MODIFICATIONS TO THE STATE LABOR
3 REGULATIONS, AND TO AMEND INTERIM APPOINTMENT CRITERIA FOR THE
4 BUILDING CODE COUNCIL AND RESIDENTIAL CODE COUNCIL.
5 Whereas, the North Carolina Department of Labor's Elevator and Amusement Device
6 Bureau is a fully receipt-supported operation that receives no State appropriations and is currently
7 facing a backlog of over 3,000 overdue inspections and critical staffing shortages, particularly in
8 North Carolina's fast-growing urban regions; and
9 Whereas, outdated statutory fee caps have constrained the Department's ability to
10 modernize operations, attract qualified inspectors, and deliver timely inspections; and
11 Whereas, authorizing modest, common-sense fee adjustments will empower the
12 Department to eliminate the inspection backlog, raise inspector pay to market rates, and launch
13 an in-house apprenticeship program to train the next generation of safety professionals; and
14 Whereas, this Act ensures that all fee increases remain capped, transparent, and tied
15 to inflation, while keeping the Elevator and Amusement Device Bureau fully receipt-supported,
16 free from new taxpayer burdens, and operated with the accountability and efficiency of a small
17 business; and
18 Whereas, it is in North Carolina's best interest to modernize the Department in a way
19 that is accountable, fiscally responsible, and tailored to the needs of our fast-growing State; Now,
20 therefore,
21 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
22
23 PART I. FEE MODIFICATIONS
24 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 95-107 reads as rewritten:
25 "§ 95-107. Assessment and collection of fees; certificates of safe operation.
26 The assessment of the fees adopted by the Commissioner pursuant to G.S. 95-69.11,
27 95-110.5, 95-111.4 95-110.5A, 95-111.4A, and 95-120 95-120A shall be made against the owner
28 or operator of the equipment and may be collected at the time of inspection. If the fees are not
29 collected at the time of inspection, the Department must bill the owner or operator of the
30 equipment for the amount of the fee assessed for the inspection of the equipment and the amount
31 assessed is payable by the owner or operator of the equipment upon receipt of the bill. Certificates
32 of safe operation may be withheld by the Department of Labor until such time as the assessed
33 fees are collected."

25 PART II. DEVELOP TRAINING AND APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM
26 SECTION 2. The Department of Labor (Department) shall consult with the North
27 Carolina Community College System to develop an in-house training and apprenticeship
28 program (Program) for elevator inspectors. The Department shall utilize the Program to fill
29 vacancies in receipt-supported inspector positions within the Elevator and Amusement Device
30 Division of the Department.
