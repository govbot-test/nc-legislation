GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 3
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/15/25
Senate Finance Committee Substitute Adopted 6/19/25
Short Title: Make Elevators Great Again.-AB (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 31, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MAKE VARIOUS FEE MODIFICATIONS TO THE STATE LABOR
3 REGULATIONS.
4 Whereas, the North Carolina Department of Labor's Elevator and Amusement Device
5 Bureau is a fully receipt-supported operation that receives no State appropriations and is currently
6 facing a backlog of over 3,000 overdue inspections and critical staffing shortages, particularly in
7 North Carolina's fast-growing urban regions; and
8 Whereas, outdated statutory fee caps have constrained the Department's ability to
9 modernize operations, attract qualified inspectors, and deliver timely inspections; and
10 Whereas, authorizing modest, common-sense fee adjustments will empower the
11 Department to eliminate the inspection backlog, raise inspector pay to market rates, and launch
12 an in-house apprenticeship program to train the next generation of safety professionals; and
13 Whereas, this Act ensures that all fee increases remain capped, transparent, and tied
14 to inflation, while keeping the Elevator and Amusement Device Bureau fully receipt-supported,
15 free from new taxpayer burdens, and operated with the accountability and efficiency of a small
16 business; and
17 Whereas, it is in North Carolina's best interest to modernize the Department in a way
18 that is accountable, fiscally responsible, and tailored to the needs of our fast-growing State; Now,
19 therefore,
20 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
21
22 PART I. FEE MODIFICATIONS
23 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 95-107 reads as rewritten:
24 "§ 95-107. Assessment and collection of fees; certificates of safe operation.
25 The assessment of the fees adopted by the Commissioner pursuant to G.S. 95-69.11,
26 95-110.5, 95-111.4 95-110.5A, 95-111.4A, and 95-120 95-120A shall be made against the owner
27 or operator of the equipment and may be collected at the time of inspection. If the fees are not
28 collected at the time of inspection, the Department must bill the owner or operator of the
29 equipment for the amount of the fee assessed for the inspection of the equipment and the amount
30 assessed is payable by the owner or operator of the equipment upon receipt of the bill. Certificates
31 of safe operation may be withheld by the Department of Labor until such time as the assessed
32 fees are collected."
33 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 95-108 reads as rewritten:
34 "§ 95-108. Disposition of fees.
*H559-v-3*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 All fees collected by the Department of Labor pursuant to G.S. 95-69.11, 95-110.5, 95-111.4
2 95-110.5A, 95-111.4A, and 95-120 95-120A shall be deposited with the State Treasurer and shall
3 be used exclusively for inspection inspection, permitting, and certification purposes. Fees
4 deposited pursuant to this section that have not been expended or encumbered at the end of the
5 fiscal year shall not revert but shall remain available for uses consistent with this section."
6 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 95-110.5(20) is repealed.
7 SECTION 1.(d) Article 14A of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by
8 adding a new section to read:
9 "§ 95-110.5A. Fees.
10 (a) Beginning July 1, 2025, the Department shall charge fees not to exceed the following:
11 Special Inspection Fee – Expedited $1,000
12 Temporary Limited Certificate for Construction Use Only; Less than 10 Floors $200.00
13 Temporary Limited Certificate for Construction Use Only; 10 or More Floors $300.00
14 Reinspection Fee of Failed New and Repair/Alteration Inspections $1,000
15 Routine/Annual Elevator Inspections; Less than 10 Floors $200.00
16 Routine/Annual Elevator Inspections; 10 or More Floors $300.00
17 Routine/Annual Wheelchair Lift and Dumbwaiter Inspections $100.00
18 Routine/Annual Escalator and Moving Walk Inspections $500.00
19 (b) Upon application to the Department for a new or alteration construction permit for a
20 device subject to this section, an applicant shall submit a permit application fee. The amount of
21 the permit application fee shall be the greater of (i) two hundred dollars ($200.00) or (ii) one
22 percent (1%) of the contract price for the alteration or installation of the device being permitted.
23 (c) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, for fiscal years beginning on or
24 after July 1, 2026, the Department shall adjust the fee amounts listed in subsection (a) of this
25 section in accordance with the percent change in the annual Consumer Price Index computed by
26 the Bureau of Labor Statistics using the most recent 12-month period for which data is available.
27 The adjustment for fees under this subsection shall be rounded to the nearest dollar ($1.00), and
28 the Commissioner shall publish any increase in fees under this subsection in the North Carolina
29 Register and on the Department's website at least 60 days prior to any increase."
30 SECTION 1.(e) G.S. 95-111.4(19) is repealed.
31 SECTION 1.(f) Article 14B of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by
32 adding a new section to read:
33 "§ 95-111.4A. Fees.
34 (a) Beginning July 1, 2025, the Department shall charge fees not to exceed the following:
35 Advance Location Notice (ALN) Application Fee $25.00
36 Special Inspection Fee – Expedited $1,000
37 Amusement Major Ride Inspections $250.00
38 Amusement Return Trip Inspections $500.00
39 Holiday/Weekend Inspections $500.00
40 Kiddie Ride Inspections $100.00
41 Go Kart Inspections (per cart) $50.00
42 Go Kart Track Inspections $200.00
43 Amusement Rock Wall Inspections $100.00
44 Roller Coaster (permanent and portable) Inspections $500.00
45 Simulators $100.00
46 Bungee Trampoline Inspections $100.00
47 Water Slide Inspections $300.00
48 Train Inspections $250.00
49 (b) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, for fiscal years beginning on or
50 after July 1, 2026, the Department shall adjust the fee amounts listed in subsection (a) of this
51 section in accordance with the percent change in the annual Consumer Price Index computed by

2 The adjustment for fees under this subsection shall be rounded to the nearest dollar ($1.00), and
3 the Commissioner shall publish any increase in fees under this subsection in the North Carolina
4 Register and on the Department's website at least 60 days prior to any increase."
5 SECTION 1.(g) G.S. 95-120(9) is repealed.
6 SECTION 1.(h) Article 15 of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by
7 adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 95-120A. Fees.
9 (a) Beginning July 1, 2025, the Department shall charge fees not to exceed the following:
10 Gondolas, Chairlifts, Inclined Railroad Inspections $500.00
11 J or T Bars and Conveyors Inspections $300.00
12 Rope Tow Inspections $200.00
13 (b) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, for fiscal years beginning on or
14 after July 1, 2026, the Department shall adjust the fee amounts listed in subsection (a) of this
15 section in accordance with the percent change in the annual Consumer Price Index computed by
16 the Bureau of Labor Statistics using the most recent 12-month period for which data is available.
17 The adjustment for fees under this subsection shall be rounded to the nearest dollar ($1.00), and
18 the Commissioner shall publish any increase in fees under this subsection in the North Carolina
19 Register and on the Department's website at least 60 days prior to any increase."
20 SECTION 1.(i) The Commissioner shall publish notice of the changes in fees created
21 by this section in the North Carolina Register and on the Department's website no later than 30
22 days after the effective date of this act.
23
24 PART II. DEVELOP TRAINING AND APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM
25 SECTION 2. The Department of Labor (Department) shall consult with the North
26 Carolina Community College System to develop an in-house training and apprenticeship
27 program (Program) for elevator inspectors. The Department shall utilize the Program to fill
28 vacancies in receipt-supported inspector positions within the Elevator and Amusement Device
29 Division of the Department.
30
31 PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
32 SECTION 3. This act is effective when it becomes law.
