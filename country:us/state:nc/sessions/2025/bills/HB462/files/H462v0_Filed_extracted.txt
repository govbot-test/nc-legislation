15 "§ 75F-102. Definitions.
16 The following definitions apply in this Chapter:
17 (1) Affiliate. – A legal entity that shares common branding with another legal
18 entity or controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with another
19 legal entity. For the purposes of this subdivision, "control" or "controlled"
20 means any of the following:
21 a. Ownership of, or the power to vote, more than fifty percent (50%) of
22 the outstanding shares of any class of voting security of a legal entity.

24 or of individuals exercising similar functions.
25 c. The power to exercise controlling influence over the management of a
26 legal entity.
27 (2) Authenticate. – To use reasonable means to determine that a request to
28 exercise any of the rights afforded under G.S. 75F-104(a)(1) to (4), inclusive,
29 is being made by, or on behalf of, the consumer who is entitled to exercise the
30 consumer rights with respect to the personal data at issue.
31 (3) Biometric data. – Personal information and data generated by automatic
32 measurements of an individual's unique biological characteristics, such as a
33 fingerprint, a voiceprint, eye retinas, irises, or other unique biological patterns
34 or characteristics that can be used to identify or authenticate a specific
35 individual. "Biometric data" does not include any of the following:
36 a. A digital or physical photograph.

19 "§ 75F-103. Applicability of Chapter.
20 (a) This Chapter applies to persons that conduct business in the State or persons that
21 produce products or services that are targeted to residents of the State and that during the
22 preceding calendar year did any of the following:
23 (1) Controlled or processed the personal data of not less than 35,000 consumers,
24 excluding personal data controlled or processed solely for the purpose of
25 completing a payment transaction.
26 (2) Controlled or processed the personal data of not less than 10,000 consumers
27 and derived more than twenty percent (20%) of their gross revenue from the
28 sale of personal data.
29 (b) This Chapter does not apply to any of the following entities:
30 (1) Any regulatory, administrative, advisory, executive, appointive, legislative, or
31 judicial body of the State or a political subdivision of the State, including any
32 board, bureau, commission, or agency of the State or a political subdivision
33 of the State, but excluding any institution of higher education.
34 (2) Any financial institution or affiliate of a financial institution, all as defined in
35 15 U.S.C. § 6809, to the extent that the financial institution or affiliate is
36 subject to Title V of the Gramm Leach Bliley Act (15 U.S.C. § 6801, et seq.,
37 as amended) and the rules and implementing regulations promulgated
38 thereunder.

47 "§ 75F-104. Consumer personal data rights.
48 (a) A consumer has the right to do all of the following:
49 (1) Confirm whether a controller is processing the consumer's personal data and
50 access the personal data, unless the confirmation or access would require the
51 controller to reveal a trade secret.

1 (2) Correct inaccuracies in the consumer's personal data, taking into account the
2 nature of the personal data and the purposes of the processing of the
3 consumer's personal data.
4 (3) Have personal data provided by, or obtained about, the consumer deleted.
5 (4) Obtain a copy of the consumer's personal data processed by the controller, in
6 a portable and, to the extent technically feasible, readily usable format that
7 allows the consumer to transmit the data to another controller without
8 hindrance, where the processing is carried out by automated means, provided

11 the consumer's personal data. If the controller does not maintain this
12 information in a format specific to the consumer, a list of specific third parties
13 to whom the controller has disclosed any consumers' personal data may be
14 provided instead.
15 (6) Opt out of the processing of the personal data for purposes of any of the
16 following:
17 a. Targeted advertising.
18 b. The sale of personal data, except as provided in G.S. 75F-106(b).
19 c. Profiling in furtherance of solely automated decisions that produce
20 legal or similarly significant effects concerning the consumer.
21 (b) A consumer may exercise rights under this section by secure and reliable means
22 established by the controller and described to the consumer in the controller's privacy notice. A
23 consumer may designate an authorized agent in accordance with G.S. 75F-105 to exercise the
24 rights of the consumer to opt out of the processing of the consumer's personal data for purposes
25 of subdivision (5) of subsection (a) of this section on behalf of the consumer. In the case of
26 processing personal data of a known child, the parent or legal guardian may exercise the
27 consumer rights on the child's behalf. In the case of processing personal data concerning a
28 consumer subject to a guardianship, conservatorship, or other protective arrangement, the
29 guardian or the conservator of the consumer may exercise the rights on the consumer's behalf.
30 (c) Except as otherwise provided in this Chapter, a controller shall comply with a request
31 by a consumer to exercise the consumer rights authorized pursuant to said sections as follows:
32 (1) A controller shall respond to the consumer without undue delay but not later
33 than 45 days after receipt of the request. The controller may extend the
34 response period by 45 additional days when reasonably necessary, considering
35 the complexity and number of the consumer's requests, provided the controller
36 informs the consumer of any such extension within the initial 45-day response
37 period and of the reason for the extension.
38 (2) If a controller declines to take action regarding the consumer's request, the
39 controller shall inform the consumer without undue delay but not later than 45
40 days after receipt of the request of the justification for declining to take action
41 and instructions for how to appeal the decision.
42 (3) Information provided in response to a consumer request shall be provided by
43 a controller, free of charge, once per consumer during any 12-month period.
44 If requests from a consumer are manifestly unfounded, excessive, or
45 repetitive, the controller may charge the consumer a reasonable fee to cover
46 the administrative costs of complying with the request or decline to act on the
47 request. The controller bears the burden of demonstrating the manifestly
48 unfounded, excessive, or repetitive nature of the request.
49 (4) If a controller is unable to authenticate a request to exercise any of the rights
50 afforded under subdivisions (1) through (5), inclusive, of subsection (a) of this

30 "§ 75F-105. Designation of agent to exercise rights of consumer, including through
31 universal opt-out mechanisms.
32 (a) A consumer may designate an authorized agent to act on the consumer's behalf to opt
33 out of the processing of the consumer's personal data for one or more of the purposes specified
34 in G.S. 75F-104(a)(5). The consumer may designate the authorized agent by way of, among other

43 the authority unless the controller has established a reasonable basis to conclude that the agent
44 lacks such authority.
