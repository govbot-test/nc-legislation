1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REIMBURSE OWNERS OF RETIRED FIRST RESPONDER CANINES FOR
3 VETERINARY CARE.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
6 Article to read:
7 "Article 12I.
8 "Retired First Responder Canine Veterinary Care Reimbursements.
9 "§ 143-166.90. Purpose.
10 In consideration of hazardous public service rendered to the people of this State, there is
11 provided a system of veterinary care reimbursement for retired first responder canines.
12 "§ 143-166.91. Definitions.
13 The following definitions apply in this Article:
14 (1) Correctional agency. – A lawfully established State or local public agency
15 having primary responsibility for the supervision, protection, care, custody,
16 control, or investigation of individuals at a State prison or local confinement
17 facility within the State.
18 (2) Covered first responder unit. ‒ Any correctional agency, fire department, or
19 law enforcement agency.
20 (3) Fire department. ‒ A lawfully established State or local public agency having
21 primary responsibility for firefighting and fire prevention.
22 (4) Law enforcement agency. – A lawfully established State or local public
23 agency within the State having primary responsibility for the prevention and
24 detection of crime or the enforcement of penal, traffic, highway, regulatory,
25 game, immigration, postal, customs, or controlled substance laws.
26 (5) Retired first responder canine. – A canine meeting the following criteria:
27 a. The canine retired on or after July 1, 2024, and prior to retirement was
28 owned by a covered first responder unit for the principal purpose of
29 aiding in the detection of criminal activity; the enforcement of laws;
30 the apprehension of offenders; the detection of accelerants, explosives,
31 or narcotics; or search and rescue operations.
32 b. The canine received certification in obedience, apprehension,
33 explosives detection, accelerants detection, narcotics detection, or

1 search and rescue work from a nationally recognized organization that
2 certifies canines performing that type of work.
3 (6) Veterinary care. – The practice, by a veterinarian, of veterinary medicine as
4 defined in G.S. 90-181. For purposes of this Article, this term includes, at a
5 minimum, each of the following:
6 a. Annual wellness examinations.
7 b. Vaccinations.
8 c. Internal and external parasite prevention treatments.
9 d. Testing and treatment of illnesses and diseases.
10 e. Medications.
11 f. Emergency care and surgeries.
12 g. Veterinary oncology or other specialty care.
13 h. Euthanasia.
14 i. Cremation.
15 "§ 143-166.92. Registration of a retired first responder canine with the Department of
16 Public Safety.
17 (a) In order for a retired first responder canine to be eligible to participate in this program,
18 upon the retirement of a first responder canine, the covered first responder unit shall register the
19 canine with the Department of Public Safety. Registration of the retired first responder canine
20 shall require the following information from the covered first responder unit:
21 (1) Verification that the canine was owned by the covered first responder unit for
22 the principal purpose of aiding in the detection of criminal activity; the
23 enforcement of laws; the apprehension of offenders; the detection of
24 accelerants, explosives, or narcotics; or search and rescue operations.
25 (2) The date the canine retired from service with the covered first responder unit.
26 (3) The name, breed, and age of the canine.
27 (b) Upon receipt of all information required in subsection (a) of this section, the
28 Department shall issue to the covered first responder unit a certification that the canine has been
29 registered as a retired first responder canine for purposes of this Article.
30 (c) The covered first responder unit shall provide a copy of the retired first responder
31 certification to the canine's new owner.
32 "§ 143-166.93. Registration of a retired first responder canine owner with the Department
33 of Public Safety.
34 (a) An owner of a retired first responder canine who seeks reimbursement for veterinary
35 care of the canine shall register with the Department of Public Safety. Registration of the owner
36 shall require the following information from the owner:
37 (1) Name, telephone number, and mailing address of the owner.
38 (2) Proof of ownership of the retired first responder canine.
39 (3) A copy of the retired first responder canine certification from the covered first
40 responder unit from which the canine retired.
41 (b) The owner shall be responsible for notifying the Department of any changes to the
42 information required for registration pursuant to subsection (a) of this section.
43 (c) Upon receipt of all information required in subsection (a) of this section, the
44 Department shall register the owner as the approved Fund recipient for any eligible
45 reimbursements paid for the retired first responder canine's veterinary care.
46 "§ 143-166.94. Application for reimbursement.
47 (a) When a retired first responder canine receives veterinary care, the registered owner
48 of the canine may apply to the Department of Public Safety pursuant to the provisions of this
49 Article for the reimbursement of that care up to the amount of one thousand five hundred dollars
50 ($1,500) in a State fiscal year.

1 (b) When seeking reimbursement in accordance with the provisions of this Article, the
2 registered owner of the retired first responder canine must submit an application for
3 reimbursement within 90 days of the date of the veterinary care received by the retired first
4 responder canine.
5 (c) The application for reimbursement shall be submitted on a form created by the
6 Department which shall, at a minimum, require the following information:
7 (1) The name of the registered owner.
8 (2) An itemized statement from the veterinarian that includes all of the following
9 information for veterinary care services provided to the retired first responder
10 canine:
11 a. The name, telephone number, and mailing address of the veterinarian.
12 b. The name of the retired first responder canine that received veterinary
13 care from the veterinarian.
14 c. The date the retired first responder canine received veterinary care
15 from the veterinarian.
16 d. The specific veterinary care services provided.
17 e. The cost of each veterinary care service provided.
18 f. Confirmation that the registered owner paid for the veterinary care
19 services.
20 (3) Any additional information required by the Department to verify the
21 authenticity of the veterinary care services or costs submitted by the registered
22 owner in the application for reimbursement.
23 (d) The Department may deny an application for reimbursement under any of the
24 following circumstances:
25 (1) The canine that received veterinary care is not a registered first responder
26 canine pursuant to G.S. 143-166.92.
27 (2) The owner has not registered with the Department pursuant to
28 G.S. 143-166.93.
29 (3) The registered owner's application for reimbursement does not contain all
30 required information set forth in G.S. 143-166.94.
31 (4) The application for reimbursement contains inaccurate or materially false
32 information.
33 (5) The registered owner has already received the maximum reimbursement
34 amount for the retired first responder canine for the State fiscal year.
35 (6) The funds allocated for this program have been exhausted for the fiscal year.
36 "§ 143-166.95. Creation of Fund; management.
37 There is established within the Department of Public Safety the Retired First Responder
38 Canine Fund. All monies in the Fund shall not revert. Awards from the Fund shall be distributed
39 in accordance with this Article.
40 The Department of Public Safety shall have the power to adopt rules for the administration
41 of the provisions of this Article and shall have the authority to use up to one percent (1%) of
42 monies received by the Retired First Responder Canine Fund for the purpose of administering
43 the Fund. The Department of Public Safety shall be vested with the power to make all
44 determinations necessary for the administration of this Article and all of its decisions and
45 determinations shall be final and conclusive and not subject to review or reversal.
46 "§ 143-166.96. Record keeping.
47 In relation to applications received under this Article, the Department of Public Safety shall
48 keep a record of the following:
49 (1) All applications received.
50 (2) All application determinations.
51 (3) All funds disbursed.

1 "§ 143-166.97. Names of owners of retired first responder canines not a public record.
2 Except as otherwise provided in this section, the name, address, and telephone number of the
3 registered owner of the retired first responder canine is confidential and shall not be considered
4 a public record as that term is defined in G.S. 132-1. Nothing in this section prohibits the
5 Department of Public Safety from sharing any information directly necessary for the
6 administration of the Fund.
7 "§ 143-166.98. Other benefits not affected; elective participation.
8 (a) No other benefits now provided for retired first responder canines shall be affected
9 by the provisions of this Article, and the benefits provided for in this Article shall not be
10 diminished, abated, or otherwise affected by the provision of benefits to retired first responder
11 canines under any other provision of law.
12 (b) Owners of retired first responder canines are not required to seek reimbursement for
13 veterinary care under this Article, and the failure to seek that reimbursement in a given year shall
14 not preclude an owner from seeking future reimbursement under this Article."

19 accordance with Article 12I of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes."
