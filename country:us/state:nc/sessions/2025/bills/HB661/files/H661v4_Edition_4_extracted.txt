18 North Carolina Fire Code, and amendments thereto, as adopted by the
19 Council.
20 (2) Council. – The North Carolina Building Code Council.

22 requirements for in-building emergency responder communications coverage
23 systems specified under Section 510 of the North Carolina Fire Code.
24 SECTION 2.3.(b) Exemptions Established. – Notwithstanding the emergency
25 responder communication coverage requirements of the Code, the following buildings and
26 structures, whether existing, newly constructed, or altered after the effective date of this act, are
27 exempt from those requirements:
28 (1) R-2 apartment occupancies of Type V construction that meet the egress
29 requirements of the North Carolina State Building Code.
30 (2) Apartment buildings and transient public lodging establishments, including
31 hotels and motels, not exceeding two stories above grade plane and that
32 provide direct exterior egress from each dwelling unit or guest room.
33 SECTION 2.3.(c) Treatment of Existing Systems in Exempted Buildings. –
34 Notwithstanding Section 901.6 of the North Carolina Fire Code or any other provision of the
35 Code that might require the removal of nonrequired, obsolete, or abandoned systems, an
36 emergency responder communication coverage system installed prior to the effective date of this
37 section in a building or structure that meets the exemption criteria established in subsection (b)
38 of this section shall only be required to have only active components of the enhancement system
39 to render them inoperative removed. Systems that are monitored by a fire alarm system shall be
40 properly disconnected to eliminate unnecessary supervisory signals.
41 SECTION 2.3.(d) Rulemaking Required. – The Council shall adopt rules to amend
42 the Code, specifically Section 510 of the North Carolina Fire Code and any related sections
43 addressing emergency responder communication coverage, to incorporate the exemptions listed
44 in subsection (b) of this section and the provisions regarding existing systems in subsection (c)
45 of this section. Notwithstanding G.S. 150B-19(4), the rules adopted by the Council pursuant to
46 this section shall be substantively identical to the provisions of subsections (b) and (c) of this
47 section. Rules adopted pursuant to this section are not subject to Part 3 of Article 2A of Chapter
48 150B of the General Statutes. Rules adopted pursuant to this section shall become effective as
49 provided in G.S. 150B-21.3(b1), as though 10 or more written objections had been received as
