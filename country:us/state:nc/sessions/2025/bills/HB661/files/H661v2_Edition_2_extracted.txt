19 "§ 160D-1502. Limit street design methodology and construction standards for certain
20 municipal streets.

12 EXEMPT MODEL HOMES FROM FIRE PROTECTION WATER SUPPLY

14 SECTION 2.1.(a) Definitions. – For the purposes of this section, the following
15 definitions apply:
16 (1) Code. – The North Carolina State Building Code, and amendments to the
17 Code, as adopted by the Councils.
18 (2) Councils. – The Building Code Council and Residential Code Council.
19 (3) Model home. – As defined in G.S. 160D-1501(a).
20 (4) Water Supply Rules. – Section 3312.1, when required, of the North Carolina
21 Fire Code, and Section 3313.1, where required, of the North Carolina Building
22 Code.
23 SECTION 2.1.(b) Water Supply Rules. – Until the effective date of the rules to
24 amend Water Supply Rules, the Office of the State Fire Marshal, the Councils, and State and
25 local governments enforcing the Code shall implement Water Supply Rules as provided in
26 subsection (c) of this section.
27 SECTION 2.1.(c) Implementation. – Notwithstanding Water Supply Rules, the fire
28 code official is authorized to reduce the fire-flow requirements for an isolated model home at a
29 subdivision project site where development of full-fire flow requirements is impractical or
30 pending.
31 SECTION 2.1.(d) Additional Rulemaking Authority. – The Council shall adopt rules
32 to amend Water Supply Rules to be consistent with subsection (c) of this section.
33 Notwithstanding G.S. 150B-19(4), the rule adopted by the Council pursuant to this section shall
34 be substantively identical to the provisions of subsection (c) of this section. Rules adopted
35 pursuant to this section are not subject to Part 3 of Article 2A of Chapter 150B of the General
36 Statutes. Rules adopted pursuant to this section shall become effective as provided in
37 G.S. 150B-21.3(b1) as though 10 or more written objections had been received as provided in
38 G.S. 150B-21.3(b2).
39 SECTION 2.1.(e) Additional Residential Code Council Rulemaking Authority. –
40 The Residential Code Council shall adopt rules to amend the 2024 North Carolina State Building
41 Code volumes specified within G.S. 143-138(a)(1) through (10) to make conforming changes to
42 codes applicable to residential construction consistent with rules adopted by the Building Code
43 Council as required by subsection (d) of this section. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection
44 are not subject to Part 3 of Article 2A of Chapter 150B of the General Statutes. Rules adopted
45 pursuant to this subsection shall become effective as provided in G.S. 150B-21.3(b1) as though
46 10 or more written objections had been received as provided in G.S. 150B-21.3(b2).
47 SECTION 2.1.(f) Sunset. – This section expires when permanent rules adopted as
48 required by subsections (d) and (e) of this section become effective.

34 This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Right to Repair Construction Defects

36 "§ 87-151. Definitions.
37 The following definitions apply in this Article:
38 (1) Action. – Any civil lawsuit, judicial action, or alternative dispute resolution
39 proceeding for damages or other relief asserting a claim for injury or loss to a
40 dwelling caused by an alleged construction defect arising out of or related to
41 the construction, repairing, or remodeling of the dwelling.
42 (2) Claimant. – An owner who asserts a claim against a general contractor
43 concerning a construction defect in the construction, repairing, or remodeling
44 of a dwelling.
45 (3) Construction defect. – A defect in or a deficiency arising out of the design or
46 construction of improvements, including the use of defective material,
47 products, or components.
48 (4) Dwelling. – A single-family dwelling or other dwelling type subject to the
49 North Carolina Residential Code, including the systems, components, and
50 improvements serving that dwelling.
51 (5) General contractor. – As defined in G.S. 87-1.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (6) Serve or service. – Personal service or delivery by certified mail to the last
2 known address of the addressee.
3 "§ 87-152. Notice and opportunity to repair.
4 (a) Notice of Claim. – Before commencing an action against a general contractor
5 regarding a construction defect, the claimant must, no less than 90 days before filing the action,
6 serve a written notice of claim on the general contractor. The notice of claim must contain the
7 following, at a minimum:
8 (1) A statement that the claimant asserts a construction defect.
9 (2) A description of the claim or claims in reasonable detail sufficient to
10 determine the general nature of the construction defect.
11 (3) A description of any effects of the construction defect, if known.
12 (b) Request for Clarification. – If a notice of claim is not sufficiently stated, the general
13 contractor may advise the claimant within 15 days of service of the notice of claim and request
14 clarification.
15 (c) General Contractor's Response to Notice of Claim. – The general contractor has 30
16 days from service of the notice of claim to inspect, offer to remedy, offer to settle with the
17 claimant, or deny a claim, in whole or in part, regarding the construction defect in writing. The
18 claimant shall receive written notice of the general contractor's election under this section within
19 30 days. Failure to respond within 30 days is deemed a denial of the claim.
20 (d) Response to General Contractor's Offer. – The claimant shall accept a general
21 contractor's offer, if any, in writing within 10 days of receipt of the offer pursuant to subsection
22 (c) of this section. Failure to respond within 10 days is deemed a denial of the general contractor's
23 offer.
24 (e) Reasonable Access to Dwelling. – If requested, the claimant shall give the general
25 contractor reasonable access to the dwelling at a mutually agreeable time to inspect a construction
26 defect and, if agreed to by the parties, repair a construction defect.
27 (f) Civil Remedy. – Nothing in this Article shall prevent a claimant from proceeding with
28 a civil action or other remedy provided by law if the parties cannot reach a resolution pursuant
29 to this Article.
30 (g) Claimant Repairs. – Nothing in this Article shall be construed to prohibit a claimant
31 from making immediate repairs to an alleged construction defect subject to this Article to protect
32 the health and safety of a dwelling's occupants or to mitigate further damage to the dwelling.
33 (h) Admissibility. – Evidence of offers to settle, repair, or remedy pursuant to this Article
34 is not admissible to prove liability for or invalidity of the claim or its amount.
35 "§ 87-153. Filing a complaint with the Board not prohibited.
36 Nothing in this Article shall prevent a claimant from filing a complaint with the Board in
37 accordance with G.S. 87-11(a1).
38 "§ 87-154. Stay of action.
39 If a claimant files an action in court before first complying with the requirements of this
40 Article, on motion of a party to the action, the court shall stay the action for 120 days and such
41 additional time that is necessary for compliance with the requirements of this Article.
42 "§ 87-155. Statute of limitations and tolling.
43 Any statute of limitations applicable to a right of action of the claimant under this Article is
44 tolled until the latter of the following events occur: (i) the claimant denies a general contractor's
45 offer, in writing, pursuant to G.S. 87-152(d), (ii) the general contractor denies the claimant's
46 claim, (iii) the repair of the defect is completed, or (iv) either party determines that the general
47 contractor cannot or will not repair the defect and provides written notice to the other party of
48 the same."
