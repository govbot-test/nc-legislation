1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ENACT THE NORTH CAROLINA CROWN ACT TO CREATE A
3 RESPECTFUL AND OPEN WORLD FOR NATURAL HAIR.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "North Carolina
6 CROWN Act."
7 SECTION 2. Article 3 of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
8 a new section to read:
9 "§ 95-28.1B. Discrimination against persons in employment.
10 (a) No person, firm, corporation, unincorporated association, State agency, unit of local
11 government, or any public or private entity shall deny or refuse employment to any person or
12 discharge any person from employment on account of the person's race, color, creed, religion,
13 sex, or national origin.
14 (b) The following definitions apply in this section:
15 (1) Protective hairstyles. – Includes, but is not limited to, such hairstyles as bantu
16 knots, braids, locks, and twists.
17 (2) Race. – Includes traits historically associated with race, including, but not
18 limited to, hair texture, hair type, and protective hairstyles."
19 SECTION 3. G.S. 95-241(a) reads as rewritten:
20 "(a) No person shall discriminate or take any retaliatory action against an employee
21 because the employee in good faith does or threatens to do any of the following:
22 (1) File a claim or complaint, initiate any inquiry, investigation, inspection,
23 proceeding or other action, or testify or provide information to any person
24 with respect to any of the following:
25 a. Chapter 97 of the General Statutes.
26 b. Article 2A or Article 16 of this Chapter.
27 c. Article 2A of Chapter 74 of the General Statutes.
28 d. G.S. 95-28.1.
29 e. Article 16 of Chapter 127A of the General Statutes.
30 f. G.S. 95-28.1A.G.S. 95-28.1A or G.S. 95-28.1B.
31 g. Article 52 of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes.
32 h. Article 5F of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes.
33 (2) Cause any of the activities listed in subdivision (1) of this subsection to be
34 initiated on an employee's behalf.
