6 CONFIDENTIALITY OF WILDLIFE RESOURCE INFORMATION
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 143-254.5 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 143-254.5. Disclosure of personal identifying and sensitive wildlife resource information.
9 (a) Social security numbers and identifying information obtained by the Commission
10 shall be treated as provided in G.S. 132-1.10. For purposes of this section, "identifying
11 information" also includes a person's mailing address, residence address, e-mail address,
12 Commission-issued customer identification number, date of birth, information subject to
13 G.S. 106-24.1 transferred to the Commission from the Department of Agriculture and Consumer
14 Services, and telephone number.
15 (b) Records that contain site-specific information regarding the occurrence of rare species
16 of plants or animals specified in the current North Carolina Wildlife Action Plan, or the location
17 of sensitive habitats, are confidential and exempt from disclosure under Chapter 132 of the
18 General Statutes when the Executive Director finds that disclosure will create a risk of harm,
19 theft, or destruction to the species or habitats or the area where the species or habitats are located.
20 This subsection does not apply to a request for release of records requested by the owner of record
21 of land where such species, habitats, or areas are located."

22 "(a) A vessel shall not be required to be numbered under this Chapter if it is:

24 federal law or a federally approved numbering system of another state, and
25 for which an identification number has been so awarded: Provided, that any
26 such vessel shall not have been within this State for a period in excess of 90
27 60 consecutive days.
28 …."

30 "(a) Every vessel operated on the waters of this State that is required to obtain an

33 approved numbering system of another state shall comply with the navigation rules, including
34 requirements for navigational lights, sound-signaling devices, and other equipment, contained in

36 2071-2073 (1993) and rules adopted pursuant thereto, see 33 C.F.R. Part 84 (1992)."

38 "§ 75A-13.3. Personal watercraft.

42 watercraft on the waters of this State, unless:

45 personal flotation devices do not satisfy this requirement; and

4 (e) A personal watercraft must at all times be operated in a reasonable and prudent
5 manner. Maneuvers that endanger life, limb, or property shall constitute reckless operation of a
6 vessel as provided in G.S. 75A-10, and include any of the following:
7 (1) Unreasonably or unnecessarily weaving through congested vessel traffic.
8 (2) Jumping the wake of another vessel within 100 feet of the other vessel or when
9 visibility around the other vessel is obstructed.
10 (3) Intentionally approaching another vessel in order to swerve at the last possible
11 moment to avoid collision.
12 (4) Repealed by Session Laws 2000-52, s. 2.
13 (5) Operating contrary to the "rules of the road" navigation rules or following too
14 closely to another vessel, including another personal watercraft. For purposes
15 of this subdivision, "following too closely" means proceeding in the same
16 direction and operating at a speed in excess of 10 miles per hour when
17 approaching within 100 feet to the rear or 50 feet to the side of another vessel
18 that is underway unless that vessel is operating in a narrow channel, in which
19 case a personal watercraft may operate at the speed and flow of other vessel
20 traffic.
21 …."
22 SECTION 3.(e) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025. Prosecutions for
23 offenses committed before the effective date of this act are not abated or affected by this act, and
24 the statutes that would be applicable but for this act remain applicable to those prosecutions.
