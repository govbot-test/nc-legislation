H.B. 747
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 2, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: 2025 Wildlife Resources Changes.-AB (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Adams.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MAKE VARIOUS CHANGES TO THE WILDLIFE LAWS, AS REQUESTED
3 BY THE WILDLIFE RESOURCES COMMISSION.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5
6 CONFIDENTIALITY OF WILDLIFE RESOURCE INFORMATION
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 143-254.5 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 143-254.5. Disclosure of personal identifying and sensitive wildlife resource information.
9 (a) Social security numbers and identifying information obtained by the Commission
10 shall be treated as provided in G.S. 132-1.10. For purposes of this section, "identifying
11 information" also includes a person's mailing address, residence address, e-mail address,
12 Commission-issued customer identification number, date of birth, information subject to
13 G.S. 106-24.1 transferred to the Commission from the Department of Agriculture and Consumer
14 Services, and telephone number.
15 (b) Records that contain site-specific information regarding the occurrence of rare species
16 of plants or animals specified in the current North Carolina Wildlife Action Plan, or the location
17 of sensitive habitats, are confidential and exempt from disclosure under Chapter 132 of the
18 General Statutes when the Executive Director finds that disclosure will create a risk of harm,
19 theft, or destruction to the species or habitats or the area where the species or habitats are located.
20 This subsection does not apply to a request for release of records requested by the owner of record
21 of land where such species, habitats, or areas are located."
22
23 AUTHORIZE THE DIVISION OF MOTOR VEHICLES TO ISSUE LIFETIME
24 CONSERVATIONIST AND WILDLIFE RESOURCES REGISTRATION PLATES

26 "() Wildlife Resources."

32 Lifetime Conservationist" logo of the North Carolina Wildlife Resources

34 …

36 accordance with G.S. 20-81.12. The plate shall bear the phrase "Wildlife

4 SECTION 2.(c) G.S. 20-81.12 reads as rewritten:

6 …
7 () Lifetime Conservationist. – The Division must transfer quarterly the money in the

19 RECREATIONAL BOATING SAFETY CONFORMING PROGRAM CHANGES
20 SECTION 3.(a) G.S. 75A-5 reads as rewritten:
21 "§ 75A-5. Application for certificate of number; fees; reciprocity; change of ownership;
22 conformity with federal regulations; records; award of certificates; renewal of
23 certificates; transfer of partial interest; destroyed or junked vessels;
24 abandonment; change of address; duplicate certificates; display.
25 …
26 (b) Reciprocity. – The owner of any vessel already covered by a number in full force and
27 effect pursuant to federal law or a federally approved numbering system of another state shall
28 record the identification number prior to operating the vessel on the waters of this State in excess
29 of the 90-day 60-day reciprocity period provided for in G.S. 75A-7(a)(1). The recordation shall
30 be made pursuant to subsection (a) of this section, except that no additional or substitute
31 identification number shall be issued.
32 …."
33 SECTION 3.(b) G.S. 75A-7(a) reads as rewritten:
34 "(a) A vessel shall not be required to be numbered under this Chapter if it is:
35 (1) A vessel that is required to be awarded an identification number pursuant to
36 federal law or a federally approved numbering system of another state, and
37 for which an identification number has been so awarded: Provided, that any
38 such vessel shall not have been within this State for a period in excess of 90
39 60 consecutive days.
40 …."
41 SECTION 3.(c) G.S. 75A-6.1(a) reads as rewritten:
42 "(a) Every vessel operated on the waters of this State that is required to obtain an
43 identification number pursuant to this Chapter, has a valid marine document issued by the federal
44 Bureau of Customs or any federal agency successor to it, or issued pursuant to a federally
45 approved numbering system of another state shall comply with the navigation rules, including
46 requirements for navigational lights, sound-signaling devices, and other equipment, contained in
47 the Inland Navigational Rules Act of 1980, codified as amended at 33 U.S.C. §§ 2001-2038,
48 2071-2073 (1993) and rules adopted pursuant thereto, see 33 C.F.R. Part 84 (1992)."
49 SECTION 3.(d) G.S. 75A-13.3 reads as rewritten:
50 "§ 75A-13.3. Personal watercraft.
51 …
Page 2 DRH10312-MH-84A
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (d) No person shall operate a personal watercraft on the waters of this State, nor shall the
2 owner of a personal watercraft knowingly allow another person to operate that personal
3 watercraft on the waters of this State, unless:
4 (1) Each person riding on or being towed behind the vessel is wearing a personal
5 flotation device approved by the United States Coast Guard. Inflatable
6 personal flotation devices do not satisfy this requirement; and
7 (2) In the case of a personal watercraft equipped by the manufacturer with a
8 lanyard-type an engine cut-off switch, the lanyard is securely attached to the
9 person, clothing, or flotation device of the operator at all times while the
10 personal watercraft is being operated in such a manner to turn off the engine
11 if the operator dismounts while the watercraft is in operation.the engine
12 cut-off switch is being used while the vessel is operating on plane or above
13 displacement speed.
14 …
15 (e) A personal watercraft must at all times be operated in a reasonable and prudent
16 manner. Maneuvers that endanger life, limb, or property shall constitute reckless operation of a
17 vessel as provided in G.S. 75A-10, and include any of the following:
18 (1) Unreasonably or unnecessarily weaving through congested vessel traffic.
19 (2) Jumping the wake of another vessel within 100 feet of the other vessel or when
20 visibility around the other vessel is obstructed.
21 (3) Intentionally approaching another vessel in order to swerve at the last possible
22 moment to avoid collision.
23 (4) Repealed by Session Laws 2000-52, s. 2.
24 (5) Operating contrary to the "rules of the road" navigation rules or following too
25 closely to another vessel, including another personal watercraft. For purposes
26 of this subdivision, "following too closely" means proceeding in the same
27 direction and operating at a speed in excess of 10 miles per hour when
28 approaching within 100 feet to the rear or 50 feet to the side of another vessel
29 that is underway unless that vessel is operating in a narrow channel, in which
30 case a personal watercraft may operate at the speed and flow of other vessel
31 traffic.
32 …."
33 SECTION 3.(e) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025. Prosecutions for
34 offenses committed before the effective date of this act are not abated or affected by this act, and
35 the statutes that would be applicable but for this act remain applicable to those prosecutions.
36
37 EFFECTIVE DATE
38 SECTION 4. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
39 law.
