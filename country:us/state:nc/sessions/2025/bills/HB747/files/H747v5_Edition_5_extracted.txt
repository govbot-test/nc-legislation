28 ALLOW THE WILDLIFE RESOURCES COMMISSION TO ISSUE ELK PERMITS BY
29 RAFFLE AND AUCTION
30 SECTION 4.(a) Directive to Commission. – The Wildlife Resources Commission
31 shall issue one resident Elk Permit by raffle and one Elk Permit by auction as provided in this
32 section. These permits shall be issued for the 2027 hunting season, pursuant to rules adopted by
33 the Commission. Any elk taken shall be reported and presented to Commission staff pursuant to
34 the conditions of the permit issued. An Elk Permit obtained by raffle or auction is nontransferable
35 and may not be resold or reassigned.
36 SECTION 4.(b) Raffle. – The Commission shall conduct a raffle for one resident
37 Elk Permit. Raffle tickets will be twenty dollars ($20.00) per ticket or a maximum of 30 raffle
38 tickets for five hundred dollars ($500.00). Purchasers of raffle tickets must either have a valid
39 North Carolina hunting license or be licensed exempt as provided in G.S. 113-276 at the time of
40 purchase. The Commission may retain from the proceeds of the raffle the actual costs of
41 administering the raffle for a resident Elk Permit, not to exceed twenty-five thousand dollars
42 ($25,000). The Commission shall deposit the remaining proceeds from the raffle in the Wildlife
43 Resources Fund created under G.S. 143-250 to be used for the conservation and management of
44 elk.
45 SECTION 4.(c) Auction. – Notwithstanding Article 3 of Chapter 143 of the General
46 Statutes or any other provision of law pertaining to public contracting, the Commission shall
47 select one nonprofit wildlife conservation organization to conduct an auction for one Elk Permit.
48 The nonprofit wildlife conservation organization selected by the Commission pursuant to this
49 section must have been involved in the reintroduction of elk into North Carolina. The nonprofit
50 wildlife conservation organization shall retain no more than twenty-five percent (25%) of the
51 proceeds of the auction for an Elk Permit. The Commission shall deposit the remaining proceeds

3 SECTION 4.(d) Continuing Authority. – For hunting seasons after the 2027 hunting
4 season, the Commission, based on its evaluation of its elk management goals, may raffle off one
5 or more Elk Permits and select one nonprofit wildlife conservation organization to conduct an
6 auction for one Elk Permit in the manner described in subsections (b) and (c) of this section. The
7 following restrictions apply to those future hunting season activities:
8 (1) The Commission must conduct an Elk Permit raffle for one or more Elk
9 Permits if it conducts an Elk Permit auction.
10 (2) The Commission shall reserve fifty percent (50%) of all Elk Permits made
11 available via raffle for residents of the State of North Carolina.
12 SECTION 4.(e) Funds Supplemental. – It is the intent of the General Assembly that
13 receipts generated by the activities authorized by this act supplement, rather than replace, other
14 funds budgeted for management of elk.
15 SECTION 4.(f) Report. – No later than March 1, 2028, and every year thereafter in
16 which the Commission conducts Elk Permit raffles and auctions, the Commission shall submit
17 to the House Wildlife Resources Committee; the Senate Agriculture, Energy, and Environment
18 Committee; and the Fiscal Research Division a report on the effectiveness of the auction and
19 raffle programs, including the amount of money raised, recommendations as to whether to
20 continue the programs, and, if so, legislative recommendations to improve the programs.

25 SECTION 6. Conforming Change. – G.S. 113-291.8 reads as rewritten:
26 "§ 113-291.8. Requirement to display hunter orange.
27 (a) Any person hunting game animals other than foxes, bobcats, raccoons, and opossum,
28 or hunting upland game birds other than wild turkeys, or hunting feral swine, with the use of
29 firearms, must wear a cap or hat on his head made of hunter orange material or an outer garment
30 of hunter orange visible from all sides. Any person hunting deer during a deer firearms season or
31 elk during an open elk season shall wear hunter orange. orange in a manner that is visible from
32 all directions. Hunter orange material is a material that is a daylight fluorescent orange color.
33 This section does not apply to a landholder, his spouse, or children, who are hunting on land
34 held by the landholder. This subsection shall be enforced by warning ticket only until October 1,
35 1992, with respect to those hunting rabbit, squirrel, grouse, pheasant, and quail.
36 (b) Any person violating this section during the 1987 big game hunting season shall be
37 given a warning of violation only. Thereafter, any Any person violating this section has
38 committed an infraction and shall pay a fine of twenty-five dollars ($25.00). An infraction is an
39 unlawful act that is not a crime. The procedure for charging and trying an infraction is the same
40 as for a misdemeanor, but conviction of an infraction has no consequence other than payment of
41 a fine. A person convicted of an infraction may not be assessed court costs.
42 Wildlife Enforcement Officers are authorized to charge persons with the infraction created
43 by this section.
44 (c) Failure to wear hunter orange material in violation of this section shall not constitute
45 negligence per se or contributory negligence per se."
