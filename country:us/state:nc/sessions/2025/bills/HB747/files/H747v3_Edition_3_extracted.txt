7 CONFIDENTIALITY OF WILDLIFE RESOURCE INFORMATION
8 SECTION 1. G.S. 143-254.5 reads as rewritten:
9 "§ 143-254.5. Disclosure of personal identifying and sensitive wildlife resource information.
10 (a) Social security numbers and identifying information obtained by the Commission
11 shall be treated as provided in G.S. 132-1.10. For purposes of this section, "identifying
12 information" also includes a person's mailing address, residence address, e-mail address,
13 Commission-issued customer identification number, date of birth, information subject to
14 G.S. 106-24.1 transferred to the Commission from the Department of Agriculture and Consumer
15 Services, and telephone number.
16 (b) Records that contain site-specific information regarding the occurrence of rare species
17 of plants or animals specified in the current North Carolina Wildlife Action Plan, or the location
18 of sensitive habitats, are confidential and exempt from disclosure under Chapter 132 of the
19 General Statutes when the Executive Director finds that disclosure will create a risk of harm,
20 theft, or destruction to the species or habitats or the area where the species or habitats are located.
21 This subsection does not apply to a request for release of records requested by the owner of record
22 of land where such species, habitats, or areas are located."

21 case a personal watercraft may operate at the speed and flow of other vessel

30 "§ 105-164.4. Tax imposed on retailers and certain facilitators.
31 (a) A privilege tax is imposed on a retailer engaged in business in the State at the
32 percentage rates of the retailer's net taxable sales or gross receipts, listed in this subsection. The
33 general rate of tax is four and three-quarters percent (4.75%). The percentage rates are as follows:
34 …
35 (1b) The rate of three percent (3%) three and one-quarter percent (3.25%) applies
36 to the sales price of each boat sold at retail, including all accessories attached
37 to the boat when it is delivered to the purchaser. The maximum tax is one
38 thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500) per article.
39 …."
40 SECTION 4.(b) Article 5 of Chapter 105 of the General Statutes is amended by
41 adding a new section to read:
42 "§ 105-164.44N. Transfer to Wildlife Resources Fund.
43 The Secretary shall transfer within 75 days after the end of each fiscal year six percent (6%)
44 of the net proceeds of the tax collected on boats under G.S. 105-164.4(a)(1b) to the Wildlife
45 Resources Fund established in G.S. 143-250."
46 SECTION 4.(c) This section is effective October 1, 2025, and applies to sales
47 occurring on or after that date.

50 SECTION 5. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
51 law.
