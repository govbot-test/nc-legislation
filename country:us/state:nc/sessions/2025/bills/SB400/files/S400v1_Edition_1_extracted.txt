GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Adult Protection Multidisciplinary Teams. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Galey and Daniel (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 25, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT CONCERNING THE ESTABLISHMENT OF LOCAL ADULT PROTECTION
3 MULTIDISCIPLINARY TEAMS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Chapter 108A of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
6 Article to read:
7 "Article 6B.
8 "Local Adult Protection Multidisciplinary Teams.
9 "§ 108A-118. Declaration of public policy; purpose.
10 (a) The General Assembly finds that it is the public policy of this State to prevent the
11 abuse, neglect, and exploitation of disabled adults. The General Assembly further finds that
12 professionals from disparate disciplines have expertise that can promote the safety and
13 well-being of disabled adults and prevent re-victimization and that multidisciplinary reviews of
14 selected cases can lead to a greater understanding of the causes of the abuse, neglect, and
15 exploitation of disabled adults. It is, therefore, the intent of the General Assembly, through this
16 Article, to promote the establishment of local adult protection multidisciplinary teams.
17 (b) The purpose of this Article is to authorize local adult protection multidisciplinary case
18 review teams to share information pertaining to selected cases in which disabled adults are being
19 served by adult protective services and to coordinate multiagency solutions for adult victims of
20 abuse, neglect, and exploitation.
21 "§ 108A-118.1. Definitions.
22 The following definitions apply in this Article:
23 (1) Case Review Multidisciplinary Team. – An adult protection multidisciplinary
24 team that reviews selected active cases in which disabled adults are being
25 served by adult protective services through a local department of social
26 services.
27 (2) Director of social services. – As defined by G.S. 108A-74.
28 (3) Disabled adult. – An individual 18 years of age or older or a lawfully
29 emancipated minor who is present in the State of North Carolina and who is
30 physically or mentally incapacitated, as defined in G.S. 108A-101(d).
31 (4) Older adult. – An individual 65 years of age or older.
32 (5) Systemic Review Multidisciplinary Team. – An adult protection
33 multidisciplinary team that works together to do all of the following:
34 a. Address systemic problems and service gaps that may exist in the
35 delivery of services to disabled adults or older adults by public and
36 nonpublic agencies.

1 b. Develop information networks.
2 c. Provide coordinated community education and outreach designed to
3 protect against abuse, neglect, and exploitation of disabled adults or
4 older adults.
5 "§ 108A-118.2. Case Review Multidisciplinary Team; creation and composition.
6 (a) Creation. – Each county may establish its own Case Review Multidisciplinary Team
7 or participate in a multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary Team, as determined in
8 accordance with subsection (b) of this section.
9 (b) Determination of Single County Versus Multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary
10 Team. – Each county board of commissioners may evaluate and determine whether the county
11 will establish its own Case Review Multidisciplinary Team or become part of a multicounty team.
12 This determination shall be made in consultation with the director of the local department of
13 social services. A multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary Team may be formed upon
14 agreement of the county boards of commissioners of each of the counties involved. The board of
15 commissioners of a county that is a member of a multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary
16 Team may choose to withdraw from the multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary Team upon
17 prior written notice to the boards of county commissioners of all other counties that are members
18 of the multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary Team.
19 (c) Mandatory Members. – Each single county Case Review Multidisciplinary Team
20 shall consist of the following persons identified in subdivisions (1) through (11) of this
21 subsection, and each multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary Team shall consist of the
22 following persons identified in subdivisions (1) through (11) of this subsection from each county
23 represented on that multicounty team:
24 (1) The director of social services.
25 (2) A staff member of the local department of social services, designated by the
26 director of social services.
27 (3) A local law enforcement officer, appointed by the board of county
28 commissioners.
29 (4) An attorney from the district attorney's office, appointed by the district
30 attorney.
31 (5) The public guardian appointed in the county by the clerk of superior court
32 under Article 11 of Chapter 35A of the General Statutes.
33 (6) A local mental health professional, appointed by the director of the area
34 authority established under Chapter 122C of the General Statutes.
35 (7) The long-term care ombudsman serving in the county, to be appointed by the
36 Office of the State Long-Term Care Ombudsman.
37 (8) The director of the local department of public health or a member of the
38 director's staff, as designated by the director.
39 (9) A local health care provider, appointed by the local board of health.
40 (10) A representative from the local area agency on aging, appointed by the
41 director of the area agency on aging serving the county.
42 (11) A victim advocate from a local domestic violence or sexual assault agency,
43 appointed by the agency's executive director.
44 (d) Members Appointed by the Chair. – The chair of the Case Review Multidisciplinary
45 Team may appoint a maximum of five additional members representing local agencies or the
46 community at large to serve on any Case Review Multidisciplinary Team, including, but not
47 limited to, representatives from financial institutions, local hospitals, and nonprofit
48 organizations.
49 (e) Vacancies. – Vacancies on a Case Review Multidisciplinary Team shall be filled by
50 the original appointing authority.

1 (f) Chair. – Each Case Review Multidisciplinary Team shall elect a member to serve as
2 chair at the pleasure of the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team. Within three months after being
3 elected as chair, the chair shall participate in the appropriate training developed under
4 G.S. 108A-118.5.
5 (g) Meetings. – Each Case Review Multidisciplinary Team shall meet at least four times
6 each year. The director of the local department of social services shall call the first meeting of
7 the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team. Thereafter, the chair of each Case Review
8 Multidisciplinary Team shall schedule the time and place of meetings, in consultation with the
9 director of the local department of social services, and shall prepare the agenda. The chair shall
10 schedule meetings no less often than once per quarter and often enough to allow adequate review
11 of the cases selected for review. The Case Review Multidisciplinary Team may conduct meetings
12 despite vacancies, absences, or failure to appoint a member.
13 "§ 108A-118.3. Case Review Multidisciplinary Team; powers and duties.
14 Each Case Review Multidisciplinary Team established pursuant to this Article has the
15 following powers and duties:
16 (1) To review, in accordance with the written operating procedures established by
17 the director of the local department of social services under G.S. 108A-118.4,
18 selected active cases in which disabled adults are being served by adult
19 protective services through the local department of social services.
20 (2) To submit annually to the board of county commissioners recommendations,
21 if any, for addressing systemic problems and service gaps that may exist in
22 the delivery of services to disabled adults by public and nonpublic agencies.
23 "§ 108A-118.4. Case Review Multidisciplinary Team; duties of the director of the local
24 department of social services.
25 (a) In addition to any other duties as a member of the Case Review Multidisciplinary
26 Team, and in connection with the reviews conducted under G.S. 108A-118.3(1), the director of
27 the local department of social services has the following powers and duties:
28 (1) To assure the development of written operating procedures in connection with
29 these reviews, including frequency of meetings, confidentiality policies,
30 training of members, and duties and responsibilities of members.
31 (2) To assure that the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team defines the categories
32 of cases that are subject to its review.
33 (3) To bring for review active cases selected by the director of the local
34 department of social services or requested by a member of the Case Review
35 Multidisciplinary Team in which a disabled adult is being served by adult
36 protective services.
37 (4) To provide staff support for these reviews.
38 (5) To maintain records, including minutes of all official meetings, lists of
39 participants for each meeting of the team, and signed confidentiality
40 statements required under G.S. 108A-118.7, in compliance with applicable
41 State laws and rules.
42 (6) To report annually to the local board of social services, or as otherwise
43 required by the board, on the activities of the Case Review Multidisciplinary
44 Team.
45 (b) For a multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary Team, the director of each local
46 department of social services represented in the multicounty team has the powers and duties
47 enumerated in subsection (a) of this section.
48 "§ 108A-118.5. Case Review Multidisciplinary Team; DHHS responsibility for training
49 team members.

1 The Department of Health and Human Services shall develop and make available for the
2 members of each Case Review Multidisciplinary Team, on an ongoing basis, training materials
3 that address all of the following:
4 (1) An overview of adult protective services law and policy.
5 (2) The role and function of a Case Review Multidisciplinary Team.
6 (3) Case Review Multidisciplinary Team confidentiality requirements.
7 (4) Case Review Multidisciplinary Team record-keeping requirements.
8 "§ 108A-118.6. Case Review Multidisciplinary Team; information sharing.
9 (a) Except as prohibited by federal law and notwithstanding any other State law to the
10 contrary, any member of a Case Review Multidisciplinary Team may disclose to other members
11 of the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team any information in the member's possession that is
12 relevant to any of the following:
13 (1) Performing reviews of selected active cases in which disabled adults are being
14 served by adult protective services through local departments of social
15 services.
16 (2) Providing, arranging, or coordinating services on behalf of disabled adults
17 whose cases have been or are currently under review by the Case Review
18 Multidisciplinary Team.
19 (b) The local director of social services shall not share any information that discloses the
20 identity of individuals who have reported suspected abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a disabled
21 adult to the local department of social services, except as allowed by State or federal law.
22 (c) Meetings of the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team are not subject to the provisions
23 of Article 33C of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes.
24 (d) The authority of a Case Review Multidisciplinary Team to disclose information
25 pursuant to this section terminates when a case is no longer an active case in which a disabled
26 adult is being served by adult protective services through a local department of social services,
27 including if the inactive status of the case is due to the disabled adult's failure to give consent to
28 receive protective services or due to the disabled adult's withdrawal of consent to receive
29 protective services.
30 "§ 108A-118.7. Case Review Multidisciplinary Team; confidentiality of records and
31 information; rules.
32 (a) All information and records acquired or generated by a Case Review
33 Multidisciplinary Team during its existence and in the exercise of its duties are confidential and
34 may be disclosed only as necessary to carry out the purposes of the Case Review
35 Multidisciplinary Team as described in this Article or as otherwise permitted by State or federal
36 law. This subsection does not preclude a court of competent jurisdiction from compelling the
37 disclosure of any information or records acquired or generated by a Case Review
38 Multidisciplinary Team if, in the court's opinion, disclosure is necessary to a proper
39 administration of justice and disclosure is not otherwise prohibited by State or federal law.
40 (b) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section, information generated in a meeting of
41 a Case Review Multidisciplinary Team may be disclosed as provided in this subsection in any of
42 the following circumstances:
43 (1) To a local board of county commissioners when the Case Review
44 Multidisciplinary Team makes its annual recommendations, if any, pursuant
45 to G.S. 108A-118.3(2); provided that such recommendations shall not identify
46 or reveal any confidential information about individual adult protective
47 services cases reviewed by the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team.
48 (2) To a local board of social services by the director of the local department of
49 social services when reporting on the activities of the Case Review
50 Multidisciplinary Team; provided that such reports shall not identify or reveal

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 any confidential information about individual adult protective services cases
2 reviewed by the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team.
3 (3) By a member of the Case Review Multidisciplinary Team to the member's
4 agency or organization as needed to provide or arrange services for a disabled
5 adult.
6 (c) Each member of a Case Review Multidisciplinary Team is required to sign a
7 statement indicating an understanding of and adherence to the confidentiality requirements of
8 this section, including the consequences of any breach of confidentiality. The chair shall submit
9 a recommendation, in writing, to the appropriate appointing authority for the removal of any
10 member who fails to (i) sign the statement required by this subsection or (ii) comply with the
11 confidentiality requirements of this section.
12 (d) The local department of social services shall make an entry in the disabled adult's
13 protective services record at the time it is selected for review by a Case Review Multidisciplinary
14 Team to indicate that it was selected for review. Additional entry into the record shall be at the
15 discretion of the director of the local department of social services.
16 (e) Nothing in this Article shall be construed to prohibit a Case Review Multidisciplinary
17 Team from sharing or accessing confidential information and records as permitted or required by
18 applicable State or federal law, including any law that permits the disclosure of confidential
19 information pursuant to the informed consent of a disabled adult or the authorized representative
20 of a disabled adult.
21 (f) The Social Services Commission may adopt rules consistent with this Article to
22 establish protocols for reviews conducted by Case Review Multidisciplinary Teams.
23 "§ 108A-118.8. Systemic Review Multidisciplinary Team allowed but not covered by this
24 Article.
25 Nothing in this Article shall be construed to prohibit a county from establishing both a Case
26 Review Multidisciplinary Team and a Systemic Review Multidisciplinary Team. However, this
27 Article applies only to a single or multicounty Case Review Multidisciplinary Team established
28 pursuant to this Article."
29 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025.
