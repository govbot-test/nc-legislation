GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA

SENATE BILL 969
Short Title: First Grade Reading Promise. (Public)
Sponsors: Senator Hanig (Primary Sponsor).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
May 4, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS FOR HIGH-DOSAGE TUTORING BASED ON THE
3 SCIENCE OF READING, TO CLARIFY THAT SCHOOLS MAY USE VIRTUAL
4 TUTORING PROGRAMS, AND TO REQUIRE READ TO ACHIEVE PUBLIC
5 ACCOUNTABILITY REPORTING.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
8 Public Instruction the sum of five million dollars ($5,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the
9 2026-2027 fiscal year to provide first grade students with access to in-person and virtual
10 high-dosage early literacy tutoring that is based on the science of reading.
11 SECTION 2. G.S. 115C-83.6(c) reads as rewritten:
12 "(c) Local school administrative units are encouraged to partner with community
13 organizations, businesses, and other groups to provide volunteers, mentors, or tutors to assist
14 with the provision of literacy interventions that enhance reading development and proficiency.
15 Tutoring may be provided virtually."
16 SECTION 3. G.S. 115C-83.10 reads as rewritten:
17 "§ 115C-83.10. Accountability measures.
18 (a) Each local board of education shall publish annually on a website maintained by that
19 local school administrative unit and report in writing to the State Board of Education by
20 September 1 of each year the following information on the prior school year:year, disaggregated
21 by school for each grade level in which there are at least 30 students:
22 (1) The number and percentage of third grade students demonstrating and not
23 demonstrating reading proficiency on the State-approved standardized test of
24 reading comprehension administered to third grade students.
25 (2) The number and percentage of third grade students who take and pass an
26 alternative assessment of reading comprehension and the name of each
27 alternative assessment used for this purpose with the number of students who
28 passed it.
29 (3) The number and percentage of third grade students retained for not
30 demonstrating reading proficiency.
31 (4) The number and percentage of third grade students exempt from mandatory
32 third grade retention by category of exemption as listed in G.S. 115C-83.7(b).
33 (5) The number and percentage of first grade students demonstrating and not
34 demonstrating reading comprehension at grade level.
35 (6) The number and percentage of second grade students demonstrating and not
36 demonstrating reading comprehension at grade level.
