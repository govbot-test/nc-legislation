Section 1:
H.B. 866
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 9, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Automatic Ord/Equitable Distribution Claim. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Stevens.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REQUIRE AUTOMATIC ENTRY OF AN ORDER PROHIBITING SPOUSES
3 FROM WASTING, CONVERTING, OR SECRETING ASSETS UPON FILING A CLAIM
4 FOR EQUITABLE DISTRIBUTION.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.1. Article 1 of Chapter 50 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
7 a new section to read:
8 "§ 50-23. Automatic order for equitable distribution claims.
9 (a) As used in this Article, "automatic order" is the standing order entered by the court to
10 prevent the waste, dissipation, or secreting of assets or increasing debt or other liabilities and
11 which is entered at the time an equitable distribution claim is filed in an action. The court must
12 enter the form order created by the Administrative Office of the Courts for the automatic order.
13 (b) The automatic order must contain the following provisions:
14 (1) Neither party shall sell, transfer, encumber, conceal, assign, remove,
15 withdraw, or in any way dispose of, without the consent of the other party in
16 writing, or by order of the court, any property, including real estate, personal
17 property, cash, accounts, stocks, mutual funds, bank accounts, loyalty points
18 or miles, cars, and boats whether individually or jointly titled, except in the
19 usual course of business, for customary and usual household expenses, or for
20 reasonable attorneys' fees in connection with any claim filed under this
21 Chapter.
22 (2) Neither party shall sell, transfer, encumber, assign, remove, withdraw, or in
23 any way dispose of any tax-deferred funds, stocks, or other assets held in any
24 individual retirement accounts, 401K accounts, profit sharing plans, Keogh
25 accounts, or any other pension or retirement account, and the parties shall
26 further refrain from applying for or requesting the payment of retirement
27 benefits or annuity payments of any kind, without the consent of the other
28 party in writing, or upon further order of the court; except that any party who
29 is already in pay status may continue to receive such payments thereunder.
30 (3) Neither party shall sell, transfer, encumber, assign, remove, withdraw, or in
31 any way dispose of any property owned by or for the benefit of the children,
32 including any 529 plan accounts, real estate, personal property, cash, accounts,
33 stocks, mutual funds, bank accounts, loyalty points or miles, and cars whether
34 individually or jointly titled, except for customary and usual expenses
35 historically paid for the benefit of the children from those assets without the
36 consent of the other party in writing or by order of the court.
*DRH30351-CI-2*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (4) Neither party shall incur unreasonable debts hereafter, including further
2 borrowing against any credit line secured by the family residence, further
3 encumbering any assets, or unreasonably using credit cards or cash advances
4 against credit cards, except in the usual course of business or for customary
5 and usual household expenses, or for reasonable attorneys' fees in connection
6 with any claim filed under this Chapter.
7 (5) Neither party shall remove the other party as beneficiary, co-owner, cosigner,
8 or authorized user of any account in subdivisions (1) through (4) of this
9 subsection nor shall they add a nonparty to any account as a beneficiary,
10 co-owner, cosigner, or authorized user of any account in subdivisions (1)
11 through (4) of this subsection without the consent of the other party in writing,
12 or by order of the court.
13 (6) Neither party shall cause the other party or the children to be removed from
14 any existing medical, hospital, and dental insurance coverage, without the
15 consent of the other party in writing, or by order of the court. Each party shall
16 maintain the existing medical, hospital, and dental insurance coverage in full
17 force and effect until the parties agree in writing or the court orders otherwise.
18 (7) Neither party shall change the beneficiaries of any existing life insurance
19 policy without the consent of the other party in writing, or by order of the
20 court. Each party shall maintain the existing life insurance, automobile
21 insurance, homeowner's insurance, and renter's insurance in full force and
22 effect until the parties agree in writing or the court orders otherwise.
23 (8) This order is entered without prejudice to either party.
24 (c) The plaintiff shall serve the automatic order with the summons and complaint when
25 there is a newly filed equitable distribution claim as required by Rule 4 of the North Carolina
26 Rules of Civil Procedure. If the equitable distribution claim is filed in an existing action, the
27 automatic order shall be served in a manner provided for in Rule 5 of the North Carolina Rules
28 of Civil Procedure for service and return of process.
29 (d) The automatic order shall be immediately binding upon the party who filed the initial
30 equitable distribution claim and upon the other party once served with the automatic order.
31 (e) The automatic order shall remain in full force and effect until a final order is entered
32 regarding the equitable distribution claim, unless terminated, modified, or amended by the court
33 upon motion of either party or upon entry of a consent order by the parties.
34 (f) Entry of the automatic order shall not limit or restrict other remedies or protections
35 available to the parties."
36 SECTION 1.2. The Administrative Office of the Courts shall develop a form order
37 for the automatic order which must contain the following information:
38 (1) The case caption.
39 (2) A finding of fact that a claim for equitable distribution claim has been filed in
40 the action.
41 (3) A decretal section containing the required provisions listed in G.S. 50-23(b),
42 as enacted by this act.
43 The Administrative Office of the Courts shall have this form order finalized and
44 available on its website for use by October 1, 2025.
45 SECTION 1.3. Sections 1.2 and 1.3 of this act are effective when they become law.
46 SECTION 2. G.S. 50-20(c) reads as rewritten:
47 "(c) There shall be an equal division by using net value of marital property and net value
48 of divisible property unless the court determines that an equal division is not equitable. If the
49 court determines that an equal division is not equitable, the court shall divide the marital property
50 and divisible property equitably. The court shall consider all of the following factors under this
51 subsection:
Page 2 DRH30351-CI-2
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (1) The income, property, and liabilities of each party at the time the division of
2 property is to become effective.
3 (2) Any obligation for support arising out of a prior marriage.
4 (3) The duration of the marriage and the age and physical and mental health of
5 both parties.
6 (4) The need of a parent with custody of a child or children of the marriage to
7 occupy or own the marital residence and to use or own its household effects.
8 (5) The expectation of pension, retirement, or other deferred compensation rights
9 that are not marital property.
10 (6) Any equitable claim to, interest in, or direct or indirect contribution made to
11 the acquisition of such marital property by the party not having title, including
12 joint efforts or expenditures and contributions and services, or lack thereof, as
13 a spouse, parent, wage earner or homemaker.
14 (7) Any direct or indirect contribution made by one spouse to help educate or
15 develop the career potential of the other spouse.
16 (8) Any direct contribution to an increase in value of separate property which
17 occurs during the course of the marriage.
18 (9) The liquid or nonliquid character of all marital property and divisible property.
19 (10) The difficulty of evaluating any component asset or any interest in a business,
20 corporation or profession, and the economic desirability of retaining such
21 asset or interest, intact and free from any claim or interference by the other
22 party.
23 (11) The tax consequences to each party, including those federal and State tax
24 consequences that would have been incurred if the marital and divisible
25 property had been sold or liquidated on the date of valuation. The trial court
26 may, however, in its discretion, consider whether or when such tax
27 consequences are reasonably likely to occur in determining the equitable value
28 deemed appropriate for this factor.
29 (11a) Acts of either party to maintain, preserve, develop, or expand; or to waste,
30 neglect, devalue or convert the marital property or divisible property, or both,
31 during the period after separation of the parties and before the time of
32 distribution.
33 (11b) In the event of the death of either party prior to the entry of any order for the
34 distribution of property made pursuant to this subsection:
35 a. Property passing to the surviving spouse by will or through intestacy
36 due to the death of a spouse.
37 b. Property held as tenants by the entirety or as joint tenants with rights
38 of survivorship passing to the surviving spouse due to the death of a
39 spouse.
40 c. Property passing to the surviving spouse from life insurance,
41 individual retirement accounts, pension or profit-sharing plans, any
42 private or governmental retirement plan or annuity of which the
43 decedent controlled the designation of beneficiary (excluding any
44 benefits under the federal social security system), or any other
45 retirement accounts or contracts, due to the death of a spouse.
46 d. The surviving spouse's right to claim an "elective share" pursuant to
47 G.S. 30-3.1 through G.S. 30-33, unless otherwise waived.
48 (11c) Whether a party was held in contempt of court for violation of the automatic
49 order entered pursuant to G.S. 50-23.
50 (12) Any other factor which the court finds to be just and proper."
51 SECTION 3. G.S. 50-21 reads as rewritten:
DRH30351-CI-2 Page 3
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 "§ 50-21. Procedures in actions for equitable distribution of property; sanctions for
2 purposeful and prejudicial delay.
3 (a) At any time after a husband and wife begin to live separate and apart from each other,
4 a claim for equitable distribution may be filed and adjudicated, either as a separate civil action,
5 or together with any other action brought pursuant to Chapter 50 of the General Statutes, or as a
6 motion in the cause as provided by G.S. 50-11(e) or (f). Upon filing the initial claim for equitable
7 distribution as provided for in this section, the automatic order shall be entered and served as
8 provided for in G.S. 50-23. Within 90 days after service of a claim for equitable distribution, the
9 party who first asserts the claim shall prepare and serve upon the opposing party an equitable
10 distribution inventory affidavit listing all property claimed by the party to be marital property
11 and all property claimed by the party to be separate property, and the estimated date-of-separation
12 fair market value of each item of marital and separate property. Within 30 days after service of
13 the inventory affidavit, the party upon whom service is made shall prepare and serve an inventory
14 affidavit upon the other party. The inventory affidavits prepared and served pursuant to this
15 subsection shall be subject to amendment and shall not be binding at trial as to completeness or
16 value. The court may extend the time limits in this subsection for good cause shown. The
17 affidavits are subject to the requirements of G.S. 1A-1, Rule 11, and are deemed to be in the
18 nature of answers to interrogatories propounded to the parties. Any party failing to supply the
19 information required by this subsection in the affidavit is subject to G.S. 1A-1, Rules 26, 33, and
20 37. During the pendency of the action for equitable distribution, discovery may proceed, and the
21 court shall enter temporary additional orders as appropriate and necessary for the purpose of
22 preventing the disappearance, waste, or destruction of marital or separate property or to secure
23 the possession thereof.
24 Real or personal property located outside of North Carolina is subject to equitable distribution
25 in accordance with the provisions of G.S. 50-20, and the court may include in its order
26 appropriate provisions to ensure compliance with the order of equitable distribution.
27 …."
28 SECTION 4. Section 4 is effective when it becomes law. Except as otherwise
29 provided, this act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to actions or claims filed on or
30 after that date.
