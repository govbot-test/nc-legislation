2 AN ACT TO REQUIRE AUTOMATIC ENTRY OF AN ORDER PROHIBITING SPOUSES

4 FOR EQUITABLE DISTRIBUTION.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.1. Article 1 of Chapter 50 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
7 a new section to read:
8 "§ 50-23. Automatic order for equitable distribution claims.
9 (a) As used in this Article, "automatic order" is the standing order entered by the court to
10 prevent the waste, dissipation, or secreting of assets or increasing debt or other liabilities and
11 which is entered at the time an equitable distribution claim is filed in an action. The court must
12 enter the form order created by the Administrative Office of the Courts for the automatic order.
13 (b) The automatic order must contain the following provisions:
14 (1) Neither party shall sell, transfer, encumber, conceal, assign, remove,
15 withdraw, or in any way dispose of, without the consent of the other party in
16 writing, or by order of the court, any property, including real estate, personal
17 property, cash, accounts, stocks, mutual funds, bank accounts, loyalty points
18 or miles, cars, and boats whether individually or jointly titled, except in the
19 usual course of business, for customary and usual household expenses, or for
20 reasonable attorneys' fees in connection with any claim filed under this
21 Chapter.
22 (2) Neither party shall sell, transfer, encumber, assign, remove, withdraw, or in
23 any way dispose of any tax-deferred funds, stocks, or other assets held in any
24 individual retirement accounts, 401K accounts, profit sharing plans, Keogh
25 accounts, or any other pension or retirement account, and the parties shall
26 further refrain from applying for or requesting the payment of retirement
27 benefits or annuity payments of any kind, without the consent of the other
28 party in writing, or upon further order of the court; except that any party who
29 is already in pay status may continue to receive such payments thereunder.
30 (3) Neither party shall sell, transfer, encumber, assign, remove, withdraw, or in
31 any way dispose of any property owned by or for the benefit of the children,
32 including any 529 plan accounts, real estate, personal property, cash, accounts,
33 stocks, mutual funds, bank accounts, loyalty points or miles, and cars whether
34 individually or jointly titled, except for customary and usual expenses
35 historically paid for the benefit of the children from those assets without the
36 consent of the other party in writing or by order of the court.

26 Rules of Civil Procedure. If the equitable distribution claim is filed in an existing action, the
27 automatic order shall be served in a manner provided for in Rule 5 of the North Carolina Rules
28 of Civil Procedure for service and return of process.
29 (d) The automatic order shall be immediately binding upon the party who filed the initial
30 equitable distribution claim and upon the other party once served with the automatic order.
31 (e) The automatic order shall remain in full force and effect until a final order is entered
32 regarding the equitable distribution claim, unless terminated, modified, or amended by the court
33 upon motion of either party or upon entry of a consent order by the parties.
34 (f) Entry of the automatic order shall not replace, limit, or restrict other remedies or
35 protections available to the parties, including filing a lis pendens or obtaining an injunction under
36 Rule 65 of the North Carolina Rules of Civil Procedure."
37 SECTION 1.2. The Administrative Office of the Courts shall develop a form order
38 for the automatic order which must contain the following information:
39 (1) The case caption.
40 (2) A finding of fact that a claim for equitable distribution claim has been filed in
41 the action.
42 (3) A decretal section containing the required provisions listed in G.S. 50-23(b),
43 as enacted by this act.
44 The Administrative Office of the Courts shall have this form order finalized and
45 available on its website for use by October 1, 2025.
46 SECTION 1.3. Section 1.1 of this act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies
47 to actions or claims filed on or after that date.

4 SECTION 3.1. G.S. 50-21 reads as rewritten:
5 "§ 50-21. Procedures in actions for equitable distribution of property; sanctions for
6 purposeful and prejudicial delay.
7 (a) At any time after a husband and wife begin to live separate and apart from each other,
8 a claim for equitable distribution may be filed and adjudicated, either as a separate civil action,
9 or together with any other action brought pursuant to Chapter 50 of the General Statutes, or as a
10 motion in the cause as provided by G.S. 50-11(e) or (f). Upon filing the initial claim for equitable
11 distribution as provided for in this section, the automatic order shall be entered and served as
12 provided for in G.S. 50-23. Within 90 days after service of a claim for equitable distribution, the
13 party who first asserts the claim shall prepare and serve upon the opposing party an equitable
14 distribution inventory affidavit listing all property claimed by the party to be marital property
15 and all property claimed by the party to be separate property, and the estimated date-of-separation
16 fair market value of each item of marital and separate property. Within 30 days after service of
17 the inventory affidavit, the party upon whom service is made shall prepare and serve an inventory
18 affidavit upon the other party. The inventory affidavits prepared and served pursuant to this
19 subsection shall be subject to amendment and shall not be binding at trial as to completeness or
20 value. The court may extend the time limits in this subsection for good cause shown. The
21 affidavits are subject to the requirements of G.S. 1A-1, Rule 11, and are deemed to be in the
22 nature of answers to interrogatories propounded to the parties. Any party failing to supply the
23 information required by this subsection in the affidavit is subject to G.S. 1A-1, Rules 26, 33, and
24 37. During the pendency of the action for equitable distribution, discovery may proceed, and the
25 court shall enter temporary additional orders as appropriate and necessary for the purpose of
26 preventing the disappearance, waste, or destruction of marital or separate property or to secure
27 the possession thereof.
28 Real or personal property located outside of North Carolina is subject to equitable distribution
29 in accordance with the provisions of G.S. 50-20, and the court may include in its order
30 appropriate provisions to ensure compliance with the order of equitable distribution.
31 …."
