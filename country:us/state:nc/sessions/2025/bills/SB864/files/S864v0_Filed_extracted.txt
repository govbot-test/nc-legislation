13 PART I. MODIFY COMPUTER SCIENCE STANDARDS
14 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 115C-81.90 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
15 "(a1) Instruction on Artificial Intelligence Literacy. – The State Board shall adopt
16 age-appropriate standards for instruction on artificial intelligence (AI) literacy for grades
17 kindergarten through 12. All courses offered pursuant to this section shall include instruction on
18 AI literacy aligned with the standards adopted by the State Board. Notwithstanding the regular
19 review of content standards required by G.S. 115C-12(9c)c., the State Board shall review and
20 update these standards every two years to keep up with advancements in AI. The standards shall
21 include at least the following:
22 (1) Responsible and ethical use of AI.
23 (2) Limitations of AI tools.
24 (3) Evaluation and verification of outputs provided by AI tools.
25 (4) Data and privacy concerns related to AI tools.
26 (5) Best practices and safety when interacting with AI or AI chatbots."
27 SECTION 1.(b) Notwithstanding the regular review of content standards pursuant
28 to G.S. 115C-12(9c)c., the State Board of Education shall revise the standard course of study for
29 computer science for grades kindergarten through 12 to include artificial intelligence literacy in
30 accordance with G.S. 115C-81.90(a1), as enacted by this section. The State Board shall adopt the
31 revised standards for implementation beginning with the 2028-2029 school year.
32 SECTION 1.(c) The State Board of Education, in consultation with the Department
33 of Public Instruction, shall update the lists of approved courses required by G.S. 115C-81.90(b)
34 and (c) to reflect course alignment with the revised computer science standards adopted pursuant
35 to subsection (b) of this section. The State Board shall update the lists for use beginning with the
36 2028-2029 school year.

10 PART II. PUBLIC SCHOOL UNIT AI POLICIES
11 SECTION 2.(a) Part 3A of Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
12 amended by adding a new section to read:
13 "§ 115C-102.13. Artificial intelligence policies.
14 The Department of Public Instruction shall develop a model artificial intelligence (AI) policy
15 to serve as guidance to public school units when developing their AI policies. At a minimum, the
16 model policy shall include the following:
17 (1) A definition of AI, generative AI, and AI tools.
18 (2) Guidance on how to develop the AI literacy of students and school staff. AI
19 literacy shall include education about nonconsensual intimate imagery.
20 (3) Data privacy and security measures to protect students and staff, including
21 protecting personally identifiable information, when using AI.
22 (4) Standards of ethical and acceptable use of AI and AI chatbots in an
23 educational setting, including standards of academic integrity when using AI."

5 PART III. AI TOOL EVALUATION FRAMEWORK
6 SECTION 3.(a) Part 3A of Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
7 amended by adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 115C-102.14. Department responsibilities regarding artificial intelligence tools.
9 (a) The Department of Public Instruction shall establish and maintain an evaluation
10 framework that provides criteria and guiding considerations for evaluating generative artificial
11 intelligence-powered educational tools (AI tools). The Department shall review and update the
12 framework at least every two years and update the criteria to reflect changes in technology,
13 evidence, or educational practice. The framework shall address at least the following:
14 (1) Student data privacy, security, and transparency.
15 (2) Alignment with the standard course of study.
16 (3) Accessibility for all students.
17 (b) The Department shall maintain a publicly available list of AI tools that have been
18 reviewed under the framework established in accordance with subsection (a) of this section.
19 (c) The Department shall establish procurement guidance, qualified vendor lists, and
20 other mechanisms to support and incentivize the adoption of AI tools that have been reviewed
21 under the framework established in accordance with subsection (a) of this section.
22 (d) The Department shall maintain a publicly available list of all AI tools being used in
23 public school units."
24 SECTION 3.(b) This section is effective when it becomes law.

26 PART IV. EDUCATOR AND ADMINISTRATOR TRAINING ON THE USE OF AI
27 SECTION 4.(a) The Department of Public Instruction shall partner with the Friday
28 Institute for Educational Innovation at North Carolina State University (Friday Institute) to
29 design, produce, and support implementation of a suite of tool-agnostic online training modules
30 and related training resources. Modules and resources shall address at least the following:
31 (1) AI fundamentals for educators, including the following:
32 a. What AI is and is not.
33 b. The basics of generative AI.
34 c. Strengths and limitations of AI as a learning tool.
35 d. AI "hallucinations," or when AI tools reference information that is
36 inaccurate or nonexistent.
37 (2) Responsible instructional use of AI, such as the following:
38 a. Lesson planning.
39 b. Differentiated instruction.
40 c. Student feedback.
41 d. Productivity workflows and how AI can improve productivity.
42 e. Appropriate boundaries for AI usage.
43 (3) Verification and quality control, including the following:
44 a. Fact-checking information provided by an AI program.
45 b. Evaluation of sources provided by an AI program.
46 c. Documenting prompts and outputs from AI programs.
47 d. Educator accountability for decisions based on input from an AI
48 program.
49 (4) Academic integrity and assessment redesign to account for greater student
50 access to AI tools.
51 (5) Data privacy and security regarding AI tools.
