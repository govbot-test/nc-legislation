Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Chiropractic Mods. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Hise, Sawrey, and Burgin (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 25, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MAKE VARIOUS CHANGES TO THE LAWS GOVERNING THE PRACTICE
3 OF CHIROPRACTIC.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 90-139(a) reads as rewritten:
6 "(a) The North Carolina State Board of Chiropractic Examiners is created to consist of
7 eight members appointed by the Governor and General Assembly. Six of the members shall be
8 practicing doctors of chiropractic, who are residents of this State and who have actively practiced
9 chiropractic in the State for at least eight consecutive years immediately preceding their
10 appointments; four of these six members shall be appointed by the Governor, and two by the
11 General Assembly in accordance with G.S. 120-121, one each upon the recommendation of the
12 President Pro Tempore of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives. No more
13 than three members of the Board may be graduates of the same college college, program, or
14 school of chiropractic. The other two members shall be persons chosen by the Governor to
15 represent the public at large. The public members shall not be health care providers nor the
16 spouses of health care providers. For purposes of Board membership, "health care provider"
17 means any licensed health care professional and any agent or employee of any health care
18 institution, health care insurer, health care professional school, or a member of any allied health
19 profession. For purposes of this section, a person enrolled in a program to prepare him to be a
20 licensed health care professional or an allied health professional shall be deemed a health care
21 provider. For purposes of this section, any person with significant financial interest in a health
22 service or profession is not a public member."
23 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 90-142 reads as rewritten:
24 "§ 90-142. Powers; duties.
25 The North Carolina State Board of Chiropractic Examiners shall have the following powers
26 and duties:
27 …
28 (13) Establish certification standards for the practice of acupuncture by doctors of
29 chiropractic."
30 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 90-142.1 reads as rewritten:
31 "§ 90-142.1. Supervised training programs authorized.
32 (a) As used in this section, "preceptorship program" means a clinical program of an
33 approved chiropractic college program in which a student of chiropractic, under the direct
34 supervision of a licensed chiropractor, observes the licensed chiropractor and may perform
35 chiropractic services as defined in G.S. 90-143(a) and subject to G.S. 90-151. For the purposes
*S405-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 of this section, "direct supervision" means that the supervising licensed chiropractor must be
2 within the immediate patient treatment area and available to the student at all times.
3 (b) Each student enrolled in a chiropractic college program that meets the accreditation
4 requirements of G.S. 90-143 may participate in a preceptorship program."
5 SECTION 1.(d) G.S. 90-143 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 90-143. Definitions of chiropractic; examinations; educational requirements.
7 (a) "Chiropractic" is herein defined to be the science of adjusting the cause of disease by
8 realigning the spine, releasing pressure on nerves radiating from the spine to all parts of the body,
9 and allowing the nerves to carry their full quota of health current (nerve energy) from the brain
10 to all parts of the body.
11 (b) It shall be the duty of the North Carolina State Board of Chiropractic Examiners
12 (hereinafter referred to as "Board") to examine for licensure to practice chiropractic in this State
13 any applicant who is or will become, within 60 days of examination, a graduate of a four-year an
14 accredited chiropractic college program that is either accredited by the Council on Chiropractic
15 Education or deemed by the Board to be the equivalent of such a college program and who
16 furnishes to the Board, in the manner prescribed by the Board, all of the following:
17 (1) Satisfactory evidence of good moral character.
18 (2) Proof that the applicant has received a baccalaureate degree from a college or
19 university accredited by a regional accreditation body recognized by the
20 United States Department of Education.Education or its international
21 equivalent.
22 (3) A transcript confirming that the applicant has received at least 4,200 hours of
23 accredited chiropractic education. The Board shall not count any hours earned
24 at an institution that was not accredited by the Council on Chiropractic
25 Education or was not, as determined by the Board, the equivalent of such an
26 institution at the time the hours were earned.a doctor of chiropractic degree
27 from an accredited chiropractic program.
28 The examination shall include the following studies: neurology, chemistry, pathology, anatomy,
29 histology, physiology, embryology, dermatology, diagnosis, microscopy, gynecology, hygiene,
30 eye, ear, nose and throat, orthopody, diagnostic radiology, North Carolina jurisprudence,
31 palpation, nerve tracing, chiropractic philosophy, theory, teaching and practice of chiropractic,
32 jurisprudence and any other related studies as the Board may consider necessary to determine an
33 applicant's fitness to practice. The Board may include as part of the examination any examination
34 developed and administered by the National Board of Chiropractic Examiners or its successor
35 organization that the Board considers appropriate, and the examination may be administered by
36 a national testing service. The Board shall set the passing scores for all parts of the examination.
37 …."
38 SECTION 1.(e) G.S. 90-146 reads as rewritten:
39 "§ 90-146. Graduates from other states.
40 A graduate of a regular an accredited chiropractic school program who comes into this State
41 from another state may be granted a license by the North Carolina State Board of Chiropractic
42 Examiners as required in this Article."
43 SECTION 1.(f) G.S. 90-151 reads as rewritten:
44 "§ 90-151. Extent and limitation of license.
45 Any person obtaining a license from the North Carolina State Board of Chiropractic
46 Examiners shall have the right to practice the science known as chiropractic, in accordance with
47 the method, thought, and practice of chiropractors, as taught in recognized chiropractic schools
48 schools, programs, and colleges, but shall not prescribe for or administer to any person any
49 medicine or drugs, drugs requiring a prescription in accordance with rules and regulations by the
50 United States Food and Drug Administration, nor practice osteopathy or surgery."
51 SECTION 1.(g) G.S. 90-154.3 reads as rewritten:
Page 2 Senate Bill 405-First Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 "§ 90-154.3. Acceptable care in the practice of chiropractic.
2 (a) It shall be unlawful for a doctor of chiropractic to examine, treat, or render any
3 professional service to a patient that does not conform to the standards of acceptable care.
4 (b) For purposes of disciplinary action, the North Carolina State Board of Chiropractic
5 Examiners may adopt rules that establish and define standards of acceptable care with respect to:
6 (1) Examination and diagnosis.
7 (2) The use of chiropractic treatment.
8 (3) Physiological therapeutic agents.
9 (4) Diagnostic radiology.imaging.
10 (5) The maintenance of patient records.
11 (6) Sanitation, safety, and the adequacy of clinical equipment.
12 (c) Repealed by Session Laws 2021-120, s. 1(a), effective October 1, 2021.
13 (d) Nothing in this section shall alter the lawful scope of practice of chiropractic as
14 defined in G.S. 90-143 or the limitation of license as defined in G.S. 90-151."
15 SECTION 2. The North Carolina State Board of Chiropractic Examiners may adopt
16 rules to implement the provisions of this act.
17 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025.
