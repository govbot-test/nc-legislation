13 "§ 18D-100. Findings.
14 The General Assembly finds all of the following:
15 (1) Cannabis prohibition, like alcohol prohibition before it, has been a wasteful
16 and destructive failure. About half of Americans admit to having used
17 cannabis despite more than eight decades of prohibition.
18 (2) Regulating cannabis similarly to alcohol will replace the uncontrolled illicit
19 market with a well-regulated system. Legalization allows regulation and
20 control to protect consumers, workers, communities, and the environment.
21 (3) The prohibition of cannabis has had an unfair, disparate impact on persons
22 and communities of color. A 2020 report by the American Civil Liberties
23 Union found black individuals are three and six-tenths times as likely as white
24 individuals to be arrested for cannabis possession, despite nearly identical use
25 rates.
26 (4) The prohibition of cannabis diverts law enforcement resources from violent
27 and property crimes and subjects civilians to unnecessary police interactions.
28 (5) Keeping cannabis illegal deprives the State of thousands of legal jobs and
29 hundreds of millions of dollars in tax revenue.
30 (6) The use of cannabis should be legal for persons 21 years of age or older and
31 subject to taxation and regulation in a manner that does all of the following:
32 a. Controls the production and distribution of cannabis under a system of
33 licensing, regulation, and taxation.
34 b. Includes lab testing, potency labeling, secure packaging, restrictions
35 on advertising, and education about responsible use and risks.

13 "§ 18D-101. Definitions.
14 Unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply in this Chapter:
15 (1) Cannabis. – All parts of the plant of the genus cannabis, the seeds thereof, the
16 resin extracted from any part of the plant, and every compound, manufacture,
17 salt, derivative, mixture, or preparation of the plant, its seeds, or its resin,
18 including cannabis concentrate. "Cannabis" does not include hemp, nor does
19 it include fiber produced from the stalks, oil, or cake made from the seeds of
20 the plant, or sterilized seed of the plant, which is incapable of germination.
21 (2) Cannabis accessories. – Any equipment, products, or materials of any kind
22 that are used, intended for use, or designed for use in planting, propagating,
23 cultivating, growing, harvesting, composting, manufacturing, compounding,
24 converting, producing, processing, preparing, testing, analyzing, packaging,
25 repackaging, storing, vaporizing, or containing cannabis, or for ingesting,
26 inhaling, or otherwise introducing cannabis into the human body.
27 (3) Cannabis cultivation facility. – An entity registered to cultivate, prepare, and
28 package cannabis, and sell cannabis to other cannabis establishments. A
29 cannabis cultivation facility may not produce cannabis concentrates, tinctures,
30 extracts, or other cannabis products unless it is also licensed as a cannabis
31 product manufacturing facility.
32 (4) Cannabis establishment. – A cannabis cultivation facility, an on-site
33 consumption establishment, a cannabis testing facility, a cannabis product
34 manufacturing facility, a cannabis transporter, or any other type of cannabis
35 business authorized and registered by the Commission.
36 (5) Cannabis product manufacturing facility. – An entity registered to purchase
37 cannabis, manufacture, prepare, and package cannabis products, and sell
38 cannabis and cannabis products to other cannabis establishments but not to
39 consumers.
40 (6) Cannabis products. – Products that are comprised of cannabis, cannabis
41 concentrate, or cannabis extract and other ingredients and are intended for use
42 or consumption, such as, but not limited to, edible products, ointments, and
43 tinctures.
44 (7) Cannabis testing facility. – An entity registered to test cannabis for potency
45 and contaminants.
46 (8) Cannabis transporter. – An entity registered to transport cannabis between
47 cannabis establishments.
48 (9) Commission. – The North Carolina Alcoholic Beverage Control Commission
49 established under G.S. 18B-200.

15 "Article 3.
16 "Registration and Licensure.
17 "§ 18D-300. Registration with the Commission.
18 (a) Each application or renewal application for an annual registration to operate a
19 cannabis establishment shall be submitted to the Commission. A renewal application may be
20 submitted up to 90 days prior to the expiration of the cannabis establishment's registration.
21 (b) The Commission shall begin accepting and processing applications to operate
22 cannabis establishments from community reinvestment applicants one year after the effective
23 date of this Chapter.
24 (c) The Commission may begin accepting and processing applications to operate
25 cannabis establishments from applicants other than community reinvestment applicants no earlier
26 than one year and 180 days after the effective date of this Chapter.
27 (d) Upon receiving an application or renewal application for a cannabis establishment,
28 the Commission shall immediately forward a copy of each application and half of the registration
29 application fee to the local regulatory authority for the locality in which the applicant desires to
30 operate the cannabis establishment, unless the locality has not designated a local regulatory
31 authority.
32 (e) Within 120 days after receiving an application or renewal application, the
33 Commission shall issue an annual registration or a conditional registration to the applicant, unless
34 the Commission finds the applicant is not in compliance with rules enacted by the Commission
35 or the Commission is notified by the relevant locality that the applicant is not in compliance with
36 ordinances and regulations in effect at the time of application.
37 (f) Applicants may apply for conditional approval if they have not purchased or leased
38 the property where their cannabis establishment would be located. If the applicant is otherwise
39 qualified, the Commission shall provide conditional approval. Once the applicant provides the
40 Commission with a completed, supplemental application that includes the premises, the
41 Commission shall forward the information to the local regulatory authority and approve or reject
42 the final application within 45 days from the date of submission.
43 (g) Upon denial of an application, the Commission shall notify the applicant in writing
44 of the specific reason for its denial.
45 (h) Cannabis establishments, and the books and records maintained and created by
46 cannabis establishments, are subject to inspection by the Commission.

11 "§ 18D-501. Restrictions on personal cultivation; penalty.
12 (a) It is unlawful to cultivate cannabis plants in any of the following ways:
13 (1) Cannabis plants may not be cultivated in a location where plants are subject
14 to public view, including view from another private property, without the use
15 of binoculars, aircraft, or other optical aids.
16 (2) A person who cultivates cannabis must take reasonable precautions to ensure
17 the plants are secure from unauthorized access and access by persons under
18 21 years of age. For purposes of illustration and not limitation, cultivating
19 cannabis in an enclosed, locked space that persons under 21 years of age do
20 not possess a key to constitutes reasonable precautions.
21 (3) Cannabis cultivation may only occur on property lawfully in possession of the
22 cultivator or with the consent of the person in lawful possession of the
23 property.
24 (b) A person who violates subsection (a) of this section is guilty of an infraction,
25 punishable by a fine of up to seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00) or up to 75 hours of
26 community service.
27 "§ 18D-502. Public smoking prohibited; penalty.
28 (a) It is unlawful to smoke cannabis in a public place.
29 (b) It is unlawful to smoke cannabis in an area of an on-site consumption establishment
30 where cannabis smoking is prohibited.
31 (c) A person who violates this section is guilty of an infraction, punishable by a fine of
32 up to fifty dollars ($50.00) or up to five hours of community service.
33 "§ 18D-503. Consuming cannabis while operating a moving vehicle prohibited; penalty.
34 (a) No person shall consume cannabis while operating or driving a motor vehicle, boat,
35 vessel, aircraft, or other motorized device used for transportation.
36 (b) Unless the conduct is covered under some other provision of law providing greater
37 punishment, a person who violates subsection (a) of this section, and only consumed cannabis,
38 is guilty of an infraction, punishable as follows:
39 (1) For a first offense, any or all of the following:
40 a. A fine not more than two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00).
41 b. Not more than 25 hours of community service.
42 c. Suspension of the person's drivers license for up to six months.
43 (2) For a second or subsequent offense, any or all of the following:
44 a. A fine not more than five hundred dollars ($500.00).
45 b. Not more than 50 hours of community service.
46 c. Suspension of the person's drivers license for up to one year.
