H.B. 712
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 2, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
HOUSE BILL DRH40397-LR-70A
Short Title: Universal Income/Pay-It-Forward Fund. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Brockman.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE NORTH CAROLINA UNIVERSAL INCOME PROGRAM
3 IN THE DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Article 10 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes is amended by
6 adding a new section to read:
7 "§ 143B-431.4. Universal Income Program established.
8 (a) The NC Universal Income Program (NCUIP) is established in the Department of
9 Commerce. The NCUIP shall incentivize individuals having difficulty finding good permanent
10 employment to volunteer in the community and to receive retraining. The Pay-It-Forward Fund
11 (Fund) is established in the Department of Commerce to support the NCUIP. The Department's
12 development of program activities and funding options shall focus on public-private partnerships,
13 tax incentives, and other methods appropriate to obtain the resources required to fund the NCUIP
14 and to provide valuable volunteer opportunities for the community and employment retraining
15 to participants. The Department of Commerce shall seek funding for the Fund from private
16 sources and from the local, State, and federal levels of government. The Department of
17 Commerce shall adopt rules to implement this section.
18 (b) In consultation with the North Carolina Department of Revenue, the Department of
19 Commerce shall implement a repayment program for NCUIP graduates to pay an extra one
20 percent (1%) in State individual income taxes, with that revenue going into the Pay-It-Forward
21 Fund to provide the universal income to others in the program.
22 (c) By January 1, 2026, the Department of Commerce shall establish and administer the
23 NCUIP to provide a monthly income of up to three thousand dollars ($3,000) per month, for up
24 to five years, to eligible individuals who are receiving job training, performing volunteer work,
25 or both, in accordance with the requirements of the program.
26 (d) By April 1, 2026, and then annually thereafter, the Secretary of the Department of
27 Commerce shall report to the General Assembly on the implementation of this section."
28 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
