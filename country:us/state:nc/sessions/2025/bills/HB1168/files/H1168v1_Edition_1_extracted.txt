Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Limit Electric Util. Hist. Fuel Cost Recovery. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives T. Brown, Reives, Prather, and Pittman (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
May 4, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO RESTORE CERTAIN LIMITATIONS PREVENTING ELECTRIC PUBLIC
3 UTILITIES FROM RECOVERING HISTORIC FUEL COSTS FROM CUSTOMERS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 62-133.2(d) reads as rewritten:
6 "(d) The Commission shall provide for notice of a public hearing with reasonable and
7 adequate time for investigation and for all intervenors to prepare for hearing. At the hearing the
8 Commission shall receive evidence from the utility, the Public Staff, and any intervenor desiring
9 to submit evidence, and from the public generally. In reaching its decision, the Commission shall
10 consider all evidence required under subsection (c) of this section as well as any and all other
11 competent evidence that may assist the Commission in reaching its decision including changes
12 in the cost of fuel consumed and fuel-related costs that occur within a reasonable time, as
13 determined by the Commission, after the test period is closed. The Commission shall incorporate
14 in its cost of fuel and fuel-related costs determination under this subsection the experienced
15 over-recovery or under-recovery of reasonable costs of fuel and fuel-related costs prudently
16 incurred by the electric public utility, during the test period, based upon the prudent standards set
17 pursuant to subsection (d1) of this section, in fixing an increment or decrement rider. The
18 Commission shall not incorporate in this determination any fuel or fuel-related costs incurred
19 prior to the test period. Upon request of the electric public utility, the Commission shall also
20 incorporate in this determination the experienced over-recovery or under-recovery of costs of
21 fuel and fuel-related costs through the date that is 30 calendar days prior to the date of the hearing,
22 provided that the reasonableness and prudence of these costs shall be subject to review in the
23 utility's next annual hearing pursuant to this section. The Commission shall use deferral
24 accounting, and consecutive test periods, in complying with this subsection, and the
25 over-recovery or under-recovery portion of the increment or decrement shall be reflected in rates
26 for 12 months, notwithstanding any changes in the base fuel cost in a general rate case. Any
27 experienced over-recovery or under-recovery of reasonable fuel and fuel-related costs prudently
28 incurred during the test period shall accrue interest at the commercial paper rate as identified by
29 the Federal Reserve for A2/P2 nonfinancial issuers, or reasonable successor thereto, on a
30 weighted average basis over the applicable time period. The burden of proof as to the correctness
31 and reasonableness of the charge and as to whether the cost of fuel and fuel-related costs were
32 reasonably and prudently incurred shall be on the utility. The Commission shall allow only that
33 portion, if any, of a requested cost of fuel and fuel-related costs adjustment that is based on
34 adjusted and reasonable cost of fuel and fuel-related costs prudently incurred under efficient
35 management and economic operations. In evaluating whether cost of fuel and fuel-related costs
*H1168-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 were reasonable and prudently incurred, the Commission shall apply the rule adopted pursuant
2 to subsection (d1) of this section. To the extent that the Commission determines that an increment
3 or decrement to the rates of the utility due to changes in the cost of fuel and fuel-related costs
4 over or under base fuel costs established in the preceding general rate case is just and reasonable,
5 the Commission shall order that the increment or decrement become effective for all sales of
6 electricity and remain in effect until changed in a subsequent general rate case or annual
7 proceeding under this section."
8 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Utilities
9 Commission the sum of ten thousand dollars ($10,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027
10 fiscal year to be used for purposes consistent with this act.
11 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2026, and applies to any rate-making
12 mechanisms filed by an electric public utility on or after that date.
