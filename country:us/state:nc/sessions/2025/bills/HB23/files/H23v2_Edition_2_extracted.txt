11 PART IV. AUTHORIZE ADDING SOUTH FORK PASSAGE STATE TRAIL TO THE
12 STATE PARKS SYSTEM
13 SECTION 4.1. The General Assembly finds:
14 (1) The proposed South Fork Passage Trail corridor offers numerous economic
15 benefits by expanding and enhancing the existing tourism industry. The
16 diverse hydrology of the river in the corridor includes shoals, flatwater, class
17 II rapids, and river tributaries that support a variety of recreational uses.
18 (2) The South Fork Passage Trail corridor is home to Spencer Mountain, the third
19 highest peak in the Charlotte region, and the corridor passes through terrain
20 that is emblematic of the Piedmont region of the State.
21 (3) The South Fork Passage Trail corridor also contains ecologically important
22 habitat, including diverse wetlands, hardwood forests, and grasslands, that
23 support numerous State and federal threatened or endangered species.
24 (4) The creation of a 60-mile hiking and paddling trail connecting Jacob Fork
25 Park in Catawba County through Betty G. Ross Park in Lincoln County to
26 George Poston Park, Goat Island Park, and Daniel Stowe Botanical Garden in
27 Gaston County would expand existing recreational offerings and support
28 ongoing efforts to conserve the South Fork of the Catawba River watershed.
29 SECTION 4.2. The General Assembly authorizes the Department of Natural and
30 Cultural Resources (Department) to add the South Fork Passage Trail in Catawba, Lincoln, and
31 Gaston Counties to the State Parks System as a State trail, as provided in G.S. 143B-135.54(b).
32 The trail, which will run alongside the South Fork River, shall begin at the confluence of the
33 Henry Fork and Jacob Fork at Jacob Fork Park and end at the boundary between the State and
34 South Carolina on Lake Wylie.
35 SECTION 4.3. The Department shall support, promote, encourage, and facilitate the
36 establishment of trail segments on State Park lands and on lands of other federal, State, local, and
37 private landowners. On segments of the South Fork Passage State Trail that cross property
38 controlled by agencies or owners other than the Department's Division of Parks and Recreation,
39 the laws, rules, and policies of those agencies or owners shall govern the use of the property.
40 SECTION 4.4. The requirement of G.S. 143B-135.54(b) that additions be
41 accompanied by adequate appropriations for land acquisition, development, and operations shall
42 not apply to the authorization set forth in this Part; provided, however, that the State may receive
43 donations of appropriate land and may purchase other needed lands for the South Fork Passage
44 State Trail with existing funds in the Land and Water Fund, the Parks and Recreation Trust Fund,
45 the Complete the Trails Fund, the federal Land and Water Conservation Fund, and other available
46 sources of funding.
