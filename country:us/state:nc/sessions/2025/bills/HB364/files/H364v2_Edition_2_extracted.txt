6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 159-171 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 159-171. Grant anticipation notes.
9 (a) A Except as provided in subsection (a1) of this section, a unit of local government is
10 authorized to borrow money for the purpose of paying appropriations made for a capital project
11 in anticipation of the receipt of moneys from grant commitments for such capital project from
12 the State or the United States or any agencies of either, and to issue its negotiable notes in
13 evidence thereof. Grant anticipation notes under this subsection shall mature not later than 12
14 months after the estimated completion date of such capital project as determined by the governing
15 body of the unit of local government and may be renewed from time to time, but no renewal shall
16 mature later than 12 months after the estimated completion date of such capital project.
17 (a1) A unit of local government is authorized to borrow money for the purpose of paying
18 appropriations made for a capital project identified for funding as a State Transportation
19 Improvement (STI) regional impact or division need project under Article 14B of Chapter 136
20 of the General Statutes (STIP project) in anticipation of the receipt of grant funding from the
21 State or the United States or any agencies of either and to issue its negotiable notes in evidence
22 thereof. Grant anticipation notes under this subsection shall mature no later than 12 months after
23 the first day of the calendar year following the calendar year in which the STIP project is
24 scheduled to be completed. Renewal of grant anticipation notes under this subsection is not
25 allowed. Prior to borrowing money under this subsection, the unit of local government shall enter
26 into an expedited project agreement with the Department of Transportation in accordance with
27 G.S. 136-66.8, the terms of which shall include all of the following:
28 (1) A provision requiring approval of the grant anticipation note from the Local
29 Government Commission.
30 (2) Project identification.
31 (3) Cost of project.
32 (4) A provision including, and requiring compliance with, the project plan as set
33 out in the agreement, including the time line, whether the project is in
34 development or delivery period, project phase, current expected completion
35 date, accelerated completion date using funds authorized under this
36 subsection, and a requirement that the project be administered accordingly.

35 (c) Grant anticipation notes issued under this section shall be special obligations of the
36 issuing unit. Neither the credit nor the taxing power of the issuing unit may be pledged for the
37 payment of grant anticipation notes, and no holder of such notes shall have the right to compel
38 the exercise of the taxing power by the issuing unit or the forfeiture of any of its property in
39 connection with any default thereon."
40 SECTION 2. G.S. 136-189.11 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
41 "(f1) Expedited Project Funded by Local Government. – The Department shall ensure any
42 amendment or modification to the State Transportation Improvement Program will not cause a
43 delay in the repayment of a grant anticipation note issued to a local government in accordance
44 with G.S. 159-171(a1) and (b1)."
45 SECTION 3. The Department of Transportation shall develop procedures and adopt
46 rules as necessary for expedited project agreements made under G.S. 136-66.8 that include the
47 terms and use of grant anticipation notes under G.S. 159-171(a1) and (b1), as enacted by Section
48 1 of this act, and the amendment or modification of the State Transportation Improvement
49 Program under Section 2 of this act. The Department of the Treasurer, in consultation with the
50 Department of Transportation, shall adopt rules as necessary that are consistent with the purposes
51 of Section 1 of this act and Article 9 of Chapter 159 of the General Statutes.
