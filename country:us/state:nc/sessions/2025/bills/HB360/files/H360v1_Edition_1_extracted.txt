6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-122 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 14-122. Forgery of deeds, wills and certain other instruments.
9 (a) General Fraud. – If any person, of his that person's own head and imagination, or by
10 false conspiracy or fraud with others, shall wittingly and falsely forge and make, or shall cause
11 or wittingly assent to the forging or making of, or shall show forth in evidence, knowing the same
12 to be forged, any deed, lease or will, or any bond, writing obligatory, bill of exchange, promissory
13 note, endorsement or assignment thereof; or any acquittance or receipt for money or goods; or
14 any receipt or release for any bond, note, bill or any other security for the payment of money; or
15 any order for the payment of money or delivery of goods, with intent, in any of said instances, to
16 defraud any person or corporation, and thereof shall be duly convicted, the person so offending
17 shall be punished as a Class H F felon.
18 (b) Residential Property Fraud. – In an instance where a violation of this section involves
19 the forgery of a deed, lease, will, lien, encumbrance, or other conveyance of an interest in real
20 property relating to residential property, the person so offending shall be punished as a Class D
21 felon. For the purposes of this subsection, the term "residential property" means real property
22 where an individual has resided for, consecutively or nonconsecutively, at least six months of a
23 period of 12 months immediately preceding the forgery."

43 a false, fictitious, or fraudulent instrument as described in this section more

27 SECTION 3. Article 4 of Chapter 47 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
28 a new section to read:
29 "§ 47-108.30. No presumption of priority for certain recorded instruments.
30 A deed or conveyance of an interest in real property shall not have a presumption of priority
31 based upon the time of registration and shall not constitute color of title if any of the following
32 criteria are met:
33 (1) The deed or conveyance does not comply with the provisions of G.S. 47-18,
34 47-20, 47-118, 47-119, 47-119.1, or 47-120, as applicable.
35 (2) The deed or conveyance does not contain a signature and acknowledgement
36 of at least one record owner at the time the conveyance is made."

29 SECTION 5. Article 2 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
30 a new section to read:
31 "§ 161-32. Title certification.
32 (a) Title Certification. – The board of commissioners of a county may, by resolution,
33 require the register of deeds not to accept for registration one or more types of conveyances of
34 real property unless the county tax assessor has certified that the grantors on the instrument are
35 consistent with the listing for the real property in the county tax records or, in the event of an
36 inconsistency, legal verification satisfactory to the county tax assessor has been provided for the
37 source of title, including, but not limited to, deed, will or other inheritance through an estate, or
38 a court order. The county commissioners may describe the form the certification must take in its
39 resolution.
40 (b) Exception to Title Certification. – If a board of county commissioners adopts a
41 resolution pursuant to subsection (a) of this section, notwithstanding the resolution, the register
42 of deeds shall accept without certification a conveyance submitted for registration under the
43 supervision of a closing attorney and containing this statement on the deed: "This instrument
44 prepared by: ________, a licensed North Carolina attorney, based upon satisfactory verification
45 of vesting of record title to the property herein through the source of title referenced herein."
46 (c) As used in this section, the term "conveyance" means a transfer of title to real property
47 by deed, devise, or other similar instrument."
48 SECTION 6. Sections 1 and 2 of this act become effective December 1, 2025, and
49 apply to offenses committed on or after that date. Sections 3 and 4 of this act become effective
50 July 1, 2025, and apply to instruments and documents presented for registration on or after that
51 date. The remainder of this act is effective when it becomes law.
