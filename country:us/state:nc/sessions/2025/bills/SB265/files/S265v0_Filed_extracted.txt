13 "§ 18D-100. Definitions.
14 Unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply in this Article:
15 (1) ALE Division. – As defined in G.S. 18B-101.
16 (2) Batch. – The hemp-derived consumable product produced during a period of
17 time under similar conditions and identified by a specific code that allows
18 traceability.
19 (3) Department. – The Department of Revenue.
20 (4) Distributor. – A person or entity that delivers or sells hemp-derived
21 consumable products for the purpose of distribution in commerce.
22 (5) Exit package. – An opaque bag or other similar opaque covering provided at
23 the point of sale that satisfies the child-resistant effectiveness standards under
24 16 C.F.R. § 1700.15(b)(1) when tested in accordance with the requirements
25 of 16 C.F.R. § 1700.20 in which hemp-derived consumable products are
26 placed by a seller after being sold to the ultimate consumer of the product.
27 (6) Final form product. – A product intended for consumer use and to be sold by
28 a seller.
29 (7) Hemp. – As defined in G.S. 90-87.
30 (8) Hemp-derived cannabinoid. – Any phytocannabinoid found in hemp,
31 including delta-9 tetrahydrocannabinol (delta-9 THC), tetrahydrocannabinolic
32 acid (THCA), cannabidiol (CBD), cannabidiolic acid (CBDA), cannabinol
33 (CBN), cannabigerol (CBG), cannabichromene (CBC), cannabicyclol (CBL),
34 cannabivarin (CBV), tetrahydrocannabivarin (THCV), cannabidivarin
35 (CBDV), cannabicitran (CBT), delta-7 tetrahydrocannabinol (delta-7 THC),
36 delta-8 tetrahydrocannibinol (delta-8 THC), or delta-10 tetrahydrocannibinol

1 (delta-10 THC). This term also includes any synthetic cannabinoid derived
2 from hemp and contained in a hemp-derived consumable product.
3 (9) Hemp-derived consumable product. – A final form hemp product that is
4 intended for human ingestion or inhalation that contains a delta-9 THC

35 engages in the process of manufacturing, preparing, or packaging of
36 hemp-derived consumable products.

41 (19) Seller. – Any person who sells a hemp-derived consumable product to the
42 ultimate consumer of the product, including an online seller.
43 (20) Serving. – A quantity of a hemp-derived consumable product reasonably
44 suitable for a person's use in a single day.
45 "§ 18D-101. Sales restrictions on hemp-derived consumable products.
46 (a) Restrictions. – No person shall do any of the following:
47 (1) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell a hemp-derived consumable
48 product to a person who is under 21 years of age. Any seller that sells a
49 hemp-derived consumable product on an internet website shall verify the age
50 of any prospective purchaser and shall use a method of delivery that requires

1 the signature of a person at least 21 years of age before the hemp-derived
2 consumable product is released.
3 (2) Engage in the business of selling a hemp-derived consumable product without
4 a valid license issued in accordance with this Chapter.
5 (3) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell at retail a hemp-derived
6 consumable product that has a concentration of more than three-tenths of one
7 percent (0.3%) on a dry weight basis of delta-9 tetrahydrocannabinol.
8 (4) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell a hemp-derived consumable
9 product that is not contained in an exit package or a child-resistant package.
10 (5) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell at retail or on an internet website
11 offering delivery in this State, a hemp-derived consumable product that is not
12 in compliance with G.S. 18D-105.
13 (6) Knowingly, or having reason to know, sell at retail hemp flower or a product
14 containing hemp flower that is not accompanied by a certificate of analysis
15 issued within the previous 12-month period demonstrating that the hemp
16 flower or product containing hemp flower has a concentration of no more than
17 three-tenths of one percent (0.3%) on a dry weight basis of delta-9
18 tetrahydrocannabinol.
19 (b) Civil Penalties. – Violation of this section shall have the following penalties:
20 (1) For the first violation, the Department may impose a civil penalty of no more
21 than five hundred dollars ($500.00).
22 (2) For the second violation within three years, the Department may impose a
23 civil penalty of no more than seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00).
24 (3) For the third violation within three years of the first violation, the Department
25 shall impose a civil penalty of no more than one thousand dollars ($1,000) and
26 suspend the seller's license for one year.
27 (4) For a fourth or subsequent violation within three years of the first violation,
28 the Department shall impose a civil penalty of no more than two thousand
29 dollars ($2,000) and revoke the seller's license.
30 (c) Compromise. – In any case in which the Department is entitled to suspend or revoke
31 a seller's license, the Department may accept from the seller an offer in compromise to pay a
32 penalty of not more than three thousand dollars ($3,000). The Department may either accept a
33 compromise or revoke a license, but not both. The Department may accept a compromise and
34 suspend the license in the same case.

36 subsection (b) of this section, for a violation of subdivision (4) of subsection (a) of this section,
37 the seller shall also pay to the Department the actual costs paid by the ALE Division for testing
38 of the samples resulting in the violation. Any fee collected pursuant to this subsection shall be
39 remitted to the ALE Division.

41 section if the seller does any of the following:

44 authorized to issue similar official state special identification cards for that
45 state, a tribal enrollment card issued by a State or federally recognized Indian
46 Tribe, a military identification card, or a passport showing the purchaser's age
47 to be at least the required age for purchase and bearing a physical description
48 of the person named on the card reasonably describing the purchaser.

50 that the purchaser was at least the required age.

36 "§ 18D-104. Testing prior to distribution.
37 (a) Requirement. – The manufacturer shall have a hemp-derived consumable product
38 tested prior to distribution to a distributor or before distributing the product to a seller. If the

40 consumer of the product when delivered to the distributor and the distributor does not open such
41 package, the distributor is not required to test the hemp-derived consumable product. If the
42 hemp-derived consumable product is not packaged in a manner that may be sold to the ultimate
43 consumer of the product when delivered to the distributor or the distributor does open such

45 distribution. The testing shall determine the presence and amounts of any of the substances listed
46 in subsection (b) of this section. No product that contains more than the maximum amount
47 indicated for any substance in subsection (b) of this section shall be distributed or sold in this
48 State.

3 "§ 18D-105. Additional requirements and restrictions for hemp-derived consumable
4 products.
5 (a) Packaging Requirements. – A hemp-derived consumable product that is sold in this
6 State shall meet both of the following requirements:
7 (1) The product shall satisfy the child-resistant effectiveness standards under 16
8 C.F.R. § 1700.15(b)(1) when tested in accordance with the requirements of 16
9 C.F.R. § 1700.20.
10 (2) The product shall be labeled with consumer protection warnings in the form
11 of statements that cover all of the following:
12 a. A list of ingredients and major FDA allergens and a nutritional fact
13 panel or have a quick response code that can be scanned that directs
14 consumers to a website containing the list of ingredients and allergens
15 and a nutritional fact panel.
16 b. A statement that use while pregnant or breastfeeding may be harmful.
17 c. A statement that consumption of certain cannabinoids may impair
18 your ability to drive and operate heavy machinery.
19 d. A statement that the product is not approved by the United States Food
20 and Drug Administration.
21 e. A statement to keep out of reach of children.
22 f. A statement to consult your physician before use.
23 g. If the product is ingestible, the amount of hemp-derived cannabinoid
24 in each serving of the product, measured in milligrams.
25 h. The total amount of hemp-derived cannabinoid in the entire package,
26 measured in milligrams.
27 i. The net weight of the product.
28 j. A web address or a quick response code that can be scanned to access
29 a website providing the product's batch number, date received, date of
30 completion, and method of analysis for the testing required under
31 G.S. 18D-106.
32 k. An expiration date in accordance with applicable federal law.
33 l. Manufacturer contact information.
