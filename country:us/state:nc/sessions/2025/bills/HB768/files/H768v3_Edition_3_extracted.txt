13 CLARIFY FIRE CODE EXEMPTIONS FROM IN-BUILDING EMERGENCY
14 RESPONDER COMMUNICATION COVERAGE REQUIREMENTS
15 SECTION 1.(a) Definitions. – For purposes of this section, the following are
16 defined:
17 (1) Code. – The North Carolina State Building Code collection, including the
18 North Carolina Fire Code, and amendments thereto, as adopted by the
19 Council.
20 (2) Council. – The North Carolina Building Code Council.
21 (3) Emergency responder communication coverage requirements. – The
22 requirements for in-building emergency responder communications coverage
23 systems specified under Section 510 of the North Carolina Fire Code.
24 SECTION 1.(b) Exemptions Established. – Notwithstanding the emergency
25 responder communication coverage requirements of the Code, the following buildings and
26 structures, whether existing, newly constructed, or altered after the effective date of this act, are
27 exempt from those requirements:
28 (1) R-2 Apartment Buildings of Type V construction that meet the egress
29 requirements of the Code.
30 (2) Apartment buildings and transient public lodging establishments, including
31 hotels and motels, not exceeding two stories above grade plane and that
32 provide direct exterior egress from each dwelling unit or guest room.
33 SECTION 1.(c) Treatment of Existing Systems in Exempted Buildings. –
34 Notwithstanding Section 901.6 of the North Carolina Fire Code or any other provision of the
35 Code that might require the removal of nonrequired, obsolete, or abandoned systems, an

15 SECTION 1.(d) Rulemaking Required. – The Council shall adopt rules to amend
16 the Code, specifically Section 510 of the North Carolina Fire Code and any related sections
17 addressing emergency responder communication coverage, to incorporate the exemptions listed
18 in subsection (b) of this section and the provisions regarding existing systems in subsection (c)
19 of this section. Notwithstanding G.S. 150B-19(4), the rules adopted by the Council pursuant to
20 this section shall be substantively identical to the provisions of subsections (b) and (c) of this
21 section. Rules adopted pursuant to this section are not subject to Part 3 of Article 2A of Chapter
22 150B of the General Statutes. Rules adopted pursuant to this section shall become effective as
23 provided in G.S. 150B-21.3(b1), as though 10 or more written objections had been received as
24 provided in G.S. 150B-21.3(b2).
25 SECTION 1.(e) Interim Application of Exemptions. – Until the effective date of the
26 permanent rules that the Council is required to adopt pursuant to subsection (d) of this section,
27 the Council and local governments enforcing the Code shall not enforce the emergency responder
28 communication coverage requirements against buildings and structures that meet the exemption
29 criteria established in subsection (b) of this section and shall apply the provisions regarding
30 existing systems established in subsection (c) of this section.
31 SECTION 1.(f) Sunset. – This section expires when permanent rules adopted as
32 required by subsection (d) of this section become effective.

5 ESTABLISH STATUTORY REQUIREMENTS FOR THE DEPARTMENT OF
6 ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY'S HANDLING OF APPLICATIONS FOR 401

10 SECTION 3.(a) G.S. 143-214.1A is amended by adding two new subsections to
11 read:
12 "(c) For projects that are eligible for a Nationwide Permit or Regional General Permit
13 issued by the United States Army Corps of Engineers, which are not subject to subsection (a) of
14 this section, and are required or elect to be covered under an Individual Water Quality
15 Certification, the Department shall perform a review of an application pursuant to all of the
16 following requirements:
17 (1) On receipt of such application, the Department shall notify the applicant of
18 any required fee within five business days. If the Department does not send
19 the applicant a fee request within five business days of receipt of an
20 application, the fee is waived. The Department's review period shall begin on
21 the date the application fee is paid, or on the sixth business day from receipt
22 when the application fee has been waived pursuant to this subdivision. For
23 application fees that are paid via interagency batch payment, the review period
24 shall start on receipt of the application by the Division of Water Resources.
25 The Department shall additionally post any public notice required under the
26 federal Clean Water Act within five business days of receipt of an application.
27 (2) Within 30 business days of the beginning of the review period, the Department
28 shall (i) determine whether or not the application is complete and notify the
29 applicant accordingly and, (ii) if the Department determines an application is
30 incomplete, specify all such deficiencies in a notice to the applicant. The
31 applicant may file an amended application or supplemental information to
32 cure the deficiencies identified by the Department for the Department's
33 review. Review of amended applications or supplemental information
34 responses provided by the applicant shall occur within 20 business days of
35 receipt. An application may be deemed incomplete only if it does not provide
36 sufficient information necessary for the Department to determine if the
37 proposed discharges into navigable waters will comply with State water
38 quality requirements. If the Department fails to issue a notice that the
39 application is incomplete within the requisite initial 30-day period, or the
40 supplemental 20-day review period, the application shall be deemed complete.
41 As used in this section, State water quality requirements means water quality
42 standards approved by the United States Environmental Protection Agency
43 pursuant to 33 U.S.C. § 1313(c)(3).
44 (3) If the Department determines that a public hearing is necessary pursuant to
45 applicable requirements, the Department shall notify the applicant within the
46 review period outlined in subdivision (2) of this subsection. If the hearing
47 officer determines that additional information is required from the applicant
48 at the conclusion of the public hearing comment period, the Department shall
49 notify the applicant within five business days of the conclusion of the
50 comment period and the supplemental information shall be subject to the
51 review time lines laid out in subdivision (2) of this subsection.
