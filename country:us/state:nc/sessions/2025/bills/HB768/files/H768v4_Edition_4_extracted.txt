GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 4
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/17/25
Senate Regulatory Reform Committee Substitute Adopted 6/18/25
Fourth Edition Engrossed 6/23/25
Short Title: Emerg. Comm. Code Exempts/Sanitary Board/401. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
April 7, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO DIRECT THE NORTH CAROLINA BUILDING CODE COUNCIL TO ADOPT
3 RULES TO AMEND THE STATE BUILDING CODE AND STATE FIRE CODE TO
4 CLARIFY EXEMPTIONS FROM IN-BUILDING EMERGENCY RESPONDER
5 COMMUNICATION COVERAGE REQUIREMENTS; TO PROVIDE FOR THE
6 FILLING OF VACANCIES ON CERTAIN SANITARY BOARDS; AND TO ESTABLISH
7 STATUTORY REQUIREMENTS FOR THE DEPARTMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL
8 QUALITY'S HANDLING OF APPLICATIONS FOR 401 CERTIFICATIONS FOR
9 PROJECTS THAT ARE ELIGIBLE FOR A NATIONWIDE PERMIT OR REGIONAL
10 GENERAL PERMIT.
11 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
12
13 CLARIFY FIRE CODE EXEMPTIONS FROM IN-BUILDING EMERGENCY
14 RESPONDER COMMUNICATION COVERAGE REQUIREMENTS
15 SECTION 1.(a) Definitions. – For purposes of this section, the following are
16 defined:
17 (1) Code. – The North Carolina State Building Code collection, including the
18 North Carolina Fire Code, and amendments thereto, as adopted by the
19 Council.
20 (2) Council. – The North Carolina Building Code Council.
21 (3) Emergency responder communication coverage requirements. – The
22 requirements for in-building emergency responder communications coverage
23 systems specified under Section 510 of the North Carolina Fire Code.
24 SECTION 1.(b) Exemptions Established. – Notwithstanding the emergency
25 responder communication coverage requirements of the Code, the following buildings and
26 structures, whether existing, newly constructed, or altered after the effective date of this act, are
27 exempt from those requirements:
28 (1) R-2 Apartment Buildings of Type V construction that meet the egress
29 requirements of the Code.
30 (2) Apartment buildings and transient public lodging establishments, including
31 hotels and motels, not exceeding two stories above grade plane and that
32 provide direct exterior egress from each dwelling unit or guest room.
33 SECTION 1.(c) Treatment of Existing Systems in Exempted Buildings. –
34 Notwithstanding Section 901.6 of the North Carolina Fire Code or any other provision of the
*H768-v-4*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 Code that might require the removal of nonrequired, obsolete, or abandoned systems, an
2 emergency responder communication coverage system installed prior to the effective date of this
3 act in a building or structure that meets the exemption criteria established in subsection (b) of
4 this section may be disconnected as follows:
5 (1) Systems that are monitored by a fire alarm system shall be properly
6 disconnected to eliminate unnecessary supervisory signals.
7 (2) Primary and secondary power sources shall be permanently removed by
8 disconnecting the primary power from the circuit breaker and removing the
9 secondary backup batteries and/or emergency generator connection.
10 (3) The system shall be permanently disconnected from the donor antenna. Where
11 the donor antenna is left in place, lightning protection for the antenna and mast
12 shall be maintained.
13 (4) Signal booster shall be labeled with signage that states "Emergency Responder
14 Communication Coverage is disconnected as permitted by Section 510 of the
15 North Carolina Fire Code."
16 SECTION 1.(d) Rulemaking Required. – The Council shall adopt rules to amend
17 the Code, specifically Section 510 of the North Carolina Fire Code and any related sections
18 addressing emergency responder communication coverage, to incorporate the exemptions listed
19 in subsection (b) of this section and the provisions regarding existing systems in subsection (c)
20 of this section. Notwithstanding G.S. 150B-19(4), the rules adopted by the Council pursuant to
21 this section shall be substantively identical to the provisions of subsections (b) and (c) of this
22 section. Rules adopted pursuant to this section are not subject to Part 3 of Article 2A of Chapter
23 150B of the General Statutes. Rules adopted pursuant to this section shall become effective as
24 provided in G.S. 150B-21.3(b1), as though 10 or more written objections had been received as
25 provided in G.S. 150B-21.3(b2).
26 SECTION 1.(e) Interim Application of Exemptions. – Until the effective date of the
27 permanent rules that the Council is required to adopt pursuant to subsection (d) of this section,
28 the Council and local governments enforcing the Code shall not enforce the emergency responder
29 communication coverage requirements against buildings and structures that meet the exemption
30 criteria established in subsection (b) of this section and shall apply the provisions regarding
31 existing systems established in subsection (c) of this section.
32 SECTION 1.(f) Sunset. – This section expires when permanent rules adopted as
33 required by subsection (d) of this section become effective.
34
35 VACANCY APPOINTMENTS FOR CERTAIN SANITARY BOARDS
36 SECTION 2. G.S. 130A-50(h) reads as rewritten:
37 "(h) For purposes of the election of the members of the sanitary district board, a sanitary
38 district board shall adopt single-member residency districts in which only a person residing in a
39 single-member residency district shall be eligible as a candidate in the election for the seat
40 apportioned to that single-member residency district, but candidates shall be elected at large by
41 the qualified voters of the entire sanitary district. The district boundaries shall be established by
42 resolution adopted by the sanitary district board following a public hearing on the matter. The
43 resolution, and a copy of a map depicting the single-member residency districts as adopted, shall
44 be filed with the county board of elections. The most recent federal decennial census data shall
45 be used as the sole basis of population for the establishment of the single-member residency
46 districts. The single-member residency districts shall be revised after the return of each federal
47 decennial census as necessary. Notwithstanding subsection (b) of this section, members of a
48 sanitary district board under this subsection shall serve staggered four-year terms and the
49 staggering shall be taken into consideration when adopting or revising the single-member
50 residency districts. Any vacancy in a sanitary district board shall be filled by the remaining
51 sanitary district board members until the next election for sanitary district board members. A

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 vacancy must be filled by a resident from the same residency district as the residency district of
2 the vacating commissioner. This subsection shall apply only to a sanitary district that provides
3 water and sewer service lying wholly within a county with more than 17 municipalities lying
4 wholly within that county."
5
6 ESTABLISH STATUTORY REQUIREMENTS FOR THE DEPARTMENT OF
7 ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY'S HANDLING OF APPLICATIONS FOR 401
8 CERTIFICATIONS FOR PROJECTS THAT ARE ELIGIBLE FOR A NATIONWIDE
9 PERMIT OR REGIONAL GENERAL PERMIT ISSUED BY THE UNITED STATES
10 ARMY CORPS OF ENGINEERS
11 SECTION 3.(a) G.S. 143-214.1A is amended by adding two new subsections to
12 read:
13 "(c) For projects that are eligible for a Nationwide Permit or Regional General Permit
14 issued by the United States Army Corps of Engineers, which are not subject to subsection (a) of
15 this section, and are required or elect to be covered under an Individual Water Quality
16 Certification, the Department shall perform a review of an application pursuant to all of the
17 following requirements:
18 (1) On receipt of such application, the Department shall notify the applicant of
19 any required fee within five business days. If the Department does not send
20 the applicant a fee request within five business days of receipt of an
21 application, the fee is waived. The Department's review period shall begin on
22 the date the application fee is paid, or on the sixth business day from receipt
23 when the application fee has been waived pursuant to this subdivision. For
24 application fees that are paid via interagency batch payment, the review period
25 shall start on receipt of the application by the Division of Water Resources.
26 The Department shall additionally post any public notice required under the
27 federal Clean Water Act within five business days of receipt of an application.
28 (2) Within 30 business days of the beginning of the review period, the Department
29 shall (i) determine whether or not the application is complete and notify the
30 applicant accordingly and, (ii) if the Department determines an application is
31 incomplete, specify all such deficiencies in a notice to the applicant. The
32 applicant may file an amended application or supplemental information to
33 cure the deficiencies identified by the Department for the Department's
34 review. Review of amended applications or supplemental information
35 responses provided by the applicant shall occur within 20 business days of
36 receipt. An application may be deemed incomplete only if it does not provide
37 sufficient information necessary for the Department to determine if the
38 proposed discharges into navigable waters will comply with State water
39 quality requirements. If the Department fails to issue a notice that the
40 application is incomplete within the requisite initial 30-day period, or the
41 supplemental 20-day review period, the application shall be deemed complete.
42 As used in this section, State water quality requirements means water quality
43 standards approved by the United States Environmental Protection Agency
44 pursuant to 33 U.S.C. § 1313(c)(3).
45 (3) If the Department determines that a public hearing is necessary pursuant to
46 applicable requirements, the Department shall notify the applicant within the
47 review period outlined in subdivision (2) of this subsection. If the hearing
48 officer determines that additional information is required from the applicant
49 at the conclusion of the public hearing comment period, the Department shall
50 notify the applicant within 15 business days of the conclusion of the comment
House Bill 768-Fourth Edition Page 3
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 period and the supplemental information shall be subject to the review time
2 lines laid out in subdivision (2) of this subsection.
3 (4) The Department shall either approve or deny an application within (i) 10
4 business days of the date the application is deemed complete if no public
5 hearing is held or (ii) 15 business days of the close of the record if a public
6 hearing is held and no additional information is required. Failure of the
7 Department to approve or deny the application within the requisite time
8 period, as applicable, shall result in a waiver of the certification requirement
9 by the State, unless the applicant agrees, in writing, to an extension of time,
10 which shall not exceed one year from the State's receipt of the application for
11 certification.
12 (5) The Department shall issue a certification, with or without conditions or
13 limitations, upon determining that the proposed discharges into navigable
14 waters subject to the federal Clean Water Act will comply with State water
15 quality requirements. The Department may include as conditions or
16 limitations in a certification any effluent limitations or other limitations
17 necessary to assure the proposed discharges into navigable waters subject to
18 the federal Clean Water Act will comply with State water quality
19 requirements. The Department shall not impose any other conditions or
20 limitations in a certification.
21 (6) The Department shall deny a certification application only if it determines that
22 no reasonable conditions or limitations would provide assurance that the
23 proposed discharges into navigable waters subject to the federal Clean Water
24 Act will comply with State water quality requirements. The denial shall
25 include a statement explaining why the Department determined the proposed
26 discharges into navigable waters subject to the federal Clean Water Act will
27 not comply with the State water quality requirements.
28 (7) The Department may grant, deny, or waive certification but shall not require
29 an applicant to withdraw an application.
30 (d) On receipt of any application for certification filed with the Department pursuant to
31 section 401 of the Clean Water Act, 33 U.S.C. § 1341(a)(1), the Department shall notify the
32 applicant of any required fee within five business days. If the Department does not send the
33 applicant a fee request within five business days of receipt of an application, the fee is waived."
34 SECTION 3.(b) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025.
35
36 EFFECTIVE DATE
37 SECTION 4. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
38 law.
