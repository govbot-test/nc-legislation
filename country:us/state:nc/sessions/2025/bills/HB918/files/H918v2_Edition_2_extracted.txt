1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT AUTHORIZING THE NORTH CAROLINA COLLABORATORY TO STUDY THE
3 INCLUSION OF THE CLASSICAL LEARNING TEST AMONG STANDARDIZED
4 TESTS CONSIDERED FOR UNDERGRADUATE ADMISSION AND THE AWARD OF
5 SCHOLARSHIPS AT CONSTITUENT INSTITUTIONS OF THE UNIVERSITY OF
6 NORTH CAROLINA.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1.(a) The North Carolina Collaboratory shall study the viability of
9 including Classical Learning Test scores as a part of an application for admission or award of
10 scholarship to a constituent institution of The University of North Carolina. In conducting this
11 study, the Collaboratory shall seek input from The University of North Carolina and the James
12 G. Martin Center for Academic Renewal. As a part of the study, the Collaboratory shall
13 determine:
14 (1) Whether an applicant's score on the Classical Learning Test serves the purpose
15 of aiding constituent institutions in determining (i) an applicant's
16 qualifications for undergraduate admission and (ii) an applicant's
17 qualifications for award of scholarships funded in whole or in part with State
18 funds and administered by an entity of The University of North Carolina.
19 (2) Whether an applicant's performance on the Classical Learning Test is
20 predictive of college success.
21 (3) Whether a score on the Classical Learning Test can be deemed concordant
22 with a score on the SAT or ACT such that The University of North Carolina
23 could use an applicant's score on the Classical Learning Test interchangeably
24 with a score on the SAT or ACT for the purposes of setting minimum
25 requirements for admission and awarding scholarships.
26 (4) The financial, operational, and administrative cost to The University of North
27 Carolina and constituent institutions associated with accepting an applicant's
28 score on the Classical Learning Test in addition to accepting an applicant's
29 score on the SAT or ACT.
30 (5) The financial, operational, and administrative cost to the State of adding the
31 CLT10 to the list of standardized tests offered one time and at no cost to the
32 student for every student in the eighth through tenth grades who has completed
33 Algebra I or who is in the last month of Algebra I pursuant to
34 G.S. 115C-174.18.
35 SECTION 1.(b) The Collaboratory shall make a final report on its study to the Joint
36 Legislative Education Oversight Committee no later than December 15, 2025.
