Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Repeal Crime Against Nature Law. (Public)
Sponsors: Senator Grafstein (Primary Sponsor).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 13, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REPEAL THE CRIMINAL OFFENSE OF CRIME AGAINST NATURE AND
3 ESTABLISH A SEPARATE OFFENSE OF BESTIALITY.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-177 is repealed.
6 SECTION 2. G.S. 7B-101(1)d. reads as rewritten:
7 "d. Commits, permits, or encourages the commission of a violation of the
8 following laws by, with, or upon the juvenile: first-degree forcible
9 rape, as provided in G.S. 14-27.21; second-degree forcible rape as
10 provided in G.S. 14-27.22; statutory rape of a child by an adult as
11 provided in G.S. 14-27.23; first-degree statutory rape as provided in
12 G.S. 14-27.24; first-degree forcible sex offense as provided in
13 G.S. 14-27.26; second-degree forcible sex offense as provided in
14 G.S. 14-27.27; statutory sexual offense with a child by an adult as
15 provided in G.S. 14-27.28; first-degree statutory sexual offense as
16 provided in G.S. 14-27.29; sexual activity by a substitute parent or
17 custodian as provided in G.S. 14-27.31; sexual activity with a student
18 as provided in G.S. 14-27.32; unlawful sale, surrender, or purchase of
19 a minor, as provided in G.S. 14-43.14; crime against nature, as
20 provided in G.S. 14-177; bestiality, as provided in G.S. 14-363.4;
21 incest, as provided in G.S. 14-178; preparation of obscene
22 photographs, slides, or motion pictures of the juvenile, as provided in
23 G.S. 14-190.5; employing or permitting the juvenile to assist in a
24 violation of the obscenity laws as provided in G.S. 14-190.6;
25 dissemination of obscene material to the juvenile as provided in
26 G.S. 14-190.7 and G.S. 14-190.8; displaying or disseminating material
27 harmful to the juvenile as provided in G.S. 14-190.14 and
28 G.S. 14-190.15; first and second degree sexual exploitation of the
29 juvenile as provided in G.S. 14-190.16 and G.S. 14-190.17; promoting
30 the prostitution of the juvenile as provided in G.S. 14-205.3(b); and
31 taking indecent liberties with the juvenile, as provided in
32 G.S. 14-202.1;"
33 SECTION 3. G.S. 75D-3(c)(1)b. reads as rewritten:
34 "b. Chapter 14 of the General Statutes of North Carolina except
35 Articles 9, 22A, 38, 40, 43, 46, 47, 59 thereof; and further
36 excepting G.S. Sections 14-78.1, 14-82, 14-86, 14-145,
*S274-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 14-146, 14-147, 14-177, 14-178, 14-179, 14-183, 14-184,
2 14-186, 14-190.9, 14-195, 14-197, 14-201, 14-202, 14-247,
3 14-248, 14-313 thereof."
4 SECTION 4. G.S. 115C-270.35(b) reads as rewritten:
5 "(b) Automatic Revocation With No Hearing. – The State Board shall automatically
6 revoke the license of a professional educator without the right to a hearing upon receiving
7 verification of the identity of the professional educator together with a certified copy of a criminal
8 record showing that the professional educator has entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere to
9 or has been finally convicted of any of the following crimes:
10 …
11 (7) Crime against nature, G.S. 14-177.
12 (7a) Bestiality, G.S. 14-363.4.
13 …
14 The State Board shall mail notice of its intent to act pursuant to this subsection by certified
15 mail, return receipt requested, directed to the professional educator's last known address. The
16 notice shall inform the professional educator that it will revoke the person's license unless the
17 professional educator notifies the State Board in writing within 10 days after receipt of the notice
18 that the defendant identified in the criminal record is not the same person as the professional
19 educator. If the professional educator provides this written notice to the State Board, the State
20 Board shall not revoke the license unless it can establish as a fact that the defendant and the
21 professional educator are the same person."
22 SECTION 5. Article 47 of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
23 a new section to read:
24 "§ 14-363.4. Bestiality.
25 (a) Offense. – A person commits the offense of bestiality when the person performs or
26 submits to any sexual act with an animal involving the sex organs of the one and the mouth, anus,
27 penis, or vagina of the other.
28 (b) Punishment. – A violation of this section is a Class I felony."
29 SECTION 6. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to offenses
30 committed on or after that date.
