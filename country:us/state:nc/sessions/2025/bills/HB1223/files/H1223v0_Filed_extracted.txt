Section 1:
H.B. 1223
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
May 5, 2026
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Reenact Funding/Meck. Emerg. Pension Fund. (Local)
Sponsors: Representative Cotham.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REENACT AND INCREASE THE FUNDING FOR THE EMERGENCY
3 PENSION FUND FOR SWORN LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICERS OF
4 MECKLENBURG COUNTY.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) Section 4 of Chapter 446 of the Public-Local Laws of 1931 as it
7 existed immediately prior to repeal is reenacted and reads as rewritten:
8 "Sec. 4. That in order to provide funds for "The Emergency Pension Fund for Sworn Law
9 Enforcement Officers of the County of Mecklenburg" Mecklenburg County" herein set out, there
10 shall be taxed assessed in the bill of costs in all criminal cases in any Court other than that of
11 Justice of Peace wherein there is a conviction or a plea of guilty, a fee of one ($1.00) dollar two
12 dollars ($2.00) to be known as "The Emergency and Pension Fee" and the same shall be collected
13 by the Clerk of the City Recorder's Court, the Clerk of the County Recorder's Court and the Clerk
14 of the Superior Court and shall be paid to the Treasurer of the "Officers Relief Board of
15 Mecklenburg County" Emergency Pension Fund for Sworn Law Enforcement Officers of
16 Mecklenburg County Board, or in cases of appeal from said City Recorder's Court or County
17 Recorder's Court to the Superior Court, the Clerk of the Superior Court shall collect said fees and
18 pay the same to the Treasurer of the "Officers Relief Board of Mecklenburg County." Board. All
19 money collected by the Clerk of the City Recorder's Court, Clerk of the County Recorder's Court
20 shall be paid over to the Treasurer of the "Officers Relief Board of Mecklenburg County" Board
21 once each week and all of said money collected by the Clerk of the Superior Court shall be paid
22 over to the Treasurer of the "Officers Relief Board of Mecklenburg County" Board on the first
23 day of each and every month and they shall accompany such remittance with a detailed and
24 itemized statement of the cases in which said funds have been collected, the blank forms for said
25 statement shall be furnished by the "Officers Relief Board of Mecklenburg County" Board herein
26 provided for. In all cases where the defendant is committed to the roads, the fee herein provided
27 shall not be charged against said defendant where said defendant serves the sentence imposed by
28 the Court and it is further provided that the one dollar ($1.00) The two dollar ($2.00) fee
29 hereinbefore set out shall not be collected in cases of conviction or plea of guilty for the violation
30 of the traffic or highway laws by use of a motor vehicle where the punishment provided by the
31 Statute is not in excess of fifty dollars ($50.00) fine or thirty days (30) imprisonment; the said
32 fee shall be collected in all other cases for violation of said laws where the punishment fixed by
33 the Statute is in excess of the penalties herein before mentioned; Provided further, that where the
34 defendant is convicted on more than one charge, the fee hereinbefore provided for shall only be
35 taxed in the cost in one conviction."
*DRH10583-MRfp-147*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 1.(b) Section 7 of Chapter 446 of the Public-Local Laws of 1931 as it
2 existed immediately prior to repeal is reenacted.
3 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective October 1, 2026, and applies to the bills of
4 costs of applicable convictions or guilty pleas occurring on or after that date.
