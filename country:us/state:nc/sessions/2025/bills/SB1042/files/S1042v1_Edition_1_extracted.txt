6 PART I. WEB AND MOBILE BASED COMMUNICATION PLATFORM FOR USE IN
7 CHILD WELFARE CASES
8 SECTION 1.1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
9 Health and Human Services the sum of two hundred and fifty thousand dollars ($250,000) in
10 recurring funds beginning in the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be allocated to the Office of Indigent
11 Defense Services to contract with an approved vendor, no later than October 1, 2026, for the
12 implementation of a secure web and mobile based communications platform to ensure
13 compliance with federal and State requirements for the following:
14 (1) Advanced notification via automated electronic methods such as text
15 messaging or email to all parties and their attorneys, to the persons providing
16 care to the juvenile, and to any other person or agency designated by the court
17 for all court hearings and for interagency team meetings initiated by the
18 county department of social services such as permanency planning review
19 meetings, child and family team meetings, and placement status change
20 meetings.
21 (2) Enhanced communication between attorneys and clients and between parties
22 to reduce court continuances, share discovery, and reduce barriers to
23 permanency.
24 SECTION 1.2. The Administrative Office of the Courts, Indigent Services of North
25 Carolina, the Guardian Ad Litem Program, and the Division of Social Services of the North
26 Carolina Department of Health and Human Services shall enter into a data sharing agreement to
27 enable the vendor selected pursuant to Section 1.1 of this act to receive the data necessary to
28 ensure timely notification of court hearings and interagency team meetings and to ensure the
29 platform receives accurate information.
30 SECTION 1.3. Section 1.1 of this act becomes effective July 1, 2026. Section 1.2
31 of this act becomes effective when it becomes law.

9 SECTION 2.2. The Department of Health and Human Services shall use the funds
10 credited as provided in G.S. 105-113.128(1), as amended by Section 2.1 of this act, to annually
11 provide the following:
12 (1) Four million dollars ($4,000,000) for gambling addiction education and
13 treatment programs.
14 (2) Twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) to be used for child welfare and
15 foster care services including:
16 a. Reimbursement for home inspections required to obtain and maintain
17 foster home and therapeutic foster home licensure.
18 b. Reimbursement for training required to obtain and maintain licensure
19 as a therapeutic foster parent.
20 c. Reimbursement for items needed by a child at the child's initial
21 placement. Such items include school supplies, toiletries, clothing,
22 diapers, and other items to assist in the transition, and which should be
23 chosen with the child's input when possible. Reimbursement shall be
24 up to two hundred fifty dollars ($250) for children through five years
25 of age and up to five hundred dollars ($500) for children from six years
26 of age.
27 d. Emergency placement.
28 (3) Seventy-five million dollars ($75,000,000) to be used for child-care services
29 including:
30 a. Increasing the child-care subsidy market rates to the seventy-fifth
31 percentile as recommended by the 2023 Child Care Market Rate Study
32 for children in three-, four-, and five-star-rated child care centers and
33 homes.
34 b. Extension of the Tri-Share Child-Care program established by S.L.
35 2023-134 and amended by S.L. 2024-34.
36 c. Increasing child-care providers inclusive of extending and expanding
37 the in-home child-care pilot program established by S.L. 2023-134.

8 PART III. PLAN FOR IMPLEMENTATION OF STATEWIDE FLOOR
9 SECTION 3. The Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Child
10 Development and Early Education (Division), shall develop a plan for implementing a statewide
11 child care subsidy reimbursement rate floor. The Division shall submit a report with their
12 recommended plan no later than December 1, 2026 to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee
13 on Health and Human Services and the Fiscal Research Division.
