5 SECTION 1. Article 23 of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes reads as rewritten:
6 "Article 23.
7 "Workplace Violence Prevention.
8 "§ 95-260. Definitions.
9 The following definitions apply in this Article:
10 (1) Civil no-contact order. – An order granted under this Article, which includes
11 a remedy authorized by G.S. 95-264.
12 (2) Employer. – Any person or entity that employs one or more employees.
13 Employer also includes the State of North Carolina and its political
14 subdivisions.
15 (2a) Mass picketing. – Picketing, with or without signs, that constitutes an obstacle
16 to the ingress and egress to and from the premises being picketed or any other
17 premises, or upon the public roads, streets, highways, or other ways of travel
18 or conveyance, either by obstructing by their persons or by placing of vehicles
19 or other physical obstructions.
20 (2b) Place of employment. – A building or conveyance of any kind, whether the
21 building or conveyance is temporary or permanent, mobile or immobile.
22 (3) Unlawful conduct. – Unlawful conduct means the commission of one or more
23 of the following acts upon an employer or employee, but does not include acts
24 of self-defense or defense of others:
25 a. Attempting to cause bodily injury or intentionally causing bodily
26 injury.
27 b. Willfully, and on more than one occasion, following, being in the
28 presence of, or otherwise harassing, as defined in G.S. 14-277.3A,
29 without legal purpose and with the intent to place the employee or
30 employer in reasonable fear for the employee's or employer's safety.
31 c. Willfully threatening, orally, in writing, or by any other means, to
32 physically injure the employee or employer in a manner and under
33 circumstances that would cause a reasonable person to believe that the
34 threat is likely to be carried out and that actually causes the employee
35 or employer to believe that the threat will be carried out.

8 "§ 95-261. Civil no-contact orders; persons protected.
9 An action for a civil no-contact order may be filed as a civil action in district court by an
10 employer on behalf of an employee or by an employer who has suffered unlawful conduct from
11 any individual or individuals that can reasonably be construed to be carried out, or to have been
12 carried out, at the employee's workplace. place of employment. The employee or employer that
13 is the subject of unlawful conduct shall be consulted prior to seeking an injunction under this
14 Article in order to determine whether any safety concerns exist in relation to the employee's or
15 employer's participation in the process. Employees or employers who are targets of unlawful
16 conduct who are unwilling to participate in the process under this Article shall not face
17 disciplinary action based on their level of participation or cooperation.
18 "§ 95-262. Commencement of action; venue.
19 (a) An action for a civil no-contact order is commenced by filing a verified complaint for
20 a civil no-contact order in any civil district court the county where the unlawful conduct took
21 place or by filing a motion in any existing civil action.
22 (b) A complaint or motion for a civil no-contact order shall be filed in the county where
23 the unlawful conduct took place.

25 "§ 95-264. Civil no-contact order; remedy.
26 (a) Upon a finding that the employee or employer has suffered unlawful conduct
27 committed by the respondent, the court may issue a temporary or permanent civil no-contact
28 order. In determining whether or not to issue a civil no-contact order, the court shall not require
29 physical injury to the employee or employer or injury to the employer's property.
30 (b) The court may grant one or more of the following forms of relief in its orders under
31 this Article:
32 (1) Order the respondent not to visit, assault, molest, or otherwise interfere with
33 the employer or the employer's employee at the employer's workplace, or
34 otherwise interfere with the employer's operations.
35 (2) Order the respondent to cease stalking the employer or the employer's
36 employee at the employer's workplace.
37 (3) Order the respondent to cease harassment of the employer or the employer's
38 employee at the employer's workplace.
39 (4) Order the respondent not to abuse or injure the employer, including the
40 employer's property, or the employer's employee at the employer's workplace.
41 (5) Order the respondent not to contact by telephone, written communication, or
42 electronic means the employer or the employer's employee at the employer's
43 workplace.
44 (6) Order other relief deemed necessary and appropriate by the court.
45 (c) A civil no-contact order shall include the following notice, printed in conspicuous
46 type: "A knowing violation of a civil no-contact order shall be punishable as contempt of court
47 which may result in a fine or imprisonment."

5 "§ 95-271. Scope of Article; other remedies available.available; severability.
6 (a) This Article does not expand, diminish, alter, or modify any duty of any employer to
7 provide a safe workplace for employees and other persons. This Article does not limit the ability
8 of an employer, employee, or victim to pursue any other civil or criminal remedy provided by
9 law. This Article does not apply in circumstances where an employee or representative of
10 employees is engaged in union organizing, union activity, a labor dispute, or any activity or action
11 protected by the National Labor Relations Act, 29 U.S.C. § 151, et seq. Nothing in this Article is
12 intended to change the National Labor Relations Act's preemptive regulation of legally protected
13 activities, nor to change the right of the State and its courts to regulate activities not protected by
14 the National Labor Relations Act.
15 (b) Nothing in this Article is intended, or shall be construed, to conflict with, restrict,
16 limit, or infringe upon rights protected by the North Carolina or United States Constitution.
17 (c) If any provision of this Article is held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be
18 invalid, void, or unenforceable, in whole or in part, the decision shall not affect the validity,
19 enforceability, or applicability of the remaining provisions of this Article, which shall remain in
20 full force and effect as if the provision held invalid, void, or unenforceable had not been
21 included."
22 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to acts or
23 omissions occurring on or after that date.
