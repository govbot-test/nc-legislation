1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE A PATHWAY TO A CONTINUING PROFESSIONAL LICENSE
3 FOR TEACHERS TEACHING UNDER A LIMITED LICENSE, TO ESTABLISH THE
4 TEACHER APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM, TO REPURPOSE FUNDS FROM THE
5 TEACHER APPRENTICE GRANT PROGRAM FOR THE TEACHER
6 APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM, AND TO REQUIRE THE BOARD OF GOVERNORS
7 OF THE UNIVERSITY OF NORTH CAROLINA AND THE STATE BOARD OF
8 COMMUNITY COLLEGES TO DEVELOP AN EXPEDITED TEACHER PIPELINE
9 PATHWAY.
10 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
11
12 PART I. CONVERTING A LIMITED LICENSE TO A CONTINUING PROFESSIONAL
13 LICENSE
14 SECTION 1. G.S. 115C-270.15 reads as rewritten:
15 "§ 115C-270.15. Examination requirements.

17 (e) Conversion to Continuing Professional License. – The Except as provided in
18 subsection (f) of this section, the State Board shall not convert an IPL or RL IPL, RL, or limited
19 license to a continuing professional license for a teacher who has not fulfilled the examination
20 requirements of this section.
21 (f) Waiver of Examination Requirements for Certain Individuals with Limited Licenses.
22 – The State Board shall convert a limited license to a continuing professional license for a teacher
23 who has available growth data under the Education Value-Added Assessment System (EVAAS)
24 and has a positive growth score for two of the three most recent years for which growth scores
25 are available."

27 PART II. TEACHER APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM
28 SECTION 2.(a) Article 17D of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
29 adding a new section to read:
30 "§ 115C-269.33. Teacher Apprenticeship Program.
31 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions shall apply in this section:
32 (1) Advanced Teaching Roles unit. – As defined in G.S. 115C-310.3(6).
33 (2) Apprentice. – A person who is employed as an apprentice by an apprenticeship
34 employer and meets all of the following criteria:
35 a. Holds a bachelor's degree.
36 b. Is eligible to hold or holds one of the following:
*S508-v-2*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 1. An emergency license.
2 2. A residency license.
3 3. A permit to teach issued by the Department in accordance with
4 rules adopted by the State Board of Education.
5 c. Submitted a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).
6 (3) Apprenticeship employer. – An Advanced Teaching Roles unit that meets all
7 of the following criteria:
8 a. Has a registered apprenticeship program under requirements
9 established by the United States Department of Labor.
10 b. Employs apprentices under the Program.
11 (4) Department. – The Department of Public Instruction.
12 (5) Program. – The Teacher Apprenticeship Program.
13 (6) RFP. – Request for proposals.
14 (b) Program Established; Purpose. – There is established the Teacher Apprenticeship
15 Program as a competitive grant program for the purpose of increasing the number of
16 professionally licensed teachers in the State and improving teacher competency, student
17 outcomes, and teacher retention in the State. The Department of Public Instruction shall
18 administer the Program in collaboration with ApprenticeshipNC as set forth in this section.
19 (c) Request for Proposals. – No later than November 1 of each year, ApprenticeshipNC
20 shall issue an RFP for the Program. Advanced Teaching Roles units may submit proposals by
21 January 1 of the following calendar year. Proposals shall include at least the following
22 information:
23 (1) A plan to establish a registered teacher apprenticeship program in
24 collaboration with ApprenticeshipNC, including at least the following
25 information:
26 a. The number of apprentices the unit intends to employ using grant
27 funds for State-funded salary supplements, State-funded enrollment
28 expenses, or both.
29 b. Specific subject areas and grade levels in the local school
30 administrative unit with teacher shortages.
31 (2) A system of supports that would be provided for apprentices, including
32 qualifications of mentor teachers and a schedule of supervision.
33 (3) Alternative sources of funding to support apprenticeships that could be paired
34 with State funds received under the Program, including federal workforce
35 development funds.
36 (4) An explanation of how the unit would incorporate its registered teacher
37 apprenticeship program with its advanced teaching roles program to enhance
38 the learning environment for apprentices.
39 (5) Strategies to encourage candidates to accept an apprenticeship instead of
40 directly entering the teacher profession on a Residency License, Emergency
41 License, or permit to teach.
42 (d) Selection of Recipients. – By March 15 of each school year in which proposals are
43 submitted, ApprenticeshipNC shall review the proposals and select local school administrative
44 units to participate in the Program, beginning in the subsequent school year. ApprenticeshipNC
45 shall notify the Department of its selections, and the Department shall allocate funds to the
46 selected Advanced Teaching Roles units in accordance with subsection (e) of this section.
47 (e) Allocation of State Grant Funds. – To the extent funds are appropriated by the General
48 Assembly for this purpose, the Department shall allocate the funds for grants to apprenticeship
49 employers based on the number of apprentices the unit intends to employ receiving grant funds
50 for State-funded salary supplements, State-funded enrollment expenses, or both, up to a
51 maximum of 10 total apprentices per unit, as follows:
Page 2 Senate Bill 508-Second Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (1) Up to seven thousand five hundred dollars ($7,500) per apprentice per year,
2 plus any additional funds necessary for benefits, to provide salary supplements
3 for apprentices in accordance subdivision (3) of subsection (f) of this section.
4 (2) Up to five thousand dollars ($5,000) per apprentice per year for the costs of
5 enrollment of an apprentice in an institution of higher education in accordance
6 with subdivision (5) of subsection (f) of this section.
7 (f) Program Requirements. – The following minimum requirements shall apply to
8 apprentices and apprenticeship employers under the Program:
9 (1) Number of apprentices. – An apprenticeship employer shall employ no more
10 than a maximum of 10 total apprentices who receive grant funds for
11 State-funded salary supplements, State-funded enrollment expenses, or both.
12 An apprenticeship employer may employ additional apprentices receiving
13 funds for salary supplements or education expenses from other sources.
14 (2) Funds for positions. – An apprenticeship employer may employ apprentices
15 using State funds allotted for teacher assistant positions, with roles and
16 responsibilities modified to meet the requirements of the Program, or other
17 available funds.
18 (3) Salary supplements. – As follows:
19 a. An apprenticeship employer shall provide the following salary
20 supplements:
21 1. For each apprentice, up to a maximum of seven thousand five
22 hundred dollars ($7,500) per apprentice.
23 2. For each mentor teacher, up to a maximum of five thousand
24 dollars ($5,000) per mentor teacher.
25 b. An apprenticeship employer may provide a salary supplement for any
26 other teacher employed by the local school administrative unit who
27 agrees to become the teacher of record for additional students to
28 facilitate the operation of the registered apprenticeship program in that
29 unit, up to a maximum of three thousand dollars ($3,000).
30 (4) Mentors. – Each apprentice shall work full time in a classroom with a mentor
31 teacher who is a member of a team of teachers that is led by a teacher with an
32 advanced teaching role. For purposes of this subdivision, a mentor teacher
33 may include a classroom excellence teacher as defined in G.S. 115C-310.3(7).
34 (5) Enrollment costs. – An apprentice shall enroll in or remain enrolled in a
35 recognized educator preparation program. An apprenticeship employer may
36 provide up to a maximum of five thousand dollars ($5,000) per apprentice per
37 year for the costs of enrollment in an institution of higher education. These
38 funds shall only be used after the apprentice has exhausted all other
39 scholarships or grants covering the cost of attendance at an institution of
40 higher education, including federal Pell grants.
41 (6) Limitations. – An apprentice shall not do any of the following:
42 a. Serve as the teacher of record for any students.
43 b. Engage in substitute teaching for more than eight hours per week.
44 c. Work as an apprentice for more than three years.
45 (g) Term. – The Department shall award grant funds to selected Advanced Teaching
46 Roles units to serve as apprenticeship employers for one or more terms of three years. Prior to
47 the conclusion of a term, ApprenticeshipNC shall evaluate the success of the Program at the unit
48 and the compliance of the unit with the requirements of this section. At the conclusion of the
49 evaluation, ApprenticeshipNC may, in its discretion, renew the apprenticeship employer for an
50 additional term. Throughout the Program, an apprenticeship employer shall provide any
Senate Bill 508-Second Edition Page 3
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 information or access requested by ApprenticeshipNC to evaluate the registered apprenticeship
2 program pursuant to this section.
3 (h) Emergency Position Conversion. – Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-105.25(b), an
4 apprenticeship employer may convert one position allocated to the unit for classroom teachers to
5 its dollar equivalent at the salary on the first step of the "A" Teachers Salary Schedule for every
6 one apprentice employed by the unit who is receiving State grant funds if all of the following are
7 met:
8 (1) The apprentice receiving State grant funds would have been eligible to fill the
9 vacant position using a residency license, emergency license, or permit to
10 teach but was instead hired into the apprentice position.
11 (2) The funds are only used for one or more of the following purposes in
12 accordance with the requirements of the Program:
13 a. Salary supplements for apprentices.
14 b. Salary supplements for mentor teachers.
15 c. Costs of enrollment in an institution of higher education.
16 d. Salary supplements for teachers identified in sub-subdivision b. of
17 subdivision (3) of subsection (f) of this section.
18 (i) Administration. – Of the funds appropriated to the Department of Public Instruction
19 for the Program for each fiscal year, the Department shall allocate the greater of fifteen percent
20 (15%) or three hundred thousand dollars ($300,000) to ApprenticeshipNC to do all of the
21 following in consultation with the Department:
22 (1) Outline the duties and responsibilities of apprentices, including on-the-job
23 training requirements.
24 (2) Collaborate with recognized educator preparation programs to establish
25 education requirements for apprentices and revise curriculum requirements
26 for student teaching to include apprenticeships under the Program.
27 (3) Create minimum competencies for apprentices that reflect the progressive
28 acquisition of ability.
29 (4) Create resources that can be used by apprenticeship employers to select and
30 train mentor teachers, including the responsibilities of a mentor teacher and
31 background information on teacher apprenticeship programs.
32 (5) Develop a process to monitor apprentices in their first years of teaching after
33 successful completion of the Program to evaluate the qualities of teacher
34 candidates that correlate to successful outcomes and lower teacher turnover
35 rates.
36 (6) Assist apprenticeship employers with the following:
37 a. Combining State and federal funds to maximize the number of
38 apprentices in the Program.
39 b. Complying with applicable State and federal law.
40 (7) Develop a training module for mentor teachers that establishes standards for
41 mentor teachers under the Program and incorporates, where applicable, any
42 preexisting standards for mentor teachers.
43 (j) Report. – No later than March 15 of each year, ApprenticeshipNC shall report to the
44 Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee on the Program, including at least the
45 following information:
46 (1) The impact of the Program for each apprenticeship employer on the following:
47 a. The number of teachers, disaggregated by licensure type.
48 b. Student outcomes.
49 c. Teacher retention.
50 (2) Successful strategies and best practices used by apprenticeship employers.
51 (3) Any barriers to expanding the Program."

2 SECTION 2.(c) Notwithstanding any other provision of law or a provision of the
3 Committee Report described in Section 43.2 of S.L. 2023-134 to the contrary, of the one million
4 dollars ($1,000,000) in recurring funds allocated for the Teacher Apprentice Grant Program

13 this section, ApprenticeshipNC shall provide its initial report on the impact of the Teacher
14 Apprenticeship Program by March 15, 2027.
15
16 PART III. EXPEDITED TEACHER PIPELINE PATHWAY STUDY
17 SECTION 3. No later than February 15, 2026, the Board of Governors of The
18 University of North Carolina, in collaboration with the State Board of Community Colleges, shall
19 report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee on a plan for an expedited pathway
20 for North Carolina high school students to enter the teaching profession. The plan shall include
21 at least the following components:
22 (1) Options for an accelerated transition pathway that allows high school students
23 to earn college credits leading to a teaching license while participating in
24 structured, paid, or other experiential learning in the classroom, including
25 applicable program design and sequencing components needed to achieve that
26 goal.
27 (2) Maximize usage and transferability of at least the following coursework
28 completion opportunities:
29 a. College transfer pathways provided through the Career and College
30 Promise Program that support entry into a recognized educator
31 preparation program.
32 b. Community college coursework leading to completion of an associate
33 degree related to teacher preparation.
34 c. Online or asynchronous coursework provided at a constituent
35 institution of The University of North Carolina leading to a bachelor's
36 degree.
37 d. Enrollment in an associate degree program or a bachelor's degree
38 program while serving (i) as a full-time employee in a public school
39 unit and (ii) as a teacher assistant or apprentice in a registered
40 apprenticeship program pursuant to G.S. 115C-269.33, as enacted by
41 Section 2(a) of this act.
42 (3) Any legislative changes or appropriations needed to implement the plan.
43
44 PART IV. EFFECTIVE DATE
45 SECTION 4. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
46 law. Section 1 of this act applies to limited licenses expiring on or after that date.

16 PART III. EXPEDITED TEACHER PIPELINE PATHWAY STUDY
17 SECTION 3. No later than February 15, 2026, the Board of Governors of The
18 University of North Carolina, in collaboration with the State Board of Community Colleges, shall
19 report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee on a plan for an expedited pathway
20 for North Carolina high school students to enter the teaching profession. The plan shall include
21 at least the following components:
22 (1) Options for an accelerated transition pathway that allows high school students
23 to earn college credits leading to a teaching license while participating in
24 structured, paid, or other experiential learning in the classroom, including
25 applicable program design and sequencing components needed to achieve that
26 goal.
27 (2) Maximize usage and transferability of at least the following coursework
28 completion opportunities:
29 a. College transfer pathways provided through the Career and College
30 Promise Program that support entry into a recognized educator
31 preparation program.
32 b. Community college coursework leading to completion of an associate
33 degree related to teacher preparation.
34 c. Online or asynchronous coursework provided at a constituent
35 institution of The University of North Carolina leading to a bachelor's
36 degree.
37 d. Enrollment in an associate degree program or a bachelor's degree
38 program while serving (i) as a full-time employee in a public school
39 unit and (ii) as a teacher assistant or apprentice in a registered
40 apprenticeship program pursuant to G.S. 115C-269.33, as enacted by
41 Section 2(a) of this act.
42 (3) Any legislative changes or appropriations needed to implement the plan.
43
44 PART IV. EFFECTIVE DATE
45 SECTION 4. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
46 law. Section 1 of this act applies to limited licenses expiring on or after that date.
