5 SECTION 1. Article 49A of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes reads as rewritten:
6 "Article 49A.
7 "Equal Employment Practices.
8 "§ 143-422.1. Short title.
9 This Article shall be known and may be cited as the Equal Employment Practices Act.
10 "§ 143-422.2. Legislative declaration.
11 (a) It is the public policy of this State to protect and safeguard the right and opportunity
12 of all persons to seek, obtain and hold employment without discrimination or abridgement on
13 account of race, religion, color, national origin, age, sex or handicap by employers which
14 regularly employ 15 or more employees.
15 (b) It is recognized that the practice of denying employment opportunity and
16 discriminating in the terms of employment foments domestic strife and unrest, deprives the State
17 of the fullest utilization of its capacities for advancement and development, and substantially and
18 adversely affects the interests of employees, employers, and the public in general.
19 (c) Repealed by Session Laws 2017-4, s. 1, effective March 30, 2017.
20 "§ 143-422.3. Investigations; conciliations.
21 The Human Relations Commission in the Civil Rights Division of the Office of
22 Administrative Hearings shall have the authority to receive charges of discrimination from the
23 Equal Employment Opportunity Commission pursuant to an agreement under Section 709(b) of
24 Public Law 88-352, as amended by Public Law 92-261, and investigate and conciliate charges of
25 discrimination. Throughout this process, the agency shall use its good offices to effect an
26 amicable resolution of the charges of discrimination.
27 "§ 143-422.4. Discrimination based upon pregnancy, childbirth, or pregnancy related
28 conditions prohibited.
29 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
30 (1) Bona fide occupational qualification or BFOQ. – A qualification that is
31 reasonably necessary to the normal operation of the particular business.
32 (2) Business necessity. – A practice that is essential to safe and efficient business
33 operations, and demonstrably related to job performance, when there is not a
34 less discriminatory alternative available.
35 (3) Pregnancy. – The condition of being pregnant, the perceived capacity to
36 become pregnant, and pregnancy related conditions.

1 (4) Pregnancy related conditions. – Medical conditions directly related to
2 pregnancy or childbirth, including miscarriage, pregnancy termination,
3 pregnancy complications, recovery from childbirth, and lactation.
4 (5) Undue hardship. – A significant difficulty or expense for the employer when
5 considered in light of factors such as: (i) the nature and cost of the
6 accommodation needed; (ii) the overall financial resources of the facility, the
7 number of persons employed at the facility, and the effect on expenses and
8 resources; (iii) the overall financial resources of the employer, the overall size
9 of the business with respect to the number of employees, and the number, type,
10 and location of facilities; and (iv) the type of operation of the employer,
11 including the composition, structure, and functions of the workforce.
12 (b) It is the public policy of this State to protect and safeguard the right and opportunity
13 of individuals to seek, obtain, and hold employment without discrimination or abridgement on
14 account of pregnancy, childbirth, or pregnancy related conditions. It is contrary to the public
15 policy of this State for an employer, because of the pregnancy, childbirth, or pregnancy related
16 condition of any individual, to refuse to hire, discharge, or otherwise discriminate against that
17 individual with respect to compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of employment.
18 However, no person violates this section solely because the individual followed a bona fide
19 occupational qualification reasonably necessary to the normal operation of the particular business
20 or because the person made a distinction or differentiation based on a bona fide business
21 necessity.
22 (c) Individuals affected by pregnancy, childbirth, or pregnancy related conditions shall
23 be treated the same for all employment-related purposes, including receipt of benefits under
24 fringe benefit programs, as other individuals not so affected but similar in their ability or inability
25 to work.
26 (d) An employer shall provide a pregnant individual with a leave of absence for the period
27 of time that the individual is sick or temporarily disabled because of pregnancy, childbirth, or
28 pregnancy related conditions. Employers must treat an individual on pregnancy related leave the
29 same as other employees on leave for sickness or other temporary disabilities.
30 (e) An employer shall allow a pregnant individual to return to the same job, or a similar
31 job of at least the same pay, if the individual has taken a leave of absence only for the actual
32 period of disability relating to pregnancy, childbirth, or pregnancy related conditions unless the
33 employer can demonstrate business necessity for not doing so.
34 (f) An employer shall provide reasonable accommodations for conditions related to
35 pregnancy, childbirth, or pregnancy related conditions unless the accommodation would impose
36 an undue hardship. Reasonable accommodations may include modified work schedules,
37 modified work assignments, seating or equipment modifications, temporary transfers, or break
38 time and space for expressing breast milk. An employer shall not deny employment opportunities
39 based on the need to provide reasonable accommodations.
40 (g) An employer claiming a BFOQ defense must prove that: (i) the qualification is
41 reasonably necessary to the essence of the business operation; (ii) substantially all individuals
42 excluded by the qualification cannot safely and efficiently perform the essential duties of the job;
43 and (iii) there are no less discriminatory alternatives available to achieve the same business
44 purpose.
45 (h) An individual who claims to be aggrieved by an unlawful discriminatory practice
46 regarding pregnancy, childbirth, or pregnancy related conditions in violation of this section may
47 file a civil action in the General Court of Justice. The action must be filed within three years of
48 the alleged discriminatory act.
49 (i) The court may award back pay, reinstatement, compensatory damages, injunctive
50 relief, and reasonable costs and attorneys' fees in an action brought pursuant to this section."

12 SECTION 5. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to acts or
