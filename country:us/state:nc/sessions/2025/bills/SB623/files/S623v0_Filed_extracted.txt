Section 1:
FILED SENATE
Mar 25, 2025
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
S.B. 623
SESSION 2025
PRINCIPAL CLERK
S D
Short Title: First Responders' Right to Assemble. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Bradley and Mohammed (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROTECT THE LABOR RIGHTS OF FIRST RESPONDERS.
3 Whereas, North Carolina is one of only two states that explicitly prohibit collective
4 bargaining for first responders, thereby denying them a fundamental labor right enjoyed by their
5 counterparts in the majority of states; and
6 Whereas, studies have demonstrated that collaborative labor-management
7 relationships in public safety sectors lead to improved public safety outcomes. The ability to
8 negotiate for better training, equipment, and protocols directly enhances first responder safety
9 and the effectiveness of service delivery; and
10 Whereas, North Carolina faces a critical shortage of first responders as threats to
11 public safety continue to expand in scale and complexity. Collective bargaining helps increase
12 job satisfaction and morale among first responders, which is essential for both recruiting new
13 staff and retaining experienced personnel to respond to current and future crises; and
14 Whereas, the dedication and sacrifice of first responders, who routinely risk their lives
15 to protect the public, merit the provision of collective bargaining rights as a means to uphold the
16 values of safety, quality, and service consistent with North Carolina's commitment to protecting
17 its citizens and supporting those who serve; Now, therefore,
18 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
19 SECTION 1. G.S. 95-98 reads as rewritten:
20 "§ 95-98. Contracts between units of government and labor unions, trade unions or labor
21 organizations concerning public employees declared to be illegal.
22 (a) Any Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, any agreement, or contract,
23 between the governing authority of any city, town, county, or other municipality, or between any
24 agency, unit, or instrumentality thereof, or between any agency, instrumentality, or institution of
25 the State of North Carolina, and any labor union, trade union, or labor organization, as bargaining
26 agent for any public employees of such city, town, county or other municipality, or agency or
27 instrumentality of government, is hereby declared to be against the public policy of the State,
28 illegal, unlawful, void and of no effect.
29 (b) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section, first responders may bargain
30 collectively with the governing authority of any city, town, county, or other municipality. This
31 section does not affect the prohibition on strikes under G.S. 95-98.1."
32 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
*DRS45293-LR-102*
