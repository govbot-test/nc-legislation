GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 123
Short Title: Criminal Falsification of Medical Records. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Pless.
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Judiciary 2, if favorable, Health, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of
the House
February 17, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CREATE THE OFFENSE OF WILLFUL DESTRUCTION, ALTERATION, OR
3 FALSIFICATION OF MEDICAL RECORDS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Article 29 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
6 a new section to read:
7 "§ 90-413. Willful destruction, alteration, or falsification of medical records.
8 (a) Offense. – It is unlawful for a health care provider to knowingly and willfully destroy,
9 alter, or falsify a medical record, or to direct any person to knowingly destroy, alter, or falsify a
10 medical record, for any of the following purposes:
11 (1) Concealing the commission of an error by the health care provider in
12 providing medical services that caused injury to or the death of a patient.
13 (2) Unlawfully obtaining money or any other thing of value.
14 (3) Concealing any material fact not covered under subdivision (1) of this
15 subsection relating to a potential claim or cause of action.
16 (b) Punishment. – Unless the conduct is covered under some other provision of law
17 providing greater punishment, violations of subsection (a) of this section are punishable as
18 follows:
19 (1) A violation of subdivision (1) of subsection (a) of this section is a Class H
20 felony.
21 (2) A violation of subdivision (2) of subsection (a) of this section is a Class I
22 felony.
23 (3) A violation of subdivision (3) of subsection (a) of this section is a Class A1
24 misdemeanor.
25 (c) Civil Remedies. – Nothing in this section shall affect any civil remedies available for
26 actions punishable under this section."
27 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to offenses
28 committed on or after that date.
