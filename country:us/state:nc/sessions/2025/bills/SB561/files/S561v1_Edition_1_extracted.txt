1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT INTEGRATING AND EXPANDING HEALTHCARE, CHILD CARE,
3 NUTRITIONAL ASSISTANCE, AND PREVENTIVE CARE PROGRAMS TO
4 STRENGTHEN SUPPORT FOR WORKING FAMILIES IN NORTH CAROLINA; AND
5 APPROPRIATING FUNDS FOR THESE PURPOSES.
6 Whereas, working families face significant financial and logistical barriers to
7 accessing essential services such as healthcare, child care, and nutrition assistance; and
8 Whereas, existing programs that provide support to working families operate
9 independently, creating inefficiencies and gaps in service delivery for low- and middle-income
10 families; and
11 Whereas, integrating and enhancing the State's family support programs and services
12 will promote economic stability, health equity, and long-term well-being for North Carolina's
13 working families; Now, therefore,
14 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
15
16 PART I. TITLE
17 SECTION 1.1. This act shall be known and may be cited as "The Working Hearts,
18 Empowered Homes Act."
19
20 PART II. DEFINITIONS
21 SECTION 2.1. The following definitions apply in this act:
22 (1) Integrated family support services. – Programs and services administered at
23 the State level that are designed to strengthen families through the provision
24 of State-funded or State-subsidized child care, nutritional assistance,
25 healthcare, or preventive care.
26 (2) Preventive care. – Healthcare services and activities aimed at reducing
27 long-term healthcare costs and improving an individual's overall well-being.
28 (3) Working families. – Households with one or more adults who are engaged in
29 employment but require support services to maintain economic stability.

9 FAMILY SUPPORT REGIONAL HUBS PILOT PROGRAM
10 SECTION 3.2.(a) Program Purpose and Funding. – Effective July 1, 2025, there is
11 appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of Health and Human Services the sum
12 of three million dollars ($3,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal
13 biennium to establish and administer a Regional Hubs Pilot Program (Pilot Program) in up to
14 three different regions of the State. The purpose of this Pilot Program is to evaluate the
15 effectiveness of utilizing regional hubs that are available in person, through the World Wide
16 Web, or through any other means of electronic access to assist working families with gaining
17 access to family support programs and services in one setting. The Pilot Program authorized by
18 this section shall terminate upon the filing of the report required by subsection (b) of this section.
19 SECTION 3.2.(b) Eligible Use of Funds. – The Department may use the funds
20 appropriated by subsection (a) of this section to enter into a contract with a third-party entity to
21 assist with the design, implementation, and operation of these regional hubs.
22 SECTION 3.2.(c) Reporting. – By February 1, 2028, the Department of Health and
23 Human Services shall conduct and submit to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on
24 Health and Human Services and the Fiscal Research Division a comprehensive evaluation of the
25 Pilot Program authorized by this section. The evaluation shall include, at a minimum, all of the
26 following information:
27 (1) A detailed breakdown of Pilot Program expenditures.
28 (2) A description of the design and operation of each regional hub.
29 (3) The number of counties and individuals served by each regional hub.
30 (4) A recommendation as to whether the Pilot Program should be implemented
31 statewide.

35 CHILD CARE INNOVATION PILOT PROGRAM

49 employers that establish or contract for on-site or near-site child care services

31 NUTRITION ASSISTANCE PILOT PROGRAM
32 SECTION 4.2.(a) Program Purpose and Funding. – Effective July 1, 2025, there is
33 appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of Health and Human Services, Division
34 of Child and Family Well-Being (DCFW), the sum of five million dollars ($5,000,000) in
35 nonrecurring funds for each year of the 2025–2027 fiscal biennium to establish the Targeted
36 Food Access Pilot Program (Pilot Program). The purpose of this Pilot Program is to improve
37 food security and nutritional outcomes for working families through targeted, community-based
38 strategies. The Pilot Program authorized by this section shall terminate upon the filing of the
39 report required by subsection (c) of this section.
40 SECTION 4.2.(b) Eligible Use of Funds. – The DCFW shall not use the funds
41 appropriated by subsection (a) of this section for any purpose other than to do one or more of the
42 following:
43 (1) To support produce prescription programs administered in partnership with
44 local health clinics or federally qualified health centers, targeting working
45 families who have at least one family member with a chronic health condition
46 or that is actively engaged in workforce training. For the purpose of this
47 subdivision, a produce prescription program is a program in which healthcare
48 providers prescribe fruits and vegetables to patients at risk for diet-related
49 health problems or food insecurity, enabling access to healthy produce at low
50 or no cost.

24 YEAR-ROUND NUTRITION SERVICE PILOT PROGRAM
25 SECTION 4.3.(a) Program, Purpose. – To the extent funds are made available for
26 the purpose, the Department of Public Instruction shall establish and administer the Year-Round
27 Nutrition Service Pilot Program (Program) in accordance with the provisions of this section. The
28 purpose of the program is to enable select local school administrative units to provide meals to
29 students at no cost to students through the summer.
30 SECTION 4.3.(b) Eligibility. – Any local school administrative unit that is or
31 contains schools that are eligible for and participating in the Community Eligibility Provision
32 Program is eligible to apply for the Program.
33 SECTION 4.3.(c) Application. – The Department of Public Instruction shall develop
34 and release an application for the Program each year. The Department shall notify the local school
35 administrative units selected to participate in the Program by July 15. The Department shall then
36 award grants to all selected local school administrative units.
37 SECTION 4.3.(d) Program Requirements. – Local school administrative units
38 participating in the Program shall do the following:
39 (1) Participate in the National School Lunch Program, School Breakfast Program,
40 and Summer Food Service Program.
41 (2) Provide two meals per day, breakfast and lunch, to students at no cost to the
42 students year-round, regardless of the school's instructional calendar.
43 SECTION 4.3.(e) Report. – No later than January 1 of each year of the Program, the
44 Department shall report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee at least the
45 following information:
46 (1) The selected local school administrative units and the award to each.
47 (2) The number of students served through the Program and the number of meals
48 served.
49 (3) The number of unfunded applications.
50 SECTION 4.3.(f) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
51 Public Instruction the sum of five million dollars ($5,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the

6 PART V. PREVENTIVE HEALTHCARE AND FAMILY WELL-BEING
7
8 PREVENTIVE CARE SERVICES
9 SECTION 5.1. Effective July 1, 2025, there is appropriated from the General Fund
10 to the Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Central Management and Support,
11 Office of Rural Health (ORH), the sum of two million dollars ($2,000,000) in recurring funds for
12 the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to be used to provide grants to local health departments, federally
13 qualified health centers, community health centers, and other safety-net providers to expand
14 telehealth services in up to ten counties to enable working families to participate in virtual
15 preventative care services, including wellness checkups, mental health counseling, and pediatric
16 consultations. In selecting grant recipients, the ORH shall prioritize safety-net providers located
17 in low-wealth counties.

19 PARENTAL HEALTH AND FAMILY SUPPORT SERVICES
20 SECTION 5.2. Effective July 1, 2025, there is appropriated from the General Fund
21 to the Department of Health and Human Services, Division of Child and Family Well-Being, the
22 sum of three million dollars ($3,000,000) in recurring funds for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal
23 biennium to be used to offer employers with 50 or more employees an incentive to offer on-site
24 child care and flexible work hours to accommodate working families.

26 PART VI. FEDERAL MATCHING FUNDS

29 matching funds to supplement State investments in integrated family support services.

31 PART VII. OVERSIGHT AND ACCOUNTABILITY
32 SECTION 7.1.(a) Creation of Family Support Advisory Board. – There is
33 established the Family Support Advisory Board (Advisory Board) within the Department of
34 Health and Human Services. The Advisory Board shall consist of the following nine members:
35 (1) The Director of the Division of Child and Family Well-Being or the Director's
36 designee.
37 (2) The Secretary of the Department of Public Instruction or a designee.
38 (3) Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of
39 the House of Representatives.
40 (4) Two members of the Senate appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the
41 Senate.
42 (5) Three members appointed by the Governor, one of whom shall be a
43 representative of a community-based organization, one of whom shall be an
44 expert in healthcare policy, and one of whom shall be an expert in social
45 services.
46 SECTION 7.1.(b) Chairperson. – A chairperson shall be elected from among the
47 members. The chairperson shall organize and direct the work of the Advisory Board.
48 SECTION 7.1.(c) Administrative Support. – The Department of Health and Human
49 Services shall provide necessary clerical and administrative support to the Advisory Board.
