4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-277.4 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 14-277.4. Obstruction of health care facilities.
7 (a) No person shall obstruct or block another person's access to or egress from a health
8 care facility or from the common areas of the real property upon which the facility is located in
9 a manner that deprives or delays the person from obtaining or providing health care services in
10 the facility.
11 (b) No person shall injure or threaten to injure a person who is or has been:been doing
12 any of the following:
13 (1) Obtaining health care services;services.
14 (2) Lawfully aiding another to obtain health care services; orservices.
15 (3) Providing health care services.
16 (b1) No person shall knowingly approach another person within 8 feet of such person,
17 unless such other person consents, for the purpose of passing a leaflet or handbill to, displaying
18 a sign to, or engaging in oral protest, education, or counseling with such other person in the public
19 way or sidewalk area within a radius of 100 feet from any entrance door to a health care facility.
20 (c) A violation of subsection (a) or (b) of this section is a Class 2 misdemeanor. A second
21 conviction for a violation of either subsection (a) or (b) of this section within three years of the
22 first shall be punishable as a Class 1 misdemeanor. A third second or subsequent conviction for
23 a violation of either subsection (a) or (b) of this section within three years of the second or most
24 recent conviction shall be punishable as a Class I felony.
25 …."
26 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to offenses
27 committed on or after that date.
