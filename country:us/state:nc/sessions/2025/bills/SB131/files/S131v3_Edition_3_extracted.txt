9 TEMPORARILY MODIFY DISTRIBUTION AND USE OF ONE-HALF OF THE NET
10 PROCEEDS FROM THE FIRST ONE CENT OF LOCAL SALES AND USE TAX
11 ALLOCATED TO BUNCOMBE COUNTY
12 SECTION 1.(a) Notwithstanding Section 6 of Chapter 134 of the 1983 Session
13 Laws, as amended by Chapter 534 of the 1983 Session Laws and S.L. 2016-19, directing that
14 one-half of the local government sales and use tax revenue be distributed to the School Capital
15 Fund Commission for Buncombe County (Commission), the Secretary of Revenue shall
16 distribute one hundred percent (100%) of the net proceeds of the local government sales and use
17 tax revenue collected by Buncombe County under Article 39 of Chapter 105 of the General
18 Statutes in accordance with G.S. 105-472 using the ad valorem method of distribution.
19 Notwithstanding the provision in G.S. 105-472 allowing counties to change the method of
20 distribution, Buncombe County is not permitted to change the method of distribution for any year
21 for which the distribution and use provisions of this act apply.
22 SECTION 1.(b) Notwithstanding the use provisions in Sections 9 through 12 of
23 Chapter 134 of the 1983 Session Laws, as amended by Chapter 534 of the 1983 Session Laws
24 and S.L. 2016-19, and applicable to the funds distributed to the Commission, Buncombe County
25 shall use the net proceeds of the local government sales and use tax revenue collected under
26 Article 39 of Chapter 105 of the General Statutes and allocated to Buncombe County under
27 subsection (a) of this section as follows:
28 (1) Fifty percent (50%) for school capital outlay purposes, as defined in
29 G.S. 115C-426(f), or to retire any indebtedness incurred by the County for
30 these purposes, and for school operating expenses. The Commission shall
31 serve in an advisory capacity to the Buncombe County Board of County
32 Commissioners with respect to the use of funds under this subdivision for
33 school capital outlay purposes by considering the capital needs of both the
34 Buncombe County School System and the Asheville City School System,
35 prioritizing those needs, and recommending projects to be funded. However,

9 MAKE TECHNICAL CHANGES TO BUNCOMBE SCHOOL CAPITAL FUND
10 COMMISSION
11 SECTION 2.(a) Chapter 134 of the 1983 Session Laws, as amended by Chapter 534
12 of the 1983 Session Laws and S.L. 2016-19, is rewritten and recodified as subsections (b) through
13 (m) of this section.
14 SECTION 2.(b) There is hereby created a Commission to be known as the School
15 Capital Fund Commission for Buncombe County, which Commission shall have and possess all
16 the powers conferred by this act and such other powers as may be useful or necessary to permit
17 it fully to carry out the purposes of this act. The Commission is hereby declared to be a
18 corporation with perpetual succession, the right to adopt and use a corporate seal, to sue and to
19 be sued, and to hold, manage and control the moneys and properties received by it under the
20 provisions of this act. It shall have the right to make reasonable rules and regulations for the
21 conduct of its business.
22 SECTION 2.(c) The Commission shall consist of five members. Members of the
23 Commission shall serve two-year terms and shall be appointed as follows: one appointed by the
24 Asheville City Board of Education, one appointed by the Buncombe County Board of Education,
25 two appointed by the Buncombe County Board of Commissioners, and one appointed by the
26 other four appointees. A member appointed by a local governing board or a school board shall
27 serve at the pleasure of that board. The member appointed by the other appointees shall serve at
28 the pleasure of the majority of the other appointees. Any vacancy shall be filled by the appointing
29 authority of the member creating the vacancy to fill the unexpired term.
30 SECTION 2.(d) A quorum shall be three members of the Commission.
31 SECTION 2.(e) The Commission shall organize, adopt bylaws or rules and
32 regulations to govern its procedure and the conduct of the business and affairs of the Commission
33 and appoint a chairman and vice-chairman from among its membership who shall serve a
34 one-year term and appoint a secretary for a one-year term and until their successors are appointed
35 by the Commission. The finance officer for Buncombe County shall be the finance officer of the
36 Commission. The county finance officer shall give bond for the faithful performance of his
37 duties as the finance officer of the Commission in an amount determined by the Commission.
38 The finance officer of the Commission shall manage the funds of the Commission only as
39 directed by the Commission. The Commission may select and appoint an attorney of the
40 Commission to serve at the pleasure of the Commission. The Commission may fix the
41 compensation for the attorney.
42 SECTION 2.(f) Regular or stated meetings of the Commission shall be held at such
43 time and place as may be provided by the Commission in its bylaws or rules and regulations, and
44 special meetings may be held on the call of the chairman after due notice.
45 SECTION 2.(g) One-half of any local government sales and use tax revenue
46 distributed to Buncombe County under G.S. 105-472 shall be paid to the Commission by the
47 Secretary of Revenue; provided that if distribution of the local government sales and use tax
48 revenue is made under G.S. 105-472(2), (the ad valorem method), then taxing districts shall
49 receive their funds under G.S. 105-472(2) as if no funds had been paid to the Commission by the
50 Secretary of Revenue, and in computing what amount is one-half of the sales and use tax revenue,
51 the Secretary of Revenue shall not reduce the total by the amount to be distributed to the taxing

1 districts. This section does not affect the distribution of any local or State sales and use tax
2 revenue to the municipalities in Buncombe County.
3 SECTION 2.(h) Any other capital funds appropriated by Buncombe County
4 pursuant to Article 40 and Article 42 of Chapter 105 of the General Statutes shall be apportioned
5 among the Asheville City Board of Education and the Buncombe County Board of Education
6 according to the membership of each unit using the process set forth in G.S. 115C-430.
7 Buncombe County shall maintain separate internal accounts for each school board in order to
8 comply with this section. These funds are not under the control of the School Capital Fund
9 Commission.
10 SECTION 2.(i) All funds received by the Commission under subsection (g) of this
11 section shall be placed in a capital reserve fund as provided in Part 2 of Article 3 of Chapter 159
12 of the General Statutes. The capital reserve fund shall be known as the Public School Capital
13 Needs Fund.
14 SECTION 2.(j) All funds in the Public School Capital Needs Fund shall be used to
15 finance new public school construction, to finance public school improvement and renovation
16 projects that exceed one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000), or to retire any indebtedness
17 incurred by the county for these purposes.
18 The Commission shall consider the capital needs of both the Buncombe County
19 School System and the Asheville City School System, prioritize those needs, and recommend
20 projects to be funded from the Public School Capital Needs Fund to the board of county
21 commissioners based on the priority of needs determined.
22 By joint agreement of the Board of County Commissioners and both boards of
23 education, money may be transferred from the account under this section of one board of
24 education to the account under this section of the other board of education, provided that the
25 agreement must require a transfer back of an equal amount of funds at some fixed date in the
26 future, plus a sum to be determined at that date which represents interest which would have
27 accrued on the funds if they had remained in the first account.
28 SECTION 2.(k) Moneys in the Public School Capital Needs Fund shall be subject
29 to appropriation by the board of county commissioners. The Commission shall disburse such
30 moneys as a ministerial duty upon receiving a written request from the board of county
31 commissioners after the county board of commissioners has adopted an ordinance and after
32 receipt of a written request from the appropriate board of education indicating it is prepared to
33 enter into a contract, and G.S. 115C-521 shall continue to apply.
34 SECTION 2.(l) Notwithstanding any other provision of this act, if the Buncombe
35 County Board of Commissioners appropriates any federal revenue sharing funds to the
36 Commission for the purpose of substituting revenue sharing funds for sales tax funds, then it
37 must at the same time appropriate from the Commission to the county an equal amount of funds
38 received under subsection (g) of this section to be used for such purposes as the Board of
39 Commissioners shall deem appropriate and are otherwise authorized by law, provided that such
40 federal revenue sharing funds shall be divided pro rata under subsection (j) of this section.
41 SECTION 2.(m) The reasonable and necessary expenses of the Commission,
42 including the compensation of its officers and employees and the cost of any bond required by
43 it, shall be paid by the County of Buncombe. The chairman of the Commission shall, on or about
44 the first day of each calendar month, certify to the governing body of Buncombe County the
45 expenses of the Commission incurred during the preceding month, and the governing body of the
46 county shall forthwith, and within five days thereafter, cause to be paid to the Commission the
47 expenses required to be paid. All such payments shall be charged to the general fund of the
48 county.
