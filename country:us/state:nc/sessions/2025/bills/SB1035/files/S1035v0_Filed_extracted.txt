13 SECTION 3. The State Board of Education shall adopt rules for pathway-aligned
14 sequences of mathematics courses to be offered in high schools in the State by July 1, 2027. The
15 State Board shall consult the Board of Governors of the University of North Carolina and the
16 State Board of Community Colleges to ensure that all pathways align with the University of
17 North Carolina minimum admission standards, North Carolina Community Colleges System
18 expectations, and workforce and industry needs. Pathway-aligned sequences of courses adopted
19 pursuant to the requirements of this section shall not apply to students who have been identified
20 as a child with a disability in accordance with Article 9 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes
21 and whose IEP team has determined cannot satisfy the standard state graduation requirements.
22 The State Board shall adopt the following pathway-aligned sequences of mathematics courses:
23 (1) A college preparatory pathway designed for students planning to attend a
24 four-year college or university that includes NC Math 3 and another high level
25 mathematics course, such as the following:
26 a. Precalculus.
27 b. Advanced functions.
28 c. Advanced Placement, International Baccalaureate, or Cambridge
29 Advanced International Certificate of Education level mathematics
30 courses.
31 d. Equivalent courses as determined by the State Board.
32 (2) A career and technical education (CTE) pathway designed for students
33 planning to pursue workforce or technical careers that includes the following:
34 a. Applied math or integrated mathematics courses aligned to CTE
35 programs.
36 b. Courses that incorporate algebra, statistics, and quantitative reasoning
37 in real-world contexts.
38 (3) A quantitative reasoning and statistics pathway designed for students planning
39 to pursue careers in fields that require data literacy and applied mathematics
40 that includes the following:
41 a. NC Math 3 or an equivalent course approved by the State Board.
42 b. Statistics, data science, or quantitative reasoning courses.
43 (4) A science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) pathway designed for
44 students planning to pursue careers in STEM fields that includes NC Math 3
45 and another high level mathematics course, such as the following:
46 a. Precalculus.
47 b. Calculus.
48 c. Other advanced mathematics courses approved by the State Board.
