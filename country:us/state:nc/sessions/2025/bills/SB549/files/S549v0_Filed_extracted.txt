1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT PROVIDING STATE EMPLOYEES WITH PAID LEAVE DESIGNATED FOR
3 BEREAVEMENT AND FOLLOWING A PREGNANCY LOSS AND APPROPRIATING
4 FUNDS FOR THOSE PURPOSES.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) Effective July 1, 2025, Article 2 of Chapter 126 of the General
7 Statutes is amended by adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 126-8.7. Paid leave for pregnancy loss.
9 (a) The State Human Resources Commission (Commission) shall adopt rules and policies
10 to provide that a permanent, probationary, or time-limited full-time State employee shall be
11 granted paid leave for pregnancy loss. For the purposes of this section, the term "pregnancy loss"
12 means a miscarriage, an unsuccessful round of intrauterine insemination or of an assisted
13 reproductive technology procedure, a failed adoption arrangement, a failed surrogacy
14 arrangement, or a diagnosis or event that impacts pregnancy or fertility.
15 (b) The Commission shall adopt rules and policies to provide that a permanent,
16 probationary, or time-limited part-time State employee shall be granted a prorated amount of
17 paid leave for pregnancy loss on an equitable basis.
18 (c) The rules and policies adopted by the Commission shall include the following
19 requirements, at the minimum:
20 (1) That the duration of leave for pregnancy loss shall be not less than 56 hours
21 of paid leave.
22 (2) That an employee requesting the paid leave shall submit a request for leave to
23 process and address their own health needs and the health needs of their
24 partners during the time period following a pregnancy loss.
25 (3) A period of minimum service before an employee becomes eligible for paid
26 leave under this section. The rules shall provide that the period of minimum
27 service may be met by aggregating employment at any of the following:
28 a. State agencies, departments, and institutions, including The University
29 of North Carolina.
30 b. Public school units that provide paid leave in accordance with this
31 section.
32 c. Community colleges located in this State.
33 (d) The leave authorized by this section:
34 (1) Is available without exhaustion of the employee's sick and vacation leave and
35 is awarded in addition to shared leave under G.S. 126-8.3, or other leave
36 authorized by State or federal law.

2 (3) May not be used for calculating an employee's retirement benefits.

8 SECTION 1.(b) Effective July 1, 2025, G.S. 126-5(c19) reads as rewritten:
9 "(c19) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Chapter, G.S. 126-8.6 applies and

16 ($1,800,000) for the 2025-2026 fiscal year and the sum of one million eight hundred thousand
17 dollars ($1,800,000) for the 2025-2027 fiscal year to fund paid leave for pregnancy loss as
18 enacted in this section.
19 SECTION 1.(d) This section becomes effective July 1, 2025, and applies to requests
20 for pregnancy loss leave occurring on or after that date.
21 SECTION 2.(a) Effective July 1, 2025, Article 2 of Chapter 126 of the General
22 Statutes is amended by adding a new section to read:
23 "§ 126-8.8. Paid bereavement leave for death of immediate family member.
24 (a) The State Human Resources Commission (Commission) shall adopt rules and policies
25 to provide that a permanent, probationary, or time-limited full-time State employee shall be
26 granted up to three consecutive workdays of paid bereavement leave on the death of any member
27 of the employee's immediate family. For the purposes of this section, "immediate family
28 member" means a spouse, parent, child, brother, sister, grandparent, or grandchild. The term
29 includes the step, half, and in-law relationships.
30 (b) The Commission shall adopt rules and policies to provide that a permanent,
31 probationary, or time-limited part-time State employee shall be granted a prorated amount of
32 paid bereavement leave on an equitable basis.
33 (c) The rules and policies adopted by the Commission shall include the following
34 requirements, at a minimum:
35 (1) That an employee requesting paid bereavement leave submit a statement
36 stating the name and relationship of the deceased.
37 (2) A period of minimum service before an employee becomes eligible for paid
38 bereavement leave under this section. The rules shall provide that the period
39 of minimum service may be met by aggregating employment at any of the
40 following:
41 a. State agencies, departments, and institutions, including The University
42 of North Carolina.
43 b. Public school units that provide paid bereavement leave in accordance
44 with this section.
45 c. Community colleges located in this State.
46 (d) The paid bereavement leave authorized by this section:
47 (1) Is available without exhaustion of the employee's sick and vacation leave and
48 is awarded in addition to shared leave under G.S. 126-8.3, or other leave
49 authorized by State or federal law.
50 (2) Has no cash value upon termination from employment.
51 (3) May not be used for calculating an employee's retirement benefits.
