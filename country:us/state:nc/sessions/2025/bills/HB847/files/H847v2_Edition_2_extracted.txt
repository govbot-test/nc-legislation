Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/30/25
Short Title: Off-Road Motorcycle Registration Eligibility. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
April 10, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CLARIFY OFF-ROAD MOTORCYCLE REGISTRATION ELIGIBILITY AS
3 MODIFIED UTILITY VEHICLES.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 20-4.01 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 20-4.01. Definitions.
7 Unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply throughout this
8 Chapter to the defined words and phrases and their cognates:
9 …
10 (27) Passenger Vehicles. –
11 …
12 g2. Modified utility vehicle. – A motor vehicle that (i) is meets the
13 configurations enumerated in sub-sub-subdivisions 1. and 2. of this
14 sub-subdivision. "Modified utility vehicle" does not include an
15 all-terrain vehicle, golf cart, or utility vehicle, as defined in this
16 section, or a riding lawn mower.
17 1. A motor vehicle that is manufactured or upfitted by a licensed
18 manufacturer, dealer, or person or business otherwise engaged
19 in vehicle manufacturing or modification for off-road use with
20 equipment required by G.S. 20-121.1(2a), except a vehicle
21 identification number, and (ii) (i) has four wheels, an overall
22 length of 110 inches or greater, an overall width of 58 inches
23 or greater, an overall height of 60 inches or greater, a (ii) has a
24 maximum speed capability of 40 miles per hour or greater, and
25 (iii) does not require an operator or passenger to straddle a seat.
26 "Modified utility vehicle" does not include an all-terrain
27 vehicle, golf cart, or utility vehicle, as defined in this section,
28 or a riding lawn mower.
29 2. A motor vehicle that is manufactured or upfitted by a licensed
30 manufacturer, dealer, or person or business otherwise engaged
31 in vehicle manufacturing or modification for off-road use with
32 equipment required by G.S. 20-121.1(2b), except a vehicle
33 identification number, and (i) has an engine displacement of
34 124cc or more, (ii) has a maximum speed capability of 40 miles
35 per hour or greater, (iii) has a saddle for the use of the rider and
*H847-v-2*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 passengers, and (iv) is designed to travel on two wheels in
2 contact with the ground.
3 h. Motorcycles. – Vehicles having a saddle for the use of the rider and
4 designed to travel on not more than three wheels in contact with the
5 ground, including autocycles, motor scooters, and motor-driven
6 bicycles, but excluding excludes tractors and utility vehicles equipped
7 with an additional form of device designed to transport property,
8 three-wheeled vehicles while being used by law-enforcement
9 agencies, electric assisted bicycles, and mopeds as defined in
10 sub-subdivision d1. of this subdivision.
11 …."
12 SECTION 2. G.S. 20-121.1 reads as rewritten:
13 "§ 20-121.1. Operation of a low-speed vehicle, mini-truck, or modified utility vehicle on
14 certain roadways.
15 The operation of a low-speed vehicle, mini-truck, or modified utility vehicle is authorized
16 with the following restrictions:
17 (1) A low-speed vehicle may be operated only on streets and highways where the
18 posted speed limit is 35 miles per hour or less. A mini-truck or modified utility
19 vehicle may be operated only on streets and highways where the posted speed
20 limit is 55 miles per hour or less. This subdivision does not prohibit a
21 low-speed vehicle, mini-truck, or modified utility vehicle from crossing a road
22 or street at an intersection where the road or street being crossed has a posted
23 speed limit of more than 35 miles per hour.
24 (2) A low-speed vehicle or mini-truck shall be equipped with headlamps, stop
25 lamps, turn signal lamps, tail lamps, reflex reflectors, parking brakes, rearview
26 mirrors, windshields, windshield wipers, speedometer, seat belts, and a
27 vehicle identification number. Any such required equipment shall be
28 maintained in proper working order.
29 (2a) A modified utility vehicle meeting the configuration specified in
30 G.S. 20-4.01(27)g2.1. shall be equipped with headlamps, stop lamps, turn
31 signal lamps, tail lamps, reflex reflectors, parking brakes, rearview mirrors, a
32 speedometer, seat belts, and a vehicle identification number. Any such
33 required equipment shall be maintained in proper working order. If a modified
34 utility the vehicle does not have a vehicle identification number, upon
35 application by the owner, the Division shall assign a vehicle identification
36 number to the modified utility vehicle prior to registration. The operator of
37 and all passengers on a modified utility vehicle that is not equipped with a
38 windshield and windshield wipers shall wear a safety helmet, with a retention
39 strap properly secured, that complies with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety
40 Standard (FMVSS) 218.
41 (2b) A modified utility vehicle meeting the configuration specified in
42 G.S. 20-4.01(27)g2.2. shall be equipped with equipment required by this
43 Chapter for the highway use of motorcycles and a vehicle identification
44 number. Any such required equipment shall be maintained in proper working
45 order. If the vehicle does not have a vehicle identification number, upon
46 application by the owner, the Division shall assign a vehicle identification
47 number to the vehicle prior to registration. The operator of the vehicle must
48 be licensed to operate a motorcycle as required by G.S. 20-7. The special
49 provisions for motorcycles under G.S. 20-140.4 apply to the operator and
50 passengers of the vehicle.

1 (3) A low-speed vehicle, mini-truck, or modified utility vehicle shall be registered
2 and insured in accordance with G.S. 20-50 and G.S. 20-309.
3 (4) Notwithstanding the provisions of any other subdivision of this section, the
4 Department of Transportation may prohibit the operation of low-speed
5 vehicles, mini-trucks, or modified utility vehicles on any road or highway if it
6 determines that the prohibition is necessary in the interest of safety.
7 (5) Low-speed vehicles must comply with the safety standards in 49 C.F.R. §
8 571.500.
9 (6) Regardless of age, a mini-truck shall not qualify as an antique vehicle or
10 historic vehicle as described in G.S. 20-79.4(b)."
11 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025.
