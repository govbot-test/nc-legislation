Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Update Register of Deeds' Supp. Pension Fund. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Hanig and Ford (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
February 27, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO UPDATE THE SUPPLEMENTAL PENSION PROVIDED TO REGISTERS OF
3 DEEDS BY PROVIDING A FLAT RATE MONTHLY SUPPLEMENTAL PENSION
4 AMOUNT TO ALL ELIGIBLE RETIRED REGISTERS OF DEEDS AND TO MAKE
5 TECHNICAL AND CONFORMING CHANGES TO THE RELATED STATUTES.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. Article 3 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes reads as rewritten:
8 "Article 3.
9 "Register of Deeds' Supplemental Pension Fund Act of 1987.
10 …
11 "§ 161-50.3. Disbursements.
12 (a) Immediately following July 1, 1988, the Department of State Treasurer shall divide
13 an amount equal to forty-five percent (45%) of the assets of the Fund at the end of the preceding
14 fiscal year into equal shares and disburse the same as monthly pension payments to all eligible
15 retired registers of deeds as of July 1, 1988, payable in accordance with the method described in
16 G.S. 161-50.5, except that such pension benefit shall be computed for a six-months basis
17 beginning with the month of July, 1988.
18 (b) Immediately following January 1, 1996, and the first of January of each succeeding
19 calendar year thereafter, the Department of State Treasurer shall divide an amount equal to
20 ninety-three percent (93%) of the assets of the Fund at the end of the preceding calendar year
21 into equal shares and disburse the same as monthly payments in accordance with the provisions
22 of this Article.
23 (c) The remaining seven percent (7%) of the Fund's assets as of December 31, 1995, and
24 at the end of each calendar year thereafter, may be used by the Department of State Treasurer in
25 administering the provisions of this Article.
26 (d) All the Fund's disbursements shall be conducted in the same manner as disbursements
27 are conducted for other special funds of the State.
28 (e) If, for any reason, the Fund shall be insufficient to pay any pension benefits or other
29 charges, then all benefits or payments shall be reduced paid pro rata for as long as the deficiency
30 in amount exists. No claim shall accrue with respect to any amount by which a pension payment
31 shall have been reduced. If a pro rata payment is required under this subsection, then the payment
32 made to each individual register of deeds eligible under G.S. 161-50.4 shall be calculated by first
33 dividing the total number of years of eligible service as a register of deeds for all registers of
34 deeds eligible under G.S. 161-50.4 on December 31 of the previous calendar year into the total
35 amount available to the Pension Fund to cover the monthly pension payments under this Article
36 and then second, by multiplying that amount by the total number of years of eligible service for
*S193-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 each individual register of deeds eligible under G.S. 161-50.4. This second total shall be the
2 amount the applicable eligible register of deeds is entitled to until either (i) the Pension Fund is
3 sufficient to cover the full amount of the monthly pension payments or (ii) the next calendar year
4 if the Pension Fund remains insufficient, at which time the monthly pro rata payment made to
5 each eligible register of deeds shall be recalculated.
6 "§ 161-50.4. Eligibility.
7 (a) Each county register of deeds who has retired with at least 12 years eligible service
8 as register of deeds from the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement System or an
9 equivalent locally sponsored plan before June 30, 1988, and those who retire on or after June 30,
10 1988, but before July 1, 1991, and who have completed at least 12 years of eligible service as
11 register of deeds is entitled to receive a monthly pension under this Article, beginning July 1,
12 1988. Effective July 1, 1991, each county register of deeds who retires with at least 10 years of
13 eligible service as register of deeds is entitled to receive a monthly pension under this Article.
14 (a1) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (a) of this section, effective January 1,
15 1996, any county register of deeds who separates from service as register of deeds after
16 completing at least 10 years of eligible service as register of deeds, but who does not commence
17 retirement with the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement System, shall have the right to
18 receive a monthly pension under this Article payable upon retirement with the Local
19 Governmental Employees' Retirement System.
20 (a2) Each county register of deeds who is not eligible to retire with the Local
21 Governmental Employees' Retirement System solely because the county has not elected to
22 participate as an employer with the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement System and who
23 has either (i) attained the age of 65, (ii) attained 30 years of creditable service regardless of age,
24 or (iii) attained the age of 60 with not less than 25 years of creditable service, and who has
25 completed at least 10 years of creditable service as a register of deeds is entitled to receive a
26 monthly pension under this Article, provided that register of deeds is not eligible to receive any
27 retirement benefits from any State or locally sponsored plan.
28 (b) Each eligible retired register of deeds as defined in subsection (a), (a1), or (a2) of this
29 section relating to service and retirement status shall be entitled to Eligibility to receive a monthly
30 pension under this Article beginning begins with the month of retirement.
31 (c) A county register of deeds who is otherwise eligible to receive a monthly pension
32 under this Article shall cease to be eligible upon forfeiture of any retirement benefits under
33 G.S. 128-38.4 or G.S. 128-38.4A.
34 "§ 161-50.5. Benefits.
35 (a) An eligible Prior to October 1, 2025, a retired register of deeds eligible under
36 G.S. 161-50.4(a) or G.S. 161-50.4(a1) shall be entitled to receive an annual pension benefit,
37 payable in equal monthly installments, equal to one share for each full year of eligible service as
38 register of deeds multiplied by his total number of years of eligible service. service of the retired
39 register of deeds. The amount of each share shall be determined by dividing the total number of
40 years of eligible service for all eligible retired registers of deeds on December 31 of each calendar
41 year into the amount to be disbursed as monthly pension payments in accordance with the
42 provisions of G.S. 161-50.3. In no event, however, shall a monthly pension under this Article
43 exceed an amount which, when added to a retirement allowance under the maximum allowance
44 at retirement from the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement System or an equivalent
45 locally sponsored plan, is greater than seventy-five percent (75%) of a register of deed's deeds'
46 equivalent annual salary immediately preceding retirement computed on the latest monthly rate,
47 including any and all supplements, to a maximum amount of one thousand five hundred dollars
48 ($1,500).
49 (a1) A register of deeds eligible under G.S. 161-50.4(a2) shall be entitled to receive an
50 annual pension benefit, payable in equal monthly installments as determined under the provisions
51 of subsection (a) of this section, but reduced by an amount equal to the benefit that would be
Page 2 Senate Bill 193-First Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 payable from the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement System if the register of deeds had
2 been a member of the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement System and all of the years
3 of local service were creditable to that System.
4 (a2) On and after October 1, 2025, a register of deeds eligible under G.S. 161-50.4 shall
5 be entitled to receive an annual pension benefit, payable in equal monthly installments of one
6 thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500).
7 (b) All monthly pensions payable under this Article shall be paid on the same business
8 day of each month that benefits are paid from the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement
9 System.
10 (c) Monthly pensions payable under this Article shall cease at the death of the pensioner
11 and no payment will be made to any beneficiaries or to the decedent's estate.
12 (d) Monthly pensions payable under this Article will cease upon the full-time
13 reemployment of a pensioner with an employer participating in the Local Governmental
14 Employees' Retirement System for as long as the pensioner is so reemployed.
15 (d1) Monthly pensions payable under this Article will cease upon the ineligibility of a
16 pensioner under G.S. 161-50.4(c) due to the forfeiture of any retirement benefits under
17 G.S. 128-38.4 or G.S. 128-38.4A.
18 …
19 (f) Nothing contained in this Article shall preclude or in any way affect the benefits that
20 a pensioner may be entitled to from any state, federal or private pension, retirement or other
21 deferred compensation plan.
22 "§ 161-50.10. Cessation of benefits under this Article.
23 (a) Monthly pensions payable under this Article cease at the death of the individual
24 eligible for or in receipt of benefits under this Article and no payment will be made to any
25 beneficiaries or to the decedent's estate.
26 (b) Monthly pensions payable under this Article cease upon an individual receiving
27 benefits under this Article entering into full-time employment with an employer participating in
28 the Local Governmental Employees' Retirement System for as long as that full-time employment
29 continues.
30 (c) Monthly pensions payable under this Article cease upon the forfeiture of any
31 retirement benefits under G.S. 128-38.4 or G.S. 128-38.4A.
32 "§ 161-50.15. Affect of Article on other benefits.
33 No provision of this Article shall preclude or in any way affect the benefits that an individual
34 receiving benefits under this Article may be entitled to from any state, federal or private pension,
35 retirement plan, or other deferred compensation plan."
36 SECTION 2.(a) This act applies to monthly supplemental pension benefits under
37 Article 3 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes payable to eligible retired registers of deeds on
38 or after October 1, 2025. If any pension benefit under Article 3 of Chapter 161 of the General
39 Statutes is payable for any month prior to October 1, 2025, but is paid on or after that date, then
40 the amount paid shall be the amount that was in effect for the applicable month.
41 SECTION 2.(b) This act is effective October 1, 2025.
