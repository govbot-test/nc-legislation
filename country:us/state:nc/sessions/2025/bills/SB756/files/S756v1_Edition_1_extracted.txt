3 SURVEYORS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 89C-19.2, as enacted in S.L. 2023-142, is recodified as
6 G.S. 14-159.15.
7 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 14-159.15, as recodified by subsection (a) of this section, reads
8 as rewritten:
9 "§ 14-159.15. Limited right of entry by professional land surveyors.
10 (a) For the purposes of this section, the following terms are defined as follows:
11 (1) Critical infrastructure. – As defined in 42 U.S.C. § 5195c.
12 (2) Critical infrastructure facilities. – Critical infrastructure that is completely
13 enclosed by a fence or other physical barriers that is obviously designed to
14 exclude intruders or clearly marked with a sign or signs posted on the property
15 that are reasonably likely to come to the attention of a person and indicate that
16 entry is forbidden without site authorization.
17 (3) Landowner. – The owner, lessee, or occupant of a parcel of land.
18 (4) Professional land surveyor. – As defined in G.S. 89C-3. For purposes of this
19 section, this term includes any agents, employees, or personnel under the
20 supervision of a professional land surveyor.
21 (a)(a1) A professional land surveyor licensed under this Chapter shall have the right to
22 enter upon the lands of others, if necessary to perform surveys for the practice of land surveying,
23 including the location of property corners, boundary lines, rights-of-way, and easements, and
24 may carry with them their customary equipment and vehicles. Whenever practicable, a
25 professional land surveyor shall notify a landowner of entry onto his or her land. An entry by a
26 professional land surveyor to perform the practice of land surveying under this section shall not
27 constitute trespass under Article 22A or 22B of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes or shall not
28 cause the professional land surveyor to be subject to arrest or a civil action by reason of the entry.
29 (b) Nothing in this section shall be construed as giving authority to a professional land
30 surveyor to destroy, injure, damage, or move anything on the lands of another without the written
31 permission of the landowner, and nothing in this section shall be construed as removing civil
32 liability for such damage.
33 (c) A professional land surveyor shall make reasonable efforts to notify adjoining
34 landowners upon whose land it is necessary to enter.
35 (d) No professional land surveyor or person under such surveyor's supervision shall have
36 a civil cause of action against a landowner or lessee for personal injury or property damage

18 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 89C-2 reads as rewritten:
19 "§ 89C-2. Declarations; prohibitions.
20 (a) In order to safeguard life, health, and property, and to promote the public welfare, the
21 practice of engineering and the practice of land surveying in this State are hereby declared to be
22 subject to regulation in the public interest. It shall be unlawful for any person to practice or to
23 offer to practice engineering or land surveying in this State, as defined in the provisions of this
24 Chapter, or to use in connection with the person's name or otherwise assume or advertise any
25 title or description tending to convey the impression that the person is either a professional
26 engineer or a professional land surveyor, unless the person has been duly licensed. The right to
27 engage in the practice of engineering or land surveying is a personal right, based on the
28 qualifications of the person as evidenced by the person's certificate of licensure, which shall not
29 be transferable.
30 (b) A professional land surveyor licensed under this Chapter has a limited right of entry
31 onto real property in accordance with G.S. 14-159.15."
32 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to acts on or after
33 that date.
