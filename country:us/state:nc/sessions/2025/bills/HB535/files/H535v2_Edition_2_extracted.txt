36 SECTION 2.1. Article 2 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes is amended by
37 adding a new section to read:
38 "§ 161-32. Identity verification and suspicious instruments.
39 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
40 (1) Instrument. – The term "instrument" shall include any of the following:
41 a. A transfer of title to real property by deed or devise or other instrument
42 transferring title to real property.
43 b. A deed of trust, mortgage, judgment, lien, encumbrance, financing
44 statement, affidavit, notice, memorandum, or any other instrument that
45 establishes a security interest in real property.
46 c. A contract, right of refusal, lease, affidavit, memorandum, or any other
47 instrument that purports to establish an interest, option, encumbrance,
48 right, or any other claim relating to or interest in real property.
49 (2) Suspicious instrument. – An instrument submitted for registration for which
50 the register of deeds has found any of the following:

5 c. The instrument is submitted by an individual who is not a trusted
6 submitter and the identity verification requirements of subsection (b)
7 of this section are not met.
8 (3) Trusted submitter. – Includes all of the following:
9 a. A title insurance company as described in Article 26 of Chapter 28 of

12 c. A financial institution as defined in G.S. 53B-2 or their authorized

15 Chapter, when an instrument is presented to the register of deeds for registration by an individual

18 (1) If an individual who is not a trusted submitter presents an instrument to the

20 the individual to produce a government-issued photographic identification

22 The register of deeds must record the name, address, identification card
23 number, and expiration date as the information appears on the identification
24 card, in a record to be kept by the register of deeds, along with the official

28 public website but shall be made available for public inspection and copying

31 register of deeds by U.S. mail, overnight delivery, or other paid third-party
32 delivery service, the register of deeds shall require the individual to submit a
33 photocopy of his or her government-issued photographic identification card

35 photocopy of the identification card the official records book and page number
36 or instrument number assigned to the instrument registered in connection to
37 the submission of the photocopy of the identification card and retain the
38 photocopy of such identification card in a record to be kept by the register of
39 deeds. Such a record may not be made available for viewing on the register of
40 deeds' official public website but shall be made available for public inspection

42 person who submits a photocopy of his or her government-issued
43 photographic identification card under this subsection may redact from the
44 photocopy of such identification card before submission all of the information
45 he or she does not wish to be made public, except for his or her name, address,
46 photograph, identification card number, and expiration date.

48 presents an instrument in the form of an electronic document, as defined in

39 SECTION 3.1. The Legislative Research Commission (LRC) shall study the
40 feasibility of creating a fraud detection alert system where everyone who submits an instrument
41 as defined in G.S. 161-32 for registration is automatically enrolled in the fraud detection alert
42 system without paying a fee. Before enrollment, the register of deeds would provide each
43 individual with a notice explaining the system – its purpose, how it works, and how to opt out of
44 enrollment.
45 As part of its study, the LRC shall do all of the following:
46 (1) Evaluate the practicality of implementing a fraud detection alert system with
47 automatic enrollment and an opt-out option, including technological and
48 staffing requirements for registers of deeds across all counties.
49 (2) Examine the fiscal impact on the State, counties, and/or registers of deeds,
50 including costs associated with developing, maintaining, and operating a fraud
51 detection alert system without charging a fee to the participant.

6 SECTION 4.1. Article 1 of Chapter 41 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
7 a new section to read:
8 "§ 41-10.2. Fraudulent instrument procedure.
9 (a) As used in this section, the term "instrument" means any of the following:
10 (1) A transfer of title to real property by deed or devise or other instrument
11 transferring title to real property.
12 (2) A deed of trust, mortgage, judgment, lien, encumbrance, financing statement,
13 affidavit, notice, memorandum, or any other instrument that establishes a
14 security interest in real property.
15 (3) A contract, right of refusal, lease, affidavit, memorandum, or any other
16 instrument that purports to establish an interest, option, encumbrance, right,
17 or any other claim relating to or interest in real property.
18 (b) The actual owner of an interest in real property subject to a recorded false, fictitious,
19 or fraudulent instrument may file a civil action and proceed pro se, without the assistance of legal
20 counsel. The district court division of the General Court of Justice shall have original jurisdiction
21 over actions instituted under this section, and a party may seek emergency relief under this
22 section by filing a civil action in district court alleging the filing or recording of a false, fictitious,
23 or fraudulent instrument in a public record or a private record generally available to the public.
24 Upon the filing of any action under this section, the plaintiff shall file a notice of lis pendens.
25 Required service of process or notice required by Rules 4 and 5 of the North Carolina Rules of
26 Civil Procedure on any party shall be sufficient if made to the party's address reflected on the
27 face of the suspicious instrument, the address for mailing tax bills for the parcel of land, or
28 reflected on the summons or other court filings. If an ex parte hearing is requested, and, upon the
29 submission of a sworn statement and evidence supporting the allegation of the recordation of a
30 false, fictitious, or fraudulent instrument, a temporary order, declaring the recording to be
31 fraudulent, shall be entered if the court finds that there is no statutory, contractual, or other legal
32 basis for the alleged false, fictitious, or fraudulent recording. A temporary order entered under
33 this subsection shall remain in effect for the later of 60 days or until the entry of a permanent
34 order. The chief district court judge may authorize a magistrate or magistrates to hear any motions
35 for emergency relief ex parte under this section. The clerk of superior court shall schedule an ex
36 parte hearing with the district court division of the General Court of Justice within 72 hours of
37 the filing for said relief, or by the end of the next day on which the district court is in session in
38 the county in which the action was filed, whichever shall first occur. If the district court is not in
39 session in said county, the party may contact the clerk of superior court in any other county within
40 the same judicial district who shall schedule an ex parte hearing with the district court division
41 of the General Court of Justice by the end of the next day on which said court division is in
42 session in that county. Upon the issuance of an ex parte order under this subsection, a hearing
43 shall be held within 10 calendar days from the date of issuance of the order or within seven
44 calendar days from the date of service of process on the other party, whichever occurs later. A
45 continuance shall be limited to one extension of no more than 10 calendar days unless all parties
46 consent or good cause is shown. The hearing shall have priority on the court calendar. If, after
47 an opportunity to be heard to all interested persons and all persons claiming an ownership interest
48 in the property, the court finds that there is no statutory, contractual, or other legal basis for the
49 recording, the court shall enter a permanent order finding that the recorded instrument is false
50 and is void as a matter of law. The court may also include in the order any of the following types
51 of relief:

13 SECTION 6.2. Article 2 of Chapter 47 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
14 a new section to read:
15 "§ 47-18.4. Source of title required for registration.
16 Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary in this Chapter, all deeds or other
17 conveyances of title (other than by court order) presented for registration must include a source
18 of title to the grantor. Source of title may include the prior deed transferring title to the current
19 grantor by reference to the book and page and the name of the grantee identified in that document,
20 a duly probated will and estate file number with the clerk of superior court, recital of the chain
21 of inheritance in the case of intestate succession, or a court order and court file number vesting
22 title in the grantor. If a deed or other conveyance of title (other than a court order) is submitted
23 for registration by anyone other than a "trusted submitter" as defined in G.S. 161-32, the register
24 of deeds shall not accept the conveyance for registration unless one of the following applies:
25 (1) The submitter provides a certified copy of the latest county tax listing or bill
26 reflecting the owner on the certified tax listing matches the grantor in the
27 document presented for registration.
28 (2) The county officer who manages tax listings certifies that the grantor on the
29 document presented for registration is consistent with the owner reflected on
30 the tax listing for the real property.
31 (3) In the event of a material inconsistency in the grantor's identity reflected on
32 the document presented for registration and the county tax records, the
33 document presented for registration shall not be recorded until legal
34 verification of the source of title of the grantor reflected on the document
35 presented for registration has been provided by one of the following methods:
36 a. The county officer who manages tax listings determines that the
37 grantor reflected in the document presented for registration should be
38 included as an owner in the tax listing for the real property.
39 b. A North Carolina licensed attorney provides a title certification of the
40 grantor listed in the document presented for registration."

39 SECTION 3.1. The Legislative Research Commission (LRC) shall study the
40 feasibility of creating a fraud detection alert system where everyone who submits an instrument
41 as defined in G.S. 161-32 for registration is automatically enrolled in the fraud detection alert
42 system without paying a fee. Before enrollment, the register of deeds would provide each
43 individual with a notice explaining the system – its purpose, how it works, and how to opt out of
44 enrollment.
45 As part of its study, the LRC shall do all of the following:
46 (1) Evaluate the practicality of implementing a fraud detection alert system with
47 automatic enrollment and an opt-out option, including technological and
48 staffing requirements for registers of deeds across all counties.
49 (2) Examine the fiscal impact on the State, counties, and/or registers of deeds,
50 including costs associated with developing, maintaining, and operating a fraud
51 detection alert system without charging a fee to the participant.

6 SECTION 4.1. Article 1 of Chapter 41 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
7 a new section to read:
8 "§ 41-10.2. Fraudulent instrument procedure.
9 (a) As used in this section, the term "instrument" means any of the following:
10 (1) A transfer of title to real property by deed or devise or other instrument
11 transferring title to real property.
12 (2) A deed of trust, mortgage, judgment, lien, encumbrance, financing statement,
13 affidavit, notice, memorandum, or any other instrument that establishes a
14 security interest in real property.
15 (3) A contract, right of refusal, lease, affidavit, memorandum, or any other
16 instrument that purports to establish an interest, option, encumbrance, right,
17 or any other claim relating to or interest in real property.
18 (b) The actual owner of an interest in real property subject to a recorded false, fictitious,
19 or fraudulent instrument may file a civil action and proceed pro se, without the assistance of legal
20 counsel. The district court division of the General Court of Justice shall have original jurisdiction
21 over actions instituted under this section, and a party may seek emergency relief under this
22 section by filing a civil action in district court alleging the filing or recording of a false, fictitious,
23 or fraudulent instrument in a public record or a private record generally available to the public.
24 Upon the filing of any action under this section, the plaintiff shall file a notice of lis pendens.
25 Required service of process or notice required by Rules 4 and 5 of the North Carolina Rules of
26 Civil Procedure on any party shall be sufficient if made to the party's address reflected on the
27 face of the suspicious instrument, the address for mailing tax bills for the parcel of land, or
28 reflected on the summons or other court filings. If an ex parte hearing is requested, and, upon the
29 submission of a sworn statement and evidence supporting the allegation of the recordation of a
30 false, fictitious, or fraudulent instrument, a temporary order, declaring the recording to be
31 fraudulent, shall be entered if the court finds that there is no statutory, contractual, or other legal
32 basis for the alleged false, fictitious, or fraudulent recording. A temporary order entered under
33 this subsection shall remain in effect for the later of 60 days or until the entry of a permanent
34 order. The chief district court judge may authorize a magistrate or magistrates to hear any motions
35 for emergency relief ex parte under this section. The clerk of superior court shall schedule an ex
36 parte hearing with the district court division of the General Court of Justice within 72 hours of
37 the filing for said relief, or by the end of the next day on which the district court is in session in
38 the county in which the action was filed, whichever shall first occur. If the district court is not in
39 session in said county, the party may contact the clerk of superior court in any other county within
40 the same judicial district who shall schedule an ex parte hearing with the district court division
41 of the General Court of Justice by the end of the next day on which said court division is in
42 session in that county. Upon the issuance of an ex parte order under this subsection, a hearing
43 shall be held within 10 calendar days from the date of issuance of the order or within seven
44 calendar days from the date of service of process on the other party, whichever occurs later. A
45 continuance shall be limited to one extension of no more than 10 calendar days unless all parties
46 consent or good cause is shown. The hearing shall have priority on the court calendar. If, after
47 an opportunity to be heard to all interested persons and all persons claiming an ownership interest
48 in the property, the court finds that there is no statutory, contractual, or other legal basis for the
49 recording, the court shall enter a permanent order finding that the recorded instrument is false
50 and is void as a matter of law. The court may also include in the order any of the following types
51 of relief:

29 document presented for registration is consistent with the owner reflected on
30 the tax listing for the real property.

32 the document presented for registration and the county tax records, the

35 presented for registration has been provided by one of the following methods:

37 grantor reflected in the document presented for registration should be
38 included as an owner in the tax listing for the real property.
39 b. A North Carolina licensed attorney provides a title certification of the
