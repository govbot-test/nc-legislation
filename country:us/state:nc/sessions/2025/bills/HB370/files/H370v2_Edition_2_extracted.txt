29 a. A state of the United States, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, the
30 United States Virgin Islands, or any territory or insular possession
31 subject to the jurisdiction of the United States.

33 federal law or formally acknowledged by an entity listed in
34 sub-subdivision a. of this subdivision.

11 "§ 50A-414. Actions for abduction prevention measures.
12 (a) A court on its own motion may order abduction prevention measures in a
13 child-custody proceeding if the court finds that the evidence establishes a credible risk of
14 abduction of the child.
15 (b) A party to a child-custody determination or another individual or entity having a right
16 under the law of this State or any other state to seek a child-custody determination for the child
17 may file a petition seeking abduction prevention measures to protect the child under this Article.
18 "§ 50A-415. Jurisdiction.
19 (a) A petition under this Article may be filed in district court if the court has jurisdiction
20 to make a child-custody determination with respect to the child at issue under Article 2 of this
21 Chapter.
22 (b) A district court of this State has temporary emergency jurisdiction under
23 G.S. 50A-204 if the court finds a credible risk of abduction.
24 "§ 50A-416. Contents of petition.
25 A petition under this Article must be verified and include a copy of any existing child-custody
26 determination, if available. The petition must specify the risk factors for abduction, including the
27 relevant factors described in G.S. 50A-417. Subject to G.S. 50A-209(e), if reasonably
28 ascertainable, the petition must contain all of the following:
29 (1) The name, date of birth, and gender of the child.
30 (2) The customary address and current physical location of the child.
31 (3) The identity, customary address, and current physical location of the
32 respondent.
33 (4) A statement of whether a prior action to prevent abduction or domestic
34 violence has been filed by a party or other individual or entity having custody
35 of the child and the date, location, and disposition of the action.
36 (5) A statement of whether a party to the proceeding has been arrested for a crime
37 related to domestic violence, stalking, or child abuse or neglect and the date,
38 location, and disposition of the case.
39 (6) Any other information required to be submitted to the court for a child-custody
40 determination under G.S. 50-13.1(a1) or G.S. 50A-209.
41 "§ 50A-417. Factors to determine risk of abduction.

43 consider any evidence that the petitioner or respondent has done or is doing any of the following:

46 (3) Has recently engaged in activities that may indicate a planned abduction,
47 including any of the following:
48 a. Abandoning employment.
49 b. Selling a primary residence.
50 c. Terminating a lease.

7 "§ 50A-419. Warrant to take physical custody of child.
8 (a) If a petition under this Article contains allegations, and the court finds, that there is a
9 credible risk that the child is imminently likely to be wrongfully removed, the court may issue
10 an ex parte warrant to take physical custody of the child.
11 (b) The respondent on a petition under subsection (a) of this section must be afforded an
12 opportunity to be heard at the earliest possible time after the ex parte warrant is executed, but not
13 later than the end of the next day that the district court is in session unless a hearing on that date
14 is impossible. In that event, the court must hold the hearing on the first possible day that the
15 district court is in session.
16 (c) An ex parte warrant under subsection (a) of this section to take physical custody of a
17 child must do all of the following:
18 (1) Recite the facts upon which a determination of a credible risk of imminent
19 wrongful removal of the child is based.
20 (2) Authorize law enforcement officers to take physical custody of the child
21 without delay.
22 (3) State the date and time for the hearing on the petition.
23 (4) Provide for the safe interim placement of the child pending further order of
24 the court.
25 (d) If feasible, before issuing a warrant and before determining the placement of the child
26 after the warrant is executed, the court may order a search of the relevant databases of the
27 National Crime Information Center system and similar databases of this State or another state to
28 determine if either the petitioner or respondent has a history of domestic violence, stalking, or
29 child abuse or neglect.
30 (e) The petition and warrant must be served on the respondent when, or as soon as
31 possible after, the child is taken into physical custody.
32 (f) A warrant to take physical custody of a child, issued by this State or another state, is
33 enforceable throughout this State. If the court finds that a less intrusive remedy will not be
34 effective, it may authorize law enforcement officers to enter private property to take physical
35 custody of the child, which may include forcible entry at any hour.
36 (g) If the court finds, after a hearing, that a petitioner sought an ex parte warrant under
37 subsection (a) of this section for the purpose of harassment or in bad faith, the court may award
38 the respondent reasonable attorneys' fees, costs, and expenses.
39 (h) This Article does not affect the availability of relief allowed under other State law.
40 "§ 50A-420. Duration of abduction prevention order.
41 An abduction prevention order remains in effect until the earliest of the following:
42 (1) The time stated in the order.
43 (2) The emancipation of the child.
44 (3) The child's attaining 18 years of age.
45 (4) The time the order is modified, revoked, vacated, or superseded by a court
46 with jurisdiction under G.S. 50A-201 through G.S. 50A-203.
47 "§ 50A-421. Uniformity of application and construction.

14 SECTION 2.(a) Article 3 of Chapter 48 of the General Statutes is amended by adding

16 "Part 2A. Information and Guidance.
17 "§ 48-3-230. Title and purpose.
18 This Part consists of and may be cited as the Information and Guidance Provisions of the
19 Uniform Unregulated Child Custody Transfer Act. The purpose of this Part is to prevent the
20 unlawful transfer of custody of minors, as prohibited by G.S. 14-321.2, by better preparing
21 adoptive parents for issues that may arise when caring for an adopted minor.
22 "§ 48-3-231. Definitions.
23 For the purposes of this Part, the following definitions apply:
24 (1) Intercountry adoption. – An adoption or placement for adoption of a minor
25 who resides in a foreign country at the time of adoption or placement. The
26 term includes an adoption finalized in the minor's country of residence or in a
27 state.
28 (2) Parent. – An individual recognized as a parent under other law of this State.
29 (3) Prospective adoptive parent. – An individual approved or permitted under
30 other law of this State to adopt a minor.
31 (4) Record. – Information inscribed on a tangible medium or stored in an
32 electronic or other medium and retrievable in perceivable form.
33 "§ 48-3-232. Scope.
34 This Part applies to placement for adoption of a minor to whom any of the following applies:
35 (1) Has been or is in foster or institutional care.
36 (2) Previously has been adopted in a state.
37 (3) Has been or is being adopted under the law of a foreign country.
38 (4) Has come or is coming to a state from a foreign country to be adopted.
39 (5) Is not a citizen of the United States.
40 "§ 48-3-233. General adoption information.
41 Within a reasonable time before an agency places a minor for adoption with a prospective
42 adoptive parent, the agency shall provide or cause to be provided to the prospective adoptive
43 parent general adoption information. The information shall address all of the following:
44 (1) Possible physical, mental, emotional, and behavioral issues concerning all of
45 the following:
46 a. Identity, loss, and trauma that a minor might experience before,
47 during, or after adoption.
48 b. A minor leaving familiar ties and surroundings.
49 (2) The effect that access to resources, including health insurance, may have on
50 the ability of an adoptive parent to meet the needs of a minor.
