7 ESTABLISH THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN EDUCATION TASK FORCE
8 SECTION 1.(a) Establishment. – There is established the Artificial Intelligence in
9 Education (AIE) Task Force to gather information and decide the best means to utilize and
10 oversee the use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools and systems in educational settings.
11 SECTION 1.(b) Voting Members. – The AIE Task Force shall consist of the
12 following six voting members:
13 (1) One member of the North Carolina Senate, appointed by the President Pro
14 Tempore of the Senate, and one member of the North Carolina House of
15 Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the House, to serve as cochairs
16 of the Task Force.
17 (2) The Superintendent of Public Instruction, or his or her designee.
18 (3) The Chair of The University of North Carolina Board of Governors, or his or
19 her designee.
20 (4) The Secretary of the Department of Information Technology, or his or her
21 designee.
22 (5) A representative of the North Carolina AI Leadership Council, to be chosen
23 by the Council.
24 SECTION 1.(c) Advisory Members. – The Chairpersons of the AIE Task Force,
25 with the advice and consent of the Superintendent of Public Instruction, the Chair of The
26 University of North Carolina Board of Governors, and the Secretary of the Department of
27 Information Technology, or their designees, may appoint ex officio nonvoting members to the
28 Task Force to serve in an advisory capacity for such terms to be determined at the discretion of
29 the Task Force. The voting members of the Task Force, upon majority vote of those present and
30 voting, may reduce or expand the number of ex officio members who may serve on the Task
31 Force, provided that such members are deemed necessary to provide expertise or access to
32 resources involving AI technology and are representative of:
33 (1) Workforce development.
34 (2) Elementary and secondary education, public or private.
35 (3) Four-year postsecondary education, public or proprietary.
36 (4) Private business entities.

1 SECTION 1.(d) Rules. – The Task Force shall adopt rules and regulations governing
2 times and places for meetings and governing the manner of conducting its business. A majority
3 of the members shall constitute a quorum for the purpose of conducting any business, and a
4 majority vote of all members present shall be required for any recommendations to the General
5 Assembly.
6 SECTION 1.(e) Purpose. – The Task Force shall be responsible for balancing
7 innovation and public interest while endeavoring to mitigate risks and unintended consequences
8 of AI and its regulation. The Task Force shall:
9 (1) Develop tentative drafts of any necessary proposed revisions to the North
10 Carolina General Statutes involving the regulation of AI technologies in
11 educational contexts.
12 (2) Review laws, policies, and procedures concerning the use of AI technology in
13 the educational context established by the United States Congress and other
14 states, focusing specifically on (i) data privacy and protection, (ii)
15 development of a framework for testing AI tools, (iii) compliance with ethical
16 standards, and (iv) liability.
17 (3) Consider implementation and use of AI in schools and compile a list of
18 recommendations of best practices and potential uses for AI in schools.
19 (4) Consider ways to allocate funding for development and use of AI in schools.
20 SECTION 1.(f) Administrative Support. – All clerical and other services required
21 by the Task Force shall be supplied by the Department of Public Instruction. The members of the
22 Task Force shall receive per diem and necessary travel and subsistence expenses in accordance
23 with the provisions of G.S. 138-5.
24 SECTION 1.(g) Term. – The Task Force shall commence within 15 days of the
25 effective date of this act, upon the call of the cochairs, and at its first meeting shall elect any
26 officers from among its members as it deems necessary for the efficient discharge of the Task
27 Force's duties. The Task Force shall dissolve on December 31, 2027.
28 SECTION 1.(h) The Task Force shall report its findings and recommendations to
29 the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee no later than March 15, 2028.
30
31 REQUIRE AGE-APPROPRIATE INSTRUCTION ON ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
32 SECTION 2.(a) Part 1 of Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
33 amended by adding a new section to read:
34 "§ 115C-81.66. Artificial intelligence instruction.
35 (a) The State Board of Education shall adopt standards for age-appropriate instruction on
36 artificial intelligence (AI) for kindergarten through grade 12. The standards shall include at least
37 the following:
38 (1) The basic concepts of artificial intelligence, including an age-appropriate
39 understanding of how AI systems source and create content.
40 (2) Real-world applications of AI.
41 (3) Responsible, ethical use of AI.
42 (4) How to access relevant AI tools.
43 (5) Critical thinking skills related to analyzing outputs of AI.
44 (b) The standards adopted by the State Board may allow for AI instruction pursuant to
45 subsection (a) of this section to be included in the standards for computer science courses offered
46 in middle school and high school.
47 (c) To the extent funds are made available for this purpose, the Department of Public
48 Instruction shall provide professional development for teachers and administrators related to AI
49 and its use in education."
