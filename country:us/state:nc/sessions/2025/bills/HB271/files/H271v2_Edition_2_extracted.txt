GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 5/13/25
Short Title: Cabarrus Co Local Omnibus. (Local)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 5, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REQUIRE A REFERENDUM IN EACH MUNICIPALITY WHOLLY
3 LOCATED WITHIN CABARRUS COUNTY ON WHETHER TO CHANGE THE
4 METHOD OF ELECTION FROM NONPARTISAN TO PARTISAN; AND TO MODIFY
5 THE PROCESS FOR FILLING VACANCIES ON THE CABARRUS COUNTY BOARD
6 OF COMMISSIONERS.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1.(a) Section 2.3 of the Charter of the City of Concord, being Section 1
9 of Chapter 861 of the 1985 Session Laws, as amended by City Ordinance No. 96-105, adopted
10 on November 14, 1996, reads as rewritten:
11 "Sec. 2.3. City Council; composition; terms of office. The City Council shall be composed
12 of seven members, each of whom shall be elected by and from the qualified voters of the city for
13 staggered terms of four years each in the manner provided by Article III of this Charter, provided
14 they shall serve until their successors are elected and qualified."
15 SECTION 1.(b) Section 3.1 of the Charter of the City of Concord, being Section 1
16 of Chapter 861 of the 1985 Session Laws, as amended by Ordinance No. 1995-02-09, adopted
17 on February 9, 1995, and Ordinance No. 96-105, adopted on November 14, 1996, reads as
18 rewritten:
19 "Sec. 3.1. Regular municipal elections; conduct and method of election. Pursuant to G.S.
20 160A-101, the Charter of the City of Concord, as set forth in Chapter 861 of the 1985 Session
21 Laws of North Carolina, as amended, is hereby further amended to implement nonpartisan
22 plurality for elections (G.S. 163-292) of the City Council.Regular municipal elections in the City
23 of Concord shall be held in the odd-numbered years and shall be conducted in accordance with
24 the uniform municipal election laws of North Carolina. The Mayor and the City Council shall be
25 elected on a partisan basis, as provided in G.S. 163-291."
26 SECTION 1.(c) This section shall not affect the filling of a vacancy that occurs for
27 the city officers of the City of Concord for a seat elected prior to the effective date of this section.
28 SECTION 1.(d) The question of changing the method of election shall be submitted
29 to the qualified voters of the City of Concord at the municipal general election to be held on
30 November 3, 2025, which election shall be conducted in accordance with the laws governing
31 elections at that time. The question to be used in the voting systems and ballots shall be:
32 "[ ] FOR [ ] AGAINST
33 Changing the method of election for the city officers of the City of Concord from
34 nonpartisan to partisan to officially disclose political affiliation of partisan candidates on the
35 ballot."
