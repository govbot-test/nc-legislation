1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO IMPLEMENT THE USE OF THE DYNAMIC SYMBOL OF ACCESS.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. The Department of Administration, in consultation with any other State
5 agency or department it deems necessary, shall implement the use of the dynamic symbol of
6 access in place of the international symbol of access for signs indicating access for, or use by,
7 persons with disabilities in all State buildings and parking areas. The symbol required under this
8 section shall be a logo with a dynamic character leaning forward with a sense of movement, be
9 readily identifiable, and be simply designed with no secondary meaning. An example of the icon
10 is available for public use from the "The Accessible Icon Project," https://accessibleicon.org. The
11 new symbol of access shall signify equivalent facilitation and accessibility as the international
12 symbol of access. The requirement of this section applies to signs installed or replaced on or after
13 January 1, 2026.
14 SECTION 2. G.S. 20-7(c) reads as rewritten:
15 "(c) Tests. – To demonstrate physical and mental ability, a person must pass an
16 examination. The examination may include road tests, vision tests, oral tests, and, in the case of
17 literate applicants, written tests, as the Division may require. The tests must ensure that an
18 applicant recognizes the handicapped international symbol of access and the dynamic symbol of
19 access, as defined in G.S. 20-37.5. The Division may not require a person who applies to renew
20 a license that has not expired to take a written test or a road test unless one or more of the
21 following applies:
22 (1) The person has been convicted of a traffic violation since the person's license
23 was last issued.
24 (2) The applicant suffers from a mental or physical condition that impairs the
25 person's ability to drive a motor vehicle.
26 The Division shall require sign and symbol testing upon initial issuance of a license. The
27 Division shall require vision testing as a part of required in-person, in-office renewals of a
28 license.
29 The Division may not require a person who is at least 60 years old to parallel park a motor
30 vehicle as part of a road test. A person shall not use an autocycle to complete a road test under
31 this subsection."
