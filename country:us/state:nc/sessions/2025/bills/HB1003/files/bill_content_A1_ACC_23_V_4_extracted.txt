Third Edition
Senator Hise
1 moves to amend the bill on page 29, lines 5 through 32,
2 by rewriting the lines to read:
3
4 "(a2) In order to engage in the practice of funeral directing or funeral service, such a
5 licensee must own, be employed by, or otherwise be an agent of a licensed funeral establishment;
6 except that such a licensee may practice funeral directing or funeral service if any of the following
7 apply:
8 (1) The licensee is employed by a college of mortuary science.
9 (2) The licensee does all of the following:
10 a. Maintains all of the licensee's business records at a location made
11 known to the Board and available for inspection by the Board under
12 the same terms and conditions as the business records of a licensed
13 funeral establishment.
14 b. Complies with rules and regulations imposed on funeral
15 establishments and the funeral profession that are designed to protect
16 consumers, to include, but not be limited to, the Federal Trade
17 Commission's laws and rules requiring General Price Lists and
18 Statements of Goods and Services.
19 c. Pays to the Board the funeral establishment license fee required by law
20 and set by the Board.
21 d. Obtains and maintains a professional liability insurance policy with
22 liability limits of at least one million dollars ($1,000,000). Certificates
23 of professional liability insurance shall be (i) submitted to the Board
24 within 30 days of the initial registration of the licensee by the Board
25 and (ii) submitted to the Board upon request. The licensee shall notify
26 the Board in writing within 30 days of any change in the insurer or any
27 cancellation or suspension of policy.
28 e. Submits an affidavit to the Board attesting that he or she owns, or was
29 being employed by, a funeral establishment directly damaged or
30 destroyed by Hurricane Helene.
31 Nothing in this subdivision shall preclude a licensee from arranging
32 cremations and cremating human remains while employed by a crematory.";
