Section 1:
H.B. 655
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 1, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Ending Offensive Indian Mascots. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Lowery.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROHIBIT THE USE OF OFFENSIVE AMERICAN INDIAN MASCOTS IN
3 PUBLIC SCHOOLS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 115C-210.4 is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
6 "(2b) As part of the annual report and presentation required under subdivision (2a)
7 of this section, the Council shall include information on public school
8 mascots. This information shall include (i) a definition of what the Council
9 finds to be an American Indian mascot and (ii) a list of all known public
10 schools with an American Indian mascot and what the mascot for that school
11 is."
12 SECTION 2. G.S. 115C-12 is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
13 "(50) To Monitor School Mascots. – The State Board of Education shall ensure that
14 no public school in the State uses an American Indian mascot, as defined
15 pursuant to G.S. 115C-210.4(2b). Within 30 days of the presentation of the
16 annual report by the State Advisory Council on Indian Education pursuant to
17 G.S. 115C-210.4, the State Board shall vote on whether it concurs or objects
18 with the Council's finding that a school is using an American Indian mascot.
19 If the State Board concurs with the Council's determination, the State Board
20 shall notify the governing body and the superintendent, or equivalent
21 authority, of the public school unit of the determination and direct that the
22 school's mascot be changed to a non-American Indian mascot within two
23 years."
24 SECTION 3.(a) G.S. 115C-276 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
25 "(u) To Monitor School Mascots. – The superintendent shall ensure that no school in a
26 public school unit has an American Indian mascot. If the superintendent has received notice
27 pursuant to G.S. 115C-12(50) that a school's mascot must be changed, the superintendent may
28 give the relevant authorities under existing school or local board policy one year to initiate the
29 process of changing the mascot. If no action has been taken to change the mascot within one year
30 of receiving notice to change the mascot, the superintendent shall have authority to choose a new
31 mascot for the school. If the school continues to have an American Indian mascot two years after
32 receiving notice to change the mascot, the Department of Public Instruction shall determine if
33 the superintendent willfully failed to comply with the requirements of this section and
34 G.S. 115C-12(50). If the Department finds that the superintendent willfully failed to comply, no
35 State funds shall be allocated to pay the superintendent's salary until the mascot is changed and
36 the Department determines the superintendent is in compliance."
*DRH30300-NH-126A*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 3.(b) G.S. 115C-218.75 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
2 "(p) To Monitor School Mascots. – A charter school shall not have an American Indian
3 mascot. If a charter school has received notice pursuant to G.S. 115C-12(50) that the school is
4 using an American Indian mascot, the school must change the mascot to a non-American Indian
5 mascot within two years of receiving the notice. If the charter school has not changed the mascot
6 within two years of receiving notice to change the mascot, the State Board shall determine if the
7 charter school willfully failed to comply with the requirements of G.S. 115C-12(50). If the State
8 Board finds that the school willfully failed to comply, the State Board shall withhold a per pupil
9 equivalent of the salary of the superintendent of the local school administrative unit in which the
10 charter school is located for each student in membership according to the allotted average daily
11 membership for that year."
12 SECTION 4. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning with
13 the 2025-2026 school year.
