Section 1:
H.B. 136
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Feb 17, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Town of Faith/Four-Year Terms. (Local)
Sponsors: Representative Warren.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE THAT REGULAR MUNICIPAL ELECTIONS FOR THE TOWN OF
3 FAITH SHALL BE HELD IN EVEN-NUMBERED YEARS AND TO EXTEND THE
4 TERMS OF OFFICE FOR MEMBERS OF THE BOARD OF ALDERMEN IN THE TOWN
5 OF FAITH FROM TWO YEARS TO FOUR YEARS.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. Section 4 of Chapter 363 of the Private Laws of 1903, as rewritten by
8 Chapter 159 of the 1961 Session Laws and Chapter 1111 of the 1979 Session Laws, reads as
9 rewritten:
10 "Sec. 4. Elections in the Town of Faith shall be conducted in accordance with Subchapter IX
11 of Chapter 163 of the General Statutes.held at the time of the general election in each
12 even-numbered year. Except as otherwise provided by this charter, the election shall be
13 conducted in accordance with the uniform municipal election laws in Chapter 163 of the General
14 Statutes."
15 SECTION 2. Section 3 of Chapter 363 of the Private Laws of 1903, as rewritten by
16 Chapter 159 of the 1961 Session Laws, reads as rewritten:
17 "Sec. 3. That the officers of said Town shall consist of five aldermen, who shall elect one of
18 their number as mayor. be elected to serve a four-year term. From the five aldermen, a mayor
19 shall be chosen by the aldermen. The mayor and aldermen when qualified as required by law,
20 may elect a town clerk, a tax collector, a treasurer, such policemen and other officers necessary
21 to the preservation and regulation of the Town's best interests."
22 SECTION 3. No municipal elections shall be conducted in the Town of Faith in
23 2025. The terms of office for the aldermen in the Town of Faith serving on the effective date of
24 this act shall be extended by one year. Regular municipal elections shall be conducted in the
25 Town of Faith every four years beginning in 2026. In 2026 and quadrennially thereafter, five
26 aldermen shall be elected to serve four-year terms.
27 SECTION 4. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to elections held
28 on or after that date.
