1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REMOVE BARRIERS TO EMPLOYMENT FOR PEOPLE WITH
3 DISABILITIES BY REMOVING THE UNEARNED INCOME LIMIT AND THE
4 RESOURCE LIMIT FROM THE HEALTH COVERAGE FOR WORKERS WITH
5 DISABILITIES ACT UNDER THE MEDICAID PROGRAM.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. No later than 90 days after this act becomes law, the Department of
8 Health and Human Services, Division of Health Benefits, shall submit the necessary
9 documentation to the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) for approval to remove
10 the unearned income limit and the resource limit from the eligibility requirements for the Health
11 Coverage for Workers with Disabilities Medicaid eligibility category. Upon approval by CMS
12 of the removal of the unearned income and resource limits, the Secretary of the Department of
13 Health and Human Services shall notify the Revisor of Statutes of the effective date approved by
14 CMS for the removal.

25 (c2) Involuntary Unemployment. – Individuals who participate in HCWD but thereafter
26 become unemployed for involuntary reasons, including health reasons, shall have continued
27 eligibility in HCWD for up to 12 months from the time of involuntary unemployment, so long as
28 the individual (i) maintains a connection with the workforce, as determined by the Department,
29 (ii) meets all other eligibility criteria for HCWD during the period, and (iii) pays applicable fees,
30 premiums, and co-payments.
31 (d) Fees, Premiums, and Co-Payments. – Individuals who participate in HCWD and have
32 countable income greater than one hundred fifty percent (150%) of FPG shall pay an annual
33 enrollment fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) to their county department of social services. Individuals
34 who participate in HCWD and have countable income greater than or equal to two hundred
35 percent (200%) of FPG shall pay a monthly premium in addition to the annual fee. The
36 Department shall set a sliding scale for premiums, which is consistent with applicable federal
37 law. An individual with countable income equal to or greater than four hundred fifty percent
38 (450%) of FPG shall pay not less than one hundred percent (100%) of the cost of the premium,
39 as determined by the Department. The premium shall be based on the experience of all
40 individuals participating in the Medical Assistance Program. Individuals who participate in
41 HCWD are subject to co-payments equal to those required under the Medical Assistance
42 Program.
43 (e) Countable Income. – In determining an individual's countable income, the
44 Department may not consider income that is disregarded under the State Medical Assistance
45 Plan's financial methodology, including the sixty-five dollar ($65.00) disregard,
46 impairment-related work expenses, student earned-income exclusions, and other SSI program
47 work incentive income disregards."
