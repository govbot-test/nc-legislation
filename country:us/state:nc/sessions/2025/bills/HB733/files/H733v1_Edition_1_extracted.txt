Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: The Everybody Eats Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Lopez, Hawkins, and Morey (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 3, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ALLOW THE STATE TO FULLY OPT OUT OF THE PROHIBITION ON FOOD
3 AND NUTRITION SERVICES AND TEMPORARY ASSISTANCE FOR NEEDY
4 FAMILIES (TANF) BENEFITS FOR INDIVIDUALS CONVICTED OF FELONIES AND
5 TO LENGTHEN THE PERIOD FOR RECERTIFICATION FOR FOOD AND
6 NUTRITION BENEFITS TO ONE YEAR.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1. G.S. 108A-25.2 reads as rewritten:
9 "§ 108A-25.2. Exemption from limitations for individuals convicted of certain drug-related
10 felonies.
11 Individuals convicted of Class H or I controlled substance felony offenses in this State shall
12 be An individual who is otherwise eligible to participate in the Work First Program and or the
13 food and nutrition services program:program shall be exempt from the application of section
14 115(a) of the federal Personal Responsibility and Work Opportunity Reconciliation Act of 1996,
15 P.L. 104-193, and shall not be denied assistance from either program solely because the
16 individual has been convicted of a drug-related felony or felonies.
17 (1) Six months after release from custody if no additional controlled substance
18 felony offense is committed during that period and successful completion of
19 or continuous active participation in a required substance abuse treatment
20 program determined appropriate by the area mental health authority; or
21 (2) If not committed to custody, six months after the date of conviction if no
22 additional controlled substance felony offense is committed during that period
23 and successful completion of or continuous active participation in a required
24 substance abuse treatment program determined appropriate by the area mental
25 health authority.
26 A county department of social services shall require individuals who are eligible for Work
27 First Program assistance and electronic food and nutrition benefits pursuant to this section to
28 undergo substance abuse treatment as a condition for receiving Work First Program or electronic
29 food and nutrition benefits, if funds and programs are available and to the extent allowed by
30 federal law."
31 SECTION 2. The Department of Health and Human Services shall direct county
32 departments of social services to require recertification of eligibility for Food and Nutrition
33 Services be made every 12 months, unless otherwise required by federal law.
34 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective January 1, 2026.
