2 AN ACT TO PROMOTE HEALTH CARE PRACTITIONER TRANSPARENCY THROUGH
3 ADVERTISEMENT REQUIREMENTS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) Article 37 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is amended by
6 adding a new Part 1 to be entitled "Health Care Practitioner Identification" and to consist of
7 G.S. 90-640.
8 SECTION 1.(b) Article 37 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is amended by
9 adding a new Part to read:
10 "Part 2. Health Care Practitioner Transparency Act.
11 "§ 90-645. Short title.
12 This Part shall be known as the "Health Care Practitioner Transparency Act."
13 "§ 90-646. Definitions.
14 The following definitions apply in this Part:
15 (1) Advertisement. – Any communication or statement that is printed, electronic,
16 or oral which names the health care practitioner in relation to their practice,
17 profession, or institution where the health care practitioner is employed,
18 volunteers, or otherwise provides health care services, including business
19 cards, letterhead, patient brochures, email, internet, audio and video, or any
20 other communication or statement used in the course of business.
21 (2) Deceptive or misleading. – Any verbal or written representation or
22 advertisement that misstates, falsely describes, or holds out in a false light the
23 profession, skills, expertise, education, board certification, or licensure of the
24 health care professional.
25 (3) Health care practitioner. – An individual who is licensed, certified, or
26 registered to engage in the practice of medicine, nursing, dentistry, pharmacy,
27 or any related occupation involving the direct provision of health care to
28 patients.
29 (4) Licensee. – A health care practitioner who holds an active license with a
30 licensing board that governs the health care practitioner's occupation in this
31 State.
32 "§ 90-647. Advertisement and representation requirements.

1 (a) An advertisement for health care services that names a health care practitioner shall
2 identify the type of license, certification, or registration held by the health care practitioner. The
3 advertisement shall not contain any deceptive or misleading information.
4 (b) A health care practitioner shall not make a representation about the health care
5 practitioner's license, certification, or registration that is deceptive or misleading.
6 (c) Any individual not licensed to practice medicine under Article 1 of this Chapter shall
7 not hold himself or herself out to the public by calling oneself a physician or any of the following
8 titles, or using any similar title or description of services with the intent to represent that the
9 individual practices medicine: "surgeon," "medical doctor," "doctor of osteopathy," "M.D.,"
10 "D.O.," "anesthesiologist," "cardiologist," "dermatologist," "endocrinologist,"
11 "gastroenterologist," "general practitioner," "gynecologist," "hematologist," "hospitalist,"
12 "internist," "intensivist," "laborist," "laryngologist," "nephrologist," "neurologist,"
13 "obstetrician," "oncologist," "ophthalmologist," "orthopedic surgeon," "orthopedist,"
14 "osteopath," "otologist," "otolaryngologist," "otorhinolaryngologist," "pathologist,"
15 "pediatrician," "primary care physician," "proctologist," "psychiatrist," "radiologist,"
16 "rheumatologist," "rhinologist," or "urologist." Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to
17 prevent a health care practitioner from using any title or abbreviation which is authorized for
18 such health care practitioner pursuant to licensing statutes.
19 "§ 90-648. Violations and enforcement.
20 (a) Any health care practitioner subject to this Article who does any of the following shall
21 be in violation of this Article:
22 (1) Knowingly aids, assists, procures, employs, or advises an unlicensed
23 individual or entity in practicing or engaging in acts outside of the scope of
24 the health care practitioner's degree of licensure.
25 (2) Knowingly delegates or contracts the performance of health care services to a
26 health care practitioner that is unqualified to perform those health care
27 services.
28 (3) Fails to comply with any provision of this Article.
29 (b) Any health care practitioner who violates this Article as provided under subsection
30 (a) of this section shall be guilty of unprofessional conduct and may be subject to disciplinary
31 action under the health care practitioner's licensure board or other appropriate governing
32 provisions.
33 (c) Each day of noncompliance with this Article by a health care practitioner shall
34 constitute a separate and distinct violation.
35 (d) Any health care practitioner who practices in more than one office shall be required
36 to comply with this Article in each practice setting.
37 (e) Health care practitioners that work in non-patient settings and do not have any direct
38 patient health care interactions are not subject to this Article."
