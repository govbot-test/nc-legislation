4 SECTION 2. Part 2 of Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended
5 by adding the following new sections to read:
6 "§ 115C-84.2A. Duty to comply with school calendar requirements.
7 (a) It is the duty of a local board of education to ensure that a local school administrative
8 unit complies with the requirements of Part 2 of this Article.
9 (b) Negligent failure to ensure compliance with Part 2 of this Article shall be deemed
10 misfeasance and willful effort to prevent compliance shall be deemed malfeasance.
11 "§ 115C-84.2B. School calendar enforcement; withholding of funds.
12 (a) The State Board of Education shall ensure that public school units comply with Part
13 2 of this Article.
14 (b) The Superintendent of Public Instruction shall notify the State Board of Education
15 and shall investigate whether the public school unit is in compliance with Part 2 of this Article if
16 the Superintendent receives information from any of the following indicating that a public school
17 unit has not complied with the Part:
18 (1) A report to the Department of Public Instruction, Superintendent of Public
19 Instruction, or State Board of Education.
20 (2) A report by an employee of the Department of Public Instruction responsible
21 for monitoring compliance of a public school unit.
22 (3) Any other source the Superintendent deems credible.
23 (c) The Superintendent shall report the findings of the investigation to the State Board of
24 Education within 60 days of the receipt of the information under subsection (b) of this section.
25 The State Board shall schedule consideration of the report no later than the next regular meeting
26 occurring more than five days after submission of the report.
27 (d) Following submission of the report by the Superintendent, if the State Board of
28 Education by a majority vote finds that the governing body has not complied with Part 2 of this
29 Article, the State Board shall direct the governing body of the public school unit to take action
30 to remedy the violation and submit documentation of the remedy to the State Board within 60
31 days. The State Board shall find that a public school unit is noncompliant with Part 2 of this
32 Article if the Superintendent's report indicates any of the following have occurred:
33 (1) The governing body adopted a resolution or policy stating that the public
34 school unit will not comply with Part 2 of this Article.
35 (2) The governing body approved any action counter to or incompatible with
36 compliance of Part 2 of this Article.
37 (e) If the governing body of the public school unit fails to comply with the State Board
38 of Education direction as provided in subsection (d) of this section, the State Board shall withhold
39 the local school administrative unit's central office administration allotment until the State Board
40 determines the local board is no longer noncompliant.
41 (f) If the State Board of Education takes action under subsection (e) of this section, the
42 State Board shall report this action to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee,
43 within 60 days of the action. The General Assembly shall consider, no later than the next session
44 of the General Assembly, the future governance of the identified public school unit.
45 "§ 115C-84.2C. School calendar enforcement; liability of local board.
46 (a) A person that resides or maintains a place of business within the boundaries of a local
47 school administrative unit may bring an action against the local board of education if the board
48 has not complied with Part 2 of this Article. For purpose of this section, "person" means any
49 natural person or corporation, nonprofit corporation, limited liability company, trust, association,
50 partnership, joint venture, subsidiary, or other business entity.
