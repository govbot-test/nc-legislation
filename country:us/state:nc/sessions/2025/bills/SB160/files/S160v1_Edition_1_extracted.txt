6 Whereas, it is the intention of the North Carolina General Assembly to promote the

12 "§ 90-667. Confidentiality of Board investigative information.
13 (a) All records, papers, investigative information, and other documents containing
14 information that the Board, its members, or its employees possess, gather, or receive as a result
15 of investigations, inquiries, assessments, or interviews conducted in connection with a licensing
16 complaint, appeal, assessment, potential impairment matter, or disciplinary matter shall not be
17 considered public records within the meaning of Chapter 132 of the General Statutes and are
18 privileged, confidential, not subject to discovery, subpoena, or any means of legal compulsion
19 for release to anyone other than the Board, its employees, or consultants involved in the
20 application for license, impairment assessment, or discipline of the licensee, except as provided
21 in subsection (b) of this section. For the purposes of this section, "investigative information"
22 means investigative files and reports, information relating to the identity and report of a physician
23 or other professional performing an expert review for the Board, and any of the Board's
24 investigative materials that are not admitted into evidence.
25 (b) The Board shall provide the licensee or applicant for license access to all information
26 in its possession that the Board intends to offer into evidence at the licensee's or applicant's
27 hearing. The Board shall not be required to produce (i) information subject to attorney-client
28 privilege or (ii) investigative information that the Board will not offer into evidence and is related
29 to advice, opinions, or recommendations of the Board's staff, consultants, or agents.
30 (c) Any licensee's notice of statement of charges, notice of hearing, and all information
31 contained in those documents shall be public records under Chapter 132 of the General Statutes.
32 (d) If the Board, its employees, or its agents possess investigative information indicating
33 a crime may have been committed, the Board may report the information to the appropriate law
34 enforcement agency or district attorney of the district in which the offense was committed. The
35 Board shall cooperate with and assist any law enforcement agency or district attorney conducting
36 a criminal investigation or prosecution of a licensee by providing relevant information. This
37 information shall be confidential under G.S. 132-1.4 and shall remain confidential after
38 disclosure to a law enforcement agency or district attorney.
39 (e) All licensees shall self-report to the Board any of the following within 30 days of their
40 arrest or indictment:
41 (1) Any felony or arrest or indictment.
42 (2) Any arrest for driving while impaired or driving under the influence.
43 (3) Any arrest or indictment for the possession, use, or sale of any controlled
44 substance.
45 (f) The Board, its members, or its staff may release confidential information concerning

10 "§ 90-668. Limitations on advanced respiratory care practitioners.
11 (a) Individuals who are licensed under this Article as advanced respiratory care
12 practitioners may use the title "advanced respiratory care practitioner." Individuals who hold
13 themselves out as advanced respiratory care practitioners without being licensed are in violation
14 of this Article.

16 practitioners may practice advanced respiratory care under the supervision of a physician
17 licensed under Article 1 of this Chapter and within the scope of rules adopted by the Board."
