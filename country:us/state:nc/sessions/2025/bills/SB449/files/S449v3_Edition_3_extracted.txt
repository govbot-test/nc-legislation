11 PART I. K-12 TECHNOLOGY COST CONSIDERATIONS AND REPORTS ON
12 BREAK/FIX RATES
13 SECTION 1.(a) Part 3A of Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
14 amended by adding the following new sections to read:
15 "§ 115C-102.10. Technology costs considerations.
16 The State Board of Education shall adopt rules requiring all public school units to evaluate
17 the following when acquiring technology, computer hardware, and software:
18 (1) The long-term cost of ownership, including costs of repairing the technology,
19 computer hardware, or software.
20 (2) Any flexibility for innovation during the life of the technology, computer
21 hardware, or software.
22 (3) Any anticipated resale or salvage value at the end of the target life cycle for
23 the technology, computer hardware, or software based on the average resale
24 or salvage value of similar technology, computer hardware, or software as a
25 percentage of the initial cost of purchase.
26 "§ 115C-102.11. Break/fix rate reporting requirement.
27 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
28 (1) Break/fix rate. – The percentage obtained by dividing the number of school
29 technology devices reported as malfunctioning or needing repair due to
30 physical damage, hardware failure, or other breakage incidents prior to the
31 stated life cycle period, not covered by insurance or a policy plan period, by
32 the total number of school technology devices in operation during that period.
33 (2) School technology device. – Any electronic or computerized equipment
34 provided for educational purposes in a public school unit, including
35 computers, tablets, interactive whiteboards, and similar devices or anything
