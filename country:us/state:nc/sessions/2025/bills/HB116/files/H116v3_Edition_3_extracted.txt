GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 3
Committee Substitute Favorable 3/4/25
Senate Elections Committee Substitute Adopted 5/20/25
Short Title: Local Bd. of Ed. Election Changes. (Local)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
February 17, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE FOR THE PARTISAN ELECTION OF THE COLUMBUS COUNTY
3 BOARD OF EDUCATION, THE GASTON COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION, AND
4 THE JOHNSTON COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-35, the Columbus County Board of
7 Education shall consist of five members elected to serve staggered four-year terms. Five members
8 shall be elected from residency districts. One member shall be elected from each residency
9 district by the qualified voters of the entire county, excluding the area encompassing the
10 Whiteville City School Administrative Unit. No person shall be eligible to file for or be elected
11 to the Columbus County Board of Education unless the person is a qualified voter and resident
12 of the residency district in which the person seeks to be elected.
13 SECTION 1.(b) Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-37, members of the Columbus County
14 Board of Education shall be elected on a partisan basis at the time of the general election in each
15 even-numbered year as terms expire. Candidates for election to the Columbus County Board of
16 Education shall be nominated at the same time and in the same manner as other county officers.
17 Members shall take office on the first Monday in December following their election. Members
18 shall serve until their successors are elected and qualified.
19 SECTION 1.(c) All vacancies in the membership of the Columbus County Board of
20 Education by death, resignation, or other causes shall be filled in accordance with
21 G.S. 115C-37.1.
22 SECTION 1.(d) This section does not affect the terms of office of any person elected
23 in 2024 to the Columbus County Board of Education. Any vacancy on the Columbus County
24 Board of Education for a member elected in 2024 shall be filled by appointment by the remaining
25 members of the Columbus County Board of Education to serve the remainder of the unexpired
26 term. When a vacancy occurs in a seat elected from a residency district, the successor shall be
27 selected from the residency district of the vacating member. Each member of the Columbus
28 County Board of Education elected in 2024, or any member appointed to fill a vacancy for a
29 member elected in 2024, shall serve until a successor has been elected and qualified.
30 SECTION 1.(e) All laws and clauses of laws in conflict with this section are hereby
31 repealed, including the following:
32 (1) Chapter 717 of the 1953 Session Laws.
33 (2) Section 1 of Chapter 114 of the 1961 Session Laws.
34 (3) Chapter 327 of the 1961 Session Laws.
35 (4) Chapter 754 of the 1961 Session Laws.

3 (7) Section 3 of Chapter 192 of the 1981 Session Laws.
4 (8) Section 2 of Chapter 234 of the 1983 Session Laws.
5 SECTION 2.(a) Section 2 of Chapter 633 of the 1977 Session Laws reads as
6 rewritten:
7 "Sec. 2. Composition of board of education.
8 (a) Notwithstanding Section 115-18 of the General Statutes of North Carolina,
9 G.S. 115C-35, the board of education shall consist of nine members. members elected to
10 staggered four-year terms. Two members shall be elected by the duly qualified voters of Gaston
11 County without regard to residence in any particular township and seven from the county at-large
12 and seven members shall be elected by the duly qualified voters of Gaston County from the
13 following townships:from residency districts. For the seven residency district seats, no person
14 shall be eligible to file for or be elected to the board of education unless the person is a qualified
15 voter and resident of the residency district in which the candidate seeks to be elected. All
16 members of the board of education shall be elected by the duly qualified voters of Gaston County.
17 (b) The residency districts for the board of education and the number of members from
18 each residency district are as follows:
19 (1) Cherryville Township shall be entitled to one member.
20 (2) Crowders Mountain Township shall be entitled to one member.
21 (3) Dallas Township shall be entitled to one member.
22 (4) Gastonia Township shall be entitled to two members.
23 (5) Riverbend Township shall be entitled to one member.
24 (6) Southpoint Township shall be entitled to one member."
25 SECTION 2.(b) Section 3 of Chapter 633 of the 1977 Session Laws reads as
26 rewritten:
27 "Sec. 3. Elections. Notwithstanding Section 115-19 of the General Statutes of North Carolina,
28 at the general election to be held in the year 1978, there shall be elected by the duly qualified
29 voters of Gaston County three members of the board of education, with one member from
30 Cherryville Township, one member from Crowders Mountain Township and one member at
31 large. The term for these three members shall commence on the first Monday in December 1978,
32 and expire at the end of two years thereafter, or on the first Monday in December 1980.
33 At the general election to be held in the year 1980, there shall be elected by the duly qualified
34 voters of Gaston County six members of the board of education, with one member from
35 Cherryville Township, one member from Crowders Mountain Township, one member from
36 Gastonia Township, one member from Riverbend Township, one member from Southpoint
37 Township and one member at large. Those members elected from Cherryville Township,
38 Crowders Mountain Township, Gastonia Township and the member elected at large shall
39 compose one class, whose term of office shall commence on the first Monday in December 1980,
40 and expire at the end of four years thereafter, or on the first Monday in December 1984. The term
41 of office for those members elected from Riverbend Township and Southpoint Township shall
42 commence on the First Monday in December 1980, and expire at the end of two years thereafter,
43 or on the first Monday in December 1982.
44 At the general election to be held in the year 1982, there shall be elected by the duly qualified
45 voters of Gaston County five members of the board of education, with one member from Dallas
46 Township, one member from Gastonia Township, one member from Riverbend Township, one
47 member from Southpoint Township and one member at large. These five members shall compose
48 another class, whose terms of office shall commence on the first Monday in December 1982, and
49 expire at the end of four years thereafter, or on the first Monday in December 1986.
50 Upon the expiration of the term of office of each class of members of the board of education
51 (the term class shall exclude the members elected for a term of two years) their successors shall

37 "Section 1. Beginning with the 1998 election, the The Johnston County Board of Education
38 shall be elected in nonpartisan partisan elections held in even-numbered years as provided in this
39 act. Members shall be nominated at the same time and manner as other county officers."
40 SECTION 3.(b) Section 3 of S.L. 1997-32, as amended by S.L. 2023-147, is
41 repealed.
42 SECTION 3.(c) Section 4 of S.L. 1997-32, as amended by S.L. 2023-143, reads as
43 rewritten:
44 "Section 4. If a vacancy occurs on the Johnston County Board of Education, the remaining
45 members of the Johnston County Board of Education shall appoint a person to fill that seat. The
46 person appointed to fill the vacancy shall serve the remainder of the unexpired term of the office.
47 vacancy shall be filled in accordance with G.S. 115C-37.1. Any person appointed to fill a
48 vacancy shall be a resident of the single-member residency district in which the vacancy occurs."
49 SECTION 3.(d) This section does not affect the terms of office of any person elected
50 in 2024 to the Johnston County Board of Education. Any vacancy on the Johnston County Board
