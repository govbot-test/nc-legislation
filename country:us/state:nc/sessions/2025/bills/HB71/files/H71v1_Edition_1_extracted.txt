14 "§ 90-667. Confidentiality of Board investigative information.
15 (a) All records, papers, investigative information, and other documents containing
16 information that the Board, its members, or its employees possess, gather, or receive as a result
17 of investigations, inquiries, assessments, or interviews conducted in connection with a licensing
18 complaint, appeal, assessment, potential impairment matter, or disciplinary matter shall not be
19 considered public records within the meaning of Chapter 132 of the General Statutes and are
20 privileged, confidential, not subject to discovery, subpoena, or any means of legal compulsion
21 for release to anyone other than the Board, its employees, or consultants involved in the
22 application for license, impairment assessment, or discipline of the licensee, except as provided
23 in subsection (b) of this section. For the purposes of this section, "investigative information"
24 means investigative files and reports, information relating to the identity and report of a physician
25 or other professional performing an expert review for the Board, and any of the Board's
26 investigative materials that are not admitted into evidence.
27 (b) The Board shall provide the licensee or applicant for license access to all information
28 in its possession that the Board intends to offer into evidence at the licensee's or applicant's
29 hearing. The Board shall not be required to produce (i) information subject to attorney-client
30 privilege or (ii) investigative information that the Board will not offer into evidence and is related
31 to advice, opinions, or recommendations of the Board's staff, consultants, or agents.
32 (c) Any licensee's notice of statement of charges, notice of hearing, and all information
33 contained in those documents shall be public records under Chapter 132 of the General Statutes.
34 (d) If the Board, its employees, or its agents possess investigative information indicating
35 a crime may have been committed, the Board may report the information to the appropriate law
36 enforcement agency or district attorney of the district in which the offense was committed. The
37 Board shall cooperate with and assist any law enforcement agency or district attorney conducting
38 a criminal investigation or prosecution of a licensee by providing relevant information. This
39 information shall be confidential under G.S. 132-1.4 and shall remain confidential after
40 disclosure to a law enforcement agency or district attorney.
41 (e) All licensees shall self-report to the Board any of the following within 30 days of their
42 arrest or indictment:
43 (1) Any felony or arrest or indictment.
44 (2) Any arrest for driving while impaired or driving under the influence.
45 (3) Any arrest or indictment for the possession, use, or sale of any controlled
46 substance.
47 (f) The Board, its members, or its staff may release confidential information concerning
48 the denial, annulment, suspension, or revocation of a license to any other health care licensing
49 board in this State, other state, or country, or authorized Department of Health and Human
50 Services personnel who are charged with the enforcement or investigative responsibilities of
51 licensure. If the Board releases this confidential information, the Board shall notify and provide
