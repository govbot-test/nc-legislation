8 "§ 96A-1. Short title; definitions.
9 (a) This Chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "North Carolina Paid Family
10 Leave Insurance Act."
11 (b) The following definitions apply in this Chapter:
12 (1) Application year. – The 12-month period beginning on the first day of the
13 calendar week in which an individual files an application for family and
14 medical leave insurance benefits.
15 (2) Assistant Secretary. – The Assistant Secretary of the Division of Employment
16 Security.
17 (3) Covered individual. – Any person who does all of the following:
18 a. Meets the monetary eligibility criteria set forth in G.S. 96-14.1(b) or
19 is self-employed, elects coverage, and meets the requirements of
20 G.S. 96A-13.
21 b. Meets the administrative requirements outlined in this Chapter and in
22 the rules adopted under this Chapter.
23 c. Submits an application.
24 (4) Covered service member. – Either:
25 a. A member of the Armed Forces, including a member of the National
26 Guard or Reserves, who is (i) undergoing medical treatment,
27 recuperation, or therapy, (ii) otherwise in outpatient status, or (iii) is
28 otherwise on the temporary disability retired list for a serious injury or
29 illness that was incurred by the member in the line of duty on active
30 duty in the Armed Forces or a serious injury or illness that existed
31 before the beginning of the member's active duty and was aggravated
32 by service in the line of duty on active duty in the Armed Forces; or
33 b. A former member of the Armed Forces, including a former member of
34 the National Guard or Reserves, who is undergoing medical treatment,
35 recuperation, or therapy for a serious injury or illness that was incurred

17 "§ 96A-2. Eligibility for benefits.
18 Beginning January 1, 2027, family and medical leave insurance benefits are payable to an
19 individual who:
20 (1) Meets the definition of "covered individual" as defined by G.S. 96A-1(b)(3);
21 and
22 (2) Meets one of the following requirements:
23 a. Because of birth, adoption, or placement through foster care, is caring
24 for a new child within 12 months of the anticipated birth, adoption, or
25 placement of that child, or has a need to be absent from work before
26 an actual placement of a child in order for an adoption or foster care
27 to proceed.
28 b. Is caring for a family member with a serious health condition.
29 c. Has a serious health condition.
30 d. Is caring for a covered service member who is the covered individual's
31 next of kin or other family member.
32 e. Because of any "qualifying exigency leave" arising out of the fact that
33 the family member of the covered individual is on active duty (or has
34 been notified of an impending call or order to active duty) in the
35 Armed Forces.
36 "§ 96A-3. Duration of benefits.
37 (a) The maximum number of weeks during which family and medical leave insurance
38 benefits are payable under G.S. 96A-2(2)c. in an application year is 18 weeks.
39 (b) The maximum number of weeks during which family and medical leave insurance
40 benefits are payable under G.S. 96A-2(2)a., (2)b., or (2)e. in an application year is 12 weeks.
41 (c) The maximum number of weeks during which family and medical leave insurance
42 benefits are payable under G.S. 96A-2(2)d. in an application year is 26 weeks.
43 (d) The first payment of benefits must be made to an individual within two weeks after
44 the claim is filed, and subsequent payments must be made every two weeks thereafter.

49 covered individual's average weekly wage that is equal to or less than one
50 hundred percent (100%) of the State average weekly wage shall be replaced
51 at a rate of ninety percent (90%) and (ii) the portion of an employee's or

10 "§ 96A-6. Reduced leave schedule.
11 (a) A covered individual shall be entitled, at the option of the covered individual, to take
12 paid family and medical leave on an intermittent or reduced leave schedule in which all of the
13 leave authorized under this Chapter is not taken sequentially. Family and medical leave insurance
14 benefits for intermittent or reduced leave schedules shall be prorated.
15 (b) The covered individual shall make a reasonable effort to schedule paid family and
16 medical leave under this section so as not to unduly disrupt the operations of the employer. The
17 covered individual shall provide the employer with prior notice of the schedule on which the
18 covered individual will take the leave, to the extent practicable. Paid family and medical leave
19 taken under this section shall not result in a reduction of the total amount of leave to which an
20 employee is entitled beyond the amount of leave actually taken.
21 (c) Nothing in this section shall be construed to entitle a covered individual to more leave
22 than required under G.S. 96A-3.
23 "§ 96A-7. Leave and employment protection.
24 (a) Any covered individual who exercises his or her right to family and medical leave
25 insurance benefits shall, upon the expiration of that leave, be entitled to be restored by the
26 employer to the position held by the covered individual when the leave commenced, or to a
27 position with equivalent seniority, status, employment benefits, pay, and other terms and
28 conditions of employment, including fringe benefits and service credits that the covered
29 individual had been entitled to at the commencement of leave.
30 (b) During any leave taken pursuant to G.S. 96A-2, the employer shall maintain any
31 health care benefits the covered individual had prior to taking such leave for the duration of the
32 leave as if the covered individual had continued in employment continuously from the date he or
33 she commenced the leave until the date the family and medical leave insurance benefits
34 terminate; provided, however, that the covered individual shall continue to pay the covered
35 individual's share of the cost of health benefits as required prior to the commencement of the
36 leave.

48 clause (i) of this subdivision and the interest described in clause (ii) of this
49 subdivision, except that if an employer who has violated this section or
50 G.S. 96A-8 proves to the satisfaction of the court that the act or omission
51 which violated the section was in good faith and that the employer had

20 "§ 96A-8. Retaliatory personnel actions prohibited.
21 (a) It shall be unlawful for an employer or any other person to interfere with, restrain,
22 deny the exercise of, or the attempt to exercise any right protected under this Chapter.
23 (b) An employer, temporary help company, employment agency, employee organization,
24 or other person shall not take retaliatory personnel action or otherwise discriminate against a
25 person because he or she exercised rights protected under this Chapter. Such rights include, but
26 are not limited to, the right to request, file for, apply for, or use benefits provided for under this
27 Chapter; to take leave from work under this Chapter; communicate to the employer or any other
28 person or entity an intent to file a claim, a complaint with the Division or courts, or an appeal; or
29 has testified or is about to testify or has assisted in any investigation, hearing, or proceeding under
30 this Chapter, at any time, including during the period in which the person receives family and
31 medical leave insurance benefits under this Chapter; inform any person about any employer's
32 alleged violation of this Chapter; and the right to inform any person of his or her rights under this
33 Chapter.
34 (c) It shall be unlawful for an employer's absence control policy to count paid family and
35 medical leave taken under this Chapter as an absence that may lead to or result in discipline,
36 discharge, demotion, suspension, or any other adverse action.
37 (d) Protections of this section shall apply to any person who mistakenly, but in good faith,
38 alleges violations of this Chapter.
39 (e) This section shall be enforced as provided in subsections (c) through (g) of
40 G.S. 96A-7.

15 "§ 96A-10. Notice.
16 (a) Each employer shall provide written notice to each employee upon hiring and
17 annually thereafter. An employer shall also provide written notice to an employee when the
18 employee requests leave under this Chapter or when the employer acquires knowledge that an
19 employee's leave may be for a qualifying reason under G.S. 96A-2. Such notice shall include (i)
20 the employee's right to family and medical leave insurance benefits under this Chapter and the
21 terms under which it may be used, (ii) the amount of family and medical leave insurance benefits,
22 (iii) the procedure for filing a claim for benefits, (iv) the right to job protection and benefits
23 continuation under G.S. 96A-7, (v) that discrimination and retaliatory personnel actions against
24 a person for requesting, applying for, or using family and medical leave insurance benefits is
25 prohibited under G.S. 96A-8, and (vi) that the employee has a right to file a complaint for
26 violations of this Chapter. An employer shall also display and maintain a poster in a conspicuous
27 place accessible to employees at the employer's place of business that contains the information
28 required by this section in English, Spanish, and any language that is the first language spoken
29 by at least five percent (5%) of the employer's workforce, provided that such notice has been
30 provided by the Division. The Assistant Secretary may adopt regulations to establish additional
31 requirements concerning the means by which employers shall provide such notice.
32 (b) Employees shall provide notice to their employers as soon as practicable of their
33 intention to take leave under this Chapter.
34 "§ 96A-11. Enforcement.
35 (a) The Assistant Secretary shall establish a system for appeals in the case of a denial of
36 family and medical leave insurance benefits. In establishing such system, the Assistant Secretary
37 may utilize any and all procedures and appeals mechanisms established under G.S. 96-15.
38 (b) Judicial review of any decision with respect to family and medical leave insurance
39 benefits shall be permitted in a court of competent jurisdiction after a party aggrieved thereby
40 has exhausted all administrative remedies established by the Assistant Secretary.
41 (c) The Assistant Secretary shall implement procedures to ensure confidentiality of all
42 information related to any claims filed or appeals taken to the maximum extent permitted by
43 applicable laws.
44 "§ 96A-12. Erroneous payments and disqualification for benefits.
45 (a) A covered individual is disqualified from family and medical leave insurance benefits
46 for one year if the individual is determined by the Assistant Secretary to have willfully made a
47 false statement or misrepresentation regarding a material fact or willfully failed to report a
48 material fact to obtain benefits under this Chapter.
49 (b) If family and medical leave insurance benefits are paid erroneously or as a result of
50 willful misrepresentation, or if a claim for family and medical leave insurance benefits is rejected
51 after benefits are paid, the Division may seek repayment of benefits from the recipient. The

3 "§ 96A-13. Elective coverage.
4 (a) A self-employed person, including a sole proprietor, partner, or joint venturer, may
5 elect coverage under this Chapter for an initial period of not less than three years. The
6 self-employed person must file a notice of election in writing with the Assistant Secretary as
7 required by the Division. The election becomes effective on the date of filing the notice. As a
8 condition of election, the self-employed person must agree to supply any information concerning
9 income that the Division deems necessary.
10 (b) A self-employed person who has elected coverage may withdraw from coverage
11 within 30 days after the end of the three-year period of coverage, or at such other times as the
12 Assistant Secretary may prescribe by rule, by filing written notice with the Assistant Secretary,
13 such withdrawal to take effect not sooner than 30 days after filing the notice.
14 "§ 96A-14. Family and medical leave insurance program.
15 (a) The Division shall establish and administer a family and medical leave insurance
16 program and begin collecting contributions as specified in this Chapter. By January 1, 2027, the
17 Division shall start receiving claims from and paying family and medical leave insurance benefits
18 to covered individuals.
19 (b) The Division shall establish reasonable procedures and forms for filing claims for
20 benefits under this Chapter and shall specify what supporting documentation is necessary to
21 support a claim for benefits, including any documentation required from a health care provider
22 for proof of a serious health condition.
23 (c) The Division shall notify the employer within five business days of a claim being
24 filed pursuant to this Chapter.
25 (d) The Division shall use information sharing and integration technology to facilitate the
26 disclosure of relevant information or records, so long as an individual consents to the disclosure
27 as required under State law.
28 (e) Information contained in the files and records pertaining to an individual under this
29 Chapter are confidential and not open to public inspection other than to public employees in the
30 performance of their official duties. However, the individual or an authorized representative of
31 an individual may review the records or receive specific information from the records upon the
32 presentation of the individual's signed authorization.
33 (f) The Department of Commerce shall adopt rules as necessary to implement this
34 Chapter.
35 "§ 96A-15. Federal income tax.
36 If the Internal Revenue Service determines that family and medical leave insurance benefits
37 under this Chapter are subject to federal income tax, the Division must advise an individual filing
38 a new claim for family and medical leave insurance benefits, at the time of filing such claim, that
39 the Internal Revenue Service has determined that benefits are subject to federal income tax and
40 that requirements exist pertaining to estimated tax payments.
41 "§ 96A-16. Family and medical leave insurance account fund; establishment and
42 investment.
43 (a) The Paid Family and Medical Leave Fund (Fund) is created in the custody of the
44 Division. Expenditures from the Fund may be used only for the purposes of the family and
45 medical leave insurance benefits program. Only the Assistant Secretary of the Division or the
46 Assistant Secretary's designee may authorize expenditures from the Fund.
47 (b) Whenever, in the judgment of the Division, there shall be in the Fund an amount of
48 funds in excess of that amount deemed by the Division to be sufficient to meet the current
49 expenditures properly payable therefrom, the Division shall have full power to invest, reinvest,
50 manage, contract, sell, or exchange investments acquired with such excess funds in the manner
51 prescribed by North Carolina law.
