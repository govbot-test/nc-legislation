GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 2
SENATE BILL 36
Elections Committee Substitute Adopted 3/18/25
Short Title: China Grove Even Year Elections. (Local)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
February 3, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE THAT REGULAR MUNICIPAL ELECTIONS IN THE TOWN OF
3 CHINA GROVE SHALL BE HELD IN EVEN-NUMBERED YEARS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) Sections 3.1, 3.2, and 3.3 of the Charter of the Town of China
6 Grove, being S.L. 2002-42, read as rewritten:
7 "Section 3.1. Regular Municipal Elections. Regular municipal elections shall be held at the
8 time of the general election in each odd-numbered year. even-numbered year. Except as
9 otherwise provided, the election shall be conducted in accordance with the uniform municipal
10 election laws of North Carolina. Elections shall be conducted on a nonpartisan plurality basis and
11 the results determined using the nonpartisan plurality method as provided in G.S. 163-292.
12 "Section 3.2. Election of Aldermen. The Town shall continue to have staggered terms for its
13 Aldermen. Two Aldermen shall be elected at the regular municipal election in 2003 to four-year
14 terms and quadrennially thereafter. Three Aldermen shall be elected at the regular municipal
15 election in 2005 to four-year terms and quadrennially thereafter.for staggered four-year terms.
16 "Section 3.3. Election of Mayor. A Mayor shall be elected in the regular municipal election
17 in 2003 and quadrennially thereafter.for a four-year term."
18 SECTION 1.(b) No municipal elections shall be conducted in the Town of China
19 Grove in 2025. The terms of office for the three Aldermen serving on the effective date of this
20 act whose terms are set to expire in 2025 are extended by one year. The terms of office of the
21 two Aldermen and the Mayor serving on the effective date of this act whose terms are set to
22 expire in 2027 are extended by one year. Regular municipal elections in the Town of China Grove
23 shall resume in even-numbered years beginning in 2026 with the same staggering of terms as
24 when elections were conducted in the odd-numbered years.
25 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to elections held
26 on or after that date.
