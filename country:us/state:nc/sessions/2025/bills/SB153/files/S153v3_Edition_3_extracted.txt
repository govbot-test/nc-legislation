1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROTECT THE BORDERS OF THE STATE BY (I) REQUIRING
3 COOPERATION WITH FEDERAL IMMIGRATION OFFICIALS, (II) ENSURING
4 STATE FUNDS ARE BEING USED FOR THE BENEFIT OF PERSONS IN THE STATE
5 LEGALLY, (III) CREATING ADDITIONAL INCENTIVES FOR LOCAL
6 GOVERNMENTS TO COMPLY WITH STATE LAWS RELATED TO IMMIGRATION,
7 AND (IV) PROHIBITING UNC CONSTITUENT INSTITUTIONS FROM BECOMING
8 SANCTUARY UNIVERSITIES.
9 Whereas, with their votes electing Donald J. Trump to be the 47th President of our
10 great nation, the American people and the people of North Carolina clearly communicated their
11 strong desire to create a safer country through increased border security and stronger immigration
12 policies; and
13 Whereas, while the federal government is ultimately responsible for the enforcement
14 of our nation's immigration laws, states and local governments have a critical role to play in
15 upholding the rule of law, fighting illegal immigration, and protecting the safety of our citizens;
16 and
17 Whereas, President Trump issued executive orders on January 20, 2025, to protect the
18 American people against invasion, to secure our borders, and to declare a national emergency at
19 the southern border of the United States, thereby continuing his promises to protect our southern
20 border and ensure immigration laws are followed and enforced; and
21 Whereas, it is the policy of the United States to take all appropriate action to secure
22 the borders of our Nation, including through cooperating fully with State and local law
23 enforcement officials in enacting federal-State partnerships to enforce federal immigration
24 priorities; and
25 Whereas, President Trump has, to ensure State and local law enforcement agencies
26 across the United States can assist with the protection of the American people, (i) instructed the
27 Secretary of Homeland Security, to the maximum extent permitted by law, and with the consent
28 of State or local officials as appropriate, to take appropriate action, through agreements under
29 section 287(g) of the INA (8 U.S.C. § 1357(g)) or otherwise, and (ii) authorized State and local
30 law enforcement officials, as the Secretary of Homeland Security determines are qualified and
31 appropriate, to perform the functions of immigration officers in relation to the investigation,
32 apprehension, or detention of aliens in the United States under the direction and the supervision
33 of the Secretary of Homeland Security; and

13 PART I. COOPERATION WITH FEDERAL IMMIGRATION OFFICIALS
14 SECTION 1.(a) The Secretary of the Department of Public Safety shall do each of
15 the following:
16 (1) Enter into a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) with the Director of U.S.
17 Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) pursuant to section 287(g) of
18 the Immigration and Nationality Act (8 U.S.C. § 1357(g)), to permit
19 designated State law enforcement officers to perform immigration law
20 enforcement functions. The designated State law enforcement officers shall
21 be required to receive appropriate training as provided by ICE and shall
22 function under the supervision of ICE officers when performing under the
23 MOA.
24 (2) Develop departmental policies requiring each of the following:
25 a. Employees of the Department shall attempt to determine if any person
26 in the custody or under the supervision of the Department is a legal
27 resident or citizen of the United States or its territories by inquiry of
28 the person, or by examination of any relevant documents, or both.
29 b. When employees are unable to determine if a person is a legal resident
30 or citizen of the United States or its territories under sub-subdivision
31 a. of this subdivision, the employees shall make a query of
32 Immigration and Customs Enforcement of the United States
33 Department of Homeland Security.
34 c. When employees determine that a person in the custody or under the
35 supervision of the Department is not a legal resident or citizen of the
36 United States or its territories, the employees shall provide requested
37 information regarding the person to Immigration and Customs
38 Enforcement of the United States Department of Homeland Security.
39 (3) Cooperate to the fullest extent allowed by law with Immigration and Customs
40 Enforcement of the United States Department of Homeland Security.
41 (4) No later than August 1, 2025, report the Memorandum of Agreement and
42 departmental policies required by this section to the Joint Legislative
43 Oversight Committee on Justice and Public Safety.

42 PART II. DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
43 SECTION 2.(a) To the extent permitted by federal law, the Department of Health
44 and Human Services (DHHS) shall take all steps necessary to cease providing State-funded
45 benefits to noncitizens residing in the United States without legal permission.
46 SECTION 2.(b) To the extent permitted by federal law, the DHHS shall develop and
47 implement a plan to review and update the eligibility criteria for all State-funded benefits to
48 ensure that noncitizens determined to be residing in the United States without legal permission
49 are ineligible to receive State-funded benefits. As a part of this plan, the DHHS shall develop a
50 method for verifying the immigration status of applicants for State-funded benefits who are
51 noncitizens prior to the initiation of benefits.

34 PART III. DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, HOUSING FINANCE AGENCY, AND
35 LOCAL HOUSING AUTHORITIES
36 SECTION 3.(a) To the extent permitted by federal law, the Department of
37 Commerce, the Housing Finance Agency, and all local housing authorities established under
38 Chapter 157 of the General Statutes shall take all steps necessary to cease providing publicly
39 funded housing benefits to noncitizens residing in the United States without legal permission.
40 SECTION 3.(b) To the extent permitted by federal law, the Department of
41 Commerce, the Housing Finance Agency, and all local housing authorities established under
42 Chapter 157 of the General Statutes shall each develop and implement a plan to review and
43 update the eligibility criteria for all publicly funded housing benefits to ensure that noncitizens
44 determined to be residing in the United States without legal permission are ineligible to receive
45 publicly funded housing benefits. As a part of this plan, the Department of Commerce, the
46 Housing Finance Agency, and all local housing authorities established under Chapter 157 of the
47 General Statutes shall develop a method for verifying the immigration status of applicants for
48 publicly funded housing benefits who are noncitizens prior to the initiation of benefits.

50 Finance Agency, and all local housing authorities established under Chapter 157 of the General

15 PART VI. UNC CONSTITUENT INSTITUTIONS TO COMPLY WITH LAWS
16 RELATED TO IMMIGRATION
17 SECTION 6. Part 3 of Article 1 of Chapter 116 of the General Statutes is amended
18 by adding a new section to read:
19 "§ 116-40.14. Adoption of sanctuary status prohibited.
20 (a) A constituent institution shall not have any policy or regulation that limits or restricts
21 the enforcement of federal immigration laws to less than the full extent permitted by federal law.
22 (b) To the extent permitted by federal law, a constituent institution shall not do any of the
23 following related to information regarding the immigration status of any individual:
24 (1) Prevent law enforcement officials or agencies from gathering the information.
25 (2) Prevent communication of the information to federal law enforcement
26 agencies."
