1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT MAKING OMNIBUS AMENDMENTS TO LAWS PERTAINING TO THE NORTH
3 CAROLINA DEPARTMENT OF LABOR.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.1. Article 16 of Chapter 95 of the General Statutes is amended by
6 adding a new section to read:
7 "§ 95-136.2. Commissioner and employees not subject to subpoena for testimony except in
8 certain circumstances.
9 (a) Neither the Commissioner nor any employee or former employee of the Department
10 is subject to a subpoena for appearance for purposes of inquiry into any occupational safety and
11 health inspection, except in one of the following circumstances:
12 (1) An enforcement proceeding is brought under this Article.
13 (2) An action is filed in which the Department is a party.
14 (3) The Commissioner consents in writing to waive the exemption provided by
15 this section.
16 (4) A court finds all of the following:
17 a. The information sought is essential to the underlying case.
18 b. There are no reasonable alternative means for acquiring the
19 information.
20 c. A significant injustice would occur if the requested testimony was not
21 available.
22 (b) This section does not apply to a subpoena requesting only documents or other
23 records."
24 SECTION 1.2. G.S. 150B-21.5 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
25 "(c1) OSHA Standard. – The Occupational Safety and Health Division of the Department
26 of Labor is not required to publish a notice of text in the North Carolina Register or hold a public
27 hearing when it proposes to adopt a rule that concerns an occupational safety and health standard
28 that is identical to a federal regulation promulgated by the Secretary of the United States
29 Department of Labor. The Division shall file the rule with the Commission for the purpose of
30 receiving written objections to the rule in accordance with G.S. 150B-21.3(b2)."
31 SECTION 1.3.(a) G.S. 95-135(d) reads as rewritten:
32 "(d) Every official act of the Commission shall be entered of record and its hearings and
33 records shall be open to the public. The Commission is authorized and empowered to make such
34 procedural rules as are necessary for the orderly transaction of its proceedings. Unless the
35 Commission adopts a different rule, the proceedings, as nearly as possible, shall be in accordance
36 with the Rules of Civil Procedure, G.S. 1A-1. The Commission may order testimony to be taken
*H568-v-2*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 by deposition in any proceeding pending before it at any stage of such proceeding. Any person,
2 firm or corporation, and its agents or officials, may be compelled to appear and testify and
3 produce like documentary evidence before the Commission. Commission, except that upon
4 motion of a respondent, the Commission shall require prehearing discovery, order that testimony
5 be taken by deposition, compel production of documents, and compel persons to appear.
6 Witnesses whose depositions are taken under this section, and the persons taking such
7 depositions, shall be entitled to the same fees as are paid for like services in the courts of the
8 State."
9 SECTION 1.3.(b) This section shall apply to citations issued on or after the effective
10 date of this act.
11 SECTION 1.4. G.S. 130A-385(e) reads as rewritten:
12 "(e) In cases where death occurred due to an injury received in the course of the decedent's
13 employment, the Chief Medical Examiner shall forward to the Commissioner of Labor a copy of
14 the medical examiner's report of the investigation, including the location of the fatal injury and
15 the name and address of the decedent's employer at the time of the fatal injury. The Chief Medical
16 Examiner shall forward this report within 30 days of receipt of the information from the medical
17 examiner. Upon written request by the Commissioner of Labor, the Chief Medical Examiner
18 shall provide the finalized autopsy report within five months of the date of the request."
19 SECTION 1.5. G.S. 95-36.3(c) reads as rewritten:
20 "(c) The Commissioner of Labor, with the written approval of the Attorney General as to
21 legality, shall have power to adopt, alter, amend or repeal appropriate rules of procedure for
22 selection of the arbitrator or panel and for conduct of the arbitration proceedings in accordance
23 with this Article: Provided, however, that such rules shall be inapplicable to the extent that they
24 are inconsistent with the arbitration agreement of the parties."
25 SECTION 1.6.(a) G.S. 95-110.2 reads as rewritten:
26 "§ 95-110.2. Scope.
27 This Article shall govern the design, construction, installation, plans review, testing,
28 inspection, certification, operation, use, maintenance, alteration, relocation and investigation of
29 accidents involving:
30 (1) Elevators, dumbwaiters, escalators, and moving walks;
31 (2) Personnel hoists;
32 (3) Inclined stairway chair lifts;
33 (4) Inclined and vertical wheelchair lifts;
34 (5) Manlifts; and
35 (6) Special equipment.
36 This Article shall not apply to devices and equipment located and operated in a single family
37 residence, residence. This Article shall not apply to conveyors and related equipment within the
38 scope of the American National Standard Safety Standard for Conveyors and Related Equipment
39 (ANSI/ASME B20.1) constructed, installed and used exclusively for the movement of materials,
40 or to mining equipment specifically covered by the Federal Mine Safety and Health Act or the
41 Mine Safety and Health Act of North Carolina or the rules and regulations adopted pursuant
42 thereto."
43 SECTION 1.6.(b) G.S. 95-110.3 reads as rewritten:
44 "§ 95-110.3. Definitions.
45 (a) The term "Commissioner" shall mean the North Carolina Commissioner of Labor or
46 his the Commissioner's authorized representative.
47 (b) The term "Director" shall mean the Director of the Elevator and Amusement Device
48 Division Bureau of the North Carolina Department of Labor.
49 …."
50 SECTION 1.6.(c) G.S. 95-110.4 reads as rewritten:
51 "§ 95-110.4. Elevator and Amusement Device Division Bureau established.

41 months;months.

43 equipment as are found to be in compliance with this Article and the rules and
44 regulations promulgated thereunder;thereunder.
45 (8) To have free access, with or without notice, to the devices and equipment
46 subject to the provisions of this Article, during reasonable hours, for purposes
47 of inspection or testing;testing.

49 with the provisions of Article 4A of Chapter 15 of the General
