27 PART II. AUTHORIZE ONLINE APPLICATION TO OBTAIN A FULL PROVISIONAL
28 DRIVERS LICENSE
29 SECTION 2.(a) G.S. 20-11(f) reads as rewritten:
30 "(f) Level 3. – A person who is at least 16 years old but less than 18 years old may obtain
31 a full provisional license if the person meets all of the following requirements:
32 (1) Has held a limited provisional license issued by the Division for at least six
33 months.
34 (2) Has not been convicted of a motor vehicle moving violation or seat belt
35 infraction or a violation of G.S. 20-137.3 during the preceding six months.
36 (3) Has a driving eligibility certificate or a high school diploma or its equivalent.
37 (4) Has completed a driving log, on a form approved by the Division, detailing a
38 minimum of 12 hours as the operator of a motor vehicle of a class for which
39 the driver is licensed. The log must show at least six hours of the required
40 driving occurred during nighttime hours. The driving log must be signed by
41 the supervising driver for any hours driven outside the provisions of
42 subdivision (e)(2) of this section and submitted to the Division at the time the
43 applicant seeks to obtain a full provisional license. If the Division has cause
44 to believe that a driving log has been falsified, the limited provisional licensee
45 shall be required to complete a new driving log with the same requirements
46 and shall not be eligible to obtain a full provisional license for six months.
47 A person who meets these requirements may obtain a full provisional license by applying online
48 or by mail."

31 PART VI. DIVISION OF MOTOR VEHICLES TO MAKE EFFORTS TO IMPROVE
32 EFFICIENCY FOR DRIVERS LICENSE APPLICANTS
33 SECTION 6. The Division of Motor Vehicles shall make a good-faith effort to
34 prioritize new resident drivers license applicants, commercial drivers license applicants, and
35 first-time drivers license applicants in providing appointment availability. The Division shall also
36 clearly outline on the Division's website the services that are available to customers online or by
37 other means that do not require an in-person office visit.
38
39 PART VII. EFFECTIVE DATE
40 SECTION 7. Parts I through III of this act become effective October 1, 2025, and
41 apply to limited learner's permit or provisional license applications submitted on or after that
42 date. Part IV of this act is effective when it becomes law and applies to limited provisional license
43 applications submitted on or after that date. Part IV of this act expires on December 31, 2025.
44 Part V of this act becomes effective October 1, 2025. The remainder of this act is effective when
45 it becomes law.
