HOUSE BILL 224

Sponsors: Representatives Lowery, B. Jones, Ward, and Chesser (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.

3 REVENUE FUND AND TO APPROPRIATE ADDITIONAL FUNDS FROM THAT

5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 143C-9-7 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 143C-9-7. Indian Gaming Education Revenue Fund.
8 (a) The "Indian Gaming Education Revenue Fund" is established in the State Treasury.
9 Funds shall be expended from the Indian Gaming Education Revenue Fund only by specific
10 appropriation by the General Assembly.
11 (b) Upon Except as provided in subsection (c) of this section, upon appropriation by the
12 General Assembly, funds received in the Indian Gaming Education Revenue Fund shall be
13 allocated quarterly by the State Board of Education to local school administrative units, charter
14 schools, and regional schools on the basis of allotted average daily membership. The funds
15 allotted by the State Board of Education pursuant to this section shall be nonreverting. Funds
16 received pursuant to this section by local school administrative units in this State shall be
17 expended for the sole purpose of educating children in the classroom.
18 (c) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, upon appropriation by the
19 General Assembly, funds received in the Indian Gaming Fund shall be allocated quarterly to the
20 North Carolina State Commission of Indian Affairs, the non-gaming Indian tribes recognized by
21 the State in Chapter 71A of the General Statutes, and the four Urban Indian Organizations that
22 are members of the North Carolina Commission of Indian Affairs. Funds received pursuant to
23 this section by the North Carolina State Commission of Indian Affairs shall be expended for the
24 Commission's operating expenses and programs. Funds received pursuant to this section by the
25 non-gaming Tribes and Urban Indian Organizations shall be expended to support cultural and
26 economic development."
27 SECTION 2.(a) There is appropriated from the Indian Gaming Fund, as renamed in
28 Section 1 of this act, to the Department of Administration, North Carolina State Commission of
29 Indian Affairs, the sum of two million dollars ($2,000,000) in recurring funds for each year of
30 the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium for the Commission's operating expenses and programs.
31 SECTION 2.(b) There is appropriated from the Indian Gaming Fund, as renamed in
32 Section 1 of this act, to the Office of State Budget and Management the sum of five million two
33 hundred fifty thousand dollars ($5,250,000) in recurring funds for each year of the 2025-2027
34 fiscal biennium to provide directed grants to the seven non-gaming Indian tribes to be used for

1 cultural, educational, and economic development. These funds shall be divided equally among
2 the following:
3 (1) The Coharie of Sampson and Harnett Counties.
4 (2) The Haliwa-Saponi of Halifax, Warren, and adjoining counties.
5 (3) The Lumbee of Robeson, Hoke, and Scotland Counties.
6 (4) The Meherrin of Hertford, Bertie, Northampton, and Gates Counties.
7 (5) The Waccamaw-Siouan from Columbus and Bladen Counties.
8 (6) The Sappony of Person County.
9 (7) The Occaneechi Band of the Saponi Nation of Orange and surrounding
10 counties.
11 SECTION 2.(c) There is appropriated from the Indian Gaming Fund, as renamed in
12 Section 1 of this act, to the Office of State Budget and Management the sum of four hundred
13 thousand dollars ($400,000) in recurring funds for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to
14 provide directed grants to the four Urban Indian Organizations to be used for cultural,
15 educational, and economic development. These funds shall be divided equally among the
16 following:
17 (1) The Cumberland County Association for Indian People of Cumberland
18 County.
19 (2) The Guilford Native Americans Association of Guilford and surrounding
20 counties.
21 (3) The Metrolina Native Americans Association of Mecklenburg and
22 surrounding counties.
23 (4) The Triangle Native American Society of Wake and surrounding counties.
24 SECTION 3. There is appropriated from the Indian Gaming Fund, as renamed in
25 Section 1 of this act, the sum of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) in recurring funds for
26 each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium to support the State Advisory Council on Indian
27 Education.
28 SECTION 4. There is appropriated from the Indian Gaming Fund, as renamed in
29 Section 1 of this act, to the Office of State Budget and Management the sum of one million one
30 hundred thousand dollars ($1,100,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to
31 provide directed grants as follows:
32 (1) Five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) to the Haliwa-Saponi Tribe,
33 Incorporated, for operating costs and capital needs of the Haliwa-Saponi
34 Tribal School.
35 (2) Five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) to Old Main STREAM Academy,
36 Inc., for operating costs and capital needs of the Old Main STREAM
37 Academy.
38 (3) One hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) to the Waccamaw-Siouan Indian
39 Tribe, Inc., to support the Waccamaw Siouan Daycare.
40 SECTION 5. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
