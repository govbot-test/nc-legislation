Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Support the State Highway Patrol. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Logan, K. Brown, G. Pierce, and Price (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 2, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT PROVIDING COMPENSATION INCREASES TO MEMBERS OF THE STATE
3 HIGHWAY PATROL, DIRECTING THE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL TO DEVELOP
4 SECONDARY EMPLOYMENT POLICIES, ENCOURAGING RETIRED MEMBERS OF
5 THE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL TO RETURN TO SERVICE WITH THE STATE,
6 SUPPORTING THE WORK OF THE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL, AND
7 APPROPRIATING FUNDS.
8 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
9
10 PART I. RETAIN AND ATTRACT MEMBERS OF THE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL
11
12 PROVIDE COMPENSATION INCREASES TO MEMBERS OF THE STATE
13 HIGHWAY PATROL
14 SECTION 1.1.(a) Effective July 1, 2025, there is appropriated from the General
15 Fund to the Reserve for Compensation Increases to provide pay raises for the State Highway
16 Patrol & Law Enforcement Support Staff the sum of forty million four hundred eighty-two
17 thousand four hundred eighty dollars ($40,482,480) in each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal
18 biennium to establish the pay scales set forth in this section.
19 SECTION 1.1.(b) Effective July 1, 2025, the law enforcement officers of the State
20 Highway Patrol shall be paid based upon the following experience-based annual salary schedule:
21 Years of Experience 2025-2027 Biennium
22 0 $56,000
23 1 $62,125
24 2 $68,250
25 3 $75,375
26 4 $80,500
27 5 $86,625
28 6 $92,750
29 7 $98,875
30 8+ $105,000
31 SECTION 1.1.(c) Effective July 1, 2025, sworn State Highway Patrol employees
32 who are not subject to an experience-based annual salary schedule shall be paid at the following
33 rates by rank:
34 Rank 2025-2027 Biennium
35 Sergeant $120,750

1 First Sergeant $132,825
2 Lieutenant $146,108
3 Captain $160,718
4 Major $176,790
5 Lieutenant Colonel $194,469
6 Deputy Commander $204,193
7 Colonel $224,612
8
9 ALLOW MEMBERS OF THE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL TO WORK OTHER JOBS
10 SECTION 1.2.(a) The Commander of the State Highway Patrol shall develop and
11 maintain secondary employment policies that allow members of the State Highway Patrol to
12 engage in permanent or temporary secondary employment so long as that employment meets
13 each of the following criteria:
14 (1) It will not have an adverse effect on the member's work or performance with
15 the State Highway Patrol or on the mission of the State Highway Patrol.
16 (2) It will not create a conflict of interest with the member's job description or
17 performance with the State Highway Patrol.
18 SECTION 1.2.(b) The policies created pursuant to this section shall allow members
19 with an active, non-probationary law enforcement certification to perform secondary
20 employment that utilizes that certification, so long as the employment does not otherwise conflict
21 with the secondary employment policies.
22 SECTION 1.2.(c) The policies created pursuant to this section shall be completed
23 by July 1, 2025.
24
25 ENCOURAGE RETIRED MEMBERS OF THE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL TO
26 RETURN TO SERVICE WITH THE STATE
27 SECTION 1.3. No later than January 1, 2026, the State Highway Patrol shall
28 evaluate and update any internal hiring policies or practices to ensure that those hiring policies
29 or practices encourage the return to work of retired members of the State Highway Patrol in a
30 manner that does not impact the retired members' retirement allowances, to the extent allowed
31 by State law, including under Chapter 135 of the General Statutes.
32
33 PART II. SUPPORT FOR THE WORK OF THE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL
34
35 FUND BULLETPROOF GLASS IN VEHICLES OF STATE LAW ENFORCEMENT
36 OFFICERS
37 SECTION 2.1.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the State Highway
38 Patrol the sum of three million two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($3,250,000) in recurring funds
39 and twenty million one hundred fifty thousand dollars ($20,150,000) in nonrecurring funds for
40 the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used to purchase, install, and maintain bulletproof glass on all
41 State Highway Patrol vehicles.
42 SECTION 2.1.(b) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the State Highway
43 Patrol the sum of two hundred twenty-one thousand dollars ($221,000) in recurring funds and
44 one million three hundred seventy-eight thousand dollars ($1,378,000) in nonrecurring funds for
45 the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used to purchase, install, and maintain bulletproof glass on all
46 State Capitol Police vehicles.
47 SECTION 2.1.(c) This section becomes effective July 1, 2025.
48
49 FUNDS TO SUPPORT HUNTERSVILLE STATE HIGHWAY PATROL STATION
50 SECTION 2.2.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the State Highway
51 Patrol the sum of six hundred forty-three thousand one hundred fifty dollars ($643,150) in
Page 2 House Bill 662-First Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used for the following purposes at the
2 State Highway Patrol Station in Huntersville, North Carolina:
3 (1) Six hundred forty thousand dollars ($640,000) for fuel infrastructure to
4 include an above-ground storage tank, fuel dispenser, FuelMaster Live
5 functionality, and a concrete island with an awning, lights, and electrical
6 outlets.
7 (2) Three thousand dollars ($3,000) for an air compressor for filling tires to be
8 located on the concrete island funded in subdivision (1) of this subsection.
9 (3) One hundred fifty dollars ($150.00) for a vehicle vacuum to be located on the
10 concrete island funded in subdivision (1) of this subsection.
11 SECTION 2.2.(b) The State Highway Patrol shall enter into a memorandum of
12 agreement with the Department of Transportation for the creation, use, and maintenance of the
13 items funded by this section.
14 SECTION 2.2.(c) This section becomes effective July 1, 2025.
15
16 PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
17 SECTION 3.1. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
18 law.
