18 PART VI. INTERSTATE COMPACT FOR SCHOOL PSYCHOLOGISTS
19 SECTION 6.(a) Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
20 Article to read:
21 "Article 17F.
22 "School Psychologist Interstate Licensure Compact.
23 "§ 115C-270.40. Purpose.
24 The purpose of this Compact is to facilitate the interstate practice of School Psychology in
25 educational or school settings, and in so doing to improve the availability of School
26 Psychological Services to the public. This Compact is intended to establish a pathway to allow
27 School Psychologists to obtain equivalent licenses to provide School Psychological Services in
28 any Member State. In this way, this Compact shall enable the Member States to ensure that safe
29 and effective School Psychological Services are available and delivered by appropriately
30 qualified professionals in their educational settings. To facilitate the objectives described above,
31 this Compact does the following:
32 (1) Enables School Psychologists who qualify for receipt of an Equivalent
33 License to practice in other Member States without first satisfying
34 burdensome and duplicative requirements.
35 (2) Promotes the mobility of School Psychologists between and among the
36 Member States in order to address workforce shortages and to ensure that safe
37 and reliable School Psychological Services are available in each Member
38 State.
39 (3) Enhances the public accessibility of School Psychological Services by
40 increasing the availability of qualified, licensed School Psychologists through
41 the establishment of an efficient and streamlined pathway for Licensees to
42 practice in other Member States.
43 (4) Preserves and respects the authority of each Member State to protect the health
44 and safety of its residents by ensuring that only qualified, licensed
45 professionals are authorized to provide School Psychological Services within
46 that state.
47 (5) Requires School Psychologists practicing within a Member State to comply
48 with the Scope of Practice laws present in the state where the School
49 Psychological Services are being provided.
50 (6) Promotes cooperation between the Member States in regulating the practice
51 of School Psychology within those states.

20 (1) If the Commission determines that a Member State has defaulted in the
21 performance of its obligations or responsibilities under this Compact or the

24 means of curing the default, and any other action that the Commission may
25 take and shall offer training and specific technical assistance regarding the
26 default.

28 Member States.

32 terminated on the effective date of termination. A cure of the default does not relieve the
