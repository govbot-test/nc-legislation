GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 361
Short Title: Funds to Fight Deed and Title Fraud. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Reives, Winslow, Schietzelt, and Liu (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
March 12, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS FOR TRAINING AND TECHNOLOGY UPGRADES
3 TO PREVENT AND DETER DEED AND TITLE FRAUD.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding G.S. 143C-5-2, there is appropriated from the
6 General Fund to the Department of the Secretary of State the sum of one million eight hundred
7 thousand dollars ($1,800,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used in
8 collaboration with the North Carolina Association of Registers of Deeds to provide antifraud
9 training for employees of county register of deeds offices. The Department of the Secretary of
10 State, in conjunction with the North Carolina Association of Registers of Deeds, may use an
11 amount not exceeding ten percent (10%) of the funds appropriated in this section for
12 administrative costs.
13 SECTION 2. Notwithstanding G.S. 143C-5-2, there is appropriated from the
14 General Fund to the Department of Justice the sum of five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000)
15 in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used to create and launch a statewide
16 public awareness campaign on fraud relating to real property.
17 SECTION 3. Notwithstanding G.S. 143C-5-2, there is appropriated from the
18 General Fund to the Department of Information Technology the sum of thirty million dollars
19 ($30,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used to provide
20 need-based grants to the county register of deeds offices, said grants to be used in accordance
21 with this section. The Department of Information Technology may award grants to the county
22 register of deeds offices for a total amount not exceeding forty percent (40%) of the funds
23 appropriated in this section to be used by the county register of deeds offices to purchase scanning
24 equipment and provide for the digitization and storing of records kept by that office, including,
25 but not limited to, identification cards, passports, and other forms of identification. The
26 Department of Information Technology may award grants to the county register of deeds offices
27 for a total amount not exceeding fifteen percent (15%) of the funds appropriated in this section
28 to be used by the county register of deeds offices for the purchase of software upgrades. The
29 Department of Information Technology may award grants to the county register of deeds offices
30 for a total amount not exceeding ten percent (10%) of the funds appropriated in this section to be
31 used by the county register of deeds offices to procure cloud storage and backup. The Department
32 of Information Technology may award grants to the county register of deeds offices for a total
33 amount not exceeding ten percent (10%) of the funds appropriated in this section to be used by
34 the county register of deeds offices for the purpose of enhancing the digital security of those
35 offices. The Department of Information Technology may award grants to the county register of

16 system in place as of the effective date of this section. The Department of Information
17 Technology shall develop a process by which county register of deeds offices may apply for the
18 grants funded by this section.
