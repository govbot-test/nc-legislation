10 SECTION 3.1.(a) Chapter 20 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
11 Article to read:
12 "Article 7C.
13 "Commercial Motor Vehicle Tower Permits and Regulation.
14 "§ 20-219.35. Definitions.
15 The following definitions apply in this Article:
16 (1) Reserved for future codification purposes.
17 (2) Reserved for future codification purposes.
18 (3) Commercial motor vehicle. – As defined in G.S. 20-4.01(3d)a. and
19 G.S. 20-4.01(3d)b.
20 (4) Tow operations. – The removal and storage of a commercial motor vehicle or
21 commercial motor vehicle combination by a tower. Tow operations does not
22 include a commercial transaction for transport of a damaged vehicle arranged
23 or authorized by an insurance company and delivered to a salvage pool
24 operator.
25 (5) Tower. – As defined in G.S. 20‑219.9, and includes owners and employee
26 operators.
27 "§ 20-219.36. Commercial motor vehicle tower permit.
28 (a) A tower engaged in the business of conducting tow operations shall obtain a
29 commercial motor vehicle tower permit from the Division of Motor Vehicles.
30 (b) A permit issued by the Division shall be valid for a period of two years unless revoked
31 or suspended by the Division.
32 (c) The Division is authorized to charge a nonrefundable fee for an initial application and
33 for the renewal application for a permit.
34 (d) A tower shall pay the costs associated with any background checks required by this
35 Article for permit applicants.
36 (e) A commercial motor vehicle tower permit is nontransferable.
37 "§ 20-219.37. Commercial motor vehicle tower permit application requirements.
38 (a) The Division shall prescribe the form of the application for a commercial motor
39 vehicle tower permit.
40 (b) The Division shall issue a permit when the applicant provides evidence of meeting all
41 of the following requirements:
42 (1) The applicant completes a criminal background check. If the permit applicant
43 is an employee operator of a business engaged in tow operations, the applicant
44 has had no felony convictions within the previous five years. If the applicant
45 is an owner of business engaged in tow operations, the applicant has had no
46 felony convictions within the previous 10 years.
47 (2) The applicant has a valid commercial drivers license for the class of motor
48 vehicle conducting tow operations.
49 (3) The applicant utilizes a storage yard that complies with the minimum space
50 requirements of the city, county, and State zoning regulations with valid
51 zoning permits.

3 (5) The applicant has completed an accredited training program, approved by the

17 pleaded guilty or nolo contendere to, any felony or misdemeanor shall notify the Division within
18 14 days.
19 (d) A tower must carry its permit when conducting tow operations.
20 "§ 20-219.39. Return of commercial cargo.
21 A permit holder shall promptly return any commercial cargo towed by the tower to the owner
22 of the commercial cargo, or to a designee of the owner, upon request. In the case of a trailer
23 containing commercial cargo, the tower shall allow the trailer containing the commercial cargo
24 to be exchanged with a trailer of similar type that is in working condition and was manufactured
25 within five years of the manufacturing date of the original trailer, or newer, as arranged by the
26 commercial cargo owner and upon verification the released trailer is covered by collision
27 insurance coverage or a rider for towing and remediation insurance. The insurance requirement
28 of this section does not apply to vehicles registered as farm vehicles.
29 "§ 20-219.40. Towing practices deemed unfair trade practice.
30 A tower engaging in tow operations without a valid permit constitutes an unfair trade practice
31 under G.S. 75-1.1."
