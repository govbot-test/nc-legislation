Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Shorter Separation for Retired ADAs and APDs. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Alexander and Johnson (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 24, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ALLOW RETIRED ASSISTANT DISTRICT ATTORNEYS AND RETIRED
3 ASSISTANT PUBLIC DEFENDERS TO RETURN TO WORK FOR THE STATE AFTER
4 A THIRTY-DAY SEPARATION.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 135-1(20) reads as rewritten:
7 (20) "Retirement" under this Chapter, except as otherwise provided, means the
8 commencement of monthly retirement benefits along with termination of
9 employment and the complete separation from active service with no intent or
10 agreement, express or implied, to return to service. A retirement allowance
11 under the provisions of this Chapter may only be granted upon retirement of
12 a member. In order for a member's retirement to become effective in any
13 month, the member must perform no work for an employer, including
14 part-time, temporary, substitute, or contractor work, at any time during the six
15 months immediately following the effective date of retirement. retirement,
16 except that in the case of a member who is a retired assistant district attorney
17 or retired assistant public defender, the period of separation required is 30
18 days. A member who is a full-time faculty member of The University of North
19 Carolina may effect a retirement allowance under this Chapter,
20 notwithstanding the six-month requirement above, provided the member
21 immediately enters the University's Phased Retirement Program for Tenured
22 Faculty as that program existed on May 25, 2011. For purposes of this
23 subdivision, all of the following shall not be considered service or work:
24 …."
25 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 135-3(d) reads as rewritten:
26 "(d) If a beneficiary who retired on an early or service retirement allowance under this
27 Chapter is reemployed by, or otherwise engaged to perform services for, an employer
28 participating in the Retirement System on a part-time, temporary, interim, or on a fee for service
29 basis, whether contractual or otherwise at any time during (i) the six months immediately
30 following the effective date of retirement, retirement or, (ii) if the beneficiary is a retired assistant
31 district attorney or retired assistant public defender, the 30 days immediately following the
32 effective date of retirement, then the option of the following subdivisions that has the lesser
33 financial impact on the member, as determined by the Retirement System, shall be applied:
34 …."
35 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 135-106(b) reads as rewritten:
*S364-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 "(b) After the commencement of benefits under this section, the benefits payable under
2 the terms of this section during the first 36 months of the long-term disability period shall be
3 equal to sixty-five percent (65%) of 1/12th of the annual base rate of compensation last payable
4 to the participant or beneficiary prior to the beginning of the short-term disability period as may
5 be adjusted for percentage increases as provided under G.S. 135-108, plus sixty-five percent
6 (65%) of 1/12th of the annual longevity payment to which the participant or beneficiary would
7 be eligible, to a maximum of three thousand nine hundred dollars ($3,900) per month reduced by
8 any primary Social Security disability benefits to which the beneficiary may be entitled, effective
9 as of the first of the month following the month of initial entitlement, and by monthly payments
10 for Workers' Compensation to which the participant or beneficiary may be entitled. When
11 primary Social Security disability benefits are increased by cost-of-living adjustments, the
12 increased reduction shall be applied in the first month following the month in which the member
13 becomes entitled to the increased Social Security benefit. The monthly benefit shall be further
14 reduced by the amount of any monthly payments from the federal Department of Veterans
15 Affairs, any other federal agency or any payments made under the provisions of G.S. 127A-108,
16 to which the participant or beneficiary may be entitled on account of the same disability.
17 Provided, in any event, the benefit payable shall be no less than ten dollars ($10.00) a month.
18 However, a disabled participant may elect to receive any salary continuation as provided in
19 G.S. 135-104 in lieu of long-term disability benefits; provided such election shall not extend the
20 first 36 consecutive calendar months of the long-term disability period. An election to receive
21 any salary continuation for any part of any given day shall be in lieu of any long-term benefit
22 payable for that day, provided further, any lump-sum payout for vacation leave shall be treated
23 as if the beneficiary or participant had exhausted the leave and shall be in lieu of any long-term
24 benefit otherwise payable. Provided that, in any event, a beneficiary's benefit shall be reduced
25 during the first 36 months of the long-term disability period by an amount, as determined by the
26 Board of Trustees, equal to a primary Social Security retirement benefit to which the beneficiary
27 might be entitled, effective as of the first of the month following the month of initial entitlement.
28 …
29 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the long-term disability benefit is payable so long as the
30 beneficiary is disabled and is in receipt of a primary Social Security disability benefit until the
31 earliest date at which the beneficiary is eligible for an unreduced service retirement allowance
32 from the Retirement System, at which time the beneficiary would receive a retirement allowance
33 calculated on the basis of the beneficiary's average final compensation at the time of disability as
34 adjusted to reflect compensation increases subsequent to the time of disability and the creditable
35 service accumulated by the beneficiary, including creditable service while in receipt of benefits
36 under the Plan. In the event the beneficiary has not been approved and is not in receipt of a
37 primary Social Security disability benefit, the long-term disability benefit shall cease after the
38 first 36 months of the long-term disability period. When such a long-term disability recipient
39 begins receiving this unreduced service retirement allowance from the System, that recipient
40 shall not be subject to the six-month applicable waiting period set forth in G.S. 135-1(20).
41 However, a beneficiary shall be entitled to a restoration of the long-term disability benefit in the
42 event the Social Security Administration grants a retroactive approval for primary Social Security
43 disability benefits with a benefit effective date within the first 36 months of the long-term
44 disability period. In such event, the long-term disability benefit shall be restored retroactively to
45 the date of cessation."
46 SECTION 2.(a) The State Treasurer shall seek a private letter ruling from the
47 Internal Revenue Service to determine if Section 1 of this act jeopardizes the status of the
48 Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System.
49 SECTION 2.(b) If the Internal Revenue Service determines that Section 1 of this act
50 jeopardizes the status of the Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System of North Carolina
51 under the Internal Revenue Code, then Section 1 of this act is repealed on the last day of the
Page 2 Senate Bill 364-First Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 month following the month of receipt of that determination by the State Treasurer. Upon receipt
2 of that determination, the State Treasurer shall notify the Revisor of Statutes of the determination
3 and the date of receipt. Within three business days of receipt of the determination, the State
4 Treasurer shall notify all employers of the repeal of Section 1 of this act and shall publicly notice
5 the receipt of this information on the Department of State Treasurer's website. Within three
6 business days of receipt of the notice from the State Treasurer, an employer shall notify all former
7 assistant district attorneys and former assistant public defenders reemployed by that employer of
8 the repeal of Section 1 of this act.
9 SECTION 2.(c) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, in order
10 to pay costs associated with the administration of the provisions of this act, the Retirement
11 Systems Division of the Department of State Treasurer may increase receipts from the retirement
12 assets of the Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System or pay costs associated with the
13 administration directly from the retirement assets.
14 SECTION 3. Effective July 1, 2025, there is appropriated from the General Fund to
15 the Department of State Treasurer the sum of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) in
16 nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used to obtain the private letter ruling
17 required under Section 2 of this act.
18 SECTION 4. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective January 1, 2027.
