15 PART I. USE OF STATE-OWNED PROPERTY FOR CHILDCARE CENTERS FOR
16 STATE EMPLOYEES
17 SECTION 1.(a) The Department of Administration shall report to the Joint
18 Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services and the Joint Legislative
19 Commission on Governmental Operations, no later than March 31, 2026, on the feasibility and
20 advisability of using obsolete or underutilized state-owned buildings (available buildings) to
21 house childcare facilities giving childcare priority to State employees. The report shall include
22 the following:
23 (1) Location of each available building.
24 (2) Estimated costs for upfitting each property to meet daycare licensing
25 standards.
26 (3) Estimated costs for asbestos and lead remediation.
27 (4) Barriers to the available buildings' use as a childcare facility.
28 SECTION 1.(b) Any project undertaken by the State to build or renovate property
29 having a budget greater than five million dollars ($5,000,000) shall include a child care center or
30 adult day care center if more than 250 people would work in the building. This requirement
31 includes current projects which have not broken ground by July 1, 2025, unless one or both of
32 the following exceptions apply:
33 (1) Inclusion of either type of center would delay the project by six months or
34 more.
35 (2) Inclusion of either type of center would increase the project cost by 10% or
36 more.

2 PART II. CHILDCARE FOR STATE EMPLOYEES
3 SECTION 2.(a) The Division of Child Development and Early Education shall
4 establish a pilot program for onsite childcare for State employees as follows:
5 (1) The Division of Child Development and Early Education shall contract with
6 a private commercial childcare provider to establish three childcare centers
7 for State employees use to be established on State-owned property that is
8 unused or underutilized.
9 (2) Priority in contracting shall be given to commercial childcare providers who
10 currently operate five or fewer facilities and provide high quality childcare.
11 (3) The Division of Child Development and Early Education must include the
12 following terms in the contract with the commercial childcare providers:
13 a. The childcare facility must operate an apprenticeship program in
14 conjunction with a public or private university or community college
15 who operates an early childcare education program.
16 b. The childcare center must comply with the requirements established
17 by the Division of Child Development and Early Education for
18 apprenticeship program.
19 c. The childcare center must maintain the operation of the apprenticeship
20 program for so long as the childcare center is housed in state-owned
21 property and the university or community college is willing to
22 continue the partnership.
23 d. The State will provide the upfit cost of the space to meet the licensure
24 requirements at reasonable levels that are customary at the operators'
25 other facilities and those similarly situated and provide use of the space
26 rent free, notwithstanding the provisions of G.S. 146-29.1.
27 e. The lease terms for state-owned property must be approved as required
28 by G.S. 146-27.
29 SECTION 2.(b) The Division of Child Development and Early Education shall
30 create the requirements for the apprenticeship program and pair the childcare centers with a
31 university or community college early education program utilizing parameters similar to the
32 Durham Childcare Apprenticeship Program.
33 SECTION 2.(c) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
34 Health and Human Resources the sum of five million dollars ($5,000,000) for the 2025-2026
35 fiscal year for the Division of Child Development and Early Education's use in establishing the
36 childcare centers as required by Section 2(a) of this act.
37 SECTION 2.(d) If any expenses are incurred by the State for asbestos or lead
38 remediation in establishing the childcare centers required by Section 2(a) of this act, the State
39 shall be eligible for reimbursement from the Asbestos and Lead Remediation Fund subject to the
40 rules of the fund for up to five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000).
41 SECTION 2.(e) By April 1, 2027, the Division of Child Development and Early
42 Education shall report on and make recommendations to the Joint Legislative Oversight
43 Committee on Health and Human Services, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on
44 General Government, and the Fiscal Research Division the status of the implementation of the
45 pilot program including successes, concerns, problems encountered, enrollment, and
46 expenditures.
47 SECTION 2.(f) Sections 2(c) and 2(d) of this act are effective July 1, 2025.

10 PART IV. ONSITE CHILDCARE AT EACH NORTH CAROLINA COMMUNITY
11 COLLEGE AND COLLEGE IN THE UNIVERSITY OF NORTH CAROLINA SYSTEM
12 SECTION 4.1.(a) Report. – The State Board of Community Colleges shall study
13 and report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee, the Joint Legislative
14 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services, and the Fiscal Research Division of the
15 General Assembly, no later than March 31, 2026, on the feasibility and advisability of
16 implementing a publicly available child care program on every community college campus that
17 offers priority enrollment to the children of community college employees and students. The
18 report shall include the following:
19 (1) Recommendations for implementing the childcare program.
20 (2) Estimated costs for implementation and maintenance of the childcare
21 program.
22 (3) Return on investment of the childcare program.
23 (4) Issues related to historical and ongoing utilization of grants or other funding.
24 (5) Barriers to implementation of the childcare program.
25 SECTION 4.1.(b) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Community
26 College System Office the sum of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) for the study required
27 by Section 4.1(a) of this act.
28 SECTION 4.1.(c) Section 4.1(b) of this act is effective July 1, 2025.
29 SECTION 4.2.(a) The Board of Governors of the University of North Carolina shall
30 study and report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee, the Joint Legislative
31 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services, and the Fiscal Research Division of the
32 General Assembly, no later than March 31, 2026, on the feasibility and advisability of
33 implementing a publicly available childcare program at each constituent institution campus that
34 offers priority enrollment to the children of university employees and students. The report shall
35 include:
36 (1) Recommendations for implementing the childcare program.
37 (2) Estimated costs for implementation and maintenance of the childcare
38 program.
39 (3) Return on investment of the childcare program.
40 (4) Issues related to historical and ongoing utilization of grants or other funding.
41 (5) Barriers to implementation of the childcare program.
42 SECTION 4.2.(b) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Board of
43 Governors the sum of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) for the study required by Section
44 4.2(a) of this act.
45 SECTION 4.2.(c) Section 4.2(b) of this act is effective July 1, 2025.

51 establish the Commercial Childcare Licensure Workgroup to examine streamlining requirements

17 and sanitation codes.
18 (2) Developing statewide, uniform application of the commercial childcare
19 licensing requirements related to the physical facility including building
20 codes, fire codes, and sanitation codes and statewide, uniform inspections
21 related to those codes.
22 (3) Resolving conflicts between various code requirements for commercial
23 childcare licensing.
24 SECTION 5.(c) By January 1, 2026, the Division of Child Development and Early
25 Education shall report the findings and recommendations of the work group to the Joint
26 Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services, the Joint Legislative Oversight
27 Committee on General Government, and the Fiscal Research Division.
