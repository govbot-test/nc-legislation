11 …
12 (b) Categories. – The following three distinct classifications of company police officers
13 are established:
14 …
15 (1a) Hospital Police Officers. – Those company police officers who are employed
16 by a hospital as that term is defined under G.S. 131E-76(3).
17 …
18 (d1) Hospital Police. – Hospital police officers have the powers contained in subsection
19 (c) of this section and also have the powers in that subsection upon that portion of any public
20 road or highway passing through or immediately adjoining the property described in that
21 subsection, wherever located. The governing body of a hospital as defined by G.S. 131E-76(2)
22 may enter into a mutual aid agreement with the governing board of a municipality or, with the
23 consent of the county sheriff, a county to the same extent as a municipal police department
24 pursuant to Chapter 160A of the General Statutes.
25 …
26 (f1) Hospital Police CJLEADS Access. – Unless contrary to any federal law, regulation,
27 or requirement, a hospital police agency certified pursuant to this Chapter shall be granted access
28 to the Criminal Justice Law Enforcement Automated Data System (CJLEADS) upon (i) request
29 of the agency and (ii) compliance with the requirements established by the Government Data
30 Analytics Center (GDAC) for access to that system, including the execution of a license and
31 usage agreement.
32 (f2) Hospital Police DCIN Access. – Pursuant to all laws, rules, and regulations not
33 inconsistent with this subsection, hospital police agencies shall be granted access to and deemed
34 participating agencies of the Division of Criminal Information Network (DCIN) created under
35 G.S. 143B-905 in a manner equivalent, at a minimum, to campus police certified by the Attorney
36 General pursuant to Chapter 74G of the General Statutes.
