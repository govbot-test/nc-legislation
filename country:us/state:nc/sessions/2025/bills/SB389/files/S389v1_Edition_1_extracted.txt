15 requirements:
16 a. Is eligible to attend a North Carolina public school pursuant to Article
17 25 of this Chapter. A child who is the age of 4 on or before April 16 is

5 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 115C-562.2 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 115C-562.2. Scholarship grants.
7 (a) The Authority shall make available no later than February 1 annually applications to
8 eligible students for the award of scholarship grants to attend any nonpublic school on a full- or
9 part-time basis. Information about scholarship grants and the application process shall be made
10 available on the Authority's Web site. Beginning March 15, the Authority shall begin awarding
11 scholarship grants to students who have applied by March 1 in the following order:
12 (1) Eligible students who received a scholarship grant for the school year prior to
13 the school year for which the students are applying.
14 (2) Eligible students qualifying for a scholarship grant in the amount provided
15 under subdivision (1) of subsection (b2) of this section.residing in households
16 with an income level not in excess of the amount required for the student to
17 qualify for the federal free or reduced-price lunch program.
18 (3) Eligible students qualifying for a scholarship grant in the amount provided
19 under subdivision (2) of subsection (b2) of this section.residing in households
20 with an income level between the amount required for the student to qualify
21 for the federal free or reduced-price lunch program and not in excess of two
22 hundred percent (200%) of that amount.
23 (4) Eligible students qualifying for a scholarship grant in the amount provided
24 under subdivision (3) of subsection (b2) of this section.residing in households
25 with an income level of between two hundred percent (200%) of the amount
26 required for the student to qualify for the federal free or reduced-price lunch
27 program and not in excess of four hundred fifty percent (450%) of that
28 amount.
29 (5) All other students.
30 …
31 (b2) Scholarship grants shall be awarded to eligible students as follows:
32 (1) For students enrolled in private schools:
33 (1)a. For students residing in households with an income level not in excess
34 of the amount required for the student to qualify for the federal free or
35 reduced-price lunch program, per year per eligible student, an amount
36 of up to one hundred percent (100%) of the average State per pupil
37 allocation for average daily membership in the prior fiscal year.
38 (2)b. For students residing in households with an income level between the
39 amount required for the student to qualify for the federal free or
40 reduced-price lunch program and not in excess of two hundred percent
41 (200%) of that amount, per year per eligible student, an amount of up
42 to ninety percent (90%) of the average State per pupil allocation for
43 average daily membership in the prior fiscal year.
44 (3)c. For students residing in households with an income level of between
45 two hundred percent (200%) of the amount required for the student to
46 qualify for the federal free or reduced-price lunch program and not in
47 excess of four hundred fifty percent (450%) of that amount, per year
48 per eligible student, an amount of up to sixty percent (60%) of the
49 average State per pupil allocation for average daily membership in the
50 prior fiscal year.

47 c. Number of students in attendance at the school as of October 1.

49 not require any additional fees based on the status of the student as a scholarship grant recipient.
