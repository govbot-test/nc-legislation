Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Assaults on First Responders. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Pyrtle, Carson Smith, Greene, and Miller (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Judiciary 2, if favorable, Emergency Management and Disaster Recovery, if
favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
February 6, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO INCREASE THE PUNISHMENT FOR COMMITTING AN ASSAULT WITH A
3 FIREARM AGAINST CERTAIN EMERGENCY, MEDICAL, AND LAW
4 ENFORCEMENT PERSONNEL.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-34.5 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 14-34.5. Assault with a firearm on a law enforcement, probation, or parole officer, or
8 on a member of the North Carolina National Guard, or on a person employed at
9 a State or local detention facility.
10 (a) Any person who commits an assault with a firearm upon a law enforcement officer,
11 probation officer, or parole officer while the officer is in the performance of his or her duties is
12 guilty of a Class D felony.
13 (a1) Any person who commits an assault with a firearm upon a member of the North
14 Carolina National Guard while the member is in the performance of his or her duties is guilty of
15 a Class D felony.
16 (b) Anyone who commits an assault with a firearm upon a person who is employed at a
17 detention facility operated under the jurisdiction of the State or a local government while the
18 employee is in the performance of the employee's duties is guilty of a Class D felony.any of the
19 following persons who are in the performance of their duties is guilty of a Class B1 felony:
20 (1) A law enforcement officer.
21 (2) A probation officer.
22 (3) A parole officer.
23 (4) A member of the North Carolina National Guard.
24 (5) An employee of a detention facility operated under the jurisdiction of the State
25 or a local government.
26 (6) An emergency medical technician or other emergency health care provider.
27 (7) A medical responder.
28 (8) A firefighter.
29 (9) A telecommunicator employed by a law enforcement agency."
30 SECTION 2. G.S. 14-34.6(c) reads as rewritten:
31 "(c) Unless a person's conduct is covered under some other provision of law providing
32 greater punishment, a person is guilty of a Class D felony if the person violates subsection (a) of
33 this section against a person not otherwise covered under G.S. 14-34.5 and uses a firearm."
*H61-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to offenses
2 committed on or after that date.
