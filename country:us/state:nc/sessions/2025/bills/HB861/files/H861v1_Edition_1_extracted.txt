1 "§ 131E-269.5. Pregnancy centers; performance standards; recovery of State funds for
2 noncompliance.
3 (a) As used in this section, the term "pregnancy center" means any nonprofit organization
4 other than a hospital, ambulatory surgical facility, or clinic that performs abortions, that holds
5 itself out as a facility that provides care for pregnant women, including pregnancy counseling
6 services, pregnancy testing, and other nonmedical pregnancy services.
7 (b) The Department shall adopt rules establishing performance standards for pregnancy
8 centers operating in this State. The rules shall, at a minimum, establish a monitoring mechanism
9 and reporting requirements to facilitate the Department periodically assessing whether pregnancy
10 centers are adhering to the established performance standards. The reporting requirements shall,
11 at a minimum, require pregnancy centers to annually report to the Department at least all of the
12 following information for the preceding five-year period:
13 (1) All contracts and agreements entered into with the State of North Carolina or
14 Carolina Pregnancy Care Fellowship (CPCF), a nonprofit organization, during
15 the preceding five-year period.
16 (2) A list of all monetary transactions between the pregnancy center and State of
17 North Carolina or CPCF.
18 (3) Itemized annual expenditures for all of the following:
19 a. Individual employees, with a breakdown of the number of full-time
20 and part-time staff, the number of hours worked by each full-time and
21 part-time staff member, and the salaries and wages paid to each staff
22 member.
23 b. Administrative supplies and materials, with an explanation of the
24 purposes for these items.
25 c. All supplies and materials provided to individuals served by the
26 pregnancy center, including:
27 1. Pregnancy tests.
28 2. Diapers.
29 3. Medicines.
30 4. Baby food.
31 5. Baby formula.
32 d. Marketing and promotional materials, including all of the following:
33 1. Flyers.
34 2. Print advertisements.
35 3. Broadcast advertisements.
36 4. Digital and social media advertisements.
37 5. Promotions for fundraisers.
38 e. Educational materials for clients or patients, including pamphlets and
39 audiovisual seminars.
40 f. Travel expenses.
41 g. Medical equipment, including ultrasound machines.
42 h. Events sponsored by the pregnancy center.
43 i. Medical training and medical equipment training offered by the
44 pregnancy center.
45 j. Contracted services, including staffing services, event services,
46 information technology services, and cleaning services.
47 k. Rent or mortgage and utilities.
48 (4) Documentation of applications for employment and applications for
49 volunteers.
50 (5) Copies of Internal Revenue Service Form 990.
