7 PART I. TITLE AND SCOPE OF ACT
8 SECTION 1.1. Title. – This act shall be known as "The Disaster Recovery Act of
9 2025 – Part I."
10 SECTION 1.2. Maximum Amounts; Effectuate Savings. – The appropriations and
11 allocations made in this act are for maximum amounts necessary to implement this act. Savings
12 shall be effected where the total amounts appropriated or allocated are not required to implement
13 this act.
14 SECTION 1.3. Scope. – Unless otherwise provided, Part II of this act applies to the
15 North Carolina counties in the affected area, as defined in Section 1.4 of this act.
16 SECTION 1.4. Definitions. – Unless otherwise provided, the following definitions
17 apply in this act:
18 (1) Affected area. – The counties designated before, on, or after the effective date
19 of this act under a major disaster declaration by the President of the United
20 States under the Stafford Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Helene.
21 (2) CDBG-DR. – Community Development Block Grant – Disaster Recovery.
22 (3) FEMA. – The Federal Emergency Management Agency.
23 (4) Helene Fund. – The Hurricane Helene Disaster Recovery Fund established in
24 Section 4.1 of S.L. 2024-51.
25 (5) NCEM. – The Division of Emergency Management of the Department of
26 Public Safety.
27 (6) NCORR. – The Department of Public Safety, Office of Recovery and
28 Resiliency.
29 (7) OSBM. – The Office of State Budget and Management.
30 (8) SERDRF. – The State Emergency Response and Disaster Relief Fund
31 established in G.S. 166A-19.42.

27 SUBPART II-C. PRIVATE ROAD AND BRIDGE REPAIR AND REPLACEMENT
28 PROGRAM
29 SECTION 2C.1.(a) Private Road and Bridge Repair and Replacement Program. –
30 The Private Road and Bridge Repair and Replacement Program (Program) is established within
31 NCEM for the repair and replacement of private roads and bridges damaged or destroyed by
32 Hurricane Helene. NCEM shall consult with the Department of Transportation in administering
33 the Program and shall develop criteria and an application process to select private roads and
34 bridges eligible for repair or replacement consistent with this subsection. NCEM may use up to
35 five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) for administrative costs for the Program. NCEM shall
36 prioritize applications for the repair and replacement of private roads or bridges that provide the
37 sole option for ingress and egress for (i) emergency services to a residential property that is
38 occupied by the owner for more than six months of the calendar year, (ii) multiple residential
39 homes, or (iii) recreation or commercial facilities. These funds may be used for program costs
40 incurred for the engineering, design, and construction of private roads and bridges, funding to
41 nonprofit organizations supporting bridge repairs, and may also be used to provide technical
42 support and assistance for individuals and local governments to comply with no-rise certification
43 requirements required by FEMA under the National Flood Insurance Program.
44 SECTION 2C.1.(b) HOA Cost-Share. – If a qualifying private road or bridge is
45 owned by a homeowners association (HOA), then NCEM shall enter into a cost-share agreement
46 with the HOA for all project engineering and construction costs. NCEM's share of costs pursuant
47 to any agreement shall not exceed seventy-five percent (75%). Any funds that the HOA pays
48 toward these projects shall be non-State dollars.
49 SECTION 2C.1.(c) Ownership Restriction. – If a private road or bridge is repaired
50 or replaced through the Program, the ownership or responsibility for maintenance or safety of the

18 SUBPART II-D. AGRICULTURAL DISASTER CROP LOSS PROGRAM
19 SECTION 2D.1. Intent to Appropriate Future Funds. – It is the intent of the North
20 Carolina General Assembly to provide additional funding for the Program in a future enactment
21 of a general law.
22 SECTION 2D.2.(a) Agricultural Disaster Crop Loss Program. – The 2024
23 Agricultural Disaster Crop Loss Program is established within the Department of Agriculture and
24 Consumer Services. The Program shall be used to provide financial assistance to farmers with
25 verified losses from an agricultural disaster in this State in 2024. The Department may use up to
26 one percent (1%) of funds allocated for the Program for administrative purposes. To be eligible
27 for financial assistance for losses of agricultural or aquaculture commodities or farm
28 infrastructure, a person must satisfy all of the following criteria:
29 (1) The person experienced a verifiable loss of agricultural or aquaculture
30 commodities or farm infrastructure as a result of an agricultural disaster in
31 2024, and the person's farm is located in an affected area for the respective
32 agricultural disaster.
33 (2) The agricultural commodity was planted but not harvested on or before the
34 eligibility date; for aquaculture commodities, the commodities were being
35 raised on or before the eligibility date; or for farm infrastructure, the
36 infrastructure existed on or before the eligibility date.
37 SECTION 2D.2.(b) Verification of Loss. – A person seeking financial assistance for
38 losses of agricultural commodities under the Program shall submit to the Department a Form 578
39 on file with the USDA Farm Service Agency or a form provided by the Department for reporting
40 acreage or plantings of crops or reporting infrastructure damage or loss that is not typically
41 reported on Form 578, along with any other documentation deemed appropriate by the
42 Department, no later than 45 days after this section becomes effective. For nursery crops,
43 fruit-bearing trees and bushes, and specialty crops where the survival level is not immediately
44 known, the Department may extend this deadline by an additional 45 days, upon written request
45 by the person received no later than 45 days after this section becomes effective and upon
46 approval by the Department. A person receiving assistance under this Program must provide a
47 signed affidavit, under penalty of perjury, certifying that each fact of the loss presented by the
48 person is accurate.
49 SECTION 2D.2.(c) Criteria. – The Department shall administer the financial
50 assistance program authorized by this section in accordance with the following criteria:

47 of the Commissioner of Agriculture, shall institute the action in the proper court for the collection
48 of the award forfeited, including interest thereon.

45 SECTION 2E.1.(c) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
46 (1) Vegetative debris. – Vegetative matter resulting from landscaping,

18 SUBPART II-F. SMALL BUSINESS INFRASTRUCTURE GRANT PROGRAM
19 SECTION 2F.1.(a) Program; Purpose. – There is established the Small Business
20 Infrastructure Grant Program to be administered by the Department of Commerce. The purpose
21 of the program is to assist small businesses by providing grants to local governments to expedite
22 infrastructure repairs impacting the operation and patronage of small businesses in the affected
23 area.
24 SECTION 2F.1.(b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
25 (1) Department. – The Department of Commerce.
26 (2) Eligible local government. – A city or county, as those terms are defined in
27 G.S. 160A-1 and G.S. 153A-1, located in the affected area.
28 (3) Program. – The Small Business Infrastructure Grant Program.
29 (4) Qualifying infrastructure needs. – Water, sewer, gas, telecommunications,
30 high-speed broadband, electrical utility, and sidewalk and curb infrastructure
31 serving one or more small businesses located in an eligible local government
32 and damaged by Hurricane Helene that, until repaired, inhibits access to or
33 operations of one or more of those small businesses. The term does not include
34 infrastructure the small business owns or is responsible for maintaining.
35 (5) Small business. – A business with a physical presence in the affected area that
36 employs 150 or fewer employees.
37 SECTION 2F.1.(c) Applications; Verification. – An eligible local government with
38 qualifying infrastructure needs, or a small business located therein, may apply for a grant under
39 the program. An applicant must (i) identify with specificity the qualifying infrastructure needs,
40 (ii) provide a cost estimate to repair the needs, (iii) provide a short summary of the detrimental
41 impact on the named small businesses and how those businesses will benefit from the grant, (iv)
42 apply to the Department on a form prescribed by the Department, and (v) include any supporting
43 documentation required by the Department. The Department may accept applications until the
44 funds available under the program have been fully awarded. The Department shall consult with
45 applicants to substantiate applications prior to awarding grants under the program and prioritize
46 grants so as to maximize the beneficial and efficient use of program funding. Grants shall be
47 awarded to the eligible local government in which the qualifying infrastructure needs are located,
48 and the eligible local government shall be responsible for using the grant amount for repairing
49 the needs identified in the application.
50 SECTION 2F.1.(d) Grant Amount. – The total grant amount per county in the
51 affected area shall not exceed ten percent (10%) of the total funds appropriated for the program.
