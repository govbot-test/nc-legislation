7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1. Article 2 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
9 a new section to read:
10 "§ 161-32. Identity verification.
11 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
12 (1) Instrument. – Any document that purports to convey title to, or an interest in,
13 real property, such as a deed, deed of trust, or other similar document.
14 (2) Suspicious instrument. – An instrument submitted for registration for which
15 the register of deeds has found any of the following:
16 a. The name or information of an acknowledging officer who has
17 purported to acknowledge the instrument does not match the official
18 records for that acknowledging officer.
19 b. The instrument purports not to be subject to the laws of the United
20 States or the laws of this State.
21 c. The document does not conform to registration requirements
22 established by the laws of this State.
23 d. The instrument is materially false, fictitious, or fraudulent.
24 (3) Trusted filer. – A person listed in the trusted filer database maintained by the
25 Administrative Office of the Courts pursuant to subsection (g) of this section.
26 (4) Trusted submitter. – Includes all of the following:
27 a. A title insurance company as described in Article 26 of Chapter 28 of
28 the General Statutes.
29 b. An attorney licensed to practice in this State.
30 c. A financial institution as defined in G.S. 53B-2.
31 (b) Identity Verification. – Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary in this
32 Chapter, when an instrument is presented to the register of deeds for registration by an individual
33 who is not a trusted filer, a trusted submitter, or the authorized agent of a trusted filer or trusted
34 submitter, the register of deeds shall require the person presenting the instrument to produce a
35 government-issued photographic identification card as follows:

3 in person, the register of deeds shall require the individual to produce a

13 document, as defined in G.S. 47-16.2(3), to the register of deeds, as provided
14 by Article 1A of Chapter 47 of the General Statutes, the register of deeds shall
15 require the individual to submit a photocopy of a government-issued
16 photographic identification card before registering the instrument. The
17 register of deeds must note on the photocopy of the identification card the
18 official records book and page number or instrument number assigned to the
19 instrument registered in connection to the submission of the photocopy of the
20 identification card and retain the photocopy of such identification card in a
21 record to be kept by the register of deeds.
22 (3) If the instrument is presented by an entity that is not a trusted filer or a trusted
23 submitter, or the authorized agent of a trusted filer or a trusted submitter, the
24 register of deeds shall require production of the following, as applicable:
25 a. A corporation must produce a certificate of good standing and a
26 secretary's certificate signed by an authorized officer of the
27 corporation.
28 b. A trust must produce a certification of trust.
29 c. An estate must produce letters testamentary or letters of
30 administration.
31 d. A limited liability company must produce a resolution signed by its
32 members.
33 e. An unincorporated nonprofit association must present a sworn
34 statement attesting to the authority of the person presenting the
35 instrument to act on behalf of the association.
36 (4) Records required to be kept by the register of deeds pursuant to this subsection
37 are confidential and not public records pursuant to G.S. 132-1 or any other
38 applicable statute and shall not be disclosed other than pursuant to a court
39 order in a superior court civil action or in response to a written request from a
40 State or federal law enforcement agency for investigative or evidentiary
41 purposes. Such written request may be in electronic format.
42 (c) Refusing to Register. – Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary in this
43 Chapter, the register of deeds shall refuse to register an instrument presented by an individual
44 who is not a trusted filer, a trusted submitter, or an authorized representative of a trusted filer or
45 trusted submitter, if one of the following occurs:
46 (1) The individual presenting the instrument for registration fails to provide the
47 register of deeds with a government-issued photographic identification card
48 as required by subsection (b) of this section.
49 (2) The name on the government-issued photographic identification card
50 presented by the individual pursuant to subsection (b) of this section does not

4 (d) Notice of Requirements. – The register of deeds shall publish on the register of deeds'

19 SECTION 2. Article 1 of Chapter 41 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
20 a new section to read:
21 "§ 41-10.2. Titles quieted; fraudulent instruments.
22 (a) As used in this section, the term "instrument" means any of the following:
23 (1) A deed or other instrument transferring title to real property.
24 (2) A deed of trust, mortgage, judgment, lien, encumbrance, financing statement,
25 affidavit, notice, memorandum, or any other instrument that establishes a
26 security interest in real property.
27 (3) A contract to purchase, option to purchase, right of refusal, or any other
28 instrument that purports to establish an interest, encumbrance, claim, or right
29 relating to real property.
30 However, for purposes of this section, the term "instrument" does not include a will or any other
31 writing under the terms of which a person's interest in real property is transferred upon the death
32 of that person.
33 (b) The owner of an interest in real property may seek expedited relief under this section
34 by filing a petition in district court alleging the filing or recording of a false, fictitious, or
35 fraudulent instrument. The petition shall be served on any person purportedly having conveyed
36 or having been conveyed an interest in real property by the recorded instrument alleged to be
37 false, fictitious, or fraudulent, and also upon any other persons claiming an ownership interest in
38 the property, including lienholders of record, any person to whom a security instrument directs
39 notice to be sent, and any person obligated to repay the indebtedness secured by the instrument.
40 In an action brought pursuant to this subsection, the following procedures apply:
41 (1) Expedited hearing. – When a petition is filed initiating an action pursuant to
42 this subsection, the court shall set the matter for a hearing which shall be held
43 on an expedited basis and within the first term of court falling after 10 days
44 from the service of the petition on all parties required to be served, unless a
45 party obtains a continuance.
46 (2) Standards for continuances. – The court shall not grant a continuance, nor
47 shall it stay the civil proceedings pending the disposition of any related
48 criminal proceedings, except for compelling and extraordinary reasons or on
49 application of the district attorney for good cause shown.
50 (3) Responsive pleadings. – Any party served with the petition may file and serve
51 a responsive pleading within 10 days after service of the complaint.

1 (4) Extensions of time for filing. – The parties served with a copy of the petition
2 shall not be entitled to an extension of time for filing and serving a responsive
3 pleading, except for compelling and extraordinary reasons.
4 (5) Rules of Civil Procedure. – Unless otherwise provided for in this subsection,
5 G.S. 1A-1, the Rules of Civil Procedure, shall apply in the district court to all
6 actions brought pursuant to this subsection.
7 (c) If the court finds that there is no statutory, contractual, or other legal justification for
8 the instrument, the court shall find that the recorded instrument is false and is void as a matter of
9 law and shall enter an order to remove the cloud from the title to the real property. The submission
10 of an original instrument demonstrating source of title, affidavit, or a lis pendens, notice of
11 previous fraud, or other similar instrument recorded by the actual owner of an interest in the real
12 property shall be considered evidence of fraudulent activity. The court also may, in its order, do
13 any of the following:
14 (1) Grant to a prevailing party possession of the real property and order the
15 ejectment of any person on the premises.
16 (2) Award attorneys' fees and other costs for the action to the prevailing party.
17 (3) Stay any proceedings involving the subject real property to which the property
18 owner is not a party.
19 (4) Upon a finding by the court that an action filed under this section is frivolous
20 or malicious, assess a civil penalty of not more than ten thousand dollars
21 ($10,000), of which the clear proceeds assessed under this subdivision shall
22 be remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with
23 G.S. 115C-457.2.
24 (5) Upon a finding by the court that a party has presented for filing or recording
25 a false, fictitious, or fraudulent instrument as described in this section more
26 than once, enter a pre-filing injunction against the party.
27 (6) Enjoin a party from listing the real property for sale, lease, or any other similar
28 advertisement.
29 (d) Upon being presented with an order duly issued by a court of competent jurisdiction
30 of this State declaring that an instrument already recorded is false and void as a matter of law,
31 the register of deeds or clerk of superior court that received the recording shall record and
32 cross-index the court's order finding the instrument to be false. The register of deeds or clerk of
33 superior court may also conspicuously mark on the first page of the original record previously
34 recorded the following statement: "THE CLAIM ASSERTED IN THIS DOCUMENT IS FALSE
35 AND IS NOT PROVIDED FOR BY THE GENERAL LAWS OF THIS STATE."
36 (e) In addition to any civil penalties provided for in this section, the presentation of an
37 instrument for recording with a register of deeds or a clerk of superior court that is determined to
38 be materially false, fictitious, or fraudulent shall constitute a violation of G.S. 75-1.1."
39 SECTION 3. G.S. 14-209 reads as rewritten:
40 "§ 14-209. Punishment for perjury.
41 If any person knowingly and intentionally makes a false statement under oath or affirmation
42 in any suit, controversy, matter or cause, or in any unsworn declaration deemed sufficient
43 pursuant to G.S. 7A-98 depending in any of the courts of the State; in any deposition or affidavit
44 taken pursuant to law; in any oath or affirmation duly administered of or concerning any matter
45 or thing where such person is lawfully required to be sworn or affirmed, of the following, that
46 person is guilty of perjury, and punished as a Class F felon.felon:
47 (1) A suit, controversy, matter or cause, or unsworn declaration deemed sufficient
48 pursuant to G.S. 7A-98 depending in any of the courts of the State.
49 (2) A deposition or affidavit taken pursuant to law.
50 (3) An oath or affirmation duly administered of or concerning any matter or thing
51 where such person is lawfully required to be sworn or affirmed.
