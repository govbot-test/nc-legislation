13 SECTION 3.(a) The Legislative Research Commission (LRC) shall study the
14 feasibility of creating a fraud detection alert system where everyone who submits an instrument
15 as defined in G.S. 161-32, as enacted by this act, for registration is automatically enrolled in the
16 fraud detection alert system without paying a fee. Before enrollment, the register of deeds would
17 provide each individual with a notice explaining the system, including its purpose, how it works,
18 and how to opt out of enrollment.
19 As part of its study, the LRC shall do all of the following:
20 (1) Evaluate the practicality of implementing a fraud detection alert system with
21 automatic enrollment and an opt-out option, including technological and
22 staffing requirements for registers of deeds across all counties.
23 (2) Examine the fiscal impact on the State, counties, and/or registers of deeds,
24 including costs associated with developing, maintaining, and operating a fraud
25 detection alert system without charging a fee to the participant.
26 (3) Evaluate how well the fraud detection alert system described in this section
27 would prevent fraud as compared to other potential systems or measures.
28 SECTION 3.(b) The LRC shall report its findings, together with any proposed
29 legislation, to the 2026 Regular Session of the 2025 General Assembly upon its convening.
