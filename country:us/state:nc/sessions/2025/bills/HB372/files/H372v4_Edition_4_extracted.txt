15 "§ 160A-205.8. No-impact home-based businesses.
16 (a) A city shall not adopt an ordinance or other regulation prohibiting no-impact
17 home-based businesses within its jurisdictional limits or require a person to apply, register, or
18 obtain any permit, license, variance, or other type of approval to operate a no-impact home-based
19 business within its jurisdictional limit. A city may impose reasonable regulations on no-impact
20 home-based businesses if the regulations are narrowly tailored for any of the following purposes:
21 (1) Ensuring the protection of public health and safety, as allowed by law.
22 (2) Ensuring that the business activity meets all of the following:
23 a. Is compatible with the residential use of the property and surrounding
24 residential use.
25 b. Is secondary to the use of the property as a residential dwelling.
26 c. Complies with State and federal law, including the payment of
27 applicable taxes.

29 purpose of selling illegal drugs, liquor, operating or maintaining a structured
30 sober living home, pornography, obscenity, or nude or topless dancing or
31 other adult-oriented businesses.
32 (b) A city shall not require as a condition of operating a no-impact home-based business
33 that the owner or occupant of the property do the following:
34 (1) Submit a petition for rezoning the property for commercial use.
