5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. Chapter 58 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
7 to read:
8 "Article 82C.
9 "Licensing and Permitting for the Installation and Servicing of Portable Fire Extinguishers and
10 Fire Suppression Systems.
11 "§ 58-82C-1. Definitions.
12 The following definitions apply in this Article:
13 (1) Engineered special hazard fire suppression system. – A fire suppression
14 system having pipe lengths, number of fittings, number and types of nozzles,
15 suppression agent flow rates, and nozzle pressures calculated based on
16 appropriate standards of the National Fire Protection Association. An
17 engineered fire suppression system may include other components, including,
18 but not limited to, detection devices, alarm devices, and control devices as
19 tested and approved by a nationally recognized testing laboratory, where the
20 component's manufacturer determines the component is compatible with the
21 fire suppression system.
22 (2) Fire suppression system. – Any firefighting system employing a suppression
23 agent with the purpose of controlling, suppressing, or extinguishing a fire in a
24 specific hazard, including pre-engineered kitchen fire suppression systems,
25 pre-engineered industrial fire suppression systems, and engineered special
26 hazard fire suppression systems.
27 (3) Firm. – Any business, person, partnership, organization, association,
28 corporation, contractor, subcontractor, or individual.
29 (4) Industrial fire suppression system. – A pre-engineered automatic fire
30 extinguishing system provided for the protection of property or equipment as
31 described by the manufacturer other than those systems covered under the
32 definition of kitchen fire suppression system.
33 (5) Kitchen fire suppression system. – An automatic fire extinguishing system
34 provided for the protection of grease removal devices, hoods, duct systems,
35 and cooking equipment. This term also includes "restaurant fire suppression
36 system."

1 (6) License. – The document issued by the State Fire Marshal which authorizes a

16 "§ 58-82C-2. State Fire Marshal to administer Article; rules and regulations.
17 (a) The State Fire Marshal, through the Office of the State Fire Marshal, shall have full
18 power and authority to administer the provisions of this Article, including adopting rules to
19 enforce the purposes and provisions of this Article.
20 (b) The State Fire Marshal shall establish rules for the following:
21 (1) Proper installation, inspection, recharging, repairing, servicing, or testing of
22 fire suppression systems or portable fire extinguishers.
23 (2) Required specifications as to the number, type, size, shape, color, and
24 information and data contained thereon of service tags to be attached to all
25 portable fire extinguishers and fire suppression systems covered by this
26 Article when they are installed, inspected, recharged, repaired, serviced, or
27 tested.
28 (c) The State Fire Marshal may adopt by rule the applicable standards of the National
29 Fire Protection Association or another nationally recognized organization if the standards are
30 judged by the State Fire Marshal to be suitable for the enforcement of this Article. All fire
31 suppression systems and portable fire extinguishers covered by G.S. 58-82C-3 shall be installed,
32 inspected, recharged, repaired, serviced, or tested in compliance with this Article and with the
33 State Fire Marshal's rules.
34 (d) The State Fire Marshal may adopt rules to require an applicant for a license or permit
35 to take an examination as to the applicant's competence to be licensed or permitted under this
36 Article. If required, the applicant must take and pass the examination according to requirements
37 prescribed by the State Fire Marshal.
38 (e) The State Fire Marshal is authorized to apply for and receive grants for the
39 administration of this Article from parties interested in upgrading and improving the quality of
40 fire protection provided by portable fire extinguishers or fire suppression systems.
41 "§ 58-82C-3. Compliance with this Article; license and permit requirement.
42 (a) Except as otherwise provided by this Article, no firm may install, inspect, repair,
43 service, or test any of the following fire suppression systems or portable fire extinguishers that
44 are required by the State Fire Marshal's rules or by other State or local fire safety rules, unless
45 the firm is licensed under the provisions of this Article:
46 (1) Portable fire extinguishers.
47 (2) Pre-engineered kitchen fire suppression systems.
48 (3) Pre-engineered industrial fire suppression systems.
49 (4) Engineered special hazard fire suppression systems.
50 (b) Except as otherwise provided by this Article, no individual may install, inspect,
51 repair, recharge, service, or test any of the following fire suppression systems or portable fire

28 Fire Marshal with each application for the issuance of a permit under this Article.
29 (c) The fees required by this Article shall not apply to employees of federal, State, or
30 local governments or to a member of a legally organized fire department while acting in the
31 member's official capacity.

48 Carolina. The coverage must include bodily injury and property damage,
49 products liability, completed operations, and contractual liability. The proof
50 of insurance must also be provided before any license can be renewed. The

22 "§ 58-82C-6. Production of license or permit on demand.
23 (a) Every permittee must have a valid permit upon his or her person at all times while
24 engaging in the installing, inspection, recharging, repairing, servicing, or testing of fire
25 suppression systems or portable fire extinguishers.
26 (b) Every licensee or permittee must be able to produce a valid license or valid permit, as
27 appropriate, upon demand by the State Fire Marshal or his or her representatives or by any local
28 authority having jurisdiction for fire protection or prevention or by any individual for whom the
29 licensee or permittee solicits to perform any of the activities covered by this Article.
30 "§ 58-82C-7. Reciprocity.
31 The State Fire Marshal may issue a license or permit under this Article to an individual who
32 holds a comparable valid permit, license, or certification issued by another state, provided the
33 minimum requirements of that state are at least equal to the minimum requirements under this
34 Article for the specific license or permit issued and the individual or firm pays the application
35 and filing fees required under this Article.
36 "§ 58-82C-8. Forms of licenses, permits, and applications; information required.
37 (a) An individual applying for a license or permit under this Article shall apply on a form
38 prescribed by the State Fire Marshal and declare under penalty of denial, suspension, or
39 revocation of the license that the statements made in the application are true, correct, and
40 complete to the best of the individual's knowledge and belief.
41 (b) Permit applications shall include the name of the licensee employing the applicant or
42 the name of the firm or governmental entity pursuant to the exemptions found in G.S. 58-82C-3.
43 (c) Each license or permit issued by the State Fire Marshal under this Article shall clearly
44 state the activity or activities for which the licensee or permittee has been issued the license or
45 permit to perform. The licensee or permittee shall not perform any activity not noted on a license
46 or permit issued by the State Fire Marshal.
47 (d) A licensee or permittee shall notify the State Fire Marshal within 30 days of any
48 change to the information provided to the State Fire Marshal on the license or permit application.
49 (e) The State Fire Marshal shall give an applicant 60 days to correct any deficiencies
50 discovered by inspection.

27 (e) A licensee or permittee whose license or permit is suspended, revoked, or not renewed

35 (g) If, during the period between the beginning of proceedings and the entry of an order
36 of suspension or revocation by the State Fire Marshal, a new license or permit has been issued to
37 the individual or firm charged, the order of suspension or revocation shall operate to suspend or
38 revoke, as the case may be, the new license or permit held by the individual or firm.
39 (h) The State Fire Marshal shall not, so long as the revocation or suspension remains in
40 effect, issue any new license or permit for the establishment of any new firm, business, or
41 corporation of any individual or applicant that has or will have the same or similar management,
42 ownership, control, employees, permittees, or licensees or will use the same or a similar name as
43 the revoked or suspended firm, business, corporation, individual, or applicant.

1 "§ 58-82C-12. Cease and desist orders; period of revocation.
2 (a) Whenever the State Fire Marshal shall have reason to believe that any individual or
3 firm is or has been violating any provisions of this Article, the State Fire Marshal may issue and
4 deliver to the individual or firm an order to cease and desist the violation.
5 (b) Any order issued by the State Fire Marshal under this section shall contain or be
6 accompanied by a notice of opportunity for hearing which may provide that a hearing will be
7 held if and only if an individual subject to the order requests a hearing within 30 days of receipt
8 of the order and notice. The order and notice shall be served by hand delivery by the State Fire
9 Marshal or by registered or certified mail.
10 (c) Failure to comply with a cease and desist order is cause for revocation of any or all
11 permits and licenses issued by the State Fire Marshal for a period of not less than six months and
12 not to exceed five years. If a new permit or license has been issued to the individual or firm, the
13 order of revocation shall operate effectively with respect to the new permits and licenses held by
14 the individual or firm.
15 (d) In the case of an applicant for a license or permit, violation of any provision of this
16 title may constitute grounds for refusal of the application.

19 issued by the State Fire Marshal under this Article shall be subject to a civil penalty imposed by
20 the State Fire Marshal of not more than one thousand dollars ($1,000) for a first offense, not less
21 than one thousand dollars ($1,000) and not more than two thousand dollars ($2,000) for a second

28 with the provisions of G.S. 58-82C-12(b) and must notify the individual or firm of the right to a

30 (c) In addition to other powers granted to the State Fire Marshal under this Article, the
31 State Fire Marshal may bring a civil action to enjoin a violation of any provision of this Article

11 "§ 58-82C-1. Definitions.
12 The following definitions apply in this Article:
13 (1) Engineered special hazard fire suppression system. – A fire suppression
14 system having pipe lengths, number of fittings, number and types of nozzles,
15 suppression agent flow rates, and nozzle pressures calculated based on
16 appropriate standards of the National Fire Protection Association. An
17 engineered fire suppression system may include other components, including,
18 but not limited to, detection devices, alarm devices, and control devices as
19 tested and approved by a nationally recognized testing laboratory, where the
20 component's manufacturer determines the component is compatible with the
21 fire suppression system.
22 (2) Fire suppression system. – Any firefighting system employing a suppression
