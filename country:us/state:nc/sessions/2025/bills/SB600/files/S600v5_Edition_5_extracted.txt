4 PART III. PROHIBIT THE MANUFACTURING, SELLING, AND DISTRIBUTING OF
5 INTRAVENOUS SOLUTION CONTAINERS AND INTRAVENOUS TUBING
6 INTENTIONALLY MADE WITH DEHP
7 SECTION 3.(a) Chapter 130A of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
8 Article to read as follows:
9 "Article 19C.
10 "DEHP Hazard Management.
11 "§ 130A-453.33. Legislative finding.
12 The General Assembly finds all of the following:
13 (1) DEHP and other ortho-phthalates are toxic chemicals used primarily to
14 produce flexibility in plastics, mainly polyvinyl chloride (PVC).
15 (2) DEHP is the most common plasticizer used in medical devices, including
16 intravenous solution containers, which are also known as IV bags, and
17 intravenous tubing.
18 (3) Over the course of its shelf life, DEHP leaches from IV bags and tubing made
19 from DEHP into the solutions being held in the medical devices.
20 (4) DEHP is classified by the United States Environmental Protection Agency as
21 an endocrine-disrupting compound since it can:
22 a. Interfere with the hormonal system in humans and animals.
23 b. Mimic or block the actions of hormones, leading to adverse effects on
24 reproductive health, development, and metabolism.
25 (5) DEHP exposure has been associated with adverse effects on reproductive
26 organs and fertility. DEHP can also disrupt normal reproductive development,
27 reduce sperm quality, and affect hormone levels in both males and females.
28 (6) DEHP is metabolized in the liver and can accumulate in the body over time.
29 Prolonged exposure to high levels of DEHP has been shown to cause liver and
30 kidney damage in animal studies.
31 (7) Inhalation or ingestion of DEHP can cause respiratory irritation and allergic
32 reactions in some individuals, particularly those with preexisting respiratory
33 conditions or sensitivities.
34 (8) Studies have suggested a potential link between DEHP exposure and certain
35 types of cancer, including breast, liver, lung, and testicular cancer.
36 (9) The United States Environmental Protection Agency has determined that
37 DEHP is a probable human carcinogen.
38 (10) The leaching of DEHP from medical devices at varying concentrations has
39 been linked to multidrug resistance in breast cancer cells, inhibiting the
40 effectiveness of breast cancer drugs. This phenomenon has been observed at
41 both high and low concentrations of DEHP, highlighting the potential impact
42 of DEHP leaching on cancer treatment outcomes.
43 (11) Exposure to DEHP has been linked to multidrug resistance in triple-negative
44 breast cancer cells, inhibiting the apoptosis mechanism induced by breast
45 cancer drugs, such as tamoxifen, and increasing cell proliferation.
46 (12) DEHP has been suggested to serve as a mitogenic factor for estrogen
47 receptor-positive breast cancer cells, potentially making them multidrug
48 resistant.

9 "Article 19C.
10 "DEHP Hazard Management.
11 "§ 130A-453.33. Legislative finding.
12 The General Assembly finds all of the following:
13 (1) DEHP and other ortho-phthalates are toxic chemicals used primarily to
14 produce flexibility in plastics, mainly polyvinyl chloride (PVC).
15 (2) DEHP is the most common plasticizer used in medical devices, including
16 intravenous solution containers, which are also known as IV bags, and
17 intravenous tubing.
18 (3) Over the course of its shelf life, DEHP leaches from IV bags and tubing made
19 from DEHP into the solutions being held in the medical devices.
20 (4) DEHP is classified by the United States Environmental Protection Agency as
21 an endocrine-disrupting compound since it can:
22 a. Interfere with the hormonal system in humans and animals.
23 b. Mimic or block the actions of hormones, leading to adverse effects on
24 reproductive health, development, and metabolism.
25 (5) DEHP exposure has been associated with adverse effects on reproductive
26 organs and fertility. DEHP can also disrupt normal reproductive development,
27 reduce sperm quality, and affect hormone levels in both males and females.
28 (6) DEHP is metabolized in the liver and can accumulate in the body over time.
29 Prolonged exposure to high levels of DEHP has been shown to cause liver and
30 kidney damage in animal studies.
31 (7) Inhalation or ingestion of DEHP can cause respiratory irritation and allergic
32 reactions in some individuals, particularly those with preexisting respiratory
33 conditions or sensitivities.
34 (8) Studies have suggested a potential link between DEHP exposure and certain
35 types of cancer, including breast, liver, lung, and testicular cancer.
36 (9) The United States Environmental Protection Agency has determined that
37 DEHP is a probable human carcinogen.
38 (10) The leaching of DEHP from medical devices at varying concentrations has
39 been linked to multidrug resistance in breast cancer cells, inhibiting the
40 effectiveness of breast cancer drugs. This phenomenon has been observed at
41 both high and low concentrations of DEHP, highlighting the potential impact
42 of DEHP leaching on cancer treatment outcomes.
43 (11) Exposure to DEHP has been linked to multidrug resistance in triple-negative
44 breast cancer cells, inhibiting the apoptosis mechanism induced by breast
45 cancer drugs, such as tamoxifen, and increasing cell proliferation.
46 (12) DEHP has been suggested to serve as a mitogenic factor for estrogen
47 receptor-positive breast cancer cells, potentially making them multidrug
48 resistant.
