9 "§ 90-750. Definitions.
10 The following definitions apply in this Article:
11 (1) ABGC. – The American Board of Genetic Counseling, its successor or
12 equivalent.
13 (2) ACGC. – Accreditation Council for Genetic Counseling.
14 (3) ACS. – Active Candidate Status conferred by the American Board of Genetic
15 Counseling.
16 (4) Active Candidate Status. – A person who has met the requirements established
17 by the ABGC to take the ABGC certification examinations in general genetics
18 and genetic counseling and has been granted this designation by ABGC.
19 (5) Board. – The Genetic Counselors Licensing Board.
20 (6) CEU. – The Continuing Education Unit as defined by the NSGC.
21 (7) Department. – The Department of Health and Human Services.
22 (8) Examination. – The ABGC certification exam.
23 (9) Genetic counseling. – The provision of services by an individual who qualifies
24 for a license under this Article and provides services to individuals located in
25 the State.
26 (10) Genetic counseling intern. – A student enrolled in a genetic counseling
27 program accredited by ACGC.
28 (11) Genetic counselor. – An individual who is licensed under this Article to
29 engage in the competent practice of genetic counseling.
30 (12) Genetic testing. – An analysis of human DNA, RNA, chromosomes, proteins,
31 or metabolites, if the analysis detects genotypes, mutations, or chromosomal
32 changes, so long as the selection of tests and laboratories by the genetic
33 counselor conforms to the acceptable and prevailing standards of care and
34 ethics of the practice of genetic counseling as determined by the Board.
35 (13) NSGC. – The National Society of Genetic Counselors, its successor or
36 equivalent.

21 "§ 90-751. Genetic Counselors Licensure Board.
22 (a) Establishment. – The Genetic Counselors Licensure Board is established. The Board
23 shall consist of five members who shall serve staggered terms. The initial Board shall be selected
24 as follows:
25 (1) One member, who is a licensed genetic counselor, shall be appointed by the
26 General Assembly, upon the recommendation of the Speaker of the House of
27 Representatives, to serve a three-year term.
28 (2) One member, who is a licensed genetic counselor, shall be appointed by the
29 General Assembly, upon the recommendation of the President Pro Tempore
30 of the Senate, to serve a three-year term.
31 (3) The Governor shall appoint the following three members:
32 a. One licensed genetic counselor to serve a three-year term.
33 b. One licensed physician to serve a three-year term.
34 c. One public member to serve a three-year term.
35 (b) Appointment. – Upon the expiration of the terms of the initial Board members, each
36 member shall be appointed by the appointing authorities designated in subdivisions (a)(1)
37 through (3) of this section for a three-year term and shall serve until a successor is appointed and
38 qualified. All members appointed to the Board, except for the public member appointed by the
39 Governor under subdivision (a)(3) of this section, shall be required to be licensed under this
40 Article and shall seek licensure in this State as soon as the first application period begins. No
41 member may serve more than two consecutive terms. Each member shall be a resident of this
42 State. A former member shall be eligible for appointment after a period of one year of not being
43 a member of the Board.
44 (c) Vacancies; Qualifications. – If a member of the Board cannot complete a term of
45 office, the vacancy shall be filled in the same manner as the original appointment for the
46 remainder of the unexpired term. No Board member shall participate in any matter before the
47 Board in which the member has a pecuniary interest or similar conflict of interest.
48 (d) Removal. – A member may be removed by the Governor for any of the following:
49 (1) Ceases to meet the qualifications specified in this section.
50 (2) Fails to attend three successive Board meetings without just cause as
51 determined by the remainder of the Board.

4 "§ 90-755. Scope of practice.
5 (a) The practice of genetic counseling shall constitute all of the following:
6 (1) Obtain and evaluate individual, family, and medical histories to determine
7 genetic risk for genetic or medical conditions and diseases in a patient, the
8 patient's children, and other family members.
9 (2) Discuss the features, natural history, means of diagnosis, genetic and
10 environmental factors, and management of risk for genetic or medical
11 conditions.
12 (3) Identify, order, and coordinate genetic laboratory tests as appropriate for the
13 genetic assessment.
14 (4) Integrate genetic laboratory test results with personal and family medical
15 history to assess and communicate risk factors for genetic or medical
16 conditions and diseases.
17 (5) Explain the clinical implications of genetic laboratory tests and their results.
18 (6) Evaluate the client's or family's responses to the condition or risk of recurrence
19 and provide client-centered counseling and anticipatory guidance.
20 (7) Identify and utilize community resources that provide medical, educational,
21 financial, and psychosocial support and advocacy.
22 (8) Provide written documentation of medical, genetic, and counseling
23 information for families and health care professionals. This may include
24 genetic testing results and findings along with published guidelines that comply
25 with the acceptable and prevailing standards of practice and ethics of both
26 genetic counseling and medicine.
27 (b) Except as otherwise provided in this section, the practice of genetic counseling shall
28 not include diagnosis, treatment, or recommendations for treatment. All genetic counselors shall
29 consult with the referring provider or refer all clients back to the referring health care provider
30 for diagnosis, treatment, recommendations for treatment, final interpretations, and any necessary
31 follow up. Genetic counselors shall provide all records, including genetic laboratory test results,
32 to the referring health care provider.

25 "§ 90-758. Restrictions.
26 (a) No individual shall hold themselves out as a genetic counselor unless the individual
27 is licensed in accordance with this Article. No person who is not so licensed may use in
28 connection with the person's name or place of business the title "genetic counselor," "licensed
29 genetic counselor," "gene counselor," "genetic consultant," "genetic associate," or any words,
30 letters, abbreviations, or insignia indicating or implying a person holds a genetic counseling
31 license.
32 (b) All genetic counselors are subject to the identification requirements imposed by
33 Article 37 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes.
34 (c) Any person so practicing without being duly licensed and registered in the State shall
35 be guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor. Any person so practicing without being duly licensed and
36 registered in the State and who is falsely representing himself or herself in a manner as being
37 licensed or registered under this Article of this Chapter shall be guilty of a Class I felony. Any
38 person so practicing without being duly licensed and registered in the State, and who is an
39 out-of-state practitioner, shall be guilty of a Class I felony.

36 SECTION 4. Part 2 of Article 13A of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes is
37 amended by adding a new section to read:
38 "§ 143B-1209.23A. Criminal record check for the Genetic Counselors Licensing Board.
39 (a) The State Bureau of Investigation may provide to the Genetic Counselors Licensing
40 Board a criminal history from the State and National Repositories of Criminal Histories for any
41 applicant seeking a license. The Genetic Counselors Licensing Board shall provide to the State
42 Bureau of Investigation, along with the request, the fingerprints of the applicant, a form signed
43 by the applicant consenting to the criminal record check and use of fingerprints and other
44 identifying information required by the State and National Repositories, and any additional
45 information required by the State Bureau of Investigation. The State Bureau of Investigation shall
46 forward a set of fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation for a national criminal history
47 record check.
48 (b) The criminal history report shall be provided to the Genetic Counselors Licensing
49 Board who shall keep all information obtained pursuant to this section confidential to the Genetic
50 Counselors Licensing Board. A criminal history report obtained as provided in this section is not
51 a public record under Chapter 132 of the General Statutes."
