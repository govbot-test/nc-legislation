Short Title: SFRF/NCPRO Revisions. (Public)

Referred to:

1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT DIRECTING THE OFFICE OF STATE BUDGET AND MANAGEMENT TO
3 RECLASSIFY UNEXPENDED STATE FISCAL RECOVERY FUND BALANCES AND
4 OTHER FUNDING, AUTHORIZING THE NORTH CAROLINA PANDEMIC
5 RECOVERY OFFICE TO REALLOCATE STATE FISCAL RECOVERY FUNDS,
6 DIRECTING THE OFFICE OF STATE BUDGET AND MANAGEMENT TO USE
7 INTEREST EARNED ON VARIOUS FUNDS FOR OPERATIONS PERTAINING TO
8 PANDEMIC RELIEF, AND EXTENDING THE DATE ON WHICH THE NORTH
9 CAROLINA PANDEMIC RECOVERY OFFICE WILL CEASE TO OPERATE.
10 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
11
12 STATE FISCAL RECOVERY FUND AND OTHER ADJUSTMENTS
13 SECTION 1.(a) Section 6.9 of S.L. 2025-97 reads as rewritten:
14 "…
15 "SECTION 6.9.(b) The Office of State Budget and Management (OSBM), in consultation
16 with NCPRO, shall identify all State Fiscal Recovery Fund (SFRF)-funded projects with
17 remaining unexpended balances that were categorized as revenue loss and used for government
18 services. The Director of the Budget may reclassify such unexpended SFRF balances to support
19 other eligible government services (i) only to the extent permitted under federal law and federal
20 guidance; (ii) only from funds that were properly obligated on or before December 31, 2024; and
21 (iii) with expenditures made consistent with 31 C.F.R. Part 35 and related United States
22 Department of the Treasury guidance. A reclassification under this subsection may occur during
23 the 2025-2026 fiscal year and, to the extent necessary to comply with federal requirements,
24 within the applicable federal period of performance, if all of the following conditions are
25 satisfied:
26 (1) The governmental service to receive SFRF support was previously supported
27 by SFRF appropriations.
28 (2) The governmental service receives a net General Fund appropriation in the
29 2025-2026 fiscal year.
30 (3) The governmental service is an eligible use of SFRF under 31 C.F.R. Part 35
31 and related United States Department of the Treasury guidance during the
32 period of performance.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 "SECTION 6.9.(c) To implement subsection (b) of this section, the Director of the Budget
2 shall make nonrecurring, budget-neutral adjustments as follows:
3 (1) Reduce, on a nonrecurring basis, the 2025-2026 or 2026-2027 fiscal year net
4 General Fund appropriation for each agency receiving SFRF pursuant to this
5 section by an amount equal to the SFRF reclassified to that agency.
6 (2) Increase, on a nonrecurring basis, the net General Fund appropriation for each
7 agency from which SFRF were reclassified under this section by an amount
8 equal to the SFRF removed from that agency.
9 (3) Notwithstanding G.S. 143C-1-2(b), the net General Fund appropriations made
10 under subdivision (2) of this subsection shall be used only for the projects for
11 which SFRF were reduced under this section and shall not revert but shall
12 remain available until the earlier of project completion or June 30, 2027.
13 (4) In no event shall a project from which SFRF are reclassified receive net
14 General Fund appropriations in excess of the project's unexpended SFRF
15 balance as of the date of reclassification.
16 …
17 "SECTION 6.9.(e) No later than April 15, 2026, OSBM and NCPRO shall jointly report to
18 the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations and the Fiscal Research Division
19 on all actions taken under this section, including:
20 (1) For each agency and project meeting the revenue-loss criteria in subsection
21 (a) of this section, the project name and the amount of remaining unexpended
22 SFRF as of December 31, 2025. The report shall also identify which projects
23 are complete and have excess SFRF funds and which SFRF projects need
24 additional funds for completion.
25 (2) For each agency and project from which unexpended SFRF were reclassified,
26 the project name and the amount of the corresponding nonrecurring net
27 General Fund appropriation provided in lieu of SFRF.
28 (3) For each agency and project receiving SFRF pursuant to the reclassification,
29 the project name and the amount of SFRF provided.
30 The report shall also certify that all SFRF were obligated on or before December 31, 2024, and
31 that expenditures are being made consistent with 31 C.F.R. Part 35 and related United States
32 Department of the Treasury guidance.
33 "SECTION 6.9.(f) The Director of the Budget shall ensure that implementation of this
34 section results in no change to the net General Fund appropriations in either the 2025-2026 or
35 2026-2027 fiscal year.years."
36 SECTION 1.(b) No later than January 15, 2027, OSBM and NCPRO shall jointly
37 update the report required by Section 6.9(e) of S.L. 2025-97, as amended by subsection (a) of
38 this section, to reflect agency and project status through December 31, 2026.
39 SECTION 1.1. Subdivision (5) of Section 6.2 of S.L. 2026-1 reads as rewritten:
40 "(5) Funds allocated to the Office of State Budget and Management by Section
41 12.2(f)(2) of S.L. 2023-134 to provide a grant to the Burke Partnership for
42 Economic Development, Inc., a nonprofit corporation, (Partnership) to install
43 water and wastewater at the Western NC Megasite shall, and remaining
44 unspent and unencumbered as of the effective date of this section, shall,
45 notwithstanding that subdivision or any provision of law to the contrary, be
46 reallocated to Burke County to be used for water and wastewater projects in
47 Burke County.identified in an application as, and used as, matching funds for
48 a grant applied for and awarded by the United States Department of
49 Commerce, Economic Development Administration. The Partnership shall
50 not expend or encumber funds identified in the application until a
51 determination regarding the grant is made. In the event the grant is not

1 awarded on or before October 1, 2026, the Partnership shall distribute the
2 funds, including any interest earned thereon, as follows: eighty percent (80%)
3 to Burke County and twenty percent (20%) to McDowell County. The
4 recipient counties shall use the funds distributed pursuant to this subdivision
5 to construct or improve water infrastructure, sewer infrastructure, or both."

7 ADDITIONAL STATE FISCAL RECOVERY FUND FLEXIBILITY
8 SECTION 2.(a) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, and subject
9 to the conditions set out in subsection (b) of this section, the North Carolina Pandemic Recovery
10 Office (NCPRO), in consultation with the Director of the Budget, may reallocate State Fiscal
11 Recovery Funds (SFRF) appropriated by this act or any act of the General Assembly, including,
12 but not limited to:
13 (1) S.L. 2021-180.
14 (2) S.L. 2021-189.
15 (3) S.L. 2022-6.
16 (4) S.L. 2022-74.
17 (5) S.L. 2023-134.
18 (6) S.L. 2024-1.
19 (7) S.L. 2024-40.
20 (8) S.L. 2024-53.
21 (9) S.L. 2024-55.
22 SECTION 2.(b) The funds set out in subsection (a) of this section may be reallocated
23 only when all of the following conditions are met:
24 (1) There is a reasonable expectation that the funds will not be expended before
25 the deadline established by applicable federal law or guidance.
26 (2) The reallocation is made to support activities that previously received an
27 appropriation from the SFRF, are authorized under this act or one of the acts
28 listed in subsection (a) of this section, and adhere to Federal SFRF
29 Expenditure Category 5 (Water, Sewer, or Broadband Infrastructure)
30 guidance. Reallocated funds shall not be used for any new activity, purpose,
31 or program.
32 SECTION 2.(c) The Director of the Budget shall reallocate funds under subsection
33 (b) of this section by making nonrecurring transfers of General Fund appropriations from each
34 agency receiving SFRF fund transfers to the agency from which the SFRF funds were transferred.
35 The authorization to reallocate under this section is not an authorization to modify the purposes
36 of the original appropriations to either the receiving or transferring agency.
37 SECTION 2.(d) The Director of the Budget may allocate any funds remaining after
38 the reallocation process authorized in subsection (b) of this section to the State Treasurer up to
39 an amount equal to the remaining unreimbursed COVID-19-related expenses incurred by the
40 North Carolina State Health Plan for Teachers and State Employees between March 3, 2021, and
41 December 31, 2024, provided the reallocation is consistent with federal law and guidelines.
42 SECTION 2.(e) To the extent that funds reallocated pursuant to this section are
43 unappropriated, those funds are hereby appropriated and available for use pursuant to this section.
44 SECTION 2.(f) Reporting. – No later than January 15, 2027, the Office of State
45 Budget and Management (OSBM) shall report to the Fiscal Research Division on the
46 reallocations made pursuant to this section in the same manner as required in Section 6.9(e) of
47 S.L. 2025-97.
