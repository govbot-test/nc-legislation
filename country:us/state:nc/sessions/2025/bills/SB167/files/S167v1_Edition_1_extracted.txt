GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
SENATE BILL 167
Short Title: Revise, Study, and Fund LEP Allotment. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Meyer, Murdock, and Robinson (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
February 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REVISE THE STUDENTS WITH LIMITED ENGLISH PROFICIENCY
3 ALLOTMENT, TO REQUIRE THE STATE BOARD OF EDUCATION TO STUDY THE
4 STUDENTS WITH LIMITED ENGLISH PROFICIENCY ALLOTMENT, AND TO
5 APPROPRIATE FUNDS TO THE STUDENTS WITH LIMITED ENGLISH
6 PROFICIENCY ALLOTMENT.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1.(a) For the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium, the State Board of Education
9 shall develop guidelines for identifying and providing services to students with limited
10 proficiency in the English language, as follows:
11 (1) The State Board of Education shall allocate these funds to local school
12 administrative units and to charter schools under a formula that takes into
13 account the average percentage of students in the units or the charters over the
14 past three years who have limited English proficiency. The State Board of
15 Education shall allocate funds to a unit or a charter school only if (i) average
16 daily membership of the unit or the charter school includes at least 20 students
17 with limited English proficiency or (ii) students with limited English
18 proficiency comprise at least two and one-half percent (2.5%) of the average
19 daily membership of the unit or charter school. For the portion of the funds
20 that is allocated on the basis of the number of identified students, the State
21 Board of Education shall not set a maximum number or percentage of
22 identified students for whom a unit or charter school may receive funds.
23 (2) Local school administrative units shall use funds allocated to them to pay for
24 classroom teachers, teacher assistants, tutors, textbooks, classroom
25 materials/instructional supplies/equipment, transportation costs, and staff
26 development of teachers for students with limited English proficiency.
27 (3) A county in which a local school administrative unit receives funds under this
28 section shall use the funds to supplement local current expense funds and shall
29 not supplant local current expense funds.
30 SECTION 1.(b) The Department of Public Instruction shall prepare a current head
31 count of the number of students classified with limited English proficiency by December 1 of
32 each year. Students in the head count shall be assessed at least once every three years to determine
33 their level of English proficiency.
34 SECTION 2. The State Board of Education shall study the allotment for students
35 with limited English proficiency (LEP), as enacted by Section 1 of this act. No later than
36 December 31, 2025, the State Board of Education shall report the results of its study to the Joint

1 Legislative Education Oversight Committee and the Fiscal Research Division. The report shall
2 include, at a minimum, the following information:
3 (1) Whether the current LEP allotment is aligned with resources needed to ensure
4 that students with limited English proficiency have the opportunity to become
5 proficient in English.
6 (2) A recommended funding factor to be included in the LEP allotment to account
7 for the number of languages spoken by students in local school administrative
8 units and charter schools.
9 (3) A recommended plan to foster more bilingual education programs in the State.
10 (4) Recommended funding models, with associated costs, to eliminate any
11 achievement gap between students who are proficient in English and students
12 with limited English proficiency.
13 (5) An analysis of the effects of eliminating in the LEP allotment any
14 consideration of (i) the concentration of students with limited English
15 proficiency and (ii) a minimum eligibility threshold. The analysis should
16 include whether to eliminate any or all of these components of the LEP
17 allotment and proposed alternative metrics for allotting these funds.
18 SECTION 3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
19 Public Instruction the sum of sixteen million two hundred thousand dollars ($16,200,000) in
20 recurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to increase the students with limited English
21 proficiency allotment.
22 SECTION 4. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
