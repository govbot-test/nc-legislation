1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE FOR EXTENDED-YEAR CONTRACTS FOR INITIALLY
3 LICENSED TEACHERS, MENTOR TEACHERS, TEACHERS IN LOW-PERFORMING
4 SCHOOLS, AND CERTAIN VETERAN TEACHERS.
5 Whereas, students who are struggling in school need an extended school year and
6 individualized instructional support; and
7 Whereas, students and teachers would benefit from additional opportunities for
8 teachers to participate in professional development; and
9 Whereas, teachers have limited time to engage in professional development during
10 the school year because their time is largely committed to working with students; and
11 Whereas, teachers need more time to learn from other teachers in the context of
12 teacher-designed and teacher-led professional development; and
13 Whereas, effective professional development requires learning that is situated in
14 teachers' actual practice and focused on students' thinking and work instead of separated from
15 teaching and learning by time and location; and
16 Whereas, communities of practice are essential to providing teachers with
17 opportunities to lead, learn from, and collaborate with their professional peers; and
18 Whereas, transformative learning takes time to become established, and professional
19 development that is sustained over the course of an academic year provides that time; and
20 Whereas, teacher evaluations should provide meaningful feedback to teachers; and
21 Whereas, the teacher evaluation process can incorporate professional development in
22 order to improve teacher performance; and
23 Whereas, teachers should be compensated for participating in professional activities
24 by way of an extended contractual year; and
25 Whereas, the General Assembly supports providing compensation for teachers in
26 accordance with advanced teaching roles; and
27 Whereas, extended-year teacher contracts and advanced teaching roles can
28 complement one another to improve teacher performance and student achievement; Now,
29 therefore,
30 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

28 SECTION 2. Article 20 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
29 adding a new section to read:
30 "§ 115C-306.1. Extended-year teacher contracts.
31 (a) Definitions. – As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
32 (1) Extended-year contract. – A contract providing for a term of employment of
33 11 months during the school year.
34 (2) IL teacher. – A teacher who holds a current North Carolina teaching license
35 in any of the following classes:
36 a. A three-year initial professional license.
37 b. A three-year limited license.
38 c. A one-year residency license.
39 d. A one-year emergency license.
40 (3) LPS teacher. – A teacher assigned by a local board of education to teach at a
41 school identified as low-performing as provided in G.S. 115C-105.37.
42 (4) Mentor teacher. – A teacher designated by the local board of education as a
43 mentor teacher. A mentor teacher may include a teacher in an advanced
44 teaching role pursuant to G.S. 115C-311.
45 (5) Veteran teacher. – A teacher who meets all of the following requirements:
46 a. Holds a current five-year North Carolina continuing professional
47 license for teaching.
48 b. Has at least six years of licensed teaching experience.
49 c. Is not an IL teacher, a mentor teacher, or an LPS teacher.

1 (b) Extended-Year Contracts. – Local boards of education shall, in awarding contracts to
2 teachers as provided in Part 3 of Article 22 of this Chapter, provide for extended-year contracts
3 as follows:
4 (1) Mandatory extended-year contracts. – The local board of education shall
5 award mandatory extended-year contracts to all IL teachers and all LPS
6 teachers. IL teachers and LPS teachers shall not be eligible to receive
7 10-month contracts.
8 (2) Optional extended-year contracts. – The local board of education shall offer
9 optional extended-year contracts as follows:
10 a. To mentor teachers in any year that the teacher is designated as a
11 mentor teacher.
12 b. To veteran teachers one year out of each three-year period the teacher
13 is employed by the local board.
14 Mentor teachers and veteran teachers may accept the extended-year contract
15 or may request and be awarded a 10-month contract.
16 (c) Designation of Mentor Teachers. – The local board of education shall annually
17 designate up to ten percent (10%) of those teachers employed by the local board as mentor
18 teachers. The local board of education shall, at a minimum, use the following criteria in
19 designating mentor teachers:
20 (1) Effectiveness in teaching and mentoring other teachers.
21 (2) Contribution to academic success of students.
22 The local board of education shall use annual teacher evaluations and other research-based
23 metrics in designating mentor teachers.
24 (d) Extended-Year Contract Duties. – A teacher who is awarded and accepts an
25 extended-year contract shall be assigned, at a minimum, the following additional job duties in
26 the eleventh month of employment:
27 (1) IL teachers. – IL teachers shall work with mentor teachers to improve
28 professional skills and increase classroom effectiveness. IL teachers shall
29 participate in professional development and support extended school year
30 instruction for students performing below grade level.
31 (2) Mentor teachers. – Mentor teachers shall do at least one of the following:
32 a. Mentor and teach other teachers, including IL teachers.
33 b. Provide professional development to other teachers.
34 c. Support extended school year instruction for students performing
35 below grade level.
36 d. Engage in other professional collaborations to improve student
37 success.
38 (3) LPS teachers. – LPS teachers shall be assigned additional job duties in the
39 discretion of the local board of education, which may include teaching an
40 extended school instructional year or engaging in high-quality professional
41 development.
42 (4) Veteran teachers. – Veteran teachers shall be assigned additional job duties
43 related to the provision of high-quality professional development in the
44 discretion of the local board of education."
