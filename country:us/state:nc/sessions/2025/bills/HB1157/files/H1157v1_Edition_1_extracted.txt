Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Red Wolf Special Registration Plate. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Ball, Ross, and Alston (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Appropriations, if favorable, Finance, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and
Operations of the House
May 4, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO AUTHORIZE THE DIVISION OF MOTOR VEHICLES TO ISSUE A RED
3 WOLF SPECIAL REGISTRATION PLATE, TO PROVIDE FOR THE DISTRIBUTION
4 OF THE FEES FROM THE PLATE TO SUPPORT RED WOLF CONSERVATION, TO
5 APPROPRIATE FUNDS FOR THE DEVELOPMENT AND INITIAL ISSUANCE OF
6 THE PLATE AND FOR CAMPUS PROMOTION OF THE PLATE, AND TO DIRECT
7 NORTH CAROLINA STATE UNIVERSITY TO PROMOTE THE PLATE ON CAMPUS.
8 Whereas, the red wolf is one of the rarest canids in the world and is native to North
9 Carolina and the southeastern United States; and
10 Whereas, North Carolina holds a unique place in the history and future of red wolf
11 conservation; and
12 Whereas, the preservation of native wildlife is important to the natural heritage,
13 environmental health, educational mission, and cultural identity of the State; and
14 Whereas, the creation of a Red Wolf special registration plate would allow residents
15 of the State to demonstrate support for the preservation of this iconic native species; and
16 Whereas, revenues generated from the issuance of the Red Wolf special registration
17 plate can provide a recurring source of support for red wolf conservation, management, research,
18 monitoring, and habitat preservation and restoration; and
19 Whereas, it is in the interest of the State to provide one-time funds to support the
20 initial design, development, and issuance of the plate so that the plate may be made available
21 without unnecessary delay; and
22 Whereas, North Carolina State University, as a leading public institution with the
23 Wolfpack identity and a broad statewide alumni and supporter network, is well positioned to help
24 promote awareness of the Red Wolf special registration plate and its conservation purpose; Now,
25 therefore,
26 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
27 SECTION 1. G.S. 20-63 reads as rewritten:
28 "§ 20-63. Registration plates furnished by Division; requirements; replacement of regular
29 plates with First in Flight plates, First in Freedom plates, or National/State
30 Mottos plates; surrender and reissuance; displaying; preservation and cleaning;
31 alteration or concealment of numbers; commission contracts for issuance.
32 …
33 (b1) The following special registration plates do not have to be a "First in Flight" plate,
34 "First in Freedom" plate, or "National/State Mottos" plate as provided in subsection (b) of this

3 special plates authorized in G.S. 20-79.7 on or after July 1, 2013, the Division may not issue the

10 "§ 20-79.4. Special registration plates.
11 …
12 (b) Types. – The Division shall issue the following types of special registration plates:
13 …
14 () Red Wolf. – Issuable to the registered owner of a motor vehicle in accordance
15 with G.S. 20-81.12. The plate shall bear the phrase "Red Wolf" and an image
16 that depicts a red wolf. The plate authorized by this subdivision is not subject
17 to the provisions of G.S. 20-79.3A or G.S. 20-79.8.
18 …."
19 SECTION 3. G.S. 20-79.7 reads as rewritten:
20 "§ 20-79.7. Fees for special registration plates and distribution of the fees.
21 …

34 section among the Special Registration Plate Account (SRPA), the Collegiate and Cultural

46 SECTION 4. G.S. 20-81.12 is amended by adding the following new subsection to
47 read:
48 "() Red Wolf. – The Division shall transfer quarterly the money in the Collegiate and
49 Cultural Attraction Plate Account derived from the sale of Red Wolf plates to the North Carolina
50 Wildlife Resources Commission to be used for red wolf conservation, management, research,
51 monitoring, and habitat preservation and restoration."

1 SECTION 5. The Revisor of Statutes is authorized to alphabetize, number, and
2 renumber the special registration plates listed in G.S. 20-63(b1), 20-79.4(b) and 20-81.12 to
3 ensure that all of the special registration plates are listed in alphabetical order and numbered
4 accordingly.
5 SECTION 6. North Carolina State University, through the College of Veterinary
6 Medicine, shall actively promote the Red Wolf special registration plate on campus and through
7 appropriate university communications channels by, at a minimum, doing all of the following:
8 (1) Include information about the availability and purpose of the plate on an
9 appropriate University webpage.
10 (2) Promote the plate through student, alumni, faculty, staff, and athletics
11 communications, as appropriate.
12 (3) Coordinate, as appropriate, with the Division of Motor Vehicles and the North
13 Carolina Wildlife Resources Commission on messaging regarding the plate
14 and the conservation purpose for which plate proceeds are used.
15 (4) Make reasonable efforts to market the plate at campus events and through
16 other outreach channels under the University's control.
17 SECTION 7. No later than September 1, 2027, North Carolina State University, in
18 consultation with the Division of Motor Vehicles and the North Carolina Wildlife Resources
19 Commission, shall report to the Joint Legislative Transportation Oversight Committee, the
20 Senate Appropriations Committee on Agriculture, Natural, and Economic Resources, the House
21 Appropriations Committee on Agriculture and Natural and Economic Resources, and the Fiscal
22 Research Division on the University's efforts to promote the plate and the number of plates
23 issued.
24 SECTION 8. There is appropriated from the Highway Fund to the Department of
25 Transportation, Division of Motor Vehicles, the sum of fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000) in
26 nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year to develop the Red Wolf special registration
27 plate authorized by this act, including plate design, programming, setup, production preparation,
28 and associated administrative costs for initial implementation.
29 SECTION 9. There is appropriated from the General Fund to The University of
30 North Carolina Board of Governors for allocation to North Carolina State University, College of
31 Veterinary Medicine, the sum of fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) in nonrecurring funds for the
32 2026-2027 fiscal year to support the initial promotion and public awareness of the Red Wolf
33 special registration plate established by this act.
34 SECTION 10. Sections 8 and 9 of this act become effective July 1, 2026. The
35 remainder of this act is effective when it becomes law.
