5 SECTION 1. Section 7.36(f) of S.L. 2023-134, as amended by Section 3J.17 of S.L.
6 2024-57, reads as rewritten:
7 "SECTION 7.36.(f) Grants for Training to Increase School Safety. – Of the funds
8 appropriated by this act for the grants provided in this section, the Executive Director of the
9 Center for Safer Schools, in consultation with the Department of Health and Human Services,
10 shall award grants to public school units to contract with community partners to address school
11 safety by providing training to help students develop healthy responses to trauma and stress. The
12 training shall be targeted and evidence-based and shall include any of the following services:
13 (1) Counseling on Access to Lethal Means (CALM) training for school health
14 support personnel, local first responders, and teachers on the topics of suicide
15 prevention and reducing access by students to lethal means.
16 (2) Training for school health support personnel on comprehensive and
17 evidence-based clinical treatments for students and their parents or guardians,
18 including any of the following:
19 a. Parent-child interaction therapy.
20 b. Trauma-focused cognitive behavioral therapy.
21 c. Behavioral therapy.
22 d. Dialectical behavior therapy.
23 e. Child-parent psychotherapy.
24 (3) Training for students and school employees on community resilience models
25 to models, violence prevention, and developing personal and interpersonal
26 skills to (i) enhance individual level protective factors, (ii) mitigate or reduce
27 risk taking or harmful behavior, and (iii) improve understanding and responses
28 to trauma and significant stress.
29 (4) Training for school health support personnel on Modular Approach to
30 Therapy for Children with Anxiety, Depression, Trauma, or Conduct
31 problems (MATCH-ADTC), including any of the following components:
32 a. Trauma-focused cognitive behavioral therapy.
33 b. Parent and student coping skills.
34 c. Problem solving.
