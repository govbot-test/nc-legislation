5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) Part 3A of Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
7 amended by adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 115C-102.10. Internet safety policy.
9 (a) Local boards of education shall adopt policies for student access to the internet on
10 devices or internet services provided by the local school administrative unit. The policies shall
11 do at least the following:
12 (1) Limit access by students to only age-appropriate subject matter and materials.
13 (2) Protect the safety and security of students when accessing email, chat rooms,
14 and other forms of electronic communication.
15 (3) Prohibit unauthorized access by students to data or information maintained by
16 the local school administrative unit, including by "hacking" and other
17 unlawful online activities.
18 (4) Prevent access to websites, web applications, or software that do not protect
19 against the disclosure, use, or dissemination of a student's personal
20 information.
21 (5) Prohibit and prevent students from accessing social media platforms, except
22 when expressly directed by a teacher solely for educational purposes.
23 (6) Prohibit the use of the TikTok application. For the purposes of this section,
24 TikTok includes any successor application or service developed or provided
25 by ByteDance Limited or an entity owned by ByteDance Limited.
26 (b) Employees of local boards of education shall not use TikTok for any job-related
27 duties, including classroom instruction or to communicate or promote any unit- or
28 school-sponsored club, extracurricular organization, or athletic team."
29 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 115C-47 is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
30 "(70) To Adopt an Internet Safety Policy. – Local boards of education shall adopt
31 policies for internet safety in accordance with G.S. 115C-102.10."
32 SECTION 2.(a) Part 1 of Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
33 amended by adding a new section to read:
34 "§ 115C-81.26. Social media and mental health.

16 SECTION 2.(b) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning

18 SECTION 3. Article 7B of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
19 adding a new Part to read:
20 "Part 8. Miscellaneous Requirements.
21 "§ 115C-76.100. Regulation of wireless communication devices.
22 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
23 (1) Wireless communication device. – Any portable wireless device that has the
24 capability to provide voice, messaging, or other data communication between
25 two or more parties, including the following:
26 a. Cellular telephones.
27 b. Tablet computers.
28 c. Laptop computers.
29 d. Paging devices.
30 e. Two-way radios.
31 f. Gaming devices.
32 (2) Wireless communication policy. – A policy governing the use of wireless
33 communication devices by students.
34 (b) The governing body of a public school unit shall establish a wireless communication
35 policy. At a minimum, except as permitted in subsection (c) of this section, the policy shall
36 prohibit students from using, displaying, or having a wireless communication device turned on
37 during instructional time.
38 (c) A wireless communication policy shall allow student use of a wireless communication
39 device during instructional time as follows:
40 (1) If authorized by a teacher for educational purposes or for use in the event of
41 an emergency. The governing body may establish parameters to be followed
42 by a teacher in granting authorizations.
43 (2) As required by the student's individualized education program or section 504
44 (29 U.S.C. § 794) plan.
45 (3) As required to manage a student's health care, in accordance with a
46 documented medical condition.
47 (d) The governing body shall establish the consequences for violations of the wireless
48 communication policy, which may include confiscation of the wireless communication device
49 and disciplinary measures under the public school unit's Code of Student Conduct.
50 (e) No later than September 1 of the year in which this section becomes effective, every
51 public school unit shall send to the Department of Public Instruction a copy of the public school
