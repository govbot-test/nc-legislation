4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 126-14.6 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 126-14.6. Ensuring dignity and nondiscrimination in State government workplaces.
7 (a) The General Assembly finds that Article I, Section 1 of the Constitution of this State
8 recognizes the equality and rights of all persons. Therefore, it is the intent of the General
9 Assembly that State employees respect the dignity of others, acknowledge the right of others to
10 express differing opinions, and the right to freedom of speech and association and that State
11 agencies employ training methods and procedures to further that intent. Further, it is the public
12 policy of this State that State employee hiring, promotion, and training shall be without regard to
13 race or ethnicity, religion, or sex and that applications for State employment shall not inquire
14 about race.
15 (b) For the purposes of this section, "promote" shall mean compelling State employees
16 or contractors to affirm or profess belief in the concepts described in subsection (c) of this section.
17 (c) The concepts listed in this subsection shall not be promoted in State government
18 workplaces or included as part of any State employee training program:
19 (1) One race or sex is inherently superior to another race or sex.
20 (2) An individual, solely by virtue of his or her race or sex, is inherently racist,
21 sexist, or oppressive.
22 (3) An individual should be discriminated against or receive adverse treatment
23 solely or partly because of his or her race or sex.
24 (4) An individual's moral character is necessarily determined by his or her race or
25 sex.
26 (5) An individual, solely by virtue of his or her race or sex, bears responsibility
27 for actions committed in the past by other members of the same race or sex.
28 (6) Any individual, solely by virtue of his or her race or sex, should feel
29 discomfort, guilt, anguish, or any other form of psychological distress.
30 (7) A meritocracy is inherently racist or sexist.
31 (8) The United States was created by members of a particular race or sex for the
32 purpose of oppressing members of another race or sex.
33 (9) The United States government should be violently overthrown.
34 (10) Particular character traits, values, moral or ethical codes, privileges, or beliefs
35 should be ascribed to a race or sex or to an individual because of the
36 individual's race or sex.

35 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 126-34.02(b) reads as rewritten:
36 "(b) The following issues may be heard as contested cases after completion of the agency
37 grievance procedure and the Office of State Human Resources review:
38 (1) Discrimination or harassment. – An applicant for State employment, a State
39 employee, or former State employee may allege discrimination or harassment
40 based on race, religion, color, ethnicity, national origin, sex, age, disability,
41 genetic information, or political affiliation if the employee believes that he or
42 she has been discriminated against in his or her application for employment
43 or in the terms and conditions of the employee's employment, or in the
44 termination of his or her employment. A violation of G.S. 126-14.6 constitutes
45 employment discrimination under this subsection.

19 SECTION 4. Article 10 of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
20 a new section to read:
21 "§ 143-162.8. No public funds for discriminatory programs.
22 (a) No State agency, unit of local government, or non-State entity may use any State
23 funds or public monies to promote, support, fund, implement, or maintain discriminatory
24 programs. For the purposes of this section, a "discriminatory program" means a program
25 promoting the concepts listed in subsection (c) of G.S. 153A-100.1.
26 (b) No State agency, unit of local government, or non-State entity shall apply for, accept,
27 or utilize federal funds, grants, or other financial assistance that require compliance with
28 discriminatory programs. Any existing programs funded through such means shall be
29 discontinued unless continued participation is expressly required by federal law.
30 (c) The following definitions apply in this section:
31 (1) Non-State entity. – As defined in G.S. 143C-1-1.
32 (2) Public monies. – Funds from any source budgeted or expended by a local
33 political subdivision of the State, including, but not limited to, revenue
34 authorized by G.S. 153A-149 or G.S. 160A-209.
35 (3) State agency. – A unit of the executive, legislative, or judicial branch of State
36 government, such as a department, institution, division, commission, board,
37 council, community college, or The University of North Carolina system.
38 (4) State funds. – As defined in G.S. 143C-1-1. The term includes any monies
39 received or held by a constituent institution of The University of North
40 Carolina, including endowment funds as defined in G.S. 116-36 and
41 institutional trust funds as defined in G.S. 116-36.1.
42 (5) Unit of local government. – As defined in G.S. 143C-1-1.
43 (d) The prohibitions contained in subsections (a) and (b) of this section include, but are
44 not limited to, using State funds or public monies to do any of the following:
45 (1) Utilize discriminatory programs in employee hirings, trainings, or
46 promotions; admissions; or the awarding of contracts.
47 (2) Maintain offices or dedicated staff positions (whether permanent,
48 time-limited, full-time, part-time, or temporary) for discriminatory programs.
49 (e) Nothing in this section shall be construed to conflict with, restrict, limit, or infringe
50 upon speech protected by the First Amendment of the U.S. Constitution.

40 (l) An employee of a State agency, unit of local government, or non-State entity may
41 bring a civil action for damages to the employee resulting from a violation of this section. The
42 civil action may be brought in the county where the employee resides or in which all or a
43 substantial part of the acts or omissions giving rise to the action occurred.
44 (m) The liability and penalty provisions contained in this section for violating its
45 provisions are in addition to, and not in lieu of, liability under any other applicable provision of
46 law or cause of action in consequence of the violation.
