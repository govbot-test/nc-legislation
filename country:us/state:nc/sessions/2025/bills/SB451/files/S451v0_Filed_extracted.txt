6 PART I. REDUCTION OF CONTINUING EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS FOR
7 CERTAIN BOARDS THAT ARE SET BY RULE
8 SECTION 1.1.(a) Reduction of Continuing Education Requirements. –
9 Notwithstanding any other provision of law or rule, covered boards shall reduce the continuing
10 education and professional development hour requirements established by the covered boards'
11 respective rules by fifty percent (50%).
12 SECTION 1.1.(b) Implementation. – The boards listed in subsection (d) of this
13 section shall implement this reduction by either (i) reducing continuing education rules per
14 renewal cycle by fifty percent (50%) or (ii) keeping the same number of hours required by
15 continuing education rules as of the effective date of this act and doubling the time allowed to
16 achieve those credit hours.
17 SECTION 1.1.(c) Applicability. – If the reduction of continuing education rules
18 would invalidate an accreditation, Interstate Compact, or federal law or rule for a type of license,
19 certification, or permit of a covered board, then the requirement to reduce the continuing
20 education rules for that specific license is suspended. The covered board shall submit a report to
21 the Joint Legislative Administrative Procedure Oversight Committee by October 1, 2025,
22 indicating the invalidation or conflict under this section and a proposed legislative
23 recommendation to reduce the requirements of continuing education rules to the maximum extent
24 allowed without invalidating the licensure, certification, or permit.
25 SECTION 1.1.(d) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
26 (1) Continuing education rules. – Any rule adopted by a covered board that sets
27 an amount of hours for professional training, professional development,
28 continuing education, continuing legal education, continuing medical
29 education, or other training required for a renewal of licensure, certification,
30 or permit for a licensee of a covered board.
31 (2) Covered board. – All of the following boards that set continuing education
32 requirements by rule:
33 a. North Carolina Board of Landscape Architects.
34 b. North Carolina Board for Licensing of Soil Scientists.
35 c. North Carolina State Board of Examiners for Engineers and
36 Surveyors.

39 PART III. CARRYOVER OF EARNED HOURS
40 SECTION 3.1.(a) Carryover of Continuing Education Hours. – Notwithstanding any
41 provision of law to the contrary, each occupational licensing board subject to this act may adopt
42 or amend its rules to allow licensees to carry over continuing education or professional
43 development hours from one compliance window to the next, provided all of the following
44 conditions are met:
45 (1) Preservation of the fifty percent (50%) reduction. – Whether a board has
46 shortened the total hours per renewal cycle or extended the continuing
47 education or professional development hour compliance window (e.g., from
48 12 months to 24 months), any carryover policies shall not undermine the intent
49 of this act to reduce the effective continuing education and professional
50 development hour burden by fifty percent (50%) on an annualized basis.
Page 12 DRS45243-NN-28B

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (2) No double counting. – Any hours used to satisfy a licensee's continuing
2 education or professional development hour requirement for one compliance
3 window or renewal cycle may not fully satisfy the requirement for a
4 subsequent compliance window or renewal cycle, unless the board's rules
5 clearly define allowable partial carryover or additional hours earned in excess
6 of the requirement.
7 (3) Uniform application. – Any hours earned prior to the effective date of this act
8 that are carried forward to a future compliance window shall be treated
9 uniformly for all licensees similarly situated. The board may set a maximum
10 limit on the number of hours carried over, how long those hours remain valid,
11 or whether partial hours (e.g., half-hour increments) are permissible.
12 SECTION 3.1.(b) Annual Renewal vs. Continuing Education Compliance Window.
13 – Nothing in this section requires a board to alter its statutory or regulatory license renewal
14 period. A board may maintain its current renewal cycle, whether it is annual or already longer
15 than one year, while granting licensees a compliance window sufficient to achieve a fifty percent
16 (50%) annualized reduction in the existing CE/PDH requirement mandated by this act.
