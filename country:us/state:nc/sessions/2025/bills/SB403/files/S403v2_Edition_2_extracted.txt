10 ADDITIONAL AGENCY VACANT POSITION CUTS
11 SECTION 1.1.(a) Reduction. – By October 1, 2025, the Governor and the Office of
12 State Budget and Management shall identify and eliminate vacant positions in State agencies
13 necessary to achieve a total reduction of not less than nineteen million seven hundred forty-two
14 thousand two hundred forty-three dollars ($19,742,243) in recurring funds, beginning with the
15 2025-2026 fiscal year. For purposes of this section, "State agency" means the principal
16 departments listed in G.S. 143B-6, with the exception of the State Bureau of Investigation and
17 the State Highway Patrol.
18 SECTION 1.1.(b) Report. – The Office of State Budget and Management shall
19 submit a report to the Fiscal Research Division with a list of each position eliminated, identified
20 by position number, title, and the amount of salary and fringe benefits associated with the
21 position, no later than December 1, 2025.

18 "§ 108A-70.39. Average processing time standards.
19 (a) Average processing time is calculated by finding the processing time for each case
20 that received a disposition during a given month and finding the average of those processing
21 times.
22 (b) The standard for average processing time is 90 days for cases in which the individual
23 has applied for the Medicaid Aid to the Disabled category (M-AD) and 45 days for all other
24 cases.
25 "§ 108A-70.40. Percentage processed timely standards.
26 (a) Percentage of applications processed timely is the percentage of cases that received a
27 timely disposition in a given month. The percentage of applications processed timely is calculated
28 by expressing the number of cases during a given month with a processing time equal to or less
29 than the standard set in G.S. 108A-70.37 as a percentage of the total cases receiving a disposition
30 during that month. When the deadline for meeting the timely decision standard in
31 G.S. 108A-70.37 falls on a weekend or holiday, an application that receives a disposition on the
32 first workday following the deadline shall be considered timely for purposes of calculating the
33 percentage of applications processed timely.
34 (b) The Department is authorized to adopt rules to establish a percentage standard for
35 each county department of social services that will be the percentage of applications processed
36 timely standard for that county department of social services. Until the Department adopts rules
37 establishing percentage standards for each county, the percentage of applications processed
38 timely standards are those established in 10A NCAC 23C .0203 as of April 2016.
39 (c) Percentage of redeterminations processed timely is the percentage of cases, out of the
40 total number of cases in a given month for which a redetermination of Medicaid eligibility was
41 required to be completed for a given month as required by federal or State law, regulation, or
42 rule, that were completed by the required deadline.
43 (d) The Department may adopt rules to establish a percentage standard for each county
44 department of social services that will be the percentage of redeterminations processed timely
45 for that county department of social services. If no rule establishing a percentage standard is in
46 effect, then the percentage of redeterminations processed timely standard is ninety-five percent
47 (95%).
