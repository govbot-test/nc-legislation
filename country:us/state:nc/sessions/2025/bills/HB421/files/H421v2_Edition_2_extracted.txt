Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Senate Transportation Committee Substitute Adopted 6/11/25
Short Title: Motor Vehicle Dealers. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 18, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CLARIFY VARIOUS MOTOR VEHICLE DEALER LAWS.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4
5 EXTEND DEALER LICENSE GRACE PERIOD
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 20-295(b) reads as rewritten:
7 "(b) Pending License Renewal Grace Period. – When an application for license renewal
8 has been timely submitted prior to expiration of the license, the license shall remain valid for up
9 to 30 days 60 days after the expiration date until the Division grants or denies the application.
10 The Division shall (i) ensure that any database maintained by the Division that indicates the status
11 of a license issued under this Article reflects that the license continues to be valid during this
12 period and (ii) send a temporary license to the renewal applicant for display to evidence extension
13 of the validity of the license to engage in business in this State while the Division reviews the
14 renewal application. The temporary license issued by the Division pursuant to this subsection
15 shall contain on its face the following notation: "This temporary license is issued pursuant to
16 G.S. 20-295 during a license renewal application review by the North Carolina Division of Motor
17 Vehicles and is valid to engage in business in this State with all rights and privileges of a license."
18 In accordance with G.S. 20-79(c), a dealer license plate issued to a dealer whose license expires
19 and is extended under this subsection remains valid as long as the temporary license issued under
20 this subsection is valid."
21
22 ALIGN DEALER LICENSE PLATE RENEWAL WITH DEALER LICENSE
23 RENEWALS
24 SECTION 2. G.S. 20-79 reads as rewritten:
25 "§ 20-79. Dealer license plates.
26 …
27 (c) Form and Duration. – A dealer license plate is subject to G.S. 20-63, except for the
28 requirement that the plate display the registration number of a motor vehicle and the requirement
29 that the plate be a "First in Flight" plate, a "First in Freedom" plate, or a "National/State Mottos"
30 plate. A dealer license plate must have a distinguishing symbol identifying the plate as a dealer
31 license plate. The symbol may vary depending upon the classification of dealer license plate
32 issued. The Division must provide suitably reduced sized license plates for motorcycle dealers
33 and manufacturers.
34 A dealer license plate is issued for a period of one year. two years. The Division shall vary
35 the expiration dates of dealer registration renewals so that an approximately equal number expires
36 at the end of each month, quarter, or other period consisting of one or more months. months to

1 coincide with G.S. 20-288(c). An otherwise valid dealer license plate issued to a dealer that
2 expires on the expiration date of the dealer's license or during the applicable pending dealer
3 license renewal grace period remains valid as long as the temporary license issued under
4 G.S. 20-295(b) is valid. A dealer license plate may be transferred from one vehicle to another.
5 When the Division issues a dealer plate, it may issue a registration that expires at the end of any
6 monthly interval. When one of the following occurs, a dealer must surrender to the Division all
7 dealer license plates issued to the dealer:
8 (1) The dealer surrenders the license issued to the dealer under Article 12 of this
9 Chapter.
10 (2) The Division suspends or revokes the license issued to the dealer under Article
11 12 of this Chapter.
12 (3) The Division rescinds the dealer license plates because of a violation of the
13 restrictions on the use of a dealer license plate.
14 To obtain a dealer license plate after it has been surrendered, the dealer must file a new
15 application for a dealer license plate and pay the required fee for the plate.
16 (c1) Dealer Plate Mandatory Replacement. – Notwithstanding G.S. 20-63.1, registration
17 plates issued under this section shall be replaced every three four years.
18 …."
19
20 EFFECTIVE DATE
21 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025.
