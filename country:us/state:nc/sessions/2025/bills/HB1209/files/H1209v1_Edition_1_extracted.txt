GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 1209
Short Title: Strengthen Ed by Embedded New Teacher Support. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Ball, R. Pierce, Prather, and Budd (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
May 5, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS TO INCREASE EMBEDDED SUPPORT FOR
3 TEACHERS PARTICIPATING IN THE NEW TEACHER SUPPORT PROGRAM.
4 Whereas, local school administrative units in North Carolina face persistent
5 challenges in recruiting, developing, and retaining high-quality teachers; and
6 Whereas, early-career teachers who make up a significant portion of the workforce in
7 North Carolina communities often enter classrooms underprepared for the complex demands of
8 teaching and without consistent, job-embedded support; and
9 Whereas, the result is predictable: higher turnover, disrupted student learning, and
10 increased long-term costs for local school administrative units already operating with limited
11 resources; and
12 Whereas, by investing in locally aligned instructional coaching, and ongoing
13 professional learning, we can ensure that every beginning teacher regardless of geography has
14 access to high-quality, practice-based support from day one; and
15 Whereas, North Carolina has already invested in the North Carolina New Teacher
16 Support Program to support beginning teachers, but the funding only allows for serving ten
17 percent (10%) of the beginning teacher population; and
18 Whereas, this model is strategic expansion and stabilization of a program that is
19 already operating well through previous investments by the General Assembly; and
20 Whereas, by embedding support systems into the fabric of local school administrative
21 units and connecting them to educator preparation programs, we will move forward from
22 fragmented efforts to a coherent, educator development continuum; and
23 Whereas, North Carolina has already invested in Advanced Teaching Roles, but the
24 funding does not take into account the need to support and train those serving in ATR positions;
25 and
26 Whereas, North Carolina has already invested in expanding the North Carolina
27 Teaching Fellows Program, but has not increased funding for the embedded support provided by
28 the North Carolina New Teacher Support Program to serve the increased number of residency
29 teaching fellows; Now, therefore,
30 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
31 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Board of Governors
32 of The University of North Carolina the one million dollars ($1,000,000) in recurring funds
33 beginning in the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be allocated for the North Carolina New Teacher
34 Support Program (Program) to establish a sustainable, statewide approach to embedding
35 structured new teacher support directly within local school administrative units.
*H1209-v-1*

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 2. The funds appropriated in Section 1 of this act shall be used in
2 programs designed to address the specific needs of teachers who will begin their teaching careers
3 in North Carolina public schools, including at least the following:
4 (1) Partnering with educator preparation programs to extend support beyond
5 graduation.
6 (2) Aligning coaching with evidence-based practices introduced during educator
7 preparation.
8 (3) Creating feedback loops between local school administrative units and
9 educator preparation programs to continuously strengthen training.
10 (4) Supporting a seamless transition from pre-service clinical experiences to
11 in-service professional practice.
12 (5) In-district coaching within a local school administrative unit that is aligned to
13 daily instruction.
14 (6) Hub-based staffing structures to maximize efficiency across low-wealth
15 counties.
16 (7) Consistent, research-based support frameworks that improve instructional
17 practice and student outcomes.
18 (8) Targeted investment in high-need rural districts, where teacher turnover is
19 highest.
20 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2026.
