Section 1:
FILED SENATE
Mar 25, 2025
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
S.B. 732
SESSION 2025
PRINCIPAL CLERK
S D
Short Title: NC Economic Progress and Well Being. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Robinson, Meyer, and Salvador (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MEASURE THE ECONOMIC PROGRESS AND WELL-BEING OF THE
3 STATE.
4 Whereas, North Carolinians have prioritized their economic well-being and its
5 advancement as essential work of our State lawmakers; and
6 Whereas, public policy choices can create the conditions for economic security and
7 progress; and
8 Whereas, the State's overall economic progress depends on the well-being of its
9 people and communities; and
10 Whereas, policymakers should monitor the impact of public policy choices on the
11 well-being of people across the State and economic progress by the assessments of people and
12 communities rather than markets and productivity alone; and
13 Whereas, the NC General Assembly should strive to conduct its work to advance for
14 every person in North Carolina, and
15 Whereas, it is essential to have a well-rounded opportunity to have economically safe,
16 stable and secure lives, including (i) access to jobs that pay enough to ensure a standard of living
17 where a person can get ahead and a high level of life satisfaction, (ii) affordable prices for the
18 basics like food, housing, health care and child care, (iii) access to the education and training that
19 supports mobility, opportunity and participation in civic life, (iv) opportunity to start and expand
20 business free from unfair competition and domination by monopolies and other unfair advantage
21 for certain kinds of businesses, (v) a community of opportunity free from concentrated
22 disadvantage, toxic exposures and connected to supports to learn, earn and live a healthy life, and
23 (vi) adequate protection from the economic fears of old age, sickness, climate disaster, and
24 unemployment; Now, therefore,
25 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
26 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
27 Commerce the recurring sum of $200,000 for each fiscal year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium
28 to conduct bi-annual analyses of Economic Progress in North Carolina. The Department shall
29 report the results of the analyses to the General Assembly no later than January 31 of every
30 odd-numbered year, and each report shall meet all of the following requirements:
31 (1) Utilize publicly available data and interview people of various
32 socio-economic backgrounds.
33 (2) Measure, for the State and each county, at least the following metrics:
34 a. Hardship, as measured by the poverty rate, child poverty, deep
35 poverty, inequality, and the supplemental poverty rate.
*DRS15304-MCa-118A*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 b. Share of jobs that (i) pay below a living income wage and (ii) provide
2 basic needs and quality conditions and benefits.
3 c. Share of a household's monthly income that goes to food, housing,
4 health care, and childcare.
5 d. Percent of people who are considered cost burdened based on the
6 amount of income spent on housing, childcare, and health care
7 premiums.
8 e. Cost of post-secondary education and job training relative to income.
9 f. Persistent poverty census tracts and concentrated poverty census
10 tracts, including the number of people living in persistent and
11 concentrated poverty neighborhoods.
12 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
