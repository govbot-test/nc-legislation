17 "§ 105-113.130. Tax on consumer fireworks.
18 (a) Tax on Consumer Fireworks. – An excise tax is levied on consumer fireworks at the
19 point of purchase at a rate of five percent (5%) of the purchase price. A person who sells
20 consumer fireworks at retail is subject to and liable for the tax imposed by this section.
21 (b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this Article:
22 (1) Consumer fireworks. – Defined in G.S. 58-82A-80.
23 (2) Cost price. – The price a person liable for the tax on consumer fireworks
24 imposed by this Article paid for the products, before any discount, rebate, or
25 allowance.
26 (3) Fireworks retailer. – A person holding any permit under Part 2 of Article 82A
27 of Chapter 58 of the General Statutes.
28 (c) Monthly Report. – The taxes levied by this Article are payable when a report is
29 required to be filed. A report is due on a monthly basis. A monthly report covers sales and other
30 activities occurring in a calendar month and is due within 20 days after the end of the month
31 covered by the report. A report shall be filed on a form provided by the Secretary and shall
32 contain the information required by the Secretary.
33 (d) Discount. – A person who is liable for the excise taxes imposed by this Article, who
34 files a timely report under this section, and who sends a timely payment may deduct from the
35 amount due with the report a discount of two percent (2%). This discount covers expenses
36 incurred in preparing the records and reports required by this Article.
37 (e) Refund. – A person liable for the excise taxes imposed by this Article and in
38 possession of unsalable consumer fireworks upon which the tax has been paid may return the
39 consumer fireworks to the manufacturer and apply to the Secretary for refund of the tax. The
40 application shall be in the form prescribed by the Secretary and shall be accompanied by a written
41 certificate signed under penalty of perjury or an affidavit from the manufacturer listing the
42 consumer fireworks returned to the manufacturer by the applicant. The Secretary shall refund the
43 tax paid, less the discount allowed, on the listed products.
44 (f) Records. – Every person liable for the excise taxes imposed by this Article shall keep
45 accurate records of the purchases, inventories, and sales of consumer fireworks. These records
46 shall be open at all times for inspection by the Secretary or an authorized representative of the
47 Secretary.
48 (g) Use. – The Secretary must credit the net proceeds of the tax collected under this
49 Article as follows:
50 (1) An amount equal to twenty-five percent (25%) to the Firefighters' Education
51 Fund established under G.S. 58-85B-1.
