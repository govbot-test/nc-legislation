GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: WC/Self-Insurance Association Amendments. (Public)
Sponsors: Senator Johnson (Primary Sponsor).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 18, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT AMENDING LAWS CONCERNING THE NORTH CAROLINA
3 SELF-INSURANCE ASSOCIATION.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 97-130 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 97-130. Definitions.
7 As used The following definitions apply in this Article:
8 (1) "Association" means the Association. – The North Carolina Self-Insurance
9 Security Association established by G.S. 97-131.
10 (1a) "Association Aggregate Security System" means the Association Aggregate
11 Security System. – The security system established by the Association under
12 G.S. 97-133 whereby individual self-insurers collectively secure their
13 aggregate self-insured workers' compensation liabilities incurred while a
14 member through the North Carolina Self-Insurance Security Association.
15 (2) "Board" means the Board. – The Board of Directors of the Association
16 established by G.S. 97-132.
17 (2a) Collateral. – Cash, certificates of deposits, surety bonds, or irrevocable letters
18 of credit provided or pledged to the Association under G.S. 97-133(a)(2a)g.
19 (3) "Commissioner" means the Commissioner. – The North Carolina
20 Commissioner of Insurance.
21 (4) "Covered claim" means an Covered claim. – An unpaid claim against an
22 insolvent individual self-insurer or group self-insurer that which relates to an
23 injury that occurs while the individual self-insurer or group self-insurer is a
24 member of the Association and that is compensable under this Chapter.
25 Notwithstanding the foregoing, no workers' compensation claim filed against
26 the Association more than five years after the applicable claims bar date
27 established by a court of competent jurisdiction in the insolvency proceeding
28 of a former member self-insurer shall be considered a covered claim against
29 the Association.
30 (5) "Fund" means the Fund. – The North Carolina Self-Insurance Security Fund
31 established by G.S. 97-133.
32 (5a) "Group" or "Group self-insurer" means a Group or group self-insurer. – A
33 group self-insurer licensed by the Commissioner under Part 1, Article 47 of
34 Chapter 58 of the General Statutes.
35 (5b) "Individual self-insurer" means an Individual self-insurer. – An individual
36 employer licensed by the Commissioner under Article 5 of this Chapter.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (6) "Member self-insurer" or "member" means an Member self-insurer or
2 member. – An individual self-insurer or group self-insurer that is required to
3 be a member of the Association under this Article or Part 1, Article 47 of
4 Chapter 58 of the General Statutes.
5 (7) "Plan" means the Plan. – The Plan of Operation authorized by G.S. 97-134.
6 (8) Repealed by Session Laws 2005-400, s. 1.2, effective January 1, 2006.
7 (9) "Servicing facility" means those Servicing facility. – Those persons delegated
8 by the Board to settle or compromise claims and to expend Fund assets to pay
9 claims."
10 SECTION 2. G.S. 97-133(a)(2a) reads as rewritten:
11 "(2a) Establish and operate the Association Aggregate Security System as defined
12 in G.S. 97-130 and G.S. 97-165 as follows:
13 a. The Association shall annually operate and provide an Association
14 Aggregate Security System through a combination of cash on deposit
15 in the Fund, securities, surety bonds, irrevocable letters of credit,
16 insurance, reinsurance, or other financial instruments or guarantees
17 owned or entered into by the Association. The Association shall assess
18 the individual self-insurers that participate in the Association
19 Aggregate Security System pursuant to subdivision (3a) of this
20 subsection.
21 b. through d. Repealed by Session Laws 2011-196, s. 10, effective July
22 1, 2011.
23 e. If the Association determines it is not feasible or practical to operate
24 the Association Aggregate Security System in any given year, it may
25 terminate or suspend the Association Aggregate Security System and
26 shall notify the Commissioner at least 90 days prior to the termination
27 or suspension of the Association Aggregate Security System for that
28 particular year. During any period that the Associate Aggregate
29 Security System is terminated or suspended, every self-insurer shall
30 deposit with the Commissioner, or continue to deposit, the amount
31 required by G.S. 97-185(b3) in the manner prescribed by
32 G.S. 97-185(c).
33 f. Group self-insurers shall not participate in the Association Aggregate
34 Security System.
35 g. As a precondition to continued participation in the Association
36 Aggregate Security System, the Association may require participating
37 self-insurers as provided in G.S. 97-185(a1) to provide collateral as
38 defined in G.S. 97-165(3a) and G.S. 97-130(2a) with the Association
39 based upon the financial condition of the participating self-insurer."
40 SECTION 3. Article 4 of Chapter 97 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
41 a new section to read:
42 "§ 97-144. Statute of repose.
43 Notwithstanding any other provision of this Article, a workers' compensation claim must be
44 filed against the Association within five years after the claims bar date established by a court of
45 competent jurisdiction in the insolvency proceeding of a former member self-insurer. Any claim
46 not filed within the five-year period shall be forever barred as a claim against the Association."
47 SECTION 4. G.S. 97-165 reads as rewritten:
48 "§ 97-165. Definitions.
49 As used The following definitions apply in this Article:
50 (1) "Act" means the Act. – The Workers' Compensation Act established in Article
51 1 of this Chapter.

3 one or more intermediaries or directly controls, is controlled by, or is under

5 (1b) Association. – The North Carolina Self-Insurance Security Association
6 established by G.S. 97-131.

9 whereby individual self-insurers collectively secure their aggregate

13 public accountant or a foreign registered public accounting firm expresses his
14 or her professional opinion that the accompanying statements fairly present
15 the financial position of the self-insurer or the guarantor, in conformity with
16 accounting principles generally accepted in the United States or prepared in
17 accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards.
18 (3) "Certified public accountant" or "CPA" means a Certified Public Accountant
19 or CPA. – A CPA who is in good standing with the American Institute of
20 Certified Public Accountants and in all states in which the CPA is licensed to
21 practice. A CPA shall be recognized as independent as long as the CPA
22 conforms to the standards of the profession, as contained in the Code of
23 Professional Ethics of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants
24 and Rules and Regulations and Code of Ethics and Rules of Professional
25 Conduct of the North Carolina State Board of Certified Public Accountant
26 Examiners, or similar code. The Commissioner may hold a hearing to
27 determine whether a CPA is independent and, considering the evidence
28 presented, may rule that the CPA is not independent for purposes of
29 expressing an opinion on financial statements prepared in accordance with
30 United States Generally Accepted Accounting Principles or International
31 Financial Reporting Standards. The Commission may require the self-insurer
32 or the guarantor to replace the CPA with another whose relationship with the
33 self-insurer or the guarantor is independent within the meaning of this
34 definition.
35 (3a) Collateral. – Cash, certificates of deposits, surety bonds, or irrevocable letters
36 of credit provided or pledged to the Association under G.S. 97-133(a)(2a)g.
37 (4) "Commissioner" means the Commissioner. – The Commissioner of Insurance.
38 (4a) "Control", "controlling", "controlled by", and "under common control with"
39 mean the Control, controlling, controlled by, and under common control with.
40 – The direct or indirect possession of the power to direct or cause the direction
41 of the management and policies of a person through ownership of or through
42 proxies for voting of greater than fifty percent (50%) of the voting securities,
43 or in the case of a not-for-profit entity, the power to direct or cause the
44 direction of the management and policies of the entity.
45 (5) "Corporate surety" means an Corporate surety. – An insurance company
46 authorized by the Commissioner to write surety business in this State.
47 (5a) "Financial statement" means a Financial statement. – A financial statement as
48 defined by accounting principles generally accepted in the United States or a
49 financial statement prepared in accordance with International Financial
50 Reporting Standards.

1 (6) "Foreign registered public accounting firm" means a Foreign registered public
2 accounting firm. – A public accounting firm that is organized and operates
3 under the laws of a non-United States jurisdiction, government, or political
4 subdivision and is registered and in good standing with the Public Company
5 Accounting Oversight Board and authorized by the Board to prepare or issue
6 any audit report with respect to any issuer.
7 (6a) "Guarantor" means a Guarantor. – A person within the same holding company
8 system who controls the applicant, whose financial statement is used by the
9 applicant to become a self-insurer under the Act, and who has guaranteed the
10 payment of the self-insurer's liability under the Act.
11 (7) "Hazardous financial condition" means that, Hazardous financial condition. –
12 That, based on its present or reasonably anticipated financial condition, a
13 self-insurer or guarantor is insolvent or, although not yet financially impaired
14 or insolvent, is unlikely to be able to meet its obligations with respect to
15 known claims and reasonably anticipated claims or to pay other obligations in
16 the normal course of business.
17 (7a) "Holding company system" means an Holding company. – An entity
18 comprising two or more affiliated persons.
19 (7b) Member self-insurer or member. – An individual self-insurer or group
20 self-insurer that is required to be a member of the Association under
21 G.S. 97-130(6), Article 4 of Chapter 97 of the General Statutes, or Part 1 of
22 Article 47 of Chapter 58 of the General Statutes.
23 (8) "Management" means those Management. – Those persons who are
24 authorized to direct or control the operations of a self-insurer.
25 (8a) "Person" means an Person. – An individual, corporation, partnership, limited
26 liability company, association, joint stock company, trust, unincorporated
27 organization, or any similar entity or any combination of the foregoing acting
28 in concert.
29 (9) "Qualified actuary" means a Qualified actuary. – A member in good standing
30 of the Casualty Actuarial Society or a member in good standing of the
31 American Academy of Actuaries, who has been approved as qualified for
32 signing casualty loss reserve opinions by the Casualty Practice Council of the
33 American Academy of Actuaries, and is in compliance with G.S. 58-2-171.
34 (10) "Self-insurer" means an Self-insurer. – An individual self-insurer as defined
35 by G.S. 97-130(5b).
36 (11) "Subsidiary of" a specific person means an Subsidiary of a specific person. –
37 An affiliate controlled by such person indirectly through one or more
38 intermediaries or an affiliate directly controlled by such person."

40 "(a1) All individual self-insurers as defined in G.S. 97-130(5b) shall participate in the
41 Association Aggregate Security System established under G.S. 97-131 unless excluded by the

43 Directors of the North Carolina Self-Insurance Security Association shall exclude all of the
44 following from the Association Aggregate Security System:
45 (1) Individual self-insurers whose licenses have previously been revoked by the

49 & Poor's or Moody's ratings if a minimum debt rating has been established by
50 the Board of Directors of the North Carolina Self-Insurance Security
51 Association for the Association Aggregate Security System.
