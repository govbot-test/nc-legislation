4 SECTION 1. Article 10 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes is amended by
5 adding a new Part to read:
6 "Part 23. State Infrastructure Bank Board.
7 "§ 143B-472.130. Establishment of State Infrastructure Bank Board.
8 (a) Establishment. – There is established a State Infrastructure Bank Board. The Board
9 shall be located in the Department of Commerce for administrative purposes only and shall
10 function independently of the Department. The Board shall establish, administer, and receive
11 federal funds for a State infrastructure bank authorized by the Intermodal Surface Transportation
12 Efficiency Act of 1991, Public Law 102-240, as amended, the National Highway System
13 Designation Act of 1995, Public Law 104-59, as amended, and any other federal law authorizing
14 and governing the use of federal funds for a transportation infrastructure banking program.
15 Through the State infrastructure bank, the Board may also administer and receive State funds and
16 private funds for transportation projects, water and sewer projects, and other infrastructure
17 projects.
18 (b) Federal Financial Assistance. – The Board may apply for, receive, and administer
19 federal financial assistance necessary to fund the State infrastructure bank. The Board shall
20 comply with all conditions and requirements related to the federal financial assistance.
21 (c) Use of Funds. – Except as otherwise prohibited under federal law, the Board may
22 utilize the following funds for the purpose of providing loans or other financial assistance to
23 governmental units, including toll authorities:
24 (1) Federal funds, State funds, and private funds to finance the costs of
25 transportation projects, including aviation projects.
26 (2) State funds and private funds to finance the costs of water and sewer projects
27 and other infrastructure projects.
28 (d) Conditions and Repayment. – Loans or other financial assistance provided under this
29 section are subject to repayment and conditioned upon the establishment of any security and the
30 payment of any fees and interest rates deemed necessary by the Board. Governmental units may
31 apply for loans and execute debt instruments payable to the State in order to obtain loans or other
32 financial assistance provided for in this section. The Board shall require that applicants pledge
33 as security for the obligations revenues derived from operation of the benefited facilities or
34 systems, other sources of revenue, or their faith and credit, or any combination thereof. The faith
35 and credit of the governmental units shall not be pledged or be deemed to have been pledged
36 unless the requirements of Article 4 of Chapter 159 of the General Statutes have been met. The

27 "§ 143B-472.131. Membership of State Infrastructure Bank Board; meetings.
28 (a) Members. – The State Infrastructure Bank Board shall consist of the following voting
29 members:
30 (1) The Secretary of Commerce.
31 (2) The Secretary of Transportation.
32 (3) The Secretary of Environmental Quality.
33 (4) The State Treasurer.
34 (5) The State Auditor.
35 (6) The State Budget Officer.
36 (b) Terms. – The Board members shall serve terms coinciding with their respective
37 offices.
38 (c) Officers. – The Board shall elect from its members a chair and vice-chair of the Board.
39 (d) Meetings. – The Board shall meet on the call of the chair or any two members but
40 shall meet at least once every six months. Each member may be represented by a designee, who
41 shall have the same voting powers as the member. The Board shall meet in offices provided by
42 the Department of Commerce. In addition, the Department of Commerce shall provide the
43 necessary staff and supplies to help the Board accomplish its objectives. The Department of
44 Transportation, Department of Environmental Quality, and the Department of the State Treasurer
45 may also provide staff assistance.
46 (e) Rules. – The Board shall adopt rules, consistent with the requirements of this Part, for
47 reviewing and approving loans or other financial assistance provided under this Part. The Board
48 shall publish the rules on the Board's website or, if the Board does not have its own website, on
49 the Department of Commerce's website.
50 (f) Quorum. – A quorum of the Board consists of a majority of the Board's total
51 membership.
