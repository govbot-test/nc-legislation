1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REGULATE LARGE-SCALE DATA CENTERS IN ORDER TO PROTECT
3 RATEPAYERS, WATER RESOURCES, AND GRID RELIABILITY; TO REQUIRE
4 ON-SITE CLEAN GENERATION, COST-BASED RATES, INFRASTRUCTURE COST
5 RESPONSIBILITY, DISCLOSURES, AND REPORTING FOR CERTAIN DATA
6 CENTERS; TO LIMIT STATE AND LOCAL INCENTIVES FOR DATA CENTERS; AND
7 TO MAKE RELATED CHANGES TO PUBLIC UTILITIES LAW.
8 Whereas, North Carolina's electric and water utility systems are essential public
9 services relied upon daily by households, small businesses, and local governments across the
10 State; and
11 Whereas, the rapid growth of large-scale data centers imposes unusually large and
12 concentrated demands on electric generation, transmission, distribution, and water infrastructure;
13 and
14 Whereas, when the extraordinary infrastructure and resource demands created by such
15 facilities are not fully borne by the facilities themselves, those costs are shifted onto residential
16 customers, small businesses, and local governments through higher utility rates and increased
17 public expenditures, exacerbating cost-of-living pressures statewide; and
18 Whereas, existing economic development incentive programs were not designed to
19 subsidize facilities whose operations require outsized public investments in energy and water
20 infrastructure without proportionate public benefit; and
21 Whereas, the General Assembly is responsible for protecting North Carolinians from
22 rising utility bills and ensuring affordability by preventing cost-shifting, protecting ratepayers,
23 and ensuring that data centers bear the full cost of the energy, water, and infrastructure required
24 to support their operations; Now, therefore,
25 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

32 "§ 62-129. Definitions and application of terms.
33 For purposes of this Article, the following definitions apply:
34 (1) Data center. – A facility or group of co-located facilities under common
35 ownership or control used to house computer systems, servers, network
36 equipment, and associated infrastructure for the storage, processing, or

1 transmission of data. Facilities under common ownership or control, or that
2 are operationally or contractually integrated, whether located on one or
3 multiple parcels, shall constitute one "data center" for purposes of determining
4 the applicability of any threshold, requirement, limitation, or fee pursuant to
5 this Article.
6 (2) Large data center. – A data center that (i) has a projected peak electricity
7 demand of 40 megawatts or greater or (ii) has a projected annual water
8 consumption in excess of one billion liters.
9 (3) On-site clean generation. – Electric generation that is both (i) directly
10 interconnected with a data center and (ii) produced by a clean energy facility
11 or clean energy resource as those terms are defined in G.S. 62-133.8.
12 "§ 62-129.1. Preconstruction disclosure; certificate to operate a large data center.
13 (a) Preconstruction Disclosure Statement. – Prior to beginning construction on any new
14 data center, the owner or operator of a proposed data center shall submit a preconstruction
15 disclosure statement, in a form prescribed by the Commission, to the Commission, the
16 Department of Environmental Quality, and each local government in whose jurisdiction the data
17 center would be sited. The preconstruction disclosure statement shall include all of the following
18 information, supported by clear and credible evidence:
19 (1) The projected peak electricity demand.
20 (2) The projected annual electricity consumption.
21 (3) The projected annual water consumption.
22 (4) A detailed description of any cooling technologies to be used in the operation
23 of the data center.
24 (5) A detailed description of any on-site clean generation to be used in the
25 operation of the data center.
26 (b) Certificate of Operation for Large Data Centers. – No person shall begin the
27 construction or operation of a large data center without having applied for and obtained a
28 certificate of operation from the Commission. The application for a certificate to operate a large
29 data center shall be made in a form prescribed by the Commission and accompanied by the fee
30 required pursuant to G.S. 62-300(a)(18). The Commission may, after notice and an opportunity
31 for interested parties to be heard, issue a certificate to operate a large data center upon finding
32 that the applicant is fit, capable, and financially able to operate the large data center in accordance
33 with this Article. As a condition for the issuance and continuation of a certificate to operate a
34 large data center, the applicant shall demonstrate to the satisfaction of the Commission all of the
35 following:
36 (1) The large data center will operate in compliance with the on-site clean
37 generation requirement set forth in G.S. 62-129.2.
38 (2) The large data center will operate in compliance with the water use standards
39 for data centers adopted by the Department of Environmental Quality under
40 G.S. 143-355.5A.
41 (3) The large data center will purchase electric service from an electric public
42 utility in accordance with G.S. 62-129.3 or G.S. 62-129.4, as applicable, at
43 rates that are commensurate to the electric public utility's full marginal cost of
44 providing service to that large data center, including any incremental costs
45 incurred by the electric public utility to construct or expand generation
46 facilities, transmission and distribution lines, substations, and any other
47 infrastructure investments necessary to serve that large data center's projected
48 load.
49 (4) The large data center will purchase water and sewer services at rates that are
50 commensurate to the full marginal cost of providing service to that large data
51 center by a unit of local government, as defined in G.S. 162A-201, or a water

1 or sewer utility, including any incremental costs incurred to construct or
2 expand water and wastewater systems.
3 (5) That other members of the using and consuming public will be held harmless
4 and protected from bearing any increased costs as a result of providing
5 electric, water, or sewer services to the large data center.
6 (6) That the applicant will operate the large data center in accordance with any
7 other requirement determined by the Commission to be necessary to protect
8 the public interest.
9 (c) Effective Date. – A certificate of operation shall be effective from the date issued by
10 the Commission unless otherwise specified and shall remain in effect until terminated either
11 under its own terms or until suspended or revoked by the Commission as provided in subsection
12 (d) of this section.
13 (d) Certificate Review; Suspension and Revocation. – Upon the request of an electric

15 the Commission may review the certificate to determine whether the owner or operator of the
16 large data center is conducting operations in compliance with this Article. After notice to the
17 large data center's owner or operator, the Commission may suspend the certificate and enter upon
18 a hearing to determine whether the certificate should be revoked. After the hearing, and for good
19 cause shown, the Commission may, in its discretion, reinstate a suspended certificate, continue a
20 suspension of a certificate, or revoke a certificate.
21 "§ 62-129.2. On-site clean generation requirement.
22 (a) No person shall begin operation of a large data center without installing sufficient
23 on-site clean generation capacity to offset at least twenty-five percent (25%) of the data center's
24 projected peak electricity demand. Such generation capacity shall be operational upon the date
25 that the large data center commences commercial operations. No off-site renewable energy
26 credits, virtual power purchase agreements, or similar instruments may be credited toward
27 satisfying the on-site clean generation requirement under this section.
28 (b) The Commission may increase the minimum percentage of on-site clean generation
29 capacity that a large data center must maintain relative to its projected peak demand upon finding
30 that additional on-site clean generation is necessary to maintain the reliability of the electric grid
31 and protect other customers from rate increases and is otherwise consistent with the public
32 interest.
33 (c) The Commission may grant a variance to a large data center from the on-site clean
34 generation requirement upon finding that (i) strict compliance with the on-site clean generation
35 requirement would be technologically infeasible for the proposed large data center and (ii) the
36 applicant will implement equivalent grid mitigation measures at the applicant's expense.
37 "§ 62-129.3. Electric rates for large data centers.
38 (a) Each electric public utility shall file for Commission approval rates for the provision
39 of electric service to large data centers. The Commission may approve a tiered rate schedule that
40 includes different prices, terms, or conditions for different classes of large data centers based on
41 load requirements or other factors related to the costs of serving large data center customers.
42 (b) The Commission shall not approve a rate schedule filed under this section unless the
43 Commission finds that the rates are sufficiently designed to ensure all of the following:
44 (1) That large data center customers are responsible for the entire cost of any
45 capital investments or incremental operational expenses necessary for the
46 electric public utility to serve large data center customers.
47 (2) That other retail customers will be protected from (i) incurring any rate
48 increases as a result of serving large data center customers or (ii) assuming
49 additional risk associated with having to bear any stranded costs as a result of
50 large data center customers ceasing operations or else consuming less
51 electricity than initially requested.

1 "§ 62-129.4. Special high-capacity rates for large data centers.
2 (a) The Commission shall require a large data center to receive electric service under a
3 special high-capacity rate upon determining that rates approved under G.S. 62-129.3 would not
4 be commensurate with the electric public utility's full marginal cost of providing service to that
5 large data center, including any incremental costs that would be incurred by the electric public
6 utility to construct or expand generation facilities, transmission and distribution lines,
7 substations, and any other infrastructure investments necessary to serve that large data center's
8 projected load.
9 (b) The Commission may establish by rule certain categories of large data centers that
10 would be required to receive electric service under a special high-capacity rate.
11 (c) Upon determination by the Commission that a special high-capacity rate is required,
12 an electric public utility shall negotiate in good faith with the owner or operator of a large data
13 center an agreement to provide electric service at such prices, terms, and conditions necessary to
14 compensate the electric public utility's full marginal cost of providing service to the large data
15 center over the term of the agreement, subject to approval by the Commission.
16 (d) The Commission shall approve the special high-capacity rate fixed by contract
17 between an electric public utility and a large data center upon finding that the rate satisfies the
18 conditions set forth under G.S. 62-129.1(b) and is otherwise consistent with the public interest.
19 "§ 62-129.5. Exclusion from economic development incentive programs.
20 (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, data centers shall be ineligible to receive
21 any infrastructure grants or other incentives funded directly or indirectly by utility ratepayers.
22 (b) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a local government shall not grant or
23 provide to a data center any local tax incentive, subsidy, or financial assistance, including
24 property tax abatements, deferrals, rebates, grants, or payments in lieu of taxes. Nothing in this
25 subsection prohibits local governments from providing generally available services or
26 infrastructure on the same terms offered to similarly situated commercial or industrial users.
27 (c) Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit local governments from
28 exercising zoning or land use authority.
29 "§ 62-129.6. Annual report.
30 (a) Each person who owns or operates a large data center shall file an annual report to
31 the Commission and the Department of Environmental Quality that provides the following
32 information regarding the large data center's operations during the prior year:
33 (1) The actual amount of electricity consumed.
34 (2) The actual amount of water used.
35 (3) The actual amount of electricity generated on-site.
36 (4) Data indicating the efficiency of the cooling system, as quantified using
37 metrics approved by the Department of Environmental Quality.
38 (5) Any additional information requested by the Commission for purposes of
39 ensuring compliance with this Article.
40 (b) Annual reports filed by large data centers are public records, as defined in G.S. 132-1,
41 and shall be made readily available by the Commission on its website.
42 "§ 62-129.7. Rules.
43 Pursuant to G.S. 62-31, the Commission may adopt rules to implement the provisions of this
44 Article. In addition, the Commission shall adopt rules for verifying the amount of generation
45 capacity maintained by a large data center in accordance with the on-site clean generation
46 requirement in G.S. 62-129.2."

6 WATER USE STANDARDS FOR DATA CENTERS
7 SECTION 2.(a) Article 38 of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended by
8 adding a new section to read:
9 "§ 143-355.5A. Water use standards for data centers.
10 (a) The Department shall adopt rules establishing water use standards for data centers
11 regulated under Article 6C of Chapter 62 of the General Statutes to ensure the protection of
12 surface water and groundwater resources. The water use standards adopted by the Department
13 shall require data centers to employ closed-loop or reclaimed water systems to the maximum
14 extent practicable.
15 (b) The water use standards adopted by the Department shall not permit a data center to
16 use an evaporative cooling system. For purposes of this subsection, an "evaporative cooling
17 system" means any cooling system that consumes water through evaporation as a primary method
18 of heat rejection."
19 SECTION 2.(b) The Department of Environmental Quality shall adopt temporary
20 rules to implement the water use standards for data centers and shall adopt permanent rules to
21 replace the temporary rules. Temporary rules adopted in accordance with this section shall remain
22 in effect until permanent rules that replace the temporary rules become effective.
