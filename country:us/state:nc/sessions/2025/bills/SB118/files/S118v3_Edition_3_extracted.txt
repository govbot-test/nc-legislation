14 "(a9) A student who is not a domiciliary of a local school administrative unit shall be

18 (1) A parent or legal guardian is (i) on active military duty and is transferred or

20 or reservation in the State.State or (ii) will be separating from active military
21 duty within a 12-month period.

24 military order transferring to a military installation or reservation located in

44 resident of another local school administrative unit.
45 b. Allow a child who is a high school junior or senior to enroll and begin
46 attending school in that unit of anticipated domicile through high
47 school graduation.

8 PART III. PROHIBIT DISCRIMINATORY ADMISSIONS POLICIES REGARDING
9 ACTIVE DUTY SERVICE MEMBERS AND VETERANS, REQUIRE MILITARY
10 ADMISSIONS DEFERMENT FOR CERTAIN PERSONS ADMITTED TO THE UNC
11 SYSTEM, AND PROVIDE IN-STATE TUITION TO CERTAIN HONORABLY
12 DISCHARGED VETERANS
13 SECTION 3.(a) Article 1 of Chapter 116 of the General Statutes is amended by
14 adding a new Part to read:
15 "Part 8A. Military Admissions and Deferment.
16 "§ 116-44.9A. Definitions.
17 For purposes of this Part, the following definitions shall apply:
18 (1) Applicant. – An individual who has applied for admission to a constituent
19 institution of The University of North Carolina.
20 (2) Armed Forces. – The United States Air Force, Army, Coast Guard, Marine
21 Corps, Navy, Space Force, and North Carolina National Guard.
22 (3) Constituent institution of The University of North Carolina. – A constituent
23 institution of higher education of The University of North Carolina, as defined
24 in G.S. 116-2(4), excluding the constituent high school, the North Carolina
25 School of Science and Mathematics.
26 (4) Reserve Armed Forces. – The reserve components of any of the Armed
27 Forces.
28 (5) Uniformed service. – Any of the following:
29 a. Armed Forces.
30 b. Reserve Armed Forces.
31 c. The Merchant Marine.
32 "§ 116-44.9B. Nondiscriminatory evaluations of applications.
33 A constituent institution of The University of North Carolina shall not deny admission to any
34 applicant solely on the basis of the applicant's indication that the applicant is serving in the
35 uniformed service or that the applicant intends to serve in the uniformed service.
36 "§ 116-44.9C. Deferment.
37 (a) Deferment Requests. – The Board of Governors of The University of North Carolina
38 shall adopt a policy requiring constituent institutions to provide for enrollment deferment for
39 members of the uniformed services and spouses of members of the uniformed services if the
40 deferment is requested at least 30 days prior to enrollment in a constituent institution.
41 (b) Deferment Periods. – Members and spouses of members of the reserve Armed Forces
42 shall be granted deferments of at least two years after entry into the reserve Armed Forces. All
43 other members and spouses of members of the uniformed services shall be granted deferments
44 of at least five years after entry into the uniformed services."
45 SECTION 3.(b) Article 14 of Chapter 116 of the General Statutes is amended by
46 adding a new section to read:
47 "§ 116-143.3B. Tuition of qualifying veterans.
48 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
49 (1) Armed Forces. – The United States Air Force, Army, Coast Guard, Marine
50 Corps, Navy, Space Force, the North Carolina National Guard, and any
51 reserve component of any of these entities.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (2) Qualifying veteran. – An individual who meets all of the following criteria:
2 a. Served active duty for not less than 90 days in the Armed Forces.
3 b. Received an Honorable Discharge from the Armed Forces.
4 c. Meets at least one of the following criteria:
5 1. Graduated from high school in North Carolina on or after
6 January 1, 2004.
7 2. Served active duty in the Armed Forces with a permanent
8 station in North Carolina for at least 90 continuous days.
9 3. Was awarded a Purple Heart.
10 (b) Any qualifying veteran admitted to an institution of higher education, as defined in
11 G.S. 116-143.1(a)(3), shall be charged the in-State tuition rate and applicable mandatory fees for
12 enrollment, without having to satisfy the 12-month residency requirement under G.S. 116-143.1.
13 (c) The individual applying for the benefit of this section has the burden of proving
14 entitlement to the benefit."
15 SECTION 3.(c) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning
16 with the 2025-2026 academic year.

18 PART IV. REGULATE COMPENSATION FOR SERVICES RELATED TO
19 VETERANS' BENEFITS MATTERS
20 SECTION 4.(a) Article 14 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes is amended by
21 adding a new section to read:
22 "§ 143B-1278. Compensation for services related to veterans' benefits matters.
23 (a) Definitions. – Unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply
24 in this section:
25 (1) Compensation. – The payment of money, anything of value, or a financial
26 benefit.
27 (2) Veterans' benefits matter. – The preparation, presentation, or prosecution of a
28 claim affecting an individual who has filed or expressed an intent to file a
29 claim for a benefit, program, service, commodity, pension, function, or status,
30 the entitlement to which is determined under the laws and regulations
31 administered by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs or the
32 Department of Military and Veterans Affairs pertaining to veterans, their
33 dependents, their survivors, and any other individual eligible for such benefits.
34 (b) Prohibitions. – A person shall not do any of the following:
35 (1) Receive compensation for preparation, presentation, or prosecution of, or
36 advising, consulting, or assisting an individual with an initial disability claim
37 related to a veterans' benefits matter, except as permitted by this section.
38 (2) Receive compensation for referring an individual to another person to prepare,
39 present, prosecute, or advise, consult, or assist the individual with a veterans'
40 benefits matter.
41 (3) Directly or by implication guarantee or advertise that any individual is certain
42 to receive specific veterans' benefits or that any individual is certain to receive
43 a specific level, percentage, or amount of veterans' benefits.
44 (4) A person seeking to receive compensation for advising, assisting, or
45 consulting any individual with any veterans' benefits matter or engaging in the
46 preparation of an initial claim for a fee shall not:
47 a. Aggressively or directly solicit business regarding any veterans'
48 benefit matter.
49 b. Gain direct access to any personal medical, financial, or government
50 benefits login, username, or password information.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (5) A person seeking to receive compensation for advising, assisting, or
2 consulting any individual with any veterans' benefits matter shall not utilize a
3 medical professional for a secondary medical exam with whom they have an
4 employment relationship.
5 (6) Enter into any agreement related to a veterans' benefits matter that does not
6 comply with subsection (c) of this section.
7 (c) Memorialization of Terms. – Before rendering any services, a person seeking to
8 receive compensation for preparation, presentation, or prosecution of, or advising, consulting, or
9 assisting an individual with, a veterans' benefits matter shall memorialize all terms of the
10 agreement in a written agreement that complies with all of the following criteria:
11 (1) The written agreement is signed by both parties and contains all terms
12 regarding the individual's payment of fees for services rendered, including
13 both of the following:
14 a. Fees for services are contingent upon a successful outcome and shall
15 not exceed five times the one-month increase in benefits.
16 b. No initial fee shall be charged.
17 (2) The written agreement must clearly provide at the outset of the business
18 relationship the following disclosure: "This business is not sponsored by, or
19 affiliated with, the United States Department of Veterans Affairs or the
20 Department of Military and Veterans Affairs, or any other federally chartered
21 veterans' service organization. Other organizations, including, but not limited
22 to, the Department of Military and Veterans Affairs, a local veterans' service
23 organization, and other federally chartered veterans' service organizations
24 may be able to provide you with this service free of charge. Products or
25 services offered by this business are not necessarily endorsed by any of these
26 organizations. You may qualify for other veterans' benefits beyond the
27 benefits for which you are receiving services here." This written disclosure
28 shall appear in at least 12-point font and shall appear in a readily noticeable
29 and identifiable place in the person's agreement with the individual seeking
30 services. This disclosure shall also be provided orally to the individual and the
31 individual shall verbally acknowledge understanding of the oral disclosure
32 and sign the document in which the written disclosure appears to represent
33 understanding of these provisions. The person offering services shall retain a
34 copy of the written disclosure while providing veterans' benefits services for
35 compensation to the individual and for at least one year after the date on which
36 the service relations terminate.
37 (d) Penalty. – A violation of this section is an unfair trade practice under G.S. 75-1.1.
38 (e) Exclusions. – Nothing in this section shall apply to attorneys who are licensed to
39 practice in the State of North Carolina."
40 SECTION 4.(b) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025.

18 PART IV. REGULATE COMPENSATION FOR SERVICES RELATED TO
19 VETERANS' BENEFITS MATTERS
20 SECTION 4.(a) Article 14 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes is amended by
21 adding a new section to read:
22 "§ 143B-1278. Compensation for services related to veterans' benefits matters.
23 (a) Definitions. – Unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply
24 in this section:
25 (1) Compensation. – The payment of money, anything of value, or a financial
26 benefit.
27 (2) Veterans' benefits matter. – The preparation, presentation, or prosecution of a
28 claim affecting an individual who has filed or expressed an intent to file a
29 claim for a benefit, program, service, commodity, pension, function, or status,
30 the entitlement to which is determined under the laws and regulations
31 administered by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs or the
32 Department of Military and Veterans Affairs pertaining to veterans, their
33 dependents, their survivors, and any other individual eligible for such benefits.
34 (b) Prohibitions. – A person shall not do any of the following:
35 (1) Receive compensation for preparation, presentation, or prosecution of, or
36 advising, consulting, or assisting an individual with an initial disability claim
37 related to a veterans' benefits matter, except as permitted by this section.
38 (2) Receive compensation for referring an individual to another person to prepare,
39 present, prosecute, or advise, consult, or assist the individual with a veterans'
40 benefits matter.
41 (3) Directly or by implication guarantee or advertise that any individual is certain
42 to receive specific veterans' benefits or that any individual is certain to receive
43 a specific level, percentage, or amount of veterans' benefits.
44 (4) A person seeking to receive compensation for advising, assisting, or
45 consulting any individual with any veterans' benefits matter or engaging in the
46 preparation of an initial claim for a fee shall not:
47 a. Aggressively or directly solicit business regarding any veterans'
48 benefit matter.
49 b. Gain direct access to any personal medical, financial, or government
50 benefits login, username, or password information.
