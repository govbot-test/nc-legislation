7 NATURAL HERITAGE PROGRAM DATA CONFIDENTIALITY
8 SECTION 1.(a) The Nature Preserves Act, Part 42 of Article 2 of Chapter 143B of
9 the General Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to read:
10 "§ 143B-135.275. Confidentiality of rare species and sensitive natural habitat information.
11 Information concerning the nature and location of any (i) rare species of plants or animals or
12 (ii) sensitive natural habitats may be made available to the public under Chapter 132 of the
13 General Statutes or under any other provision of law, regardless of whether the rare species or
14 sensitive natural habitats are located on public or private property, unless the Department of
15 Natural and Cultural Resources determines that the disclosures would create a risk of harm, theft,
16 or destruction to those species or habitats."
17 SECTION 1.(b) This section is effective when it becomes law.

2 "§ 121-6. Historical publications.
3 …
4 (b) Editing and Publishing of Official Messages and Other Papers of Governor. – During
5 the term of office of each Governor of this State, a copy of all official messages delivered to the
6 General Assembly, addresses, speeches, statements, news releases, proclamations, executive
7 orders, weekly calendars, articles, transcripts of news conferences, lists of appointments, and
8 other official releases and papers of the Governor shall be kept in the Governor's office for
9 delivery to the Department of Natural and Cultural Resources at the end of each quarter during
10 the Governor's administration. These papers shall be compiled and a selection made therefrom
11 by a skilled and competent editor. The editor shall edit, according to acceptable scholarly
12 standards, the selected materials which shall be published in a documentary volume as soon as
13 practicable after the conclusion of the term of office of each Governor. If, for any reason, a
14 Governor serves less than a full term, a documentary volume shall be edited and published for
15 such portion of a term as he shall have served. If a Governor serves more than one term, a
16 documentary volume shall be edited and published for each term served. Funds for editorial
17 assistance, printing, binding, and distribution shall may be paid from the Contingency and
18 Emergency Fund. The number of copies of each volume to be printed shall be determined by the
19 Department of Natural and Cultural Resources in consultation with the Governor whose papers
20 are being published.
21 (c) It shall be the duty and the responsibility for the Department of Natural and Cultural
22 Resources to edit and publish a second or new series of the most significant records of colonial
23 North Carolina. From records which have been compiled in the North Carolina State Archives
24 concerning the colonial period of North Carolina, a selection of the most significant documents
25 shall be made therefrom by a skilled and competent editor. The editor shall edit, according to
26 acceptable scholarly standards, the selected materials which shall be published in documentary
27 volumes not to exceed approximately 700 pages each in length until full and representative
28 published colonial records of North Carolina shall have been achieved. The number of copies of
29 each volume to be so printed shall be determined by the Department of Natural and Cultural
30 Resources, and such determination shall be based on the number of copies the Department can
31 reasonably expect to sell in a period of 10 years from the date of publication. In any year during
32 which the Department of Natural and Cultural Resources has completed a volume and has it
33 ready for publication, the Department may include in its base budget for that year sufficient funds
34 to pay the estimated costs of publishing the volume. In the event that the volume is not published
35 during that year, the appropriation made, or any unencumbered balance, shall revert to the general
36 fund.General Fund."

27 EXPAND AND CODIFY FREE SCHOOL GROUP ADMISSION
28 SECTION 6.(a) Article 2 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes is amended by

30 "§ 143B-53.15. Free admission for school groups.

32 visiting sites owned or managed by the Department. For purposes of this section, "fees" refers to
33 any regular admission charge and does not include activity fees or a separate admission charge
34 for special or temporary exhibitions."
