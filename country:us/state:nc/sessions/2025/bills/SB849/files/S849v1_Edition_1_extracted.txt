4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) Chapter 75 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
6 Article to read:
7 "Article 9.
8 "Entertainment Event Ticket Sales and Resales."
9 SECTION 1.(b) The following provisions are to be located in Article 9 of Chapter
10 75 of the General Statutes, as established by subsection (a) of this section:
11 (1) Subsection (a) of G.S. 75-44 is recodified as G.S. 75-151, to be titled
12 "Definitions."
13 (2) Subsection (b) of G.S. 75-44 is recodified as G.S. 75-152, to be titled "Ticket
14 price transparency."
15 (3) Subsection (c) of G.S. 75-44 is recodified as G.S. 75-157, to be titled
16 "Enforcement."
17 SECTION 2. Article 9 of Chapter 75 of the General Statutes, as established by
18 Section 1 of this act, reads as rewritten:
19 "Article 9.
20 "Entertainment Event Ticket Sales and Resales.
21 "§ 75-151. Definitions.
22 As used in this section Article, the following definitions apply:
23 (1) Bot. – Any machine, device, computer program, or computer software used to
24 purchase tickets that, on its own or with human assistance, bypasses security
25 measures or access control systems on a retail ticket purchasing platform or
26 other controls or measures on a retail ticket purchasing platform that assist in
27 implementing a limit on the number of tickets that can be purchased for an
28 entertainment event.
29 (2) Clearly and conspicuously disclose. – To present information in a manner that
30 is difficult to miss and easily understandable, including by meeting the
31 following standards:
32 a. In the case of a visual disclosure, its size, contrast, location, the length
33 of time it appears, and other characteristics stand out from and are
34 equal to or greater in size than any accompanying text or other visual
35 elements so that it is easily noticed, read, and understood.
36 b. It is unavoidable.

6 (3) Entertainer. – An individual, group, or team that performs at an entertainment
7 event.
8 (1)(4) Entertainment event. – A sporting game or contest, concert, or other
9 entertainment performance with a live presentation element in this State for

11 (5) Fan club. – A membership-based program, established by an entertainer,
12 venue, or event organizer, or an agent or affiliate of such, that offers members
13 the opportunity to purchase tickets for an entertainment event prior to the
14 tickets being offered for sale to the general public.
15 (2)(6) Mandatory fee. – Any fee or surcharge that a consumer must pay in order to
16 purchase a ticket to an entertainment event.
17 (3)(7) Resale. – The second or subsequent sale of a ticket through a website or other
18 electronic means.by any method, including in-person transactions, telephone,
19 mail, email, facsimile, or electronic means through websites or mobile
20 applications.
21 (4)(8) Reseller. – A person engaged in the resale of tickets. This term includes agents
22 and affiliates of a reseller.
23 (5)(9) Secondary ticket exchange. – An electronic marketplace that enables persons
24 to sell, purchase, and resell tickets. This term includes agents and affiliates of
25 a secondary ticket exchange.
26 (10) Speculative ticket. – A ticket not in the actual or constructive possession of a
27 reseller at the time of listing, sale, or advertisement, including tickets not
28 owned by the reseller or under contract to be transferred to the reseller at the
29 time of sale.
30 (6)(11) Ticket issuer. – The person that is the first seller of tickets for an entertainment
31 event, including a musician or musical group, an operator of a venue, sponsor
32 or a promoter of an entertainment event, a sports team participating in an
33 entertainment event, a sports league whose teams are participating in an
34 entertainment event, a theater company, a marketplace or service operated for
35 consumers to make an initial purchase of tickets, or an agent of any of the
36 persons listed in this subdivision.
37 (7)(12) Ticketing link website. – A website that purports to sell entertainment event
38 tickets when no transactions occur on the website but instead links or redirects
39 to a reseller or secondary ticket exchange website.
40 (13) Ticketing session. – The period of time beginning when the price of a ticket
41 to an entertainment event is first displayed to a person through a website or
42 application and ending when the person has not purchased the ticket within
43 the time period prescribed by the secondary ticket exchange, ticket issuer, or
44 reseller.
45 "§ 75-152. Ticket price transparency.
46 A secondary ticket exchange, ticket issuer, or reseller shall meet the following requirements
47 when listing a ticket for sale or resale:
48 (1) At any time the price of the ticket is displayed to the purchaser, the listing
49 shall clearly and conspicuously disclose the total price of the ticket, including
50 all mandatory fees and the maximum order processing fee, if any.

1 (2) The total price of the ticket initially displayed at the beginning of a ticketing
2 session shall not be increased during that ticketing session, except by the
3 addition of the charges permitted under subdivision (4) of this
4 subsection.section.
5 (3) The listing shall clearly and conspicuously disclose to the consumer the
6 existence and actual dollar amount of each mandatory fee, if any, prior to the
7 completion of the transaction. The descriptor used to identify each mandatory
8 fee shall not be deceptive or misleading.
9 (4) The following charges are not mandatory fees and may be added to the ticket
10 price and shall be disclosed to the purchaser prior to purchase of the ticket:
11 a. Actual charges required to deliver a non-electronic ticket to the
12 address specified by the purchaser by the delivery method designated
13 by the purchaser.
14 b. Taxes or fees imposed on the transaction by any government.
15 c. A reasonable fee for processing the order.
16 (5) The listing shall clearly and conspicuously disclose to the consumer the
17 location of the seat corresponding to the ticket, including the row and section
18 number, or if there is no assigned seat, the general admission area.
19 (6) The issuer, reseller, or secondary ticket exchange shall clearly and
20 conspicuously disclose in the listing and at the point-of-sale whether this is an
21 initial sale or resale of a ticket, including the following:
22 a. If a ticket is being resold through a secondary ticketing exchange that
23 is not the ticket issuer, the secondary ticketing exchange shall clearly
24 and conspicuously disclose that it is not the ticket issuer and provide
25 an active link and the website address of where a consumer may
26 purchase a ticket from the ticket issuer.
27 b. If the secondary ticket exchange is also the ticket issuer, the secondary
28 ticket exchange shall clearly and conspicuously disclose on each page
29 or interface when a consumer is participating in the resale process.
30 "§ 75-153. Prohibition of speculative ticket sales.
31 In addition to any other restrictions imposed by a ticket issuer, a reseller or secondary ticket
32 exchange shall not (i) sell or offer to sell speculative tickets or (ii) resell more than one copy of
33 the same ticket to an entertainment event.
34 "§ 75-154. Access to presale and fan club tickets.
35 (a) A ticket issuer, reseller, or secondary ticket exchange shall not resell a ticket before
36 it has been made available to the public through the ticket's initial sale, unless it is authorized by
37 an entertainer, venue, or event organizer.
38 (b) A ticket reseller or secondary ticket exchange shall not purchase, sell, or resell a ticket
39 through a fan club ticket presale or fan club program unless authorized by the fan club.
40 "§ 75-155. Ticketing website use of branding and intellectual property.
41 In any advertisements, promotional materials, or online content, including websites, website
42 addresses, and social media, a reseller, secondary ticketing exchange, and operator of a ticketing
43 link website shall not do any of the following:
44 (1) Use the name, graphic, logo, image, trademark, trade dress, or any other
45 intellectual property of an entertainer, venue, or event organizer without prior
46 authorization.
47 (2) Resemble the branding of or otherwise visually imply a connection to an
48 entertainer, venue, or event organizer, even if there has not been a violation of
49 an intellectual property right, if the resemblance was intentionally designed to
50 create the false appearance of an agent, affiliate, or other relationship with an
51 entertainer, venue, or event organizer, when no such relationship exists.

1 (3) State or imply an affiliation with or endorsement by an entertainer, venue, or

3 relationship or endorsement exists.
4 "§ 75-156. Prohibition on circumvention of internet ticket sales limitations.
5 A person shall not use or create a bot to perform any of the following actions:
6 (1) Purchase tickets for any single internet ticket sale.
7 (2) Use multiple Internet Protocol (IP) addresses, purchaser accounts, or email
8 addresses to purchase tickets for any single internet ticket sale in excess of the
9 purchase limit imposed by the ticket issuer or eight tickets, whichever is less.
10 (3) Circumvent or disable an electronic queue, waiting period, presale code, or
11 other sales volume limitation system associated with an internet ticket sale.
12 (4) Circumvent or disable a security measure, access control system, or any other
13 control or measure used to facilitate authorized entry to an event.
14 "§ 75-157. Enforcement.
15 (a) A violation of this section Article is an unfair trade practice under G.S. 75-1.1 and is
16 subject to all of the investigative, enforcement, and penalty provisions of an unfair trade practice
17 under this Article.Chapter.
18 (b) In addition to any other relief or penalty allowed under this Chapter or other State
19 law, a court may issue a civil penalty of up to fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000) per day that a
20 violation of this Article has occurred and the greater of (i) one thousand dollars ($1,000) or (ii)
21 five times the total ticket price, per ticket listed, advertised, sold, or resold in violation of this
22 Article.
23 (c) If a court finds that a violation of this Article was willful, the court may impose an
24 additional civil penalty of ten thousand dollars ($10,000) per ticket sold or resold in violation of
25 this Article.
26 (d) The Attorney General shall ensure consumers are able to report violations of this
27 Article on the Department of Justice's website and by telephone.
28 (e) No later than August 1 of each year, the Attorney General shall report to the Joint
29 Legislative Oversight Committee on Justice and Public Safety on the number and types of
30 enforcement actions taken pursuant to this Article. A copy of the report shall also be made
31 publicly available on the Department of Justice's website."
32 SECTION 3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of

34 the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be allocated to the Office of the Attorney General to create one or
35 more full-time equivalent (FTE) positions to support enforcement of the entertainment event
36 ticket sale requirements of Article 9 of Chapter 75 of the General Statutes, as enacted by this act.
