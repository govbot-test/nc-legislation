2 SECTION 2.1.(a) Findings. – The General Assembly finds that it is in the best
3 interest of the State for the Department of Information Technology to lead the State's
4 cybersecurity efforts comprehensively rather than having State agencies handle cybersecurity
5 individually in a fragmentary way.
6 SECTION 2.1.(b) Funding. – For the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium, of the funds
7 available in the Information Technology Reserve, the Department of Information Technology
8 may use up to the sum of twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) each fiscal year to enhance
9 Department capabilities with respect to each of the following areas, and the funds are hereby
10 appropriated for that purpose:
11 (1) Continuation of cybersecurity projects funded in S.L. 2023-134.
12 (2) State agency adherence to plans and policies related to cybersecurity incident,
13 security alert, advisory response, security awareness, and agency
14 cybersecurity training protocols.
15 (3) Monitoring and ensuring State agency adherence to risk assessment policy for
16 identification and remediation of critical security vulnerabilities, including,
17 but not limited to, significant cybersecurity incidents.
18 (4) Review of State agency incident response plans to ensure security standards
19 are met with respect to cybersecurity incidents.
20 SECTION 2.2. For the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium, of the funds available in the
21 Information Technology Reserve, the sum of three million eight hundred thousand dollars
22 ($3,800,000) in each fiscal year may be used by the Department of Information Technology to
23 support the further development and integration of the NC HealthConnex system, and the funds
24 are hereby appropriated for that purpose. Funds shall be used to develop new technical
25 integrations and reconnections between health care provider electronic health record systems and
26 NC HealthConnex.

28 "§ 143B-1378. Assessment of agency compliance with cybersecurity standards.plans and

32 standards established pursuant to this section. The assessment shall include, at a minimum, the
33 rate of compliance with the enterprise-wide security standards and an assessment of security
34 organization, security practices, security information standards, network security architecture,

37 agencies to fully comply with the standards as well as the costs over the lifecycle of the State

39 CIO for purposes of this assessment. The State CIO shall include the information obtained from
40 the assessment in the State Information Technology Plan. The State CIO shall consider an
41 agency's noncompliance with cybersecurity plans and standards when reviewing agency requests
42 under Parts 3 and 4 of this Article."
