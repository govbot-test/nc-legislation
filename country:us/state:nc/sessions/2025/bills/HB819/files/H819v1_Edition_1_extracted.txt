7 PART II. CYBERSECURITY/DEPARTMENT ADMINISTRATION
8 SECTION 2.1.(a) Findings. – The General Assembly finds that it is in the best
9 interest of the State for the Department of Information Technology to lead the State's
10 cybersecurity efforts comprehensively rather than having State agencies handle cybersecurity
11 individually in a fragmentary way.
12 SECTION 2.1.(b) Funding. – For the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium, of the funds
13 available in the Information Technology Reserve, the Department of Information Technology
14 may use up to the sum of twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) each fiscal year to enhance
15 Department capabilities with respect to each of the following areas, and the funds are hereby
16 appropriated for that purpose:
17 (1) Continuation of cybersecurity projects funded in S.L. 2023-134.
18 (2) State agency adherence to plans and policies related to cybersecurity incident,
19 security alert, advisory response, security awareness, and agency
20 cybersecurity training protocols.
21 (3) Monitoring and ensuring State agency adherence to risk assessment policy for
22 identification and remediation of critical security vulnerabilities, including,
23 but not limited to, significant cybersecurity incidents.
24 (4) Review of State agency incident response plans to ensure security standards
25 are met with respect to cybersecurity incidents.
26 SECTION 2.2. For the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium, of the funds available in the
27 Information Technology Reserve, the sum of three million eight hundred thousand dollars
28 ($3,800,000) in each fiscal year may be used by the Department of Information Technology to
29 support the further development and integration of the NC HealthConnex system, and the funds
30 are hereby appropriated for that purpose. Funds shall be used to develop new technical
31 integrations and reconnections between health care provider electronic health record systems and
32 NC HealthConnex.
33 SECTION 2.3. G.S. 143B-1378 reads as rewritten:
34 "§ 143B-1378. Assessment of agency compliance with cybersecurity standards.plans and
35 standards; reporting requirements.
36 At a minimum, the State CIO shall annually assess the ability of each State agency, and each
37 agency's contracted vendors, to comply with the current cybersecurity enterprise-wide set of
38 standards established pursuant to this section. The assessment shall include, at a minimum, the
39 rate of compliance with the enterprise-wide security standards and an assessment of security
40 organization, security practices, security information standards, network security architecture,
41 and current expenditures of State funds for information technology security. The assessment of
42 a State agency shall also estimate the initial cost to implement the security measures needed for
43 agencies to fully comply with the standards as well as the costs over the lifecycle of the State
44 agency information system. Each State agency shall submit information required by the State
45 CIO for purposes of this assessment. The State CIO shall include the information obtained from
46 the assessment in the State Information Technology Plan. The State CIO shall consider an
47 agency's noncompliance with cybersecurity plans and standards when reviewing agency requests
48 under Parts 3 and 4 of this Article."
