GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025

Short Title: Language Access & State Holidays Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Batch, Chaudhuri, and Mohammed (Primary Sponsors).

March 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ENSURE NORTH CAROLINIANS CAN ACCESS ESSENTIAL STATE
3 SERVICES IN THEIR PRIMARY LANGUAGE AND TO FORMALLY RECOGNIZE
4 KEY CULTURAL AND RELIGIOUS HOLIDAYS THAT REFLECT THE
5 COMMUNITIES THAT CALL NORTH CAROLINA HOME.
6 Whereas, North Carolina is one of the fastest-growing states in the country, with many
7 residents speaking languages other than English at home; and
8 Whereas, making state services, education, and healthcare more accessible benefits
9 public safety, economic growth, and community engagement; and
10 Whereas, recognizing widely observed cultural and religious holidays ensures that
11 North Carolina respects the traditions of the people who live and work here; Now, therefore,
12 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
13

13 PART II. REQUIRE SCHOOL DOCUMENTS TO BE TRANSLATED INTO SEVERAL
14 LANGUAGES AND TO ENCOURAGE EXCUSED ABSENCES FOR CERTAIN
15 RELIGIOUS HOLIDAYS
16 SECTION 2.1.(a) Part 3 of Article 7B of Chapter 115C is amended by adding a new
17 section to read:
18 "§ 115C-76.41. Parent rights to receive information.
19 (a) The Department of Public Instruction and the governing body of all public school
20 units shall make available a copy of any document for parents translated into Mandarin Chinese,
21 Hindi, Telugu, Korean, Spanish, Arabic, French, Vietnamese, German, Cherokee, Tagalog,
22 Russian, Portuguese, Tamil, Hmong, Khmer, Burmese, Karen, Karenni, Chin, Bunong, Rhede,
23 Kho, Jari, and Minhar.
24 (b) As used in this section, a "document for parents" is any form, communication, notice,
25 or report that the school will provide to parents or that a parent has the ability to access, regardless
26 of whether the document is printed or electronic, including the following:
27 (1) Enrollment forms.
28 (2) Consent forms.
29 (3) Educational or informational documents required to be provided to parents
30 under G.S. 115C-12, 115C-47, 115C-150.12C, 115C-218.75, 115C-238.66,
31 or 116-239.8.
32 (4) School policies accessible to parents, including policies shared on the school's
33 website or learning management system.
34 (5) Disciplinary notices or forms.
35 (6) Notices, forms, and records related to a student's disability or disability
36 testing.
37 (7) Notices, forms, and reports required under this Article.
38 (c) The Department and the governing body of public school units are encouraged to
39 offer interpretation services for parent-teacher conferences and other critical meetings to ensure
40 parents can fully engage in their child's education."
41 SECTION 2.1.(b) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies
42 beginning with the 2025-2026 school year.
43 SECTION 2.2.(a) G.S. 115C-302.1(d)(2) reads as rewritten:
44 "(2) Use. – Personal leave may be used only upon the authorization of the teacher's
45 immediate supervisor, as follows:
46 a. Unless the request is approved by the principal, a teacher shall not take
47 personal leave on the first day the teacher is required to report for the
48 school year, on a required teacher workday, on days scheduled for
49 State testing, or on the day before or the day after a holiday or
50 scheduled vacation day.

2 subdivision, if the request is made at least five days in advance, the
3 request shall be automatically granted subject to the availability of a
4 substitute teacher, and the teacher cannot be required to provide a
5 reason for the request.
6 c. Supervisors and principals are encouraged to make all reasonable
7 efforts to accommodate a request for personal leave if the teacher is
8 requesting the leave for observance of a religious holiday."
9 SECTION 2.2.(b) G.S. 115C-316 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
10 "(e) Local boards of education are encouraged to adopt a policy that schools will make all
11 reasonable efforts to accommodate a school employee's request to use leave for the observance
12 of a religious holiday."
13 SECTION 2.2.(c) G.S. 115C-379(b) reads as rewritten:
14 "(b) In addition to any excused absences authorized pursuant to subsection (a) of this
15 section, the rules shall require school principals to authorize the following excused absences:
16 (1) Religious observance. – A minimum of two excused absences each academic
17 year for religious observances required by the faith of a student or the student's
18 parent or legal guardian. The principal is encouraged to allow excused
19 absences for religious holidays beyond those required to be given, as policies
20 allow.
21 …."
22 SECTION 2.3.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department
23 of Public Instruction the sum of one million dollars ($1,000,000) in recurring funds for the
24 2025-2026 school year to be allocated to public school units on an as-needed basis to cover the
25 cost of providing substitute teachers if a teacher is on personal leave to observe a religious holiday
26 pursuant to G.S. 115C-302.1(d)(2).
27 SECTION 2.3.(b) This section becomes effective July 1, 2025.

29 PART III. EXPANSION OF MULTILINGUAL HEALTH CARE SERVICES
30 SECTION 3.1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
31 Health and Human Services (Department) the sum of one hundred thousand ($100,000) in
32 nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to develop and make available, free of charge,
33 on the Department's web site the following forms and documents translated into English, Spanish,
34 Mandarin Chinese, Hindi, Telugu, Korean, Arabic, French, Vietnamese, German, Cherokee,
35 Tagalog, Russian, Portuguese, Tamil, Hmong, Khmer, Burmese, Karen, Karenni, Chin, Bunong,
36 Rhede, Kho, Jari, and Minhar:
37 (1) Informed consent forms and explanatory materials regarding informed
38 consent, developed in consultation with the appropriate occupational licensing
39 boards, that can be used by healthcare providers licensed in this State when
40 treating patients with limited English proficiency.
41 (2) Information regarding all of the following:
42 a. The Adult Care Home Residents' Bill of Rights set forth in
43 G.S. 131D-21.
44 b. The Nursing Home Patients' Bill of Rights set forth in G.S. 131E-117.
45 c. The Home Care Clients' Bill of Rights set forth in G.S. 131E-144.3.
46 (3) Any public health information determined by the State Health Director to be
47 essential to maintaining public health and safety, including at least all of the
48 following:
49 a. Information about health hazards, including communicable and
50 non-communicable diseases.
51 b. Information about public health emergencies.
Page 4 Senate Bill 606-First Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 c. Information about the immunization requirements of Part 2 of Article
2 6 of Chapter 130A of the General Statutes.
3 d. Information about the Newborn Screening Program authorized by
4 G.S. 130A-125.
5 SECTION 3.2. Every healthcare provider that is a recipient of State funds, as defined
6 in G.S. 143C-1-1, is encouraged to employ multilingual staff or provide interpreter services for
7 patients with limited English proficiency. As used in this section, the term "healthcare provider"
8 means any of the following:
9 (1) An individual who is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized under
10 Chapter 90 of the General Statutes to provide health care services in the
11 ordinary course of business or practice of a profession or in an approved
12 education or training program.
13 (2) A health care facility licensed under Chapters 131E or 122C of the General
14 Statutes, where health care services are provided to patients.
15 SECTION 3.3. This Part becomes effective July 1, 2025.

17 PART IV. EXPAND CULTURAL AND RELIGIOUS HOLIDAYS
18 SECTION 4.1. G.S. 103-4 reads as rewritten:
19 "§ 103-4. Dates of public holidays.
20 (a) The following are declared to be legal public holidays:
21 (1) New Year's Day, January 1.
22 (1a) Martin Luther King, Jr.'s, Birthday, the third Monday in January.
23 (1b) Lunar New Year.
24 (2) Robert E. Lee's Birthday, January 19.
25 (3) Washington's Birthday, the third Monday in February.
26 (3a) Greek Independence Day, March 25.
27 (4) Anniversary of signing of Halifax Resolves, April 12.
28 (5) Confederate Memorial Day, May 10.
29 (6) Anniversary of Mecklenburg Declaration of Independence, May 20.
30 (7) Memorial Day, the last Monday in May.
31 (8) Good Friday.
32 (8a) Eid al-Adha.
33 (9) Independence Day, July 4.
34 (10) Labor Day, the first Monday in September.
35 (10a) First Responders Day, September 11.
36 (11) Columbus Day, the second Monday in October.
37 (11a) Yom Kippur.
38 (12) Veterans Day, November 11.
39 (13) Tuesday after the first Monday in November in years in which a general
40 election is to be held.
41 (14) Thanksgiving Day, the fourth Thursday in November.
42 (15) Christmas Day, December 25.
43 (16) Diwali.
44 (17) Eid al-Fitr.
45 (18) Rosh Hashanah.
46 (b) Whenever any public holiday shall fall upon Sunday, the Monday following shall be
47 a public holiday."
48 SECTION 4.2 State agencies are encouraged to accommodate employees who
49 observe the cultural and religious set out in G.S. 103-4, as amended by Section 4.1 of this Part,
50 by allowing excused absences.
