6 PART I. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS
7 SECTION 1.1. The North Carolina General Assembly makes the following findings
8 for agriculture in 2024:
9 (1) Agriculture and agribusiness in North Carolina constituted an economic
10 impact of one hundred eleven billion one hundred million dollars
11 ($111,100,000,000), posting an eight billion dollar ($8,000,000,000) increase
12 from the previous year.
13 (2) North Carolina is one of the nation's top producers of a diversity of agriculture
14 commodities, including sweet potatoes, tobacco, eggs, cucumbers, Christmas
15 trees, trout, peanuts, broilers, and cotton.
16 (3) North Carolina suffered continual damage to agricultural crops and
17 commodities from natural disasters that impacted every portion of the State,
18 including drought, excessive rainfall, and tropical storms.
19 (4) Every county in the State was included in one or more secretarial disaster
20 declarations by the United States Department of Agriculture based on a
21 finding of significant production losses for agriculture.

5 PART III. 2024 AGRICULTURAL DISASTER CROP LOSS PROGRAM
6 SECTION 3.1.(a) Agricultural Disaster Crop Loss Program. – The 2024
7 Agricultural Disaster Crop Loss Program is established within the Department of Agriculture and
8 Consumer Services. The Program shall be used to provide financial assistance to farmers with
9 verified losses from an agricultural disaster in this State in 2024. The Department may use up to
10 one percent (1%) of funds allocated for the Program for administrative purposes. To be eligible
11 for financial assistance for losses of agricultural commodities, a person must satisfy all of the
12 following criteria:
13 (1) The person experienced a verifiable loss of agricultural commodities as a
14 result of an agricultural disaster in 2024, and the person's farm is located in an
15 affected area for the respective agricultural disaster.
16 (2) The agricultural commodity was planted but not harvested on or before
17 January 1, 2024, or, for aquaculture commodities, the commodities were being
18 raised on or before January 1, 2024.
19 SECTION 3.1.(b) Verification of Loss. – A person seeking financial assistance for
20 losses of agricultural commodities under the Program shall submit to the Department a Form 578
21 on file with the USDA Farm Service Agency or a form provided by the Department for reporting
22 acreage or plantings of crops or loss that is not typically reported on Form 578, along with any
23 other documentation deemed appropriate by the Department, no later than 30 days after this
24 section becomes effective. For nursery crops, fruit-bearing trees and bushes, and specialty crops
25 where the survival level is not immediately known, the Department may extend this deadline by
26 an additional 30 days, upon written request by the person received no later than 30 days after this
27 section becomes effective and upon approval by the Department. A person receiving assistance
28 under this Program must provide a signed affidavit, under penalty of perjury, certifying that each
29 fact of the loss presented by the person is accurate.
30 SECTION 3.1.(c) Criteria. – The Department shall administer the financial
31 assistance program authorized by this section in accordance with the following criteria:
32 (1) In determining the payment calculation for agricultural commodities, the
33 Department shall use a formula based on acreage, county loss estimates,
34 USDA National Agricultural Statistics Service averages, and any other
35 measure the Department deems appropriate. Funds shall be distributed based
36 on county averages for yields and State averages for price. Calculations shall
37 be based on county or State averages in price, whichever the Department
38 determines is appropriate.
39 (2) The Department shall gather all claim information, except from those
40 applicants granted a deadline extension, no later than 30 days after this section
41 becomes law. The Department shall, as closely as possible, estimate the
42 amount of the funds needed to be held in reserve for payments related to losses
43 of nursery, bush, tree, and specialty crops for which losses will not be fully
44 known or calculated. The Department shall set aside funds as it deems
45 appropriate based on the estimated percentage of these losses.
46 (3) Payments made under this Program shall be made to the person who filed the
47 Form 578 or Department form for claims related to agricultural commodity
48 losses.
49 SECTION 3.1.(d) Audit; Documentation. – The Department may audit the financial
50 and other records of each recipient of funds in order to ensure that the funds are used in
51 accordance with the requirements of this Program. The Department may require any

9 SECTION 3.1.(e) Expenditure of Awarded Funds. – Awarded funds shall be used
10 for agricultural production expenses and recovery of losses due to the impacts of the agricultural
11 disaster. The Department shall develop guidelines and procedures to ensure that funds are
12 expended for the purposes allowed by this section and may require any documentation it
13 determines necessary to verify the appropriate use of financial assistance awards, including
14 receipts. All distributed funds are subject to federal and State income tax.
15 SECTION 3.1.(f) Refund of Award. – If the Department determines that a person
16 who received financial assistance provided inaccurate information, then the person shall refund
17 the entire amount of the financial assistance. If the person does not refund the appropriate
18 amount, the North Carolina Department of Revenue shall collect the money from the person
19 pursuant to G.S. 105-242.
20 SECTION 3.1.(g) Definitions. – For purposes of this section, the following
21 definitions apply:
22 (1) Agricultural commodity. – Any crop, flowering plant, aquaculture species,
23 fruit, grain, native grass, ornamental plant, sod, tree, or vegetable. An
24 agricultural commodity does not include stored grain.
25 (2) Agricultural disaster. – A secretarial disaster designation declared by the
26 United States Department of Agriculture Secretary for qualifying counties in
27 this State.
28 (3) Department. – The Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services.
29 (4) Person. – Any individual, trust, estate, partnership, receiver, association,
30 company, limited liability company, corporation, or other entity or group.
31 (5) Program. – The 2024 Agricultural Disaster Crop Loss Program.
32 SECTION 3.1.(h) Program Reporting Requirement. – The Department shall submit
33 a report to the Fiscal Research Division no later than the first day of the month six months after
34 this section becomes effective, and every six months thereafter until all funds are expended or
35 the Program expires, containing, at a minimum, all of the following data:
36 (1) The number of applicants by agricultural commodity and the county in which
37 the person incurred the verified loss.
38 (2) The number and amount of grants awarded by agricultural commodity.
39 (3) The geographic distribution of the grants awarded.
40 (4) The total amount of funding available to the Program, the total amount
41 encumbered, and the total amount disbursed to date.
42 (5) Any refunds made to the Program pursuant to subsection (f) of this section.
43 SECTION 3.1.(i) Expiration and Reversion. – The Program shall expire 30 months
44 after this section becomes effective. Any funds allocated to the Program not expended or
45 encumbered by that date shall revert to the State Emergency Response and Disaster Relief Fund.
