5 PART I. RESTRICT WAKE SURFING IN LAKE GLENVILLE
6 SECTION 1.1.(a) Definition. – For purposes of this section, wake surfing means the
7 operation of a motorboat, as defined in G.S. 75A-2, to which weight has been added in the stern
8 via water-filled tanks or other ballasts for the purpose of creating an artificially enlarged wake
9 that is or is intended to be surfed by another person towed behind the motorboat.
10 SECTION 1.1.(b) Prohibition. – No person may engage in wake surfing within 200
11 feet of the shoreline or any structure, moored vessel, kayak, canoe, paddleboard, or swimmer.
12 SECTION 1.2. Violation. – A violation of this Part is a Class 1 misdemeanor
13 punishable by a fine of not less than one hundred dollars ($100.00) in addition to any other
14 punishment prescribed for that offense.
15 SECTION 1.3. Enforcement. – This Part is enforceable by law enforcement officers
16 of the Wildlife Resources Commission, by sheriffs and deputy sheriffs, and by other peace
17 officers with general subject matter jurisdiction.
18 SECTION 1.4. Applicability. – This Part applies only to the waters of Lake Glenville
19 in Jackson County.
20 SECTION 1.5. This Part becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to offenses
21 committed on or after that date.

23 PART II. TRANSYLVANIA RURAL DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY
24 SECTION 2.1. Establishment. – There is established a separate and independent
25 body corporate and politic to be known as the Transylvania Rural Development Authority (the
26 "Authority"). The Authority shall have all the powers and duties granted to a rural development
27 authority established under Chapter 988 of the Session Laws of 1965, as amended by Chapter
28 931 of the Session Laws of 1969 and S.L. 2023-143. The board of commissioners of Transylvania
29 County shall have no authority over the Authority.
30 SECTION 2.2. Membership; Organization; Removal. – (a) There shall be nine
31 members of the Authority who shall be appointed by the Transylvania Economic Alliance. Each
32 member shall be a resident of Transylvania County and shall serve a term of five years. In order
33 to stagger the members' terms, the appointing authority shall, in appointing the initial members,
34 appoint one member to serve a term of two years, one member to serve a term of three years, and
35 one member to serve a term of five years. A member shall hold office until the member's

1 successor is appointed and qualified. Vacancies for unexpired terms shall be promptly filled by
2 the appointing authority, and the person appointed shall serve for the remainder of the unexpired
3 term. A member shall receive no compensation for the member's services but shall, within the
4 limit of available funds, be entitled to reimbursement of necessary expenses, including travel
5 expenses, incurred in the discharge of duties.
6 (b) The members of the Authority shall select from among their membership a
7 person to serve as chair, vice-chair, and any other officers they deem necessary to conduct the
8 business of the Authority. The Authority shall adopt rules and regulations consistent with the
9 provisions of Chapter 988 of the Session Laws of 1965, as amended, as necessary for the proper
10 discharge of its duties. The chair may appoint committees that are authorized by the Authority's
11 rules and regulations. The Authority shall meet regularly at the times and places specified in its
12 rules and regulations, and special meetings may be called pursuant to those rules. All meetings
13 shall be open to the public. A majority of the members shall constitute a quorum for all purposes.
14 (c) A member of the Authority may only be removed by the appointing authority
15 for inefficiency or neglect of duty or misconduct in office. No member shall be removed unless
16 the member has been given a copy of the charges at least 10 days prior to the hearing and has
17 been given an opportunity to be heard in person or by counsel.
18 SECTION 2.3. Staff; Employees. – Within the limits of available funds, the
19 Authority shall appoint the Transylvania Economic Alliance to operate the Authority. The
20 Authority may delegate to one or more of its members, agents, or employees the powers and
21 duties which it deems necessary to carry out its powers and duties, subject always to the
22 supervision and control of the Authority.
23 SECTION 2.4. Interest of Members or Employees. – No member or employee of the
24 Authority shall (i) acquire any interest, direct or indirect, in any development project or any
25 property included, or planned to be included, in any development project, or in any area which
26 the member or employee may have reason to believe may be included in any development project
27 or (ii) have any interest, direct or indirect, in any contract or proposed contract for materials or
28 services to be furnished or used by the Authority or in connection with any development project.
29 The acquisition of any interest in a development project or in any property or contract as
30 described in this section shall constitute misconduct in office. If any member or employee of the
31 Authority shall have already owned or controlled within the preceding two years any interest,
32 direct or indirect, in any property later included or planned to be included in any development
33 project under the jurisdiction of the Authority, or has any interest in any contract for material or
34 services to be furnished or used in connection with any development project, the member or
35 employee shall disclose the interest in writing to the Authority, and the disclosure shall be entered
36 upon the minutes of the Authority. Failure to make a disclosure required by this section shall
37 constitute misconduct in office.
38 SECTION 2.5. Certificate of Incorporation. – The Authority shall file a copy of this
39 act with the Secretary of State and, upon receipt of the act, the Secretary of State shall issue a
40 certificate of incorporation. In any suit, action, or proceeding involving or relating to the validity
41 or enforcement of any contract or act of the Authority, a copy of the certificate of incorporation
42 duly certified by the Secretary of State shall be admissible in evidence and shall be conclusive
43 proof of the legal establishment of the Authority.
44 SECTION 2.6. Environmental Issues. – Notwithstanding any other provision of law,
45 neither Transylvania County or any other county, the State of North Carolina, or any other
46 governmental unit that provides funds to the Authority for a development project or other project
47 shall be liable for any environmental issues, known or unknown, related to the project solely
48 because of providing funds.
49 SECTION 2.7. This Part applies only to Transylvania County.
