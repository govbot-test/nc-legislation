Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 3
Committee Substitute Favorable 3/5/25
Committee Substitute #2 Favorable 4/16/25
Short Title: NC Digital Assets Investments Act. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
February 12, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO AUTHORIZE THE STATE TREASURER TO INVEST IN QUALIFYING
3 DIGITAL ASSETS, EXAMINE THE FEASIBILITY TO ALLOW MEMBERS OF THE
4 STATE RETIREMENT INCOME PLAN AND MEMBERS OF THE 457(B) DEFERRED
5 COMPENSATION PLAN TO INVEST IN DIGITAL ASSETS, AND STUDY A STATE
6 RESERVE TO HOLD SEIZED OR FORFEITED DIGITAL ASSETS.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1. Article 6 of Chapter 147 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
9 a new section to read:
10 "§ 147-69.2E. Investments in digital assets.
11 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
12 (1) Designated funds. – Any of the funds described in G.S. 147-69.1(b) and
13 G.S. 147-69.2(a).
14 (2) Digital asset. – A virtual currency, cryptocurrency, native electronic asset,
15 stablecoin, nonfungible token, or any other asset that is only digital and that
16 confers economic, proprietary, or access rights or powers.
17 (3) Secure custody solution. – A technological product or a blended product and
18 service that employs advanced security measures to safeguard private keys
19 and prevent unauthorized access.
20 (4) Private key. – A unique element of cryptographic data used for signing
21 transactions on a blockchain that is known to the owner of the unique element.
22 (b) The State Treasurer may invest the cash of the designated funds in digital assets only
23 after obtaining an independent assessment by a third-party consultant that all of the following
24 requirements for proposed investments have been met:
25 (1) The digital assets are maintained with a secure custody solution.
26 (2) The potential investment is appropriate for the designated fund's
27 circumstances from a total portfolio perspective.
28 (3) The control environment meets institutional investment industry requirements
29 for independent risk and compliance oversight, operational robustness, and
30 regulatory compliance.
31 (c) An investment in digital assets from any of the designated funds shall not exceed, in
32 the aggregate, five percent (5%) of the balance of the designated fund."
33 SECTION 2. The Treasurer shall examine the feasibility of allowing members of
34 the Supplemental Retirement Income Plan and members of the 457(b) Deferred Compensation
35 Plan to elect to invest in digital assets, as defined in G.S. 147-69.2E, which are held as
*H92-v-3*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 exchange-traded products. If the Treasurer determines such investment is appropriate and the
2 Supplemental Retirement Board of Trustees agrees after reviewing the Treasurer's findings, then
3 the Treasurer and Board may adopt rules to implement allowing State employees to invest,
4 including the following:
5 (1) Identifying appropriate investment vehicles.
6 (2) Determining whether a maximum contribution allowable for members of the
7 Supplemental Retirement Income Plan and members of the 457(b) Deferred
8 Compensation Plan should be established and the amount of that contribution.
9 (3) Establishing educational materials to inform State employees about digital
10 asset basics and digital asset investment risks.
11 (4) Any other rules the Treasurer deems necessary.
12 SECTION 3. The State Bureau of Investigation, in cooperation with the State
13 Treasurer, local law enforcement agencies, and other statewide law enforcement agencies, shall
14 study the feasibility of establishing the North Carolina Digital Asset Reserve (Reserve), with the
15 purpose of retaining digital assets seized and forfeited to the State. The study shall include at
16 least the following:
17 (1) The State agency or department best suited to administer the Reserve.
18 (2) The best method to hold assets in the Reserve.
19 (3) The process for placing seized or forfeited assets in the Reserve.
20 (4) The best method to time sales from the Reserve to maximize revenue to the
21 Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund and benefit local boards of education.
22 (5) Any other issues the stakeholders deem necessary.
23 The State Bureau of Investigation shall report the results of this study, including
24 proposed legislation to create, implement, and administer the Reserve, to the Joint Legislative
25 Oversight Committee on General Government no later than March 1, 2026.
26 SECTION 4. If House Bill 506, 2025 Regular Session, becomes law,
27 G.S. 147-69.2E, as enacted in that act, is repealed. If Senate Bill 709, 2025 Regular Session,
28 becomes law, then G.S. 147-69.2E, as enacted in that act, is repealed.
29 SECTION 5. If House Bill 506, 2025 Regular Session, or Senate Bill 709, 2025
30 Regular Session, becomes law, then G.S. 147-69.2E(b), as enacted in Section 1 of this act, reads
31 as rewritten:
32 "(b) The State Treasurer Investment Authority may invest the cash of the designated funds
33 in digital assets only after obtaining approval by the Board of Directors. The approval shall be
34 based on an independent assessment by a third-party consultant that all of the following
35 requirements for proposed investments have been met:
36 (1) The digital assets are maintained with a secure custody solution.
37 (2) The potential investment is appropriate for the designated fund's
38 circumstances from a total portfolio perspective.
39 (3) The control environment meets institutional investment industry requirements
40 for independent risk and compliance oversight, operational robustness, and
41 regulatory compliance."
42 SECTION 6. This act is effective when it becomes law.
