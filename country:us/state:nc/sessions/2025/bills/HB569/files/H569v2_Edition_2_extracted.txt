6 PART I. ABATEMENT OF PFAS EXCEEDANCES IN PUBLIC WATER SYSTEMS
7 SECTION 1. Article 1 of Chapter 130A of the General Statutes is amended by
8 adding a new section to read:
9 "§ 130A-19.1. Abatement of PFAS exceedances.
10 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
11 (1) Permissible concentration level. – For an individual per- and polyfluoroalkyl
12 substances (PFAS) compound, or combined PFAS compounds, any maximum
13 contaminant level that may be established by the United States Environmental
14 Protection Agency for the PFAS compound in question, or combined
15 compounds.
16 (2) PFAS manufacturer. – Persons that originally make PFAS compounds
17 through processes including, but not limited to, electrochemical fluorination
18 (ECF), telomerization, fluorocarbon polymerization, and production of
19 fluoropolymers. The term shall not include a person that uses previously made
20 PFAS compounds obtained from a PFAS manufacturer, for example: (i) to
21 produce commercial or consumer goods, such as weatherproof caulking, or
22 (ii) as intermediary products for use in the manufacture of commercial goods,
23 such as a greaseproof coating for a pizza box.
24 (3) Responsible party. – A PFAS manufacturer whose discharge or release of
25 PFAS into the environment has caused or contributed to the presence of PFAS
26 in a public water system as described in subsection (b) of this section.
27 (4) Secretary. – Means the Secretary of Environmental Quality.
28 (b) Pursuant to the Secretary's enforcement powers under G.S. 130A-19, authority to
29 adopt rules under G.S. 130A-315, and federally delegated duty to enforce the Federal Safe
30 Drinking Water Act in North Carolina, the Secretary may order a responsible party to pay a public
31 water system any actual and necessary costs incurred by the public water system to remove,
32 correct, or abate any adverse effects upon the water supply resulting from contamination for
33 which the person is responsible if the Secretary determines all of the following:
34 (1) The person is a PFAS manufacturer.

1 (2) The PFAS manufacturer discharged or released PFAS into the environment
2 that has caused or contributed to the presence of PFAS in the public water
3 system.
4 (3) The concentration of PFAS in the public water system, including any raw
5 water intake, regardless of the system's raw water source, including surface
6 water, public well, or pumped groundwater storage, has exceeded a
7 permissible concentration level.
8 Such costs shall include costs to procure, implement, maintain, and operate technology to
9 reduce PFAS concentrations in finished drinking water below the permissible concentration
10 level. If a responsible party refuses to comply with an order, the Secretary may institute an action
11 in the superior court of the county where the public water system exists to enforce the order.
12 (c) A responsible party shall be jointly and severally liable for all actual and necessary
13 costs imposed by the Secretary pursuant to subsection (b) of this section. Nothing in this section
14 shall limit or diminish any rights of contribution for costs incurred herein.
15 (d) A public water system shall reimburse ratepayers of the system through a reduction
16 in future rates charged if (i) the public water system has previously expended funds to remove,
17 correct, or abate any adverse effects upon its water supply resulting from PFAS contamination,
18 (ii) the amount of funds expended by the public water system for that purpose has been included
19 in rates charged to its ratepayers, and (iii) the funds expended by the public water system are
20 subsequently reimbursed by the responsible party as the result of an order issued pursuant to
21 subsection (b) of this section.
22 (e) The remedy under this section is in addition to those provided by existing statutory
23 and common law."
24
25 PART II. IMPLEMENTATION FUNDING
26 SECTION 2.(a) Department Funding. – The sum of three hundred thousand dollars
27 ($300,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year is appropriated from the General
28 Fund to the Department of Environmental Quality (Department) to implement the requirements
29 of this act. These funds shall be deposited into the PFAS Public Water Protection Fund, which is
30 established in the Department as a special fund. The Department may establish time-limited
31 positions with the funds appropriated by this subsection.
32 SECTION 2.(b) Report. – The Department shall report to the Joint Legislative
33 Oversight Committee on Agriculture and Natural and Economic Resources no later than
34 December 15, 2025, and annually thereafter, on their use of the funds appropriated by this act,
35 including abatement orders issued by the Secretary of Environmental Quality using the authority
36 conferred by G.S. 130A-19.1, as enacted by Section 1 of this act.
37
38 PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
39 SECTION 3. Section 2 of this act becomes effective September 1, 2025. Section 1
40 of this act is effective when it becomes law and applies retroactively to costs incurred by a public
41 water system on or after January 1, 2017, to remove, correct, or abate any adverse effects upon a
42 water supply resulting from contamination, irrespective of when a maximum contaminant level
43 was established by the United States Environmental Protection Agency for the PFAS compound
44 in question. The remainder of this act is effective when it becomes law.
