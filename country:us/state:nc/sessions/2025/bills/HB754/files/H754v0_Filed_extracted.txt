9 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 108A-112 reads as rewritten:
10 "§ 108A-112. Legislative intent and purpose.
11 (a) Determined to fight the growing problem of fraud and financial exploitation targeting
12 disabled adults and older adults in North Carolina, the General Assembly enacts this Article to
13 facilitate the collection of records needed to investigate and prosecute such these incidents.
14 (b) The General Assembly finds that older adults in this State are at a statistically higher
15 risk of being targeted for financial exploitation, regardless of diminished capacity or other
16 disability, because of their accumulation of substantial assets and wealth compared to younger
17 age groups.
18 (c) The General Assembly recognizes that financial institutions have duties imposed by
19 law and by contract to conduct customer-directed transactions in a timely manner and in
20 accordance with their customers' instructions.
21 (d) The General Assembly intends to provide financial institutions with the tools and
22 protections to take action based upon the reasonable belief that older adults or disabled adults
23 who have accounts with the financial institutions have been or are the subject of financial
24 exploitation and to provide financial institutions and their employees immunity from liability for
25 taking actions as authorized in this Article.
26 (e) The General Assembly intends to balance the rights of older adults and disabled adults
27 to direct and control their assets, funds, and investments and to exercise their constitutional rights
28 consistent with due process with the need to provide financial institutions the ability to place
29 narrow, 30-day limited restrictions on these rights in an effort to decrease older adults or disabled
30 adults' risk of loss due to abuse, neglect, or financial exploitation."

9 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 108A-112 reads as rewritten:
10 "§ 108A-112. Legislative intent and purpose.
11 (a) Determined to fight the growing problem of fraud and financial exploitation targeting
12 disabled adults and older adults in North Carolina, the General Assembly enacts this Article to
13 facilitate the collection of records needed to investigate and prosecute such these incidents.
14 (b) The General Assembly finds that older adults in this State are at a statistically higher
15 risk of being targeted for financial exploitation, regardless of diminished capacity or other
16 disability, because of their accumulation of substantial assets and wealth compared to younger
17 age groups.
18 (c) The General Assembly recognizes that financial institutions have duties imposed by
19 law and by contract to conduct customer-directed transactions in a timely manner and in
20 accordance with their customers' instructions.
21 (d) The General Assembly intends to provide financial institutions with the tools and
22 protections to take action based upon the reasonable belief that older adults or disabled adults
23 who have accounts with the financial institutions have been or are the subject of financial
24 exploitation and to provide financial institutions and their employees immunity from liability for
25 taking actions as authorized in this Article.
26 (e) The General Assembly intends to balance the rights of older adults and disabled adults
27 to direct and control their assets, funds, and investments and to exercise their constitutional rights
28 consistent with due process with the need to provide financial institutions the ability to place
29 narrow, 30-day limited restrictions on these rights in an effort to decrease older adults or disabled
30 adults' risk of loss due to abuse, neglect, or financial exploitation."
