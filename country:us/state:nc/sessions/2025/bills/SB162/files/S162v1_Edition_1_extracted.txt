1 (a) An order for custody of a minor child entered pursuant to this section shall award the
2 custody of such child to such person, agency, organization or institution as will best promote the
3 interest and welfare of the child. In making the determination, the court shall consider all relevant
4 factors including acts of domestic violence between the parties, the safety of the child, and the
5 safety of either party from domestic violence by the other party. An order for custody must
6 include written findings of fact that reflect the consideration of each of these factors and that
7 support the determination of what is in the best interest of the child. Between the parents, whether
8 natural or adoptive, no presumption shall apply as to who will better promote the interest and
9 welfare of the child. Joint custody to the parents shall be considered upon the request of either
10 parent.
11 (a1) There exists a rebuttable presumption that joint custody and shared parenting is in the
12 best interest of the child. For purposes of this section, "shared parenting" means the child spends
13 as close as possible to an equal amount of time with each parent. In determining the terms of a
14 shared parenting schedule, the court shall consider all of the following relevant factors:
15 (1) The wishes of the child's parent or parents as to his or her custody.
16 (2) The wishes of the child as to his or her custody, with due consideration given
17 to the influence a parent or other custodian may have over the child's wishes.
18 (3) The interaction and interrelationship of the child with his or her parent or
19 parents, his or her siblings, and any other person who may significantly affect
20 the child's best interests.
21 (4) The motivations of the adults participating in the custody proceeding.
22 (5) The child's adjustment and continuing proximity to his or her home, school,
23 and community.
24 (6) The physical and mental health of all individuals involved.
25 (7) A finding by the court that an act of domestic violence has been committed by
26 one of the parties against a child of the party or any other parties. The court
27 shall further determine the extent to which the domestic violence and abuse
28 has affected the child and the child's relationship to each party, with
29 consideration given to efforts made by a party toward completion of any
30 domestic violence treatment, counseling, or program.
31 (8) The extent to which the child has been cared for, nurtured, and financially
32 supported by any party.
33 (9) The intent of the parent or parents in placing a child with another person,
34 agency, organization, or institution.
35 (10) The circumstances under which a child was placed or allowed to remain with
36 another person, agency, organization, or institution, including whether a
37 parent seeking custody was previously prevented from doing so as a result of
38 an act of domestic violence, or whether the child was placed with another
39 person, agency, organization, or institution to allow the parent now seeking
40 custody to seek employment, work, or attend school.
41 (11) The likelihood that a party will allow the child frequent, meaningful, and
42 continuing contact with the other parent, unless the court finds that a
43 continuing relationship with the other parent will endanger the health and
44 safety of the child.
45 (a2) The presumption that joint custody and shared parenting is in the best interest of the
46 child may be rebutted if one or more of the following conditions exist:
47 (1) The court, after consideration of relevant factors under subsection (a1) of this
48 section, finds by clear and convincing evidence that shared parenting and joint
49 custody is not in the best interest of the child.
50 (2) The parties have reached an agreement on all issues related to custody of the
51 child.
