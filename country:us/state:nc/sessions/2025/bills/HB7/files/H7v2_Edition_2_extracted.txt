Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/2/25
Short Title: NC REACH Act. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
January 30, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REQUIRE AT LEAST THREE CREDIT HOURS OF INSTRUCTION IN
3 AMERICAN HISTORY OR AMERICAN GOVERNMENT IN ORDER TO GRADUATE
4 FROM A CONSTITUENT INSTITUTION OF THE UNIVERSITY OF NORTH
5 CAROLINA WITH A BACCALAUREATE DEGREE OR A COMMUNITY COLLEGE
6 WITH AN ASSOCIATE DEGREE.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8
9 PART I. TITLE
10 SECTION 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "North Carolina
11 Reclaiming College Education on America's Constitutional Heritage Act" or the "NC REACH
12 Act."
13
14 PART II. REQUIRE THREE CREDIT HOURS OF INSTRUCTION IN AMERICAN
15 HISTORY OR AMERICAN GOVERNMENT AT CONSTITUENT INSTITUTIONS OF
16 THE UNIVERSITY OF NORTH CAROLINA
17 SECTION 2.(a) G.S. 116-11(3) reads as rewritten:
18 "(3) The Except as provided in subdivision (15) of this section, the Board shall
19 determine the functions, educational activities and academic programs of the
20 constituent institutions. The Board shall also determine the types of degrees
21 to be awarded. The powers herein given to the Board shall not be restricted by
22 any provision of law assigning specific functions or responsibilities to
23 designated institutions, the powers herein given superseding any such
24 provisions of law. The Board, after adequate notice and after affording the
25 institutional board of trustees an opportunity to be heard, shall have authority
26 to withdraw approval of any existing program if it appears that the program is
27 unproductive, excessively costly or unnecessarily duplicative. The Board shall
28 review the productivity of academic degree programs every two years, using
29 criteria specifically developed to determine program productivity."
30 SECTION 2.(b) G.S. 116-11 is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
31 "(15) The Board of Governors shall adopt a policy requiring each constituent
32 institution of The University of North Carolina to require at least three credit
33 hours of instruction in American history or American government, in
34 accordance with G.S. 116-11.5, in order to graduate from the institution with
35 a baccalaureate degree."

1 SECTION 2.(c) Article 1 of Chapter 116 of the General Statutes is amended by
2 adding a new section to read:
3 "§ 116-11.5. American history or American government instruction required for
4 graduation with a baccalaureate degree.
5 (a) Each constituent institution of The University of North Carolina that offers a
6 baccalaureate degree shall require as a condition of graduation with a baccalaureate degree that
7 all students complete at least three credit hours of instruction in American history or American
8 government that provides a comprehensive overview of the major events and turning points of
9 American history and government. A course required pursuant to this section shall meet the
10 following criteria:
11 (1) Require each student to read at least the following documents in their entirety:
12 a. The Constitution of the United States of America.
13 b. The Declaration of Independence.
14 c. The Emancipation Proclamation.
15 d. At least five essays from the Federalist Papers, as determined by the
16 instructor.
17 e. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s Letter from Birmingham Jail.
18 f. The Gettysburg Address.
19 g. The North Carolina State Constitution.
20 h. George Washington's Farewell Address.
21 (2) Include a cumulative final exam on the principles in the documents listed in
22 subdivision (1) of this subsection. The final exam shall meet the following
23 criteria:
24 a. Comprise at least twenty percent (20%) of the student's total course
25 grade.
26 b. Focus substantially on the provisions and principles of the documents
27 listed in subdivision (1) of this subsection, the perspectives of the
28 authors of the documents, and the relevant historic contexts at the time
29 the documents were written.
30 (b) The chancellor of each constituent institution, or his or her designee, may exempt a
31 student from the requirements of this section if the student has successfully completed at least
32 three credit hours of instruction in American history or American government in a
33 postsecondary-level course provided outside the constituent institution that satisfies the
34 requirements of this section, including credit based on the following:
35 (1) A passing score on an Advanced Placement exam.
36 (2) A passing score on an International Baccalaureate exam.
37 (3) A passing score on an AS-Level or A-Level Cambridge Advanced
38 International Certificate of Education (AICE) course.
39 (4) A passing grade in a dual enrollment course.
40 (c) The Board of Governors shall ensure that the requirements of this section are
41 incorporated into the degree requirements of all baccalaureate programs and do not do any of the
42 following:
43 (1) Add to the total number of credit hours required for a degree. The Board of
44 Governors shall instead remove one elective course requirement.
45 (2) Conflict with the accreditation process for any constituent institution.
46 (d) The Board of Governors shall ensure that each constituent institution complies with
47 the requirements of this section. No later than November 15, 2026, and annually thereafter, the
48 Board shall report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee on compliance with
49 this section by each constituent institution. The report shall include, at a minimum, a copy of the
50 syllabus for each course that is offered to comply with this section.

1 (e) The Board of Governors, in its discretion, may remove the chancellor of a constituent
2 institution for failure to comply with this section over more than one academic year."
3
4 PART III. REQUIRE THREE CREDIT HOURS OF INSTRUCTION IN AMERICAN
5 HISTORY OR AMERICAN GOVERNMENT AT NORTH CAROLINA COMMUNITY
6 COLLEGES
7 SECTION 3.(a) G.S. 115D-5 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
8 "(aa) The State Board of Community Colleges shall adopt a policy requiring each
9 community college to require at least three credit hours of instruction in American history or
10 American government, in accordance with G.S. 115D-11, in order to graduate from the
11 community college with an associate degree."
12 SECTION 3.(b) Article 1 of Chapter 115D of the General Statutes is amended by
13 adding a new section to read:
14 "§ 115D-11. American history or American government instruction required for
15 graduation with an associate degree.
16 (a) Each community college shall require as a condition of graduation with an associate
17 degree that all students complete at least three credit hours of instruction in American history or
18 American government that provides a comprehensive overview of the major events and turning
19 points of American history and government. A course required pursuant to this section shall meet
20 the following criteria:
21 (1) Require each student to read at least the following documents in their entirety:
22 a. The Constitution of the United States of America.
23 b. The Declaration of Independence.
24 c. The Emancipation Proclamation.
25 d. At least five essays from the Federalist Papers, as determined by the
26 instructor.
27 e. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s Letter from Birmingham Jail.
28 f. The Gettysburg Address.
29 g. The North Carolina State Constitution.
30 h. George Washington's Farewell Address.
31 (2) Include a cumulative final exam on the principles in the documents listed in
32 subdivision (1) of this subsection. The final exam shall meet the following
33 criteria:
34 a. Comprise at least twenty percent (20%) of the student's total course
35 grade.
36 b. Focus substantially on the provisions and principles of the documents
37 listed in subdivision (1) of this subsection, the perspectives of the
38 authors of the documents, and the relevant historic contexts at the time
39 the documents were written.
40 (b) The president of a community college, or his or her designee, may exempt a student
41 from the requirements of this section if the student has successfully completed at least three credit
42 hours of instruction in American history or American government in a postsecondary-level
43 course provided outside the community college that satisfies the requirements of this section,
44 including credit based on the following:
45 (1) A passing score on an Advanced Placement exam.
46 (2) A passing score on an International Baccalaureate exam.
47 (3) A passing score on an AS-Level or A-Level Cambridge Advanced
48 International Certificate of Education (AICE) course.
49 (4) A passing grade in a dual enrollment course.

1 (c) The State Board of Community Colleges shall ensure that the requirements of this
2 section are incorporated into the degree requirements of all associate degree programs and do not
3 do either of the following:
4 (1) Add to the total number of credit hours required for a degree. The State Board
5 of Community Colleges shall instead remove one elective course requirement.
6 (2) Conflict with the accreditation process for any institution.

16 G.S. 115D-6.5."

18 PART IV. ALIGN FOUNDING PRINCIPLES COURSE REQUIREMENTS
19 SECTION 4.(a) G.S. 115C-81.45(d) is amended by adding a new subdivision to
20 read:
21 "(3a) A passing grade in an advanced course, as defined in G.S. 115C-174.26(a), or
22 a dual enrollment course, shall satisfy the Founding Principles of the United
23 States of America and the State of North Carolina course requirement if, in
24 addition to meeting the requirements of this section, the course requires
25 students to read the following documents in their entirety:
26 a. The Constitution of the United States of America.
27 b. The Declaration of Independence.
28 c. The Emancipation Proclamation.
29 d. At least five essays from the Federalist Papers, as determined by the
30 teacher.
31 e. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s Letter from Birmingham Jail.
32 f. The Gettysburg Address.
33 g. The North Carolina State Constitution.
34 h. George Washington's Farewell Address."
35 SECTION 4.(b) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning
36 with courses offered in the 2025-2026 school year.
37
38 PART V. EFFECTIVE DATE
39 SECTION 5. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes law
40 and applies beginning in the 2026-2027 academic year to students accepted for initial enrollment
41 in a baccalaureate program at a constituent institution or initial enrollment in an associate degree
42 program at a community college.
