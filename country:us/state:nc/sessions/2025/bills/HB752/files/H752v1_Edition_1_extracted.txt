6 GENERAL ASSEMBLY FINDINGS
7 SECTION 1. The General Assembly finds that:
8 (1) Transportation infrastructure is a foundational pillar for economic
9 development, enabling the efficient movement of goods, services, and people
10 across North Carolina, strengthening communities' abilities to connect and
11 grow.
12 (2) North Carolina's railroads serve as a critical component of the State's logistics
13 and supply chain infrastructure, necessary for freight movement, reducing
14 transportation costs, and providing a sustainable alternative to road
15 transportation, vital for supporting North Carolina's manufacturing and
16 agricultural industries. Upgrading our railroad infrastructure will reduce
17 transportation costs, improve freight efficiency, and will help alleviate road
18 congestion.
19 (3) Airports are integral anchors for regional economies, serving as key
20 transportation hubs, direct and indirect job creators, catalysts for trade,
21 tourism, and business growth, and supports for key industries. They attract
22 infrastructure investment and enhance connectivity, which strengthens North
23 Carolina's communities and expands its access to global opportunities.
24 (4) Located near more than seventy percent (70%) of the U.S. industrial base and
25 easily accessed by Interstates 95 and 40 via U.S. Highways 70 and 17, the Port
26 of Morehead City fills an important role in our chemical products and bulk
27 shipping industries. Along with the Port of Wilmington, it contributes heavily
28 to the economy of North Carolina, helping generate sixteen billion one
29 hundred million dollars ($16,100,000,000) in economic output. The
30 construction of a roll-on-roll-off facility would greatly enhance the port's
31 efficiency and capacity, fostering job creation, strengthening supply chains,
32 and driving further economic growth across North Carolina.
33 (5) Investing in transportation infrastructure is essential for keeping North
34 Carolina competitive in both national and global markets, ensuring efficient
35 trade, business growth, and economic resilience.

2 RAILROAD DEVELOPMENT FUND
3 SECTION 2.1. Article 2D of Chapter 136 of the General Statutes reads as rewritten:
4 "Article 2D.
5 "Railroad Revitalization.Development.
6 …
7 "§ 136-44.41. Special Economic Development Fund for Rail.
8 (a) Creation. – The Special Economic Development Fund for Rail (Rail Fund) is
9 established as a special fund within the General Fund to be administered by the Division of Rail
10 in accordance with this Article.
11 (b) Purpose. – The purpose of the Rail Fund is primarily for rail construction, rail
12 improvements, industry rail connections, railcar and locomotive acquisition, and railyard
13 construction. A portion of the Rail Fund may also be used for project preparation uses such as
14 preliminary engineering and due diligence.
15 (c) Source. – The Rail Fund shall consist of funds transferred to the Rail Fund and
16 revenue generated by Rail Fund investments and interest. Monies in the Rail Fund shall be
17 invested in accordance with G.S. 147-69.1, except that all interest or income received and
18 accruing on the monthly balance of the Rail Fund shall be paid into the Rail Fund. Unexpended
19 funds appropriated to the Rail Fund shall not revert.
20 (d) Uses. – Funds in the Rail Fund shall be used in accordance with the purpose of this
21 section.
22 (e) Cost-Sharing. – An allocation of funds under this section shall be in accordance with
23 the following requirements:
24 (1) No more than sixty percent (60%) of the annual allocations from the Rail Fund
25 shall be for the State match on federal grant applications.
26 (2) No more than thirty percent (30%) of the annual allocations from the Rail
27 Fund shall be for project preparation uses such as preliminary engineering and
28 due diligence.
29 (f) Criteria. – The Division shall establish procedures and criteria and adopt rules for
30 allocating funds from the Rail Fund in accordance with the purpose of this section and the
31 following criteria:
32 (1) The rail project is projected to increase capital investment, jobs, and revenue,
33 independently determined by the Department.
34 (2) Allocations shall be competitive and, after consultation with the Department
35 of Commerce, awarded to proposals with the greatest projected economic
36 impact benefitting North Carolina.
37 (g) Administrative Costs. – The Department may use no more than three percent (3%) of
38 the Rail Fund for administrative costs.
39 (h) Report. – No later than March 1, 2026, the Division shall submit an initial report on
40 the implementation of this section to the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental
41 Operations, the Joint Legislative Transportation Oversight Committee, and the Fiscal Research
42 Division and thereafter shall submit an annual report no later than December 1 of each year. The
43 report shall include the following information: use of funds, projects approved, and estimated
44 economic impact."
45 SECTION 2.2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Special Economic
46 Development Fund for Rail the sum of fifty million dollars ($50,000,000) in nonrecurring funds.

1 "Airport Special Economic Development Fund.
2 "§ 63-76. Special Economic Development Fund for Airports.
3 (a) Creation. – The Special Economic Development Fund for Airports (Airports Fund) is
4 established as a special fund within the General Fund to be administered by the Division of
5 Aviation in accordance with this Article.
6 (b) Purpose. – The purpose of the Airports Fund is for airport capital improvements and
7 to incentivize and retain economically strategic flights.
8 (c) Source. – The Airports Fund shall consist of funds transferred to the Airports Fund
9 and revenue generated by Airports Fund investments and interest. Monies in the Airports Fund
10 shall be invested in accordance with G.S. 147-69.1, except that all interest or income received
11 and accruing on the monthly balance of the Airports Fund shall be paid into the Airports Fund.
12 Unexpended funds appropriated to the Airports Fund shall not revert.
13 (d) Uses. – Funds in the Airports Fund shall be used in accordance with the purpose of
14 this section.
15 (e) Limitations. – An allocation of funds under this section shall be in accordance with
16 the following limitations:
17 (1) No more than eighty percent (80%) of the annual allocations from the Airports
18 Fund shall be used for airport capital improvements, including those necessary
19 to attract or retain commercial aviation operators or manufacturers that require
20 the use of an airport.
21 (2) No more than seventeen percent (17%) of the annual allocations from the
22 Airports Fund shall be used to incentivize and retain economically strategic
23 flights.
24 (3) Up to twenty percent (20%) of the annual allocations from the Airports Fund
25 may be applied to airport safety improvements, to the extent that the
26 Department determines that the lack of airport safety improvements is
27 inhibiting economic growth.
28 (f) Cost-Sharing. – The total allocation of funds from the Airports Fund shall not exceed
29 fifty percent (50%) of the cost for airport improvements.
30 (g) Criteria. – The Division shall establish procedures and criteria and adopt rules for
31 allocating funds from the Airports Fund in accordance with the purpose of this section and the
32 following criteria:
33 (1) The airport project is projected to increase jobs, wages, capital investment,
34 and tourism to North Carolina, with a particular emphasis on educational
35 tourism, as independently determined by the Department.
36 (2) Allocations shall be competitive and, after consultation with the Department
37 of Commerce, awarded to proposals with the greatest projected economic
38 impact benefitting North Carolina.
39 (h) Administrative Costs. – The Department may use no more than three percent (3%) of
40 the Airports Fund for administrative costs.
41 (i) Report. – No later than March 1, 2026, the Division shall submit an initial report on
42 the implementation of this section to the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental
43 Operations, the Joint Legislative Transportation Oversight Committee, and the Fiscal Research
44 Division and thereafter shall submit an annual report no later than December 1 of each year. The
45 report shall include the following information: use of funds, projects approved, and estimated
46 economic impact."
47 SECTION 3.2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Special Economic
48 Development Fund for Airports the sum of one hundred million dollars ($100,000,000) in
49 nonrecurring funds.
