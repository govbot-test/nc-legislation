3 (d) Training and Protocol Requirements. – Each K-12 school unit shall provide its
4 adopted mental health training program program, a suicide prevention education requirement,
5 and suicide risk referral protocol to school personnel at no cost to the employee. Employees shall
6 receive an initial mental health training of at least six hours and subsequent mental health
7 trainings of at least two hours. The initial mental health training shall occur within the first six
8 months of employment. Subsequent mental health trainings shall occur in the following school
9 year and annually thereafter. Additionally, school personnel shall complete one hour of suicide
10 prevention education each year to coincide with subsequent mental health trainings. In the
11 discretion of the K-12 school unit, the initial mental health training may be waived in the event
12 the employee completed an initial mental health training at another K-12 school unit. School
13 personnel may meet mental health training and suicide prevention education requirements in any
14 of the following ways:
15 (1) Electronic delivery of instruction.
16 (2) Videoconferencing.
17 (3) Group, in-person training.
18 (4) Self-study.
19 (5) Completion of approved programs.
20 (e) Review and Update. – Beginning August 1, 2025, and every five years thereafter, the
21 Superintendent of Public Instruction shall review the State Board of Education's minimum
22 requirements for a school-based mental health plan, model mental health training program,
23 suicide prevention education, and model suicide risk referral protocol and recommend any
24 needed changes to the State Board of Education. The State Board shall update its policies to
25 reflect those recommendations and publish the updates to K-12 school units. A K-12 school unit
26 shall update its adopted school-based mental health plan in accordance with any updates provided
27 by the State Board.
28 (f) Reporting; State Audit. – By September 15 of each year, each K-12 school unit shall
29 report to the Department of Public Instruction on (i) the content of the school-based mental health
30 plan adopted in the unit, including the mental health training program program, a suicide
31 prevention education requirement, and suicide risk referral protocol, and (ii) prior school year
32 compliance with requirements of this section. The Department of Public Instruction may also
33 audit K-12 school units at appropriate times to ensure compliance with the requirements of this
34 section. The Department shall report the information it receives pursuant to this subsection to the
35 Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee and the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee
36 on Health and Human Services by December 15 of each year.

41 participation in, or implementation of any component of a school-based mental health plan,
42 mental health training program, a suicide prevention education requirement, or suicide risk
43 referral protocol required by this section, unless that act or omission amounts to gross negligence,
44 wanton conduct, or intentional wrongdoing. Nothing in this section shall be construed to impose
45 any specific duty of care or standard of care on a K-12 school unit."
