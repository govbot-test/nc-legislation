HOUSE BILL 724
Short Title: NC-FACT. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Logan, Ross, Belk, and Reives (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 3, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CREATE THE NC-FEDERAL ALIGNMENT FOR CRITICAL
3 TECHNOLOGIES TASK FORCE (NC-FACT) WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT OF
4 COMMERCE AND TO REQUIRE THAT TASK FORCE TO STUDY WAYS TO FOSTER
5 AND PROMOTE SCIENCE, TECHNOLOGY, AND INNOVATION IN THE STATE.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1.(a) There is established the NC-Federal Alignment for Critical
8 Technologies Task Force (Task Force) within the Department of Commerce, Office of Science,
9 Technology & Innovation (Office). The Task Force shall consist of members of the Office and,
10 at the discretion of the Office, may consist of members of constituent institutions of The
11 University of North Carolina and the private sector.
12 SECTION 1.(b) The Task Force established under subsection (a) of this section shall
13 have the following duties:
14 (1) Research ways to increase collaboration among State, federal, and private
15 entities involved in the promotion of science, technology, and innovation in
16 the State.
17 (2) Research ways to streamline State, federal, and private resources to promote
18 the science and technology industries in the State.
19 (3) Develop short-, mid-, and long-term economic development-related strategies
20 and recommendations to increase the patronage and prevalence of the science
21 and technology industries in the State, with particular emphasis on attracting
22 out-of-state investment into the State.
23 (4) Perform any other studies, evaluations, or determinations the Task Force
24 considers necessary.
25 SECTION 1.(c) No later than April 1 of each year, the Task Force shall submit a
26 report with its findings and recommendations to the Joint Legislative Economic Development
27 and Global Engagement Oversight Committee. These findings and recommendations shall
28 include specific, concrete, and actionable steps to be undertaken by the State and upon which the
29 General Assembly could act.
30 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
