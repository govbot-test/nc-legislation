7 USE OF TAX-MAINTAINED PROPERTIES FOR PUBLIC HEARINGS
8 SECTION 1.(a) Article 7 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes is amended by
9 adding a new section to read:
10 "§ 143B-279.21. Use of schools and other public buildings for public hearings.
11 (a) The governing authority having control over (i) public buildings or (ii) schools
12 governed by the governing body of a public school unit which have facilities for group meetings
13 is hereby authorized and directed to permit the use of such buildings without charge, except
14 custodial and utility fees, by the Department for public hearings. Provided, that the use of such
15 buildings or schools by the Department for public hearings shall not be permitted at times during
16 the school day or which would interfere with normal school activities or functions normally
17 carried on in such school buildings, and such use shall be subject to reasonable rules and
18 regulations of the governing body of the public school unit and other governing authorities.
19 (b) The Department of Environmental Quality shall be entitled to use any school
20 governed by the governing body of a public school unit or other State, county, or municipal
21 building, or a part thereof, or any other building, or a part thereof, which is supported or
22 maintained, in whole or in part, by or through tax revenues; provided, however, that this section
23 shall not be construed to permit the Department to use any tax-exempt church property for such
24 purposes without the express consent of the individual church involved for the purpose of
25 conducting public hearings."
26 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 115C-47 is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
27 "(70) To Provide Access to School Facilities to the Department of Environmental
28 Quality. – A local board of education shall adopt policies to allow the
29 Department of Environmental Quality to have access to facilities that can
30 facilitate group meetings on school property of a school governed by the local
31 school board pursuant to G.S. 143B-279.21(a)."
32 SECTION 1.(c) Part 2 of Article 14A of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
33 amended by adding a new section to read:
34 "§ 115C-238.35A. Provide access to school facilities to the Department of Environmental
35 Quality.

4 SECTION 1.(d) Subsections (a), (b), and (c) of this section are effective when they
5 become law and apply beginning with the 2025-2026 school year.

7 CLARIFY REQUIREMENTS FOR HAZARDOUS WASTE RECYCLING
8 SECTION 2. Article 9 of Chapter 130A of the General Statutes is amended by
9 adding a new section to read:
10 "§ 130A-295.07. Requirements for hazardous waste recycling.
11 (a) For purposes of this section, "off-site recycling facility" means any facility receiving
12 shipments of hazardous waste from off-site to be recycled or processed for recycling through any
13 process conducted at the facility. A facility owned or operated by a generator of hazardous waste
14 to recycle their own waste is not an off-site recycling facility.
15 (b) An off-site recycling facility shall comply with the requirements of this Article and
16 with 15A NCAC 13A .0113.
17 (c) A hazardous waste facility that (i) accepts hazardous waste from off-site for recycling
18 and (ii) meets the definition of "commercial" in G.S. 130A-290 is subject to the requirements of
19 this Article and rules adopted by the Commission applicable to commercial hazardous waste
20 facilities."

7 REVISE STEWARDSHIP LAWS
8 SECTION 6. G.S. 143-214.15 reads as rewritten:
9 "§ 143-214.15. Compensatory mitigation for diverse habitats.
10 (a) The Department of Environmental Quality shall seek more net gains of aquatic
11 resources through compensatory mitigation by increasing wetland establishment of diverse
12 habitats, including emergent marsh habitat, shallow open water, and other forested and
13 non-forested wetland habitats.
14 (b) The Department of Environmental Quality shall further establish with the district
15 engineer of the Wilmington District of the United States Army Corps of Engineers compensatory
16 mitigation credit ratios that incentivize the creation or establishment of diverse wetland habitats
17 to support waterfowl and other wildlife.
18 (c) The Department of Environmental Quality shall work in cooperation with the Wildlife
19 Resources Commission to ensure that all purchased mitigation lands or conservation easements
20 on these lands maximize opportunities for public recreation, including hunting, and promote
21 wildlife and biological diversity. prioritize management practices that promote wildlife and
22 biological diversity and, where feasible, provide opportunities for public recreation, including
23 hunting by property owners and lessees. The Department and the Commission shall pursue the
24 voluntary involvement of third-party groups to leverage resources and ensure that there is no
25 additional cost to private mitigation bankers or the taxpayers in achieving these mitigation
26 credits.
27 (d) The Stewardship Program of the Department of Environmental Quality shall maintain
28 an inventory of all its land holdings and determine how many of those holdings are potential
29 wildlife habitats, either as currently held or with some modification. The Stewardship Program
30 shall maximize use of these mitigation land holdings as ecological research sites and for hunting
31 leases when the Stewardship Program determines it is feasible to do so.
32 (e) If private individuals, corporations, or other nongovernmental entities wish to
33 purchase any of the inventory of land suitable for wildlife habitat, then the Stewardship Program
34 of the Department of Environmental Quality shall issue a request for proposal to all interested
35 respondents for the purchase of the land. The State shall accept a proposal and proceed to dispose
36 of the land only if the Department determines that the proposal meets both of the following
37 requirements:
38 (1) The proposal provides for the maintenance in perpetuity of management
39 measures listed in the original mitigation instrument or otherwise needed on
40 an ongoing or periodic basis to maintain the functions of the mitigation site.
41 (2) Where the functions of the mitigation site include provision of recreation or
42 hunting opportunities to members of the general public, the proposal includes
43 measures needed to continue that level of access.
44 The instrument conveying a property interest in a mitigation site shall be executed in the
45 manner required by Article 16 of Chapter 146 of the General Statutes, and shall reflect the
46 requirements of this subsection.
47 (f) The Department of Environmental Quality shall report to the Environmental Review
48 Commission by March 1 of each year in which there are changes in inventory during the
49 preceding year under the provisions of this section regarding the changes."
