4 PART IV. MASTER'S IN SCHOOL ADMINISTRATION INTERNSHIP STIPENDS
5 SECTION 4.(a) Article 19 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
6 adding the following new section to read:
7 "§ 115C-284.2. Master's in school administration internship stipends.
8 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
9 (1) Authority. – The State Education Assistance Authority.
10 (2) Department. – The Department of Public Instruction.
11 (3) MSA intern. – A participant in an approved full-time master's in school
12 administration program who is completing an internship pursuant to
13 G.S. 115C-284.1(d)(1).
14 (4) Principal Fellow MSA intern. – An MSA intern who is a participant in the
15 Principal Fellows Program.
16 (5) School administrator. – A principal or an assistant principal.
17 (b) Stipend. – To the extent funds are made available for this purpose, the Department
18 shall provide MSA interns with a full-time internship position with a 10-month stipend during
19 the internship period of the master's program. The stipend shall be at the beginning salary of an
20 assistant principal or, for a teacher who becomes an intern, at least as much as that person would
21 earn as a teacher on the teacher salary schedule. If the funds provided for this purpose are
22 insufficient to fully fund the stipend required by this subsection, the Department shall use funds
23 appropriated to the State Public School Fund for this purpose. The North Carolina Principal
24 Fellows Program or the school of education where the MSA intern participates in a full-time
25 master's in school administration program shall supply the Department of Public Instruction with
26 certification of eligible full-time interns in order to receive the stipends provided in this section.
27 (c) Payback. – For MSA interns who are not Principal Fellow MSA interns, the
28 Department shall condition receipt of the stipend authorized pursuant to subsection (b) of this
29 section on an agreement to be signed by the intern to reimburse the Authority on behalf of the
30 Department in cash for up to twenty thousand dollars ($20,000) of the stipend funds provided to
31 the intern if the intern does not serve as a school administrator in a public school unit for two
32 years within seven years after graduation from the master's in school administration program,
33 exclusive of any authorized deferment for extenuating circumstances. To the extent practicable,
34 the Authority may model the agreement on the service repayment agreement in place for principal
35 fellows pursuant to G.S. 116-74.48(c). The Authority shall transfer any funds received in
36 repayment from a recipient pursuant to this subsection to the Department.
37 (d) Administration. – Of the funds appropriated to the Department pursuant to this
38 section, the Department may retain up to two percent (2%) each fiscal year for the administrative
39 costs associated with the payback agreement described in subsection (c) of this section. The
40 Department shall use these funds to enter into a memorandum of understanding with the
41 Authority to accept repayment on behalf of the Department and to monitor the acceptability of
42 compliance of the recipient with that agreement. The Authority shall forgive repayment of the
43 stipend funds if it finds it is impossible for the recipient to work for two years as a school
44 administrator, within seven years after completion of the preparation program supported by those
45 funds, because of the death or permanent disability of the recipient. If the recipient repays the
46 stipend funds by cash payments to the Authority, all funds shall be repaid within 10 years after
47 completion of the master's in school administration internship supported by the stipend. If the
48 recipient completes the master's in school administration program, repayment of stipend funds
49 shall begin no later than 27 months after the completion of the program. Should a recipient
50 present extenuating circumstances, the Authority may extend the period to repay the stipend
51 funds in cash to no more than a total of 12 years.
