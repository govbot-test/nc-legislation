5 SECTION 1. Chapter 50 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article

7 "Article 6.
8 "Complex Family Financial Cases.
9 "§ 50-110. Definitions.
10 The following definitions shall apply in this Article:
11 (1) Chief hearing officer. – A hearing officer designated by the Chief Justice of
12 the North Carolina Supreme Court who determines which cases are designated
13 as complex family financial cases, assigns all cases designated as complex
14 family financial cases, and prepares any required reports.
15 (2) Complex family financial case. – Any claim or claims approved for hearing
16 as a complex family financial case as provided for by this Article. Claims
17 eligible for hearing as a complex family financial case are equitable
18 distribution, alimony, post separation support, child support, or any
19 combination of those claims.
20 (3) Hearing officer or complex family financial hearing officer. – An individual
21 who has been appointed pursuant to this Article to hear and enter orders in
22 complex family financial cases.
23 "§ 50-111. Complex family financial hearing officer.

27 as chief hearing officer.

29 following minimum qualifications:
30 (1) Attorney licensed in North Carolina and in good standing with the North
31 Carolina State Bar.
32 (2) Substantial involvement handling complex family financial cases during the
33 10 calendar years prior to the year of application, including the following:
34 a. Average at least 600 hours per year handling complex family financial
35 cases.

39 "§ 50-112. Authority and duties of a complex family financial case hearing officer.
40 (a) A hearing officer under this Article has the following authority and responsibilities in
41 all complex family financial cases:
42 (1) To conduct hearings and to ensure that the parties' due process rights are
43 protected.
44 (2) To take testimony and establish a record.
45 (3) To evaluate evidence and make decisions regarding the issues being heard.
46 (4) To enter temporary, interim, and final orders related to the issues being heard
47 that have the same force and effect as orders entered by a district court judge.
48 (5) To enter orders granting or denying any motion filed under G.S. 1A-1 or any
49 local rules of court for the county in which the action was filed related to
50 actions under this Chapter that have the same force and effect as orders entered
51 by a district court judge.

11 "§ 50-113. Designation of a complex family financial claim.
12 (a) A party designating a claim as a complex family financial claim shall file a Notice of
13 Designation in the district court in which the action has been filed and shall contemporaneously
14 serve the notice on all parties or counsel and on the chief hearing officer. The Notice of
15 Designation shall, in good faith and based on information reasonably available, succinctly state
16 each applicable factor provided in G.S. 50-114, the reasons supporting each factor for
17 designation as a complex family financial claim, and any other information supporting
18 designation as a complex family financial claim. Any factor or reasons supporting the designation
19 not asserted shall be deemed conclusively waived.
20 (b) Within 30 days after service of the Notice of Designation, any other party may, in
21 good faith, file and serve an opposition to the designation of the claim as a complex family
22 financial claim. The opposition to the designation of the claim shall assert all reasons for which
23 the party opposing designation objects to the designation, and any reason not asserted shall be
24 deemed conclusively waived. The opposition to the designation shall be served on each opposing
25 party and the chief hearing officer and shall be filed in the district court in which the action has
26 been filed.
27 (c) Based on the written Notice of Designation and any opposition filed, the chief hearing
28 officer shall determine whether the action should be designated as a complex family financial
29 claim by written order entered within 45 days of service of the Notice of Designation.
30 (d) Each party shall pay equal shares of the additional filing fee as required under
31 G.S. 7A-305. Only one additional filing fee shall be required per complex family financial case.
32 (e) Once an order granting designation of a claim as a complex family financial claim is
33 entered, that claim shall be designated and administered as a complex family financial case and
34 assigned to a hearing officer by the chief hearing officer. All proceedings related to the claims
35 designated as a complex family financial claim shall be before the hearing officer to whom the
36 complex family financial case has been assigned. If complex family financial claim status is
37 denied, the claim shall be heard with any other claims filed under this Chapter.
38 (f) Complex family financial cases are subject to all provisions of Article 1 of this
39 Chapter, the North Carolina Rules of Civil Procedure, the North Carolina Rules of Evidence, and
40 any applicable local rules of court for the county in which the complex family financial case is
41 pending.
42 "§ 50-114. Factors for complex family financial case determination.
43 The chief hearing officer shall consider each of the following factors in determining whether
44 a claim or claims shall be designated as a complex family financial case:
45 (1) Valuation and classification issues related to trusts, including active and
46 passive increases or decreases in value.
47 (2) Valuation and classification issues related to businesses, including active or
48 passive increases or decreases in value.
49 (3) Valuation and classification of real property, including active or passive
50 increases or decreases in value.

1 (4) Valuation and classification issues regarding complex retirement or other
2 employment benefits, including employee stock ownership plans, stock
3 options, profit sharing, defined contribution plans, and defined benefit plans.
4 (5) Valuation and classification of profits, bonuses, or other income or assets
5 received after the date of separation.
6 (6) Active or passive changes in value to separate property during the marriage.
7 (7) Tax issues arising from the distribution of assets and debts, including tax loss
8 carryforwards, refunds, credits, or tax consequences.
9 (8) Whether there are loans or transfers between businesses or shareholders.
10 (9) Whether there are third-party defendants.
11 (10) Validity of a premarital or property settlement agreement pled in defense to
12 an equitable distribution, alimony, post separation support, or child support
13 case.
14 (11) Total value of real and personal property.
15 (12) Calculation of income for spousal support, child support, or both when income
16 includes non W-2 income.
17 (13) Total length of time requested for trial on the issues detailed on the Notice of
18 Designation.
19 Claims which have been filed in excess of 365 days shall be given priority over claims filed
20 less than 365 days, and requested trial dates for the complex family financial case in excess of
21 15 days shall be given priority.
22 "§ 50-115. Complex family financial hearings.
23 (a) Motion hearings shall be held virtually unless the assigned hearing officer determines
24 good cause exists to hold in the hearing in person. If an in-person hearing is ordered, it shall be
25 held at the courthouse in the county in which the action was filed in an available district or
26 superior courtroom staffed by a deputy or assistant clerk and bailiff.
27 (b) Hearings on the issues designated as a complex family financial case shall be held in
28 person at the courthouse in the county in which the action was filed in an available district or
29 superior courtroom staffed by a deputy or assistant clerk and bailiff.
30 (c) All complex family financial hearings must be recorded and exhibits maintained as
31 required for any other matter.
32 (d) Any hearing on final disposition of the complex family financial case must be
33 scheduled on consecutive days.
34 "§ 50-116. Appeal from orders of the complex family financial case hearing officer.
35 Appeals of orders entered by a hearing officer shall be as provided for in G.S. 7A-27(b)."
36 SECTION 2.1. Three hearing officers shall be appointed by the Chief Justice of the
37 North Carolina Supreme Court who meet the requirements of G.S. 50-111(b). The Chief Justice
38 of the North Carolina Supreme Court shall designate one of the hearing officers as chief hearing
39 officer. The Chief Justice of the North Carolina Supreme Court has the authority to create
40 additional rules or procedures necessary to give effect to the provisions of this act.

49 (3) The manner of disposition of each complex family financial case, including
