5 SECTION 1. Chapter 50 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
6 to read:
7 "Article 6.
8 "Complex Family Financial Cases.
9 "§ 50-110. Definitions.
10 The following definitions shall apply in this Article:
11 (1) Chief hearing officer. – A hearing officer designated by the Chief Justice of
12 the North Carolina Supreme Court who determines which cases are designated
13 as complex family financial cases, assigns all cases designated as complex
14 family financial cases, and prepares any required reports.
15 (2) Complex family financial case. – Any claim or claims approved for hearing
16 as a complex family financial case as provided for by this Article. Claims
17 eligible for hearing as a complex family financial case are equitable
18 distribution, alimony, post separation support, child support, or any
19 combination of those claims.
20 (3) Hearing officer or complex family financial hearing officer. – An individual
21 who has been appointed pursuant to this Article to hear and enter orders in
22 complex family financial cases.
23 "§ 50-111. Complex family financial hearing officer.
24 (a) Complex family financial hearing officers shall be employees of the Administrative
25 Office of the Courts and shall be appointed by the Chief Justice of the North Carolina Supreme
26 Court. The Chief Justice of the North Carolina Supreme Court shall designate one hearing officer
27 as chief hearing officer.
28 (b) To serve as a hearing officer or chief hearing officer, the individual must meet the
29 following minimum qualifications:
30 (1) Attorney licensed in North Carolina and in good standing with the North
31 Carolina State Bar.
32 (2) Substantial involvement handling complex family financial cases during the
33 10 calendar years prior to the year of application, including the following:

41 "§ 50-112. Authority and duties of a complex family financial case hearing officer.
42 (a) A hearing officer under this Article has the following authority and responsibilities in
43 all complex family financial cases:
44 (1) To conduct hearings and to ensure that the parties' due process rights are
45 protected.
46 (2) To take testimony and establish a record.
47 (3) To evaluate evidence and make decisions regarding the issues being heard.
48 (4) To enter temporary, interim, and final orders related to the issues being heard
49 that have the same force and effect as orders entered by a district court judge.
50 (5) To enter orders granting or denying any motion filed under G.S. 1A-1 or any
51 local rules of court for the county in which the action was filed related to

2 by a district court judge.
3 (6) To subpoena witnesses and documents.
4 (b) A hearing officer under this Article is authorized to conduct hearings on complex
5 family financial cases statewide.
6 (c) A hearing officer must complete at least nine hours of continuing legal education
7 credits in family law or related fields each year, including taxation, trial advocacy, evidence,
8 negotiation (including training in mediation, arbitration, and collaborative law), real property,
9 estate planning and probate law, trusts, business organizations, employee benefits, bankruptcy,

11 mediation training, and parenting coordinator training will not qualify for family law or related
12 field hours.
13 "§ 50-113. Designation of a complex family financial claim.
14 (a) A party designating a claim as a complex family financial claim shall file a Notice of
15 Designation in the district court in which the action has been filed and shall contemporaneously
16 serve the notice on all parties or counsel and on the chief hearing officer. The Notice of
17 Designation shall, in good faith and based on information reasonably available, succinctly state
18 each applicable factor provided in G.S. 50-114, the reasons supporting each factor for
19 designation as a complex family financial claim, and any other information supporting
20 designation as a complex family financial claim. Any factor or reasons supporting the designation
21 not asserted shall be deemed conclusively waived.
22 (b) Within 30 days after service of the Notice of Designation, any other party may, in
23 good faith, file and serve an opposition to the designation of the claim as a complex family
24 financial claim. The opposition to the designation of the claim shall assert all reasons for which
25 the party opposing designation objects to the designation, and any reason not asserted shall be
26 deemed conclusively waived. The opposition to the designation shall be served on each opposing
27 party and the chief hearing officer and shall be filed in the district court in which the action has
28 been filed.
29 (c) Based on the written Notice of Designation and any opposition filed, the chief hearing
30 officer shall determine whether the action should be designated as a complex family financial
31 claim by written order entered within 45 days of service of the Notice of Designation.
32 (d) Each party shall pay equal shares of the additional filing fee as required under
33 G.S. 7A-305. Only one additional filing fee shall be required per complex family financial case.
34 (e) Once an order granting designation of a claim as a complex family financial claim is
35 entered, that claim shall be designated and administered as a complex family financial case and
36 assigned to a hearing officer by the chief hearing officer. All proceedings related to the claims
37 designated as a complex family financial claim shall be before the hearing officer to whom the
38 complex family financial case has been assigned. If complex family financial claim status is
39 denied, the claim shall be heard with any other claims filed under this Chapter.
40 (f) Complex family financial cases are subject to all provisions of Article 1 of this
41 Chapter, the North Carolina Rules of Civil Procedure, the North Carolina Rules of Evidence, and
42 any applicable local rules of court for the county in which the complex family financial case is
43 pending.
44 "§ 50-114. Factors for complex family financial case determination.
45 The chief hearing officer shall consider each of the following factors in determining whether
46 a claim or claims shall be designated as a complex family financial case:
47 (1) Valuation and classification issues related to trusts, including active and
48 passive increases or decreases in value.
49 (2) Valuation and classification issues related to businesses, including active or
50 passive increases or decreases in value.

1 (3) Valuation and classification of real property, including active or passive
2 increases or decreases in value.
3 (4) Valuation and classification issues regarding complex retirement or other
4 employment benefits, including employee stock ownership plans, stock
5 options, profit sharing, defined contribution plans, and defined benefit plans.
6 (5) Valuation and classification of profits, bonuses, or other income or assets
7 received after the date of separation.
8 (6) Active or passive changes in value to separate property during the marriage.
9 (7) Tax issues arising from the distribution of assets and debts, including tax loss
10 carryforwards, refunds, credits, or tax consequences.
11 (8) Whether there are loans or transfers between businesses or shareholders.
12 (9) Whether there are third-party defendants.
13 (10) Validity of a premarital or property settlement agreement pled in defense to
14 an equitable distribution, alimony, post separation support, or child support
15 case.
16 (11) Total value of real and personal property.
17 (12) Calculation of income for spousal support, child support, or both when income
18 includes non W-2 income.
19 (13) Total length of time requested for trial on the issues detailed on the Notice of
20 Designation.
21 Claims which have been filed in excess of 365 days shall be given priority over claims filed
22 less than 365 days, and requested trial dates for the complex family financial case in excess of
23 15 days shall be given priority.
24 "§ 50-115. Complex family financial hearings.
25 (a) Motion hearings shall be held virtually unless the assigned hearing officer determines
26 good cause exists to hold in the hearing in person. If an in-person hearing is ordered, it shall be
27 held at the courthouse in the county in which the action was filed in an available district or
28 superior courtroom staffed by a deputy or assistant clerk and bailiff.
29 (b) Hearings on the issues designated as a complex family financial case shall be held in
30 person at the courthouse in the county in which the action was filed in an available district or
31 superior courtroom staffed by a deputy or assistant clerk and bailiff.
32 (c) All complex family financial hearings must be recorded and exhibits maintained as
33 required for any other matter.
34 (d) Any hearing on final disposition of the complex family financial case must be
35 scheduled on consecutive days.
36 "§ 50-116. Appeal from orders of the complex family financial case hearing officer.
37 Appeals of orders entered by a hearing officer shall be as provided for in G.S. 7A-27(b)."
38 SECTION 2.1. Three hearing officers shall be appointed by the Chief Justice of the
39 North Carolina Supreme Court who meet the requirements of G.S. 50-111(b). The Chief Justice
40 of the North Carolina Supreme Court shall designate one of the hearing officers as chief hearing
41 officer. The Chief Justice of the North Carolina Supreme Court has the authority to create
42 additional rules or procedures necessary to give effect to the provisions of this act.

47 the following information:
48 (1) The total number of cases designated as complex family financial cases by
49 county.
50 (2) The total number of complex family financial cases disposed of.

8 "Complex Family Financial Cases.
9 "§ 50-110. Definitions.
10 The following definitions shall apply in this Article:
11 (1) Chief hearing officer. – A hearing officer designated by the Chief Justice of
12 the North Carolina Supreme Court who determines which cases are designated
13 as complex family financial cases, assigns all cases designated as complex
14 family financial cases, and prepares any required reports.
15 (2) Complex family financial case. – Any claim or claims approved for hearing
16 as a complex family financial case as provided for by this Article. Claims
17 eligible for hearing as a complex family financial case are equitable
18 distribution, alimony, post separation support, child support, or any
19 combination of those claims.
20 (3) Hearing officer or complex family financial hearing officer. – An individual
21 who has been appointed pursuant to this Article to hear and enter orders in
22 complex family financial cases.

27 as chief hearing officer.

30 (1) Attorney licensed in North Carolina and in good standing with the North
31 Carolina State Bar.
32 (2) Substantial involvement handling complex family financial cases during the
33 10 calendar years prior to the year of application, including the following:

41 "§ 50-112. Authority and duties of a complex family financial case hearing officer.
42 (a) A hearing officer under this Article has the following authority and responsibilities in
43 all complex family financial cases:
44 (1) To conduct hearings and to ensure that the parties' due process rights are
45 protected.
46 (2) To take testimony and establish a record.
47 (3) To evaluate evidence and make decisions regarding the issues being heard.
48 (4) To enter temporary, interim, and final orders related to the issues being heard
49 that have the same force and effect as orders entered by a district court judge.
50 (5) To enter orders granting or denying any motion filed under G.S. 1A-1 or any
51 local rules of court for the county in which the action was filed related to

2 by a district court judge.
3 (6) To subpoena witnesses and documents.
4 (b) A hearing officer under this Article is authorized to conduct hearings on complex
5 family financial cases statewide.
6 (c) A hearing officer must complete at least nine hours of continuing legal education
7 credits in family law or related fields each year, including taxation, trial advocacy, evidence,
8 negotiation (including training in mediation, arbitration, and collaborative law), real property,
9 estate planning and probate law, trusts, business organizations, employee benefits, bankruptcy,

11 mediation training, and parenting coordinator training will not qualify for family law or related
12 field hours.
13 "§ 50-113. Designation of a complex family financial claim.
14 (a) A party designating a claim as a complex family financial claim shall file a Notice of
15 Designation in the district court in which the action has been filed and shall contemporaneously
16 serve the notice on all parties or counsel and on the chief hearing officer. The Notice of
17 Designation shall, in good faith and based on information reasonably available, succinctly state
18 each applicable factor provided in G.S. 50-114, the reasons supporting each factor for
19 designation as a complex family financial claim, and any other information supporting
20 designation as a complex family financial claim. Any factor or reasons supporting the designation
21 not asserted shall be deemed conclusively waived.
22 (b) Within 30 days after service of the Notice of Designation, any other party may, in
23 good faith, file and serve an opposition to the designation of the claim as a complex family
24 financial claim. The opposition to the designation of the claim shall assert all reasons for which
25 the party opposing designation objects to the designation, and any reason not asserted shall be
26 deemed conclusively waived. The opposition to the designation shall be served on each opposing
27 party and the chief hearing officer and shall be filed in the district court in which the action has
28 been filed.
29 (c) Based on the written Notice of Designation and any opposition filed, the chief hearing
30 officer shall determine whether the action should be designated as a complex family financial
31 claim by written order entered within 45 days of service of the Notice of Designation.
32 (d) Each party shall pay equal shares of the additional filing fee as required under
33 G.S. 7A-305. Only one additional filing fee shall be required per complex family financial case.
34 (e) Once an order granting designation of a claim as a complex family financial claim is
35 entered, that claim shall be designated and administered as a complex family financial case and
36 assigned to a hearing officer by the chief hearing officer. All proceedings related to the claims
37 designated as a complex family financial claim shall be before the hearing officer to whom the
38 complex family financial case has been assigned. If complex family financial claim status is
39 denied, the claim shall be heard with any other claims filed under this Chapter.
40 (f) Complex family financial cases are subject to all provisions of Article 1 of this
41 Chapter, the North Carolina Rules of Civil Procedure, the North Carolina Rules of Evidence, and
42 any applicable local rules of court for the county in which the complex family financial case is
43 pending.
44 "§ 50-114. Factors for complex family financial case determination.
45 The chief hearing officer shall consider each of the following factors in determining whether
46 a claim or claims shall be designated as a complex family financial case:
47 (1) Valuation and classification issues related to trusts, including active and
48 passive increases or decreases in value.
49 (2) Valuation and classification issues related to businesses, including active or
50 passive increases or decreases in value.

1 (3) Valuation and classification of real property, including active or passive
2 increases or decreases in value.
3 (4) Valuation and classification issues regarding complex retirement or other
4 employment benefits, including employee stock ownership plans, stock
5 options, profit sharing, defined contribution plans, and defined benefit plans.
6 (5) Valuation and classification of profits, bonuses, or other income or assets
7 received after the date of separation.
8 (6) Active or passive changes in value to separate property during the marriage.
9 (7) Tax issues arising from the distribution of assets and debts, including tax loss

12 (9) Whether there are third-party defendants.

15 case.
16 (11) Total value of real and personal property.
17 (12) Calculation of income for spousal support, child support, or both when income
18 includes non W-2 income.
19 (13) Total length of time requested for trial on the issues detailed on the Notice of
20 Designation.
21 Claims which have been filed in excess of 365 days shall be given priority over claims filed
22 less than 365 days, and requested trial dates for the complex family financial case in excess of
23 15 days shall be given priority.
24 "§ 50-115. Complex family financial hearings.
25 (a) Motion hearings shall be held virtually unless the assigned hearing officer determines
26 good cause exists to hold in the hearing in person. If an in-person hearing is ordered, it shall be
27 held at the courthouse in the county in which the action was filed in an available district or
28 superior courtroom staffed by a deputy or assistant clerk and bailiff.
29 (b) Hearings on the issues designated as a complex family financial case shall be held in
30 person at the courthouse in the county in which the action was filed in an available district or
31 superior courtroom staffed by a deputy or assistant clerk and bailiff.
32 (c) All complex family financial hearings must be recorded and exhibits maintained as
33 required for any other matter.
34 (d) Any hearing on final disposition of the complex family financial case must be
35 scheduled on consecutive days.
36 "§ 50-116. Appeal from orders of the complex family financial case hearing officer.
37 Appeals of orders entered by a hearing officer shall be as provided for in G.S. 7A-27(b)."
