1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CREATE THE NORTH CAROLINA-IRELAND TRADE COMMISSION.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Chapter 143 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
5 Article to read:
6 "Article 85.
7 "North Carolina-Ireland Trade Commission.
8 "§ 143-821. North Carolina-Ireland Trade Commission created; membership.
9 (a) Commission Created. – The North Carolina-Ireland Trade Commission is created.
10 The Commission is administratively located in the Department of Commerce but shall exercise
11 its powers independently of the Secretary of Commerce.
12 (b) Membership. – The Commission shall consist of the following seven members:
13 (1) Two members of the Senate, one from each party, to be appointed by the
14 President Pro Tempore of the Senate.
15 (2) Two members of the House of Representatives, one from each party, to be
16 appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
17 (3) An individual who is active in Irish-American cultural and civic affairs, with
18 preference for an individual with a history of cooperation in the legislative
19 process, to be appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the Senate.
20 (4) An individual active in public higher education who has an interest in North
21 Carolina-Ireland trade relations, to be appointed by the Speaker of the House
22 of Representatives.
23 (5) An individual recognized for the individual's achievements and experience in
24 international business, to be appointed by the Governor.
25 (c) Terms. – Members of the Commission shall serve terms of three years and are eligible
26 for reappointment.
27 (d) Vacancies. – Whenever a vacancy on the Commission exists, the appointing authority
28 shall appoint an individual to fill the vacancy.
29 (e) Officers. – Members of the Commission may elect a chair and vice-chair of the
30 Commission.
31 (f) Quorum. – A majority of the Commission constitutes a quorum for the transaction of
32 business.
33 (g) Administrative Services. – The Department of Commerce shall provide
34 administrative services to the Commission.
35 (h) Meetings. – The Commission shall meet at least twice per year.
36 "§ 143-822. Duties of North Carolina-Ireland Trade Commission.
*DRS15420-MUa-95A*

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 The North Carolina-Ireland Trade Commission has the following duties:
2 (1) Encourage bilateral trade between this State and Ireland.
3 (2) Encourage mutual investment in this State and Ireland, especially in
4 infrastructure and employment.
5 (3) Promote business, educational, and cultural exchanges between this State and
6 Ireland.
7 (4) Take joint action on policy issues of mutual agreement between this State and
8 Ireland.
9 (5) Submit an annual report to the General Assembly that includes the following:
10 a. The number of times the Commission met during the preceding year.
11 b. The minutes of the Commission's meetings.
12 c. Any recommendations or requests that may assist the Commission's
13 work.
14 (6) Address any other related issues, as determined necessary by the
15 Commission."
16 SECTION 2. By October 1, 2027, the North Carolina-Ireland Trade Commission
17 shall submit its first annual report under G.S. 143-822(5), as enacted by Section 1 of this act.
18 SECTION 3.(a) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
19 Commerce the sum of ten thousand dollars ($10,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027
20 fiscal year to provide administrative services to the North Carolina-Ireland Trade Commission.
21 SECTION 3.(b) This section becomes effective July 1, 2026.
22 SECTION 4. Except as otherwise provided, this act becomes effective October 1,
23 2026.
