39 WATER SEWER ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM
40 SECTION 3.3.(a) Award Authorized. – Pursuant to Part 2H of Article 10 of Chapter
41 143B of the General Statutes, the Department of Commerce may commit in a Governor's Letter
42 to reimburse, over a period of time not less than 10 years, a business, as defined in
43 G.S. 143B-437.51, for the costs incurred for an eligible project. For purposes of this section, an
44 eligible project is the construction of, or improvements to, new or existing water or sewer lines,
45 facilities, or equipment for new, proposed, or existing buildings by the business, and can include
46 water reuse and water reclamation projects, if the business commits, in a company performance
47 agreement, to the requirements of subsection (c) of this section.
48 SECTION 3.3.(b) Award Amount. – The total amount that may be committed for
49 reimbursement under this section is equal to the lesser of (i) fifty million dollars ($50,000,000)
50 or (ii) fifty percent (50%) of the cost of the eligible project. The maximum amount that can be
51 paid out in a single calendar year for all but the final year of the award term is the lesser of
Page 4 House Bill 389-Fourth Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 one-tenth of the amount committed or one-half of the costs incurred and verified by the business
2 for that calendar year.
3 SECTION 3.3.(c) Company Performance Agreement. – For a project to be eligible
4 under this section, a business operating a manufacturing site within the same county and river
5 basin as the eligible project must commit to the following requirements in addition to those
6 contained in G.S. 143B-437.72(b):
7 (1) Invest at least two billion dollars ($2,000,000,000) of private funds in the
8 development or expansion of the manufacturing site served by or located
9 within the same county as the eligible project.
10 (2) For the greater of the term of the agreement or 10 years, create at least 500
11 new jobs having an overall average compensation that exceeds the average
12 wage for all insured private employers in the county in which the eligible
13 project is located and maintain both the new and existing jobs of the business
14 in that county.
15 (3) Annually verify costs incurred for the eligible project.
16 (4) Ensure that the eligible project will supply at least sixty percent (60%) of the
17 total aggregate water and wastewater needs required by the manufacturing site
18 as developed or expanded by the investment made under subdivision (1) of
19 this subsection.
20 (5) Not seek or accept any other grant from the State, including a grant pursuant
21 to Part 2G of Article 10 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes, for any jobs
22 created as part of the company performance agreement.
23 SECTION 3.3.(d) One NC Commitment Exclusion. – The amount committed
24 pursuant to this section shall not be included in calculating the amount authorized to be
25 committed pursuant to G.S. 143B-437.71(b1).
26 SECTION 3.3.(e) Agreement Binding. – A Governor's Letter issued pursuant to this
27 section is a binding obligation of the State and is not subject to State funds being appropriated
28 by the General Assembly.
29 SECTION 3.3.(f) Match Requirement. – Notwithstanding G.S. 143B-437.72(c)(1),
30 a local government is not required to match the funds allocated by the State for an eligible project
31 under this section.
32 SECTION 3.3.(g) Effective Date. – This section is effective when this act becomes
33 law.

16 SCHOOL SAFETY GRANTS
17 SECTION 4.2.(a) Definitions. – For the purposes of this section, the following
18 definitions shall apply:
19 (1) Community partner. – A public or private entity, including, but not limited to,
20 a nonprofit corporation or a local management entity/managed care
21 organization (LME/MCO), that partners with a public school unit to provide
22 services or pay for the provision of services for the unit.
23 (2) School health support personnel. – School psychologists, school counselors,
24 school nurses, and school social workers.
25 SECTION 4.2.(b) Program; Purpose. – The Executive Director of the Center for
26 Safer Schools shall establish the School Safety Grants Program (Program) for the 2025-2026
27 fiscal year. The purpose of the Program shall be to improve safety in public school units by
28 providing grants in the 2025-2026 fiscal year for (i) services for students in crisis, (ii) school
29 safety training, (iii) safety equipment in schools, and (iv) subsidizing the School Resource Officer
30 Grants Program.
31 SECTION 4.2.(c) Grant Applications. – A public school unit may submit an
32 application to the Executive Director of the Center for Safer Schools for one or more grants
33 pursuant to this section in the 2025-2026 fiscal year. The application shall include an assessment,
34 to be performed in conjunction with a local law enforcement agency, of the need for improving
35 school safety within the public school unit that would receive the funding or services. The
36 application shall identify current and ongoing needs and estimated costs associated with those
37 needs.
38 SECTION 4.2.(d) Criteria and Guidelines. – The Executive Director of the Center
39 for Safer Schools shall develop criteria and guidelines for the administration and use of the grants
40 pursuant to this section, including any documentation required to be submitted by applicants. In
41 assessing grant applications, the Executive Director shall consider at least all of the following
42 factors:
43 (1) The level of resources available to the public school unit that would receive
44 the funding.
45 (2) Whether the public school unit has received other grants for school safety.
46 (3) The overall impact on student safety in the public school unit if the identified
47 needs are funded.
48 SECTION 4.2.(e) Grants for Students in Crisis. – Of the funds appropriated by this
49 section for the grants provided in this section, the Executive Director of the Center for Safer
50 Schools, in consultation with the Department of Health and Human Services, shall award grants
