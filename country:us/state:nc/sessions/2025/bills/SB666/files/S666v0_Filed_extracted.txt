FILED SENATE
Mar 25, 2025
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
S.B. 666
SESSION 2025
PRINCIPAL CLERK
S D
Short Title: 2025 Water Safety Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Senator Lee (Primary Sponsor).
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE FUNDS FOR PFAS MITIGATION AND RESEARCH, TO DIRECT
3 THE COMMISSION FOR PUBLIC HEALTH TO ADOPT STANDARDS FOR PFAS IN
4 DRINKING WATER, AND TO DIRECT THE DEPARTMENT TO ADOPT PFAS
5 DISCHARGE LIMITS FOR SIGNIFICANT INDUSTRIAL USERS AND PUBLICLY
6 OWNED TREATMENT WORKS DISCHARGING DIRECTLY TO SURFACE WATERS.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8
9 PFAS MITIGATION GRANTS
10 SECTION 1.(a) Mitigation Grants. – Article 9 of Chapter 130A of the General
11 Statutes is amended by adding a new Part to read:
12 "Part 9. PFAS Mitigation.
13 "§ 130A-310.80. Definitions.
14 In addition to the definitions in G.S. 130A-2 and G.S. 130A-290, the following definitions
15 apply in this Part:
16 (1) Distressed unit. – As defined in G.S. 159G-20.
17 (3) Fund – The PFAS Mitigation and Fund established in G.S. 130A-310.84.
18 (4) PFAS. – Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, including perfluorooctanoic
19 acid (PFOA), perfluorooctanesulfonate (PFOS), hexafluoropropylene oxide
20 dimer acid (HFPO-DA, also known as GenX), perfluorohexanesulfonic acid
21 (PFHxS), and perfluorononanoic acid (PFNA), and Perfluorobutanesulfonic
22 acid (PFBS).
23 (5) SWIA. – The State Water Infrastructure Authority established in Article 5 of
24 Chapter 159G of the General Statutes.
25 "§ 130A-310.82. Purpose.
26 The purpose of this Part is to provide funding to support the mitigation of the impacts of
27 environmental contamination due to PFAS on local public water and wastewater systems.
28 "§ 130A-310.84. PFAS Mitigation Fund.
29 (a) Fund Established. – The PFAS Mitigation Fund is established within the Department.
30 The purpose of the Fund is to support statewide efforts to detect, reduce, mitigate, and prevent
31 exposure to PFAS and to support scientific research and technology development related to PFAS
32 removal, treatment, monitoring, and precursor identification. The fund consists of any funds
33 appropriated to it by the General Assembly and grants from federal agencies or other non-State
34 entities.
*DRS35246-MHa-88*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (b) Uses of Fund. – The Fund may only be used by SWIA to provide grants to units of
2 local government operating public water or wastewater treatment systems for any of the
3 following:
4 (1) PFAS sampling and monitoring in drinking water, wastewater, surface water,
5 and groundwater.
6 (2) Installation or upgrade of water treatment technologies for PFAS removal.
7 (3) Emergency response and remediation of PFAS contamination in soil, surface
8 water, and groundwater.
9 (c) Funding Criteria and Oversight. – SWIA shall establish criteria and application
10 procedures for local PFAS response grants, and shall prioritize grants to public water systems
11 and public wastewater systems (i) for which contamination from PFAS has caused the greatest
12 impacts on public health and the environment and (ii) that are or meet the criteria to be
13 categorized as a distressed unit.
14 (d) Report. – SWIA shall report annually as a part of the report required by G.S. 159G-72
15 regarding projects funded under this section. The report shall include the project type (sampling
16 and monitoring, treatment technologies, or emergency response), the project recipient, a brief
17 description of project and the amount of funding provided."
18 SECTION 1.(b) Conforming Change. – G.S. 159G-71 reads as rewritten:
19 "§ 159G-71. State Water Infrastructure Authority; powers and duties.
20 The Authority has the following additional duties:
21 …
22 (13) To award grants to mitigate the impacts of environmental contamination due
23 to PFAS on local public water and wastewater systems."
24 SECTION 2. Funding. – The sum of fifty-six million dollars ($56,000,000) in
25 recurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year is appropriated from the General Fund to the
26 Department of Environmental Quality for the PFAS Mitigation Fund established in Part 9 of
27 Article 9 of Chapter 130A of the General Statutes, as enacted by Section 1 of this act.
28
29 RESEARCH GRANTS
30 SECTION 3.(a) PFAS Research Funding. – The sum of fourteen million dollars
31 ($14,000,000) in recurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year is appropriated from the General
32 Fund to the North Carolina Collaboratory at the University of North Carolina (Collaboratory).
33 These funds will be used to support scientific research on PFAS conducted by or in collaboration
34 with public or nonprofit academic institutions, including any of the following:
35 a. Detection methods for known and emerging PFAS and PFAS
36 precursors.
37 b. Fate and transport of PFAS and PFAS precursors in environmental
38 media.
39 c. Innovative PFAS remediation, filtration, and destruction technologies.
40 d. Public health and toxicological impact assessments of PFAS and
41 related compounds.
42 e. Evaluation of the health impacts of PFAS mixtures found in the State's
43 drinking water to more closely model real-world public health
44 scenarios.
45 SECTION 3.(b) Directive. – The Collaboratory shall consult with affected
46 stakeholders, scientific experts, and State and local officials to ensure funding is targeted to
47 research in areas of highest environmental and public health impact.
48 SECTION 3.(c) Restrictions. – The restrictions of G.S. 116-255(c) apply to funds
49 appropriated by this section.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 3.(d) Report. – The Collaboratory shall include in the report required by
2 G.S. 116-256 documentation of its use of the funds allocated by this section and updates
3 regarding the research funded by this section.
4
5 ESTABLISH PFAS STANDARDS FOR DRINKING WATER
6 SECTION 4. Article 10 of Chapter 130A of the General Statutes is amended by
7 adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 130A-315.1 Maximum contaminant levels for certain contaminants established.
9 (a) Maximum contaminant levels are established for all of the following contaminants:
10 (1) Perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) at 4.0 parts per trillion (ppt).
11 (2) Perfluorooctanesulfonic acid (PFOS) at 4.0 ppt.
12 (3) Perfluorononanoic acid (PFNA) at 10 ppt.
13 (4) Hexafluoropropylene oxide dimer acid (HFPO-DA, also known as GenX) at
14 at 10 ppt.
15 (5) Perfluorohexanesulfonic acid (PFHxS) at 10 ppt.
16 (6) Perfluorobutanesulfonic acid (PFBS) at 10 ppt.
17 (7) Mixtures containing two or more of PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA, and PFBS at
18 1 (unitless).
19 (b) The Commission shall adopt rules to establish a compliance schedule with respect to
20 the maximum contaminants levels set forth in subsection (a) of this section for all public water
21 systems, including community water systems and transient and non-transient non-community
22 water systems that is substantially identical to the compliance schedule set forth in 40 C.F.R. Part
23 141, Subpart Z."
24
25 ESTABLISH PFAS STANDARDS FOR DIRECT DISCHARGERS AND SIGNIFICANT
26 INDUSTRIAL USERS
27 SECTION 5.(a) No later than October 1, 2025, the Department of Environmental
28 Quality, in consultation with the North Carolina Collaboratory, shall develop:
29 (1) Science-based PFAS concentration limits for commonly detected PFAS,
30 which shall be used to establish monitoring, permitting, and pollution
31 reduction requirements for direct dischargers to surface waters and significant
32 industrial users ("regulated entities") as provided in subsection (c) of this
33 section. Concentrations limits for significant industrial users shall be made
34 applicable through a pretreatment permit to be issued by the applicable
35 publicly owned treatment works (POTW).
36 (2) Source reduction and treatment requirements to be imposed, including
37 requiring regulated entities to do one or more of the following, as applicable:
38 a. Conduct a PFAS source identification and minimization plan,
39 approved by the Department, to reduce PFAS inputs into their
40 wastewater streams.
41 b. Install and operate pretreatment technology to remove or reduce PFAS
42 to below concentration limits before discharge.
43 c. Submit to enhanced monitoring and reporting as required by the
44 Department.
45 d. Cease discharge of identified PFAS compounds where the Department
46 determines that feasible alternatives exist.
47 (3) A compliance schedule of up to 36 months to begin no later than January 1,
48 2026, for regulated entities to achieve the requirements developed pursuant to
49 subdivisions (1) and (2) of this Section. During this time, the Department shall
50 coordinate with the North Carolina Collaboratory to do all of the following:
51 a. Provide technical assistance for PFAS treatment technologies.
DRS35246-MHa-88 Page 3
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 b. Assess cost-effective alternatives.
2 c. Develop sector-based PFAS guidance for best available technology
3 (BAT).
4 SECTION 5.(b) All National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES)
5 permits and pretreatment permits, as applicable, for regulated entities subject to this section shall
6 include enforceable limits or conditions for PFAS discharges based on the concentration limits
7 established pursuant to subsection (a) of this section and available treatment options, as
8 determined by the Department. The Department may, however, exempt regulated entities from
9 the requirements of this subsection, and requirements established under subdivision (2) of
10 subsection (a) of this section, if the regulated entity demonstrates, through sampling and analysis
11 verified by the Department, that PFAS discharge concentrations are below background levels or
12 that the discharges do not contribute to PFAS exceedances downstream.
13 SECTION 5.(c) This section applies only to the following regulated entities:
14 (1) Significant industrial users, as that term is defined under 15A NCAC 02H
15 .0903, that:
16 a. Discharge wastewater containing PFAS compounds to a POTW; and
17 b. Have exceedances of PFAS concentration limits established pursuant
18 to subsection (a) of this section.
19 (2) Direct dischargers that:
20 a. Discharge wastewater containing PFAS compounds to surface waters
21 of the State under an NPDES permit; and
22 b. Have exceedances of PFAS concentration limits established pursuant
23 to subsection (a) of this section.
24 SECTION 5.(d) The Environmental Management Commission shall adopt
25 temporary and permanent rules to implement the provisions of this section. Permanent rules
26 adopted pursuant to this section are not subject to Part 3 of Article 2A of Chapter 150B of the
27 General Statutes. The Department may issue interim guidance pending rule adoption.
28
29 SEVERABILITY AND EFFECTIVE DATE
30 SECTION 6. If any provision of this act or the application thereof to any person or
31 circumstances is held invalid, such invalidity shall not affect other provisions or applications of
32 this act that can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and, to this end, the
33 provisions of this act are declared to be severable.
34 SECTION 7. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
35 law.
