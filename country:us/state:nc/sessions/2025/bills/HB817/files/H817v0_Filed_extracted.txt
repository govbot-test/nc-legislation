Section 1:
H.B. 817
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 7, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Triad Regional Grants. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Lambeth.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS FOR VARIOUS GRANTS BENEFITTING THE
3 TRIAD REGION.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding G.S. 143C-5-2, there is appropriated from the
6 General Fund to the Office of State Budget and Management the sum of fourteen million five
7 hundred twenty-five thousand dollars ($14,525,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026
8 fiscal year to be allocated in the form of grants to the following entities for the following
9 purposes:
10 (1) Fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) to Hasten International, Inc., a nonprofit
11 organization, to be used for mobile medical clinic services.
12 (2) Two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($250,000) to Camel City Track
13 Foundation, a nonprofit organization, to be used for an expansion of existing
14 facilities.
15 (3) Five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) to the Samantha and Kyle Busch
16 Bundle of Joy Fund, a nonprofit organization, to be used to provide assistance
17 to low- and middle-income families struggling with the high cost of fertility
18 treatments.
19 (4) One million two hundred thousand dollars ($1,200,000) to the Piney Grove
20 Volunteer Fire and Rescue Department, Inc., a nonprofit organization, to
21 enhance unit transportation and firefighting capabilities.
22 (5) One million five hundred thousand dollars ($1,500,000) to Winston-Salem
23 Speedway, LLC, for stadium improvements to attract large events to benefit
24 the regional economy.
25 (6) Two hundred thousand dollars ($200,000) to North Carolina Folk Festival, a
26 nonprofit organization, to be used for the annual Folk Fest in Greensboro.
27 (7) Five million three hundred twenty-five thousand dollars ($5,325,000) to
28 Historic Bethabara Park, Inc., a nonprofit organization, to be used for repairs,
29 replacements, and enhancements related to the historic site.
30 (8) One million five hundred thousand dollars ($1,500,000) to Forsyth County for
31 the Cure Violence program to address gun violence.
32 (9) Four million dollars ($4,000,000) to Creative Corridors Coalition, a nonprofit
33 organization, to be used for design and construction of the Peter Oliver Public
34 Park, a park and open-air pavilion structure.
35 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
