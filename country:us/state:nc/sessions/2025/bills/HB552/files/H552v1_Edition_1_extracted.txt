6 The Department shall administer the program and shall prioritize grants to recipients that the
7 Department determines will have the greatest net economic benefit at the regional and State
8 levels. In determining priority, the Department shall give preference to a project that satisfies one
9 or more of the following: (i) it is sited entirely within a development tier one or two area, as
10 defined in G.S. 143B-437.08, (ii) it incorporates, in a significant amount, as determined by the
11 Department, practices of precision agriculture, artificial-intelligence-driven automation,
12 biotechnology, or a combination thereof, and (iii) in which research and development
13 compensation is at least ten percent (10%) of the recipient's annual payroll expenses. Grants to a
14 recipient under this subsection must comply with all of the following:
15 (1) A grant to a recipient may not exceed one hundred thousand dollars
16 ($100,000) in any single calendar year.
17 (2) A grant to a recipient may not exceed five hundred thousand dollars
18 ($500,000) total.
19 (3) The grant term may not exceed five years.
20 (4) The Department certifies that the recipient has invested or intends to invest at
21 least five million dollars ($5,000,000) of private funds in improvements to
22 real property and additions to tangible personal property in the project within
23 a two-year period beginning with the time the grant is awarded.
24 (5) The recipient employs, and agrees to maintain for the entire grant term
25 employment of, at least 25 full-time employees or equivalent full-time
26 contract employees at the project that is the subject of the grant at the time the
27 grant is awarded.
28 (6) The recipient meets an average weekly wage that is at least equal to one
29 hundred ten percent (110%) of the average wage for all insured private
30 employers in the county.
31 (7) The Department requires the recipient to enter into an agreement requiring
32 that the recipient (i) meet performance criteria to protect the State's investment
33 and ensure that the projected benefits of the project are secured, including
34 employment level requirements, compensation requirements, investment
35 amount and time line requirements, and other criteria the Department
36 considers appropriate and (ii) repay or reimburse an appropriate portion of the
37 grant based on the extent of any failure to meet the performance criteria."
38 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
