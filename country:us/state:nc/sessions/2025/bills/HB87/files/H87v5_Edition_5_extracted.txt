9 SECTION 1. G.S. 116-204 reads as rewritten:
10 "§ 116-204. Powers of Authority.
11 The Authority is hereby authorized and empowered:

13 (14) To certify and submit a list of qualifying scholarship granting organizations
14 to the Secretary of the Treasury of the United States in accordance with (i)

17 SECTION 2. Article 23 of Chapter 116 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
18 a new Part to read:
19 "Part 8. Compliance with Requirements of Federal Tax Credit for Contributions of Individuals
20 to Scholarship Granting Organizations.
21 "§ 116-209.110. State election for federal tax credit for contributions of individuals to
22 scholarship granting organizations.
23 Pursuant to subsection (1) of Section 2 of Article V of the North Carolina Constitution and
24 the provisions of G.S. 105-1.1 and in accordance with section 25F of the Internal Revenue Code
25 (26 U.S.C. § 25F), the General Assembly voluntarily elects the State of North Carolina to do the
26 following:
27 (1) Participate in the federal tax credit established under 26 U.S.C. § 25F for
28 individuals who make qualified contributions to scholarship granting
29 organizations.
30 (2) Identify scholarship granting organizations located in this State in accordance
31 with G.S. 116-209.111.

1 (a) The Authority shall submit to the Secretary of the Treasury of the United States and
2 publish on the website of the Authority a list of scholarship granting organizations that meet the
3 requirements of section 25F of the Internal Revenue Code (26 U.S.C. § 25F) and are located in
4 this State. As part of the submission, the Authority shall certify its authority to submit the list on
5 behalf of the State and comply with any other requirements of 26 U.S.C. § 25F, its associated
6 regulations, or other applicable guidance issued by the Secretary of the Treasury of the United
7 States.
8 (b) The Authority may establish rules governing the process and documentation
9 necessary for an entity to qualify to be included as a scholarship granting organization on the list
10 produced and submitted by the Authority pursuant to subsection (a) of this section and in
11 accordance with 26 U.S.C. § 25F, its associated regulations, or other applicable guidance issued
12 by the Secretary of the Treasury of the United States.
13 (c) The Authority may enter into an agreement with other State agencies, including the
14 Department of Revenue, to meet the requirements of this section."
15 SECTION 3. The State Education Assistance Authority shall comply with all federal
16 regulations pertaining to the administration of the federal tax credit established under 26 U.S.C.
17 § 25F to ensure that the State is eligible to participate in taxable years beginning after December
18 31, 2026. The State Education Assistance Authority shall establish any necessary rules by July
19 1, 2026, or within 120 days of the publication of federal regulations, whichever is later.
20 SECTION 4. In accordance with 26 U.S.C. § 25F and any applicable federal
21 regulation and guidance, qualifying scholarship granting organizations on the list produced
22 pursuant to G.S. 116-204(14), as enacted by this act, may provide scholarships for any qualified
23 elementary or secondary education expense, including home school expenses, to the extent
24 allowed under federal law.
25 SECTION 5. This act is effective when it becomes law.
