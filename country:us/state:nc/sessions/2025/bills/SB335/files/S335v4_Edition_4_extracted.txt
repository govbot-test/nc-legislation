GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 4
Health Care Committee Substitute Adopted 4/2/25
Commerce and Insurance Committee Substitute Adopted 4/8/25
Fourth Edition Engrossed 4/10/25
Short Title: Pharmacists/Test and Treat/Influenza & Strep. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 20, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ALLOW PHARMACISTS TO TEST AND TREAT FOR CERTAIN ILLNESSES
3 WITH CERTAIN MEDICATIONS APPROVED BY THE BOARD OF PHARMACY, TO
4 PROVIDE FOR FAIR AND EQUITABLE REIMBURSEMENT OF HEALTH CARE
5 SERVICES OR PROCEDURES THAT ARE PERFORMED BY A PHARMACIST
6 WITHIN THAT PHARMACIST'S SCOPE OF PRACTICE AND THAT ARE
7 EQUIVALENT TO SERVICES PERFORMED BY OTHER HEALTH CARE
8 PROFESSIONALS, AND TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS TO THE DEPARTMENT OF
9 INSURANCE.
10 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
11 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 90-85.3 reads as rewritten:
12 "§ 90-85.3. Definitions.
13 …
14 (b2) "CLIA-waived test" means a laboratory test authorized by the Food and Drug
15 Administration and waived under the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments of 1988.
16 (b3) "Clinical pharmacist practitioner" means a licensed pharmacist who meets the
17 guidelines and criteria for such title established by the joint subcommittee of the North Carolina
18 Medical Board and the North Carolina Board of Pharmacy and is authorized to enter into drug
19 therapy management agreements with physicians in accordance with the provisions of
20 G.S. 90-18.4.
21 …."
22 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 90-85.3A reads as rewritten:
23 "§ 90-85.3A. Practice of pharmacy.
24 …
25 (b) A pharmacist may advise and educate patients and health care providers concerning
26 therapeutic values, content, uses, and significant problems of drugs and devices; assess, record,
27 and report adverse drug and device reactions; take and record patient histories relating to drug
28 and device therapy; administer drugs; monitor, record, and report drug therapy and device usage;
29 perform drug utilization reviews; and participate in drug and drug source selection and device
30 and device source selection as provided in G.S. 90-85.27 through G.S. 90-85.31.
31 …
32 (e) A pharmacist may order and perform a CLIA-waived test and initiate treatment
33 pursuant to the result of the CLIA-waived test for (i) influenza and (ii) streptococcus infections
*S335-v-4*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 in accordance with statewide protocols. A pharmacist shall not treat a health condition under this
2 section with any controlled substance classified in Schedules I through IV."
3 SECTION 1.(c) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025.
4 SECTION 2.(a) Article 3 of Chapter 58 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
5 a new section to read:
6 "§ 58-3-241. Healthcare services provided by pharmacists.
7 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
8 (1) Healthcare provider. – Either of the following:
9 a. An individual who is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized under
10 Chapter 90 of the General Statutes to provide healthcare services in
11 the ordinary course of business or practice of a profession or in an
12 approved education or training program.
13 b. A health care facility licensed under Chapter 131E or Chapter 122C of
14 the General Statutes and where healthcare services are provided to
15 patients.
16 (2) Healthcare services. – Any of the following health or medical procedures or
17 services rendered by a healthcare provider:
18 a. Testing, diagnosis, or treatment of a health condition, illness, injury,
19 or disease. This includes testing, diagnosis, or treatment rendered by a
20 pharmacist acting within the pharmacist's scope of practice.
21 b. Dispensing of drugs, medical devices, medical appliances, or medical
22 goods for the treatment of a health condition, illness, injury, or disease.
23 c. Administration of a vaccine or medication.
24 (3) Pharmacist. – An individual licensed to practice pharmacy under Article 4A
25 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes or the relevant laws of another state.
26 (b) A health benefit plan offered by an insurer in this State shall cover healthcare services
27 provided by a pharmacist if all of the following conditions are met:
28 (1) The service or procedure was performed within the pharmacist's licensed
29 lawful scope of practice.
30 (2) The health benefit plan would have covered the service if the service or
31 procedure had been performed by another healthcare provider.
32 (c) The participation of a pharmacy in a drug benefit provider network of a health benefit
33 plan shall not satisfy any requirement that insurers offering health benefit plans include
34 pharmacists in medical benefit provider networks.
35 (d) An insurer shall accept a claim under this section regardless of whether it is submitted
36 by a pharmacist or a pharmacy submitting the claim on behalf of a pharmacist the pharmacy
37 employs or contracts with."
38 SECTION 2.(b) G.S. 58-3-230 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
39 "(d) Insurers that delegate credentialing agreements or requirements for pharmacists
40 licensed under Article 4A of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes or the relevant laws of another
41 state to a contracted healthcare facility shall accept the credentialing for all pharmacists employed
42 by, or contracted with, those healthcare facilities."
43 SECTION 2.(c) G.S. 58-56-26 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
44 "(e) Notwithstanding any provision of this Article to the contrary, all requirements relating
45 to the coverage of prescription drugs and pharmacy services under this Chapter that apply to
46 health benefit plans are applicable to a third-party administrator in the same way they are
47 applicable to an insurer."
48 SECTION 2.(d) Article 56A of Chapter 58 of the General Statutes is amended by
49 adding a new section to read:
50 "§ 58-56A-55. Health benefit plan requirements applicable.
Page 2 Senate Bill 335-Fourth Edition
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 All requirements relating to the coverage of prescription drugs and pharmacy services under
2 this Chapter that apply to health benefit plans are applicable to a pharmacy benefits manager in
3 the same way they are applicable to an insurer."
4 SECTION 2.(e) This section is effective October 1, 2025, and applies to insurance
5 contracts entered into, renewed, or amended on or after that date.
6 SECTION 3. No later than October 1, 2025, the State Health Director shall issue a
7 standing order authorizing a pharmacist to order and perform a CLIA-waived test and initiate
8 treatment for influenza and streptococcus infections in accordance with G.S. 90-85.3A(e) as
9 amended by Section 1 of this act. The standing order shall include protocols for testing and
10 treatment of influenza and streptococcus infections that balance patient safety with ensuring
11 access to care provided by pharmacists. The standing order shall remain in effect until the earlier
12 of the date the permanent rules described in Section 4 of this act become effective or January 1,
13 2027.
14 SECTION 4. No later than October 1, 2025, the North Carolina Medical Board and
15 the North Carolina Board of Pharmacy, in conjunction with the State Health Director, shall adopt
16 rules to implement the provisions of Section 1 of this act. At a minimum, those rules shall include:
17 (1) An approved course of treatment pharmacists may implement for influenza
18 and streptococcus infections.
19 (2) Protocols for testing and treatment of influenza and streptococcus infections
20 that balance patient safety with ensuring access to care provided by
21 pharmacists.
22 (3) If the Boards deem it appropriate, rules (i) limiting the number of times a
23 patient can be treated by a pharmacist in a given time span and (ii) creating an
24 audit mechanism to enforce those rules.
25 (4) Patient parameters necessitating referral to a primary, urgent, or emergency
26 care provider.
27 (5) Any other rules the Boards deem necessary.
28 SECTION 5. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
29 law.
