3 SECTION 1.(c) This section becomes effective October 1, 2025.
4 SECTION 2.(a) Article 3 of Chapter 58 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
5 a new section to read:
6 "§ 58-3-241. Healthcare services provided by pharmacists.
7 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
8 (1) Healthcare provider. – Either of the following:
9 a. An individual who is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized under
10 Chapter 90 of the General Statutes to provide healthcare services in
11 the ordinary course of business or practice of a profession or in an
12 approved education or training program.
13 b. A health care facility licensed under Chapter 131E or Chapter 122C of
14 the General Statutes and where healthcare services are provided to
15 patients.
16 (2) Healthcare services. – Any of the following health or medical procedures or
17 services rendered by a healthcare provider:
18 a. Testing, diagnosis, or treatment of a health condition, illness, injury,
19 or disease. This includes testing, diagnosis, or treatment rendered by a
20 pharmacist acting within the pharmacist's scope of practice.
21 b. Dispensing of drugs, medical devices, medical appliances, or medical
22 goods for the treatment of a health condition, illness, injury, or disease.
23 c. Administration of a vaccine or medication.
24 (3) Pharmacist. – An individual licensed to practice pharmacy under Article 4A
25 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes or the relevant laws of another state.
26 (b) A health benefit plan offered by an insurer in this State shall cover healthcare services
27 provided by a pharmacist if all of the following conditions are met:
28 (1) The service or procedure was performed within the pharmacist's licensed
29 lawful scope of practice.
30 (2) The health benefit plan would have covered the service if the service or
31 procedure had been performed by another healthcare provider.
32 (c) The participation of a pharmacy in a drug benefit provider network of a health benefit
33 plan shall not satisfy any requirement that insurers offering health benefit plans include
34 pharmacists in medical benefit provider networks."
35 SECTION 2.(b) G.S. 58-3-230 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
36 "(d) Insurers that delegate credentialing agreements or requirements for pharmacists
37 licensed under Article 4A of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes or the relevant laws of another
38 state to a contracted healthcare facility shall accept the credentialing for all pharmacists employed
39 by, or contracted with, those healthcare facilities."
40 SECTION 2.(c) G.S. 58-3-200(d) reads as rewritten:
41 "(d) Services Outside Provider Networks. – No insurer shall penalize an insured or subject
42 an insured to the out-of-network benefit levels offered under the insured's approved health benefit
43 plan, including an insured receiving an extended or standing referral under G.S. 58-3-223, unless
44 contracting health care providers able to meet health needs of the insured are reasonably available
45 to the insured without unreasonable delay. Upon notice or request from the insured, the insurer
46 shall determine whether a health care provider able to meet the needs of the insured is available
47 to the insured without unreasonable delay by reference to the insured's location and the specific
48 medical needs of the insured."
