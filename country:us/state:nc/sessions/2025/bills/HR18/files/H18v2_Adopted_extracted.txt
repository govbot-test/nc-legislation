Resolution

HOUSE RESOLUTION 18
Adopted 1/29/25
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Calendar 1/29/2025
January 29, 2025
1 A HOUSE RESOLUTION HONORING THE LIFE AND MEMORY OF JOSEPH "JOE"
2 ROBERT JOHN, SR., LONGTIME MEMBER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
3 IN THE NORTH CAROLINA GENERAL ASSEMBLY.
4 Whereas, Joseph "Joe" Robert John, Sr., was born on Thursday, October 12, 1939, to
5 Joseph Andrew John and Stephanie Ann John in East Chicago, Indiana; and
6 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., received a bachelor's degree in English with a
7 minor in Political Science, a master's degree in Comparative Literature, and a Juris Doctorate
8 from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill; and
9 Whereas, upon completing his education and becoming a three-time alumnus of the
10 University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Joseph Robert John, Sr., remained an avid, lifelong
11 fan of the University of North Carolina Tar Heels, celebrating many Tar Heel victories in Kenan
12 Stadium and the Dean Dome; and
13 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., assisted indigent clients upon assuming his first
14 position as a lawyer with the Greensboro Legal Aid Foundation, before subsequently working as
15 an Assistant District Attorney and Chief Assistant District Attorney, and as a litigation attorney
16 for Pell, Pell, Weston and John; and
17 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., had a highly distinguished career in public service
18 which spanned the highest levels of the government of North Carolina across all three branches,
19 serving as Director of the State Crime Lab, Acting Commissioner of the North Carolina
20 Department of Motor Vehicles, and Judge on the North Carolina Court of Appeals; and
21 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., was a highly capable public servant who dedicated
22 his time, energy, and talents to the betterment of the State, representing North Carolina House
23 District 40 in Wake County in the North Carolina House of Representatives from 2017 to 2025;
24 and
25 Whereas, while serving in the legislature, Joseph Robert John, Sr., focused on
26 Criminal Justice, Judicial Elections, Public Transportation, and Education. He served on
27 numerous committees, including Appropriations; Appropriations – Justice and Public Safety;
28 Education – Community Colleges; Election Law and Campaign Finance Reform; Judiciary 3;
29 Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House; and Transportation; and
30 Whereas, while serving in the legislature, Joseph Robert John, Sr., participated in
31 various caucuses across several different areas of interest, including the Bipartisan Safety and
32 Criminal Justice Caucus, the Early Childhood Caucus, the Economic Development and Foreign
33 Trade Caucus, the Joint Caucus on Arts and Arts Education, and the Life Science Caucus; and
*H18-v-2*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 Whereas, among his many contributions to improving the State of North Carolina,
2 Joseph Robert John, Sr., served on the North Carolina Courts Commission and was appointed to
3 chair the Commission by Governor Roy Cooper; and
4 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., was an effective advocate for several important
5 election initiatives, including filing bills to create a nonpartisan redistricting commission and
6 nonpartisan judicial elections; and
7 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., was a primary sponsor of numerous bills that
8 became law and have a significant impact on the workings of the judiciary and the criminal justice
9 system; and
10 Whereas, the colleagues of Joseph Robert John, Sr., in the House of Representatives
11 held him in the highest regard, listening to and depending on his wisdom and experience to seek
12 to improve the workings of the judiciary and the criminal justice system; and
13 Whereas, in the eight years he represented House District 40, Joseph Robert John, Sr.,
14 consistently demonstrated a deep commitment to serving the people of North Carolina and held
15 the service of his constituents as a top priority, tirelessly advocating for their well-being—one of
16 the many qualities that made him a much beloved legislator by his colleagues and the hundreds
17 of thousands of people that he helped; and
18 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., has been a champion of justice and equity, standing
19 firm in his dedication to ethical governance and the principles of the rule of law; and
20 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., upheld the highest standards of dignity and respect
21 in public service, conducting himself with grace and humility throughout his public service
22 career; and
23 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., provided thoughtful and principled leadership,
24 using his extensive knowledge and wisdom to address the most pressing challenges facing the
25 State; and
26 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., earned the trust and admiration of his colleagues
27 and constituents through his unwavering integrity; and
28 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., served as an active member of the North Carolina
29 and Wake County Bar Associations and served on the boards of numerous community
30 organizations, including the Leesville Road High School Pride Athletic Association, the
31 Greensboro Center for Creative Arts, Family and Children's Services of Greater Greensboro, and
32 the North Carolina Forensic Science Advisory Board; and
33 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., was honored by the North Carolina State Bar with
34 the "Fifty Years of Service" award; and
35 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., was recognized for significant contributions to his
36 State and community, receiving the highest civilian honor, the Order of the Long Leaf Pine award
37 from Governor Jim Hunt; and
38 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., passed away on Monday, January 20, 2025, after
39 a lifetime of working on behalf of the people of his community and the State; and
40 Whereas, Governor Josh Stein proclaimed that the flags of North Carolina be lowered
41 to half-staff on Thursday, January 30, 2025, in mourning for and recognition of the exemplary
42 life of Joseph Robert John, Sr.; and
43 Whereas, Joseph Robert John, Sr., is survived by a devoted family, including his wife
44 of 42 years, Evelyn John; his children and their spouses, Steffi John Briley, Joseph Andrew
45 "Andy" John II and his wife Allison, and Joseph "Joe" Robert John, Jr., and his wife Julie; his
46 grandchildren, Lessie Ann, Annabelle, Aden, Ella, Remy, Dagny, and Samantha; his siblings,
47 Andrea Webb and her husband Gary, and their three sons, and the Hon. Steven John and his wife
48 Susan, and their son; and several other close family members; Now, therefore,
49 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives:
Page 2 House Resolution 18
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 1. The House of Representatives recognizes and expresses its
2 appreciation for the public service rendered by Joseph "Joe" Robert John, Sr., which has
3 contributed to the betterment of the State of North Carolina.
4 SECTION 2. The House of Representatives extends its sincerest sympathy to the
5 family of Joseph "Joe" Robert John, Sr., in the loss of one of its most distinguished and
6 outstanding members.
7 SECTION 3. The Principal Clerk shall transmit a copy of this resolution to the family
8 of Joseph "Joe" Robert John, Sr.
9 SECTION 4. This resolution is effective upon adoption.
