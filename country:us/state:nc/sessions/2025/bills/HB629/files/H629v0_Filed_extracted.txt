1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO EXTEND AND CLARIFY THE PRIMARY CARE PAYMENT REFORM TASK
3 FORCE.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Section 9E.28 of S.L. 2023-134 reads as rewritten:
6 "SECTION 9E.28.(a) There is established the North Carolina Primary Care Payment
7 Reform Task Force (Task Force) within the Department of Health and Human Services, Division
8 of Health Benefits, for budgetary purposes only.
9 …
10 "SECTION 9E.28.(b) The Task Force established under subsection (a) of this section shall
11 have the following duties:
12 (1) Establish a definition of primary care to be utilized by the Task Force. This
13 term should be applicable to services and care provided under the NC
14 Medicaid program, the State Health Plan, and commercial insurance.
15 (2) Conduct an actuarial evaluation of the current healthcare spend on primary
16 care services, both as it relates to the NC Medicaid program and the
17 commercial market, including Medicare Advantage plans.
18 (3) Determine the adequacy of the primary care delivery system in North
19 Carolina, including the impact this system has on the supply of the primary
20 care providers in this State.
21 (4) Study the primary care payment landscape in other states, specifically
22 considering states that have implemented a minimum primary care spend.
23 (5) Identify data collection and measurement systems to inform creation of a
24 primary care investment target for the NC Medicaid program, the State Health
25 Plan, and commercial insurance. This includes a method by which to measure
26 improvements made toward that target.
27 (5a) Collect and compile data and other information related to healthcare spend on
28 primary care services in a manner that is compliant with the Health Insurance
29 Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA). Within 30 days of a
30 request for data or information from the Task Force, all entities shall comply
31 with the Task Force's request.
32 (6) Evaluate the need for a permanent Primary Care Payment Reform Task Force,
33 or other similar entity, including which State agency or body is best suited to
34 oversee the work of that group.
35 (7) Perform any other studies, evaluations, or determinations the Task Force
36 considers necessary.

1 "SECTION 9E.28.(b1) The Department of Health and Human Services shall develop, and
2 the Task Force and the Department of Health and Human Services shall implement, a detailed

4 section that includes all of the following:
5 (1) Guidelines for authorizing access to the data, including guidelines for
6 authentication of authorized access.
7 (2) Privacy compliance standards.
8 (3) Privacy and security audits.
9 (4) Breach planning, notification, and procedures.
10 (5) Data retention and disposition policies.
11 (6) Data security policies, including electronic, physical, and administrative
12 safeguards such as data encryption and training of employees.
13 "SECTION 9E.28.(b2) The data collected by the Task Force under subsection (b) of this
14 section, regardless of where it is housed, shall not be considered a public record within the
15 meaning of Chapter 132 of the General Statutes.
16 "SECTION 9E.28.(c) No later than April 1, 2024, and April 1, 2026, the Task Force shall
17 submit a report with its findings and recommendations to the Joint Legislative Oversight
18 Committee on Health and Human Services and the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on
19 Medicaid. These findings and recommendations shall include specific, concrete, and actionable
20 steps to be undertaken by the State and upon which the General Assembly could act.
21 "SECTION 9E.28.(d) This section shall expire on May 1, 2024.December 31, 2026."
22 SECTION 2. This act is effective retroactive to July 1, 2023.
