5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 115C-84.2(d) reads as rewritten:
7 "(d) Opening and Closing Dates. – Local boards of education shall determine the dates of
8 opening and closing the public schools under subdivision (a)(1) of this section. Except for
9 year-round schools, the opening date for students shall be no earlier than the Monday closest to
10 August 26, August 11, and the closing date for students shall be no later than the Friday closest
11 to June 11. On a showing of good cause, the State Board of Education may waive the requirement
12 that the opening date for students be no earlier than the Monday closest to August 26 and may
13 allow the local board of education to set an opening date no earlier than the Monday closest to
14 August 19, to the extent that school calendars are able to provide sufficient days to accommodate
15 anticipated makeup days due to school closings. A local board may revise the scheduled closing
16 date if necessary in order to comply with the minimum requirements for instructional days or
17 instructional time. For purposes of this subsection, the term "good cause" means that schools in
18 any local school administrative unit in a county have been closed eight days per year during any
19 four of the last 10 years because of severe weather conditions, energy shortages, power failures,
20 or other emergency situations.
21 The required opening and closing dates under this subsection shall not apply to any school
22 that a local board designated as having a modified calendar for the 2003-2004 school year or to
23 any school that was part of a planned program in the 2003-2004 school year for a system of
24 modified calendar schools, so long as the school operates under a modified calendar."
25 SECTION 2. G.S. 115C-174.12(a)(4) reads as rewritten:
26 "(4) All annual assessments of student achievement adopted by the State Board of
27 Education pursuant to G.S. 115C-174.11(c)(1) and (3) and all final exams for
28 courses shall be administered within the final 10 instructional days of the
29 school year for year-long courses and within the final five instructional days
30 of the semester for semester courses. Exceptions shall be permitted to
31 accommodate a student's individualized education program and section 504
32 (29 U.S.C. § 794) plans and for the administration of final exams for courses
33 with national or international curricula required to be held at designated times.
34 A local board of education that has implemented a school calendar that
35 concludes the fall semester prior to December 31 shall be permitted to
36 administer assessments prior to the conclusion of that semester."
