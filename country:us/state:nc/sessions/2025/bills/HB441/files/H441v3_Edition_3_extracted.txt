9 SHRIMP TRAWL TRANSITION PAYMENT PROGRAM
10 SECTION 1.(a) Shrimp Trawl Transition Program. – The Shrimp Trawl Transition
11 Program (Program) is established within the Department of Environmental Quality's Division of
12 Marine Fisheries (Division). Funding for the Program shall come from the North Carolina
13 Commercial Fishing Resource Fund and the North Carolina Marine Resources Fund. The
14 Division may use up to two million dollars ($2,000,000) annually from the cash balance of the
15 North Carolina Marine Resource Endowment Fund to address shortfalls in making Program
16 payments. The Program shall be used to provide annual transition payments to eligible holders
17 of commercial fishing licenses with verifiable inshore landings of shrimp between January 1,
18 2023, and June 30, 2025. The Program shall expire October 1, 2028. To be eligible for a transition
19 payment, a person must satisfy all of the following criteria:
20 (1) The person held a Standard Commercial Fishing License or a Retired Standard
21 Commercial Fishing License at any time between January 1, 2023, and June
22 30, 2025.
23 (2) The person reported on trip tickets submitted to the Division of Marine
24 Fisheries the sale of brown shrimp, pink shrimp, or white shrimp caught in
25 inshore waters with a trawl net between January 1, 2023, and June 30, 2025.
26 SECTION 1.(b) Retained Eligibility. – A person who holds a Standard Commercial
27 Fishing License or a Retired Standard Commercial Fishing License who harvests shrimp using
28 legal commercial gear within the area described in G.S. 113-187(d)(3a) or using any gear,
29 including trawl nets, outside of the area described in G.S. 113-187(d)(3a), may still be eligible
30 for a transition payment from the Program, provided the person otherwise meets the criteria in
31 subsection (a) of this section.
32 SECTION 1.(c) Documentation. – A person seeking a transition payment pursuant
33 to the Program shall submit documentation verifying that the person meets the criteria in
34 subsection (a) of this section, including copies of all trip tickets submitted for brown shrimp, pink

3 SECTION 1.(d) Program Payment Criteria. – The Division shall administer the
4 Program in accordance with the following criteria:
5 (1) The annual transition payment amount awarded to a Program applicant shall
6 be set at the sum of (i) the average monthly value of the eligible shrimp trip
7 tickets submitted by the applicant under subsection (a) of this section
8 multiplied by 12 and (ii) one hundred eighty dollars ($180.00). For applicants
9 that hold more than one Standard Commercial Fishing License or Retired
10 Standard Commercial Fishing License, the annual transition payment amount
11 awarded shall be set at the sum of (i) the average monthly value of the eligible
12 shrimp trip tickets submitted by the applicant under subsection (a) of this
13 section associated with the license with the highest reported shrimp landings
14 multiplied by 12 and (ii) one hundred eighty dollars ($180.00). For the
15 purposes of this subdivision, the "value of the eligible shrimp tickets" means
16 the value of shrimp caught by trawl net in any inshore waterbody between
17 January 1, 2023 and June 30, 2025, but shall not include any values reported
18 on shrimp trip tickets where most of the catch occurred in the ocean as
19 reported on trip tickets submitted to the Division.
20 (2) The Division shall gather all trip ticket information from Program applicants
21 no later than December 1, 2025. If the total of Program payments calculated
22 and verified by the Division for each Program applicant is greater than the
23 funds available for the Program in the North Carolina Commercial Fishing
24 Resource Fund and the North Carolina Marine Resources Fund, then the
25 Division shall provide to each eligible applicant a portion of the Program
26 payments by allocating the funds available in proportion to the Program
27 payments documented by each applicant.
28 SECTION 1.(e) Reporting. – The Department shall submit a report to the Joint
29 Legislative Oversight Committee on Agriculture and Natural and Economic Resources and the
30 Fiscal Research Division no later than October 1, 2025, and annually thereafter until all funds
31 are expended or the Program expires containing, at a minimum, all of the following data:
32 (1) The number of Program applicants.
33 (2) The number and amount of transition payments awarded.
34 (3) The total amount of funding available to the Program, the total amount
35 encumbered, and the total amount disbursed to date.
36 SECTION 1.(f) Effective Date and Expiration. – If House Bill 442, 2025 Regular
37 Session, becomes law, then this section is effective when it becomes law and expires October 1,
38 2028.
