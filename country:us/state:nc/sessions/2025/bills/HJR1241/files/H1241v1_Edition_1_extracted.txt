1 A JOINT RESOLUTION HONORING THE LIFE AND MEMORY OF LOUIS MILFORD
2 PATE, JR., SENATOR AND FORMER MEMBER OF THE HOUSE OF
3 REPRESENTATIVES.
4 Whereas, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., was born September 22, 1936, to Mary Best Pate
5 and Louis Pate, Sr., in Duplin County, North Carolina, and was raised in Virginia; and
6 Whereas, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., graduated from Hopewell High School; and
7 Whereas, in 1962, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., enlisted in the United States Air Force and
8 was stationed at Seymour Johnson Air Force Base in Goldsboro, North Carolina, and it was
9 during his time at Seymour Johnson that he met Joyce Cameron Garner, who would become his
10 beloved wife for 59 years; and
11 Whereas, during a distinguished 20-year military career, Louis Milford Pate, Jr.,
12 served as a B-57 navigator during the Vietnam War and later served as a B-52 and F-111
13 navigator for later missions while stationed at Florida, Nevada, and the United Kingdom; and
14 Whereas, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., served his nation with distinction earning various
15 awards throughout his military career such as Distinguished Flying Cross, Air Medal with three
16 bronze oak leaves, Air Force Commendation Medal, Combat Readiness Medal, and Air Force
17 Good Conduct Medal; and
18 Whereas, while serving his country, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., remained committed to
19 his family and education, helping raise three children while earning a bachelor's degree in
20 business administration and a Master of Business Administration degree from Golden Gate
21 University; and
22 Whereas, following his retirement from the Air Force, Louis Milford Pate, Jr.,
23 returned to Mount Olive, joined Garner Brothers Building Supply, and continued his lifelong
24 commitment to public service within his community by serving on the Mount Olive Town
25 Council before being elected Mayor of Mount Olive from 1991 to 1994 and again from 1999 to
26 2002; and
27 Whereas, in 1995, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., was elected to the North Carolina House
28 of Representatives, representing portions of Lenoir and Wayne Counties in the 11th House
29 District, and later returned to the House of Representatives in 2003, serving until 2009; and
30 Whereas, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., was elected to the North Carolina Senate and
31 represented the 5th Senate District from 2011 to 2019, including service as Deputy President Pro
32 Tempore from 2013 until his retirement in 2019; and
33 Whereas, beyond his public service, Louis Milford Pate, Jr., was a devoted member
34 of First Baptist Church of Mount Olive, an active participant in the Rotary Club, the Wayne
35 Oratorio Society, and the Third Century Singers, reflecting his lifelong faith and deep
36 appreciation for music and community; and
