5 PROHIBIT NORTH CAROLINA LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCIES AND OFFICERS
6 FROM ASSISTING IMMIGRATION AND CUSTOMS ENFORCEMENT
7 SECTION 1.(a) Chapter 17F of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
8 section to read:
9 "§ 17F-15. Prohibit law enforcement agencies and officers from participating in
10 immigration enforcement.
11 (a) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, no criminal justice agency, as
12 defined by G.S. 17C-2, or sheriff's office shall assist United States Immigration and Customs
13 Enforcement in the (i) apprehension or arrest of persons for alleged or suspected immigration
14 violations or (ii) service of warrants for removal from the United States in any of the following
15 locations.
16 (b) Any current or future memorandum, agreement, or contract made between a criminal
17 justice agency or sheriff's office and United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement shall
18 be void to the extent that the performance of the memorandum, agreement, or contract would
19 violate subsection (a) of this section.
20 (c) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, no criminal justice officer, as
21 defined by G.S. 17C-2, or justice officer, as defined by G.S. 17E-2, shall do any of the following:
22 (1) Assist United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement in the (i)
23 apprehension or arrest of persons for alleged or suspected immigration
24 violations or (ii) service of warrants for removal from the United States.
25 (2) Be deputized by United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement for any
26 of the actions referenced in subdivision (1) of this subsection."
27 SECTION 1.(b) The Criminal Justice Education and Training Standards
28 Commission and the Sheriffs' Education and Training Standards Commission shall promulgate
29 rules consistent with G.S. 17F-15, as created by this section.
30 SECTION 1.(c) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies to
31 assistance provided on or after that date.

16 UPHOLD PROMISES OF FEDERAL AGENCIES TO NORTH CAROLINIANS
17 SECTION 3. It is the intent of the General Assembly that the State of North Carolina,
18 including its agencies, departments, divisions, counties, and municipalities, shall take every
19 action necessary to keep the promises of federal agencies to working North Carolinians.
20
21 KEEP NORTH CAROLINA CITIZENS ELIGIBLE FOR MEDICAID
22 SECTION 4. It is the intent of the General Assembly to ensure that benefits through
23 the North Carolina Medicaid Program remain available to eligible North Carolina citizens
24 regardless of any federal action.
25
26 CONSIDER SUPPLEMENTAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH FUNDING
27 SECTION 5.(a) The Department of Health and Human Services shall consider
28 alternatives to decreased or terminated federal funding related at a minimum to all of the
29 following:
30 (1) State biomedical initiatives.
31 (2) State health initiatives.
32 (3) State biomedical and health-related research.
33 SECTION 5.(b) No later than March 1, 2026, the Department of Health and Human
34 Services shall report to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services
35 regarding any of its findings and recommendations made pursuant to the requirements of
36 subsection (a) of this section.
37
38 STATEWIDE STUDY AND REPORT ON FUNDS ELIMINATED BY THE
39 DEPARTMENT OF GOVERNMENT EFFICIENCY
40 SECTION 6.(a) No later than March 15, 2026, the State Board of Education, the
41 Board of Governors of the University of North Carolina, and the State Board of Community
42 colleges shall study and separately report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee
43 on federal funds provided for education that have been eliminated by the federal Department of
44 Government Efficiency and any actions that have already been taken or that need to be taken to
45 replace those funds or, if the funds cannot be replaced, to achieve the same purposes for which
46 those funds were provided.
47 SECTION 6.(b) At a minimum, the reports required by this section shall include
48 each of the following:
49 (1) An estimate of the total cost of reduced, suspended, or cancelled grants from
50 the National Institutes of Health to the in-state operations of entities registers,
51 headquartered, or doing business in the State.
