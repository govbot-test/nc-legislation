6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. Chapter 64 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
8 to read:
9 "Article 4.
10 "Prohibit Adversarial Foreign Government Acquisition of Certain Lands.
11 "§ 64-60. Title.
12 This act shall be known and be cited as the North Carolina Farmland and Military Protection
13 Act.
14 "§ 64-61. Purpose.
15 The General Assembly finds that it is in the public interest for the State to exercise its
16 governmental police power to guard its agricultural land from the potential of adversarial foreign
17 government control in order to ensure that the State's farmers are able to produce a safe, abundant,
18 and affordable supply of food and fiber for the benefit of the people of this State and the United
19 States and to protect our vital resources.
20 "§ 64-62. Definitions.
21 As used in this Article, the following definitions apply:
22 (1) Adversarial foreign government. – A state-controlled enterprise or the
23 government of a country or group subject to International Traffic in Arms
24 Regulations in 22 C.F.R. § 126. This term does not include either of the
25 following:
26 a. An entity that has received a determination from the Committee on
27 Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS) that there are no
28 unresolved national security concerns with respect to the entity in
29 connection to a matter submitted to CFIUS and which CFIUS
30 concluded all action pursuant to section 721 of the Defense Production
31 Act of 1950.
32 b. An entity that has a national security agreement with CFIUS and
33 maintains the validity of such national security agreement.
34 (2) Agricultural land. – Any real property situated in this State that is used for
35 agricultural production purposes as defined in G.S. 106-581.1(1) through (4).
36 The term does not include real property situated in this State that is leased for

5 (3) Controlling interest. – Possession of more than fifty percent (50%) of the

7 percent (50%) or less of the ownership interest in an entity if an owner directs
8 the business and affairs of the entity without the requirement or consent of any

10 (4) Interest. – Any estate, remainder, or reversion, or any portion of the estate,
11 remainder, or reversion, or an option pursuant to which one party has a right
12 to cause the transfer of legal or equitable title to agricultural land or real
13 property situated within a 75-mile radius of a military installation. The term
14 does not include a noteholder under an obligation secured by a deed of trust.
15 (5) Military installation. – Fort Bragg, Pope Army Airfield, Marine Corps Base
16 Camp Lejeune, New River Marine Corps Air Station, Cherry Point Marine
17 Corps Air Station, Military Ocean Terminal at Sunny Point, the United States
18 Coast Guard Air Station at Elizabeth City, Naval Support Activity Northwest,
19 Blakeslee Air Force Recreation Area, Air Route Surveillance Radar
20 (ARSR-4) at Fort Fisher, and Seymour Johnson Air Force Base, in its own
21 right and as the responsible entity for the Dare County Bombing Range, North
22 Carolina National Guard facilities, and any facility located within the State
23 that is subject to the installations' oversight and control.
24 (6) State-controlled enterprise. – A business enterprise, however denominated, in
25 which a foreign government has a controlling interest.
26 "§ 64-63. Adversarial foreign government acquisition of agricultural land and real
27 property prohibited.
28 (a) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, no adversarial foreign
29 government shall purchase, acquire, lease, or hold any interest in the following:
30 (1) Agricultural land.
31 (2) Real property situated within a 75-mile radius of a military installation.
32 (b) Any interest in agricultural land or real property situated within a 75-mile radius of a
33 military installation purchased, acquired, leased, or held in violation of this section shall be
34 subject to divestiture pursuant to G.S. 64-64.
35 (c) A violation of this section by an adversarial foreign government may, at the discretion
36 of the noteholder, be deemed a default under a loan, mortgage, or deed of trust and shall provide
37 the lender the automatic right to trigger default on the loan, mortgage, or deed of trust.
38 (d) The responsibility for determining whether an individual or other entity is subject to
39 this Article rests solely with the adversarial foreign government and the State of North Carolina
40 and no other individual or entity. An individual or other entity who is not an adversarial foreign
41 government shall bear no civil or criminal liability for failing to determine or make inquiry of
42 whether an individual or other entity is an adversarial foreign government.
43 (e) No title to real property shall be invalid or subject to divesture by reason of a violation
44 of this section by any former owner or the individual or entity holding or owning a former interest
45 in the real property.
46 (f) This Article does not create or authorize a private right of action to enforce the
47 provision of the Article.
48 "§ 64-64. Divestiture procedure.
49 (a) Upon receipt of information that leads the Attorney General to believe that a violation
50 of G.S. 64-63 may have occurred, the Attorney General shall investigate the alleged violation
51 and may issue subpoenas requiring any of the following:

4 (b) The Attorney General shall enforce a violation of G.S. 64-63 by commencing a
5 receivership proceeding under Article 38A of Chapter 1 of the General Statutes seeking the
6 appointment of a general receiver pursuant to G.S. 1-507.24(e1). The following apply to a
7 receivership proceeding initiated pursuant to this section:
8 (1) Proceeds of the sale shall be paid as follows:
9 a. The costs of the receivership and sale.
10 b. To secured parties, in their order of priority, except for liens which
11 under the terms of the sale are to remain on the property.

15 accordance with G.S. 115C-457.2.
16 (2) At the receivership sale, any secured party shall be able to place a bid in an
17 amount that is not more than the amount owed plus any costs incurred to the
18 secured party as of the date of the sale, as established in the court order for the
19 sale of the property.
20 (3) Upon commencement of an action under this section, the Attorney General
21 shall file a notice of lis pendens as soon as practicable with the register of
22 deeds of the county or counties in which the real property is situated. Upon
23 the entry of an order for the sale of the property under this section, the
24 Attorney General shall record a copy of the order as soon as practicable in the
25 office of the register of deeds of the county or counties where the real property
26 is situated."
