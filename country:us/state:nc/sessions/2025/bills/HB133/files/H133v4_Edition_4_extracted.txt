10 "Article 4.
11 "Prohibit Adversarial Foreign Party Acquisition of Certain Lands.
12 "§ 64-60. Title.
13 This act shall be known and be cited as the North Carolina Farmland and Military Protection
14 Act.
15 "§ 64-61. Purpose.
16 The General Assembly finds that it is in the public interest for the State to exercise its
17 governmental police power to guard its agricultural land and military installations from the
18 potential of adversarial foreign government control in order to ensure that the State's farmers are
19 able to produce a safe, abundant, and affordable supply of food and fiber and to protect our
20 national security for the benefit of the people of this State and the United States.
21 "§ 64-62. Definitions.
22 As used in this Article, the following definitions apply:
23 (1) Adversarial foreign government. – A state-controlled enterprise or the
24 government of a country or group listed in International Traffic in Arms
25 Regulation 22 C.F.R. § 126.1(d).
26 (2) Agricultural land. – Any land situated in this State that is used for agricultural
27 production purposes as defined in G.S. 106-581.1(1) through (4). The term
28 does not include land situated in this State that is leased for agricultural
29 research and development purposes or other activities for the purpose of
30 producing inputs or products for farmers or other end users, provided that the
31 acreage leased by the lessee does not exceed 250 acres in the aggregate.
32 (3) De minimis direct interest. – Any ownership of land resulting from:

44 "§ 64-63. Prohibited foreign party acquisition of certain lands prohibited.
45 (a) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, no prohibited foreign party
46 shall purchase, acquire, lease, or hold any direct interest in the following:
47 (1) Agricultural land.
48 (2) Property situated within a 50-mile radius of a military installation.
49 (b) A prohibited foreign party shall not acquire by grant, purchase, devise, descent, or
50 otherwise any interest, other than a de minimis direct interest, in land described in subsection (a)
51 of this section in this State regardless of how the prohibited foreign party intends to use the land.

27 "§ 64-64. Divestiture procedure.
28 (a) Upon receipt of information that leads the Attorney General to believe that a violation
29 of G.S. 64-63 may have occurred, the Attorney General shall investigate the alleged violation
30 and may issue subpoenas requiring any of the following:
31 (1) Appearances of witnesses.
32 (2) Production of relevant records.
33 (3) Giving of relevant testimony.
34 (b) The Attorney General shall enforce a violation of G.S. 64-63 by commencing a
35 receivership proceeding under Article 38A of Chapter 1 of the General Statutes seeking the
36 appointment of a general receiver pursuant to G.S. 1-507.24(e1). The following apply to a
37 receivership proceeding initiated pursuant to this section:
38 (1) Proceeds of the sale shall be paid as follows:
39 a. The costs of the receivership and sale.
40 b. To secured parties, in their order of priority, except for liens which
41 under the terms of the sale are to remain on the property.
42 c. No proceeds shall be distributed from the receivership sale to the
43 prohibited foreign party. Any excess proceeds are forfeited and shall
44 be remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with
45 G.S. 115C-457.2.
46 (2) At the receivership sale, any secured party shall be able to place a bid in an
47 amount that is not more than the amount owed plus any costs incurred to the
48 secured party as of the date of the sale, as established in the court order for the
49 sale of the property.
50 (3) Upon commencement of an action under this section, the Attorney General
51 shall file a notice of lis pendens as soon as practicable with the register of

9 SECTION 2. Article 2 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
10 a new section to read:
11 "§ 161-14.04. Affidavit of parties to a deed or conveyance.
12 When recording a deed or other document that conveys an ownership interest in land
13 described by G.S. 64-63(a), the register of deeds shall record the affidavit as an exhibit to the
14 deed or other document that conveys an ownership interest in land as required by G.S. 64-63(f)
15 according to the requirements of G.S. 161-22. Notwithstanding G.S. 161-10, the affidavit shall
16 not be considered an additional instrument. Attachment of the affidavit required by G.S. 64-63(f)
17 is the presenter's responsibility and not a recording requirement to be enforced by the register of
18 deeds. The register of deeds shall not be liable for failure of the document to include the affidavit.
19 Failure to attach the affidavit does not impair constructive notice, lien priority, validity, or
20 insurability."
21 SECTION 3. G.S. 1-507.24 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
22 "(e1) Receiver for Sale of Real Property Owned by Prohibited Foreign Party. – A general
23 receiver may be appointed for the purpose of conducting a sale of real property in accordance
24 with G.S. 64-64 upon a finding by the court that an interest in the real property is held by a
25 prohibited foreign party in violation of G.S. 64-63(a)."
26 SECTION 4. The North Carolina Real Estate Commission, in coordination with the
27 Department of Military and Veterans Affairs, will develop and publish the map described in
28 G.S. 64-62(6) by April 1, 2027, and shall maintain the map by publishing an updated version
29 annually each April 1 thereafter.
30 SECTION 5. If any section or provision of this act is declared unconstitutional or
