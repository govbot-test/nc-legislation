2 PART II. EXPAND NC PRE-K PROGRAM
3 SECTION 2.1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
4 Health and Human Services, Division of Child Development and Early Education, the sum of
5 two hundred million dollars ($200,000,000) in recurring funds for each year of the 2025-2027

7 number of slots by 32,000 and cover all eligible children 4 years of age.

9 PART III. INCREASE SUBSIDIZED CHILD CARE FUNDING

12 thirty-five million dollars ($35,000,000) in recurring funds for each year of the 2025-2027 fiscal
13 biennium to increase funding for subsidized child care.

15 PART IV. FUND PUBLIC SCHOOL LUNCHES
16 SECTION 4.1. G.S. 115C-263 reads as rewritten:
17 "§ 115C-263. School nutrition services.
18 (a) As a part of the function of the public school system, local boards of education public
19 school units shall have a school food authority to provide to the extent practicable school nutrition
20 services in the schools under their jurisdiction. jurisdiction by offering students lunch at no cost
21 to the student. A school food authority may offer students a breakfast option at a cost to the
22 student, unless the student is eligible for the School Breakfast Program. All school nutrition
23 services made available under this authority shall be provided in accordance with federal
24 guidelines established by the Food and Nutrition Service of the United States Department of
25 Agriculture.
26 (b) The To the extent that funds are made available for the purpose, the State Board of
27 Education shall allocate funds to school food authorities at public school units for the purpose of
28 providing healthy lunches at no cost to students. In issuing the allocation, the Board shall do the
29 following:
30 (1) Determine the amount to allocate to each school food authority based on an
31 evaluation of the authority's nutrition services. The Board shall develop the
32 method and criteria for the evaluation, which at a minimum shall account for
33 the following:
34 a. The size of the schools served by the school food authority as
35 determined by Average Daily Membership.
36 b. The number of students that are eligible for free or reduced-price lunch
37 and the estimated receipts from the National School Lunch Program.
38 c. The funds actually expended in the prior year, which may be waived
39 if the school food authority was not in operation in the year prior.
40 d. The extent to which the nutrition services promote health and wellness
41 by providing food that is of high quality and nutrient content.
42 e. The extent to which the school food authority uses locally sourced
43 food and products.
44 f. Any other information the Board deems relevant to the cost and
45 operation of the nutrition services.
46 (2) Ensure the funds are distributed on a fair and equitable basis.
47 (3) Issue the allocation at the beginning of each fiscal year, except that the Board
48 may reserve for future allocation a reasonable amount, not to exceed ten
49 percent (10%) of the total funds available for providing nutrition services.
50 (c) The State Board of Education may adopt rules regulating the provision of school
51 nutrition that impose additional restrictions that are not inconsistent with the federal guidelines.

34 Public Instruction the sum of one hundred fifteen million dollars ($115,000,000) in recurring
35 funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to provide lunch to students in public school units at no cost
36 to the students in accordance with this section. Should this appropriation not be sufficient to
37 fulfill the provisions of this section for the 2025-2026 fiscal year, the State Board of Education
38 may use funds appropriated to State Aid for Public Schools for this purpose.
39 SECTION 4.8. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
40 Public Instruction the sum of three million one hundred sixty-six thousand six hundred dollars
41 ($3,166,600) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to satisfy any outstanding
42 school nutrition program debt. Should this appropriation not be sufficient to satisfy all school
43 nutrition program debt by the end of the 2025-2026 fiscal year, the State Board of Education
44 shall use funds appropriated to State Aid for Public Schools for this purpose.

46 rules to enact this section until permanent rules are adopted.

48 PART V. FUNDS FOR PUBLIC CHILD CARE PROVIDED BY COMMUNITY
49 COLLEGES
50 SECTION 5.1. Appropriation. – There is appropriated from the General Fund to the
51 Community Colleges System Office the sum of ten million dollars ($10,000,000) in nonrecurring
Page 4 DRH30146-LUfa-3
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be allocated in accordance with this section among
2 community colleges with an active child care program that is available to the public to support
3 and maintain that program. The State Board of Community Colleges shall adopt a formula for
4 allocating these funds. Funds shall be allocated based on the number of children served by the
5 program, and the State Board shall prioritize the award of funds based on the financial needs of
6 the program. These funds shall not revert at the end of the 2025-2026 fiscal year but shall remain
7 available until expended.
8 SECTION 5.2. Report. – Any community college that does not have a publicly
9 available child care program shall report to the State Board of Community Colleges on the
10 feasibility and advisability of implementing a publicly available child care program at that
11 community college, including recommendations for implementing such a child care program,
12 estimated costs, and barriers to implementation. The State Board shall aggregate that information
13 and report it to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee no later than March 31,
14 2026.
15
16 PART VI. REPORT ON HIGH SCHOOL CHILD CARE APPRENTICESHIP
17 PROGRAM
18 SECTION 6.1. No later than December 31, 2026, the Department of Public
19 Instruction, in consultation with each partner agency, shall report to the Joint Legislative
20 Education Oversight Committee on the feasibility and advisability of implementing a child care
21 apprenticeship program in public high schools. The report shall include at least the following
22 information:
23 (1) Estimated costs and a time line for implementing a statewide or regional child
24 care apprenticeship program.
25 (2) Expected student and community impacts of a child care apprenticeship
26 program, including impacts on student performance, attrition, well-being, and
27 any other factor the Department deems relevant to the report.
28 SECTION 6.2. No later than February 28, 2027, the Joint Legislative Education
29 Oversight Committee shall hold a committee meeting and receive an in-person presentation from
30 the Department, in partnership with each partner agency, on the report provided pursuant to
31 Section 6.1 of this act.
32 SECTION 6.3. For purposes of this section, the term "partner agency" shall refer to
33 the Department of Health and Human Services, the Board of Governors of The University of
34 North Carolina, and the State Board of Community Colleges.
35
36 PART VII. EFFECTIVE DATE
37 SECTION 7.1. Part I of this act is effective for taxable years beginning on or after
38 January 1, 2025. The remainder of this act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
