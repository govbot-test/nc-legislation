Short Title: Senior Property Tax Relief Modernization Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Rubin.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MODIFY THE ELDERLY OR DISABLED PROPERTY TAX HOMESTEAD
3 EXCLUSION, TO EXPAND THE PROPERTY TAX HOMESTEAD CIRCUIT BREAKER
4 AND TO REIMBURSE LOCAL GOVERNMENTS FOR THEIR RESULTING REVENUE
5 LOSS, AND TO PROVIDE GRANT FUNDING TO THE NORTH CAROLINA
6 ASSOCIATION OF COUNTY COMMISSIONERS TO SUPPORT MORE FREQUENT
7 PROPERTY TAX REAPPRAISALS.
8 Whereas, property taxes fund essential local services, including public schools, public
9 safety, and infrastructure; and
10 Whereas, rapidly rising home values have increased property tax burdens, particularly
11 for seniors and disabled homeowners living on fixed or limited incomes; and
12 Whereas, current property tax relief programs contain a sharp income cutoff that
13 denies all relief to homeowners just above the eligibility threshold, creating inequitable
14 outcomes; and
15 Whereas, modernizing the homestead exemption to provide a gradual, income-based
16 phaseout will expand access to relief while eliminating an unfair benefits "cliff"; and
17 Whereas, allowing eligible homeowners to combine the homestead exemption with
18 the circuit breaker program will provide more complete and effective protection against
19 unaffordable tax burdens for people on fixed or limited incomes; and
20 Whereas, addressing barriers faced by residents with heirs' property or shared
21 ownership will ensure that relief reaches those who are responsible for maintaining and paying
22 for their homes; and
23 Whereas, reimbursing local governments for the cost of expanded relief will preserve
24 funding for essential local services; Now, therefore,
25 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

17 home, so long as the residence is unoccupied or occupied by the owner's spouse or other

19 (h) Deferred Taxes. – The difference between the taxes due under this section and the
20 taxes that would have been payable in the absence of this section are a lien on the real property
21 of the taxpayer as provided in G.S. 105-355(a). The difference in taxes must be carried forward
22 in the records of each taxing unit as deferred taxes. The deferred taxes for the preceding three
23 fiscal years are due and payable in accordance with G.S. 105-277.1F when the property loses its
24 eligibility for deferral as a result of a disqualifying event described in subsection (i) of this
25 section. On or before September 1 of each year, the collector must send to the mailing address of
26 a residence on which taxes have been deferred a notice stating the amount of deferred taxes and
27 interest that would be due and payable upon the occurrence of a disqualifying event.
28 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the interest rate on liens under this
29 subsection shall be the lesser of (i) the rate established in G.S. 105-360 or (ii) the yield on 10-Year
30 U.S. Treasury Notes as of the date the lien attaches.
31 (i) Disqualifying Events. – Each of the following constitutes a disqualifying event:
32 (1) The owner transfers the residence. Transfer of the residence is not a
33 disqualifying event if (i) the owner transfers the residence to a co-owner of
34 the residence or, as part of a divorce proceeding, to his or her spouse and (ii)
35 that individual occupies or continues to occupy the property as his or her
36 permanent residence.
37 (2) The owner dies. Death of the owner is not a disqualifying event if (i) the
38 owner's share passes to a co-owner of the residence or to his or her spouse and
39 (ii) that individual occupies or continues to occupy the property as his or her
40 permanent residence.
41 (3) The owner ceases to use the property as a permanent residence.
42 (j) Gap in Deferral. – If an owner of a residence on which taxes have been deferred under
43 this section is not eligible for continued deferral for a tax year, the deferred taxes are carried
44 forward and are not due and payable until a disqualifying event occurs. If the owner of the
45 residence qualifies for deferral after one or more years in which he or she did not qualify for
46 deferral and a disqualifying event occurs, the years in which the owner did not qualify are
47 disregarded in determining the preceding three years for which the deferred taxes are due and
48 payable.

26 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the North Carolina
27 Association of County Commissioners ("Association") the nonrecurring sum of twenty million
28 dollars ($20,000,000) for the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be used by the Association to provide
29 grants to local governments for the purpose of transitioning those governments to shortened
30 reappraisal cycles and thereby ensure more frequent and accurate property valuations. Grant
31 funds provided under this section shall be used by local governments for one-time capital
32 investments in technological infrastructure that improve local capacity and efficiency in
33 conducting reappraisals, including the acquisition of specialized software, data migration, and
34 the implementation of digital systems. In awarding grants under this section, the Association
35 shall prioritize awarding grants to local governments operating on a reappraisal cycle of more
36 than four years. For purposes of this section, "local governments" means counties, cities, or towns
37 conducting reappraisals of property under Subchapter II of Chapter 105 of the General Statutes
38 as of the effective date of this section.
39 SECTION 3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
40 Revenue ("Department") the sum of two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($250,000) in
41 nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be used by the Department to study how to
42 efficiently and effectively implement a system of automatic income eligibility verification for
43 applicants for property tax relief under G.S. 105-277.1, 105-277.1B, and 105-277.1C.
44 SECTION 4. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
45 Justice ("Department") the recurring sum of two million dollars ($2,000,000) beginning with the
46 2026-2027 fiscal year to support eight full-time equivalent attorney positions at the Department
47 to assist local governments and the Department of Revenue with property tax appeals cases
48 arising under Chapter 105 of the General Statutes.
49 SECTION 5. Section 1 of this act is effective for taxes imposed for taxable years
50 beginning on or after July 1, 2027. Sections 2, 3, and 4 of this act become effective July 1, 2026.
51 The remainder of this act is effective when it becomes law.

19 (h) Deferred Taxes. – The difference between the taxes due under this section and the

31 (i) Disqualifying Events. – Each of the following constitutes a disqualifying event:

33 disqualifying event if (i) the owner transfers the residence to a co-owner of

35 that individual occupies or continues to occupy the property as his or her
36 permanent residence.
37 (2) The owner dies. Death of the owner is not a disqualifying event if (i) the
38 owner's share passes to a co-owner of the residence or to his or her spouse and
39 (ii) that individual occupies or continues to occupy the property as his or her
40 permanent residence.
41 (3) The owner ceases to use the property as a permanent residence.
42 (j) Gap in Deferral. – If an owner of a residence on which taxes have been deferred under

46 deferral and a disqualifying event occurs, the years in which the owner did not qualify are
47 disregarded in determining the preceding three years for which the deferred taxes are due and
48 payable.
