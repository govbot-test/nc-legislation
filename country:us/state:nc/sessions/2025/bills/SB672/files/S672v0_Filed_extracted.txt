13 "§ 131E-214.21. Short title and purpose.
14 This Article may be cited as the "Medical Debt Protection Act." The purpose of this Article
15 is to reduce burdensome medical debt and to protect patients in their dealings with medical
16 creditors, medical debt buyers, and medical debt collectors with respect to such debt. This Article
17 is a consumer protection statute and shall be liberally and remedially construed to effectuate its
18 purposes.

11 "§ 131E-214.29. Prohibited acts in collecting medical debt.
12 (a) The following prohibited collection actions may not be used by any medical creditor
13 or medical debt collector to collect debts owed for health care services:
14 (1) Causing an individual's arrest.
15 (2) Causing an individual to be held in civil contempt or imprisoned under
16 G.S. 5A-21 or G.S. 1-302 if the only reason supporting the contempt is the
17 debtor's failure to pay a judgment for medical debt.
18 (3) Foreclosing on an individual's real property.
19 (4) Placing a lien on an individual's real property or engaging in any action that
20 would result in a lien being placed on an individual's real property.
21 (5) Acquiring a security interest or lien in the individual's real property, by
22 agreement or otherwise.
23 (6) Garnishing wages or State income tax refunds.
24 (b) No medical creditor or medical debt collector may communicate with or report any
25 information to any consumer reporting agency regarding a consumer's medical debt, unless the
26 communication is made to resolve an erroneous reporting.
27 (c) Any medical debt or portion of medical debt that is reported to a consumer reporting
28 agency in violation of this section shall be void.
29 (d) Nothing in this Article shall be construed as exempting a medical creditor or medical
30 debt collector from the provisions of Chapter 75 or Chapter 58 of the General Statutes, as those
31 Chapters are applicable.
32 "§ 131E-214.30. Limitations on extraordinary collection actions.
33 (a) No medical creditor or medical debt collector shall engage in any permissible
34 extraordinary collection actions until 180 days after the first bill for a medical debt has been sent.
35 (b) If a patient is eligible for financial assistance under the large health care facility's
36 MDMP, a medical creditor or medical debt collector shall not engage in any extraordinary
37 collection actions against the eligible patient.
38 (c) Medical creditors and medical debt collectors shall not engage in any extraordinary
39 collection actions during a state or federally declared state of emergency or a public health
40 emergency in the area where the patient lives.
41 (d) At least 30 days before taking any extraordinary collection actions, a medical creditor
42 or medical debt collector must provide to the patient a notice containing the following:
43 (1) In the case of large health care facilities and medical debt collectors collecting
44 debt for health care services provided by such facilities, stating that financial
45 assistance and payment plans are available for eligible individuals and
46 providing a plain-language summary of the MDMP.
47 (2) Identifying the extraordinary collection actions that will be initiated in order
48 to obtain payment.
49 (3) Providing a deadline after which such extraordinary collection actions will be
50 initiated, which date is no earlier than 30 days after the date of the notice.

26 "§ 131E-214.35. Interest on medical debt.
27 (a) Interest on medical debt shall be limited to no more than two percent (2%) per annum.
28 Late payments shall not result in a default interest rate. If a patient is charged a late fee, that fee
29 shall not exceed the lesser of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) or one percent (1%). Patients eligible
30 for financial assistance shall not be charged any interest or late fees.
31 (b) The rate of interest provided in subsection (a) of this section shall also apply to any
32 judgments on medical debt, notwithstanding any other provision of law or agreement to the
33 contrary.
34 "§ 131E-214.36. Medical debt payment plans.
35 (a) Medical creditors and medical debt collectors shall offer any patient that qualifies for
36 financial assistance or who owes a medical debt over five hundred dollars ($500) a payment plan
37 of not less than 36 months and shall not require the patient to make monthly payments exceeding
38 five percent (5%) of the patient's household income.
39 (b) Any medical creditor or medical debt collector that agrees to a payment plan for a
40 medical debt shall provide a written copy of the payment plan to the consumer within five
41 business days of entering into the payment plan. This plan shall prominently disclose the rate of
42 any interest being applied to the debt in compliance with G.S. 131E-214.35 and the date by which
43 the account will be paid off in full, assuming the payments set by the schedule are made without
44 interruption.
45 (c) A consumer need not make a payment on the payment plan until the written copy has
46 been provided.
47 (d) A medical debt payment plan may be accelerated or declared in default or no longer
48 operative due to nonpayment only after the patient fails to make scheduled payments on the
49 payment plan for at least three consecutive months. Before declaring the payment plan no longer
50 operative, the medical creditor or medical debt collector shall make at least three reasonable
51 attempts to contact the patient by telephone or other method preferred by the patient.
