9 "§ 90-637. Short title.
10 This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Interstate Massage Compact."
11 "§ 90-637.1. Purpose.
12 The purpose of this Compact is to reduce the burdens on state governments and to facilitate
13 the interstate practice and regulation of massage therapy with the goal of improving public access
14 to, and the safety of, massage therapy services. Through this Compact, the member states seek
15 to establish a regulatory framework which provides for a new multistate licensing program.
16 Through this additional licensing pathway, the member states seek to provide increased value
17 and mobility to licensed massage therapists in the member states, while ensuring the provision
18 of safe, competent, and reliable services to the public.
19 This Compact is designed to achieve the following objectives, and the member states hereby
20 ratify the same intentions by subscribing hereto:
21 (1) Increase public access to massage therapy services by providing for a
22 multistate licensing pathway.
23 (2) Enhance the member states' ability to protect the public's health and safety.
24 (3) Enhance the member states' ability to prevent human trafficking and licensure
25 fraud.
26 (4) Encourage the cooperation of member states in regulating the multistate
27 practice of massage therapy.
28 (5) Support relocating military members and their spouses.
29 (6) Facilitate and enhance the exchange of licensure, investigative, and
30 disciplinary information between the member states.
31 (7) Create an Interstate Commission that will exist to implement and administer
32 the Compact.
33 (8) Allow a member state to hold a licensee accountable, even where that licensee
34 holds a multistate license.

7 "§ 90-637.2. Definitions.
8 As used in this Compact, except as otherwise provided and subject to clarification by the
9 rules of the Commission, the following definitions apply:
10 (1) Active military member. – Any person with full-time duty status in the armed
11 forces of the United States, including members of the National Guard and
12 Reserve.
13 (2) Adverse action. – Any administrative, civil, equitable, or criminal action
14 permitted by a member state's laws which is imposed by a licensing authority
15 or other regulatory body against a licensee, including actions against an
16 individual's authorization to practice such as revocation, suspension,
17 probation, surrender in lieu of discipline, monitoring of the licensee, limitation
18 of the licensee's practice, or any other encumbrance on licensure affecting an
19 individual's ability to practice massage therapy, including the issuance of a
20 cease and desist order.
21 (3) Alternative program. – A nondisciplinary monitoring or prosecutorial
22 diversion program approved by a member state's licensing authority.
23 (4) Authorization to practice. – A legal authorization by a remote state pursuant
24 to a multistate license permitting the practice of massage therapy in that
25 remote state, which shall be subject to the enforcement jurisdiction of the
26 licensing authority in that remote state.
27 (5) Background check. – The submission of an applicant's criminal history record
28 information, as further defined in 28 C.F.R. § 20.3(d), as amended from the
29 Federal Bureau of Investigation and the agency responsible for retaining state
30 criminal records in the applicant's home state.
31 (6) Charter member states. – Member states who have enacted legislation to adopt
32 this Compact where such legislation predates the effective date of this
33 Compact as defined in G.S. 90-637.12.
34 (7) Commission. – The government agency whose membership consists of all
35 states that have enacted this Compact, which is known as the Interstate
36 Massage Compact Commission, as defined in G.S. 90-637.8, and which shall
37 operate as an instrumentality of the member states.
38 (8) Continuing competence. – A requirement, as a condition of license renewal,
39 to provide evidence of participation in, and completion of, educational or
40 professional activities that maintain, improve, or enhance massage therapy
41 fitness to practice.
42 (9) Current significant investigative information. – Investigative information that
43 a licensing authority, after an inquiry or investigation that complies with a
44 member state's due process requirements, has reason to believe is not
45 groundless and, if proved true, would indicate a violation of that state's laws
46 regarding the practice of massage therapy.
47 (10) Data system. – A repository of information about licensees who hold
48 multistate licenses, which may include, but is not limited to, license status,
49 investigative information, and adverse actions.

39 substantial equivalent which the Commission may approve by rule.

48 such offense.

50 disposition, of a misdemeanor offense related to the practice of massage

25 "§ 90-637.5. Authority of Interstate Massage Compact Commission and member state
26 licensing authorities.
27 (a) Nothing in this Compact, nor any rule of the Commission, shall be construed to limit,
28 restrict, or in any way reduce the ability of a member state to enact and enforce laws, regulations,
29 or other rules related to the practice of massage therapy in that state, where those laws,
30 regulations, or other rules are not inconsistent with the provisions of this Compact.
31 (b) Nothing in this Compact, nor any rule of the Commission, shall be construed to limit,

40 (e) Insofar as practical, a member state's licensing authority shall cooperate with the
41 Commission and with each entity exercising independent regulatory authority over the practice
42 of massage therapy according to the provisions of this Compact.

47 "§ 90-637.7. Active military members and their spouses.
