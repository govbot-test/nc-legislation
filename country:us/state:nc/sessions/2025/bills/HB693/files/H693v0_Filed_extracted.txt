9 "§ 90-637. Short title.
10 This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Interstate Massage Compact."
11 "§ 90-637.1. Purpose.
12 The purpose of this Compact is to reduce the burdens on state governments and to facilitate
13 the interstate practice and regulation of massage therapy with the goal of improving public access
14 to, and the safety of, massage therapy services. Through this Compact, the member states seek
15 to establish a regulatory framework which provides for a new multistate licensing program.
16 Through this additional licensing pathway, the member states seek to provide increased value
17 and mobility to licensed massage therapists in the member states, while ensuring the provision
18 of safe, competent, and reliable services to the public.
19 This Compact is designed to achieve the following objectives, and the member states hereby
20 ratify the same intentions by subscribing hereto:
21 (1) Increase public access to massage therapy services by providing for a
22 multistate licensing pathway.
23 (2) Enhance the member states' ability to protect the public's health and safety.
24 (3) Enhance the member states' ability to prevent human trafficking and licensure
25 fraud.
26 (4) Encourage the cooperation of member states in regulating the multistate
27 practice of massage therapy.
28 (5) Support relocating military members and their spouses.
29 (6) Facilitate and enhance the exchange of licensure, investigative, and
30 disciplinary information between the member states.
31 (7) Create an Interstate Commission that will exist to implement and administer
32 the Compact.
33 (8) Allow a member state to hold a licensee accountable, even where that licensee
34 holds a multistate license.
35 (9) Create a streamlined pathway for licensees to practice in member states, thus
36 increasing the mobility of duly licensed massage therapists.

14 individual's authorization to practice such as revocation, suspension,
15 probation, surrender in lieu of discipline, monitoring of the licensee, limitation
16 of the licensee's practice, or any other encumbrance on licensure affecting an
17 individual's ability to practice massage therapy, including the issuance of a
18 cease and desist order.

20 diversion program approved by a member state's licensing authority.
21 (4) Authorization to practice. – A legal authorization by a remote state pursuant
22 to a multistate license permitting the practice of massage therapy in that
23 remote state, which shall be subject to the enforcement jurisdiction of the
24 licensing authority in that remote state.
25 (5) Background check. – The submission of an applicant's criminal history record
26 information, as further defined in 28 C.F.R. § 20.3(d), as amended from the
27 Federal Bureau of Investigation and the agency responsible for retaining state
28 criminal records in the applicant's home state.
29 (6) Charter member states. – Member states who have enacted legislation to adopt
30 this Compact where such legislation predates the effective date of this
31 Compact as defined in G.S. 90-637.12.
32 (7) Commission. – The government agency whose membership consists of all
33 states that have enacted this Compact, which is known as the Interstate
34 Massage Compact Commission, as defined in G.S. 90-637.8, and which shall
35 operate as an instrumentality of the member states.
36 (8) Continuing competence. – A requirement, as a condition of license renewal,
37 to provide evidence of participation in, and completion of, educational or
38 professional activities that maintain, improve, or enhance massage therapy
39 fitness to practice.
40 (9) Current significant investigative information. – Investigative information that
41 a licensing authority, after an inquiry or investigation that complies with a
42 member state's due process requirements, has reason to believe is not
43 groundless and, if proved true, would indicate a violation of that state's laws
44 regarding the practice of massage therapy.
45 (10) Data system. – A repository of information about licensees who hold
46 multistate licenses, which may include, but is not limited to, license status,
47 investigative information, and adverse actions.
48 (11) Disqualifying event. – Any event which shall disqualify an individual from
49 holding a multistate license under this Compact, which the Commission may
50 by rule specify.

41 "§ 90-637.3. Member state requirements.
42 (a) To be eligible to join this Compact, and to maintain eligibility as a member state, a
43 state must do all of the following:
44 (1) License and regulate the practice of massage therapy.
45 (2) Have a mechanism or entity in place to receive and investigate complaints
46 from the public, regulatory or law enforcement agencies, or the Commission
47 about licensees practicing in that state.
48 (3) Accept passage of a national licensing examination as a criterion for massage
49 therapy licensure in that state.
50 (4) Require that licensees satisfy educational requirements prior to being licensed
51 to provide massage therapy services to the public in that state.

29 "§ 90-637.4. Multistate license requirements.
30 (a) To qualify for a multistate license under this Compact and to maintain eligibility for
31 such a license, an applicant must meet all the following requirements:
32 (1) Hold an active single-state license to practice massage therapy in the
33 applicant's home state.
34 (2) Have completed at least 625 clock hours of massage therapy education or the
35 substantial equivalent which the Commission may approve by rule.
36 (3) Have passed a national licensing examination or the substantial equivalent
37 which the Commission may approve by rule.
38 (4) Submit to a background check.
39 (5) Have not been convicted or found guilty, or have entered into an agreed
40 disposition, of a felony offense under applicable state or federal criminal law,
41 within five years prior to the date of their application, where such a time period
42 shall not include any time served for the offense, and provided that the
43 applicant has completed any and all requirements arising as a result of any
44 such offense.
45 (6) Have not been convicted or found guilty, or have entered into an agreed
46 disposition, of a misdemeanor offense related to the practice of massage
47 therapy under applicable state or federal criminal law, within two years prior
48 to the date of their application where such a time period shall not include any
49 time served for the offense, and provided that the applicant has completed any
50 and all requirements arising as a result of any such offense.

40 "§ 90-637.6. Adverse actions.
41 (a) A licensee's home state shall have exclusive power to impose an adverse action
42 against a licensee's multistate license issued by the home state.
43 (b) A home state may take adverse action on a multistate license based on the
44 investigative information, current significant investigative information, or adverse action of a
45 remote state.
46 (c) A home state shall retain authority to complete any pending investigations of a
47 licensee practicing under a multistate license who changes their home state during the course of
48 such an investigation. The licensing authority shall also be empowered to report the results of
49 such an investigation to the Commission through the data system as described in this Compact.
50 (d) Any member state may investigate actual or alleged violations of the scope of practice
51 laws in any other member state for a massage therapist who holds a multistate license.
