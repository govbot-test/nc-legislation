GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA

Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ELIMINATE CERTAIN SUBBASIN DESIGNATIONS AND REMOVE THE
3 REQUIREMENT FOR AN INTERBASIN TRANSFER CERTIFICATE FOR WATER
4 TRANSFERS BETWEEN THOSE CERTAIN SUBBASINS WITHIN THE SAME MAJOR
5 RIVER BASIN AND TO REVISE 2020 FARM ACT TMDL TRANSPORT FACTOR
6 CALCULATION APPLICABILITY.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8
9 ELIMINATE SUBBASIN DESIGNATIONS FOR IBT PROCESS
10 SECTION 1.(a) Legislative Findings. – The General Assembly finds the following,
11 with respect to eliminating the Haw River, Deep River, and Contentnea Creek subbasin
12 designations:
13 (1) These subbasins serve rapidly growing areas facing urgent water supply and
14 economic development pressures.
15 (2) Flexibility in intrabasin transfers within the Neuse and Cape Fear major river
16 basins strengthens drought resilience and supports emergency water
17 management.
18 (3) Reducing unnecessary regulatory barriers allows municipalities and utilities
19 in these subbasins to better plan and finance regional water infrastructure,
20 improving affordability for consumers.
21 (4) Existing watershed planning and management systems within the Cape Fear
22 River and Neuse River major river basins mitigate potential environmental
23 impacts caused by intrabasin transfers.
24 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 143-215.22G reads as rewritten:

1 "SECTION 15.(b) Subsection (a) of this section applies only to wastewater discharge permit
2 applications for a local government located in the Neuse River Basin with a customer base of
3 fewer than 15,000 connections.Basin.
4 "SECTION 15.(c) The Department of Environmental Quality, in conjunction with affected
5 parties, may begin the modeling necessary to determine new transport zones and delivery factors
6 for the Neuse River Basin for point source discharges and nutrient offset credits. Once the
7 Department has completed the watershed modeling, it shall provide the Environmental
8 Management Commission a list of qualified professionals from which the Commission shall
9 select at least two to validate the modeling. If each of the professionals selected by the
10 Commission validate the model, the Commission may use the modeling and other information
11 provided during the public comment period to adopt new transport zones and delivery factors, if
12 warranted, by rule.
13 "SECTION 15.(d) This section is effective when it becomes law. Subsection (a) of this
14 section shall expire when the rule required by subsection (c) of this section becomes effective."
