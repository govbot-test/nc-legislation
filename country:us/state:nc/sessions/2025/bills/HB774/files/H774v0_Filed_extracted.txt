Short Title: School Breakfast for All. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Biggs.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE BREAKFAST TO PUBLIC SCHOOL STUDENTS AT NO COST TO
3 THE STUDENTS AND TO ESTABLISH THE FARM-TO-TABLE INITIATIVE.
4 Whereas, one in five kids goes hungry every day in North Carolina, putting them at
5 risk of poor health and educational outcomes; and
6 Whereas, hunger negatively impacts a child's ability to learn, concentrate, and
7 perform well in school; and
8 Whereas, research shows that students who eat a nutritious breakfast demonstrate
9 higher academic achievement, improved behavior, and increased school attendance; and
10 Whereas, the economic benefits of school breakfast at no cost to the student and
11 family include reduced absenteeism, lower healthcare costs, and long-term workforce
12 productivity; and
13 Whereas, North Carolina is home to a rich agricultural industry, and sourcing locally
14 grown fruits, vegetables, dairy, and proteins for school meals stimulates economic growth in
15 rural communities; and
16 Whereas, partnerships between schools, farmers, and food distributors create new
17 market opportunities for North Carolina's agricultural sector while ensuring students receive
18 nutritious meals; and
19 Whereas, funding and policy support within School Breakfast for All legislation
20 would align with North Carolina's goals for economic sustainability, public health, and student
21 success; Now, therefore,
22 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
23 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 115C-263(a) reads as rewritten:
24 "(a) As a part of the function of the public school system, local boards of education public
25 school units shall have a school food authority to provide to the extent practicable school nutrition
26 services in the schools under their jurisdiction. The school food authority shall provide breakfast
27 service at no cost to students in accordance with G.S. 115C-263.5 and may offer other nutrition
28 services to the extent practicable. All school nutrition services made available under this authority
29 shall be provided in accordance with federal guidelines established by the Food and Nutrition
30 Service of the United States Department of Agriculture."
31 SECTION 1.(b) Part 2 of Article 17 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
32 amended by adding a new section to read:
33 "§ 115C-263.5. Universal breakfast program.
34 (a) To the extent funds are made available for the purpose, the Department of Public
35 Instruction shall allocate sufficient funds to public schools to provide breakfast at no cost to
36 students attending the school in prekindergarten through grade 12 who elect to receive breakfast.

10 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 115C-264 reads as rewritten:
11 "§ 115C-264. Operation.
12 (a) Local boards of education operating school nutrition programs shall participate in the
13 federal School Breakfast Program, National School Lunch Program established by the federal
14 government. and, if eligible, the Community Eligibility Provision Program. School nutrition
15 programs shall be under the jurisdiction of the Division of School Nutrition of the Department
16 of Public Instruction.
17 …
18 (c) All school nutrition services shall be operated on a nonprofit basis, and any earnings
19 therefrom over and above the cost of operation as defined herein shall be used to reduce the cost
20 of food, to serve more nutritious food, or to provide free or reduced-price meals to economically
21 disadvantaged children and for no other purpose. The term "cost of operation" means the actual
22 cost incurred in the purchase and preparation of food, the salaries of all personnel directly
23 engaged in providing nutrition services, and the cost of nonfood supplies as outlined under
24 standards adopted by the State Board of Education. "Personnel" means school nutrition
25 supervisors or directors, bookkeepers or other staff directly engaged in nutrition services record
26 keeping, and those persons directly involved in preparing and serving food. School nutrition
27 personnel shall be paid from the funds of the school nutrition program only for services rendered
28 on behalf of the school nutrition program. Any cost incurred in the provisions and maintenance
29 of school nutrition services over and beyond the cost of operation shall be included in the budget
30 request filed annually by local boards of education with boards of county commissioners. Local
31 boards of education are not required to comply with G.S. 115C-522(a) in the purchase of supplies
32 and food for such school nutrition services.
33 …."
34 SECTION 1.(d) Part 2 of Article 17 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is
35 amended by adding a new section to read:
36 "§ 115C-264.6. Farm-to-table initiative.
37 (a) The Department of Public Instruction shall develop a farm-to-table initiative to
38 incorporate locally sourced and fresh ingredients into the universal breakfast program under
39 G.S. 115C-263.5.
40 (b) Schools shall be encouraged to partner with local farmers and food producers to
41 procure fresh, seasonal, and nutritional food options.
42 (c) The initiative shall provide education opportunities for students to learn about
43 agriculture, nutrition, and sustainable food systems."
44 SECTION 2.(a) G.S. 115C-150.14 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
45 "(a1) A school governed by this Article shall provide school nutrition services in
46 accordance with Part 2 of Article 17 of this Chapter."
47 SECTION 2.(b) G.S. 115C-218.75 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
48 "(p) A charter school shall provide school nutrition services in accordance with Part 2 of
49 Article 17 of this Chapter."
