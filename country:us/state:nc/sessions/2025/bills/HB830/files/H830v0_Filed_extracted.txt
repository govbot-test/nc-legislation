1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT ALLOWING MOBILE TATTOO PARLORS.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Part 11 of Article 8 of Chapter 130A of the General Statutes is amended
5 by adding a new section to read:
6 "§ 130A-283.1. Mobile tattoo parlors.
7 (a) Purpose and Authorization. – A motor vehicle, as defined in Article 1 of Chapter 20
8 of the General Statutes, may be used as a mobile tattoo parlor for the practice of tattooing, as
9 defined in G.S. 130A-283, as long as it meets the requirements of this section and all applicable
10 rules adopted by the Commission.
11 (b) Permitting Process. – The Department, through local health departments, shall issue
12 a permit to operate a mobile tattoo parlor to any applicant who meets all of the following criteria:
13 (1) Submits a completed application on a form provided by the Department.
14 (2) Pays an initial permit fee of five thousand dollars ($5,000) or an annual
15 renewal fee of two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500), as appropriate.
16 (3) Passes an inspection conducted by the local health department confirming
17 compliance with this section, laws pertaining to fixed-location tattoo parlors
18 under G.S. 130A-283, regulations pertaining to fixed-location tattoo parlors
19 under 15A NCAC 18A .3200, and rules adopted by the Commission under
20 subsection (c) of this section.
21 Permits issued under this section are valid for one year and may be renewed annually upon
22 payment of the renewal fee and a satisfactory reinspection by the Department.
23 (c) Additional Compliance. – In addition to the requirements of this Part, individuals
24 operating mobile tattoo parlors shall comply with all of the following:
25 (1) Chapter 20 of the General Statutes (Motor Vehicles).
26 (2) Occupational Safety and Health Administration standards, including 29
27 C.F.R. § 1910.1030 (Bloodborne Pathogens).
28 (3) All local ordinances regulating business operations, parking, and the practice
29 of tattooing.
30 (4) Federal regulations set forth in 40 C.F.R. Part 261 regarding the disposal of
31 hazardous waste.
32 (d) Facility and Equipment Requirements. – A mobile tattoo parlor shall comply with all
33 the following requirements:
34 (1) Be equipped with a functional sink with hot and cold running water, soap, and
35 single-use towels.

1 (2) Include a separate, accessible toilet facility or written agreement with a nearby
2 facility for client and operator use.
3 (3) Maintain an adequate supply of clean water of not less than 20 gallons and a
4 wastewater storage tank of not less than 25 gallons.
5 (4) Contain a sharps container and biohazard waste receptacle compliant with
6 State and federal law.
7 (5) Be equipped with an autoclave or other Department-approved sterilization
8 device for reusable instruments.
9 (e) Operational Restrictions. – No tattooing or related services may be performed in a
10 mobile tattoo parlor while the vehicle is in motion. The operator of a mobile tattoo parlor shall
11 remain parked in a legal parking space, in compliance with local zoning and parking laws, at all
12 times while clients are present. Each operator shall maintain a log of all tattoo procedures,
13 including client names, dates, and descriptions of services, that is available for inspection by the
14 local health department upon request of the local health department.
15 (f) Permanent Address. – The permanent address of the owner of a mobile tattoo parlor
16 shall serve as the location for receipt of all correspondence from the Department or local health
17 department.
18 (g) Recordkeeping. – The owner of a mobile tattoo parlor shall maintain a permanent
19 physical business address in North Carolina where the following records shall be kept and made
20 available for inspection by the Department or local health department upon request:
21 (1) Appointment logs.
22 (2) Weekly itineraries of the mobile parlor's locations.
23 (3) Permit numbers.
24 (4) Vehicle identification numbers (VINs) for each operating mobile tattoo parlor.
25 (5) Proof of biohazard waste disposal contracts.
26 (h) Rules. – The Commission, in consultation with the Department, shall adopt rules to
27 regulate the operation, permitting, and inspection of mobile tattoo parlors. These rules shall
28 include, but are not limited to, all of the following:
29 (1) Minimum standards for facilities, including ventilation, lighting, and
30 sterilization equipment.
31 (2) Personnel requirements, including training in bloodborne pathogens and
32 infection control.
33 (3) Safety and sanitary requirements, including proper handling and disposal of
34 needles, inks, and biohazardous waste.
35 (4) Procedures for documenting compliance with local zoning and parking
36 regulations.
37 All requirements applicable to fixed-location tattoo parlors under G.S. 130A-283 and 15A
38 NCAC 18A .3200 apply to mobile tattoo parlors unless they conflict with this section or rules
39 adopted under this section.
40 (i) Penalties and Enforcement. – Any person that violates this section or rules adopted
41 under this section is subject to all of the following:
42 (1) The Secretary of Health and Human Services may assess a civil penalty, not
43 to exceed five hundred dollars ($500.00) for each violation. Each day of a
44 continuing violation shall constitute a separate violation. The clear proceeds
45 of civil penalties provided for in this section shall be remitted to the Civil
46 Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with G.S. 115C-457.2.
47 (2) The Department may suspend or revoke a mobile tattoo parlor permit issued
48 by the local health department for a violation of this section. Repeated
49 violations, defined as three or more within a 12-month period, may result in
50 permanent revocation of the permit and a prohibition on reapplication for a

9 SECTION 2. G.S. 130A-29(c)(8) reads as rewritten:
10 "(8) Establishing permit requirements for the sanitation of premises, utensils,
11 equipment, and procedures to be used by a person engaged in tattooing,
12 tattooing under G.S. 130A-283 or G.S. 130A-283.1, as provided in Part 11 of
13 Article 8 of this Chapter."
14 SECTION 3. The Commission for Public Health shall begin rulemaking to
15 implement G.S. 130A-283.1(h), as enacted by this act, and G.S. 130A-29(c)(8), as amended by
16 this act. The Department of Health and Human Services shall notify the Revisor of Statutes when
17 the rules adopted pursuant to G.S. 130A-283.1, as enacted by this act, and G.S. 130A-29(c)(8),
18 as amended by this act, become effective.
19 SECTION 4. Section 3 of this act is effective when it becomes law. The remainder
20 of this act becomes effective July 1, 2026.
