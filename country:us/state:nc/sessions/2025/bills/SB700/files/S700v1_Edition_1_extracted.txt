14 (a) The Office of Engineering and Codes is created within the Department of Labor and
15 the Office shall exercise its powers and duties independently of the Department. The Department
16 of Labor shall provide clerical and professional services to the Office of Engineering and Codes
17 for the purpose of carrying out its powers and duties under this Article and the laws of this State.
18 For purposes of this section, the phrase "clerical and professional services" includes, but is not
19 limited to, budgetary, human resources, information technology, and legal.

22 143 of the General Statutes.
23 (2) North Carolina Manufactured Housing Board – Manufactured Home
24 Warranties, Article 9A of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes.
25 (3) Uniform Standards Code for Manufactured Homes, Article 9B of Chapter 143
26 of the General Statutes.
27 (4) North Carolina Code Officials Qualification Board, Article 9C of Chapter 143
28 of the General Statutes.
29 (5) North Carolina Home Inspector Licensure Board, Article 9F of Chapter 143
30 of the General Statutes.
31 (6) Engineering and Building Codes Division.

33 Statutes, shall be the head of the Office of Engineering and Codes and shall be a person appointed
34 by the Commissioner of Labor subject to confirmation by the General Assembly by joint
35 resolution. The State Engineer shall be a person other than the Commissioner of Labor and shall
36 serve a three-year term. If a vacancy arises or exists pursuant to this subsection when the General

13 "§ 95-281. Execution of laws; adopt rules to enforce laws.
14 The State Engineer shall see that all laws the State Engineer is responsible for administering
15 are faithfully executed and, to that end, the State Engineer is authorized to adopt rules in
16 accordance with Chapter 150B of the General Statutes in order to enforce, carry out, and make
17 effective the provisions of those laws. The State Engineer is also authorized to adopt such further

20 "§ 95-282. Public office; inspection of records.
21 The Office of Engineering and Codes shall be a public office and the records, reports, books,
22 and papers thereof on file therein shall be accessible to the inspection of the public, except that
23 the records compiled as a part of an investigation for the crimes, shall not be considered as public
24 records and may be made available to the public only upon an order of a court of competent
25 jurisdiction. Provided that such records shall upon request be made available to the district
26 attorney of any district if the same concerns persons or investigations in the district attorney's
27 district.
28 "§ 95-283. Hearings and investigations.
29 All hearings and investigations undertaken by the Office of Engineering and Codes as
30 required by this Article may be conducted by the State Engineer personally or by one or more
31 employees in the Office of Engineering and Codes or, if requested by the State Engineer, one or
32 more employees in the Department of Labor. If the State Engineer or any investigator appointed
33 to conduct the investigations is of the opinion that there is evidence to charge any person or
34 persons with a criminal violation, the State Engineer may arrest with warrant or cause the person
35 or persons to be arrested. All hearings shall, unless otherwise specifically provided, be held in
36 accordance with this Article and Article 3A of Chapter 150B of the General Statutes and at a
37 time and place designated in a written notice given by the State Engineer to the person cited to
38 appear. The notice shall state the subject of inquiry and the specific charges, if any.
39 "§ 95-284. Designated hearing officers.
40 In any contested case involving the Office of Engineering and Codes, the State Engineer may
41 designate a member of the State Engineer's staff to serve as a hearing officer. When the State
42 Engineer is unable or elects not to hear a contested case and elects not to designate a hearing
43 officer to hear a contested case, the State Engineer shall apply to the Director of the Office of
44 Administrative Hearings for the designation of an administrative law judge to preside at the
45 hearing of a contested case. Upon receipt of the application, the Director shall, without undue
46 delay, assign an administrative law judge to hear the case.

39 in detailed technical areas or as representative of citizen advice in specific areas of interest.
