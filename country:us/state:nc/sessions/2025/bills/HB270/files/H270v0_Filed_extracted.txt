3 CONVICTED OF A CRIMINAL OFFENSE AND SENTENCED TO DEATH.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 15-187 is repealed.
6 SECTION 2. G.S. 15-188 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 15-188. Manner and place of execution.
8 (a) Any person convicted of a criminal offense and sentenced to death shall be executed
9 in accordance with this section and the remainder of this Article. The warden of Central Prison
10 may obtain and employ the drugs necessary to carry out the provisions of this Article, regardless
11 of contrary provisions in Chapter 90 of the General Statutes.
12 (b) In accordance with G.S. 15-187, the mode of executing a death sentence must in every
13 case be by administering to the convict or felon an intravenous injection of a substance or
14 substances in a lethal quantity sufficient to cause death and until the person is dead, and that
15 procedure shall be determined by the Secretary of the Department of Adult Correction, who shall
16 ensure compliance with the federal and State constitutions; and when any person, convict or felon
17 shall be sentenced by any court of the State having competent jurisdiction to be so executed, the
18 punishment shall only be inflicted within a permanent death chamber which the superintendent
19 of the State penitentiary is hereby authorized and directed to provide within the walls of the North
20 Carolina penitentiary at Raleigh, North Carolina. A person convicted of a criminal offense and
21 sentenced to death shall be executed by electrocution or, at the election of the convicted person,
22 by firing squad or lethal injection, if it is available at the time of election, under the direction of
23 the Secretary of the Department of Adult Correction. The election for death by electrocution,
24 firing squad, or lethal injection must be made in writing 14 days before each execution date or it
25 is waived. If the convicted person receives a stay of execution or the execution date has passed
26 for any reason, then the election expires and must be renewed in writing 14 days before a new
27 execution date. If the convicted person waives the right of election, then the penalty must be
28 administered by electrocution.
29 (c) If the mode of executing a death sentence is by lethal injection, the warden of Central
30 Prison may obtain and employ the drugs necessary to carry out the provisions of this Article,
31 regardless of contrary provisions in Chapter 90 of the General Statutes. The superintendent of
32 the State penitentiary shall also cause to be provided, in conformity with this Article, the
33 necessary appliances for the infliction of the punishment of death and qualified personnel to set
34 up and prepare the injection, administer the preinjections, insert the IV catheter, and to perform
35 other tasks required for this procedure in accordance with the requirements of this
36 Article.appliances.

19 SECTION 3. G.S. 15-188.1 reads as rewritten:
20 "§ 15-188.1. Health care professional assistance.
21 (a) Any assistance rendered with an execution under this Article by any licensed health
22 care professional, including, but not limited to, physicians, nurses, and pharmacists, shall not be
23 cause for any disciplinary or corrective measures by any board, commission, or other authority
24 created by the State or governed by State law which oversees or regulates the practice of health
25 care professionals, including, but not limited to, the North Carolina Medical Board, the North
26 Carolina Board of Nursing, and the North Carolina Board of Pharmacy.
27 (b) The infliction of the punishment of death under this Article, including by
28 administration of the required lethal substances under this Article substances, shall not be
29 construed to be the practice of medicine."
