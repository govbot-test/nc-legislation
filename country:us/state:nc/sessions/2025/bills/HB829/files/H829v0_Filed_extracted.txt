13 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 74F-7 reads as rewritten:
14 "§ 74F-7. Qualifications for license.
15 (a) An applicant shall be licensed as a locksmith if the applicant meets all of the following
16 qualifications:
17 (1) Is of good moral and ethical character, as evidenced in part by a criminal
18 history record check conducted in accordance with G.S. 74F-18.
19 (2) Is at least 18 years of age.
20 (3) Successfully completes an examination administered by the Board that
21 measures the knowledge and skill of the applicant in locksmith services and
22 the laws applicable to licensed locksmiths.
23 (4) Pays the required fee under G.S. 74F-9.
24 Applications for locksmith license shall be made on forms provided by the Board and shall
25 be accompanied by the application fee under G.S. 74F-9.
26 (b) An applicant for locksmith business license shall submit all of the requirements to the
27 Board:
28 (1) If the applicant is a corporation or company, articles of incorporation or
29 organization with the North Carolina Secretary of State.
30 (2) An Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the Internal Revenue Service.
31 (3) Mailing address in the State of North Carolina.
32 (4) Required locksmith business license fee as established in G.S. 74F-9.
33 (5) The names and contact information for all individuals performing locksmith
34 services on behalf of the locksmith business or working under the direct
35 control or supervision of a locksmith working for the locksmith business.
36 Applications for locksmith business license shall be made on forms provided by the Board
37 and filed with the Board by the owner, a partner, a member of the limited liability company, or
38 an officer of the corporation and shall be accompanied by the application fee under G.S. 74F-9.
39 (c) If a locksmith business is solely owned by a natural person, that person must be
40 licensed by the Board as a locksmith. If it is owned by a partnership, at least one partner must be
41 licensed by the Board as a locksmith. If it is owned by a corporation, the president, vice-president,
42 or the chairman of the board of directors must be licensed by the Board as a locksmith. If it is
43 owned by a limited liability company, at least one member must be licensed by the Board as a
44 locksmith. The licensee required by this subsection must be actively engaged in the operation of
45 the locksmith business."
