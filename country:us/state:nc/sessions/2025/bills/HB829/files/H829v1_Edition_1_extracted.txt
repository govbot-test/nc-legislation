1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED

6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 74F-3 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 74F-3. Licenses required; violation.
9 (a) No person shall perform or offer to perform locksmith services in this State unless the
10 person has been licensed under the provisions of this Chapter. Every person providing locksmith
11 services as defined under G.S. 74F-4(5) to buildings containing medical records, pharmaceutical
12 records, educational records, criminal records, voting records, tax records, legal records, or
13 personnel records, G.S. 74F-4(5), including any person providing locksmith services who is
14 employed by or working for a school, college, university, hospital, company, institution, or
15 government facility shall be licensed in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.
16 (a1) The provisions of this Chapter shall apply to corporations, companies, firms,
17 associations, or sole proprietorships that provide locksmith services to the public. A licensed
18 locksmith who owns and operates a corporation, company, firm, association, or sole
19 proprietorship that provides locksmith services shall obtain a locksmith business license from the
20 Board. The locksmith business license shall be renewed annually.
21 (b) Unless the conduct is covered under some other provision of law providing greater
22 punishment, a violation of this section is a Class 1 misdemeanor for the first offense. A second
23 or subsequent offense is a Class I felony."
24 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 74F-4 reads as rewritten:
25 "§ 74F-4. Definitions.
26 The following definitions apply in this Chapter:
27 (1) Apprentice. – A person who has been issued an apprenticeship designation by
28 the Board.
29 (1a) Board. – The North Carolina Locksmith Licensing Board.
30 (2) Code book. – A compilation, in any form, of key codes and combinations.
31 (3) License. – A certificate issued by the Board recognizing the person named
32 therein as having met the requirements to perform locksmith services as
33 defined in this Chapter.

1 (4) Locksmith. – A person who has been issued a license by the Board. In order
2 to engage in the practice of locksmith services, a locksmith must own, be
3 employed by, or otherwise be an agent of a locksmith business.
4 (4a) Locksmith business. – A corporation, company, firm, association, or sole
5 proprietorship that provides locksmith services to the public.
6 (5) Locksmith services. – Services that include repairing, rebuilding, rekeying,
7 repinning, servicing, adjusting, or installing locks, mechanical or electronic
8 locking devices, access control devices, egress control devices, safes, vaults,
9 and safe-deposit boxes for compensation or other consideration, including
10 services performed by safe technicians. The definition also includes any
11 method of bypassing a locking mechanism of any kind, whether in a
12 commercial, residential, or automotive setting, for compensation.
13 (6) Locksmith tools. – Any tools that are designed or used to open a mechanical
14 or electrical locking device in a way other than that which was intended by
15 the manufacturer."
16 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 74F-7 reads as rewritten:
17 "§ 74F-7. Qualifications for license.
18 (a) An applicant shall be licensed as a locksmith if the applicant meets all of the following
19 qualifications:
20 (1) Is of good moral and ethical character, as evidenced in part by a criminal
21 history record check conducted in accordance with G.S. 74F-18.
22 (2) Is at least 18 years of age.
23 (3) Successfully completes an examination administered by the Board that
24 measures the knowledge and skill of the applicant in locksmith services and
25 the laws applicable to licensed locksmiths.
26 (4) Pays the required fee under G.S. 74F-9.
27 Applications for locksmith license shall be made on forms provided by the Board and shall
28 be accompanied by the application fee under G.S. 74F-9.
29 (b) An applicant for locksmith business license shall submit all of the requirements to the
30 Board:
31 (1) If the applicant is a corporation or company, articles of incorporation or
32 organization with the North Carolina Secretary of State.
33 (2) An Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the Internal Revenue Service.
34 (3) Mailing address in the State of North Carolina.
35 (4) Required locksmith business license fee as established in G.S. 74F-9.
36 (5) The names and contact information for all individuals performing locksmith
37 services on behalf of the locksmith business or working under the direct
38 control or supervision of a locksmith working for the locksmith business.
39 Applications for locksmith business license shall be made on forms provided by the Board
40 and filed with the Board by the owner, a partner, a member of the limited liability company, or
41 an officer of the corporation and shall be accompanied by the application fee under G.S. 74F-9.
42 (c) If a locksmith business is solely owned by a natural person, that person must be
43 licensed by the Board as a locksmith. If it is owned by a partnership, at least one partner must be
44 licensed by the Board as a locksmith. If it is owned by a corporation, the president, vice-president,
45 or the chairman of the board of directors must be licensed by the Board as a locksmith. If it is
46 owned by a limited liability company, at least one member must be licensed by the Board as a
47 locksmith. The licensee required by this subsection must be actively engaged in the operation of
48 the locksmith business."
