1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE FOR THE STATE BOARD OF COMMUNITY COLLEGES TO
3 SOLICIT A LEARNING MANAGEMENT SYSTEM FOR ALL COMMUNITY
4 COLLEGES, TO PROVIDE AN EXEMPTION FROM THE DEPARTMENT OF
5 INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY FOR THE COMMUNITY COLLEGES SYSTEM
6 OFFICE, AND TO MAKE VARIOUS CHANGES TO THE GENERAL STATUTES
7 RELATED TO THE NORTH CAROLINA LONGITUDINAL DATA SYSTEM.
8 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

10 PART I. COMMUNITY COLLEGE LEARNING MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS
11 SECTION 1.(a) The State Board of Community Colleges shall conduct a
12 competitive solicitation, including a request for information or a request for proposals, to provide
13 a learning management system to all community colleges. The competitive solicitation shall be
14 completed by December 31, 2025, and the transition to the new learning management system
15 shall be completed by December 31, 2027. Answers to the competitive solicitation shall include
16 information on how the learning management system would align with the learning management
17 systems (i) offered by the Department of Public Instruction to local school administrative units
18 and (ii) used by the constituent institutions of The University of North Carolina.
19 SECTION 1.(b) By December 31, 2025, the State Board shall report to the Senate
20 Appropriations Committee on Education/Higher Education, the House Appropriations
21 Committee on Education, and the Fiscal Research Division on the information received.

28 Privacy Act, 20 U.S.C. § 1232g.

35 performance. Student data includes State and national assessments, course
36 enrollment and completion, grade point average, remediation, retention,
37 degree, diploma or credential attainment, enrollment, discipline records, and
38 demographic data. Student data does not include juvenile delinquency records,
39 criminal records, and medical and health records.

41 System.System, including components referred to as the North Carolina
42 Longitudinal Data Service.

44 UID. – The identifier assigned to each student by one of the following:
45 a. A local school administrative unit public school unit based on the

47 b. An institution of higher education, nonpublic school, or other State
48 agency operating or overseeing an educational program, if the student
49 has not been assigned an identifier by a local school administrative
50 unit.public school unit.

4 "§ 116E-2. Purpose of the North Carolina Longitudinal Data System.
5 (a) The North Carolina Longitudinal Data System is a statewide data system that contains
6 individual-level student data and workforce data from all levels of education and the State's
7 workforce. The purpose of the System is to do the following:
8 (1) Facilitate and enable the exchange of student data among agencies and
9 institutions within the State.
10 (2) Generate timely and accurate information about student performance that can
11 be used to improve the State's education system and guide decision makers at
12 all levels.
13 (3) Facilitate and enable the linkage of student data and workforce data.
14 (b) The linkage of student data and workforce data for the purposes of the System shall
15 be limited to no longer than five years from the later of the date of the student's completion of
16 secondary education or the date of the student's latest attendance at an institution of higher
17 education in the State.
18 "§ 116E-4. Powers and duties of the Center.
19 (a) The Center shall have the following powers and duties with respect to the System:
20 …
21 (4) Before the use of any individual data in the System, the Center shall do the
22 following:
23 a. Create and publish an inventory of the individual student data
24 proposed to be accessible in the System and required to be reported by
25 State and federal education mandates.System.
26 b. Develop and implement policies to comply with FERPA FERPA,
27 IDEA, HIPAA, CJIS, the Internal Revenue Code, and any other
28 privacy measures, measures relevant to data available to the System,
29 as required by law or the Center.
30 c. Develop a detailed data security and safeguarding plan that includes
31 the following:
32 1. Authorized access and authentication for authorized access.
33 2. Privacy compliance standards.
34 3. Privacy and security audits.
35 4. Breach notification and procedures.
36 5. Data retention and disposition policies.
37 (5) Oversee routine and ongoing compliance with FERPA FERPA, IDEA,
38 HIPAA, CJIS, the Internal Revenue Code, and other relevant privacy laws and
39 policies.
40 (6) Ensure that any contracts that govern databases that are outsourced to private
41 vendors include express provisions that safeguard privacy and security and
42 include penalties for noncompliance.
43 (7) Designate a standard and compliance time line for electronic transcripts that
44 includes the use of UID to ensure the uniform and efficient transfer of student
45 data between local school administrative units and institutions of higher
46 education.
47 (8) Review research requirements and set policies for the approval of data
48 requests from State and local agencies, the General Assembly, and the public.
49 (9) Establish an advisory committee on data quality to advise the Center on issues
50 related to data auditing and tracking to ensure data validity.

12 (a) There is created the North Carolina Longitudinal Data System. The System shall be
13 located administratively within the Department of Public Instruction but shall exercise its powers
14 and duties independently of the Department of Public Instruction and the State Board of
15 Education.Information Technology.

17 (1) Effectively organize, manage, disaggregate, and analyze individual student
18 and workforce data.
19 (2) Examine student progress and outcomes over time, including preparation for
20 postsecondary education and the workforce.
21 (c) The System shall be considered an authorized representative of the Department of
22 Public Instruction, The University of North Carolina, and the North Carolina System of
23 Community Colleges under applicable federal and State statutes for purposes of accessing and
24 compiling student record data for research purposes.

26 (1) Serve as a data broker for the System, including data maintained by the

45 including the following:
46 a. The required Requiring use of de-identified data in data research and
47 reporting.
48 b. The required Requiring disposition of information that is no longer
49 needed.
50 c. Providing data security, including the capacity for audit trails.

2 privacy and security standards.
3 e. Implementing guidelines and policies that prevent the reporting of

6 reporting with existing State agencies as appropriate.
7 (4) Facilitate approved public information requests.
8 (5) Develop a process for obtaining information and data requested by the General
9 Assembly and Governor of current de-identified data and research.

11 (1) Direct access to data shall be restricted to authorized staff of the System.
12 (2) Only de-identified data shall be used in the analysis, research, and reporting
13 conducted by the System.
14 (3) The System and recipients of data in fulfillment of approved data requests
15 shall only use aggregate aggregated data in the release of data in reports and
16 in response to data requests.public reports.

18 under consideration shall not be reported in any form by the System.
19 (5) The System shall not release information that may not be disclosed under
20 FERPA, IDEA, HIPAA, CJIS, the Internal Revenue Code, and other relevant
21 privacy laws and policies.
22 (6) Individual or personally identifiable data accessed through the System shall
23 not be a public record under G.S. 132-1.
24 (f) The System may receive funding from the following sources:
25 (1) State appropriations.
26 (2) Grants or other assistance from local school administrative units, public
27 school units, community colleges, constituent institutions of The University
28 of North Carolina, or private colleges and universities.
29 (3) Federal grants.
30 (4) Any other grants or contributions from public or private entities received by
31 the System.
32 (g) Ownership of all data collected and maintained by the System remains with the
33 contributors to the System. Management and disclosure of data by the System does not change
34 ownership of the data.
35 "§ 116E-6. Data sharing.
36 (a) Local school administrative units, charter schools, Public school units, community
37 colleges, constituent institutions of The University of North Carolina, and State agencies shall
38 do all of the following:
39 (1) Comply with the data requirements and implementation schedule for the
40 System as set forth by the Center.
41 (2) Transfer student data and workforce data to the System in accordance with the
42 data security and safeguarding plan developed by the Center under
43 G.S. 116E-5.
44 (b) Private colleges and universities, the North Carolina Independent Colleges and
45 Universities, Inc., and nonpublic schools may transfer student data and workforce data to the
46 System in accordance with the data security and safeguarding plan developed under G.S. 116E-5.
47 (c) All data sharing supported by the System shall comply with all applicable federal and
48 State data and data privacy laws and regulations."
