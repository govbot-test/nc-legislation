Short Title: Increase Punishment for Assaulting Teachers. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Cotham.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO INCREASE THE PUNISHMENT FOR ASSAULTING A SCHOOL EMPLOYEE
3 OR SCHOOL VOLUNTEER.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-33 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 14-33. Misdemeanor assaults,Assaults, batteries, and affrays, simple and aggravated;
7 punishments.
8 …
9 (c) Unless the conduct is covered under some other provision of law providing greater
10 punishment, any person who commits any assault, assault and battery, or affray is guilty of a
11 Class A1 misdemeanor if, in the course of the assault, assault and battery, or affray, he or she:
12 …
13 (6) Assaults a school employee or school volunteer when the employee or
14 volunteer is discharging or attempting to discharge his or her duties as an
15 employee or volunteer, or assaults a school employee or school volunteer as a
16 result of the discharge or attempt to discharge that individual's duties as a
17 school employee or school volunteer. For purposes of this subdivision, the
18 following definitions shall apply:
19 a. "Duties" means:
20 1. All activities on school property;
21 2. All activities, wherever occurring, during a school authorized
22 event or the accompanying of students to or from that event;
23 and
24 3. All activities relating to the operation of school transportation.
25 b. "Employee" or "volunteer" means:
26 1. An employee of a local board of education; or a charter school
27 authorized under G.S. 115C-218.5, or a nonpublic school
28 which has filed intent to operate under Part 1 or Part 2 of
29 Article 39 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes;
30 2. An independent contractor or an employee of an independent
31 contractor of a local board of education, charter school
32 authorized under G.S. 115C-218.5, or a nonpublic school
33 which has filed intent to operate under Part 1 or Part 2 of
34 Article 39 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes, if the
35 independent contractor carries out duties customarily
36 performed by employees of the school; and

1 3. An adult who volunteers his or her services or presence at any
2 school activity and is under the supervision of an individual
3 listed in sub-sub-subdivision 1. or 2. of this sub-subdivision.
4 …
5 (c1) No school personnel as defined in G.S. 14-33(c)(6) who takes reasonable actions in
6 good faith to end a fight or altercation between students shall incur any civil or criminal liability
7 as the result of those actions.
8 (c2) Unless the conduct is covered under some other provision of law providing greater
9 punishment, any person who commits any assault, assault and battery, or affray is guilty of a
10 Class I felony if, in the course of the assault, assault and battery, or affray, he or she assaults a
11 school employee or school volunteer when the employee or volunteer is discharging or
12 attempting to discharge his or her duties as an employee or volunteer, or assaults a school
13 employee or school volunteer as a result of the discharge or attempt to discharge that individual's
14 duties as a school employee or school volunteer. No school personnel who takes reasonable
15 actions in good faith to end a fight or altercation between students shall incur any civil or criminal
16 liability as the result of those actions. For purposes of this subsection, the following definitions
17 apply:
18 (1) Duties. – Means any of the following:
19 a. All activities on school property.
20 b. All activities, wherever occurring, during a school authorized event or
21 the accompanying of students to or from that event.
22 c. All activities relating to the operation of school transportation.
23 (2) Employee or Volunteer. – Means any of the following:
24 a. An employee of a local board of education; or a charter school
25 authorized under G.S. 115C-218.5, or a nonpublic school which has
26 filed intent to operate under Part 1 or Part 2 of Article 39 of Chapter
27 115C of the General Statutes.
28 b. An independent contractor or an employee of an independent
29 contractor of a local board of education, charter school authorized
30 under G.S. 115C-218.5, or a nonpublic school which has filed intent
31 to operate under Part 1 or Part 2 of Article 39 of Chapter 115C of the
32 General Statutes, if the independent contractor carries out duties
33 customarily performed by employees of the school.
34 c. An adult who volunteers his or her services or presence at any school
35 activity and is under the supervision of an individual listed in
36 sub-subdivision a. or b. of this subdivision.
37 …."
38 SECTION 2. G.S. 115C-289.1 reads as rewritten:
39 "§ 115C-289.1. Supervisor duty to report; intimidation of school employee.
40 (a) When a supervisor of a school employee has actual notice that the school employee
41 has been the victim of an assault by a student in violation of G.S. 14-33(c)(6)14-33(c2) resulting
42 in physical injury, as that term is defined in G.S. 14-34.7, the supervisor shall immediately report
43 to the principal the assault against the school employee. For the purpose of this subsection, the
44 term "supervisor of a school employee" does not include the principal or superintendent.
45 (b) A principal, superintendent, or supervisor of a school employee shall not, by threats
46 or in any other manner, intimidate or attempt to intimidate that school employee from reporting
47 to law enforcement an assault by a student under G.S. 14-33(c)(6).14-33(c2).
48 …."
49 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to offenses
50 committed on or after that date.
