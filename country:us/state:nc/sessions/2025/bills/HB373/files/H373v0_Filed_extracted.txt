Section 1:
H.B. 373
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Mar 11, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: UNC Tuition Discounts for Certain Students. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Campbell.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PERMIT CONSTITUENT INSTITUTIONS OF THE UNIVERSITY OF NORTH
3 CAROLINA TO PROVIDE DISCOUNTED TUITION TO PERSONS RECEIVING
4 MILITARY TUITION ASSISTANCE OR PERSONS ENROLLED IN AN
5 EMPLOYER-SPONSORED FINANCIAL SUPPORT PROGRAM.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 116-143 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 116-143. State-supported institutions of higher education required to charge tuition and
9 fees.
10 …
11 (c) Inasmuch as the giving of tuition and fee waivers, or especially reduced rates,
12 represent in effect a variety of scholarship awards, the said practice is hereby prohibited except
13 when expressly authorized by statute.
14 (d) Notwithstanding the above provision relating to the abolition of free tuition,
15 subsection (c) of this section, the Board of Governors of The University of North Carolina may,
16 in its discretion, provide do any of the following:
17 (1) Provide regulations under which personnel may during the period of normal
18 employment enroll in The University of North Carolina free of charge for
19 tuition and fees, provided such enrollment does not interfere with normal
20 employment obligations and further provided that such enrollments are not
21 counted for the purpose of receiving General Fund appropriations as follows:
22 (1)a. Except as provided in subdivision (2) of this subsection,
23 sub-subdivision b. of this subdivision, a full-time faculty member of
24 the rank of full-time instructor or above and any full-time staff member
25 of The University of North Carolina may enroll in not more than three
26 courses per year.
27 (2)b. A full-time or part-time campus law enforcement officer may enroll in
28 the number of courses per year determined by regulation.
29 (2) Permit constituent institutions to provide discounted tuition to enrolled
30 students, in the discretion of the constituent institution, as follows:
31 a. For qualifying military students, by an amount of up to the difference
32 in the maximum amount of military tuition assistance funds the student
33 receives and the applicable rate of tuition. For purposes of this
34 sub-subdivision, a "qualifying military student" is a student who meets
35 all of the following criteria:
*DRH10167-MTa-51A*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 1. Qualifies as a resident for tuition purposes under
2 G.S. 116-143.1.
3 2. Receives either federal military tuition assistance funds or
4 military tuition assistance funds for members of the North
5 Carolina National Guard under Article 15 of Chapter 127A of
6 the General Statutes.
7 b. For students who are enrolled in an employer-sponsored financial
8 support program that is approved by the Board of Governors, by an
9 amount of up to the difference in the maximum amount provided by
10 the employer and the applicable rate of tuition. For purposes of this
11 sub-subdivision, an employer-sponsored financial support program is
12 a program in which the employer of a student has committed to
13 provide financial support to the student to offset the costs of tuition or
14 fees in the student's degree or credential program.
15 (d1) No later than February 15 of each year, the Board of Governors of The University of
16 North Carolina shall report to the Joint Legislative Education Oversight Committee and the Fiscal
17 Research Division on the discounted tuition provided in the previous academic year pursuant to
18 subdivision (2) of subsection (d) of this section, including at least the following information:
19 (1) The number of students that receive a discount pursuant to either
20 sub-subdivision of subdivision (2) of subsection (d) of this section.
21 (2) The annual financial impact on each constituent institution resulting from the
22 discounted tuition provided.
23 …."
24 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning with
25 the 2025-2026 academic year.
