Section 1:
H.B. 766
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 3, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Expand Access to Teen Mental Health First Aid. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Lofton.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT APPROPRIATING FUNDS TO THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN
3 SERVICES, DIVISION OF CHILD AND FAMILY WELL-BEING, TO EXPAND
4 ACCESS TO TEEN MENTAL HEALTH FIRST AID TRAINING.
5 Whereas, ten percent (10%) of North Carolina's high school students reported having
6 attempted suicide in the last year and twenty-two percent (22%) of these same students reported
7 having seriously contemplated suicide; and
8 Whereas, 1 in 5 teens in the United States will likely experience a mental health
9 challenge by the age of eighteen and sixty-four percent (64%) of these teens are unlikely to seek
10 professional help; and
11 Whereas, teen Mental Health First Aid (tMHFA) is an evidence-based course
12 developed to teach teens who are in grades nine through twelve, or ages fourteen through
13 eighteen, how to identify, understand, and respond to signs of mental health and substance use
14 challenges among their friends and peers; and
15 Whereas, the tMHFA curriculum covers common signs and symptoms of mental
16 health and substance use challenges in this age group and how to connect teens with trusted and
17 responsible adults for support and assistance during a mental health or substance use crisis; and
18 Whereas, the University of North Carolina has trained 1,519 teens in ten counties in
19 the tMHFA curriculum and established a cohort of 68 tMHFA instructors; and
20 Whereas, completion of the tMHFA course has shown a fifty percent (50%) increase
21 in the willingness of teens to tell someone and seek help from an adult or health care professional
22 about mental health and substance use challenges; Now, therefore,
23 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
24 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
25 Health and Human Services, Division of Child and Family Well-Being, the sum of one million
26 eight hundred seventy-three thousand eight hundred sixty-three dollars ($1,873,863) in
27 nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to expand access to Teen Mental Health First
28 Aid training in North Carolina.
29 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
*DRH30331-MGa-136*
