Section 1:
H.B. 503
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Mar 24, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Don't Tread on Me Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Reives.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ENACT THE DON'T TREAD ON ME: AN INDIVIDUAL FREEDOMS ACT.
3 Whereas, the right to individual privacy is a cornerstone of a free society, and the
4 government has no business prying into private lives without a truly compelling reason; and
5 Whereas, medical decisions belong to individuals and families, not politicians, and
6 no one should have to navigate government red tape to make personal health choices; and
7 Whereas, parents – not the government – are responsible for raising their children,
8 deciding their education, and making healthcare decisions without interference from political
9 agendas; and
10 Whereas, every North Carolinian has a constitutional right to a strong public
11 education, and that means access to books, ideas, and knowledge without government censorship
12 or political gatekeeping; and
13 Whereas, discrimination by the government – whether based on race, religion, sex,
14 sexual orientation, or political beliefs – has no place in a free society, and equal protection under
15 the law must apply to everyone; Now, therefore,
16 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
17 SECTION 1. Chapter 99D of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
18 Article 1, consisting of G.S. 99D-1. Article 1 shall be titled "Civil Rights."
19 SECTION 2. The Revisor of Statutes shall change references to "Chapter" in
20 G.S. 99D-1 to "Article."
21 SECTION 3. Chapter 99D of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
22 Article to read:
23 "Article 2.
24 "Individual Freedoms.
25 "§ 99D-10. Individual Freedom Bill of Rights.
26 Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the following fundamental rights
27 are recognized and may not be violated absent a compelling State interest coupled by means to
28 achieve that interest that are narrowly tailored. For purposes of this Article, "narrowly tailored"
29 means are those that are no broader than necessary to accomplish the intended purpose. This bill
30 of rights includes all of the following:
31 (1) No person shall be subjected to warrantless surveillance, tracking, or data
32 collection by any agency of the State or political subdivision of the State.
33 (2) No agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may require a
34 person to disclose private medical decisions, religious beliefs, or personal
35 political affiliations to obtain government benefits, employment, or services.
36 In addition, no agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may
*DRH50018-MC-127A*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 make determinations with respect to government benefits, employment, or
2 services if such information is displayed or known.
3 (3) No agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may disclose,
4 weaponize, or track personal health information unlawfully.
5 (4) No agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may deny or restrict
6 reproductive health care, contraception, or any other medical treatment based
7 on ideology rather than medical science.
8 (5) No agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may override
9 parental authority without due process in cases of abuse, neglect, or harm.
10 (6) Education must be fact-based, ensuring (i) parental concerns are addressed
11 without restricting access to accurate historical and scientific information and
12 (ii) teachings provided are historically accurate and free from political
13 manipulation.
14 (7) No agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may compel a
15 person to affirm or promote a particular political ideology in public education
16 or workplace settings.
17 (8) No agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may deny a person
18 employment, housing, or public services based on private political beliefs,
19 medical history, or lawful personal conduct.
20 (9) No agency of the State or political subdivision of the State may interfere with
21 employment and housing rights in a way that is not fair and merit-based, free
22 from quotas and ideological discrimination."
23 SECTION 4. This act is effective when it becomes law.
