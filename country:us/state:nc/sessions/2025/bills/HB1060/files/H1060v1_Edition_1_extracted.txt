Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: AG Investigations/Certain ICE & CBP Incidents. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Greenfield, Morey, Rubin, and Budd (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 28, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT REQUIRING THE ATTORNEY GENERAL TO INVESTIGATE INCIDENTS
3 INVOLVING FEDERAL IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT THAT RESULT IN
4 SERIOUS BODILY INJURY OR DEATH.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. Article 1 of Chapter 114 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
7 a new section to read:
8 "§ 114-8.8. Investigations into incidents involving federal law enforcement.
9 (a) Definitions. – For purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
10 (1) CBP. – Customs and Border Protection of the United States Department of
11 Homeland Security.
12 (2) Federal immigration enforcement. – Officers, agents, or other personnel
13 employed by CBP or ICE, or any State or local law enforcement officers or
14 employees authorized to perform immigration officer functions pursuant to
15 section 287(g) of the Immigration and Nationality Act, 8 U.S.C. § 1357.
16 (3) ICE. – Immigration and Customs Enforcement of the United States
17 Department of Homeland Security.
18 (b) Investigations. – The Attorney General shall investigate all incidents occurring in this
19 State that involve federal immigration enforcement and result in serious bodily injury or death to
20 any party. The investigation shall determine the cause and circumstances of the incident.
21 (c) Reports. – The Attorney General shall publicly release all findings and
22 recommendations from any investigation described in subsection (b) of this section and deliver
23 copies of such findings to the Speaker of the House of Representatives and the President Pro
24 Tempore of the Senate within seven days of completing an investigation. The Attorney General
25 may redact the names and personally identifying information of any victims and minors
26 mentioned in such findings."
27 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
28 Justice the sum of four hundred fifty thousand dollars ($450,000) in nonrecurring funds for the
29 2026-2027 fiscal year to be used to implement the requirements of this act.
30 SECTION 3. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to incidents
31 occurring on or after that date.
