11 SECTION 1.1. Article 8 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
12 adding a new section to read:
13 "§ 115C-81.16. Age-appropriate instruction for students.
14 (a) Instruction on gender identity, sexual activity, or sexuality shall not be included in the
15 curriculum provided in grades kindergarten through sixth grade, regardless of whether the
16 information is provided by school personnel or third parties. For the purposes of this section,
17 curriculum includes the standard course of study and support materials, locally developed
18 curriculum, supplemental instruction, and textbooks and other supplementary materials, but does
19 not include verbal responses to student-initiated questions. Instruction provided in accordance
20 with G.S. 115C-81.29 with parental consent does not violate this section.
21 (b) A student in grades seven through 12 shall not participate in instruction on gender
22 identity, sexual activity, or sexuality without the prior written consent of the student's parent or
23 legal guardian. Each governing board of a public school unit shall adopt a policy and provide a
24 mechanism to allow a parent or a legal guardian to consent to instruction required under this
25 section on an annual basis."

17 (2) Fifth grade. – Students in fifth grade shall be instructed in single-sex student
18 groups as follows:
19 a. Recall that puberty is characterized by the development of secondary
20 sex characteristics and onset of reproductive capacity.
21 b. Differentiate between accurate and inaccurate sources of information
22 about puberty and development.
23 c. Summarize the functions of the male and female reproductive systems.
24 d. Illustrate how societal influences can impact reproductive health
25 behavioral choices and consequences.

13 "§ 14-190.15. Disseminating harmful material to minors; exhibiting harmful performances
14 to minors.
15 (a) Disseminating Harmful Material. – A person commits the offense of disseminating
16 harmful material to minors if, with or without consideration and knowing the character or content
17 of the material, he:the person does any of the following:
18 (1) Sells, furnishes, presents, or distributes to a minor material that is harmful to
19 minors; orminors.
20 (2) Allows a minor to review or peruse material that is harmful to minors.
21 (b) Exhibiting Harmful Performance. – A person commits the offense of exhibiting a
22 harmful performance to a minor if, with or without consideration and knowing the character or
23 content of the performance, he the person allows a minor to view a live performance that is
24 harmful to minors.
25 (c) Defenses. – Except as provided in subdivision (3), a mistake of age is not a defense
26 to a prosecution under this section. It is an affirmative defense to a prosecution under this section
27 that:
28 (1) The defendant was a parent or legal guardian of the minor.
29 (2) The defendant was a school, church, museum, public library, governmental
30 agency, medical clinic, or hospital carrying out its legitimate function; or an

32 out a legitimate duty of his employment.
33 (3) Before disseminating or exhibiting the harmful material or performance, the
34 defendant requested and received a driver's license, student identification
35 card, or other official governmental or educational identification card or paper
36 indicating that the minor to whom the material or performance was
37 disseminated or exhibited was at least 18 years old, and the defendant
38 reasonably believed the minor was at least 18 years old.
39 (4) The dissemination was made with the prior consent of a parent or guardian of
40 the recipient.
41 (d) Punishment. – Violation of this section is a Class 1 misdemeanor."
