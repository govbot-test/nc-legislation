Section 1:
H.B. 604
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Mar 31, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Rural and Downtown Community Eco. Dev. Grants. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Reives.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CREATE THE RURAL COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT GRANT PROGRAM
3 AND THE DOWNTOWN REVITALIZATION GRANT PROGRAM AND TO
4 APPROPRIATE MONEY FOR PURPOSES CONSISTENT WITH THOSE PROGRAMS.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. The General Assembly finds that:
7 (1) Downtown areas are critical to the economic, cultural, and social well-being
8 of North Carolina's communities. They serve as vital hubs for commerce,
9 public engagement, and community identity. Yet many downtowns, in urban
10 and rural areas alike, have faced challenges, including declining activity,
11 vacant storefronts, and reduced investment.
12 (2) Rural communities across North Carolina also face broad, significant
13 challenges, including economic stagnation, depopulation, and limited access
14 to growth opportunities. These challenges prevent the vitality of local
15 economies from being realized, reduce residents' well-being, and diminish the
16 long-term sustainability of these regions.
17 (3) By providing targeted investments, the State can empower downtown areas
18 and rural communities to advance their economic development, enhance their
19 government services provision, and support the growth of local businesses.
20 These investments are essential for creating self-sustaining growth, fostering
21 local workforce development, and positioning these areas as desirable places
22 to live, work, and do business.
23 SECTION 2.(a) Appropriation. – There is appropriated from the General Fund to
24 the Department of Commerce (the Department) the sum of forty million dollars ($40,000,000) in
25 nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be allocated to the Rural Economic
26 Development Division (REDD) and used for purposes consistent with subsection (b) of this
27 section. Notwithstanding G.S. 143C-1-2(b), funds allocated in this section shall not revert but
28 shall remain available for purposes consistent with this section until June 30, 2027. The
29 Department may use up to five percent (5%) of the funds appropriated in this section for
30 administration of the Community Grant Program created in subsection (b) of this section.
31 SECTION 2.(b) Rural Community Development Grant Program. – The funds
32 allocated to REDD by subsection (a) of this section shall be used to create the Rural Community
33 Development Grant Program (the Community Grant Program). The Community Grant Program
34 shall provide grants to local governments in the State to attract business investment and to
35 promote population and revenue growth within those communities for the purposes of (i)
36 reversing the trend of rural depopulation and (ii) transitioning those communities to "commuter

1 towns" or "bedroom communities" that promote and sustain the growth of larger adjacent
2 communities. All county and municipal governments within the State are eligible to apply for
3 grant funding under the Community Grant Program. REDD shall establish guidelines for
4 providing for administration of the Community Grant Program. Those guidelines shall include
5 the following provisions, which shall apply to each grant under the Community Grant Program:
6 (1) Funds shall only be used for purposes that REDD determines are likely to
7 improve a community's economic and living conditions such that the
8 community is better able to sustain consistent economic and population
9 growth and is better able to balance (i) the livability of the community, (ii) the
10 provision of government services, (iii) full employment and full housing, and
11 (iv) maintaining public order.
12 (2) Grants may only be awarded to local governments that REDD determines
13 have satisfactorily demonstrated that a lack of community amenities within
14 their locale has previously prevented them from attracting business
15 investment and promoting the population and revenue growth necessary to
16 stimulate job and revenue growth within their community. Examples of
17 relevant community amenities include restaurants, hotels, child care centers,
18 grocery stores, pools, parks, and hiking trails.
19 (3) Grants shall be awarded on a competitive basis based upon, at a minimum, the
20 following factors: (i) the amount of non-State investment in the project, (ii)
21 the projected wage and job growth from the project, and (iii) the existence of
22 demonstrated community support for the project. REDD shall determine the
23 relative weight due and priority of the factors listed in (i) through (iii) of this
24 subdivision, and any other factors it may choose to include, and shall publicize
25 that information on its website.
26 (4) Grants awarded shall require a match in the amount of one dollar ($1.00) of
27 non-State funds for every one dollar ($1.00) of State funds.
28 (5) Individual grant awards may not exceed two million dollars ($2,000,000).
29 (6) All county and municipal governments seeking grant funding under this
30 section shall submit an application for funding, on a form and in a manner
31 approved by the Department, as a necessary precondition to receiving funding.
32 (7) The application required by subdivision (6) of this subsection shall include, at
33 a minimum, a requirement that the applicant will use any grant funding it
34 receives for projects that are part of a comprehensive local government plan
35 intended to promote (i) downtown revitalization or (ii) community
36 development, or both.
37 SECTION 3.(a) Appropriation. – There is appropriated from the General Fund to
38 the Department of Commerce the sum of forty million dollars ($40,000,000) in nonrecurring
39 funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be used for the purposes consistent with subsection (b) of
40 this section. Notwithstanding G.S. 143C-1-2(b), funds allocated in this section shall not revert
41 but shall remain available for purposes consistent with this section until June 30, 2027. The
42 Department may use up to five percent (5%) of the funds appropriated in this section for
43 administration of the Revitalization Program created in subsection (b) of this section.
44 SECTION 3.(b) Downtown Revitalization Grant Program. – The funds allocated to
45 the Department by subsection (a) of this section shall be used to create the Downtown
46 Revitalization Grant Program (the Revitalization Program). The Revitalization Program shall
47 provide grants to local governments and municipal service district administrator entities for
48 purposes consistent with this section. All county and municipal governments and municipal
49 service district administrator entities within the State are eligible to apply for funding under the
50 Revitalization Program. The Department shall establish guidelines for providing for
Page 2 DRH10266-NIfa-4A

1 administration of the Revitalization Program. Those guidelines shall include the following
2 provisions, which shall apply to each grant from the Revitalization Program:
3 (1) Grants may only be awarded for the purpose of amenitizing the downtown
4 areas of eligible recipients in a manner that the Department determines is
5 likely to enhance the overall appeal, functionality, or livability of a downtown
6 area by attracting the people, businesses, or investment necessary to revitalize
7 a downtown area and create a vibrant, desirable place to live, work, and visit.
8 (2) Individual grant awards may not exceed two million dollars ($2,000,000).
9 (3) All county and municipal governments and municipal service district
10 administrator entities seeking grant funding under this section shall submit an
11 application for funding, on a form and in a manner approved by the
12 Department, as a necessary precondition to receiving funding.
13 (4) The application required by subdivision (3) of this subsection shall include, at
14 a minimum, a requirement that the applicant will use any grant funding it
15 receives for projects that are part of a comprehensive local government plan
16 intended to promote (i) downtown revitalization or (ii) community
17 development, or both.
18 SECTION 4. No later than September 15 of each year, the Department shall submit
19 a report on the activities conducted with the funds appropriated in this act to the Joint Legislative
20 Oversight Committee on Agriculture and Natural and Economic Resources and the Fiscal
21 Research Division. The information provided in the report shall include, at a minimum, the
22 number of grants awarded and the name and location of all local governments and municipal
23 service district administrator entities receiving grants. Funds allocated under this act are not
24 subject to the provisions of G.S. 143C-6-23.
25 SECTION 5. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
