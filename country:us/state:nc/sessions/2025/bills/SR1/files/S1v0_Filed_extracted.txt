17 I. ORDER OF BUSINESS
18 RULE 1. Rules controlling the Senate of North Carolina and its committees. –
19 The following rules shall govern and control all actions and procedures of the Senate and its
20 committees.
21 RULE 1.1. Emergencies. – In the event of a disaster, natural or otherwise, that
22 precludes the General Assembly from meeting in the Legislative Building, the members will be
23 notified by the President Pro Tempore where and when the Senate will convene.
24 RULE 2. Convening hour. – (a) The Presiding Officer shall take the Chair at the
25 hour fixed by the Senate upon adjournment on the preceding legislative day and shall call the
26 members to order. In case the Senate adjourned on the preceding legislative day without having
27 fixed the hour of reconvening, the Senate shall reconvene on the next legislative day at 2:00 P.M.
28 (b) At the hour fixed by the Senate upon adjournment on the preceding legislative
29 day, or at the hour to which the Senate has previously recessed on that same legislative day, and
30 upon direction from the President Pro Tempore of the Senate or the Chair of the Committee on
31 Rules and Operations of the Senate, the Principal Clerk of the Senate may convene the Senate
32 for the purpose of announcing to the Chamber that the Senate stands in recess until a later,
33 specified hour.
34 RULE 3. Opening the session. – The Presiding Officer shall, upon order being
35 obtained, have the sessions of the Senate opened with prayer.

4 RULE 10. Points of order. – (a) The Presiding Officer shall preserve order and
5 decorum and proceed with the business of the Senate according to the rules adopted. The
6 Presiding Officer shall decide all questions of order, subject to an appeal to the Chair of the
7 Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate, whose decision may be appealed to the Senate
8 by any member, on which appeal no member shall speak more than once unless by leave of the
9 Senate. A two-thirds vote of the membership of the Senate present and voting is necessary to
10 sustain any appeal from the ruling of the Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the
11 Senate.
12 (b) In the event the Senate Rules do not provide for or cover any point of order
13 raised by any Senator, the rules of the United States House of Representatives shall govern.
14 (c) When a Senator is called to order, that Senator shall take the assigned seat
15 until the Presiding Officer determines whether that Senator was in order or not; if decided to be
16 out of order, that Senator shall not proceed without the permission of the Senate; and every
17 question of order shall be decided by the Presiding Officer, subject to an appeal to the Chair of
18 the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate, whose decision may be appealed to the
19 Senate by any Senator; and if a Senator is called to order for words spoken, the words to which
20 an exception is made shall be immediately preserved by the Principal Clerk, so that the Presiding
21 Officer, Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate, or Senate may be better
22 able to judge the matter.
23 RULE 11. Debating and voting by Lieutenant Governor. – The Lieutenant
24 Governor, as President of the Senate, being a Constitutional Officer, shall not have the right to
25 debate any question or to address the Senate upon any proposition unless by permission of the
26 majority of members present and shall have the right to vote only when there is a tie vote upon
27 any question or election.
28 RULE 12. Obtaining recognition. – (a) When any Senator is about to speak in debate
29 or deliver any matter to the Senate, that Senator shall rise and respectfully address the Presiding
30 Officer. No member shall speak further until recognized by the Presiding Officer. The Presiding
31 Officer shall recognize the first to rise and, when two or more members rise at the same time, the
32 Presiding Officer shall name the member to speak, giving priority to the President Pro Tempore,
33 the Deputy President Pro Tempore, or the Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of
34 the Senate.
35 (b) A Senator who has the floor may yield the floor to another Senator only for
36 the purpose of allowing another Senator to state a question. Only the Presiding Officer may award
37 the floor to any Senator.
38 (c) A Senator who has obtained the floor may be interrupted only for the
39 following reasons:
40 (1) A request that the member speaking yield for a question;
41 (2) A point of order; or
42 (3) A parliamentary inquiry.
43 (d) When a Senator refers to a bill, the bill number and short title must be used.

45 RULE 14. Limitations on individual debate. – (a) No Senator shall speak on the
46 same reading more than (i) twice on the main question, (ii) 30 minutes for the first speech and
47 15 minutes for the second speech, and (iii) once on any motion or appeal, and then no longer than
48 10 minutes; provided, however, the President Pro Tempore, the Deputy President Pro Tempore,
49 or the Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate may alter some or all of the
50 limitations in this rule following consultation with the minority leader of the Senate and approval
51 of the majority of the members present.

3 RULE 15. Senatorial Statements. – A Senatorial Statement may be utilized by a
4 Senator to recognize constituents and events of significance and when a Resolution is not
5 permitted under Rule 40.1. A Senatorial Statement may not be used to explain a vote, debate a
6 bill, or in any way disrupt the regular business of the Senate. A Senatorial Statement shall be in
7 writing and in a form prescribed by the Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the
8 Senate. A Senatorial Statement shall speak in the voice of the Senator submitting the Statement,
9 is not a statement by the Senate, and is not subject to a vote by the Senate. A Senator may use a
10 Moment of Personal Privilege under Rule 15.1 for a time not to exceed three minutes to read
11 some or all of a Senatorial Statement provided the Presiding Officer determines the question
12 raised in the Statement is one of privilege. Upon motion supported by a majority present and
13 voting, a Senatorial Statement may be spread upon the Journal.
14 RULE 15.1. Moments of Personal Privilege. – (a) Upon recognition by the Presiding
15 Officer for that purpose, any Senator may speak for a time not exceeding three minutes on a
16 Moment of Personal Privilege.
17 (b) A Moment of Personal Privilege may not be used to explain a vote, debate a
18 bill, or in any way disrupt the regular business of the Senate, nor shall such opportunities be used
19 to solicit support or sponsors for any bill. The Presiding Officer shall determine if the question
20 raised is one of privilege and shall, without the point of order being raised, enforce this rule,
21 subject to an appeal to the Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate whose
22 decision may be appealed to the Senate by any Senator, on which appeal no member shall speak
23 more than once unless by leave of the Senate. A two-thirds vote of the membership of the Senate
24 present and voting is necessary to sustain any appeal from the ruling of the Chair of the
25 Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate.
26 RULE 15.2. Explanation of vote. – Any Senator may explain that Senator's vote on
27 any bill that day by obtaining permission of the Presiding Officer for that purpose. No more than
28 three minutes shall be consumed in such explanation.
29 RULE 16. Final orders of business. – Senatorial Statements, Moments of Personal
30 Privilege, and explanations of votes shall be the last orders of the Senate's business that day and
31 explanations of votes shall be made after the final vote has been taken.
32 RULE 17. General decorum. – (a) Male Senators and male visitors shall not wear
33 any head covering in the Senate Chamber while the Senate is in session, unless his religion
34 requires his head to be covered. All persons on the Senate floor while the Senate is in session
35 shall be dressed in business attire, including coat and tie for men.
36 (b) No derogatory remark reflecting personally upon any Senator or Senators
37 shall be in order upon the floor of the Senate unless preceded by a motion or resolution of censure.
38 (c) When the Presiding Officer is putting a question, or a division by counting is
39 in progress, no Senator shall walk out of or across the Chamber, nor when a Senator is speaking,
40 pass between that Senator and the Presiding Officer.
41 (d) When a motion to adjourn or for recess is affirmatively determined, no
42 member or officer shall leave that member's or officer's place until adjournment or recess is
43 declared by the Presiding Officer.
44 (e) Smoking shall not be allowed in the Senate Chamber.
45 (f) No remark soliciting the donation of funds for the support of any person or
46 organization shall be in order upon the floor of the Senate, unless the remark has some relevance
47 to a bill or resolution before the body. No article of any kind soliciting business or donations may
48 be placed by any person anywhere in the Senate Chamber or in any Senate office.
49 (g) The President Pro Tempore may authorize and provide for the broadcasting
50 of Senate sessions via television or internet.

14 III. MOTIONS
15 RULE 18. Motions generally. – Any motion shall be reduced to writing, if requested
16 by the Presiding Officer or a Senator, and read by the Presiding Officer or Reading Clerk before
17 the same is debated. Any motion may be withdrawn by the introducer at any time before decision
18 or amendment. No motion relating to a bill shall be in order which does not identify the bill by
19 its number and short title. Except as otherwise specifically provided in these rules, no second is
20 required.
21 RULE 19. Motion; order of precedence. – When a question is before the Senate, no
22 motion shall be received except those herein specified, which motions shall have precedence as
23 follows:
24 (1) To adjourn.
25 (2) To resolve into the Committee of the Whole Senate.
26 (3) To lay on the table.
27 (4) For the previous question.
28 (5) To postpone indefinitely.
29 (6) To postpone to a certain day.
30 (7) To re-refer to a standing committee.
31 (8) To refer to a select committee.
32 (9) To amend.
33 RULE 20. Motions requiring a second. – The motions to adjourn, to resolve into the
34 Committee of the Whole Senate, to lay on the table, and to call for the previous question shall be
35 seconded and decided without debate.
36 RULE 21. Motions to postpone to certain day and to commit. – The respective
37 motions to postpone to a certain day, to resolve into the Committee of the Whole Senate, or to
38 commit to a standing or select committee shall preclude debate on the main question.
39 RULE 22. Motion to substitute. – Subject to Rule 19, a member may offer a motion
40 to substitute to any motion, except the motions for the previous question, to table, to resolve into
41 the Committee of the Whole Senate, or to adjourn. No motion to substitute shall be offered to a
42 motion to substitute.
43 RULE 23. Motion for previous question. – (a) The previous question may be moved
44 upon a pending single motion, any pending amendment or amendments, and/or the pending bill
45 to its passage on that reading or all readings or the pending resolution to its adoption. An
46 authorized Senator moving the previous question shall specify to what the motion applies. Unless
47 specified, the motion shall be deemed to apply to the current question.
48 (b) The previous question shall be as follows: "Shall the main question be now
49 put?" and until it is decided shall preclude all amendments and debate. If this question is decided
50 in the affirmative, the "main question" shall be on the passage of the bill, resolution, or other

13 RULE 24. Motion to reconsider. – (a) When a question has been once put and
14 decided, any Senator who voted in the majority may move to reconsider the vote thereof. No
15 motion for reconsideration shall be in order unless made on the same day or on the next legislative
16 day on which the vote took place. When the next legislative day has by motion of the Senate been
17 restricted as to matters which may be considered, a motion to reconsider shall be in order on the
18 next succeeding day upon which regular business is conducted. No question shall be reconsidered
19 more than once.
20 (b) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this rule, a motion to reconsider is in order
21 at any time if made by the Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate or the
22 President Pro Tempore for the sole purpose of correcting grammatical errors in bills in the
23 possession of the Senate.
24 (c) If a bill has gone out of the possession of the Senate and a motion to reconsider
25 under these rules is passed, the bill shall not be enrolled unless it again passes third reading. The
26 Principal Clerk shall notify the House of Representatives and the Enrolling Clerk of any action
27 under this subsection.
28 RULE 24.1. Motion to adjourn; motion to stand in recess; standard stipulations.
29 – A motion to adjourn or stand in recess shall be subject to receipt and referral of committee
30 reports, the introduction of bills, referral and re-referral of bills, reading of Senatorial Statements,
31 receipt of House messages, ratification of bills, announcement of committee assignments, and
32 messages from the Governor.
33 A Senator may request that a motion to adjourn be made in honor or in memory of a
34 person or an event of significance. The request shall be in writing and shall be submitted to the
35 Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate on the day prior to the day the
36 motion to adjourn is to be made.
37 IV. VOTING
38 RULE 25. Use of electronic voting system. – (a) Votes on the following questions
39 shall be taken on the electronic voting system, and the ayes and noes shall be recorded on the
40 Journal:
41 (1) All questions on which the Constitution of North Carolina requires that the
42 ayes and noes be taken and recorded on the Journal;
43 (2) All questions on which a call for the ayes and noes under Rule 26(b) has been
44 sustained;
45 (3) Second and third readings of bills proposing amendment of the Constitution
46 of North Carolina; and
47 (4) The vote on approval of a bill that was vetoed by the Governor.
48 (b) Votes on the following questions shall be taken on the electronic voting
49 system, and the resulting totals shall be recorded on the Journal:

1 (1) Second reading of all public bills, all amendments to public bills offered after
2 second reading, third reading if a public bill was amended after second
3 reading, and all conference reports on public bills.
4 (2) Any other question upon direction of the Presiding Officer or upon motion of
5 any Senator supported by one-fifth of the Senators present.
6 (c) When the electronic voting system is used, the Presiding Officer shall fix and
7 announce the time, not to exceed one minute, which shall be allowed for voting on the question
8 before the Senate. The system shall be set to lock automatically and to record the vote when that
9 time has expired. Once the system has locked and recorded a vote, the vote shall be printed by
10 the system.
11 (d) The voting station at each Senator's desk in the Chamber shall be used only
12 by the Senator to whom the station is assigned. Under no circumstances shall any other person
13 vote at a Senator's station. It is a breach of the ethical obligation of a Senator either to request
14 that another vote at the requesting Senator's station or to vote at another Senator's station. The
15 Presiding Officer shall enforce this rule without exception.
16 (e) When the electronic voting system is used, the Presiding Officer shall state
17 the question and shall then state substantially the following: "All in favor vote 'aye'; all opposed
18 vote 'no'; _____ seconds will be allowed for voting on this question; the Clerk will record the
19 vote." After the machine locks and records the vote, the Presiding Officer shall announce the
20 vote and declare the result, and no member may vote thereafter.
21 (f) One copy of the machine printout of the vote record shall be filed in the Office
22 of the Principal Clerk, and one copy shall be filed in the Legislative Library where it shall be
23 open to public inspection.
24 (g) When the Presiding Officer ascertains that the electronic voting system is
25 inoperative before a vote is taken or while a vote is being taken on the electronic system, the
26 Presiding Officer shall announce that fact to the Senate, and any partial electronic system voting
27 record shall be voided. In such a case, if the Constitution of North Carolina or the Rules of the
28 Senate require a call of the ayes and noes, the Clerk shall call the roll of the Senate, and the ayes
29 and noes shall be taken manually and shall be recorded on the Journal. All other votes shall be
30 taken as prescribed in Senate Rule 26. If, after a vote is taken on the electronic system, it is
31 discovered that a malfunction caused an error in the electronic system printout, the Presiding
32 Officer shall direct the Reading Clerk and the Principal Clerk to verify and correct the printout
33 record and so advise the Senate.
34 (h) For the purpose of identifying motions on which the vote is taken on the
35 electronic system (the identification codes having no relation to the order of precedence of

17 RULE 28. Dividing question. – (a) If a bill is subject to division into separate parts
18 so that each part states a separate and distinct proposition capable of standing alone, a Senator
19 may move that the question be divided. The motion shall:
20 (1) Be in writing,
21 (2) Be submitted to the Principal Clerk at the time the motion is made, and
22 (3) Clearly state how the question is to be divided.
23 Upon a majority vote of the membership of the Senate present and voting, the motion shall be
24 adopted.
25 (b) If the motion to divide the question is adopted, then there shall be no further
26 amendment or debate on any of the distinct propositions.
27 (c) If the question is divided and any part thereof fails, then the bill or resolution
28 and any pending amendments shall be removed from the calendar and re-referred to the
29 committee from which the bill or resolution was reported.
30 (d) Only one motion to divide the question shall be in order during consideration
31 of a bill or resolution.
32 RULE 29. Duty to vote; excuses. – (a) Every Senator who is within the Senate
33 Chamber when the question is stated by the Presiding Officer shall vote thereon unless that
34 Senator is excused by the Senate.
35 (b) A Senator who is a member of a committee shall, upon request, be excused
36 from deliberations and voting on the bill while it is before the committee. The Senator must make
37 the request to the chair of the committee when the bill is first taken up for consideration and
38 before any motion or vote on the bill or any amendment to the bill. The Senator making the
39 request for excuse in committee must renew that request for excuse on the floor of the Senate as
40 set forth in this rule.
41 (c) Any Senator may move to be excused at any time from voting on any matter.
42 The Senator may make a brief statement of the reasons for the motion which question shall be
43 taken without debate on the motion.
44 (d) The Senator may send forward to the Principal Clerk, on a form provided by
45 the Clerk, a concise statement of the reason for the motion, and the Clerk shall include this
46 statement in the Journal.
47 (e) The Senator so excused shall not debate the bill or any amendment to the bill,
48 vote on the bill, offer or vote on any amendment to the bill, or offer or vote on any motion
49 concerning the bill at that reading, any subsequent reading, or any subsequent consideration of
50 the bill.

11 RULE 55. Amending titles of bills. – When a bill is materially modified or the scope

16 RULE 56. Corrections of typographical errors in bills. – The Enrolling Clerk is

18 ratification. Before the correction is made, the Enrolling Clerk shall have the approval of the
19 Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate or, in the Chair's absence, the
20 Vice-Chair of said Committee, the President Pro Tempore, or the Deputy President Pro Tempore.
21 RULE 56.1. Amendments and committee substitutes adopted by the House to
22 bills originating in the Senate. – (a) Whenever the House has adopted an amendment or a
23 committee substitute for a bill originating in the Senate, and has returned the bill to the Senate
24 for concurrence in that amendment or committee substitute, the Senate may not concur in that
25 amendment or committee substitute until the next legislative day following the day on which the
26 Senate receives that measure, unless otherwise calendared by the President Pro Tempore or the
27 Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate pursuant to Rule 47.
28 (b) The Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate, (or in that
29 Chair's absence the President Pro Tempore) may, or upon motion supported by a majority of the
30 membership of the Senate present and voting shall, refer the bill to an appropriate committee for
31 consideration of the amendment or committee substitute.
32 (c) The Presiding Officer shall, in placing the bill on the calendar, rule whether
33 the amendment or committee substitute is a material amendment under Article II, Section 23, of
34 the State's Constitution. If the measure is referred to committee, the committee shall:
35 (1) Report the bill with the recommendation either that the Senate do concur or
36 that the Senate do not concur; and
37 (2) Advise the Presiding Officer as to whether or not the amendment or committee
38 substitute is a material amendment under Article II, Section 23, of the State's
39 Constitution.
40 (d) If the amendment or committee substitute for a bill is not a material
41 amendment, the question before the Senate shall be concurrence. In the event there is more than
42 one House amendment, the question shall be concurrence in all the House amendments, and the
43 question may not be divided, notwithstanding Rule 28. The question that shall be put before the
44 Senate by the Presiding Officer shall be: "Does the Senate concur in the House amendments
45 (committee substitute) to S.B.______?".
46 (e) If the amendment or committee substitute for a bill is a material amendment,
47 the receiving of that bill on messages shall constitute first reading, and the question before the
48 Senate shall be concurrence on second reading. If the motion is passed, the question then shall
49 be concurrence on third reading on the next legislative day.
50 (f) No committee substitute adopted by the House to a bill originating in the
51 Senate may be amended by the Senate.

1 RULE 57. Conference committee. – The President Pro Tempore of the Senate, or in
2 the President Pro Tempore's absence, the Deputy President Pro Tempore, upon motion, may
3 appoint a conference committee when the Senate fails to concur in amendments or committee
4 substitutes put by the House to a bill originating in the Senate, or when the House of
5 Representatives fails to concur in amendments or committee substitutes put by the Senate to a
6 bill originating in the House. Senate conferees shall include the primary sponsor of the bill. In
7 considering the bill committed to the conferees, only such matters as are germane to the bill shall
8 be considered by the conferees, and the conference report shall deal only with such matters. The
9 matters referred to the conference committee by the conference committee chairs shall go to and
10 be considered by the conferees appointed by the Senate and the House of Representatives. While
11 the bill is in conference committee, the Senate's position shall be determined by a majority of the
12 Senate conferees. Upon agreement by the Senate and House of Representatives, a conference
13 report shall be drafted reflecting the matters considered and agreed upon by the conferees. The
14 conference report shall not be amended. A conference report in order under this Rule when
15 reported to the Senate shall be calendared for consideration of the question of adoption on any
16 date specified by the Chair of the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate, or in the
17 absence of the Chair, by the President Pro Tempore. In the absence of any such specification it
18 shall be calendared for the next legislative day.
19 RULE 57.1. Germaneness of amendment or committee substitute. – (a) All floor
20 amendments shall be germane to the subject matter of the measure under consideration. The
21 question of germaneness of the proposed floor amendment is in order at any time the measure is
22 before the body prior to final action on its adoption.
23 (b) In committee, the germaneness of amendments or committee substitutes shall
24 be determined by the chair of the committee in which such amendments or committee substitutes
25 are offered.
26 RULE 58. Certification of passage of bills. – The Principal Clerk shall certify the
27 passage of bills by the Senate, with the date thereof, together with the fact whether passed by
28 vote of three-fifths or two-thirds of the membership of the Senate present and voting, whenever
29 such vote may be required by the Constitution or laws of the State.
30 RULE 59. Transmittal of bills to House. – No bill shall be sent from the Senate on
31 the day of its passage except on the last day of the session, unless otherwise ordered by a majority
32 vote of the membership of the Senate present and voting.
33 RULE 59.1. Engrossment. – Bills which originate in the Senate and that are amended
34 shall be engrossed before being sent to the House.
35 RULE 59.2. Vetoed bills. – (a) Unless directed otherwise by the President Pro
36 Tempore of the Senate on behalf of the Senate, the Principal Clerk or an employee designated by
37 the Principal Clerk shall receive Senate bills not approved by the Governor. The veto message
38 shall be read in the Senate on the next legislative day following its receipt by the Principal Clerk.
39 (b) Upon a veto message's being read in the Senate, the Chair of the Committee
40 on Rules and Operations of the Senate shall either refer the bill and the Governor's objections
41 and veto message to committee or place the bill on the calendar for a day certain. The Chair of
42 the Committee on Rules and Operations of the Senate or the Chair's designee shall give the Senate
43 Minority Leader at least 24 hours' notice that a vetoed bill may be considered by the Senate.

37 All administrative rules, regulations, and orders involving all individuals employed to perform

40 RULE 74. Public hearings. – Any Senator may request in writing a public hearing
41 by the committee considering the bill on a public bill. Requests may be granted at the discretion
42 of the chair. Notice shall be given not less than five calendar days prior to public hearings. Such
43 notices shall be issued as information for the press, and the information shall be posted in the
44 places designated by the Principal Clerk.
45 RULE 75. Public hearings; filing of written statements. – Persons desiring to
46 appear and be heard at a public hearing are encouraged to file with the chair of the committee a
47 brief or a written statement of the remarks to be made at least 24 hours before the time of the
48 hearing.
49 RULE 76. Voting in joint sessions. – When the Senate sits jointly with the House,
50 either in committee or in joint session, the Senate reserves the right to vote separately.
