41 "Premixed Cocktail Franchise Law.
42 "§ 18B-1400. Purpose.
43 Pursuant to the authority of the State under the Twenty-First Amendment to the United States
44 Constitution, the General Assembly finds that regulation of the business relations between
45 premixed cocktail manufacturers and importers and the wholesalers of such products is necessary
46 to:
47 (1) Maintain stability and healthy competition in the premixed cocktail industry
48 in this State.
49 (2) Promote and maintain a sound, stable, and viable three-tier system of
50 distribution of premixed cocktails to the public.

1 (3) Promote the compelling interest of the public in fair business relations
2 between premixed cocktail suppliers and wholesalers, and in the continuation
3 of premixed cocktail franchise agreements on a fair basis.
4 (4) Maintain a uniform system of control over the sale, purchase, and distribution
5 of premixed cocktails in the State.
6 (5) Prevent unfair or unlawful trade practices by enabling wholesalers to refuse
7 to participate in such practices without fear of arbitrary or unlawful retribution
8 from suppliers.
9 (6) Provide wholesalers with rights and remedies in addition to those existing by
10 contract or common law.
11 (7) Govern all agreements between suppliers and wholesalers, including any
12 renewals or amendments.
13 (8) Protect wholesalers against unfair treatment by suppliers.
14 (9) Preserve investments made by wholesalers in franchise agreements through
15 minimization of arbitrary termination.
16 (10) Promote consumer choice by ensuring an independent wholesale distribution
17 tier that enables wholesalers to distribute competing products of other
18 suppliers.
19 (11) Prevent vertical integration of the premixed cocktail market.
20 "§ 18B-1401. Definitions.
21 The following definitions apply in this Article:
22 (1) Supplier. – A manufacturer, bottler, or importer of premixed cocktails,
23 including anyone who holds a distillery, premixed cocktail importer, or
24 nonresident premixed cocktail vendor permit.
25 (2) Wholesaler. – The holder of a premixed cocktail wholesaler permit.
26 "§ 18B-1402. Franchise agreement.
27 (a) Nature of Agreement. – A franchise agreement is a commercial relationship between
28 a wholesaler and supplier of a definite or indefinite duration, whether written or oral, including:
29 (1) A relationship whereby a wholesaler is granted the right to offer and sell the
30 brands of premixed cocktails offered by the supplier; or
31 (2) An agreement whereby a supplier grants to a wholesaler a license to use a
32 trade name, trademark, service mark, or related characteristic and in which
33 there is a community of interest in the marking of the products of the supplier
34 by lease or otherwise.
35 (b) Existence of Agreement. – A franchise agreement as described in subsection (a) of
36 this section exists when:
37 (1) The supplier has shipped malt premixed cocktails to a wholesaler or accepted
38 an order for premixed cocktails from the wholesaler;
39 (2) A wholesaler has paid or the supplier has accepted payment for an order of
40 premixed cocktails intended for sale within this State;
41 (3) The supplier and wholesaler have filed with the Commission a distribution
42 agreement as required by G.S. 18B-1403; or
43 (4) A supplier acquires the right to manufacture a premixed cocktail product, or
44 the trade name for such product, or the right to distribute a product, for which
45 a wholesaler has a franchise agreement.
46 "§ 18B-1403. Filing of distribution agreement; no discrimination.
47 (a) Filing. – It is unlawful for a supplier to provide premixed cocktails to a wholesaler
48 unless the Commission has received notification from the supplier designating the brands of the
49 supplier which the wholesaler is authorized to sell and the territory in which such sales may take
50 place. If the supplier sells several brands, the agreement need not apply to all brands. A franchise
51 agreement applies to all supplier products under the same brand name, and different categories

21 "§ 18B-1405. Cause for termination of franchise agreement.
22 (a) Meaning of Good Cause. – Good cause for altering or terminating a franchise
23 agreement, or failing to renew or causing a wholesaler to resign from such an agreement, exists
24 when the wholesaler fails to comply with provisions of the agreement which are reasonable,
25 material, not unconscionable, and which are not discriminatory when compared with the
26 provisions imposed, by their terms or in the manner of enforcement, on other similarly situated
27 wholesaler by the supplier. The meaning of good cause set out in this section may not be modified
28 or superseded by provisions in a written franchise agreement prepared by a supplier if those
29 provisions purport to define good cause in a manner different than specified in this section. In
30 any dispute over alteration, termination, failure to renew, or causing a wholesaler to resign from
31 a franchise agreement, the burden is on the supplier to establish that good cause exists for the
32 action.
33 (b) Notice of Cause. – At least 90 days before altering, terminating, or failing to renew a
34 franchise agreement for good cause, the supplier must give the wholesaler written notice of the
35 intended action and the specific reasons for it. If the cause for the alteration, termination, or
36 failure to renew is subject to correction by the wholesaler, and the wholesaler makes such
37 correction within 45 days of receipt of the notice, the notice shall be void.
38 (c) Termination for Cause without Advance Notice. – A supplier may terminate or fail to
39 renew a franchise agreement for any of the following reasons and the termination shall be
40 complete upon receipt by the wholesaler of a written notice of the termination and the reason:

42 or the filing of any petition by or against the wholesaler under any bankruptcy
43 or receivership law which materially affects the wholesaler's ability to remain
44 in business.
45 (2) Revocation of the wholesaler's State or federal permit or license for more than
46 30 days.
47 (3) Conviction of the wholesaler, or of a partner or individual who owns ten
48 percent (10%) or more of the partnership or stock of the wholesaler, of a
49 felony which might reasonably be expected to adversely affect the goodwill

32 "§ 18B-1406. Remedies for wrongful termination.
33 (a) Injunctive Relief. – A wholesaler whose franchise agreement is altered, terminated,
34 or not renewed in violation of this Article may bring an action to enjoin such unlawful alteration,

36 has its principal place of business or in any county in which the wholesaler receives or distributes
37 the products in issue. Any injunction issued pursuant to this subsection shall require the

39 to otherwise serve the territory.
40 (b) Monetary Damages. – In lieu of injunctive relief, a wholesaler whose franchise
41 agreement is altered, terminated, or not renewed in violation of this Article shall be entitled to
42 recover monetary damages from the supplier. The amount to which the wholesaler is entitled

45 including any State and local taxes paid on the inventory by the wholesaler,
46 plus a reasonable charge for handling of the products upon surrender of the
47 inventory to the supplier.
48 (2) The fair market value of all assets, including ancillary businesses of the
49 wholesaler used in distributing the supplier's products. The total compensation
50 to be paid to the wholesaler shall be reduced, however, by any amount
51 received by the wholesaler from sale of assets of the business used in

6 "§ 18B-1407. Transfer or merger of wholesaler's business.
7 (a) Right of Transfer to Designated Family Member. – An individual's interest in a
8 wholesaler business, including the rights under the franchise agreement with the supplier, may
9 be transferred or assigned to a designated family member. The transfer or assignment shall not
10 be effective until written notice is given to the supplier, but the supplier's consent is not required
11 for the transfer or assignment. "Designated family member" means the wholesaler's spouse, child,
12 grandchild, parent, brother, sister, niece, or nephew. With respect to an incapacitated individual
13 having an ownership interest in a wholesaler, the term "designated family member" also means
14 the person appointed by the court as the conservator of such individual's property. The term also
15 includes the appointed and qualified personal representative and the testamentary trustee of a
16 deceased wholesaler.
17 (b) Approval of Certain Transfers and Mergers. – Upon notice to and approval by the
18 supplier, an individual owning an interest in a wholesaler may sell, assign, or transfer that
19 interest, including the wholesaler's rights under its franchise agreement with the supplier, to any
20 qualified person. Likewise, a wholesaler may merge with another wholesaler in the State,
21 transferring to the new wholesaler entity the merging wholesaler's existing franchise rights.
22 Within 30 days of receipt of notice of the intended sale, assignment, transfer, or merger, the
23 supplier shall request any additional relevant, material information reasonably necessary for
24 deciding whether to approve the transaction. The supplier shall have 30 days from receipt of that
25 information to object to the sale, assignment, transfer, or merger. The supplier may object only
26 if the proposed transferee, or the wholesalership resulting from the merger, fails to meet
27 qualifications and standards that are nondiscriminatory, material, reasonable, and consistently
28 applied to North Carolina wholesalers by the supplier. The burden shall be upon the supplier to
29 prove that the proposed transferee or merged wholesaler is not qualified. In determining whether
30 the proposed transferee or merged wholesaler is a qualified person, the supplier shall consider,
31 but is not limited to, the following factors:
32 (1) Whether the proposed transferee has the financial capacity to purchase the
33 wholesaler or the specified interest upon terms that will not jeopardize the
34 future operation of the business, or whether the new entity resulting from a
35 merger will have such financial capacity to operate successfully, and whether
36 under such ownership the wholesaler will be able to provide financial support
37 necessary to the successful operation of the business, including market
38 spending, capital expenditures, and any equity capitalization or refinancing
39 requirements.
40 (2) Whether the proposed transferee, or the new entity resulting from a merger,
41 has the proven business experience to hire and maintain a management team
42 to successfully operate the business.
43 (3) If the proposed transferee does not have experience in the premixed cocktail
44 business, whether the transferee has other experience to enable it to operate a

47 (4) Whether the proposed transferee, or a party to the merger, already is a
48 wholesaler for the supplier in a different territory and, if so, whether sufficient

22 (a) Meaning of Good Cause. – Good cause for altering or terminating a franchise

27 wholesaler by the supplier. The meaning of good cause set out in this section may not be modified

29 provisions purport to define good cause in a manner different than specified in this section. In
30 any dispute over alteration, termination, failure to renew, or causing a wholesaler to resign from

47 inventory to the supplier.
48 (2) The fair market value of all assets, including ancillary businesses of the

50 to be paid to the wholesaler shall be reduced, however, by any amount
