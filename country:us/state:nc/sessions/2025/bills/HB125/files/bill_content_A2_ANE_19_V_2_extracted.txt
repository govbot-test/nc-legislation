5 "(c) Collection Techniques. – The State Controller, in conjunction with the Office of the
6 Attorney General, shall establish policies and procedures to govern techniques for collection of
7 accounts receivable. These techniques may include use of credit reporting bureaus, judicial
8 remedies authorized by law, and administrative setoff by a reduction of a tax refund pursuant to
9 the Setoff Debt Collection Act, Chapter 105A of the General Statutes, or a reduction of another
10 payment, other than payroll, due from the State to a person to reduce or eliminate an account
11 receivable that the person owes the State.
12 The State Controller shall negotiate a contract with a third party to perform an audit and
13 collection process of inadvertent overpayments by State agencies to vendors as a result of pricing
14 errors, neglected rebates and discounts, miscalculated freight charges, unclaimed refunds,
15 erroneously paid excise taxes, and related errors. The third party shall be compensated only from
16 funds recovered as a result of the audit. Savings realized in excess of costs shall be transferred
17 from the agency to the Office of State Budget and Management and placed in a special reserve
18 account for future direction by the General Assembly. Any disputed savings shall be settled by
19 the State Controller. Subject to availability and appropriation by the General Assembly, the State
20 Controller may use recovered audit funds for computer systems maintenance and improvements,
21 financial reporting, governmental accounting training, debt collection, and e-commerce costs.
22 This paragraph does not apply to the purchase of medical services by State agencies or payments
23 used to reimburse or otherwise pay for health care services."".
