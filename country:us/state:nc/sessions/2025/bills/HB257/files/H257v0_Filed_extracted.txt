1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO APPROPRIATE FUNDS FOR VARIOUS PUBLIC PURPOSES IN SCOTLAND
3 AND HOKE COUNTIES.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Office of State
6 Budget and Management the sum of fifty-four million dollars ($54,000,000) in nonrecurring
7 funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to provide a directed grant to Hoke County to be used as
8 follows:
9 (1) Eleven million dollars ($11,000,000) for water and sewer needs.
10 (2) Ten million dollars ($10,000,000) for courthouse construction overages.
11 (3) Ten million dollars ($10,000,000) for construction of a new sheriff's office
12 and law enforcement center.
13 (4) Ten million dollars ($10,000,000) for construction of a new workforce
14 training center at Sandhills Community College.
15 (5) Five million dollars ($5,000,000) for construction of a new social services
16 building.
17 (6) Five million dollars ($5,000,000) for construction of a new mental
18 health/rehabilitation facility.
19 (7) Three million dollars ($3,000,000) for capital improvements for the County's
20 volunteer fire departments.
21 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Office of State
22 Budget and Management the sum of eighty-three million two hundred thousand dollars
23 ($83,200,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to provide a directed grant to
24 Scotland County to be used as follows:
25 (1) Eighty million dollars ($80,000,000) for construction of a new courthouse and
26 jail facility.
27 (2) One million dollars ($1,000,000) to enable the Parks and Recreation
28 Department to upgrade the County's splash pad.
29 (3) One million dollars ($1,000,000) for various public capital improvements.
30 (4) One million two hundred thousand dollars ($1,200,000) to build a new
31 EMS/fire substation in the Town of Wagram.
32 SECTION 3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Office of State
33 Budget and Management the sum of thirty-three million dollars ($33,000,000) in nonrecurring
34 funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to provide a directed grant to the City of Laurinburg to be
35 used as follows:
36 (1) Four million dollars ($4,000,000) for the Town's Main Street restoration.
