6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 14-415.1 reads as rewritten:
8 "§ 14-415.1. Possession of firearms, etc., by felon prohibited.
9 (a) It shall be is unlawful for any person who has been convicted of a felony to purchase,
10 own, possess, or have in his the person's custody, care, or control any firearm or any weapon of
11 mass death and destruction as defined in G.S. 14-288.8(c). For the purposes of this section, a
12 firearm is (i) any weapon, including a starter gun, which will or is designed to or may readily be
13 converted to expel a projectile by the action of an explosive, or its frame or receiver, or (ii) any
14 firearm muffler or firearm silencer. This section does not apply to an antique firearm, as defined
15 in G.S. 14-409.11.
16 Every person violating the provisions of this section shall be punished as subsection is guilty
17 of a Class G felon.felony.
18 (a1) A person who violates subsection (a) of this section during the commission or
19 attempted commission of a felony under (i) this Chapter or (ii) Article 5 of Chapter 90 of the
20 General Statutes is guilty of a Class F felony.
21 (a2) A person who violates subsection (a) of this section and brandishes a firearm or a
22 weapon of mass death and destruction during the commission or attempted commission of a
23 felony under (i) this Chapter or (ii) Article 5 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is guilty of a
24 Class D felony. For the purposes of this subsection, to brandish is to display all or part of the
25 firearm or weapon of mass death and destruction or otherwise make the presence of the firearm
26 or weapon of mass death and destruction known to another person.
27 (a3) A person who violates subsection (a) of this section and discharges a firearm or a
28 weapon of mass death and destruction during the commission or attempted commission of a
29 felony under (i) this Chapter or (ii) Article 5 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is guilty of a
30 Class C felony.
31 (b) Prior convictions which cause disentitlement under this section shall only include:
32 (1) Felony convictions in North Carolina that occur before, on, or after December
33 1, 1995; and
34 (2) Repealed by Session Laws 1995, c. 487, s. 3, effective December 1, 1995.
35 (3) Violations of criminal laws of other states or of the United States that occur
36 before, on, or after December 1, 1995, and that are substantially similar to the
