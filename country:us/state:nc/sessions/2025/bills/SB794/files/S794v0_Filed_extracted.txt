8 SECTION 1.(a) Chapter 15B of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
9 Article to read:
10 "Article 3.
11 "North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Act.
12 "§ 15B-50. Short title.
13 This Article may be cited as the "North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Act."
14 "§ 15B-51. Definitions.
15 As used in this Article, the following definitions apply, unless the context requires otherwise:
16 (1) Assistance Fund. – The North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Fund.
17 (2) Commission. – The Governor's Crime Commission.
18 (3) Grant Program. – The North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance
19 Competitive Grant Program.
20 (4) Victim. – A person who suffered physical, sexual, financial, or emotional
21 harm as a result of the commission of a crime.
22 (5) Victim assistance program. – A program operated by a public agency or
23 nonprofit organization that (i) provides services to victims of crime and (ii)
24 demonstrates a record of providing effective services to victims of crime.
25 "§ 15B-52. North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Fund.
26 (a) Fund Created. – The North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Fund is established
27 as a special fund within the Department of Public Safety. The Governor's Crime Commission is
28 responsible for administering the Assistance Fund using personnel and other administrative
29 resources of the Department of Public Safety.
30 (b) Fund Purpose. – The Assistance Fund shall be utilized to distribute grant funds
31 pursuant to the North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Grant Program created by this
32 Article.
33 (c) Fund Sources. – The Assistance Fund may receive any of the following:
34 (1) Funds appropriated by the General Assembly.
35 (2) Funds received pursuant to State law.
36 (3) Gifts, grants, or donations from any public or private sources.

1 "§ 15B-53. North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Competitive Grant Program.
2 (a) Grant Program. – There is established the North Carolina Victims of Crime
3 Assistance Competitive Grant Program to be administered by the Governor's Crime Commission
4 for the purpose of awarding and distributing grants to public or nonprofit organizations serving
5 residents of the State through victim assistance programs. The Grant Program shall be funded by
6 the North Carolina Victims of Crime Assistance Fund created by this Article.
7 (b) Grant Program Guidelines. – The Commission shall develop guidelines and
8 procedures for the administration of the Grant Program, including at a minimum the following
9 requirements and limitations:
10 (1) The maximum grant amount shall not exceed one million dollars
11 ($1,000,000).
12 (2) The maximum total award amount to a grantee during a single fiscal year shall
13 not exceed one million dollars ($1,000,000). For purposes of this subdivision,
14 the total award amount shall only include funds awarded in the fiscal year in
15 question and shall not include funds awarded to that grantee during prior fiscal
16 years, regardless of whether all or part of those funds awarded during prior
17 fiscal years remain unspent.
18 (3) Each grantee shall operate one or more victim assistance programs serving
19 victims in the State in at least one of the following ways:
20 a. Responding to the emotional and physical needs of crime victims.
21 b. Assisting victims of crime in stabilizing their lives after a
22 victimization.
23 c. Helping victims understand and participate in the criminal justice
24 system.
25 d. Providing victims of crime with a measure of safety and security.
26 (4) Each grant applicant shall include in its application a detailed explanation of
27 how the applicant plans to use the grant funds, including at a minimum each
28 of the following:
29 a. A description of the geographic area the organization serves and the
30 needs of victims in that area.
31 b. A plan to address the needs of victims using the grant funds.
32 c. A description of the organization's capacity to implement its plan to
33 address the needs of victims, including the organization's staffing
34 level, systems, partnerships, existing funding, and existing programs.
35 (5) No later than one year after the receipt of grant funds pursuant to the Grant
36 Program, grantees shall report to the Governor's Crime Commission each of
37 the following:
38 a. Progress on the development and implementation of its plan as
39 delineated in its grant application.
40 b. A detailed explanation of the uses of the grant funds.
41 c. The number of victims assisted by the use of the grant funds.
42 d. The amount of grant funds remaining at the time of reporting.
43 e. Any additional information required by the Commission.
44 (c) Reporting. – No later than March 1 of each year, the Governor's Crime Commission
45 shall report to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Justice and Public Safety regarding
46 the Grant Program, including at a minimum each of the following:
47 (1) The number, amount, and recipient of all grant funds awarded in the prior
48 calendar year.
49 (2) All information received pursuant to the grantee reports required by
50 subsection (b) of this section.
