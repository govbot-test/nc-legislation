GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 905
Short Title: Public School Part-Time Enrollment. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Cotham, Chesser, and Riddell (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Education - K-12, if favorable, Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and
Operations of the House
April 14, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REQUIRE LOCAL SCHOOL ADMINISTRATIVE UNITS AND CHARTER
3 SCHOOLS TO ALLOW PART-TIME ENROLLMENT OF STUDENTS THAT
4 OTHERWISE ATTEND NONPUBLIC SCHOOL.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. Article 25 of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
7 adding a new section to read:
8 "§ 115C-364.1. Admission for part-time students.
9 (a) Local school administrative units and charter schools shall allow students that attend
10 nonpublic school to enroll part time to access any courses, programs, or services offered by the
11 local school administrative unit or the charter school, so long as the student is eligible for
12 admission to public school under G.S. 115C-5 and G.S. 115C-364.
13 (b) For each student enrolled in public school part time pursuant to this section, the State
14 Board of Education shall allocate to the enrolling local school administrative unit or charter
15 school the following amounts:
16 (1) For students scheduled for instructional time equaling one-third or more of the
17 amount of instructional time used to determine full-time equivalency under
18 G.S. 115C-419, one hundred percent (100%) of the average per pupil
19 allocation for average daily membership for the local school administrative
20 unit in which the school is located.
21 (2) For students scheduled for instructional time equaling less than one-third of
22 the amount of instructional time used to determine full-time equivalency
23 under G.S. 115C-419, fifty percent (50%) of the average per pupil allocation
24 for average daily membership for the local school administrative unit in which
25 the school is located."
26 SECTION 2.(a) G.S. 115C-47 is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
27 "(70) To Allow Part-Time Students. – Local boards of education shall develop a
28 policy for students that attend nonpublic school to enroll part time in a public
29 school under the board's jurisdiction in accordance with G.S. 115C-364.1. The
30 board shall make the policy available on the board's website."
31 SECTION 2.(b) G.S. 115C-218.45 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
32 "(a1) Any student that attends nonpublic school is qualified to enroll in a charter school
33 part time in accordance with G.S. 115C-364.1. A charter school shall make information regarding

1 part-time enrollment available on the school's website. Students enrolled in a charter school part
2 time shall not count toward the enrollment growth of the charter school under G.S. 115C-218.7."
3 SECTION 3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
4 Public Instruction the sum of five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) in nonrecurring funds for
5 the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be allocated to local boards of education and charter schools for the
6 administrative costs of implementing the provisions of this act.
7 SECTION 4. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
8 Public Instruction the sum of two hundred seventy million dollars ($270,000,000) in recurring
9 funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to be allocated to local school administrative units and charter
10 schools to fund part-time students.
11 SECTION 5. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025, and applies to requests to
12 begin part-time enrollment in the 2025-2026 school year.
