17 CREATE NEW CRIMINAL OFFENSE FOR EXPOSING A CHILD TO A
18 CONTROLLED SUBSTANCE
19 SECTION 2.(a) Article 39 of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by
20 adding a new section to read:
21 "§ 14-318.7. Exposing a child to a controlled substance.
22 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
23 (1) Child. – Any person who is less than 16 years of age.
24 (2) Controlled substance. – A controlled substance, controlled substance
25 analogue, drug, marijuana, narcotic drug, opiate, opioid, opium poppy, poppy
26 straw, or targeted controlled substance, all as defined in G.S. 90-87.
27 (3) Ingest. – Any means used to take into the body, to eat or drink, or otherwise
28 consume or absorb into the body in any way.
29 (b) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
30 exposed to a controlled substance is guilty of a Class H felony.
31 (c) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
32 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance is
33 guilty of a Class E felony.
34 (d) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
35 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance,
36 resulting in serious physical injury as defined in G.S. 14-318.4, is guilty of a Class D felony.
37 (e) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
38 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance,
39 resulting in serious bodily injury as defined in G.S. 14-318.4, is guilty of a Class C felony.
40 (f) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
41 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance, and
42 the ingestion is the proximate cause of death, is guilty of a Class B1 felony.
43 (g) This section does not apply to a person that intentionally gives a child a controlled
44 substance that has been prescribed for the child by a licensed medical professional when given
45 to the child in the prescribed amount and manner."
46 SECTION 2.(b) This section becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to
47 offenses committed on or after that date.

18 CONTROLLED SUBSTANCE
19 SECTION 2.(a) Article 39 of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by
20 adding a new section to read:
21 "§ 14-318.7. Exposing a child to a controlled substance.
22 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
23 (1) Child. – Any person who is less than 16 years of age.
24 (2) Controlled substance. – A controlled substance, controlled substance
25 analogue, drug, marijuana, narcotic drug, opiate, opioid, opium poppy, poppy
26 straw, or targeted controlled substance, all as defined in G.S. 90-87.
27 (3) Ingest. – Any means used to take into the body, to eat or drink, or otherwise
28 consume or absorb into the body in any way.
29 (b) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
30 exposed to a controlled substance is guilty of a Class H felony.
31 (c) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
32 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance is
33 guilty of a Class E felony.
34 (d) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
35 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance,
36 resulting in serious physical injury as defined in G.S. 14-318.4, is guilty of a Class D felony.
37 (e) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
38 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance,
39 resulting in serious bodily injury as defined in G.S. 14-318.4, is guilty of a Class C felony.
40 (f) A person who knowingly, recklessly, or intentionally causes or permits a child to be
41 exposed to a controlled substance and, as a result, the child ingests the controlled substance, and
42 the ingestion is the proximate cause of death, is guilty of a Class B1 felony.
43 (g) This section does not apply to a person that intentionally gives a child a controlled
44 substance that has been prescribed for the child by a licensed medical professional when given
45 to the child in the prescribed amount and manner."
46 SECTION 2.(b) This section becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to
47 offenses committed on or after that date.

37 SECTION 7.(a) If any provision of this act or its application is held invalid, the
38 invalidity does not affect other provisions or applications of this act that can be given effect
39 without the invalid provisions or application and, to this end, the provisions of this act are
40 severable.
41 SECTION 7.(b) Prosecutions for offenses committed before the effective date of
42 this act are not abated or affected by this act, and the statutes that would be applicable but for
43 this act remain applicable to those prosecutions.
44 SECTION 7.(c) Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
45 law.
