1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROVIDE FUNDS TO INVEST IN INITIATIVES TO IMPROVE THE HEALTH
3 AND HABITATS OF THE STATE'S WILDLIFE POPULATIONS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Purpose and Findings. – The purpose of this act is as follows:
6 (1) To provide additional funding and awareness of Chronic Wasting Disease in
7 the State's deer population.
8 (2) To enhance wildlife conservation programs, especially northern bobwhite
9 quail habitat restoration and red wolf recovery initiatives.
10 (3) To expand hunting and fishing accessibility through modernized licensing
11 structures and public-private partnerships.
12 (4) To protect rural economies and private landowner rights while supporting
13 voluntary conservation efforts.
14 SECTION 2. CWD Monitoring and Incentives. – The sum of five million dollars
15 ($5,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year is appropriated from the
16 General Fund to the Wildlife Resources Commission. The Commission will use these funds to
17 expand statewide monitoring, reporting, and mitigation efforts for Chronic Wasting Disease
18 (CWD), including the creation of an incentive program to provide cash bounties to any person
19 reporting a confirmed positive CWD case, a reduced-price hunting license to hunters
20 participating in CWD tracking programs, and grants for research into CWD transmission and
21 mitigation to universities and nonprofit wildlife research entities.
22 SECTION 3. Quail Conservation and Habitat Restoration. – The sum of five million
23 dollars ($5,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year is appropriated from the
24 General Fund to the Wildlife Resources Commission. The Commission will use these funds to
25 establish a statewide habitat restoration initiative for northern bobwhite quail, to include the
26 following programs:
27 (1) A cost-share grant program to provide matching grants of up to five thousand
28 dollars ($5,000) to landowners who agree to participate in northern bobwhite
29 quail habitat restoration activities on their lands.
30 (2) A quail translocation program in areas of the State identified by the
31 Commission for reintroduction of northern bobwhite quail.
32 (3) A study regarding sustainable quail hunting regulations by the Commission.
33 The Commission shall provide a report on this study, including any
34 recommendations for funding needs and legislative changes, to the chairs of
35 the House Committee on Wildlife Resources and the chairs of the Senate

3 SECTION 4.(a) Red Wolf Conservation and Rural Landowner Support. – The sum
4 of five million dollars ($5,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2025-2026 fiscal year is
5 appropriated from the General Fund to the Wildlife Resources Commission. The Commission
6 will use these funds to establish the following initiatives for red wolf conservation and rural
7 landowner support:
8 (1) A cost-share grant program to provide matching grants of up to ten thousand
9 dollars ($10,000) to landowners who agree to provide protected habitat for red
10 wolf populations on their land.
11 (2) Grants to local governments and local tourism development organizations in
12 rural areas of the State with nearby red wolf populations to promote and
13 establish red wolf tourism programs and activities.
14 The Commission shall provide a report on its implementation of this section,
15 including any recommendations for funding needs and legislative changes to accommodate
16 making these initiatives permanent, to the chairs of the House Committee on Wildlife Resources
17 and the chairs of the Senate Committee on Agriculture, Energy, and Environment no later than
18 December 1, 2027.
19 SECTION 4.(b) Red Wolf Wildlife Crossings. – The Department of Transportation
20 will utilize the grant received from the Federal Highway Administration's Wildlife Crossings
21 Pilot Program to install wildlife crossings for red wolves on U.S. Highway 64 in and adjacent to
22 the Alligator River National Wildlife Refuge.
23 SECTION 5.(a) WRC Fee Adjustments. – The Wildlife Resources Commission
24 shall adopt rules to revise its fees and fee payment options for lifetime hunting and fishing
25 licenses in a revenue neutral manner as set forth in this section:
26 (1) Create the following age-based categories: infants (0-2 years), young children
27 (3-6 years), youth (7-12 years), teenagers (13-17 years), young adults (age
28 18-25 years), adults (26-39 years), middle-aged adults (40-59 years), and
29 seniors (60 years and over), with the middle-aged adult category charged the
30 maximum fee, seniors receiving a fifty percent (50%) discount on the
31 maximum fee, and younger age groups charged less than older age groups.
32 (2) Offer a multi-generational license discount to families purchasing three or
33 more lifetime licenses at the same time.
34 (3) Offer a monthly installment payment option for licenses that exceed one
35 thousand dollars ($1,000) in cost.
36 SECTION 5.(b) Other Funding Sources. – The Commission shall seek additional
37 sources of funds from non-State sources for the initiatives funded by this act, including grants
38 from federal and nonprofit wildlife conservation organizations.
39 SECTION 5.(c) Report. – The Wildlife Resources Commission shall report on its
40 implementation of this section as a part of the annual report required by G.S. 143-250.
41 SECTION 6. Effective Date. – This act becomes effective July 1, 2025.
