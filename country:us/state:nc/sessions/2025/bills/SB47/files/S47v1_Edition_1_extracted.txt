GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
SENATE BILL 47
Short Title: State Emps./No Payroll Dues Deduction. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Hise, Johnson, and B. Newton (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
February 5, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT REPEALING PUBLIC EMPLOYEE PAYROLL DEDUCTION FOR PAYMENTS
3 TO EMPLOYEES' ASSOCIATIONS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 143B-426.40A reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 143B-426.40A. Assignments of claims against State.
7 …
8 (g) Payroll Deduction for Payments to Certain Employees' Associations Allowed. – An
9 employee of the State or any of its political subdivisions, institutions, departments, bureaus,
10 agencies or commissions, or any of its local boards of education or community colleges, who is
11 a member of a domiciled employees' association that has at least 2,000 members, 500 of whom
12 are employees of the State, a political subdivision of the State, or public school employees, may
13 authorize, in writing, the periodic deduction each payroll period from the employee's salary or
14 wages a designated lump sum to be paid to the employees' association. A political subdivision
15 may also allow periodic deductions for a domiciled employees' association that does not
16 otherwise meet the minimum membership requirements set forth in this paragraph. The total
17 membership count and the State, political subdivision of the State, or public school employee
18 membership count of a domiciled employees' association that has at least 2,000 members, 500 of
19 whom are employees of the State, a political subdivision of the State, or public school employees,
20 shall be verified and certified annually by the State Auditor.
21 An employee of any local board of education who is a member of a domiciled employees'
22 association that has at least 40,000 members, the majority of whom are public school teachers,
23 may authorize in writing the periodic deduction each payroll period from the employee's salary
24 or wages a designated lump sum or sums to be paid for dues and voluntary contributions for the
25 employees' association. The total membership count and the public school teacher membership
26 count of a domiciled employees' association that has at least 40,000 members, the majority of
27 whom are public school teachers, shall be verified and certified annually by the State Auditor.
28 An authorization under this subsection shall remain in effect until revoked by the employee.
29 A plan of payroll deductions pursuant to this subsection for employees of the State and other
30 association members shall become void if the employees' association engages in collective
31 bargaining with the State, any political subdivision of the State, or any local school administrative
32 unit. This subsection does not apply to county or municipal governments or any local
33 governmental unit, except for local boards of education.
34 …."
35 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
