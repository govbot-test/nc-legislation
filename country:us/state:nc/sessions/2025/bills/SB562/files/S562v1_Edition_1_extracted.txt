1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE NORTH CAROLINA CYBERSECURITY AND QUANTUM
3 RESILIENCE STUDY COMMISSION AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS FOR THAT
4 PURPOSE.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1.(a) This act shall be known and may be cited as the North Carolina
7 Cybersecurity and Quantum Resilience Study Act.
8 SECTION 1.(b) The North Carolina Cybersecurity and Quantum Resilience Study
9 Commission (Commission) is established to investigate the potential impacts of emerging
10 quantum computing technologies on the security of State systems, legacy encryption methods,
11 and critical infrastructure—including the Internet of Things (IoT) and smart city initiatives. The
12 Commission shall review vulnerabilities and provide recommendations on necessary future
13 measures to enhance the state's cybersecurity posture. To help guide the Commission work, the
14 General Assembly finds that:
15 (1) Advances in quantum computing pose potential risks to traditional encryption
16 methods, such as Rivest–Shamir–Adleman (RSA) and Elliptic Curve
17 Cryptography (CC), that many State and local systems rely on.
18 (2) Emerging technologies and IoT integrations, common in smart city initiatives,
19 could be at risk if quantum computing breakthroughs compromise existing
20 security protocols.
21 (3) North Carolina is home to a robust academic and technological ecosystem
22 which can contribute significantly to understanding and mitigating these risks.
23 (4) A proactive study is necessary to understand the scope of these vulnerabilities
24 and to inform potential legislative or administrative actions in the future.
25 SECTION 1.(c) The Commission shall be composed of twenty-one (21) members,
26 with seven members appointed by the Governor, seven members appointed by the President Pro
27 Tempore of the Senate, and seven members appointed by the Speaker of the House of
28 Representatives, as follows:
29 (1) Five representatives from cabinet agencies appointed by the Governor.
30 (2) Three cybersecurity experts from both the public and private sectors appointed
31 by the President Pro Tempore.
32 (3) Two academic experts in quantum computing and cybersecurity from North
33 Carolina institutions appointed by the President Pro Tempore
34 (4) Three industry representatives involved in IoT, smart infrastructure, and
35 cryptographic technologies appointed by the Speaker.

5 SECTION 1.(d) The Commission is charged with the following duties:
6 (1) Conducting a comprehensive review of state IT systems and critical
7 infrastructure to identify vulnerabilities associated with legacy encryption
8 methods.
9 (2) Evaluating the potential impact of quantum computing on these systems.
10 (3) Analyzing current and emerging quantum-resistant cryptographic standards.
11 (4) Assessing risks in IoT and smart city implementations.
12 (5) Providing a roadmap and recommendations for necessary legislative,
13 regulatory, or administrative measures to bolster cybersecurity against future
14 quantum threats.
15 SECTION 1.(e) The Commission's tasks and deliverables include:
16 (1) Risk assessment to identify and document systems and sectors most
17 vulnerable to quantum-related cyber threats and evaluate the state's current
18 cybersecurity measures and determine gaps in protection.
19 (2) Research collaboration to engage with local universities and industry experts
20 to gather insights on quantum-resistant cryptographic techniques.
21 (3) Develop a recommendation roadmap to propose a timeline for transitioning to
22 quantum-safe encryption methods where needed and outline potential policies
23 or incentives for upgrading critical infrastructure security.
24 SECTION 1.(f) The Commission shall submit a comprehensive report with findings,
25 a detailed risk assessment, and recommended actions to the General Assembly by July 1, 2026.
26 SECTION 2.(a) Effective July 1, 2025, there is appropriated from the General Fund
27 to the General Assembly the sum of two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($250,000) to fund the
28 work of the Commission, including research initiatives, public hearings, stakeholder meetings,
29 and report development. The Commission may explore potential partnerships or federal grant
30 opportunities to supplement research and study efforts.
31 SECTION 2.(b) The Commission shall be convened within 30 days of this act's
32 enactment. The Commission members shall elect a chair and vice-chair. Members of the
33 Commission shall receive reimbursement as provided by Chapter 138 of the General Statutes.
34 SECTION 2.(c) Sensitive information received by the Commission shall remain
35 confidential and does not constitute a public record as defined by G.S. 132-1. For the purposes
36 of this subsection, the chair and vice-chair of the Commission may designate jointly information
37 as sensitive after balancing the need for public access against security concerns and
38 confidentiality requirements.
39 SECTION 2.(d) The Joint Legislative Committee on Information Technology shall
40 monitor the commission's progress. The Commission's report and recommendations will be
41 reviewed by the General Assembly to determine any further legislative or administrative actions
42 necessary during the 2026 Regular Session of the 2025 General Assembly, with provisions for
43 subsequent studies or actions as needed.
44 SECTION 3. Except as otherwise provided, this act is effective when it becomes
45 law.
