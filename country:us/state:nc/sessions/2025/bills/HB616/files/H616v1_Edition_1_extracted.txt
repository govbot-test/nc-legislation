GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 616
Short Title: Selectsite Readiness Program Modifications. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Johnson.
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Finance, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 1, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MODIFY THE SELECTSITE READINESS PROGRAM.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Section 11.12 of S.L. 2023-134 reads as rewritten:
5 "SELECTSITE READINESS PROGRAM
6 "SECTION 11.12.(a) Funds appropriated by Section 11.4 of S.L. 2022-74 to the Department
7 of Commerce (Department) and allocated to the nonprofit corporation with which the
8 Department contracts pursuant to G.S. 143B-431.01(b) that remain unspent as of June 30, 2023,
9 shall be transferred to the North Carolina Selectsite Fund established in subsection (c) of this
10 section to shall be used for purposes consistent with subdivision (e)(1) of this section. Of the
11 funds appropriated from the Economic Development Project Reserve established in Section 2.2
12 of this act to the Department to be allocated to the nonprofit corporation with which the
13 Department contracts pursuant to G.S. 143B-431.01(b), the sum of ten million dollars
14 ($10,000,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2024-2025 fiscal year shall be used to support local
15 governments or a partnership of local governments in conducting due diligence as described in
16 subdivision (b)(5) of this section.
17 "SECTION 11.12.(b) Purpose. – It is in the best economic and developmental interests of
18 the State to support the development of selectsites to ensure the State's ongoing competitiveness
19 for major manufacturing opportunities, including, but not limited to, the aerospace, automotive,
20 clean energy, food processing, semiconductor, and life science industries. The purpose of this
21 section is to establish a competitive grant program serving to do the following:
22 (1) Identify and evaluate up to 15 selectsites of less than 1,000 acres for preferred
23 development and marketing.
24 (2) Assist local governments or a partnership of local governments in the
25 acquisition of a newly identified or existing selectsite.
26 (3) Support local governments or a partnership of local governments to analyze,
27 plan, perform engineering design, permit, install, or upgrade public
28 infrastructure, including publicly owned water, gas, and sewer systems;
29 transportation infrastructure; and the electrical utility lines necessary to meet
30 the needs of prospective employers for selectsites.
31 (4) Support local governments or a partnership of local governments to fund
32 on-site preparation, including permitting, clearing, grading, or other related
33 expenses for selectsites.
34 (5) Support local governments or a partnership of local governments in
35 conducting due diligence, including, but not limited to, the following: site

5 (6) Facilitate coordination between the economic development entities and the

9 "SECTION 11.12.(c) Fund Established. – There is created in the Department a special fund
10 to be known as the North Carolina Selectsite Fund for grants awarded by the Economic
11 Development Partnership of North Carolina (EDPNC) for purposes consistent with this section.
12 EDPNC shall be responsible for administering the program. The provisions prohibiting EDPNC
13 from the awarding of grants contained in G.S. 143B-431.01 do not apply to the Fund.
14 "SECTION 11.12.(d) Definitions. – The definitions in Section 11.11(c) of S.L. 2022-74
15 apply in this section. For purposes of this section, a "selectsite" is a parcel of contiguous property
16 consisting of less than 1,000 acres that is viable for industrial development and listed in the report
17 pursuant to subsection (h) of this section. For purposes of this section, "Fund" is the North
18 Carolina Selectsite Fund.
19 "SECTION 11.12.(e) Allocation. – EDPNC shall allocate monies in the Fund on the
20 following basis:
21 (1) Unspent funds transferred pursuant to subsection (a) of this section shall be
22 used On a biennial basis, no more than one hundred fifty thousand dollars
23 ($150,000) shall be used for engaging a national site selection firm through a
24 competitive bid process to produce a biennial report identifying and
25 evaluating 15 10 new selectsites for preferred development and marketing, of
26 which seven five must be less than 500 acres and of which an additional two
27 three must be less than 100 acres. Geographic diversity must be a priority
28 consideration in the selection of new sites beginning with the report due in
29 2026.
30 (2) Funds appropriated to the Fund for local government grants shall be allocated
31 for the purposes outlined in subdivisions (b)(2), (b)(3), (b)(4), and (b)(5) of
32 this section for selectsites determined pursuant to subdivision (1) of this
33 subsection. EDPNC shall prioritize local government grants that have the
34 greatest potential to reduce the time for site readiness and reduce the risk of
35 unforeseen conditions that could affect the site viability for advanced
36 manufacturing projects. EDPNC shall base the grant amount on total
37 development needs for the selectsite, prior investment in the selectsite by one
38 or more local governments, the ability of one or more local governments to
39 invest in the selectsite, and the ability and level of participation promised by
40 the local government in exchange for a grant from the Fund. Monies may only
41 be granted for, and used to acquire, a selectsite for which (i) one or more local
42 governments have a binding option or offer to purchase and (ii) all basic due
43 diligence has been completed, including, but not limited to, boundary surveys,
44 title searches, State Historic Preservation Office reviews, Phase I
45 environmental, Threatened & Endangered Species studies, and wetlands
46 delineation.
47 "SECTION 11.12.(f) Matching Funds. – If a grant is awarded that includes site acquisition
48 assistance, the local governments to which a grant is awarded shall provide the remainder of the
49 cost of purchasing the selectsite not provided by the grant.
50 "SECTION 11.12.(g) Agreements Required. – Monies may be disbursed from the Fund only
51 in accordance with agreements entered into between EDPNC and a local government or a

17 "SECTION 11.12.(i) Program Guidelines. – EDPNC shall develop guidelines related to the
18 administration of this program. At least 20 days before the effective date of any guidelines or
19 nontechnical amendments to the guidelines, EDPNC shall publish the proposed guidelines on its
20 website and provide notice to persons who have requested notice of proposed guidelines. In
21 addition, EDPNC must accept oral and written comments on the proposed guidelines and shall,
22 in its discretion, consider such comments before finalizing the guidelines during the 15 business
23 days beginning on the first day that EDPNC has completed these notifications. Guidelines
24 adopted under this section shall not be subject to the requirements of Article 2A of Chapter 150B
25 of the General Statutes."
26 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to appropriations
27 to, and unencumbered funds in, the North Carolina Selectsite Fund on or after that date.
