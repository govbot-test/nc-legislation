to:
1 A HOUSE RESOLUTION URGING THE UNITED STATES CONGRESS TO WORK MORE
2 CLOSELY WITH THE UNITED KINGDOM ON TRADE ISSUES AND FOREIGN
3 INVESTMENT, EXPRESSING SUPPORT FOR THE UNITED STATES-UNITED
4 KINGDOM ALLIANCE, STRENGTHENING FRIENDSHIP AND ECONOMIC TIES
5 BETWEEN THE STATE OF NORTH CAROLINA AND THE UNITED KINGDOM,
6 ACKNOWLEDGING THE IMPACT OF BRITISH CULTURE ON NORTH CAROLINA,
7 AND REAFFIRMING THE BONDS OF FRIENDSHIP AND COOPERATION BETWEEN
8 THE STATE OF NORTH CAROLINA AND THE UNITED KINGDOM OF GREAT
9 BRITAIN AND NORTHERN IRELAND.
10 Whereas, North Carolina and the United Kingdom have enjoyed decades of friendly
11 and enduring relations, marked by robust economic partnerships, vibrant cultural ties, and shared
12 values that have strengthened the relations between the two regions; and
13 Whereas, North Carolina has enjoyed a rich cultural infusion from the United
14 Kingdom, which has led to the namesakes of our largest communities, including the City of
15 Charlotte in the County of Mecklenburg, named after Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, Queen
16 Consort of Great Britain, and the capital of our State, Raleigh, named after Sir Walter Raleigh,
17 an English explorer who established the first English colony in North America at Fort Raleigh
18 on Roanoke Island, and whose eponymous city is located in the County of Wake, named after
19 Margaret Wake Tryon, the wife of royal governor William Tryon; and
20 Whereas, North Carolina has more than a fourth of its one hundred counties and
21 numerous towns named after members of British Parliament, Lords Proprietor, Royal Governors,
22 and other notable figures of the United Kingdom that have shaped the governance and virtues of
23 North Carolinian society; and
24 Whereas, North Carolina has enjoyed a wealth of immigration from all portions of
25 the United Kingdom, shaping its economy, culture, and communities from the colonial era
26 onward, playing a crucial role in the State's early development, cultural identity, and long-term
27 prosperity; and
28 Whereas, the United Kingdom currently stands as one of the largest originators of
29 foreign direct investment in North Carolina, with over $1 billion invested into North Carolina's
30 economy since 2014; and
31 Whereas, the United Kingdom is an important trading partner of North Carolina, as
32 the sixth largest export destination of the State, with over $2.5 billion in trade with the United
33 Kingdom; and
34 Whereas, the United Kingdom has played an essential role in job creation in North
35 Carolina across various sectors, with 218 companies employing over 43,200 North Carolinians,
36 thereby contributing significantly to the State's employment and economy; and

1 Whereas, North Carolina has emerged as a premier destination for British investment
2 and ranks among the top US states selected by the UK as a beneficiary of foreign direct
3 investment, reflecting the State's favorable business environment, developed infrastructure, and
4 skilled workforce; and
5 Whereas, trade with the United Kingdom plays an instrumental role in North
6 Carolina's economic activity, enhancing prosperity and growth, and fostering mutually beneficial
7 exchanges of goods, services, and technologies, thereby solidifying the United Kingdom as a
8 vital trading partner and ally; and
9 Whereas, Governor Roy Cooper and British Minister for International Trade Penny
10 Mordaunt recognized the importance of the trade relationship between the United Kingdom and
11 North Carolina, signing a Memorandum of Understanding on Cooperation and Trade Relations
12 on July 15, 2022, to strengthen economic ties and transition to a clean energy economy; and
13 Whereas, over 24,075 British expatriates currently live or work in North Carolina,
14 leading to the establishment of the British International School of Charlotte, opened in 2004 and
15 the fifth school opened in the United States by the British Schools of America; and
16 Whereas, four daily direct flights now connect North Carolina with the United
17 Kingdom, including service from the Charlotte Douglas and Raleigh-Durham International
18 Airports to London Heathrow; and
19 Whereas, the alliance between the United States and the United Kingdom has been a
20 cornerstone of global peace, stability, and prosperity, with both nations collaborating on
21 upholding human rights, advancing democracy, and addressing shared security challenges, while
22 also driving innovation, economic growth, and diplomatic efforts worldwide; and
23 Whereas, the democratic values championed by both the United Kingdom and the
24 United States, which serve as cornerstones of global order and collaboration, form the foundation
25 of the lasting friendship between North Carolina and the United Kingdom; and
26 Whereas, North Carolina remains committed to its friendship with the United
27 Kingdom and to the US-UK alliance and peaceful trade between the nations; Now, therefore,
28 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives:
29 SECTION 1. The House of Representatives recognizes the historic significance of
30 the friendship and alliance between the United States and the United Kingdom.
31 SECTION 2. The House of Representatives commends the people of the United
32 Kingdom for their remarkable achievements in promoting democracy, economic prosperity, and
33 international cooperation.
34 SECTION 3. The House of Representatives applauds the United Kingdom for its
35 continued partnership and economic cooperation with the State of North Carolina.
36 SECTION 4. The House of Representatives affirms the steadfast and enduring
37 friendship and cooperation between the United Kingdom and the State of North Carolina and
38 expresses its commitment to further strengthening these ties.
39 SECTION 5. The House of Representatives urges Congress to work more closely
40 with the United Kingdom on mutually beneficial trade relations to encourage increased
41 investment and collaboration between English and American companies in the economy of both
42 the United States and the United Kingdom, respectively.
43 SECTION 6. The House of Representatives expresses to Congress full support for
44 the longstanding alliance between the United States and the United Kingdom, which remains
45 critical for global security and prosperity.
46 SECTION 7. The Principal Clerk shall transmit a copy of this resolution to the
47 Embassy of the United Kingdom in Washington, D.C., the British Consulate General in Atlanta,
48 Georgia, the UK Government Office in Raleigh, North Carolina, each member of North
49 Carolina's Congressional Delegation, the Clerk of the United States House of Representatives
50 and the Secretary of the United States Senate, and the President of the United States.
51 SECTION 8. This resolution is effective upon adoption.
