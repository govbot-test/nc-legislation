H.B. 134
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Feb 17, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Prohibit Misbranding of Certain Food Products. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Dixon.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PROHIBIT THE MISBRANDING OF CERTAIN FOOD PRODUCTS.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 106-549.15 reads as rewritten:
5 "§ 106-549.15. Definitions.
6 As used in this Article, except as otherwise specified, the following terms shall have the
7 meanings stated below:
8 …
9 (1a) "Agricultural food animal" means a domesticated animal belonging to the
10 bovine, caprine, ovine, or porcine species.
11 …
12 (5a) "Cell-cultured food product" means a food product having one or more
13 sensory attributes that resemble a type of tissue originating from an
14 agricultural food animal but that, in lieu of being derived from meat
15 processing, is derived from manufacturing cells, in which one or more stem
16 cells are initially isolated from an agricultural food animal, are grown in vitro,
17 and may be manipulated, as part of a manufacturing operation.
18 (5b) "Close proximity" means any of the following:
19 a. Immediately before or after the name of the product.
20 b. In the line of the label immediately before or after the line containing
21 the name of the product.
22 c. Within the same phrase or sentence containing the name of the
23 product.
24 …
25 (9a) "Identifying meat term" means any word or phrase that states, indicates,
26 suggests, or describes a meat product, regardless of whether the word or
27 phrase is used individually, as a portmanteau, or as a compound word. This
28 term includes all of the following:
29 a. A common name for the species of the agricultural food animal subject
30 to slaughter and processing, including a calf or cow, goat or kid, hog
31 or pig, lamb, or sheep.
32 b. A common name for a characteristic of a species of the agricultural
33 food animal subject to slaughter and processing based on age, breed,
34 or sex.
35 c. Meat; beef or veal; cabrito or chevon; lamb or mutton; or pork.
*DRH30067-TQ-9*

1 d. A common name used to describe a major cut of a meat of an
2 agricultural food animal slaughtered and processed, including a major
3 meat cut specified in 9 C.F.R. § 317.344, or the common name of an
4 organ or offal, including heart, liver, kidney, or tongue.
5 e. Any other common name that a reasonable purchaser would
6 immediately and exclusively associate with a meat product prepared
7 for sale in normal commercial channels such as bacon, baloney,
8 bologna, bone, brat or bratwurst, brisket, burger or hamburger, butt,
9 chop, chuck, cold cut, cutlet, filet, flat iron, frank or frankfurter, ham,
10 hock, hot dog, jerky, liverwurst, loin, London broil, lunch meat, New
11 York strip, pepperoni, porterhouse, ribeye, roast, rib or sparerib,
12 salami, sausage, shank, sirloin, tenderloin, or a comparable word or
13 phrase.
14 (9b) "Insect-protein food product" means a food product having one or more
15 sensory attributes that resemble a type of tissue originating from an
16 agricultural food animal but that, in lieu of being derived from meat
17 processing, is derived from manufacturing insect parts.
18 …
19 (12a) "Manufactured-protein food product" means a cell-cultured food product,
20 insect-protein food product, or plant-protein food product.
21 …
22 (14) "Meat food product" means any product capable of use as human food that is
23 made wholly or in part from any meat or other portion of the carcass of any
24 cattle, sheep, swine, goats, bison, fallow deer, or red deer, or any cell-cultured
25 food product made from cells of those animals, excepting products that
26 contain meat or other portions of such carcasses only in a relatively small
27 proportion or historically have not been considered by consumers as products
28 of the meat food industry, and that are exempted from definition as a meat
29 food product by the Board under such conditions as it may prescribe to assure
30 that the meat or other portions of such carcasses contained in such product are
31 not adulterated and that such products are not represented as meat food
32 products. This term as applied to food products of equines shall have a
33 meaning comparable to that provided in this subdivision with respect to cattle,
34 sheep, swine, goats, and bison.
35 (15) "Misbranded" shall apply to any carcass, part thereof, meat or meat food
36 product under one or more of the following circumstances:
37 a. If its labeling is false or misleading in any particular;
38 b. If it is offered for sale under the name of another food;
39 c. If it is imitation of another food, unless its label bears, in type of
40 uniform size and prominence, the word "imitation" and immediately
41 thereafter, the name of the food imitated;
42 …
43 m. The product is a cell-cultured food product and it is not labeled in
44 accordance with G.S. 106-549.28A.
45 …
46 (20a) "Plant-protein food product" means a food product having one or more
47 sensory attributes that resemble a type of tissue originating from an
48 agricultural food animal but that, in lieu of being derived from meat
49 processing, is derived from manufacturing plant or fungus parts.
50 …

1 (21a) "Qualifying term" means a word, compound word, or phrase that would
2 clearly disclose to a reasonable purchaser of meat products that a food product
3 is not a meat product. This includes the following terms: "cell-cultured,"
4 "fake," "grown in a lab," "insect," "insect-based," "insect-protein,"
5 "lab-created," "lab-grown," "meat free," "meatless," "plant," "plant-based,"
6 "vegan," "vegetable," "vegetarian," or "veggie."
7 (21b) "Ratite" means a bird whose breastbone is smooth so that flight muscles
8 cannot attach, such as an ostrich, an emu, and a rhea. These birds are subject
9 to the provisions of this Article and Article 49C to the same extent as any other
10 meat food product.
11 …."
12 SECTION 1.(b) Article 49B of Chapter 106 of the General Statutes is amended by
13 adding a new section to read:
14 "§ 106-549.28A. Labeling of manufactured-protein food products.
15 (a) The label of any manufactured-protein food product that contains an identifying meat
16 term shall also contain in at least 20-point font or the size of the surrounding type, whichever is
17 greater, and in close proximity to the name of the product, an appropriate qualifying term.
18 (b) A manufactured-protein food product that does not meet the requirements of this
19 section shall be deemed misbranded for purposes of this Article."
