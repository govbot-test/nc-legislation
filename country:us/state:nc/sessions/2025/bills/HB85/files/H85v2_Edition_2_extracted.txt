GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 2
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/8/25
Short Title: Removal of Precinct Officials. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
February 11, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CLARIFY THAT PRECINCT OFFICIALS MAY BE REMOVED FROM THEIR
3 POSITION DUE TO FAILURE TO DISCHARGE THEIR DUTIES, TO PROVIDE THAT
4 CERTAIN PRECINCT OFFICIALS MAY BE PROHIBITED FROM SERVING AS A
5 PRECINCT OFFICIAL IN SUBSEQUENT ELECTIONS, TO ESTABLISH THE
6 MINIMUM NUMBER OF EMERGENCY ELECTION-DAY ASSISTANTS FOR EACH
7 COUNTY, AND TO SPECIFY TRAINING REQUIREMENTS FOR PRECINCT
8 OFFICIALS.
9 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
10 SECTION 1.(a) Article 5 of Chapter 163 of the General Statutes is amended by
11 adding a new section to read:
12 "§ 163-41.3. Removal of precinct officials; vacancies."
13 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 163-41(d) is recodified as G.S. 163-41.3.
14 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 163-41, as amended by this section, reads as rewritten:
15 "§ 163-41. Precinct chief judges and judges of election; appointment; terms of office;
16 qualifications; vacancies; oaths of office.
17 (a) Appointment of Chief Judge and Judges. – At the meeting required by G.S. 163-31 to
18 be held on the Tuesday following the third Monday in August of the year in which they are
19 appointed, the county board of elections shall appoint one person to act as chief judge and two
20 other persons to act as judges of election for each precinct in the county. Their terms of office
21 shall continue for two years from the specified date of appointment and until their successors are
22 appointed and qualified, except that if a nonresident of the precinct is appointed as chief judge
23 or judge for a precinct, that person's term of office shall end if the county board of elections
24 appoints a qualified resident of the precinct of the same party to replace the nonresident chief
25 judge or judge. It shall be their the duty of the precinct officials to conduct the primaries and
26 elections within their respective precincts. Persons appointed to these offices must be registered
27 voters and residents of the county in which the precinct is located, of good repute, and able to
28 read and write. Not more than one judge in each precinct shall belong to the same political party
29 as the chief judge.
30 (b) The As used in this Article, the term "precinct official" shall mean chief judges and
31 judges appointed pursuant to this section, and all assistants appointed pursuant to G.S. 163-42,
32 unless the context of a statute clearly indicates a more restrictive meaning.
33 (b1) No person shall be eligible to serve as a precinct official, as that term is defined above,
34 who holds if any of the following apply:
35 (1) The person holds any elective office under the government of the United
36 States, or of the State of North Carolina or any political subdivision thereof.

13 "§ 163-41.3. Removal of precinct officials; filling vacancies.
14 (a) Any precinct official may be removed from office, including on the day of the election
15 or primary, for incompetency or failure to discharge the duties of office by the county board in
16 accordance with G.S. 163-33. The county board may prohibit a precinct official who has been

21 "(2) To appoint all chief judges, judges, assistants, and other officers of elections,
22 and designate the precinct in which each shall serve; and, after notice and
23 hearing, to remove any chief judge, judge of elections, assistant, or other
24 officer of election appointed by it for incompetency, failure to discharge the
25 duties of office, failure to qualify within the time prescribed by law, fraud, or
26 for any other satisfactory cause. cause, as provided in G.S. 163-41.3. In
27 exercising the powers and duties of this subdivision, the board may act only
28 when a majority of its members are present at any meeting at which such
29 powers or duties are exercised."
30 SECTION 4. G.S. 163-42(c) reads as rewritten:
31 "(c) In addition, a county board of elections by unanimous vote of all of its members may
32 appoint any registered voter in the county as an emergency election-day assistant, as long as that
33 voter is otherwise qualified to be a precinct official. The State Board of Elections shall determine
34 for each election the number of emergency election-day assistants each county may have, based
35 on population, expected turnout, and complexity of election duties. duties; however, each county
36 must have, at a minimum, six emergency election-day assistants. The county board by unanimous
37 vote of all of its members may assign emergency election-day assistants on the day of the election
38 to any precinct in the county where the number of precinct officials is insufficient because of an
39 emergency occurring within 48 hours of the opening of the polls emergency, the removal of a
40 precinct official, or any other reason that prevents an appointed a precinct official from serving.
41 serving throughout the day of the primary or election. A person appointed to serve as an
42 emergency election-day assistant shall be trained and paid like other precinct assistants in
43 accordance with G.S. 163-46. A county board of elections shall apportion the appointments as of
44 emergency election-day assistant assistants among registrants of each political party so as to
45 make possible the staffing of each precinct with officials of more than one party, and the county
46 board shall make assignments so that no precinct has precinct officials all of whom are registered
47 with the same party."
48 SECTION 5. G.S. 163-82.24(a) reads as rewritten:
49 "(a) Training. – The State Board of Elections shall conduct training programs in election
50 law and procedures. Every county elections director shall receive training conducted by the State

18 to those curbside voting.
19 (5) The procedure for opening and closing of polls.
20 (6) The prohibition regarding election-related activity in the voting place and
21 buffer zone of that voting place.
22 (7) The potential for removal from office for failure to comply with all provisions

24 SECTION 6. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to elections
25 conducted on or after that date.
