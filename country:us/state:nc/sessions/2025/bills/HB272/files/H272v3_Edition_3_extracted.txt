11 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
12 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 135-4.5 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
13 "(h) Purchases of Service Credit for Advanced Law Enforcement or Advanced
14 Corrections Certificates. – Notwithstanding any other provision of this Article to the contrary, a
15 member in service with five or more years of membership service who has been awarded either
16 (i) the Advanced Law Enforcement Certificate issued by either the North Carolina Criminal
17 Justice Education and Training Standards Commission under the authority granted under Chapter
18 17C of the General Statutes or the North Carolina Sheriffs' Education and Training Standards
19 Commission under the authority granted under Chapter 17E of the General Statutes or (ii) the
20 Advanced Corrections Certificate issued by the North Carolina Criminal Justice Education and
21 Training Standards Commission under the authority granted under Chapter 17C of the General
22 Statutes may purchase creditable service subject to all of the following:
23 (1) The member may purchase up to a total of four years of creditable service
24 under this subsection.
25 (2) Service may only be purchased under this subsection upon or after receipt of
26 the applicable certificate.
27 (3) G.S. 135-4(b) does not apply to purchases under this subsection.
28 (4) Purchases of service under this subsection shall be made by paying a lump
29 sum amount to the Annuity Savings Fund. Purchases shall not be associated
30 with any specific compensation or period of time. The amount payable shall
31 be equal to the full liability increase of the Retirement System due to the
32 additional service credits purchased plus an administrative fee that is set by
33 the Board of Trustees. The full liability increase shall be calculated on the
34 basis of the same assumptions used for the purposes of the actuarial valuation
35 of the liabilities of the Retirement System, except for the following

13 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 135-4(b) reads as rewritten:
14 "(b) In Unless specifically provided under G.S. 135-4.5, in no case shall more than one
15 year of service be creditable for all services in one year. Service rendered for the regular school
16 year in any district shall be equivalent to one year's service. Service rendered by a school
17 employee in a job-sharing position shall be credited at the rate of one-half year for each regular
18 school year of employment."

6 SECTION 3.(a) The State Treasurer shall seek a favorable private letter ruling from
7 the Internal Revenue Service to determine that this act does not jeopardize the status of the
8 Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System by January 1 of the following calendar year
9 or within 120 days from this act becoming law, whichever is later.
10 SECTION 3.(b) If the State Treasurer has met the obligation under Section 3(a) of
11 this act, and the Internal Revenue Service declines to make a private letter ruling or does not
12 issue a response by 15 days prior to the effective date of this act, then this act is repealed. The
13 State Treasurer shall notify the Revisor of Statutes that the Internal Revenue Service declines to
14 make a private letter ruling or did not issue a response. Within three business days of notifying
15 the Revisor of Statutes, the State Treasurer shall notify all employers of the repeal of this act and
16 shall publicly notice the receipt of this information on the Department of State Treasurer's
17 website.
18 SECTION 3.(c) If the Internal Revenue Service fails to make a favorable
19 determination that this act does not jeopardize the status of the Teachers' and State Employees'
20 Retirement System of North Carolina under the Internal Revenue Code, then this act is repealed
21 on the last day of the month following the month of receipt of that determination by the State
22 Treasurer. Upon receipt of that determination, the State Treasurer shall notify the Revisor of
23 Statutes of the determination and the date of receipt. Within three business days of not receiving
24 a determination that is favorable, the State Treasurer shall notify all employers of the repeal of
25 this act and shall publicly notice the receipt of this information on the Department of State
26 Treasurer's website.
27 SECTION 3.(d) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, in order
28 to pay costs associated with the administration of the provisions of this act, the Retirement
29 Systems Division of the Department of State Treasurer may increase receipts from the retirement
30 assets of the Teachers' and State Employees' Retirement System or pay costs associated with the
31 administration directly from the retirement assets.
