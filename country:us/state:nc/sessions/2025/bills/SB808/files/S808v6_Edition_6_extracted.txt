11 "§ 88B-1. Short title.

13 "§ 88B-2. Definitions.
14 The following definitions apply in this Chapter:
15 (1) Apprentice. – A person who is not a manager or operator and who is engaged
16 in learning the practice of cosmetic art under the direction and supervision of
17 a cosmetologist.
18 (2) Board. – The North Carolina Board of Cosmetic Art Examiners.
19 (3) Booth. – A workstation located within a licensed cosmetic art shop or mobile
20 cosmetic art shop that is operated primarily by one individual in performing
21 cosmetic art services for consumers.
22 (4) Booth renter. – A person who rents a booth in a cosmetic art shop.shop or
23 mobile cosmetic art shop.
24 (5) Cosmetic art. – All or any part or combination of cosmetology, hair design,
25 esthetics, natural hair care, hair braiding, or manicuring, including the
26 systematic manipulation with the hands or mechanical apparatus of the scalp,
27 face, neck, shoulders, hands, and feet. Practices included within this
28 subdivision shall not include the practice of massage or bodywork therapy as
29 set forth in Article 36 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes.
30 (6) Cosmetic art school. – Any building or part thereof where cosmetic art is
31 taught.

31 renewed on or before the first day of February of each year. As provided in G.S. 88B-20, a late
32 fee shall be charged for licenses renewed after February 1. Any license not renewed by March 1

34 been expired for one year or less shall have the license reinstated immediately upon payment of
35 the reinstatement fee, the late fee, and all unpaid license fees. The licensee shall submit to the
36 Board, as a part of the renewal process, a list of all licensed cosmetologists who practice cosmetic
37 art in the shop and shall identify each as an employee or a booth renter.

39 beginning October 1, 1998. A late fee shall be charged for renewals after that date. Any license

41 develop and implement a plan for staggered license renewal and may prorate license fees to
42 implement such a plan.
