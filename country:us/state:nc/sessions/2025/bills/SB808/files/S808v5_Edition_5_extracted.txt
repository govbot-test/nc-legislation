12 "§ 88B-1. Short title.
13 This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the North Carolina Cosmetic Art Act.
14 "§ 88B-2. Definitions.
15 The following definitions apply in this Chapter:
16 (1) Apprentice. – A person who is not a manager or operator and who is engaged
17 in learning the practice of cosmetic art under the direction and supervision of
18 a cosmetologist.
19 (2) Board. – The North Carolina Board of Cosmetic Art Examiners.
20 (3) Booth. – A workstation located within a licensed cosmetic art shop or mobile
21 cosmetic art shop that is operated primarily by one individual in performing
22 cosmetic art services for consumers.
23 (4) Booth renter. – A person who rents a booth in a cosmetic art shop.shop or
24 mobile cosmetic art shop.
25 (5) Cosmetic art. – All or any part or combination of cosmetology, hair design,
26 esthetics, natural hair care, hair braiding, or manicuring, including the
27 systematic manipulation with the hands or mechanical apparatus of the scalp,
28 face, neck, shoulders, hands, and feet. Practices included within this
29 subdivision shall not include the practice of massage or bodywork therapy as
30 set forth in Article 36 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes.
31 (6) Cosmetic art school. – Any building or part thereof where cosmetic art is
32 taught.

27 braiding, twisting, or wrapping the hair by hand or a mechanical device. Hair
28 braiding is the intertwining of hair into a patterned style, which may include
29 the addition of decorative or nonstructural elements such as beads, thread, or
30 ornaments, provided they do not alter the natural hair or require chemicals,
31 adhesives, or heat used on natural hair so that the natural hair is not
32 permanently altered. Hair braiding also includes minor trimming of hair or
33 hair extensions incidental to the braiding of hair.
34 (11c) Hair design. – The act of arranging, dressing, curling, waving, cleansing,

37 apparatus, or appliances or by use of cosmetic or chemical preparations or
38 antiseptics.

40 design.

44 issued by the Board.

46 fingernails and toenails, and the hands and feet, including the decoration of
47 the fingernails and the application of nail extensions and artificial nails. The
48 term "manicuring" shall not include the treatment of pathologic conditions.
49 (13) Manicurist. – An individual licensed by the Board to practice only that part of
50 cosmetic art that constitutes manicuring.

11 "§ 88B-13. Applicants licensed in other states.
12 (a) The Board shall issue a license to an applicant licensed in another state as an
13 apprentice, a cosmetologist, hair designer, esthetician, natural hair care specialist, hair braider,
14 or manicurist in another state if the applicant shows:
15 (1) The applicant is an active practitioner in good standing.
16 (2) The applicant has practiced at least one of the three years immediately
17 preceding the application for a license.
18 (3) There is no disciplinary proceeding or unresolved complaint pending against
19 the applicant at the time a license is to be issued by this State.
20 (4) The licensure requirements in the state in which the applicant is licensed are
21 substantially equivalent to those required by this State.
22 (b) Instead of meeting the requirements in subsection (a) of this section, any applicant
23 who is licensed as a cosmetologist, hair designer, esthetician, natural hair care specialist, or
24 manicurist in another state shall be admitted to practice in this State under the same reciprocity
25 or comity provisions that the state in which the applicant is licensed grants to persons licensed in
26 this State.
27 (c) The Board may establish standards for issuing a license to an applicant who is licensed
28 as a teacher in another state. These standards shall include a requirement that the licensure
29 requirements in the state in which the teacher is licensed shall be substantially equivalent to those
30 required in this State and that the applicant shall be licensed by the Board to practice in the area
31 in which the applicant is licensed to teach.

32 "§ 88B-21. Renewals; expired licenses; inactive status.
33 (a) Each license to operate a cosmetic art shop or mobile cosmetic art shop shall be
34 renewed on or before the first day of February of each year. As provided in G.S. 88B-20, a late
35 fee shall be charged for licenses renewed after February 1. Any license not renewed by March 1
36 of each year shall expire. A cosmetic art shop or mobile cosmetic art shop whose license has
37 been expired for one year or less shall have the license reinstated immediately upon payment of
38 the reinstatement fee, the late fee, and all unpaid license fees. The licensee shall submit to the
39 Board, as a part of the renewal process, a list of all licensed cosmetologists who practice cosmetic
40 art in the shop and shall identify each as an employee or a booth renter.
41 (b) Cosmetologist licenses shall be renewed on or before October 1 every three years
42 beginning October 1, 1998. A late fee shall be charged for renewals after that date. Any license
43 not renewed shall expire on October 1 of the year that renewal is required. The Board may
44 develop and implement a plan for staggered license renewal and may prorate license fees to
45 implement such a plan.
