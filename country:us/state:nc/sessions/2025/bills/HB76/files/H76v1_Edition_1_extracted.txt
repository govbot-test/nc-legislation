SESSION 2025

HOUSE BILL 76
Short Title: Protect Access to In Vitro Fertilization. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Cotham, Campbell, Paré, and Schietzelt (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
February 11, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT PROTECTING THE RIGHT TO ACCESS ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE
3 TECHNOLOGY.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
6 to read:
7 "Article 1O.
8 "Assisted Reproductive Technology.
9 "§ 90-21.160. Definitions.
10 The following definitions apply in this Article:
11 (1) Assisted reproductive technology. – All treatments or procedures that include
12 the handling of human oocytes or human embryos, including in vitro
13 fertilization, gamete intrafallopian transfer, and zygote intrafallopian transfer.
14 (2) Health care provider. – Either of the following:
15 a. An individual who is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized under
16 this Chapter to provide health care services in the ordinary course of
17 business or practice of a profession or in an approved education or
18 training program.
19 b. A health care facility licensed under Chapter 131E of the General
20 Statutes to provide health care services to patients.
21 The term "health care provider" includes (i) an agent or employee of a
22 health care facility that is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized to
23 provide health care services, (ii) the officers and directors of a health care
24 facility, and (iii) an agent or employee of a health care provider who is
25 licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized to provide health care services.
26 (3) Health care service. – A health or medical procedure or service rendered by a
27 health care provider that meets either of the following criteria:
28 a. Provides testing, diagnosis, or treatment of a health condition, illness,
29 injury, or disease.
30 b. Dispenses drugs, medical devices, medical appliances, or medical
31 goods for the treatment of a health condition, illness, injury, or disease.
32 "§ 90-21.162. Right to access assisted reproductive technology.
33 Neither the State nor any of its political subdivisions shall prohibit, unreasonably limit, or
34 interfere with any of the following:
35 (1) The right of a patient to access assisted reproductive technology.

9 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
