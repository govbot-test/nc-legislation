SENATE BILL DRS35010-LG-6
Short Title: Adopt Bottlenose Dolphin/State Marine Mammal. (Public)
Sponsors: Senator Hanig (Primary Sponsor).
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT ADOPTING THE BOTTLENOSE DOLPHIN AS THE STATE MARINE
3 MAMMAL.
4 Whereas, bottlenose dolphins are marine mammals that are abundant along North
5 Carolina's coastline; and
6 Whereas, bottlenose dolphins have a short, thick beak and a curved mouth, giving the
7 appearance that they are always smiling; and
8 Whereas, bottlenose dolphins are usually gray in color and can range in length from
9 6 to 13 feet long; and
10 Whereas, bottlenose dolphins are social animals that travel in groups of 10-15, called
11 "pods"; and
12 Whereas, bottlenose dolphins produce high-pitched clicks, called "echolocation," to
13 help them navigate and find food; and
14 Whereas, bottlenose dolphins can swim speeds of over 20 miles per hour, dive as deep
15 as 800 feet below the surface, and can launch themselves up to 15 feet out of the water; and
16 Whereas, the State of North Carolina does not have an official State marine mammal;
17 Now, therefore,
18 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
19 SECTION 1. Chapter 145 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
20 section to read:
21 "§ 145-52. State marine mammal.
22 The bottlenose dolphin is adopted as the official marine mammal of the State of North
23 Carolina."
24 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law.
