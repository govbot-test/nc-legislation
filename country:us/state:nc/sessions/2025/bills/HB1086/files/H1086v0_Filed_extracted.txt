15 PART I. CAREER-READY LEAD TEACHER ACADEMY PILOT PROGRAM
16 SECTION 1.1. The purpose of this Part is to provide alternative pathways to career
17 advancement in licensed child care. To that end, the North Carolina Partnership for Children,
18 Inc., in collaboration with the North Carolina Community Colleges System Office, shall develop
19 and implement a two-year pilot program that expands the child care workforce academies
20 currently operating in Johnston and Wayne Counties. The pilot program shall establish
21 career-ready lead teacher academies across the State designed to provide free, comprehensive
22 training and support to individuals, with no experience or education in child care, who are
23 interested in pursuing a career in child care.
24 SECTION 1.2. Local partnerships in the following counties shall participate in the
25 pilot program described under this Part: Johnston and Wayne. The North Carolina Partnership
26 for Children, Inc. (NCPC), in collaboration with the Community Colleges System Office, shall
27 select 10 additional local partnerships to participate in the pilot program with at least one local
28 partnership selected from each of the four local partnership regions of the statewide Smart Start
29 network, which regions are the West, Mid-West, Mid-East, and East.
30 SECTION 1.3. The Community Colleges System Office and local community
31 colleges shall coordinate to ensure that graduates of the career-ready lead teacher academies meet
32 all requirements to be credentialed with the North Carolina Early Childhood Credential as lead
33 teachers in child care in this State.
34 SECTION 1.4.(a) Each child care career-ready lead teacher academy (academy)
35 shall seek to enroll no less than 10 students, with a goal of enrolling 15 students in each course.
36 The academy shall operate up to eight hours per day over a two- to three-week period. A

13 SECTION 1.4.(c) Students shall be eligible to receive an additional one-time stipend
14 in the amount of five hundred dollars ($500.00) in State funds after completing one year of
15 employment as a lead teacher in a licensed child care program in this State. To receive the
16 additional stipend under this subsection, the graduate shall do each of the following:
17 (1) Begin full-time employment as a lead teacher in a licensed child care center
18 or family child care home within three months from the date of graduation
19 from an academy.
20 (2) Complete the one-year of employment required by this subsection within a
21 consecutive 12-month period at the same child care program.
22 SECTION 1.4.(d) The stipends provided under this section shall be available to a
23 graduate of an academy who is employed by a licensed child care facility in this State, including
24 a graduate who establishes a family child care home.
25 SECTION 1.5. The North Carolina Partnership for Children, Inc., and local
26 partnerships shall (i) collaborate with the community college in the county or counties in which
27 the local partnership is located, as practicable, to implement the career-ready lead teacher
28 academy and (ii) ensure information about the career-ready lead teacher academy in the
29 respective county is made available to the public.
30 SECTION 1.6. The North Carolina Partnership for Children, Inc. (NCPC), in
31 collaboration with the local partnerships and community colleges participating in the pilot
32 program, shall submit a progress report on the pilot program to the Joint Legislative Oversight
33 Committee on Health and Human Services and the Fiscal Research Division by March 31, 2027,
34 with any concerns or recommendations for program expansion. A final report shall be submitted
35 by December 31, 2027, and shall include, at a minimum, the following:
36 (1) The local partnerships and community colleges participating in the pilot
37 program.
38 (2) The number of students enrolled in each academy, by county.
39 (3) The number of students who successfully completed the academy, by county.
40 (4) The number of newly credentialed graduates employed as lead teachers in
41 licensed child care programs, by county.
42 (5) The outcomes achieved from the pilot program, including any
43 recommendations for expanding the program statewide.
44 (6) The number of new child care slots available as a result of adding the new
45 lead teachers.
46 (7) Total program costs, including any administrative costs borne by the county.
47 (8) The amount of funds needed to expand the program statewide.

30 behavioral health initiatives described in subsection (a) of this section to the Joint Legislative
31 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services, the Secretary of the Department of Health

33 November 15, 2028. The progress report and final report shall include all of the following:
34 (1) The name of each local partnership that received funds.
35 (2) The number of children served by each local partnership.
36 (3) The types of mental and behavioral health services provided by each local
37 partnership.
38 (4) Recommendations for continuing and/or expanding mental and behavioral
39 health initiatives for children, families, and staff in child care facility settings.
40 SECTION 2.1.(c) Additional funds allocated in this Part to the North Carolina

11 PART III. CHILD CARE REFORMS/CHILD CARE CENTER ADMINISTRATORS
12 SECTION 3.1. G.S. 110-91(8) is amended by adding the following sub-subdivisions
13 to read:
14 "a1. A licensed child care center may use the combined education and
15 experience of two staff persons to meet the requirements for a Level II
16 or Level III administrator designation as set forth in rules established
17 by the Commission and shall receive the same quality rating
18 improvement system (QRIS) credit toward a star-rated license as the
19 center would receive if the requirements were met by one staff person,
20 so long as the staff person who has the early childhood education and
21 experience is assigned to the center and on-site at least 20 hours per
22 week.
23 a2. The Department shall allow a child care center administrator to earn
24 the North Carolina Early Childhood Administration Credential
25 without completing coursework in EDU 261 and EDU 262, or the
26 equivalent of both, when the administrator has (i) an associate degree
27 or higher in business administration or a related field or (ii) two years
28 of verifiable business or administrative work experience."
29 SECTION 3.2. This Part is effective when it becomes law.

31 PART IV. STUDY INSURANCE PROGRAM FOR CHILD CARE PROVIDERS
32 SECTION 4.1.(a) The Department of Insurance shall utilize a consulting firm, an
33 actuarial firm, a brokerage firm, or any combination thereof, to study the feasibility of
34 establishing liability insurance coverage, including State-supported captive insurance,
35 risk-pooling, joint underwriting association, joint reinsurance, or other organizations, for North
36 Carolina child care providers in order to provide liability insurance coverage to participating
37 child care providers, which has the potential to support risk distribution while promoting strong
38 governance and shared accountability. All of the following shall be part of the study undertaken
39 in accordance with this subsection:
40 (1) The collection of loss data and operational information from child care
41 providers statewide in order to estimate program feasibility, premium
42 requirements, and necessary surplus levels.
43 (2) An evaluation of the viability of a program that is designed primarily to
44 provide general liability coverage, including a focus on sexual abuse and
45 molestation (SAM) coverage, whether offered separately or bundled.
46 (3) A determination of the amount of public funding needed to initially capitalize
47 the insurer and an exploration of potential alternative solutions to that public
48 funding.
49 (4) An analysis of conditions in the traditional, excess and surplus (E&S), and
50 unauthorized foreign reinsurance markets, including costs, affordability,

2 experience in accessing adequate liability coverage.
3 (5) A determination of whether commercial insurance carriers would be willing

6 (6) The recommended design for the most viable program for North Carolina
