4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Article 12 of Subchapter II of Chapter 105 of the General Statutes is
6 amended by adding a new section to read:
7 "§ 105-277.1E. Secure home and future property tax relief.
8 (a) Classification. – A permanent residence owned and occupied by a qualifying owner
9 is designated a special class of property under Article V, Section 2(2) of the North Carolina
10 Constitution and is taxable in accordance with this section. An owner who receives relief under
11 this section may not receive other property tax relief.
12 (b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply to this section:
13 (1) Owner. – Defined in G.S. 105-277.1.
14 (2) Permanent residence. – Defined in G.S. 105-277.1.
15 (c) Qualifying Owner. – For the purpose of qualifying for the secure home and future
16 property tax relief under this section, a qualifying owner is an owner who meets all of the
17 following requirements as of January 1 preceding the taxable year for which the benefit is
18 claimed:
19 (1) The owner has owned and occupied the property as a permanent residence for
20 the previous 15 consecutive years.
21 (2) The owner is a North Carolina resident.
22 (d) Tax Limitation. – A qualifying owner may defer the portion of the principal amount
23 of tax that is imposed for the current tax year on his or her permanent residence that exceeds the
24 amount due under this subsection. The principal amount of tax due for the current tax year on a
25 permanent residence of a qualifying owner may not exceed the greater of the following:
26 (1) The principal amount of tax due for the preceding tax year, increased by two
27 percent (2%).
28 (2) The amount determined under subsection (e) of this section.
29 (e) Recalculation. – When the value of a property increases due to a physical change to
30 the land or an improvement on the land, other than a change listed under G.S. 105-287(b), the
31 principal amount of tax due on a permanent residence receiving relief under this section is the
32 product of the appraised value of the permanent residence with the change or improvement as of
33 January 1 in the year after the change or improvement occurs multiplied by the property tax rate
34 in effect for the taxable year beginning July 1 of the year in which the property is reappraised.
35 Upon the occurrence of an event triggering this subsection, the taxpayer shall notify the tax
36 assessor of the change no later than the close of the listing period following the change. The

1 provisions of G.S. 105-312 shall apply for failing to notify the tax assessor as required under this
2 subsection.
3 (f) Temporary Absence. – An otherwise qualifying owner does not lose the benefit of
4 this section because of a temporary absence from his or her permanent residence for reasons of
5 health, or because of an extended absence while confined to a rest home or nursing home, so long
6 as the residence is unoccupied or occupied by the owner's spouse or other dependent.
7 (g) Deferred Taxes. – The difference between the taxes due under this section and the
8 taxes that would have been payable in the absence of this section are a lien on the real property
9 of the taxpayer as provided in G.S. 105-355(a). The difference in taxes must be carried forward
10 in the records of each taxing unit as deferred taxes. The deferred taxes for the preceding three
11 fiscal years are due and payable in accordance with G.S. 105-277.1F when the property loses its
12 eligibility for deferral as a result of a disqualifying event described in subsection (h) of this
13 section. On or before September 1 of each year, the collector must send to the mailing address of
14 a residence on which taxes have been deferred a notice stating the amount of deferred taxes and
15 interest that would be due and payable upon the occurrence of a disqualifying event.
16 (h) Disqualifying Events. – A property loses its eligibility for the benefit provided under
17 this section as a result of a disqualifying event. The tax for the fiscal year that begins in the
18 calendar year in which the disqualifying event occurs is computed as if the property had not been
19 classified for that year for a benefit under this section. Each of the following constitutes a
20 disqualifying event:
21 (1) The owner transfers the residence. Transfer of the residence is not a
22 disqualifying event if (i) the owner transfers the residence to a co-owner of
23 the residence or, as part of a divorce proceeding, to his or her spouse and (ii)
24 that individual occupies or continues to occupy the property as his or her
25 permanent residence.
26 (2) The owner dies. Death of the owner is not a disqualifying event if (i) the
27 owner's share passes to a co-owner of the residence or to his or her spouse and
28 (ii) that individual occupies or continues to occupy the property as his or her
29 permanent residence.
30 (3) The owner ceases to use the property as a permanent residence.
31 (i) Multiple Owners. – A permanent residence owned and occupied by husband and wife
32 is entitled to the full benefit of the secure home and future property tax relief notwithstanding
33 that only one of them meets the length of occupancy and ownership requirements of this section.
34 When a permanent residence is owned and occupied by two or more persons other than husband
35 and wife, no relief is allowed unless all of the owners qualify.
36 (j) Application. – An application for property tax relief provided by this section should
37 be filed during the regular listing period but may be filed and must be accepted at any time up to
38 and through June 1 preceding the tax year for which the relief is claimed. Persons may apply for
39 this property tax relief by entering the appropriate information on a form made available by the
40 assessor under G.S. 105-282.1.
41 (k) Education. – The Local Government Division of the North Carolina Department of
42 Revenue shall post information pertaining to the tax relief provided under this section
43 electronically on its website, which shall include information on who qualifies and how a
44 qualifying taxpayer may apply for tax relief. The Local Government Division of the North
45 Carolina Department of Revenue, in conjunction with county tax assessors, shall also create an
46 educational presentation to be used by county tax assessors at community workshops for the
47 purpose of educating taxpayers on the relief provided under this section.
48 (l) Report. – The Local Government Division of the North Carolina Department of
49 Revenue shall submit a report to the Revenue Laws Study Committee regarding the effectiveness
50 of the tax relief provided under this section. The report shall contain, at a minimum, (i) property
51 tax trends, (ii) the amount of taxpayers receiving the benefit provided in this section, (iii) the
