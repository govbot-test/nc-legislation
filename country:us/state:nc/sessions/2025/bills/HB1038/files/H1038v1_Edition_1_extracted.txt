Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: At-Large Elections/Jacksonville City Council. (Local)
Sponsors: Representatives Gable and Shepard (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Election Law, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 23, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REQUIRE THAT ALL MEMBERS OF THE JACKSONVILLE CITY COUNCIL
3 BE ELECTED AT-LARGE.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Section 2-8 of the Charter of the City of Jacksonville, being Chapter
6 911 of the 1967 Session Laws, as amended by City Ordinance No. 89-21 adopted on April 26,
7 1989, and City Ordinance No. 06-59 adopted on November 21, 2006, reads as rewritten:
8 "Sec. 2-8. – Election of mayor and council – Generally.
9 At a regular election to be held by the Onslow County Board of Elections on the Tuesday
10 after the first Monday in November, there shall be elected a mayor and six councilmembers,
11 Council members, all of whom shall reside within the city and who shall run and be elected in
12 the following manner: Two of the councilmembers shall run at large and four of the
13 councilmembers shall be elected from the wards established by the City of Jacksonville under
14 this ordinance and map published herewith or as such wards may hereafter be constituted, and
15 there shall be elected only one councilmember from each ward who shall be a resident of the
16 ward for which he is elected and shall be voted on by the qualified voters of such ward. The
17 candidate receiving the highest number of votes for the office of councilmember from the ward
18 of his residence shall be declared elected to that office. The two candidates running at large
19 shall be voted on by all qualified voters of the city and the two candidates running at large who
20 receive the highest number of votes for the office of councilmember shall be declared elected
21 to that office. The candidate for mayor shall be elected by the electors of the city at large. The
22 candidate receiving the highest number of votes for the office of mayor shall be declared
23 elected mayor.
24 Effective December 27, 2006, at the next regular municipal election following
25 pre-clearance of this Charter Amendment by the Department of Justice, if said pre-clearance
26 occurs on or before the last day of the filing period for said election, the candidates receiving
27 the highest number of votes for the Office of Mayor and the candidates receiving the highest
28 number of votes for Ward 3 and Ward 4 (minority ward) Council Members shall be elected for
29 an initial four (4) year term; and the two candidates receiving the highest number of votes for
30 At-Large Council Members and the candidates receiving the highest number of votes for Ward
31 1 (minority ward) and Ward 2 Council Members shall be elected for an initial two (2) year
32 term. Subsequent elections for the Mayor and all City Council Members shall be for four-year
33 terms and until their successors are elected and qualified.at-large by the qualified voters in the
34 entire City of Jacksonville. The mayor shall be elected to serve a four-year term and until a
*H1038-v-1*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 successor is elected and qualified. Council members shall be elected to serve four-year
2 staggered terms and until their successors are elected and qualified."
3 SECTION 2. Beginning in 2027 with the regular municipal elections for the City of
4 Jacksonville, and biennially thereafter, all Council members shall be elected "at-large" by the
5 qualified voters of the City. No Council members for the City of Jacksonville shall be elected by
6 ward but the same staggering of terms shall remain in place as when members of the City Council
7 were elected by ward.
8 SECTION 3. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to elections held
9 on or after that date.
