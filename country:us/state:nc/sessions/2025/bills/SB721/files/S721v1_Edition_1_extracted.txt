GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
SENATE BILL 721
Short Title: Modify Public-Initiated Criminal Processes. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Smith and Bradley (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO DISALLOW THE ISSUANCE OF A WARRANT FOR ARREST WHEN A
3 CRIMINAL CHARGE IS TAKEN OUT BY A NON-LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER,
4 EXCEPT IN CASES OF DOMESTIC VIOLENCE.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 15A-304(b) reads as rewritten:
7 "(b) When Issued. –
8 …
9 (3) When Citizen-initiated. – If the finding of probable cause pursuant to
10 subsection (d) of this section is based solely upon an affidavit or oral
11 testimony under oath or affirmation of a person who is not a sworn law
12 enforcement officer, the issuing official shall not issue a warrant for arrest and
13 instead shall issue a criminal summons, unless one of the following
14 circumstances exists:a crime of domestic violence, as defined in G.S. 50B-1,
15 is alleged.
16 a. There is corroborating testimony of the facts establishing probable
17 cause from a sworn law enforcement officer or at least one
18 disinterested witness.
19 b. The official finds that obtaining investigation of the alleged offense by
20 a law enforcement agency would constitute a substantial burden for
21 the complainant.
22 c. The official finds substantial evidence of one or more of the
23 circumstances listed in subdivision (1) of this subsection."
24 SECTION 2. This act is effective October 1, 2025, and applies to criminal processes
25 issued on or after that date.
