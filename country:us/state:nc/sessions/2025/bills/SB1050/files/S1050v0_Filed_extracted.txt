7 PART I. OFFICE OF STATE FIRE MARSHAL EMERGENCY SUPPORT FUNCTIONS
8 SECTION 1.(a) Article 1 of Chapter 166A of the General Statutes is amended by
9 adding a new part to read:
10 "Part 6A. Certain Emergency Response Planning and Operations.
11 "§ 166A-19.45. Office of State Fire Marshal Disaster Response.
12 (a) Purpose. – The purpose of this Part is to align operational authority between certain
13 State agencies and associated emergency response activities. The combination of firefighting,
14 search and rescue, and hazardous materials response align with the responsibilities of the Office
15 of the State Fire Marshal, and shall be managed, directed, and implemented during emergency
16 response by the State Fire Marshal or their designee. The Office shall support the costs associated
17 with implementing the provisions of this Part with the IRF, consistent with the limitation and use
18 listed under G.S. 58-6-25(12).
19 (b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this Part:
20 (1) Office. – The Office of the State Fire Marshal.
21 (2) EMAC. – The Emergency Management Assistance Compact under Article 4
22 of this Chapter.
23 (3) ESF. – Emergency Support Function, as defined under the Incident Command
24 System and National Incident Management System.
25 (4) Lead State Agency. – As defined in the NCEOP.
26 (5) Lead Technical Agency. – As defined in the NCEOP.
27 (6) NCEOP. – The North Carolina Emergency Operations Plan.
28 (7) National Incident Management System (NIMS). – Established under
29 Homeland Security Presidential Directive #5, issued February 28, 2003, as
30 amended.
31 (8) Presidential Policy Directive/PPD-8. – Issued by the President of the United
32 States on March 30, 2011, to develop the National Preparedness Goal to
33 identify core capabilities necessary for preparedness to guide activities that
34 will enable the nation to achieve those directives and goals.
35 (9) IRF. – The Insurance Regulatory Fund established in G.S. 58-6-25.
36 (10) USAR. – Urban Search and Rescue team.

22 "§ 166A-19.50. Office of State Fire Marshal Disaster Response Fund.
23 (a) Establishment. – There is established the Office of State Fire Marshal Disaster
24 Response Fund within the Office a nonreverting special fund within the Office of State Fire
25 Marshall of the Department of Insurance. The Office shall administer the Fund consistent with
26 this section.
27 (b) Purpose; Uses. – The funds shall be used by the Office for all of the following:
28 (1) Provide immediate emergency grants to fire departments and rescue squads to
29 restore or maintain operational needs and capabilities following a disaster.
30 (2) For disaster readiness, preparedness, and response activities, including the
31 purchase of equipment, supplies, and contracted services necessary to support
32 emergency operations.
33 (c) Administration. – The Office may use up to five percent (5%) of the Fund to
34 administer the Fund each fiscal year.
35 (d) Procurement Exception. – Notwithstanding Articles 3, 3C, or 8 of Chapter 143 of the
36 General Statutes Article 15 of Chapter 143B of the General Statutes, or any other provision of
37 law to the contrary, the Office may use the funds in the Fund if, in the determination of the State
38 Fire Marshal, a large scale emergency or disaster warrants the use or procurement of resources
39 or services listed in subsection (b) of this section. To the extent practicable, the Office shall seek
40 competitive pricing and shall maintain documentation of the actions taken under this subsection.
41 (e) Reporting Requirement. – On October 1 of each year, the Office shall submit a report
42 on the uses, balance, and other significant information of the Fund to the Joint Legislative
43 Emergency Management Oversight Committee, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on
44 General Government, and the Fiscal Research Division."
