12 SECTION 3. G.S. 95-227 reads as rewritten:
13 "§ 95-227. Enforcement.
14 (a) For the purpose of enforcing the standards provided by this Article, the provisions of
15 G.S. 95-129, G.S. 95-130 and G.S. 95-136 through G.S. 95-142 shall apply under this Article in
16 a similar manner as they apply to places of employment under OSHANC; however,
17 G.S. 95-129(4), 95-130(2), and 95-130(6) do not apply to migrant housing. For the purposes of
18 this Article, the term:
19 (1) "Employer" in G.S. 95-129, G.S. 95-130 and G.S. 95-136 through
20 G.S. 95-142 shall be construed to mean an operator.
21 (2) "Employee" shall be construed to mean a migrant.
22 (3) Repealed by Session Laws 2007-548, s. 5, effective August 31, 2007.
23 (b) The Commissioner may establish a new division to enforce this Article.
24 (c) The Department of Labor of North Carolina shall maintain a list of operators and the
25 physical address of their migrant housing units, number of beds, and the date of the annual
26 preoccupancy inspection and certification.
27 (d) The Department of Labor of North Carolina shall maintain a summary of any
28 inspections filed annually with the Division that enforce this Article, including the number and
29 type of citations issued and the violations found, if any. For each violation, the Department shall
30 document in the summary the name of the operator and identifying information of the migrant
31 housing under G.S. 95-226(c)(2). The Department shall cross-reference each physical address
32 documented under this subsection to the physical address on each application for inspection for
33 compliance with the requirements of this Article as pertaining not only to a named operator, but
34 also to the physical address of the migrant housing.
35 (d1) The Department of Labor of North Carolina shall not provide a preoccupancy
36 inspection to any operator known to the Department to be barred by any State or federal authority
37 from participation in temporary foreign agricultural labor programs or to be otherwise in
38 violation of this Article. If the Department of Labor of North Carolina determines that an operator
39 has falsified the affidavit or any information contained in the affidavit required by
40 G.S. 95-226(h), then the Department shall not provide a preoccupancy inspection to the operator
41 for three years from the date of the affidavit.
42 (e) The Commissioner shall report no later than May 1 of each year to the Chairpersons
43 of the Senate Appropriations Committee on Natural and Economic Resources, the Chairpersons
44 of the House of Representatives Appropriations Committee on Agriculture and Natural and
45 Economic Resources, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Agriculture and Natural and
46 Economic Resources, and the Fiscal Research Division regarding the number of annual
47 preoccupancy certifications issued, the number of operators with one hundred percent (100%)
48 compliance at the preoccupancy inspection, the number of postoccupancy inspections conducted
49 by the Department of Labor of North Carolina, the number and type of citations and fines issued,
50 the total number of migrant worker beds in the State, and the identification of operators who fail
51 to apply for or obtain permits to operate migrant housing pursuant to this Article."
