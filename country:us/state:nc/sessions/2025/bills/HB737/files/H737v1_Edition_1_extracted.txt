Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Licensing Course Removal/Insurance Producers. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Humphrey and Balkcom (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Insurance, if favorable, Finance, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of
the House
April 3, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ELIMINATE THE TRAINING COURSE REQUIREMENTS FOR INSURANCE
3 PRODUCER LICENSURE.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 58-33-30 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 58-33-30. License requirements.
7 The Commissioner shall not issue or continue any license of an insurance producer, limited
8 representative, adjuster, or motor vehicle damage appraiser except as follows:
9 …
10 (d) Education and Training. –
11 (1) Each applicant must have had special education, training, or
12 experience of sufficient duration and extent reasonably to satisfy the
13 Commissioner that the applicant possesses the competence necessary
14 to fulfill the responsibilities of an insurance producer, limited
15 representative, adjuster, or motor vehicle damage appraiser. The
16 Commissioner shall not require an individual who applies for an
17 insurance producer license in this State to complete any specific
18 amount of instruction or any specific course of instruction.
19 (2) All individual applicants for licensing as insurance producers under
20 G.S. 58-33-26(c1)(1), (2), (4), (6), or (7) shall furnish evidence
21 satisfactory to the Commissioner of successful completion of at least
22 20 hours of instruction for each license, which shall in all cases include
23 the general principles of insurance and any other topics relevant to the
24 license that the Commissioner establishes by administrative rules. Any
25 applicant who submits satisfactory evidence of having successfully
26 completed a producer training course that has been approved by the
27 Commissioner and that is offered by or under the auspices of a
28 property, casualty, life or accident and health or sickness insurance
29 company admitted to do business in this State or a professional
30 insurance association shall be deemed to have satisfied the educational
31 requirements of this subdivision.Any producer training course
32 developed for the purpose of preparing an applicant for the
33 examination required by subsection (e) of this section must be
34 approved by the Commissioner.

1 (3) Each resident applicant for a Medicare supplement and long-term care
2 insurance license shall furnish evidence satisfactory to the
3 Commissioner of successful completion of 10 hours of instruction,
4 which shall in all cases include the principles of Medicare supplement
5 and long-term care insurance and federal and North Carolina law
6 relating to such insurance. A resident applicant who submits
7 satisfactory evidence of having successfully completed a producer
8 training course that has been approved by the Commissioner and that
9 is offered by or under the auspices of a licensed life or health insurer
10 or a professional insurance association satisfies the educational
11 requirements of this subdivision.
12 (e) Examination. –
13 …
14 (5) The Commissioner shall collect in advance the examination and
15 registration fees provided in G.S. 58-33-125 and in subsection
16 subdivision (4) of this section. subsection. The Commissioner shall
17 make or cause to be made available to all applicants, for a reasonable
18 fee to offset the costs of production, materials that he considers
19 necessary for the applicants' proper preparation for examinations. The
20 Commissioner may contract directly with publishers and other
21 suppliers for the production of the preparatory materials, and contracts
22 so let by the Commissioner shall not be subject to Article 3 of Chapter
23 143 of the General Statutes. However, the Commissioner shall: (i)
24 submit all proposed contracts for supplies, materials, printing,
25 equipment, and contractual services that exceed one million dollars
26 ($1,000,000) authorized by this subdivision to the Attorney General or
27 the Attorney General's designee for review as provided in
28 G.S. 114-8.3; and (ii) include in all contracts to be awarded by the
29 Commissioner under this subdivision a standard clause which provides
30 that the State Auditor and internal auditors of the Commissioner may
31 audit the records of the contractor during and after the term of the
32 contract to verify accounts and data affecting fees and performance.
33 The Commissioner shall not award a cost plus percentage of cost
34 contract for any purpose.
35 …."
36 SECTION 2. G.S. 58-33-32 reads as rewritten:
37 "§ 58-33-32. Interstate reciprocity in producer licensing.
38 …
39 (g) An individual who applies for an insurance producer license in this State who was
40 previously licensed for the same lines of authority in that individual's home state shall not be
41 required to complete any prelicensing education or examination. This exemption is available only
42 if:
43 …."
44 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to licensure
45 applications submitted on or after that date.
