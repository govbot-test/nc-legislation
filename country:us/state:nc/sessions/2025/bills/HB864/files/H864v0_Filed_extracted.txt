Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REVISE CERTAIN TRAFFIC LAWS AND THE DESIGN OF COLLEGIATE
3 INSIGNIA PLATES.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5
6 IMPEDING TRAFFIC
7 SECTION 1. G.S. 20-141(h) reads as rewritten:
8 "(h) No person shall operate a motor vehicle on the highway at such a slow speed as to
9 impede the normal and reasonable movement of traffic except when reduced speed is necessary
10 for safe operation or in compliance with law; provided, this provision shall not apply to farm
11 tractors and other motor vehicles operating at reasonable speeds for the type and nature of such
12 vehicles. No person shall operate a motor vehicle in the left lane of a multilane highway at a
13 speed that impedes the normal and reasonable movement of traffic."
14
15 MINIMUM OPERATING SPEED ON INTERSTATES AND PRIMARY HIGHWAYS
16 SECTION 2. G.S. 20-141(c) reads as rewritten:
17 "(c) Except while towing another vehicle, or when an advisory safe-speed sign indicates
18 a slower speed, or as otherwise provided by law, it shall be unlawful to operate a passenger
19 vehicle upon the interstate and primary highway system at less than the following speeds:
20 (1) Forty Forty-five miles per hour in a speed zone of 55 miles per hour.
21 (2) Forty-five Fifty miles per hour in a speed zone of 60 miles per hour or greater.
22 These minimum speeds shall be effective only when appropriate signs are posted indicating
23 the minimum speed."
24
25 TRUCKS IN LEFT LANE
26 SECTION 3. G.S. 20-146 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
27 "(f) A motor vehicle having a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of 26,001 pounds or
28 more shall not operate in the left most lane of a controlled-access highway with six or more
29 lanes."
30
31 COLLEGIATE INSIGNIA PLATES
32 SECTION 4.(a) G.S. 20-63(b1) is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
33 "() Collegiate Insignia Plate."
34 SECTION 4.(b) G.S. 20-81.12(b30) reads as rewritten:
35 "(b30) Collegiate Insignia Plates. – Except for a collegiate insignia plate for a public military
36 college or university, the Division must receive 300 or more applications for a collegiate insignia

2 collegiate insignia plate on a background authorized under G.S. 20-63(b1), for a college or

15 SECTION 4.(c) The Revisor of Statutes is authorized to alphabetize, number, and
16 renumber the special registration plates listed in G.S. 20-63(b1) to ensure that all special
17 registration plates are listed in alphabetical order and numbered accordingly.
18 SECTION 4.(d) A college or university with a collegiate insignia plate in production
19 on the effective date of this section may submit an application to the Division on or after the
20 effective date of this section to convert the plate authorized on a standard background to a
21 full-color background plate authorized under G.S. 20-63(b1), as amended by subsection (a) of
22 this section. Notwithstanding the minimum number of applications required in G.S. 20-63(b1)
23 and G.S. 20-81.12(b30), as amended by subsection (b) of this section, upon submission of
24 artwork that is approved by the Division and the alumni or alumnae association of the college or
25 university and meets all statutory requirements, the Division shall discontinue production and
26 issuance of the applicant college or university collegiate insignia plate on a standard background
27 and produce and issue the applicant college or university collegiate insignia plate on a full-color
28 background in its place. The Division must allow a vehicle owner with a valid college or
29 university collegiate insignia plate on a standard background that has been converted to a
30 full-color background plate pursuant to this subsection to request a full-color plate upon renewal
31 or to continue to renew the standard background plate in the vehicle owner's possession until the
32 plate is otherwise required by law to be surrendered, canceled, revoked, or replaced.
33
34 EFFECTIVE DATE
35 SECTION 5. Section 4 of this act becomes effective October 1, 2025. The remainder
36 of this act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to offenses on or after that date.
