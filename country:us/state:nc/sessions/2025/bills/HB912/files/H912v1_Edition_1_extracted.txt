GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 912
Short Title: The Hometown Opp. for Mentoring Educators Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Winslow, Paré, Willis, and Biggs (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 14, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CREATE THE HOMETOWN TEACHER GRANT PROGRAM, CALLED THE
3 H.O.M.E. (HOMETOWN OPPORTUNITIES FOR MENTORING EDUCATORS) ACT.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Article 17D of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
6 adding a new section to read:
7 "§ 115C-269.33. Hometown Teacher Grant Program.
8 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions shall apply in this section:
9 (1) Eligible student. – A student of a local school administrative unit who either
10 attended school for five years at a school in, or graduated from a high school
11 in, the local school administrative unit.
12 (2) Eligible teacher. – An eligible student who agrees to remain employed as a
13 teacher in a school in the same local school administrative unit where qualified
14 as an eligible student.
15 (3) Program. – The Hometown Teacher Grant Program.
16 (b) Program Established; Participation. – The Department shall establish the Hometown
17 Teacher Grant Program. The purpose of the Program is to provide grants to local school
18 administrative units to award funds for salary supplements for eligible teachers. The Department
19 shall establish criteria for participation in the Program. The Department shall adopt standards to
20 ensure that only eligible teachers receive an award of funds under the Program. Local school
21 administrative units shall submit to the Department a list identifying the number of eligible
22 teachers participating in the Program each year pursuant to a process to be established by the
23 Department.
24 (c) Agreement; Award. – To the extent funds are available for the Program, funds shall
25 be awarded to an eligible teacher who enters into an agreement to remain employed as required
26 by this section. Eligible teachers shall receive a salary supplement each month in the amount of
27 five hundred dollars ($500.00) as long as the eligible teacher remains in compliance with the
28 agreement.
29 (d) Promotion. – Local boards of education receiving grants under the Program shall
30 make efforts to promote the Program to high school students.
31 (e) Report to the General Assembly. – The Department shall report no later than May 15,
32 2026, and annually thereafter while funds are awarded under the Program, to the Joint Legislative
33 Education Oversight Committee regarding the Program, including at least the following
34 information:
35 (1) Funds awarded under the Program, including the following:

1 a. Demographic information regarding eligible teachers.
2 b. Number of award recipients by local school administrative unit.
3 (2) Placement rates, including the number of eligible teachers who have been
4 employed in the same local school administrative unit in which they were
5 eligible students.
6 (3) Recommendations to improve the Program and increase the number of
7 teachers in North Carolina."
8 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
9 Public Instruction the sum of ten million dollars ($10,000,000) in recurring funds for each fiscal
10 year of the 2025-2027 fiscal biennium for administration of the Program enacted by Section 1 of
11 this act.
12 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025, and applies to eligible teachers
13 entering agreements to participate in a program established in this act on or after that date.
