GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 422
Short Title: Beyond The Choice Act. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Charles Smith, Goodwin, Loftis, and Campbell (Primary
Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Higher Education, if favorable, Homeland Security and Military and Veterans
Affairs, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
March 18, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT PROVIDING THAT VETERANS, THEIR DEPENDENTS, AND OTHER
3 COVERED INDIVIDUALS UNDER THE VETERANS ACCESS, CHOICE, AND
4 ACCOUNTABILITY ACT WHO QUALIFY FOR ADMISSION TO AN INSTITUTION
5 OF HIGHER EDUCATION ARE ELIGIBLE TO BE CHARGED IN-STATE TUITION
6 WITHOUT REGARD TO THE NUMBER OF YEARS THAT HAVE ELAPSED SINCE
7 THEIR DISCHARGE FROM A PERIOD OF ACTIVE DUTY SERVICE OF NINETY
8 DAYS OR MORE.
9 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
10 SECTION 1. G.S. 116-143.3A reads as rewritten:
11 "§ 116-143.3A. Waiver of 12-month residency requirement for certain veterans and other
12 individuals.
13 …
14 (b) Waiver of 12-Month Residency Requirement for Certain Individuals. – Any veteran,
15 dependent of a veteran, or other "covered" individual under 38 U.S.C. § 3679 ("The Choice Act")
16 who qualifies for admission to an institution of higher education as defined in
17 G.S. 116-143.1(a)(3) is eligible to be charged the in-State tuition rate and applicable mandatory
18 fees for enrollment, to the extent required by 38 U.S.C. § 3679, enrollment without satisfying the
19 12-month residency requirement under G.S. 116-143.1, provided the individual meets all of the
20 following criteria:
21 …."
22 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning with
23 the 2025-2026 academic year.
