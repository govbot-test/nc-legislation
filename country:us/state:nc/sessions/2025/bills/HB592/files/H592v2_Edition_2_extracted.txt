9 "DEHP Hazard Management.
10 "§ 130A-453.33. Legislative finding.
11 The General Assembly finds all of the following:
12 (1) DEHP and other ortho-phthalates are toxic chemicals used primarily to
13 produce flexibility in plastics, mainly polyvinyl chloride (PVC).
14 (2) DEHP is the most common plasticizer used in medical devices, including
15 intravenous solution containers, which are also known as IV bags, and
16 intravenous tubing.
17 (3) Over the course of its shelf life, DEHP leaches from IV bags and tubing made
18 from DEHP into the solutions being held in the medical devices.
19 (4) DEHP is classified by the United States Environmental Protection Agency as
20 an endocrine-disrupting compound since it can:
21 a. Interfere with the hormonal system in humans and animals.
22 b. Mimic or block the actions of hormones, leading to adverse effects on
23 reproductive health, development, and metabolism.
24 (5) DEHP exposure has been associated with adverse effects on reproductive
25 organs and fertility. DEHP can also disrupt normal reproductive development,
26 reduce sperm quality, and affect hormone levels in both males and females.
27 (6) DEHP is metabolized in the liver and can accumulate in the body over time.
28 Prolonged exposure to high levels of DEHP has been shown to cause liver and
29 kidney damage in animal studies.
30 (7) Inhalation or ingestion of DEHP can cause respiratory irritation and allergic
31 reactions in some individuals, particularly those with preexisting respiratory
32 conditions or sensitivities.
33 (8) Studies have suggested a potential link between DEHP exposure and certain
34 types of cancer, including breast, liver, lung, and testicular cancer.
35 (9) The United States Environmental Protection Agency has determined that
36 DEHP is a probable human carcinogen.

1 (10) The leaching of DEHP from medical devices at varying concentrations has
2 been linked to multidrug resistance in breast cancer cells, inhibiting the
3 effectiveness of breast cancer drugs. This phenomenon has been observed at
4 both high and low concentrations of DEHP, highlighting the potential impact
5 of DEHP leaching on cancer treatment outcomes.
6 (11) Exposure to DEHP has been linked to multidrug resistance in triple-negative
7 breast cancer cells, inhibiting the apoptosis mechanism induced by breast
8 cancer drugs, such as tamoxifen, and increasing cell proliferation.
9 (12) DEHP has been suggested to serve as a mitogenic factor for estrogen
10 receptor-positive breast cancer cells, potentially making them multidrug
11 resistant.
12 "§ 130A-453.34. Definitions.
13 The following definitions apply in this Article:
14 (1) DEHP. – Di(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate.
15 (2) Health care practitioner. – An individual who is authorized to practice some
16 component of the healing arts by a license, permit, certificate, or registration
17 issued by a State licensing agency or board.
18 (3) Intentionally added DEHP. – DEHP that a manufacturer has intentionally
19 added to a product and that has a functional or technical effect on the product.
20 (4) Intravenous solution container. – A container used to house medicine, fluid,
21 or nutrition therapy that is intravenously delivered to a patient in a hospital,
22 outpatient facility, or other health care facility.
23 (5) Intravenous tubing. – Tubing used to intravenously administer fluids,
24 medication, or nutrients directly to an adult, child, or infant.
25 (6) Ortho-phthalate. – A class of chemicals that are esters of ortho-phthalic acid,
26 including DEHP or any of the following:
27 a. Benzyl butyl phthalate (BBP).
28 b. Dibutyl phthalate (DBP).
29 c. Dicyclohexyl phthalate (DCHP).
30 d. Diethyl phthalate (DEP).
31 e. Diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP).
32 f. Diisodecyl phthalate (DIDP).
33 g. Diisononyl phthalate (DINP).
34 h. Di-n-hexyl phthalate (DnHP).
35 i. Di-n-octyl phthalate (DNOP).
36 j. Di-n-pentyl phthalate (DnPP).
37 k. Diisoheptyl phthalate (DIHP).
38 (7) Unintentionally added DEHP. – DEHP in an intravenous solution container
39 or intravenous tubing product that is not used for functional or technical effect
40 on the product.
41 "§ 130A-453.35. Prohibitions.
42 (a) Intravenous Solution Containers. – Beginning January 1, 2030, a person or entity shall
43 not manufacture, sell, or distribute into commerce in the State of North Carolina intravenous
44 solution containers made with intentionally added DEHP.
45 (b) Intravenous Tubing. – Beginning January 1, 2035, a person or entity shall not
46 manufacture, sell, or distribute into commerce in the State of North Carolina intravenous tubing
47 made with intentionally added DEHP.
48 (c) Replacement. – A person may not replace DEHP, pursuant to this Article, with
49 another ortho-phthalate in a new or revised medical device.

1 (d) Maximum Quantity. – An intravenous solution container or intravenous tubing
2 product shall not have unintentionally added DEHP present at a quantity at or above 0.1 percent
3 weight per weight (w/w).
4 (e) Exemptions. – The following items, as described in Title 21 of the Code of Federal
5 Regulations, are exempt from these provisions:
6 (1) Human blood collection and storage bags.
7 (2) Apheresis and cell therapy blood kits and bags, including integral tubing.
8 (f) Delayed Compliance. – A person or entity, due to pending United States Food and
9 Drug Administration approval for the DEHP-free intravenous solution container or due to the
10 manufacturer not having adequate equipment to manufacture the DEHP-free intravenous solution
11 container, shall meet the requirement in subsection (a) of this section by January 1, 2032, if all
12 of the following conditions are met:
13 (1) The person or entity notified its North Carolina customers, no later than
14 October 1, 2025, that it has commenced development of the DEHP-free
15 intravenous solution container to meet the requirements of this section.
16 (2) The person or entity provides notice to its customers and posts to its official
17 internet website, no later than January 1, 2028, that it will not meet the
18 deadline imposed pursuant to subsection (a) of this section."
