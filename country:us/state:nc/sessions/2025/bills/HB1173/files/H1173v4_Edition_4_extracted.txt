11 SECTION 4.(a) Article 13A of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by
12 adding a new section to read:
13 "§ 14-50.32. Use, carry, or possess a firearm by a member of a criminal gang in relation to
14 or in furtherance of a serious felony, drug crime, or a crime of violence.
15 (a) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
16 (1) Brandish. – To display all or part of a firearm or otherwise make the presence
17 of the firearm known to another person.
18 (2) Crime of violence. – Any offense that would be chargeable by indictment
19 under the laws of this State for which the State proves, based on the
20 defendant's actual conduct in committing the offense, that the defendant or
21 another participant acting in concert with the defendant, used, attempted to
22 use, or threatened to use physical force against the person or property of
23 another.
24 (3) Drug crime. – Any offense that would be chargeable by indictment under
25 Article 5 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes.
26 (4) Firearm. – Any (i) weapon, including a starter gun, which will or is designed
27 to or may readily be converted to expel a projectile by the action of an
28 explosive, or its frame or receiver, or (ii) firearm muffler or firearm silencer.
29 This term does not include an antique firearm as defined in G.S. 14-409.11.
30 (5) Serious felony. – Any offense that would be chargeable under Chapter 14 of
31 the General Statutes that is a Class A, B1, B2, C, D, or E felony.
32 (b) It shall be unlawful for any member of a criminal gang to use or carry a firearm during
33 and in relation to, or to possess a firearm in furtherance of, any serious felony, drug crime, or
34 crime of violence. A violation of this subsection is a Class G felony, unless subsection (c) or (d)
35 of this section applies.
36 (c) If a firearm is brandished during the commission of the offense, then the violation is
37 a Class F felony.
38 (d) If a firearm is discharged during the commission of the offense, then the violation is
39 a Class D felony."

42 "§ 14-50.33. Sell, deliver, give, or transfer a firearm to a juvenile by a member of a criminal
43 gang.

22 SECTION 5.(a) Article 4 of Chapter 8C of the General Statutes is amended by
23 adding a new section to read:
24 "Rule 416. Evidence of criminal gang activity.
25 (a) For purposes of this rule, the term "criminal gang activity" is as defined in
26 G.S. 14-50.16A.
27 (b) In any proceeding in which a person is accused of conducting, participating in, or
28 conspiring to commit criminal gang activity, or in any proceeding under Article 13B of Chapter
29 14 of the General Statutes, evidence of the defendant's commission of criminal gang activity may
30 be considered for the purpose of proving any element of the alleged criminal offense.
31 (c) In any proceeding in which the prosecution intends to offer evidence under this rule,
32 the prosecutor shall disclose the evidence to the defendant, including statements of witnesses or
33 a summary of the substance of any testimony that is expected to be offered, at least 10 days in
34 advance of trial, unless the time is shortened or lengthened or pretrial notice is excused by the
35 judge upon good cause shown.
36 (d) This rule shall not be the exclusive means to admit or consider the evidence described
37 in this rule."
38 SECTION 5.(b) This section becomes effective December 1, 2026, and applies to
39 offenses committed on or after that date.
