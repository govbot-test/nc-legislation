1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ESTABLISH A PILOT PROGRAM WITHIN THE NORTH CAROLINA
3 DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION, FERRY DIVISION, TO OPERATE A
4 HOVERCRAFT FERRY ON THE HATTERAS-OCRACOKE ROUTE AND TO STUDY

6 REPLACING THE OBSOLESCING DIESEL FERRY FLEET.
7 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
8 SECTION 1.(a) Pilot Program Established. – The Department of Transportation,
9 Ferry Division (Ferry Division), shall establish and operate a hovercraft pilot program to evaluate
10 the feasibility of replacing the ferry fleet servicing the Hatteras-Ocracoke route with hovercraft
11 or air cushion vehicle (ACV) ferries.
12 SECTION 1.(b) Purpose. – The purpose of the pilot program is to:
13 (1) Improve travel times between Hatteras Island and Ocracoke Island.
14 (2) Reduce reliance on routine dredging of the Hatteras Inlet channel and achieve
15 long-term savings in dredging expenditures.
16 (3) Evaluate the potential to retire or repurpose the current diesel ferry fleet on
17 this route.
18 (4) Determine whether a fee-for-ride model, that is free for residents and charges
19 a fee for visitors, can provide a sustainable revenue stream to offset
20 operational costs.
21 SECTION 1.(c) Procurement. – The Ferry Division shall issue a Request for
22 Proposal (RFP) for the lease or purchase of one or more hovercrafts or ACVs suitable for
23 passenger and light vehicle service under the unique environmental conditions of Hatteras Inlet.
24 SECTION 1.(d) Operations. – The pilot program shall operate for a minimum of
25 two years from the date the vessel is launched.
26 (1) The Ferry Division shall design and implement a tiered fare structure whereby
27 island residents ride free of charge and visitors are charged a reasonable fee,
28 with revenues credited to the Ferry Fund.
29 (2) The Ferry Division may continue to operate any diesel ferries whose service
30 is displaced by the service provided by the pilot ferry for commercial vehicles
31 only during the pilot program period.
32 SECTION 1.(e) Feasibility Study. – The Ferry Division shall conduct a feasibility
33 study evaluating:
34 (1) The economic, environmental, and operational impacts of transitioning the
35 Hatteras-Ocracoke route to hovercraft service permanently.

1 (2) The long-term cost-savings from the reduced dredging operations.
2 (3) The practicality of retiring the existing diesel ferry fleet or dedicating it solely
3 to commercial vehicle transport.
4 (4) The financial viability of a visitor fee-based model, including impacts on
5 tourism.
6 SECTION 1.(f) Report. – The Ferry Division shall submit to the Joint Legislative
7 Transportation Oversight Committee and the Fiscal Research Division an interim report on the
8 study required by Section 1(e) of this act by March 1, 2027, and a final report by December 1,
9 2027.
10 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
11 Transportation, Ferry Division, for the 2026-2027 fiscal year the sum of twenty-five million
12 dollars ($25,000,000) in nonrecurring funds to implement the requirements of this act.
13 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2026.
