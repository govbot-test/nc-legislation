GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 7, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: Prevent Sexual Exploitation/Women and Minors. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative N. Jackson.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO PREVENT THE SEXUAL EXPLOITATION OF WOMEN AND MINORS.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Chapter 66 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new Article
5 to read:
6 "Article 51A.
7 "Prevent Sexual Exploitation of Women and Minors.
8 "§ 66-505. Short title; definitions.
9 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Prevent Sexual Exploitation of
10 Women and Minors Act."
11 (b) The following definitions apply in this Article:
12 (1) Authorized representative. – With respect to an individual:
13 a. A person authorized in writing under State or other applicable law by
14 the individual to act on behalf of the individual with regard to the
15 matter in question; or
16 b. In the case of an individual under the age of 18, a parent or legal
17 guardian of the individual.
18 (2) Coerced consent. – Purported consent obtained from a person lacking the
19 capacity to consent or obtained from a person with capacity to consent under
20 the following circumstances:
21 a. Through fraud, duress, misrepresentation, undue influence, or
22 nondisclosure.
23 b. Through exploiting or leveraging the person's (i) immigration status,
24 (ii) pregnancy, (iii) disability, (iv) substance abuse disorder, (v)
25 juvenile status, or (vi) economic circumstances.
26 (3) Consent. – An agreement that is informed and thorough and does not include
27 coerced consent.
28 (4) Distribute. – As defined in G.S. 66-500.
29 (5) Eligible person. – With respect to a pornographic image published to an online
30 entity, the term includes the following:
31 a. An individual depicted in the pornographic image who has not
32 provided consent, or who has withdrawn consent in compliance with
33 the laws applicable to the jurisdiction, for the distribution of the
34 pornographic image.
35 b. An authorized representative.
36 c. A law enforcement officer acting pursuant to a valid court order.

1 (6) Intimate visual depiction. – Any visual depiction of an individual meeting all
2 of the following criteria:
3 a. The individual is reasonably identifiable from the visual depiction
4 itself or information displayed in connection with the visual depiction,
5 including through (i) facial recognition, (ii) an identifying marking on
6 the individual, including a birthmark or piercing, (iii) an identifying
7 feature of the background of the visual depiction, (iv) voice matching,
8 or (v) written confirmation from an individual who is responsible, in
9 whole or in part, for the creation or development of the visual
10 depiction.
11 b. The individual depicted is engaging in sexual activity or the exposed
12 or substantially exposed genitals, anus, pubic area, or post-pubescent
13 female nipple of the individual depicted is visible.
14 (7) News-gathering organization. – As defined in G.S. 66-500.
15 (8) Online entity. – An individual or group of individuals working together or an
16 entity defined in G.S. 66-500.
17 (9) Online entity operator. – A provider for an online entity.
18 (10) Performer. – Any person portrayed in a visual depiction engaging in, or
19 assisting another person to engage in, sexual activity.
20 (11) Pornographic image. – A visual depiction of actual or feigned sexual activity
21 or an intimate visual depiction.
22 (12) Publish. – As defined in G.S. 66-500.
23 (13) Sexual activity. – As defined in G.S. 14-190.13.
24 (14) Visual depiction. – Any photograph, film, video, picture, digital image, or
25 computer-generated image or picture, whether made or produced by
26 electronic, mechanical, or other means.
27 "§ 66-506. Age verification obligations.
28 (a) An online entity operator may not publish or allow a user to publish a pornographic
29 image to the online entity unless the operator has verified that each individual appearing in the
30 pornographic image:
31 (1) Was not less than 18 years of age when the pornographic image was created;
32 (2) Has provided explicit written evidence of consent for each act of sexual
33 activity in which the individual engaged during the creation of the
34 pornographic image; and
35 (3) Has provided explicit written consent for the distribution of the specific
36 pornographic image.
37 (b) Separate consent is required for the act of sexual activity and for distribution of the
38 intimate visual depiction, as follows:
39 (1) Consent for sex act. – Consent described in subdivision (2) of subsection (a)
40 of this section does not imply or constitute evidence of consent described in
41 subdivision (3) of that subsection.
42 (2) Consent for distribution of image. – Consent described in subdivision (3) of
43 subsection (a) of this section does not imply or constitute evidence of consent
44 described in subdivision (2) of that subsection.
45 (c) To carry out the obligations of subsection (a) of this section, an online entity operator
46 shall obtain all of the following, either from the user or entity seeking to publish the pornographic
47 image or through other means:
48 (1) A consent form created or approved by the Attorney General under subsection
49 (d) of this section from each individual appearing in the pornographic image
50 that includes:
51 a. The name, date of birth, and signature of the individual.
Page 2 DRH30340-LR-36G
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 b. A statement that the individual is not less than 18 years of age, unless
2 no reasonable person could conclude that the individual is less than 30
3 years of age.
4 c. A statement that the consent is for distribution of the specific
5 pornographic image.
6 d. A statement that explains coerced consent and that the individual has
7 the right to withdraw the individual's consent at any time.
8 (2) Not less than one form of valid identification for each individual appearing in
9 the pornographic image (i) issued by an agency of the federal government or
10 of a state, local, or foreign government; and (ii) containing the name, date of
11 birth, signature, and photograph of the individual; and on which the name,
12 date of birth, and signature of the individual match the name, date of birth,
13 and signature of the individual on the consent form required under subsection
14 (a) of this section.
15 (d) The Attorney General may adopt rules for the implementation and enforcement of
16 this Article and shall create and make available to the public a consent form for purposes of this
17 section.
18 "§ 66-507. Removal of images.
19 (a) An online entity operator shall establish a procedure for removing a pornographic
20 image from the online entity at the request of a person and designate one or more employees of
21 the operator to be responsible for handling requests for removal of pornographic images.
22 (b) An online entity operator shall display a prominently visible notice on the website or
23 mobile application of the online entity that provides instructions on how a person can request the
24 removal of a pornographic image.
25 (c) If an online entity operator receives a request from an eligible person, through any
26 request mechanism offered by the operator under subsection (b) of this section to remove a
27 pornographic image that is being hosted by the online entity without the consent of an individual
28 who appears in the pornographic image, the operator shall remove the pornographic image as
29 quickly as possible, and in any event not later than 72 hours after receiving the request.
30 (d) If an online entity operator receives a request from a person other than an eligible
31 person (through any request mechanism offered by the operator under subsection (b) of this
32 section) to remove a pornographic image that is being hosted by the online entity without the
33 consent of an individual who appears in the pornographic image, then not later than 72 hours
34 after receiving the request, the operator shall do the following:
35 (1) Review the records of the operator with respect to the pornographic image to
36 determine whether the pornographic image was published to the platform in
37 accordance with the verification requirements of G.S. 66-506; and
38 (2) Remove the pornographic image if the operator determines that the
39 pornographic image was not published to the platform in accordance with the
40 verification requirements of G.S. 66-506.
41 (e) An online entity operator shall remove a pornographic image temporarily if any
42 question arises as to the consent of a performer. This requirement is in addition to the
43 requirements of subsections (c) and (d) of this section.
44 (f) At the request of a performer, a pornographic image distributed or published by an
45 online entity operator must be removed within 72 hours of the request being made, regardless of
46 the age or consent of the performer.
47 (g) In the case of a pornographic image that has been removed from an online entity in
48 accordance with this section, the online entity operator shall block the pornographic image, and
49 any altered or edited version of the pornographic image, from being distributed on or published
50 to the online entity again.
51 "§ 66-508. Obligations of users.

1 (a) A user of an online entity may not distribute or publish a pornographic image of an
2 individual to the online entity without the consent of the individual.
3 (b) For purposes of subsection (a) of this section, whether an individual has provided
4 consent to the publishing of a pornographic image shall be determined in accordance with this
5 Article.
6 "§ 66-509. Enforcement.
7 (a) Violations of this Article are subject to the imposition of civil penalties. In
8 determining the amount of the penalty, the Attorney General shall consider the degree and extent
9 of harm caused by the violation. A civil penalty under this Article shall accrue on a per day and
10 per image basis. The clear proceeds of civil penalties assessed pursuant to this section shall be
11 remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with G.S. 115C-457.2.
12 (b) A violation of this Article is a Class 1 misdemeanor in addition to any other sanctions
13 allowed by law.
14 (c) The Attorney General may impose a civil penalty on any online entity operator that
15 violates this Article in an amount of not more than ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for each day
16 during which a pornographic image remains on the online entity, beginning 24 hours after the
17 Attorney General provides notice of the violation to the operator.
18 (d) If an online entity operator violates this Article with respect to a pornographic image,
19 any eligible person may bring a civil action against the online entity operator for damages in an
20 amount equal to the greater of (i) ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for each day during which a
21 pornographic image remains on the online entity in violation of that section, calculated on a per
22 day and per image basis or (ii) actual damages. A prevailing eligible person shall be awarded
23 attorneys' fees.
24 (e) The Attorney General may impose a civil penalty on any online entity operator that
25 violates G.S. 66-507(b) in an amount of not more than ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for each
26 day during which the online entity remains in violation, beginning 24 hours after the Attorney
27 General provides notice of the violation to the operator.
28 (f) The Attorney General may impose a civil penalty on any online entity operator that
29 violates G.S. 66-507(c) in an amount of not more than five thousand dollars ($5,000) for each
30 day during which the online entity remains in violation of that section, beginning 24 hours after
31 the Attorney General provides notice of the violation to the operator. If an online entity operator
32 violates G.S. 66-507(d) with respect to a pornographic image, any eligible person may bring a
33 civil action against the online entity operator in an appropriate district court of the United States
34 for damages in an amount equal to the greater of (i) ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for each day
35 during which the pornographic image remains on the online entity in violation of that section,
36 calculated on a per day and per image basis or (ii) actual damages.
37 (g) An online entity operator shall not be liable for a violation of this Article if, in
38 allowing the publishing of a pornographic image to the online entity, the operator reasonably
39 relied on verification materials that were later found to be in violation of this Article, provided
40 that the operator removes the pornographic image not later than 24 hours after receiving notice
41 that the verification materials are in violation of this Article.
42 (h) If an online entity operator fails to remove a pornographic image within 24 hours of
43 receiving notice that the verification materials are in violation of this Article, damages shall be
44 calculated with respect to each day on or after the date on which that 24-hour period expires.
45 (i) If an online entity operator violates this Article with respect to a pornographic image,
46 any eligible person may bring a civil action against the online entity operator for damages in an
47 amount equal to the greater of (i) ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for each day during which the
48 pornographic image remains on the online entity in violation of this Article or (ii) actual damages.
49 (j) If a user of an online entity violates this Article with respect to a pornographic image,
50 any eligible person may bring a civil action against the user for damages in an amount equal to
51 the greater of (i) ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for each day during which the pornographic

1 image remains on the online entity in violation of this Article, calculated on a per day and per
2 image basis or (ii) actual damages.
3 "§ 66-510. Severability.
4 If any provision of this Article is held invalid or unenforceable, the invalidity or
5 unenforceability shall not affect other provisions or applications of this Article that can be given
6 effect without the invalid or unenforceable provision or application and, to this end, the
7 provisions of this Article are severable."
8 SECTION 2. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to acts or
9 omissions occurring before, on, or after that date; except that, prosecutions under G.S. 66-510,
10 as enacted by this act, may be brought only for acts or omissions occurring on or after that date.
