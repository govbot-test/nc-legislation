Section 1:
H.B. 939
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
Apr 10, 2025
SESSION 2025
HOUSE PRINCIPAL CLERK
H D
Short Title: School Chaplains. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Eddins.
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ALLOW PUBLIC SCHOOLS TO HIRE CHAPLAINS.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1. Article 7B of Chapter 115C of the General Statutes is amended by
5 adding a new Part to read:
6 "Part 7. School Personnel.
7 "§ 115C-77.80. School chaplains.
8 (a) The governing body of a public school unit may employ, contract for, or accept as a
9 volunteer a qualified chaplain to serve as a school chaplain. To have a school chaplain serve in a
10 public school unit, the governing body of the public school unit shall adopt a school chaplain
11 policy that includes at least the following:
12 (1) Requires chaplains to have certified training through the National School
13 Chaplain Association, Federation of Fire Chaplains, or Association of Law
14 Enforcement Chaplains.
15 (2) A detailed description of the support, services, and programs the school
16 chaplain will provide.
17 (3) Continuing education requirements for school chaplains.
18 (4) Provisions for the discipline and termination of a school chaplain, if necessary.
19 (b) Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-332(b), 115C-218.90(b), 115C-238.73(b),
20 116-239.12(b), and any other provision of law to the contrary, before a school chaplain begins
21 serving in a public school unit, the governing body of the public school unit shall conduct a
22 criminal history check of the chaplain in accordance with G.S. 115C-332. A copy of the criminal
23 history check shall be sent to the Superintendent of Public Instruction for review. If the
24 Superintendent finds that the chaplain has been convicted of any crime defined in
25 G.S. 115C-332(a)(1), the Superintendent shall direct the public school unit to revoke the offer of
26 employment to the chaplain or reject the chaplain as a volunteer.
27 (c) School chaplains shall not be liable in civil damages to any party for any loss or
28 damage caused by any act or omission relating to the performance of school chaplain duties,
29 unless that act or omission amounts to gross negligence, wanton conduct, or intentional
30 wrongdoing.
31 (d) Nothing in this section requires a public school unit to hire or accept a volunteer
32 school chaplain. The employment of a school chaplain pursuant to this section shall not be
33 construed as an endorsement of any particular religion."
34 SECTION 2. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning with
35 the 2025-2026 school year.
