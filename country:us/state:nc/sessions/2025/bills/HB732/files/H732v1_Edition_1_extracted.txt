1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REQUIRE A PERMIT FOR THE PURCHASE OF AN ASSAULT WEAPON OR
3 LONG GUN; TO REQUIRE A SEVENTY-TWO-HOUR WAITING PERIOD BEFORE A
4 PURCHASED FIREARM MAY BE DELIVERED OR OTHERWISE POSSESSED; TO
5 PROHIBIT THE SALE OF AN ASSAULT WEAPON OR LONG GUN TO PERSONS
6 UNDER A CERTAIN AGE; TO PROHIBIT THE SALE OR POSSESSION OF A BUMP
7 STOCK OR TRIGGER CRANK; TO REQUIRE THE SAFE STORAGE OF A FIREARM;
8 TO REVISE RECIPROCITY LAW FOR A CONCEALED HANDGUN PERMIT; TO
9 REQUIRE THE REPORTING OF A LOST OR STOLEN FIREARM; TO REQUIRE ANY
10 PERSON WHO OWNS A FIREARM TO CARRY FIREARM LIABILITY INSURANCE;
11 TO LIMIT THE SIZE OF AMMUNITION MAGAZINES; TO REPEAL THE
12 PREEMPTION OF LOCAL REGULATION OF FIREARMS; TO ALLOW THE
13 DESTRUCTION OF A SEIZED FIREARM; TO REQUIRE THE DEPARTMENT OF
14 PUBLIC SAFETY TO DEVELOP A ROSTER OF HANDGUNS THAT MEET CERTAIN
15 DESIGN AND SAFETY STANDARDS AND PROHIBIT THE SALE, TRANSFER,
16 OWNERSHIP, OR POSSESSION OF HANDGUNS THAT ARE NOT INCLUDED ON
17 THE ROSTER; TO REPEAL THE "STAND YOUR GROUND LAWS" AND CODIFY
18 COMMON LAW REGARDING USE OF FORCE AGAINST AN INTRUDER; TO
19 PROHIBIT LEAVING A FIREARM IN AN UNATTENDED MOTOR VEHICLE UNLESS
20 FIREARM IS SAFELY STORED; TO PROHIBIT THE SALE OR POSSESSION OF
21 GHOST GUNS; TO PROHIBIT POSSESSION OF A SEMIAUTOMATIC FIREARM BY
22 PERSONS UNDER TWENTY-ONE YEARS OF AGE; TO REQUIRE THE SHERIFF
23 UPON DENIAL, REVOCATION, OR REFUSAL TO RENEW A CONCEALED
24 HANDGUN PERMIT OR PISTOL PERMIT TO TRANSMIT THE PROHIBITION
25 RECORD TO THE NATIONAL INSTANT CRIMINAL BACKGROUND CHECK
26 SYSTEM; TO DIRECT THE STATE TREASURER TO DIVEST THE PENSION FUND
27 OF GUN STOCKS; TO AUTHORIZE THE ISSUANCE OF AN EXTREME RISK
28 PROTECTION ORDER TO RESTRICT TEMPORARILY A PERSON'S ACCESS TO
29 FIREARMS IF THERE IS EVIDENCE THAT THE PERSON POSES A DANGER OF
30 PHYSICAL HARM TO SELF OR OTHERS; AND TO REQUIRE A COURT TO ORDER
31 THE SEIZURE OF ANY FIREARM, AMMUNITION, OR PERMITS A DEFENDANT
32 FAILS TO SURRENDER AFTER THE ISSUANCE OF AN EMERGENCY OR EX
33 PARTE DOMESTIC VIOLENCE PROTECTIVE ORDER.

3 SECTION 4.(a) Article 52A of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by

5 "§ 14-409A. Bump stocks and trigger cranks prohibited.
6 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
7 (1) Bump stock. – Any device or instrument for a firearm that increases the rate
8 of fire achievable with the firearm by using energy from the recoil of the
9 firearm to generate a reciprocating action that facilitates repeated activation
10 of the trigger.
11 (2) Firearm. – As defined in G.S. 14-409.39.
12 (3) Trigger crank. – Any device or instrument to be attached to a firearm that
13 repeatedly activates the trigger of the firearm through the use of a lever or
14 other part that is turned in a circular motion. The term does not include any
15 weapon initially designed and manufactured to fire through the use of a crank
16 or lever.
17 (b) Prohibition. – It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to manufacture,
18 sell, give away, transfer, use, or possess bump stocks, trigger cranks, or any other similar device
19 or instrument added to a firearm by a person other than the manufacturer that is designed to
20 increase the rate of fire achievable by the firearm.
21 (c) Punishment. – Any person violating this section is guilty of a Class I felony."
22 SECTION 4.(b) This section becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to
23 the sale, giving away, transfer, use, or possession of bump stocks, trigger cranks, or other similar
24 devices and instruments added to a firearm by a person other than the manufacturer that is
25 designed to increase the rate of fire achievable by the firearm on or after that date.

6 "§ 50E-10. Duration; renewal of ERPOs.
7 (a) Duration of Ex Parte ERPO. – Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, an ex

9 not held or a continuance was not granted, an ex parte ERPO shall be effective for not more than

22 petitioner seeks no modification of the ERPO, the ERPO may be renewed if the petitioner's
23 motion or affidavit states that there has been no material change in relevant circumstances since
24 entry of the ERPO and states the reason for the requested renewal.

27 "§ 50E-11. Termination of an Extreme Risk Protection Order.
28 (a) Motion. – The respondent may request the termination of a final ERPO by filing a
29 motion with the court. The respondent may submit no more than one motion for termination for
30 every 12-month period the final ERPO is in effect, starting from the date of the final ERPO and
31 continuing through any renewals.
32 (b) Hearing. – Upon receipt of a request for a hearing to terminate a final ERPO, the court
33 shall set a date for a hearing. Notice of the request for a hearing shall be served on the petitioner
34 in accordance with Rule 5 of the Rules of Civil Procedure. The court shall set the date for the
35 hearing no sooner than 10 days and no later than 30 days from the date of service of the request
36 upon the petitioner.
37 (c) Burden of Proof; Termination. – The respondent shall have the burden of proving, by
38 a preponderance of the evidence, that the respondent does not pose a danger of causing physical
39 harm to self or others by having in his or her care, custody, possession, ownership, or control a
40 firearm. If the court finds after the hearing that the respondent has met his or her burden, the
41 court shall terminate the final ERPO.
