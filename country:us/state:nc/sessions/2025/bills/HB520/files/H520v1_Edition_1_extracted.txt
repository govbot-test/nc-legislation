Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Abuse and Deception by Telemarketers. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Brody, Bell, Dahle, and Gable (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Judiciary 2, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
March 27, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ADDRESS ABUSE OF PRIVACY AND ACTS OF DECEPTION FROM
3 TELEMARKETERS BY DISPLAYING PHONE NUMBERS THAT ARE FRAUDULENT
4 AND MISREPRESENTATIVE OF THE ACTUAL CALLER.
5 Whereas, the citizens and businesses of North Carolina claim the right of privacy and
6 exclusive use of the phone lines and numbers for which they contract for; and
7 Whereas, the citizens and businesses of North Carolina claim a right of privacy and
8 enjoyment of that privacy from telemarketers who spoof, or otherwise disguise their actual phone
9 numbers, preventing the receiving phone from identifying who is calling, and
10 Whereas, the citizens and businesses of North Carolina claim some telemarketers are
11 violating these rights and fraudulently displaying numbers which are assigned to other people,
12 assigned to various businesses, using numbers that are not assigned to anyone (including the
13 caller), or made up combinations of numbers and letters to disguise the real identification of the
14 caller; Now, therefore,
15 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
16 SECTION 1. G.S. 75-100 is amended by adding a new subdivision to read:
17 "(10) A telephone number is the property of a telephone subscriber, subject to the
18 terms and conditions of the subscriber's contract with a telephone carrier."
19 SECTION 2. G.S. 75-102(i) reads as rewritten:
20 "(i) No telephone solicitor or its agent shall cause misleading information to be
21 transmitted to users of caller identification technologies or otherwise block or misrepresent the
22 origin of the telephone solicitation. No provider of telephone caller identification services shall
23 be held liable for violations of this subsection committed by other individuals or entities. It is not
24 a violation of this subsection for a telephone solicitor to utilize the name and number of the entity
25 the solicitation is being made on behalf of rather than the name and number of the telephone
26 solicitor."
27 SECTION 3. Article 4 of Chapter 75 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
28 a new section to read:
29 "§ 75-104.1. Telephone carriers.
30 (a) A telephone carrier shall not knowingly and intentionally transmit, sell, or otherwise
31 provide the numbers of telephone subscribers to any entity the telephone carrier knows will use
32 the number to violate provisions of this Act.
33 (b) A telephone carrier shall not be held liable for a telemarketer's violation of
34 G.S. 75-102(i)."
35 SECTION 4. G.S. 75-105 reads as rewritten:

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 "§ 75-105. Enforcement.
2 …
3 (b) A telephone subscriber who has received a telephone solicitation from or on behalf
4 of a telephone solicitor in violation of this Article may bring any of the following actions in civil
5 court:
6 (1) An action to enjoin further violations of this Article by the telephone solicitor.
7 (2) An action to recover five hundred dollars ($500.00) for the first violation, one
8 thousand dollars ($1,000) for the second violation, and five thousand dollars
9 ($5,000) for the third and any other violation that occurs within two years of
10 the first violation.
11 (b1) A telephone subscriber who has received a telephone solicitation in violation of
12 G.S. 75-102(i), or a telephone subscriber whose phone number or name was displayed in a way
13 to give the perception that a call originated from that subscriber or number when the call was
14 actually placed from a different origin and in violation of G.S. 75-102(i), may bring any action
15 authorized pursuant this section or Chapter against the telemarketer initiating the call. In an action
16 brought under this subsection, the court shall impose an additional civil fine of ten thousand
17 dollars ($10,000) for each call placed in knowing violation of G.S. 75-102(i). Any person who
18 knowingly violates G.S. 75-105(i) shall be guilty of a Class H felony.
19 …."
20 SECTION 5. This act becomes effective December 1, 2025, and applies to phone
21 calls placed on or after that date.
