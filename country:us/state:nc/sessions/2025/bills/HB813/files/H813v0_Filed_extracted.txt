5 "§ 131D-2.2A. Requirements for registered residential facilities; compliance with
6 disclosure statement; Department's right to inspect.
7 (a) Registration. – Registered residential facilities shall apply annually for registration
8 with the Division of Health Service Regulation (Division) on forms and in the manner prescribed
9 by the Division. The Division shall approve registration for any registered residential facility that
10 meets all of the requirements of this section and pays the registration fee required by
11 G.S. 131D-2.5(b).
12 (b) Disclosure Statement. – In order to be approved for registration by the Division, a
13 registered residential facility shall provide a disclosure statement with its completed registration
14 application that includes a description of all of the following items, services, and requirements:
15 (1) The facility's emergency response system and an explanation of how the
16 resident should call for emergency assistance.
17 (2) Charges for rentals or services offered by the facility, which shall not be
18 changed without at least 30 days' written notice to residents.
19 (3) Any limitations of tenancy.
20 (4) Any limitations of services, including any services not provided by the
21 registered residential facility and procedures for initiating termination of a
22 rental contract due to changes in a resident's condition or due to other reasons.
23 (5) Resident responsibilities.
24 (6) Any financial or legal relationship between the housing management and any
25 licensed home care or hospice agencies referred by the housing management
26 to residents.
27 (7) A listing of all licensed home care and hospice agencies and other community
28 services located in the same area as the facility and their contact information.
29 (8) An appeals process for any dispute between the facility and a resident related
30 to rental charges, charges for services, or termination of a rental contract.
31 (9) Procedures for initial and annual resident screening as required by subsection
32 (d) of this section and procedures for the facility to make referrals of services
33 to residents.
34 (10) Contact information for the Division of Health Service Regulation Complaint
35 Intake Unit complaint hotline and the appropriate county department of social
36 services.
37 (11) Contact information for the owner or manager of the registered residential
38 facility as well as information on the number and type of staff available at the
39 facility and a schedule of on-site working hours at the facility.
40 The Division shall approve or disapprove the disclosure statement as a part of the registration
41 process. Upon approval of its disclosure statement and registration application, a registered
42 residential facility has a continuing duty to comply with the disclosure statement and is required
43 to notify the Division within 14 days after making any change to a disclosure statement approved
44 by the Division. The registered residential facility shall include the approved disclosure statement
45 as part of the resident's annual rental contract and shall ensure that the resident or the resident's
46 legal representative signs the approved disclosure statement on an annual basis.
47 (c) Agreement for Housing and Services. – The housing management of a registered
48 residential facility is required to have a financial interest, or financial affiliation, or formal written
49 agreement that makes personal care services accessible and available to residents through at least
50 one licensed home care or hospice agency. The housing management shall give each resident a
51 choice of any provider and keep charges for housing and personal care services separate. The

1 housing management shall ensure that all residents, or their compensatory agents, are capable,
2 through informed consent, of entering into a contract for housing and services.
3 (d) Resident Screening. – Each registered residential facility shall conduct an initial
4 screening of each resident prior to admission and then a follow-up screening at least once
5 annually. The purpose of the initial and annual screenings required by this subsection is to
6 identify a resident's need for assistance with activities of daily living, independent activities of
7 daily living, and any ancillary services needed to maintain the resident's safety, well-being, and
8 functional status. A registered residential facility shall make service referrals to each resident
9 based on that resident's screenings. A registered residential facility may assist a resident with
10 self-administration of medications if the assistance is provided by appropriately trained staff and
11 a licensed nurse has approved the delegation of medication assistance according to the home care
12 agency's established plan of care for the resident.
13 (e) Handling of Resident Funds Prohibited. – A registered residential facility and its
14 owners, agents, employees, and other persons affiliated with the registered residential facility are
15 prohibited from serving as a resident's legal representative or payee, or handling a resident's
16 funds.
17 (f) Employee Background Checks. – A registered residential facility is prohibited from
18 hiring or using as a volunteer any individual listed on the North Carolina Health Care Personnel
19 Registry established under G.S. 131E-256. A registered residential facility shall not hire any
20 employee prior to conducting a criminal history background check of the employee and shall not
21 refer any licensed home care or hospice agency to a resident prior to verifying that the licensed
22 home care or hospice agency conducts criminal history background checks of its employees.
23 (g) Adverse Action by the Division of Health Service Regulation. – The Division of
24 Health Service Regulation may deny, suspend, or revoke the registration of a registered
25 residential facility for any of the following reasons:
26 (1) Upon a determination by the Division that a facility is providing services that
27 require a license rather than registration.
28 (2) Upon a determination by the Division that a facility is not in compliance with
29 its approved disclosure statement.
30 (3) Upon a determination by the Division that a county department of social
31 services has substantiated a finding of abuse, neglect, or exploitation by a
32 facility. Any owner of a registered residential facility whose registration is
33 revoked by the Division under this subdivision is prohibited from registering
34 another registered residential facility or applying for a license to operate
35 another health care facility under this Chapter, Chapter 122C, Chapter 131E,
36 or Article 7 of Chapter 110 of the General Statutes for a period of 60 months
37 following the date of the revocation.
38 Any facility wishing to contest the Division's actions under this subsection is entitled to an
39 administrative hearing as provided in the Administrative Procedure Act, Chapter 150B of the
40 General Statutes.
41 (h) Adverse Action by the Secretary. – The Secretary may deny, suspend, amend, or
42 revoke the registration of a registered residential facility, or suspend the admission of any new
43 residents to a registered residential facility, in any case in which the Secretary finds that there
44 has been a substantial failure to comply with this Part, any applicable rules adopted under this
45 Part, or any other applicable State laws or rules. Any facility wishing to contest the Secretary's
46 actions under this subsection is entitled to an administrative hearing as provided in the
47 Administrative Procedure Act, Chapter 150B of the General Statutes.
48 (i) Duty to Report Suspected Abuse, Neglect, and Exploitation of Residents. – A
49 registered residential facility that knows or has cause to suspect that a resident is being abused,
50 neglected, or exploited shall report this information to local law enforcement, the North Carolina
