1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ENHANCE PUBLIC SAFETY BY INCENTIVIZING COMMUNITY
3 PARTICIPATION IN NEIGHBORHOOD WATCH PROGRAMS AND PROVIDING A
4 STATE-MANDATED BONUS FOR POLICE OFFICERS ENGAGED IN COMMUNITY
5 OUTREACH ACTIVITIES.
6 Whereas, public safety is a fundamental priority for the State of North Carolina; and
7 Whereas, increased community involvement in neighborhood watch programs has
8 been shown to reduce crime and improve trust between law enforcement and the communities
9 they serve; and
10 Whereas, supporting law enforcement officers in their engagement with local
11 communities will promote safer, stronger, and more connected neighborhoods; and
12 Whereas, Article I, Section 3, and Article VII, Section 1 of the North Carolina
13 Constitution authorize the General Assembly to regulate public safety measures; Now, therefore,
14 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

1 "§ 17F-11.1. Definitions.
2 As used in this Article, unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply:
3 (1) Approved community safety meeting. – A neighborhood watch program
4 meeting or training session approved by the Secretary of the Department of
5 Public Safety as being legitimately constituted and operated for the purpose
6 of improving the safety of communities in the State.
7 (2) Certified Community Safety Attendance System. – A digital system
8 implemented by the State to verify and track participation in neighborhood
9 watch programs and police engagement initiatives.
10 (3) Commission. – The Community Safety Partnership Commission.
11 (4) Department. – The Department of Public Safety.
12 (5) Eligible participants. – Residents who actively engage in certified
13 neighborhood watch programs and law enforcement officers participating in
14 community engagement efforts.
15 (6) Fund. – The Community Safety Partnership Fund.
16 (7) Grant Program. – The Community Safety Partnership Competitive Grant
17 Program.
18 (8) Law enforcement officer. – A full-time sworn law enforcement officer
19 certified under Chapter 17C or Chapter 17E of the General Statutes and
20 employed in that capacity by the State or any county or municipal government
21 of the State.
22 (9) Neighborhood watch programs. – Organized community efforts, recognized
23 by local law enforcement, that aim to reduce crime and improve neighborhood
24 safety.
25 "§ 17F-11.2. The Community Safety Partnership Fund.
26 (a) Fund Created. – The Community Safety Partnership Fund is established as a special
27 fund within the Department of Public Safety. The Department is responsible for administering
28 the Fund using personnel and other administrative resources of the Department.
29 (b) Fund Purpose. – The Fund shall be utilized to distribute grant funds pursuant to the
30 Community Safety Partnership Grant Program created by this Article.
31 (c) Fund Sources. – The Fund may receive any of the following:
32 (1) Funds appropriated by the General Assembly.
33 (2) Funds received pursuant to State law.
34 (3) Gifts, grants, or donations from any public or private source.
35 "§ 17F-11.3. The Community Safety Partnership Competitive Grant Program.
36 (a) Grant Program. – There is established the Community Safety Partnership Competitive
37 Grant Program to be administered by the Department of Public Safety for the purpose of
38 awarding and distributing grants to county and municipal governments, including internal
39 departments, divisions, or offices of those governments, and nonprofit organizations to support
40 community-led safety projects, which may include any of the following:
41 (1) Improved street lighting.
42 (2) Increased security cameras or maintenance of security cameras.
43 (3) Community alert systems.
44 (4) Other projects with the goal of supporting community-led safety efforts.
45 (b) Funding. – The Grant Program shall be funded by the Community Safety Partnership
46 Fund created by this Article.
47 (c) Grant Program Guidelines. – The Department shall develop guidelines and
48 procedures for the administration of the Grant Program, including, at a minimum, the following
49 requirements and limitations:
50 (1) The Department shall prioritize awarding grants to applicants serving
51 high-crime and underserved areas of the State.

1 (2) The maximum grant amount shall not exceed one hundred thousand dollars
2 ($100,000).
3 (3) The maximum total award amount to a grantee during a single fiscal year shall

8 how the applicant plans to use the grant funds, including, at a minimum, each

24 c. The number of persons served by use of the grant funds.

29 the Grant Program, including, at a minimum, each of the following:
30 (1) The number, amount, and recipient of all grant funds awarded in the prior
31 calendar year.
32 (2) All information received pursuant to the grantee reports required by
33 subsection (c) of this section.
34 (3) The current, unencumbered funds available within the Community Safety
35 Partnership Fund.
36 (e) Disbursements. – If grant funds awarded under this section cannot be paid due to
37 insufficient funds in the Fund, payment shall be delayed until sufficient funds are available and
38 no further grant awards shall be made until sufficient funds are available.
39 "§ 17F-11.4. Approved Community Safety Meeting Attendance System.
40 (a) Development. – The Department of Revenue shall develop a statewide digital
41 tracking system to record attendance at approved community safety meetings.
42 (b) Participation. – Any chair or other entity in charge of an approved community safety
43 meeting in which any participant requests the recordation of that participant's attendance at the
44 meeting shall record or cause to be recorded all of the following:
45 (1) First and last name of the participant.
46 (2) Date of the meeting.
47 (3) Neighborhood watch organization for which the meeting was held.
48 (4) Location of the meeting.
49 (5) Signature of the participant.
50 (6) Name and signature of the chair or a representative of the entity in charge of
51 the meeting.

10 "§ 17F-11.5. Community Safety Partnership Citizen Incentive Program.
11 The Department of Public Safety shall notify the public, as well as chairs or representatives
12 of entities in charge of approved community safety meetings, of the opportunity for qualifying
13 attendees of those meetings to receive a tax credit pursuant to G.S. 105-153.12.
14 "§ 17F-11.6. Community Safety Partnership Officer Incentive Program.
15 (a) Incentive Program. – A law enforcement officer that attends at least 10 approved
16 community safety meetings in the same calendar year shall receive from the officer's employer a
17 one-time bonus of one thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500) within 60 days of that officer
18 attending the tenth meeting. A law enforcement officer that attends at least 20 approved
19 community safety meetings in the same calendar year shall receive from the officer's employer
20 an additional one-time bonus under this section of five hundred dollars ($500.00) within 60 days
21 of that officer attending the twentieth meeting.
22 (b) Attendance System. – For purposes of implementing this section, State, county, and
23 municipal government employers of law enforcement officers shall develop a community safety
24 attendance system for tracking law enforcement officer attendance at approved community safety
25 meetings. Upon request, the law enforcement officer shall be furnished with a copy of the
26 officer's attendance record for the requested calendar year.
27 (c) Bonus payments made pursuant to this section shall supplement the salary of a law
28 enforcement officer.
29 "§ 17F-11.7. Community Safety Partnership Commission.
30 (a) There is established the Community Safety Partnership Commission of the
31 Department of Public Safety to ensure transparency and proper implementation of this Article.
32 (b) The Commission shall consist of eight members as follows:
33 (1) Three members appointed by the General Assembly upon the joint
34 recommendation of the President Pro Tempore of the Senate and the Speaker
35 of the House of Representatives pursuant to G.S. 120-121.
36 (2) Two members that are representatives of law enforcement agencies, one upon
37 the recommendation of the Criminal Justice Education and Training Standards
38 Commission and one upon the recommendation of the Sheriffs' Education and
39 Training Standards Commission.
40 (3) Two members that are community representatives from neighborhood watch
41 programs appointed by the Governor.
42 (4) One member appointed by the Secretary of the Department of Public Safety.
43 (c) Members shall serve terms of four years. A member shall continue to serve until the
44 member's successor is duly appointed, but a holdover under this provision does not affect the
45 expiration date of the succeeding term.
46 (d) In case of a vacancy on the Commission before the expiration of a member's term, a
47 successor shall be appointed within 30 days of the vacancy for the remainder of the unexpired
48 term by the appropriate appointing official or entity pursuant to subsection (b) of this section.
49 Vacancies in legislative appointments shall be filled under G.S. 120-122.
50 (e) The Commission shall elect one of its members as chairman to serve until the
51 expiration of the member's term.
