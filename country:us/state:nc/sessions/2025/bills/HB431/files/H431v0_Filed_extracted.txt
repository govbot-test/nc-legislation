13 PART I. IDENTIFY VERIFICATION AND SUSPICIOUS INSTRUMENTS
14 SECTION 1. Article 2 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
15 a new section to read:
16 "§ 161-32. Identity verification and suspicious instruments.
17 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this section:
18 (1) Instrument. – Any document that purports to convey title to, or an interest in,
19 real property, such as a deed, deed of trust, or other similar document.
20 (2) Suspicious instrument. – An instrument submitted for registration for which
21 the register of deeds has found any of the following:
22 a. The name or information of an acknowledging officer who has
23 purported to acknowledge the instrument does not match the official
24 records for that acknowledging officer.
25 b. The instrument purports not to be subject to the laws of the United
26 States or the laws of this State.
27 c. The document does not conform to registration requirements
28 established by the laws of this State.
29 d. The instrument is materially false, fictitious, or fraudulent and has not
30 been submitted by a trusted submitter or an authorized representative
31 of a trusted submitter.
32 (3) Trusted submitter. – Includes all of the following:
33 a. A title insurance company as described in Article 26 of Chapter 28 of
34 the General Statutes.
35 b. An attorney licensed to practice in this State.
36 c. A financial institution as defined in G.S. 53B-2.

41 submitter or an authorized representative of a trusted submitter and fails to
42 provide the register of deeds with a government-issued photographic
43 identification card as required by subsection (b) of this section.
44 (3) The individual presenting the instrument for registration is not a trusted
45 submitter or an authorized representative of a trusted submitter and the name
46 on the government-issued photographic identification card presented by the
47 individual pursuant to subsection (b) of this section does not match the name
48 of the grantor or conveying party in the instrument presented for registration.
49 (d) Additional Actions for Suspicious Instruments. – Upon finding that an instrument
50 presented for registration is a suspicious instrument under subdivision (a)(2) of this section, the
51 register of deeds may do any of the following:

25 PART II. FRAUD DETECTION ALERT SYSTEM
26 SECTION 2. Article 2 of Chapter 161 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
27 a new section to read:
28 "§ 161-33. Fraud detection alert system.
29 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
30 (1) Fraud detection alert system. – A system which sends automated recording
31 notifications.
32 (2) Land record. – A deed, deed of trust, mortgage, or other document purporting
33 to convey or encumber an interest in real property.
34 (3) Monitored identity. – A personal, trust, or business name submitted by a
35 registrant for monitoring under a fraud detection alert system.
36 (4) Recording notification. – A notification sent by electronic mail indicating to
37 a registrant that a land record associated with the registrant's monitored
38 identity has been recorded in the office of the register of deeds.
39 (5) Registrant. – A person who enrolls in a fraud detection alert system.
40 (b) The register of deeds shall ensure that registration for a fraud detection alert system
41 is possible through an electronic registration portal, which portal shall include at least all of the
42 following features:
43 (1) Be accessible through a direct link on the register of deeds' official public
44 website.
45 (2) Allow a registrant to subscribe to receive recording notifications for at least
46 five monitored identities per valid electronic mail address provided.
47 (3) Include a method by which a registrant may unsubscribe from the system.
48 (4) List a phone number at which the register of deeds' office may be contacted
49 during normal business hours with questions related to the system.
50 (5) Send an automated electronic mail message to a registrant confirming the
51 registrant's successful registration for or action to unsubscribe from the

22 SECTION 3.(a) The Legislative Research Commission (LRC) shall study the
23 feasibility of amending G.S. 161-33, as enacted in Section 2 of this act, to create a fraud detection
24 alert system where everyone who submits a land record for registration is automatically enrolled
25 in the fraud detection alert system without paying a fee. Before enrollment, the register of deeds
26 would provide each individual with a notice explaining the system – its purpose, how it works,
27 and how to opt out of enrollment.
28 As part of its study, the LRC shall do all of the following:
29 (1) Evaluate the practicality of implementing a fraud detection alert system with
30 automatic enrollment and an opt-out option, including technological and
31 staffing requirements for registers of deeds across all counties.
32 (2) Examine the fiscal impact on the State, counties, and/or registers of deeds,
33 including costs associated with developing, maintaining, and operating a fraud
34 detection alert system without charging a fee to the participant.
35 (3) Evaluate how well such a fraud detection alert system would prevent fraud as
36 compared to other potential systems or measures.
37 SECTION 3.(b) The LRC shall report its findings, together with any proposed
38 legislation, to the 2026 Regular Session of the 2025 General Assembly upon its convening.

40 PART III. PROVIDING FOR AN EXPEDITED CAUSE OF ACTION TO QUIET TITLE
41 AFFECTED BY FRAUDULENT INSTRUMENTS
42 SECTION 4. Article 1 of Chapter 41 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
43 a new section to read:
44 "§ 41-10.2. Titles quieted; fraudulent instruments.
45 (a) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
46 (1) Instrument. – Any of the following:
47 a. A transfer of title to real property by deed or devise or other instrument
48 transferring title to real property.
49 b. A deed of trust, mortgage, judgment, lien, encumbrance, financing
50 statement, affidavit, notice, memorandum, or any other instrument that
51 establishes a security interest in real property.

1 c. A contract, right of refusal, or any other instrument that purports to
2 establish an interest, encumbrance, claim, or right relating to real
3 property.
4 (b) The actual owner of an interest in real property subject to a recorded false, fictitious,
5 or fraudulent instrument may seek expedited relief under this section by filing an action in district
6 court alleging the filing or recording of a false, fictitious, or fraudulent instrument in a public
7 record or a private record generally available to the public. The notice shall be served and proof
8 of service shall be made in any manner provided by the Rules of Civil Procedure for service of
9 summons, including service by registered mail or certified mail, return receipt requested. After a
10 minimum of five days' notice in a manner prescribed in Rule 5 of the Rules of Civil Procedure
11 and opportunity to be heard to all persons claiming an ownership interest in the property, any
12 lienholder of record, including any person to whom a security instrument directs notice to be sent
13 and any person obligated to repay the indebtedness secured by the instrument, the court shall
14 convene a hearing. For purposes of the notice requirement in this subsection, the party seeking
15 relief may serve the person listed as grantor in the recorded purportedly false, fictitious, or
16 fraudulent instrument at the address listed in the instrument. If the court finds that there is no
17 statutory, contractual, or other merited legal justification for the instrument, the court shall find
18 that the recorded instrument is false and is void as a matter of law and shall enter an order to
19 remove the cloud from the title to the real property. The submission of an original instrument
20 demonstrating source of title, affidavit, or a lis pendens, notice of previous fraud, or other similar
21 instrument recorded by the actual owner of an interest in the real property shall be considered
22 evidence of fraudulent activity. The court may also include in the order any of the following
23 types of relief:
24 (1) Grant to a prevailing party possession of the real property and order the
25 ejectment of any party on the premises.
26 (2) Award attorneys' fees and other costs for the action to the prevailing party.
27 (3) Stay any proceedings involving the subject real property in which the property
28 owner or owners are not a party to.
29 (4) Upon a finding by the court that an action filed under this section is frivolous
30 or malicious, assess a civil penalty of not more than ten thousand dollars
31 ($10,000), of which the clear proceeds assessed under this subdivision shall
32 be remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture Fund in accordance with
33 G.S. 115C-457.2.
34 (5) Upon a finding by the court that a party has presented for filing or recording
35 a false, fictitious, or fraudulent instrument as described in this section more
36 than once, enter a pre-filing injunction against the party.
37 (6) Enjoin a party from listing the real property for sale, lease, or any other similar
38 advertisement.
39 (c) Upon being presented with an order duly issued by a court of competent jurisdiction
40 of this State declaring that an instrument already recorded is false and void as a matter of law,
41 the register of deeds or clerk of superior court that received the recording shall record and
42 cross-index the court's order finding the instrument to be false. The register of deeds or clerk of
43 superior court may also conspicuously mark on the first page of the original record previously
44 recorded the following statement: "THE CLAIM ASSERTED IN THIS DOCUMENT IS FALSE
45 AND IS NOT PROVIDED FOR BY THE GENERAL LAWS OF THIS STATE."
46 (d) In addition to any civil penalties provided for in this section, the presentation of an
47 instrument for recording with a register of deeds or a clerk of superior court that is determined to
48 be materially false, fictitious, or fraudulent shall constitute a violation of G.S. 75-1.1.
49 (e) The Administrative Office of the Courts shall develop a form for the expedited relief
50 filing established by this section, and the clerk of superior court shall make the form available
51 for a petitioner. In addition to the requirements set forth in this section, the form shall include
