1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REMOVE THE MATH III GRADUATION REQUIREMENT, TO MODIFY THE
3 HIGH SCHOOL MATHEMATICS GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS, TO REQUIRE
4 ADDITIONAL HIGH SCHOOL MATHEMATICS INSTRUCTION FOR CERTAIN
5 STUDENTS, TO REQUIRE A HIGH SCHOOL UNITED STATES HISTORY TEST, TO
6 MODIFY THE FOUNDING PRINCIPLES HIGH SCHOOL COURSE REQUIREMENTS,
7 AND TO REQUIRE THE UNIVERSITY OF NORTH CAROLINA'S CONSTITUENT
8 INSTITUTIONS TO ALIGN MINIMUM ADMISSIONS REQUIREMENTS WITH THE
9 MATHEMATICS REQUIREMENTS FOR HIGH SCHOOL GRADUATION.
10 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
11 SECTION 1.(a) Beginning with the 2025-2026 school year, the State Board of
12 Education shall not require any student to complete NC Math III as one of the four required
13 mathematics courses for high school graduation.
14 SECTION 1.(b) Public school units shall encourage all students to complete
15 additional mathematics courses beyond the required courses. Public school units shall encourage
16 students who are interested in pursuing advanced degrees to work with school counselors to
17 determine a sequence of mathematics courses that would make them most competitive for
18 admission to an institution of higher education. NC Math III and all other math courses existing
19 on the effective date of this act shall continue to be made available to all high school students.
20 SECTION 1.(c) The State Board of Education may adopt emergency rules in
21 accordance with G.S. 150B-21.1A to implement this section for the 2025-2026 school year.

33 SECTION 2.(c) This section applies beginning with students entering the ninth grade
34 in the 2026-2027 school year.
35 SECTION 3.(a) In response to Computer Science becoming mathematics courses,
36 the State Board of Education shall develop a plan to remove licensing barriers for individuals
37 who were licensed or eligible to be licensed to teach Computer Science prior to it becoming a
38 mathematics course.
39 SECTION 3.(b) Notwithstanding any law or rule to the contrary, a public school
40 unit may allow any licensed teacher, regardless of licensure area, to teach Computer Science if
41 the public school unit determines the teacher possesses the necessary content knowledge to
42 effectively teach the course.
43 SECTION 3.(c) This section expires June 30, 2028.
