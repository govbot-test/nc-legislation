13 "§ 130A-309.250. Definitions.
14 Unless a different meaning is required by the context, the following definitions apply
15 throughout this Part:
16 (1) Collector. – Any publicly or privately owned solid waste management
17 services or recycler that gathers discarded packaging materials from
18 residential, commercial, industrial, or governmental establishments.
19 (2) Fulfillment service provider. – A person that handles, on behalf of a
20 responsible producer, products in which the producer retains a property
21 interest and offers at least two of the following services as part of a
22 commercial activity: (i) warehousing, (ii) packaging, and (iii) addressing and
23 shipping. For purposes of this Part, "fulfillment service provider" does not
24 include postal or freight service providers.
25 (3) Packaging material. – Any part of a package or container, regardless of
26 recyclability or compostability, including material types such as paper, plastic,
27 glass, or metal, that is used for the containment, protection, handling, delivery,
28 transport, distribution, and presentation of a product that is sold, offered for
29 sale, or distributed in North Carolina. Bags are included within this definition.
30 Packaging material does not include any of the following:
31 a. Material, or a category of material, intended to be used for long-term
32 storage or protection of a durable product that can be expected to be
33 usable for that purpose for a period of at least five years.

48 (a) Registration Required. – Each responsible producer shall register with the Department
49 before selling, offering for sale, or distributing in North Carolina a product contained, protected,
50 delivered, presented, or distributed in or using packaging materials.

1 (b) Stewardship Plan Required. – No responsible producer shall sell, offer for sale, or
2 distribute in North Carolina a product contained, protected, delivered, presented, or distributed
3 in or using packaging materials unless the producer participates as a member of a producer
4 responsibility organization for which a stewardship plan is approved by the Department, and
5 through that participation, satisfies the requirements of subsections (c), (d), and (e) of this section.
6 (c) Non-Reusable Packaging Material Reduction Requirements. – According to the
7 following schedule, each responsible producer shall reduce the total amount of non-reusable
8 packaging materials used for containing, protecting, delivering, presenting, and distributing
9 products sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State (i) by unit, (ii) on average, and (iii) in
10 the aggregate:
11 (1) Ten percent (10%) after two years following approval of the stewardship plan.
12 (2) Twenty percent (20%) after four years following approval of the stewardship
13 plan.
14 (3) Thirty percent (30%) after six years following approval of the stewardship
15 plan.
16 (4) Forty percent (40%) after eight years following approval of the stewardship
17 plan.
18 The reductions required by this subsection shall be measured against the total amount of
19 packaging material the responsible producer used for containing, protecting, delivering,
20 presenting, and distributing the products it sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State during
21 the respective calendar year.
22 (d) Recycling-Rate Requirements. – Each responsible producer shall ensure that all
23 non-reusable packaging material, in the aggregate, used for containing, protecting, delivering,
24 presenting, and distributing the products it sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State is
25 recycled at a rate consistent with the following schedule:
26 (1) Fifty percent (50%) after five years following approval of the stewardship
27 plan.
28 (2) Eighty percent (80%) after eight years following approval of the stewardship
29 plan.
30 (3) Ninety percent (90%) after 12 years following approval of the stewardship
31 plan.
32 (e) Postconsumer Waste Material Content Requirements. – Each responsible producer
33 shall ensure that all non-reusable packaging material used for containing, protecting, delivering,
34 presenting, and distributing the products it sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State
35 incorporates, on average and in the aggregate, the following amount, by weight, of postconsumer
36 waste material in accordance with the following schedule:
37 (1) Fifty percent (50%) after five years following approval of the stewardship
38 plan.
39 (2) Eighty percent (80%) after eight years following approval of the stewardship
40 plan.
41 (3) Ninety percent (90%) after 12 years following approval of the stewardship
42 plan.
43 "§ 130A-309.252. Producer responsibility organizations; participation fees.
44 (a) Membership Requirement. – Each responsible producer shall establish or join a
45 producer responsibility organization to act as an agent on behalf of the producer for the purposes
46 of satisfying its obligations under this Part. A producer may participate in more than one producer
47 responsibility organization if each organization is established for a different category of
48 packaging materials. Any producer responsibility organization formed for the purpose of
49 complying with this Part shall be established and operated as an organization described in section
50 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

1 (b) Participation Fees. – A producer responsibility organization shall establish
2 participation fees for its member producers that are sufficient to cover all costs of developing and
3 implementing the stewardship plan, including each producer's proportionate share of
4 administrative, enforcement, and outreach and education costs. The participation fee must reflect
5 the per ton costs associated with the collection, processing, transportation, and recycling and
6 disposal of a producer's packaging material.
7 (c) Fee Structure. – A producer responsibility organization shall structure the
8 participation fees to provide producers with financial incentives to reward waste reduction and
9 recycling compatibility innovations and practices and to discourage designs or practices that
10 increase costs of managing the products. The producer responsibility organization may adjust
11 fees to be paid by each participating producer based on factors that affect system costs. At a
12 minimum, fees shall be variable based on the following:
13 (1) Costs to provide curbside collection of packaging material, or other levels of
14 collection service, that is, at a minimum, as convenient as the recycling
15 collection service offered in the particular jurisdiction prior to the
16 implementation of the stewardship plan.
17 (2) Costs to process a producer's packaging material for acceptance by secondary
18 material markets.
19 (3) Whether the packaging material includes labels, inks, and adhesives
20 containing heavy metals or other hazardous wastes that would contaminate
21 the recycling process.
22 (4) Whether the packaging material is specifically designed to be reusable or
23 refillable and has a high reuse or refill rate.
24 (5) The percentage of postconsumer waste material in a producer's packaging
25 material.
26 (6) Contributions to greenhouse gas emissions from the production, use,
27 collection, processing, and marketing of the packaging material.
28 The producer responsibility organization shall annually evaluate, revise, and submit an
29 updated fee assessment schedule to the Department.
30 (d) Producer Responsibility Fund. – A producer responsibility organization shall
31 establish and manage a producer responsibility fund. The organization shall deposit into the fund
32 all payments received from responsible producers in accordance with this section and shall
33 expend those funds for the following uses:
34 (1) To reimburse participating collectors in accordance with G.S. 130A-309.240.
35 (2) To fund collection and convenience obligations, required pursuant to
36 G.S. 130A-309.241.
37 (3) To fund the outreach and education programming, required pursuant to
38 G.S. 130A-309.243.
39 (4) To fund the actual operating costs of the organization, which may not exceed
40 the estimated operating costs indicated in the stewardship plan approved by
41 the Department pursuant to G.S. 130A-309.239.
42 (5) To pay fees assessed by the Department for the purposes of implementing this
43 Part.
44 (e) Payment of Participation Fees. – No later than 30 days after the approval of the
45 stewardship plan under G.S. 130A-309.239, and quarterly thereafter, a responsible producer shall
46 pay the producer responsibility organization any outstanding participation fees, to be deposited
47 into the producer responsibility fund.
48 (f) Producer Information Requirement. – A responsible producer participating in a
49 producer responsibility organization shall provide the organization with all information
50 necessary (i) for determining the producer's compliance with the stewardship plan and (ii) for
51 calculating the participation fees to be assessed to the producer.

3 "§ 130A-309.255. Collector reimbursement.
4 (a) Collector Reimbursement. – A producer responsibility organization shall be
5 responsible for calculating and dispersing funding for collectors that elect to be compensated by
6 the producer responsibility organization for the recovery, recycling, and processing of packaging
7 materials.
8 (b) Eligibility. – Collectors must provide for recycling of all recyclable packaging
9 material listed under a stewardship plan in order to be eligible for reimbursement under this
10 section.
11 (c) Calculation of Reimbursement. – The reimbursement rate shall be based on the cost
12 of residential curbside collection, including the cost of curbside containers where relevant, as
13 well as the processing cost for each readily recyclable material, the cost of handling non-readily
14 recyclable material types collected as part of a recycling operation, the transportation cost of
15 recycling for each material type, and any other cost factors as determined by the Department.
16 (d) Collector Responsibilities. –
17 (1) To facilitate the producer responsibility organization's determination of the
18 cost of recycling, participating collectors shall annually report to the
19 organization all information necessary for the organization to determine the
20 collector's incurred net costs associated with the collection, processing,
21 transportation, and recycling and disposal of packaging material covered
22 under the stewardship plan.
23 (2) Participating collectors shall not charge fees to customers for reimbursable
24 costs.
25 "§ 130A-309.256. Collection and convenience.
26 (a) A producer responsibility organization shall provide for widespread, convenient, and
27 equitable access to collection opportunities for the packaging material identified under the
28 stewardship plan.
29 (b) A producer responsibility organization may rely on a range of means to collect various
30 categories of packaging material, including curbside collection, depot drop-off, and retailer
31 take-back. However, a producer responsibility organization shall provide for curbside or
32 multifamily recycling collection services if all of the following circumstances apply:
33 (1) The category of packaging materials is suitable for residential curbside
34 recycling collection and can be effectively sorted by the facilities receiving
35 the packaging material.
36 (2) The recycling facility providing processing and sorting service agrees to
37 accept the category of packaging material.
38 (3) The provider of residential curbside recycling service agrees to the producer
39 responsibility organization's reimbursement arrangement.
40 (4) The category of packaging material is not handled through a deposit and return
41 scheme or a buyback system that relies on a collection system other than
42 curbside collection.
43 "§ 130A-309.257. Labeling.
44 Responsible producers shall indicate on all packaging material used for containing,
45 protecting, delivering, presenting, or distributing products sold, offered for sale, or distributed in
46 the State (i) the percentage of postconsumer waste material content, (ii) whether the packaging
47 material is readily recyclable, and (iii) whether the packaging material is compostable. Such
48 labels must be in a form deemed appropriate by the Department pursuant to rules adopted by the
49 Commission.

18 (a) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this Part:
19 (1) Compostable. – An item that reliably and entirely breaks down and becomes
20 part of usable compost no later than 180 days after proper disposal.
21 Compostable items may be made from organic materials such as bagasse,
22 sugarcane, bamboo, paper, hay, or certified compostable plastics that meet the
23 American Society for Testing and Materials (ASTM) standard specifications
24 for industrially compostable plastics D6400 and compostable plastic coatings
25 D6868. Permitted compostable plastics, often marketed as biodegradable,
26 must be clearly labeled with the applicable standard on the product.
27 (2) Covered product. – Any product subject to the requirements of this Part,
28 including food packaging.
29 (3) Food package or food packaging. – A package, compostable container, or
30 packaging component that is intended for direct food content.
31 (4) Intentionally added PFAS. – Any of the following:
32 a. PFAS added to a product or one of the product's components that have
33 a function or technical effect in the product, including the PFAS
34 components of products that constitute added chemicals or products
35 thereof.
36 b. PFAS used or produced during the manufacture or processing of a
37 product introduced into or onto the product, including any source of
38 PFAS that is reasonably known to be present, such as using processing
39 agents, mold release agents, or fluorination.
40 (5) Manufacturer. – The person who manufactures a product or whose brand
41 name is affixed to the product. If the product is imported into the United
42 States, the term "manufacturer" includes the importer or first domestic
43 distributor of the product, if the person that manufactured or assembled the
44 product or whose brand name is affixed to the product does not have a
45 principal place of business in the United States.
46 (6) PFAS. – Perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances, a class of fluorinated
47 organic chemicals containing at least one fully fluorinated carbon atom.
48 (7) Product. – An item manufactured, assembled, packaged, or otherwise
49 prepared for sale to consumers, including its product components, sold or
50 distributed for personal, residential, commercial, or industrial use, including
