13 "§ 130A-309.250. Definitions.
14 Unless a different meaning is required by the context, the following definitions apply
15 throughout this Part:
16 (1) Collector. – Any publicly or privately owned solid waste management
17 services or recycler that gathers discarded packaging materials from
18 residential, commercial, industrial, or governmental establishments.
19 (2) Fulfillment service provider. – A person that handles, on behalf of a
20 responsible producer, products in which the producer retains a property
21 interest and offers at least two of the following services as part of a
22 commercial activity: (i) warehousing, (ii) packaging, and (iii) addressing and
23 shipping. For purposes of this Part, "fulfillment service provider" does not
24 include postal or freight service providers.
25 (3) Packaging material. – Any part of a package or container, regardless of
26 recyclability or compostability, including material types such as paper, plastic,
27 glass, or metal, that is used for the containment, protection, handling, delivery,
28 transport, distribution, and presentation of a product that is sold, offered for
29 sale, or distributed in North Carolina. Bags are included within this definition.
30 Packaging material does not include any of the following:
31 a. Material, or a category of material, intended to be used for long-term
32 storage or protection of a durable product that can be expected to be
33 usable for that purpose for a period of at least five years.
34 b. Medical devices and packaging that are included with products
35 regulated as a drug, medical device, or dietary supplement under the
36 Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act, 21 U.S.C. § 321, et seq.

43 rules adopted by the Commission pursuant to this Part.
44 "§ 130A-309.251. Requirements for responsible producers.
45 (a) Registration Required. – Each responsible producer shall register with the Department
46 before selling, offering for sale, or distributing in North Carolina a product contained, protected,
47 delivered, presented, or distributed in or using packaging materials.
48 (b) Stewardship Plan Required. – No responsible producer shall sell, offer for sale, or
49 distribute in North Carolina a product contained, protected, delivered, presented, or distributed
50 in or using packaging materials unless the producer participates as a member of a producer

1 responsibility organization for which a stewardship plan is approved by the Department, and
2 through that participation, satisfies the requirements of subsections (c), (d), and (e) of this section.
3 (c) Non-Reusable Packaging Material Reduction Requirements. – According to the
4 following schedule, each responsible producer shall reduce the total amount of non-reusable
5 packaging materials used for containing, protecting, delivering, presenting, and distributing
6 products sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State (i) by unit, (ii) on average, and (iii) in
7 the aggregate:
8 (1) Ten percent (10%) after two years following approval of the stewardship plan.
9 (2) Twenty percent (20%) after four years following approval of the stewardship
10 plan.
11 (3) Thirty percent (30%) after six years following approval of the stewardship
12 plan.
13 (4) Forty percent (40%) after eight years following approval of the stewardship
14 plan.
15 The reductions required by this subsection shall be measured against the total amount of
16 packaging material the responsible producer used for containing, protecting, delivering,
17 presenting, and distributing the products it sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State during
18 the respective calendar year.
19 (d) Recycling-Rate Requirements. – Each responsible producer shall ensure that all
20 non-reusable packaging material, in the aggregate, used for containing, protecting, delivering,
21 presenting, and distributing the products it sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State is
22 recycled at a rate consistent with the following schedule:
23 (1) Fifty percent (50%) after five years following approval of the stewardship
24 plan.
25 (2) Eighty percent (80%) after eight years following approval of the stewardship
26 plan.
27 (3) Ninety percent (90%) after 12 years following approval of the stewardship
28 plan.
29 (e) Postconsumer Waste Material Content Requirements. – Each responsible producer
30 shall ensure that all non-reusable packaging material used for containing, protecting, delivering,
31 presenting, and distributing the products it sold, offered for sale, or distributed in this State
32 incorporates, on average and in the aggregate, the following amount, by weight, of postconsumer
33 waste material in accordance with the following schedule:
34 (1) Fifty percent (50%) after five years following approval of the stewardship
35 plan.
36 (2) Eighty percent (80%) after eight years following approval of the stewardship
37 plan.
38 (3) Ninety percent (90%) after 12 years following approval of the stewardship
39 plan.
40 "§ 130A-309.252. Producer responsibility organizations; participation fees.
41 (a) Membership Requirement. – Each responsible producer shall establish or join a
42 producer responsibility organization to act as an agent on behalf of the producer for the purposes
43 of satisfying its obligations under this Part. A producer may participate in more than one producer
44 responsibility organization if each organization is established for a different category of
45 packaging materials. Any producer responsibility organization formed for the purpose of
46 complying with this Part shall be established and operated as an organization described in section
47 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.
48 (b) Participation Fees. – A producer responsibility organization shall establish
49 participation fees for its member producers that are sufficient to cover all costs of developing and
50 implementing the stewardship plan, including each producer's proportionate share of
51 administrative, enforcement, and outreach and education costs. The participation fee must reflect

1 the per ton costs associated with the collection, processing, transportation, and recycling and
2 disposal of a producer's packaging material.
3 (c) Fee Structure. – A producer responsibility organization shall structure the
4 participation fees to provide producers with financial incentives to reward waste reduction and
5 recycling compatibility innovations and practices and to discourage designs or practices that
6 increase costs of managing the products. The producer responsibility organization may adjust
7 fees to be paid by each participating producer based on factors that affect system costs. At a
8 minimum, fees shall be variable based on the following:
9 (1) Costs to provide curbside collection of packaging material, or other levels of
10 collection service, that is, at a minimum, as convenient as the recycling
11 collection service offered in the particular jurisdiction prior to the
12 implementation of the stewardship plan.
13 (2) Costs to process a producer's packaging material for acceptance by secondary
14 material markets.
15 (3) Whether the packaging material includes labels, inks, and adhesives
16 containing heavy metals or other hazardous wastes that would contaminate
17 the recycling process.
18 (4) Whether the packaging material is specifically designed to be reusable or
19 refillable and has a high reuse or refill rate.
20 (5) The percentage of postconsumer waste material in a producer's packaging
21 material.
22 (6) Contributions to greenhouse gas emissions from the production, use,
23 collection, processing, and marketing of the packaging material.
24 The producer responsibility organization shall annually evaluate, revise, and submit an
25 updated fee assessment schedule to the Department.
26 (d) Producer Responsibility Fund. – A producer responsibility organization shall
27 establish and manage a producer responsibility fund. The organization shall deposit into the fund
28 all payments received from responsible producers in accordance with this section and shall
29 expend those funds for the following uses:
30 (1) To reimburse participating collectors in accordance with G.S. 130A-309.240.
31 (2) To fund collection and convenience obligations, required pursuant to
32 G.S. 130A-309.241.
33 (3) To fund the outreach and education programming, required pursuant to
34 G.S. 130A-309.243.
35 (4) To fund the actual operating costs of the organization, which may not exceed
36 the estimated operating costs indicated in the stewardship plan approved by
37 the Department pursuant to G.S. 130A-309.239.
38 (5) To pay fees assessed by the Department for the purposes of implementing this
39 Part.
40 (e) Payment of Participation Fees. – No later than 30 days after the approval of the
41 stewardship plan under G.S. 130A-309.239, and quarterly thereafter, a responsible producer shall
42 pay the producer responsibility organization any outstanding participation fees, to be deposited
43 into the producer responsibility fund.
44 (f) Producer Information Requirement. – A responsible producer participating in a
45 producer responsibility organization shall provide the organization with all information
46 necessary (i) for determining the producer's compliance with the stewardship plan and (ii) for
47 calculating the participation fees to be assessed to the producer.

12 "§ 130A-309.254. Review and approval of proposed stewardship plans.
13 (a) No later than 90 days after the submission of the stewardship plan, the Department
14 shall make a determination to approve the plan as submitted, approve the plan with conditions,
15 or deny the plan.
16 (b) In reviewing any stewardship plan under this Part, the Department shall consider
17 whether the stewardship plan:
18 (1) Adequately addresses all requirements set forth in G.S. 130A-309.238 with
19 sufficient detail.
20 (2) Provides a feasible strategy detailing how responsible producers will satisfy
21 the requirements established in G.S. 130A-309.236.
22 (3) Proposes participation fees that:
23 a. Sufficiently cover the entire cost of implementing the stewardship plan
24 and complying with all other requirements of this Part, including any
25 rules adopted by the Commission pursuant to this Part.
26 b. Adequately incentivize waste reduction and recycling compatibility
27 innovations and practices and discourage designs and practices that
28 increase the costs of managing packaging materials.
29 c. Equitably allocate implementation costs among responsible producers.
30 (4) Provides for widespread, convenient, and equitable collection opportunities,
31 as required pursuant to G.S. 130A-309.241.
32 (5) Provides for effective outreach and education programming, as required
33 pursuant to G.S. 130A-309.243.
34 (6) Provides a feasible strategy detailing how responsible producers will satisfy
35 the labeling requirements pursuant to G.S. 130A-309.242, including a
36 description of how the producer responsibility organization will verify each
37 producer's labeling claims through an independent, third-party audit.
38 (7) Proposes a schedule of collector reimbursement payments that are calculated
39 in a reasonable manner, pursuant to G.S. 130A-309.240, and that cover the
40 costs of collecting, recycling, processing, and disposing of the packaging
41 materials listed under the stewardship plan.
42 (8) Establishes a reasonable process for participating collectors to apply for and
43 receive reimbursements from the producer responsibility organization.
44 (9) Provides sufficient information to establish that the producer responsibility
45 organization engaged in a reasonable stakeholder review process, as required
46 pursuant to G.S. 130A-309.238(c).
47 (10) Satisfies any other requirements adopted by the Commission.
48 (c) No later than six months after the date the stewardship plan is approved, the producer
49 responsibility organization and its member producers shall implement the approved plan.

22 "§ 130A-309.256. Collection and convenience.
23 (a) A producer responsibility organization shall provide for widespread, convenient, and
24 equitable access to collection opportunities for the packaging material identified under the
25 stewardship plan.
26 (b) A producer responsibility organization may rely on a range of means to collect various
27 categories of packaging material, including curbside collection, depot drop-off, and retailer
28 take-back. However, a producer responsibility organization shall provide for curbside or
29 multifamily recycling collection services if all of the following circumstances apply:
30 (1) The category of packaging materials is suitable for residential curbside
31 recycling collection and can be effectively sorted by the facilities receiving
32 the packaging material.
33 (2) The recycling facility providing processing and sorting service agrees to
34 accept the category of packaging material.
35 (3) The provider of residential curbside recycling service agrees to the producer
36 responsibility organization's reimbursement arrangement.
37 (4) The category of packaging material is not handled through a deposit and return
38 scheme or a buyback system that relies on a collection system other than
39 curbside collection.
40 "§ 130A-309.257. Labeling.

42 protecting, delivering, presenting, or distributing products sold, offered for sale, or distributed in

44 material is readily recyclable, and (iii) whether the packaging material is compostable. Such
45 labels must be in a form deemed appropriate by the Department pursuant to rules adopted by the
46 Commission.

19 revenue during the immediately preceding calendar year.

28 demonstrate that the producer, pursuant to subsection (a) of this section, is excluded from

16 "§ 130A-309.271. Civil penalties.
17 (a) The Secretary may assess a civil penalty of not more than five thousand dollars
18 ($5,000) or, if the violation involves a hazardous waste, as defined in G.S. 130A-290, of not more
19 than twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000) against any person who violates a requirement of
20 this Part.
21 (b) If any action or failure to act for which a penalty may be assessed under subsection
22 (a) of this section is a repeat offense, the Secretary may assess a penalty not to exceed ten
23 thousand dollars ($10,000) per occurrence. A penalty for multiple occurrences shall not exceed
24 two hundred thousand dollars ($200,000) in any month.
25 (c) In determining the amount of the penalty, the Secretary shall consider the factors set
26 out in G.S. 143B-282.1(b). The procedures set out in G.S. 143B-282.1 shall apply to civil penalty
27 assessments that are presented to the Commission for final agency decision.
28 (d) The Secretary shall notify any person assessed a civil penalty for the assessment and
29 the specific reasons therefor by registered or certified mail or by any means authorized by
30 G.S. 1A-1, Rule 4. Contested case petitions shall be filed pursuant to G.S. 150B-23 within 30
31 days of receipt of the notice of assessment.
32 (e) Requests for remission of civil penalties shall be filed with the Secretary. Remission
33 requests shall not be considered unless made within 30 days of receipt of the notice of assessment.
34 Remission requests must be accompanied by a waiver of the right to a contested case hearing
35 pursuant to Chapter 150B of the General Statutes and a stipulation of the facts on which the
