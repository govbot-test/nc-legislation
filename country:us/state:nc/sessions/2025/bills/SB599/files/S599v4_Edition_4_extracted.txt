1 SECTION 2.3.(e) CTE Course Value Ranking. – The Department of Commerce, in
2 consultation with the State Board, shall assign a value ranking for each industry certification and

1 SECTION 2.4.(b) Definitions. – As used in this section, the following definitions
2 shall apply:
3 (1) Adjusted market value of taxable real property. – A county's assessed taxable
4 real property value, using the latest available data published by the
5 Department of Revenue, divided by the county's sales assessment ratio
6 determined under G.S. 105-289(h).
7 (2) Composite value. – For each eligible county, the sum of the following:
8 a. The taxable real property factor multiplied by sixty-five percent
9 (65%).
10 b. The median household income factor multiplied by twenty-five
11 percent (25%).
12 c. The effective tax rate factor multiplied by ten percent (10%).
13 (3) County allocation factor. – For each eligible county, the supplement factor for
14 that county divided by the sum of all supplement factors for the State.
15 (4) Effective tax rate. – The actual county tax rate multiplied by the most recent
16 annual sales assessment ratio for that county.
17 (5) Effective tax rate factor. – For each eligible county, the effective tax rate for
18 that county divided by the median effective tax rate in the State.
19 (6) Eligible county. – A county that has an adjusted market value of taxable real
20 property of less than sixty-three billion dollars ($63,000,000,000).
21 (7) Eligible local school administrative unit. – A local school administrative unit
22 located in whole or in part in an eligible county.
23 (8) Eligible school. – A public school that is located in an eligible county and
24 governed by a local school administrative unit.
25 (9) Maintenance of effort amount. – For each local school administrative unit in
26 each fiscal year, the supplant factor multiplied by the total State and non-State
27 funds expended for salaries for teachers from the fiscal year for which the
28 most recent salary data are available.
29 (10) Median household income. – A county's median household income for the
30 most recent 12 months for which data are available, as that term is used in
31 G.S. 143B-437.08.
32 (11) Median household income factor. – For each eligible county, the median
33 household income in the State divided by the median household income for
34 that county.
35 (12) Non-State funds. – Any funds held by a local school administrative unit, other
36 than nonrecurring federal funds received as a result of legislation enacted by
