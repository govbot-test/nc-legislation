8 SECTION 2.4.(b) Definitions. – As used in this section, the following definitions
9 shall apply:
10 (1) Adjusted market value of taxable real property. – A county's assessed taxable
11 real property value, using the latest available data published by the
12 Department of Revenue, divided by the county's sales assessment ratio
13 determined under G.S. 105-289(h).
14 (2) Composite value. – For each eligible county, the sum of the following:
15 a. The taxable real property factor multiplied by sixty-five percent
16 (65%).
17 b. The median household income factor multiplied by twenty-five
18 percent (25%).
19 c. The effective tax rate factor multiplied by ten percent (10%).
20 (3) County allocation factor. – For each eligible county, the supplement factor for
21 that county divided by the sum of all supplement factors for the State.
22 (4) Effective tax rate. – The actual county tax rate multiplied by the most recent
23 annual sales assessment ratio for that county.
24 (5) Effective tax rate factor. – For each eligible county, the effective tax rate for
25 that county divided by the median effective tax rate in the State.
26 (6) Eligible county. – A county that has an adjusted market value of taxable real
27 property of less than sixty-three billion dollars ($63,000,000,000).
28 (7) Eligible local school administrative unit. – A local school administrative unit
29 located in whole or in part in an eligible county.
30 (8) Eligible school. – A public school that is located in an eligible county and
31 governed by a local school administrative unit.
32 (9) Maintenance of effort amount. – For each local school administrative unit in
33 each fiscal year, the supplant factor multiplied by the total State and non-State
34 funds expended for salaries for teachers from the fiscal year for which the
35 most recent salary data are available.
36 (10) Median household income. – A county's median household income for the
37 most recent 12 months for which data are available, as that term is used in
38 G.S. 143B-437.08.
39 (11) Median household income factor. – For each eligible county, the median
