1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO REVISE THE NORTH CAROLINA BOARD OF DENTAL EXAMINERS.
3 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
4 SECTION 1.(a) The title of G.S. 90-22 reads as rewritten:
5 "§ 90-22. Practice of dentistry regulated in public interest; Article liberally construed;
6 Board of Dental Examiners; composition; qualifications and terms of members;
7 vacancies; nominations and elections; compensation; expenditures by Board."
8 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 90-22(a) reads as rewritten:
9 "(a) Purpose of the Practice of Dentistry. – The practice of dentistry in the State of North
10 Carolina is hereby declared to affect affects the public health, safety and welfare and to be is
11 subject to regulation and control by the State in the public interest. It is further declared to be a
12 matter of public interest and concern that the dental profession merit and receive the confidence
13 of the public and that only qualified persons be permitted to practice dentistry in the State of
14 North Carolina. This Article shall be liberally construed to carry out these objects and purposes."
15 SECTION 1.(c) G.S. 90-22(b) reads as rewritten:
16 "(b) Creation. – The North Carolina State Board of Dental Examiners heretofore
17 previously created by Chapter 139, Public Laws 1879 and by Chapter 178, Public Laws 1915, is
18 hereby continued as the agency of the State for the regulation of the practice of dentistry in this
19 State. Said It is a matter of public interest that State officials must possess and exercise power to
20 review the acts of private parties and disapprove those that fail to accord with State policy. The
21 potential for State supervision is not an adequate substitute for a decision by the State.
22 (b1) Board of Dental Examiners. – The Board of Dental Examiners shall consist of six 10
23 members, as follows:
24 (1) Six dentists who are licensed to practice dentistry in North Carolina, one
25 Carolina and have been actively engaged in the practice of dentistry in this
26 State for at least five years immediately preceding their appointment.
27 (2) Two dental hygienist who is hygienists who are licensed to practice dental
28 hygiene in North Carolina and one person who have been actively engaged in
29 the practice of dentistry in this State for at least five years immediately
30 preceding their appointment.
31 (3) Two public consumer members, each of whom shall be a citizen and resident
32 of North Carolina and who shall meet each of the following criteria:
33 a. Not be licensed to practice neither dentistry nor dentistry, dental
34 hygiene. hygiene, or any other dental profession.
35 b. Does not have any financial interest in the provision of dental service.
36 c. Never has been licensed to practice dentistry or dental hygiene.

1 d. Never has been registered, certified, or licensed by an occupational
2 licensing agency in the health care field.
3 e. Is not employed by or does not participate in the management of a
4 business entity or other organization receiving funds from the Board.
5 f. Does not use or receive a substantial amount of tangible goods,
6 services, or money from the Board, other than compensation or
7 reimbursement authorized by law for Board membership, attendance,
8 or expenses.
9 g. Not be affiliated with or employed by the Board or by any member on
10 the Board.
11 h. Is not a spouse of a dentist or dental hygienist.
12 (b2) Limitation on Voting. – The dental hygienist or the consumer member members
13 cannot participate or vote in any matters of the Board which involves the issuance, renewal or
14 revocation of the license to practice dentistry in the State of North Carolina. The consumer
15 member cannot participate or vote in any matters of the Board which involve the issuance,
16 renewal or revocation of the license to practice dental hygiene in the State of North Carolina.
17 (b3) Members of the Board licensed to practice dentistry in North Carolina shall have been
18 elected in an election held as hereinafter provided in which every person licensed to practice
19 dentistry in North Carolina and residing or practicing in North Carolina shall be entitled to vote.
20 Terms. – Each member of said appointed to the Board shall be elected serve for a term of three
21 years and years. Each member may be reappointed for one additional consecutive term. No
22 member shall serve more than two three-year terms; however, a member shall serve until his or
23 her successor shall be elected and shall qualify. Each year there shall be elected two dentists for
24 such terms of three years each. Every three years there shall be elected one dental hygienist for
25 a term of three years. Dental hygienists shall be elected to the Board in an election held in
26 accordance with the procedures hereinafter provided in which those persons licensed to practice
27 dental hygiene in North Carolina and residing or practicing in North Carolina shall be entitled to
28 vote. Every three years a person who is a citizen and resident of North Carolina and licensed to
29 practice neither dentistry nor dental hygiene shall be appointed to the Board for a term of three
30 years by the Governor of North Carolina. Any vacancy occurring on said Board shall be filled
31 by a majority vote of the remaining members of the Board to serve until the next regular election
32 conducted by the Board, at which time the vacancy will be filled by the election process provided
33 for in this Article, except that when the seat on the Board held by a person licensed to practice
34 neither dentistry nor dental hygiene in North Carolina shall become vacant, the vacancy shall be
35 filled by appointment by the Governor for the period of the unexpired term. No dentist shall be
36 nominated for or elected to membership on said Board, unless, at the time of such nomination
37 and election such person is licensed to practice dentistry in North Carolina and actually engaged
38 in the practice of dentistry. No dental hygienist shall be nominated for or elected to membership
39 on said Board unless, at the time of such nomination and election, such person is licensed to
40 practice dental hygiene in North Carolina and is currently employed in dental hygiene in North
41 Carolina. No person shall be nominated, elected, or appointed to serve more than two consecutive
42 terms on said Board."
43 SECTION 1.(d) Repeal of the Board of Dental Elections. – G.S. 90-22(c) through
44 (e) are repealed.
45 SECTION 1.(e) Article 2 of Chapter 90 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
46 a new section to read:
47 "§ 90-22A. Appointment of Board members; vacancies; compensation; terms.
48 (a) Appointment of Board Members. – The Board of Dental Examiners shall be appointed
49 as follows:
50 (1) Four members shall be appointed by the Governor as follows:
51 a. Two dentists that meet the requirements of G.S. 90-22(b1)(1).

6 a. Two dentists that meet the requirements of G.S. 90-22(b1)(1) by
7 recommendation of the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
8 b. Two dentists that meet the requirements of G.S. 90-22(b1)(1) by
9 recommendation of the President Pro Tempore of the Senate.
10 (3) Two members shall be appointed by the Commissioner of Labor as follows:

12 shall be appointed by the Commissioner of Labor.
13 b. One public consumer member who meets the requirements of
14 G.S. 90-22(b1)(3).
15 (b) Vacancies; Removal. – A vacancy shall be filled in the same manner as the original
16 appointment, except that all unexpired terms of Board members appointed by the General
17 Assembly shall be filled in accordance with G.S. 120-122. Appointees to fill vacancies shall
18 serve the remainder of the unexpired term and until their successors have been duly appointed
19 and qualified, not to exceed 60 days. The appointing authority shall have the power to remove
20 their respective Board member for neglect of duty, incompetence, or unprofessional conduct.
21 (c) Compensation. – Each member of the Board shall receive per diem and
22 reimbursement for travel and subsistence as provided in G.S. 93B-5.
23 (d) Officers. – The officers of the Board shall be a chair, who shall be a licensed dentist,
24 a vice-chair, and other officers deemed necessary by the Board to carry out the purposes of this
25 Article. All officers shall be elected annually by the Board for one-year terms and shall serve
26 until their successors are elected and qualified."
27 SECTION 1.(f) G.S. 90-43 reads as rewritten:
28 "§ 90-43. Compensation and expenses Expenses of Board.
29 Notwithstanding G.S. 93B-5(a), each member of the North Carolina State Board of Dental
30 Examiners shall receive as compensation for his services in the performance of his duties under
31 this Article a sum not exceeding one hundred dollars ($100.00) for each day actually engaged in
32 the performance of the duties of his office, said per diem to be fixed by said Board, and all
33 legitimate and necessary expenses incurred in attending meetings of the said Board.
34 The Board is authorized and empowered to expend from funds collected hereunder such
35 additional sum or sums as it may determine necessary in the administration and enforcement of
36 this Article, and employ such personnel as it may deem requisite to assist in carrying out the
37 administrative functions required by this Article and by the Board."
38 SECTION 2. Transition of Board Members. – Notwithstanding G.S. 90-22, as
39 amended by this act, and G.S. 90-22A, as enacted by this act, the current members of the North
40 Carolina Board of Dental Examiners (Board) shall each finish their current respective terms on
41 the Board. Prior to the expiration or termination of each member's current term, the new member
42 for that seat shall be appointed pursuant to G.S. 90-22 and G.S. 90-22A, as set forth in this act.
43 The Governor shall appoint the dental hygienist and each public consumer seat as each becomes
44 eligible for appointment in accordance with G.S. 90-22 and G.S. 90-22A, as set forth in this act.
45 The licensed dentist seats that become eligible for appointment after this act becomes law shall
46 be appointed for three-year terms in the following order:
47 (1) The first two seats: the Governor shall appoint two licensed dentists in
48 accordance with G.S. 90-22A(a)(1).
49 (2) The third and fifth seats: the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall
50 appoint two licensed dentists in accordance with G.S. 90-22A(a)(2)a.

3 The licensed dental hygienist seats that become eligible for appointment after this act

9 SECTION 3. The North Carolina Board of Dental Examiners shall adopt temporary
10 rules to implement the provisions of this act and shall adopt permanent rules to replace the
11 temporary rules. Those temporary rules shall remain in effect until permanent rules that replace
12 those rules become effective.
