Section 1:
FILED SENATE
Apr 30, 2026
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
S.B. 1061
SESSION 2025
PRINCIPAL CLERK
S D
Short Title: Protect Moms Who Serve. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Applewhite and Murdock (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to:
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT DIRECTING THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES, IN
3 CONSULTATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF MILITARY AND VETERANS
4 AFFAIRS, TO STUDY HEALTH ISSUES AFFECTING WOMEN WHO SERVE IN THE
5 MILITARY; AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS FOR THIS PURPOSE.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1.(a) The Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) shall study
8 the following issues affecting women who serve in the military:
9 (1) Coordinating effectively between veterans health care facilities and
10 non-veterans health care facilities in the delivery of maternity care and other
11 health care services.
12 (2) Facilitating access to community resources to address social determinants of
13 health, including housing, nutrition, and employment status.
14 (3) Identifying mental and behavioral health risk factors in the prenatal and
15 postpartum periods and ensuring that pregnant and postpartum veterans get
16 the treatments they need.
17 (4) Facilitating access to childbirth preparation classes, parenting classes,
18 nutrition counseling, breastfeeding support, lactation classes, and breast
19 pumps.
20 (5) Reducing maternal mortality and severe maternal morbidity, with a particular
21 focus on racial and ethnic disparities in maternal health outcomes.
22 (6) Referring veterans who are mothers to the VA Community Care Network and
23 recommendations for how that process can be improved.
24 (7) Existing barriers to maternity care affecting veterans who are mothers.
25 SECTION 1.(b) In conducting the study required by subsection (a) of this section,
26 the Department of Health and Human Services shall solicit feedback from mothers who are
27 currently serving, or who formerly served, in the military. In addition, the DHHS shall consult
28 with the Department of Military and Veterans Affairs (DMVA), as necessary. The DMVA shall
29 cooperate with the DHHS and provide any assistance or information requested with respect to
30 the study required by subsection (a) of this section.
31 SECTION 1.(c) By April 1, 2027, the DHHS shall report its findings, and any
32 recommendations for legislation, to the Senate Health Care Committee, the Joint Legislative
33 Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services, the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee
34 on General Government, and the Fiscal Research Division.
35 SECTION 2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
36 Health and Human Services the sum of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) in nonrecurring
*DRS45450-MGa-167*
General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year for the purpose of conducting the study described in Section
2 1 of this act.
3 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective July 1, 2026.
