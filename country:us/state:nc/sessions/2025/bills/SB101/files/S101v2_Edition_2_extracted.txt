10 PART I. PROTECT CERTAIN TAX-ADVANTAGED ACCOUNTS
11 SECTION 1.(a) Article 16 of Chapter 1C of the General Statutes is amended by
12 adding the following new section to read:
13 "§ 1C-1601.5. Certain tax-advantaged accounts exempt.
14 (a) For purposes of this section, the following definitions shall apply:
15 (1) Qualifying account. – Any of the following:
16 a. An education savings and investment plan account qualified under
17 section 529 of the Internal Revenue Code.
18 b. An ABLE account qualified under section 529A of the Internal
19 Revenue Code.
20 (2) Qualifying purpose. – Any of the following:
21 a. For education savings and investment plan accounts, any purpose
22 permitted under section 529 of the Internal Revenue Code.
23 b. For ABLE accounts, any purpose permitted under section 529A of the
24 Internal Revenue Code.
25 (b) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, including G.S. 1C-1601(e), funds
26 located in a qualifying account or withdrawn from the account and used for a qualifying purpose
27 shall not be subject to liens, attachment, garnishment, levy, seizure, any involuntary sale or
28 assignment by operation or execution of law, or the enforcement of any other judgment or claim
29 to pay any debt or liability of any account owner, beneficiary, or contributor to an account.
30 (c) Nothing in this section shall be construed to do any of the following:
31 (1) Prohibit the distribution of funds from an ABLE account following the death
32 of the account owner under G.S. 147-86.73(e) or section 529A of the Internal
33 Revenue Code.
34 (2) Limit the enforcement of judgments or claims on funds that meet any of the
35 following criteria:
36 a. Were not used for a qualifying purpose.

7 PART II. LIVE ORGAN DONOR PROTECTIONS
8 SECTION 2.(a) G.S. 58-3-25 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
9 "(d) No insurer shall refuse to insure or to continue to insure an individual; limit the
10 amount, extent, or kind of coverage available to an individual; charge an individual a different
11 amount for the same coverage; or otherwise discriminate against an individual in the offering,
12 issuance, cancellation, price, or conditions of a policy, or in the amount of coverage provided
13 under a policy, based solely and without any additional actuarial risks on the status of an
14 individual as a living organ donor. This subsection shall apply to health benefit plans and life,
15 accident and health, disability, disability income, and long-term care insurance policies. For the
16 purposes of this subsection, the phrase "a living organ donor" shall mean a living individual who
17 donates one or more of that individual's human organs, including bone marrow, to be medically
18 transplanted into the body of another individual."
19 SECTION 2.(b) Article 2 of Chapter 126 of the General Statutes is amended by
20 adding a new section to read:
21 "§ 126-8.7. Paid leave for State employees and State-supported personnel for organ
22 donation.
23 (a) Full-Time Employees. – The State Human Resources Commission shall adopt rules
24 and policies to provide that a permanent, full-time State employee may take, in addition to any
25 other leave available to the employee, up to (i) 30 days of paid leave for the purposes of serving
26 as a living organ donor and (ii) seven days for serving as a bone marrow donor. The employee
27 must have been continuously employed by the State for at least 12 months immediately preceding
28 the first request for paid organ or bone marrow donation leave.
29 (b) Part-Time Employees. – The State Human Resources Commission shall adopt rules
30 and policies to provide that a permanent, part-time State employee may take, in addition to any
31 other leave available to the employee, a prorated amount of up to (i) 30 days of paid leave for the
32 purposes of serving as a living organ donor and (ii) seven days for serving as a bone marrow
33 donor. The employee must have been continuously employed by the State for at least 12 months
34 immediately preceding the first request for paid organ or bone marrow donation leave.
35 (c) Program Requirements. – The paid leave for organ or bone marrow donation
36 authorized by this section:
37 (1) Is available without exhaustion of the employee's sick and vacation leave.
38 (2) Is in addition to, and not in lieu of, shared leave under G.S. 126-8.3, or other
39 leave authorized by federal or State law.
40 (3) May not be used for retirement purposes.
41 (4) Has no cash value upon termination from employment.
42 (d) Applicability. – This section applies to all (i) State employees and (ii) State-supported
43 personnel, with the appropriate governing board adopting rules and policies to provide paid leave
44 for organ donation to its employees as provided by this section.
45 (e) Reporting. – By April 1, 2026, and then annually thereafter, the State Human
46 Resources Commission, the State Board of Education, the State Board of Community Colleges,
47 and all State agencies, departments, and institutions shall annually report to the Office of State
48 Human Resources on the paid organ donation leave program."
49 SECTION 2.(c) G.S. 126-5 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
50 "(c22) The provisions of G.S. 126-8.7 shall apply to all State employees, public school
51 employees, and community college employees."
