GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 3
Committee Substitute Favorable 4/29/25
Third Edition Engrossed 5/6/25
Short Title: Civil Procedure Amendment. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
April 1, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT AMENDING THE CIVIL PROCEDURE STATUTE RELATING TO ACCRUAL OF
3 ACTION AND LIMITING USE OF STATE FUNDING.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 1-15 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 1-15. Statute runs from accrual of action.
7 (a) Civil actions can only be commenced within the periods prescribed in this Chapter,
8 after the cause of action has accrued, except where in special cases a different limitation is
9 prescribed by statute.
10 (b) Repealed by Session Laws 1979, c. 654, s. 3.
11 (c) Except as provided by subsection (d) of this section or where otherwise provided by
12 statute, a cause of action for malpractice arising out of the performance of or failure to perform
13 professional services shall be deemed to accrue at the time of the occurrence of the last act of the
14 defendant giving rise to the cause of action: Provided that whenever there is bodily injury to the
15 person, economic or monetary loss, or a defect in or damage to property which originates under
16 circumstances making the injury, loss, defect or damage not readily apparent to the claimant at
17 the time of its origin, and the injury, loss, defect or damage is discovered or should reasonably
18 be discovered by the claimant two or more years after the occurrence of the last act of the
19 defendant giving rise to the cause of action, suit must be commenced within one year from the
20 date discovery is made: Provided nothing herein shall be construed to reduce the statute of
21 limitation in any such case below three years. Provided further, that in no event shall an action
22 be commenced more than four years from the last act of the defendant giving rise to the cause of
23 action: Provided further, that where damages are sought by reason of a foreign object, which has
24 no therapeutic or diagnostic purpose or effect, having been left in the body, a person seeking
25 damages for malpractice may commence an action therefor within one year after discovery
26 thereof as hereinabove provided, but in no event may the action be commenced more than 10
27 years from the last act of the defendant giving rise to the cause of action.
28 (d) A cause of action arising out of the performance of or failure to perform services
29 while in the course of facilitating or perpetuating gender transition shall be commenced within
30 10 years from the day the claimant reaches 18 years of age. "Gender transition" is defined in
31 G.S. 90-21.150(5) for the purposes of this section.
32 (e) A medical professional or entity may not seek a contractual waiver of the liability
33 arising out of the performance of or failure to perform services while in the course of facilitating
34 or perpetuating gender transition. Any attempted waiver is contrary to the public policy of this
35 State and is null and void.

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 (f) G.S. 90-21.19 does not apply to damages for a cause of action arising out of the
2 performance of or failure to perform services while in the course of facilitating or perpetuating
3 gender transition."
4 SECTION 2. G.S. 143C-6-5.6 reads as rewritten:
5 "§ 143C-6-5.6. Limitation on use of State funds for gender transition procedures.
6 (a) The following definitions apply in this section:
7 (1) Cross-sex hormones. – As defined in G.S. 90-21.150.
8 (2) Minor. – As defined in G.S. 90-21.150.
9 (3) Puberty-blocking drugs. – As defined in G.S. 90-21.150.
10 (4) Surgical gender transition procedure. – As defined in G.S. 90-21.150.
11 (b) No State funds may be used, directly or indirectly, for the performance of or in
12 furtherance of surgical gender transition procedures, or to provide puberty-blocking drugs or
13 cross-sex hormones to a minor, or to support the administration of any governmental health plan
14 or government-offered insurance policy offering surgical gender transition procedures,
15 puberty-blocking drugs, or cross-sex hormones to a minor.
16 (b1) No State funds may be used, directly or indirectly, for the performance of or in
17 furtherance of surgical gender transition procedures, or to provide puberty-blocking drugs or
18 cross-sex hormones to any prisoner incarcerated in the State prison system or the Statewide
19 Misdemeanor Confinement Program or otherwise in the custody of the Department of Adult
20 Correction, or to support the administration of any governmental health plan or
21 government-offered insurance policy offering surgical gender transition procedures,
22 puberty-blocking drugs, or cross-sex hormones to any prisoner incarcerated in the State prison
23 system or the Statewide Misdemeanor Confinement Program or otherwise in the custody of the
24 Department of Adult Correction. Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to prevent State
25 funds from being used, directly or indirectly, to address medical complications resulting in
26 imminent physical harm, including the treatment of any infection, injury, disease, or disorder that
27 has been caused by or exacerbated by a previously performed or privately funded gender
28 transition procedure.
29 (c) Subsection (b) Subsections (b) and (b1) of this section shall not apply to the State
30 Health Plan for Teachers and State Employees."
31 SECTION 2.5. Severability. – If any provision of this act or its application is held
32 invalid, the invalidity does not affect other provisions or applications of this act that can be given
33 effect without the invalid provisions or application, and to this end the provisions of this act are
34 severable.
35 SECTION 3. Section 1 of this act is effective when it becomes law and applies to
36 causes of action accruing before, on, or after that date. This section revives any cause of action
37 arising out of the performance of or failure to perform services while in the course of facilitating
38 or perpetuating gender transition otherwise time-barred under G.S. 1-15 as it existed immediately
39 before the enactment of this act, whether or not such cause of action has been asserted in a
40 pending civil action or appeal. Section 2 of this act becomes effective July 1, 2025. The remainder
41 of this act is effective when it becomes law.
