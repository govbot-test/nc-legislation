6 "Chapter 99C.
7 "Actions Relating to Winter Sports Safety and Accidents.
8 "§ 99C-1. Definitions.
9 When used in this Chapter, unless the context otherwise requires:
10 (1) Competitor. – A skier actually engaged in competition or in practice therefor
11 for competition with the permission of the ski area operator on any slope or
12 trail or portion thereof of a slope or trail designated by the ski area operator
13 for the purpose of competition.
14 (1a) Freestyle terrain. – Constructed and natural features in ski areas intended for
15 winter sports including, but not limited to, sports, including terrain parks and
16 terrain park features such as jumps, rails, fun boxes, half-pipes, quarter-pipes,
17 and freestyle-bump terrain.
18 (1b) Inherent risks of skiing. – Risks or conditions that are part of the sport of
19 skiing, including the following:
20 a. Changing weather conditions.
21 b. Snow conditions as they exist or may change, such as ice, hard pack,
22 powder, packed powder, wind pack, corn, crust, slush, cut-up snow,
23 and machine-made snow.
24 c. Surface or subsurface conditions, such as bare spots, forest growth,
25 rocks, stumps, streambeds, cliffs, extreme terrain, trees, or other
26 natural objects, and collisions with any of the preceding terms.
27 d. Impact with lift towers, signs, posts, fences or enclosures, hydrants,
28 water pipes, or other man-made structures and their components, both
29 padded and unpadded.
30 e. Variations in steepness or terrain, whether natural or as a result of
31 slope design, snowmaking, or grooming operations, including roads,
32 freestyle terrain, jumps, and catwalks or other terrain modifications.
33 f. Collisions with other skiers.
34 g. Failing to properly load or unload a tramway.

5 "§ 99C-4. Competition.
6 The ski area operator shall, prior to the beginning of a competition, allow each competitor a
7 reasonable visual inspection of the course or area where the competition is to be held. The
8 competitor shall be held to assume risk of all course conditions including, but not limited to,
9 including weather and snow conditions, course construction or layout, and obstacles which a
10 visual inspection should have revealed. No liability shall attach to a ski area operator for injury
11 or death of any competitor proximately caused by such an assumed risk.
12 "§ 99C-5. Operation of passenger tramway.
13 The operation of a passenger tramway shall not constitute the operation of a common carrier.
14 "§ 99C-6. Assumption of risk.
15 Skiers are deemed to have knowledge of and to assume the inherent risks of skiing.
16 "§ 99C-7. Liability of ski area operator.
17 No ski area operator who has complied with G.S. 99C-2 shall be liable to any person who
18 voluntarily participates in skiing for any damage or injury to property or persons that arises out
19 of a person's participation in the activity that takes place in a ski area.
20 "§ 99C-8. Misuse of ski passes and season passes; unauthorized access to and use of ski
21 area.
22 (a) Violation. – It shall be unlawful for any person to do any of the following acts:
23 (1) Use a stolen, fictitious, or borrowed ski pass or season pass for the purpose of
24 obtaining services of any kind from a ski area.
25 (2) Ski at a ski area without a valid ski pass or season pass.
26 (3) Provide a stolen or fictitious ski pass or season pass or to loan a ski pass or
27 season pass to another person for the purpose of allowing another to obtain
28 services of any kind from a ski area.
29 (4) Provide a ski pass or season pass to another person who is not the lawful owner
30 and authorized user of the season pass for the purpose of allowing another to
31 obtain services of any kind from a ski area.
32 (5) Access closed lifts, tramways, or ski trails.
33 (6) Access lifts, tramways, or ski trails without a ski pass or season pass.
34 (b) Punishment. – A person who violates this section is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor.
35 "§ 99C-9. Hit and run ski collision.
36 (a) Violation. – It shall be unlawful for a skier to leave the vicinity when the skier knows
37 or should reasonably know that they were involved in a collision with another skier before giving
38 his or her name and current address to an employee or agent of the ski area operator, a member
39 of the ski patrol, or the other skier or person with whom the skier collided, except to seek medical
40 treatment for anyone injured as a result of the collision or when remaining at the scene places the
41 skier or others at significant risk of injury. If a skier does leave the vicinity for a reason permitted
42 by this subsection, the skier must provide the required information to an employee or agent of
43 the ski area operator or a member of the ski patrol as soon as practical after leaving the vicinity.
44 If the other skier involved in the collision is unknown, the skier shall leave the personal
45 identification required by this subsection with the ski area operator or their employee or agent or
46 a member of the ski patrol.
47 (b) Punishment. – A person who violates this section is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor.
48 "§ 99C-10. Civil action.
49 A ski area operator may bring a civil action against a person who misuses a ski pass or season
50 pass or who accesses or uses a ski area without authorization from the ski area operator and shall
51 be entitled to recover an amount equal to the regular cost of a full season pass to the ski area.

14 "§ 99C-11. Limited immunity.
15 A ski area operator's agent or employee who detains or causes the arrest of any person shall
16 not be held civilly liable for detention, malicious prosecution, false imprisonment, or false arrest
17 of the person detained or arrested, if (i) within the premises of the ski area, (ii) in a reasonable
18 manner for a reasonable length of time, and (iii) the agent or employee had probable cause to
19 believe, at the time of the detention, that the person committed a violation under this section. If
20 the person detained is a minor under 18 years of age, the agent or employee shall call or notify,
21 or make a reasonable effort to call or notify, the parent or guardian of the minor during the period
22 of detention. An agent or employee shall not be held civilly liable for failing to notify the parent
23 or guardian of the minor.
24 "§ 99C-12. Medical bills as evidence.
25 Evidence offered to prove past medical expenses shall be limited to evidence of the amounts
26 actually paid to satisfy the bills that have been satisfied, regardless of the source of payment, and
27 evidence of the amounts actually necessary to satisfy the bills that have been incurred but not yet
28 satisfied. This section does not impose upon any party an affirmative duty to seek a reduction in
29 billed charges to which the party is not contractually entitled.
30 "§ 99C-13. Public policy of waivers.
31 It is the public policy of the State of North Carolina that waivers of liability executed in
32 conjunction with winter sports are an express acknowledgement of the inherent risks set out in
33 G.S. 99C-1(1b) enumerated in that document.
34 "§ 99C-14. Ski patrol and volunteers.
35 The provisions of G.S. 95-25.3 (minimum wage), 95-25.4 (overtime), 95-25.5 (youth
36 employment), and 95-25.15(b) (record keeping) do not apply to a bona fide volunteer who
37 volunteers at a ski area. For the purposes of this section, a "bona fide volunteer" is (i) a volunteer
38 ski patroller, competition or special event volunteer, volunteer ski instructor, or volunteer
39 mountain host or (ii) any other person performing volunteer services at a ski area, if the individual
40 receives no compensation for the services other than meals, lodging, or the use of ski area
41 facilities or any combination of meals, lodging, or use of ski area facilities."
42 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 95-25.5, as amended by S.L. 2025-56, reads as rewritten:
43 "§ 95-25.5. Youth employment.
44 …
45 (o) Nothing in this section prohibits a qualified youth under 18 years of age from
46 volunteering at a ski area as defined by G.S. 99C-14. As used in this subsection, the term
47 "qualified youth under 18 years of age" means a bona fide volunteer who receives no
48 compensation but who may be offered and may accept meals, lodging, or use of the ski area
49 facilities or any combination thereof."

20 "§ 99C-8. Misuse of ski passes and season passes; unauthorized access to and use of ski
