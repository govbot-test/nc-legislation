2 AN ACT REVISING THE LAW REGARDING THE MANDATORY USE OF CHILD
3 PASSENGER RESTRAINT SYSTEMS.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 20-137.1 reads as rewritten:
6 "§ 20-137.1. Child restraint systems required.
7 (a) Every driver who is transporting one or more passengers of less than 16 years of age
8 shall have all such passengers properly secured in a child passenger restraint system or seat belt
9 which meets federal standards applicable at the time of its manufacture. For purposes of this
10 section, a "child passenger restraint system" means any device designed to restrain or position a
11 child in a motor vehicle, including a booster seat.
12 (a1) A child less than eight years of age and less than 80 pounds in weight 57 inches in
13 height shall be properly secured in a weight-appropriate height and weight appropriate child
14 passenger restraint system. In vehicles equipped with an active passenger-side front air bag, if
15 the vehicle has a rear seat, a child less than five years of age and less than 40 pounds in weight
16 shall be properly secured in a rear seat, unless the child restraint system is designed for use with
17 air bags. If system as follows:
18 (1) Beginning as a newborn, a child shall be properly secured in a rear facing child
19 passenger restraint system with transition to a forward-facing system
20 according to the manufacturer's instructions related to the child's height and
21 weight requirements for use of the system as indicated by the federally
22 required label on the car seat which states those requirements.
23 (2) The child shall be properly secured in a child passenger restraint system in a
24 rear seat of a vehicle and may only be positioned in the front seat of a vehicle
25 if one of the following exceptions applies:
26 a. The vehicle does not have a rear seat. However, under no
27 circumstances shall a child be placed in the front seat of a vehicle that
28 has an active front airbag using a rear-facing child passenger restraint
29 system. When height and weight requirements for use of a child
30 passenger restraint system require the seat to be rear-facing, the seat
31 must be secured in a vehicle with a rear seat.
32 b. The vehicle is not equipped with an active passenger-side front air bag.
33 c. The child passenger restraint system is designed for use with front air
34 bags when used according to the manufacturer's instructions as
35 indicated by the federally required label on the car seat.
