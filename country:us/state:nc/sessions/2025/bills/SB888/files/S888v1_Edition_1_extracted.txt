2 AN ACT PROHIBITING THE DISSEMINATION OF IMMIGRATION DATA FOR
3 IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT UNLESS CERTAIN CONDITIONS ARE
4 SATISFIED.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6
7 PART I. IMMIGRATION DATA PRIVACY
8 SECTION 1.1. Chapter 64 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new
9 Article to read:
10 "Article 4.
11 "Immigration Data Privacy.
12 "§ 64-70. Release of immigration data.
13 (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, no department, agency, or institution of
14 this State shall disseminate to any federal, state, or local government entity, law enforcement
15 officer, or law enforcement agency any immigration-related data for any purposes related to civil
16 immigration enforcement unless one of the following is satisfied:
17 (1) The subject of the information provides consent.
18 (2) The requesting agency presents a lawful judicial order, judicial subpoena, or
19 judicial warrant.
20 (b) When responding to a lawful judicial order, judicial subpoena, or judicial warrant, the
21 department providing the information shall disclose only those records or information
22 specifically requested.
23 (c) Within three business days of receiving a request for information, the department
24 providing the information shall send a notification to the individual about whom such information
25 was requested that such a request was made and the identity of the entity that made such request.
26 "§ 64-71. Reporting requirements.
27 Any department, agency, or institution of this State that receives a request for immigration
28 data shall document and retain information regarding the request. Information related to the
29 requests shall be reported to the Office of the Attorney General on an annual basis. The report
30 shall include all of the following information:
31 (1) The total number of immigration data requests.
32 (2) The name of the requesting agency or department.
33 (3) The resolution of the request.
34 (4) The type of legal authority presented by the requesting agency or department."

36 PART II. APPROPRIATION
*S888-v-1*

General Assembly Of North Carolina Session 2025
1 SECTION 2.1. Appropriation. – There is appropriated from the General Fund to the
2 Office of State Budget and Management the sum of one million five hundred thousand dollars
3 ($1,500,000) in recurring funds beginning in the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be allocated to State
4 departments and agencies for implementation of this act.
5 SECTION 2.2. Allowable Uses of Funds. – The funds appropriated in Section 2.1
6 of this act may be used for any of the following:
7 (1) Information technology updates to restrict, track, and audit access to
8 identifying information.
9 (2) Development of data governance and privacy compliance systems, including
10 logging and reporting mechanisms.
11 (3) Training of State agency personnel on requirements enacted by this act.
12 (4) Staffing and administrative costs associated with compliance, oversight, and
13 reporting requirements.
14 (5) Preparation and publication of annual reports required under this act.
15 SECTION 2.3. Reporting. – State agencies receiving funds under Section 2.1 of this
16 act shall report to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on General Government no later
17 than April 1, 2027, on the use of funds and implementation progress.
