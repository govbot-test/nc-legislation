Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 2
Commerce and Insurance Committee Substitute Adopted 5/6/25
Short Title: Modify Licensing Real Est. Appraisers. (Public)
Sponsors:
Referred to:
March 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO MODIFY THE LICENSING PROCEDURES OF THE NORTH CAROLINA
3 APPRAISERS ACT.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1.(a) G.S. 93E-1-4 is amended by adding the following new subdivisions
6 to read:
7 "(5a) "Appraiser Qualifications Board" means the board established by the
8 Appraisal Foundation to set nationwide minimum education, experience, and
9 examination standards for real estate appraiser licensure and certification.
10 (5b) "Approved PAREA program" means a course or program developed by a
11 third-party provider that has been approved by the Appraiser Qualifications
12 Board and the Appraisal Board, which meets the standards for Practical
13 Applications of Real Estate Appraisal and satisfies the experience
14 requirements for real estate appraiser licensure or certification as required by
15 this Chapter.
16 …
17 (9a) "Practical Applications of Real Estate Appraisal" means the program
18 framework established by the Appraiser Qualifications Board, providing an
19 alternative to the traditional supervisor and trainee method for satisfying real
20 estate appraisal experience requirements through technology-based training,
21 case studies, and supervision by qualified mentors."
22 SECTION 1.(b) G.S. 93E-1-6 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:
23 "(a1) An applicant for licensure as a residential real estate appraiser may satisfy the
24 requirements of subdivision (6) of subsection (a) of this section by completing either of the
25 following pathways:
26 (1) Traditional Supervisor and Trainee Pathway. – Complete the supervised
27 experience requirements established by the Appraiser Qualifications Board
28 and adopted by the Appraisal Board.
29 (2) PAREA Pathway. – Successfully graduate from an approved Practical
30 Applications of Real Estate Appraisal program and pass the State licensing
31 examination."
32 SECTION 2. The North Carolina Appraisal Board may adopt rules to implement the
33 provisions of this act.
34 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to applications
35 for licensure on or after that date.
