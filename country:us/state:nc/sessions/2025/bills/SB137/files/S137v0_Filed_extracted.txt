1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO RESTORE THE AUTHORITY TO INITIATE DOWN-ZONING IN
3 HENDERSON COUNTY.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 160D-601(d), as amended by Section 3K.1(a) of S.L. 2024-57,
6 reads as rewritten:
7 "(d) Down-Zoning. – No amendment to zoning regulations or a zoning map that
8 down-zones property shall be initiated, enacted, or enforced initiated nor is it enforceable without
9 the written consent of all property owners whose property is the subject of the down-zoning
10 amendment. amendment, unless the down-zoning amendment is initiated by the local
11 government and is for the purpose of farmland preservation. For purposes of this section,
12 "down-zoning" means a zoning ordinance that affects an area of land in one of the following
13 ways:
14 (1) By decreasing the development density of the land to be less dense than was
15 allowed under its previous usage.
16 (2) By reducing the permitted uses of the land that are specified in a zoning
17 ordinance or land development regulation to fewer uses than were allowed
18 under its previous usage.
19 (3) By creating any type of nonconformity on land not in a residential zoning
20 district, including a nonconforming use, nonconforming lot, nonconforming
21 structure, nonconforming improvement, or nonconforming site element."
22 SECTION 2.(a) This act applies only to Henderson County and the portions of the
23 Town of Fletcher, the Town of Mills River, the Village of Flat Rock, the Town of Laurel Park,
24 and the City of Saluda that are located in Henderson County.
25 SECTION 2.(b) This act is effective when it becomes law and applies retroactively
26 to January 31, 2025.
