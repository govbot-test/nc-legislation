14 PART IV. NONDISCRIMINATORY ADMISSIONS EVALUATIONS AND MILITARY
15 DEFERMENT
16 SECTION 4.(a) Article 1 of Chapter 116 of the General Statutes is amended by
17 adding a new Part to read:
18 "Part 8A. Military Admissions and Deferment.
19 "§ 116-44.9A. Definitions.
20 For purposes of this Part, the following definitions shall apply:
21 (1) Applicant. – An individual who has applied for admission to a constituent
22 institution of The University of North Carolina.
23 (2) Armed Forces. – The United States Air Force, Army, Coast Guard, Marine
24 Corps, Navy, Space Force, and North Carolina National Guard.
25 (3) Constituent institution of The University of North Carolina. – A constituent
26 institution of higher education of The University of North Carolina, as defined
27 in G.S. 116-2(4), excluding the constituent high school, the North Carolina
28 School of Science and Mathematics.
29 (4) Reserve Armed Forces. – The reserve components of any of the Armed
30 Forces.
31 (5) Uniformed service. – Any of the following:
32 a. Armed Forces.
33 b. Reserve Armed Forces.
34 c. The Merchant Marine.
35 "§ 116-44.9B. Nondiscriminatory evaluations of applications.
36 A constituent institution of The University of North Carolina shall not deny admission to any
37 applicant solely on the basis of the applicant's indication that the applicant is serving in the
38 uniformed service or that the applicant intends to serve in the uniformed service.
39 "§ 116-44.9C. Deferment.
40 (a) Deferment Requests. – The Board of Governors of The University of North Carolina
41 shall adopt a policy requiring constituent institutions to provide for enrollment deferment for
42 members of the uniformed services and spouses of members of the uniformed services if the
43 deferment is requested at least 30 days prior to enrollment in a constituent institution.
44 (b) Deferment Periods. – Members and spouses of members of the reserve Armed Forces
45 shall be granted deferments of at least two years after entry into the reserve Armed Forces. All
46 other members and spouses of members of the uniformed services shall be granted deferments
47 of at least five years after entry into the uniformed services."
48 SECTION 4.(b) This section is effective when it becomes law and applies beginning
49 with the 2025-2026 academic year.
