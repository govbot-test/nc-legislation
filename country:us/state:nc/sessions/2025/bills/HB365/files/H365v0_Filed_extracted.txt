11 PART I. DIRECT THE STATE BOARD OF COMMUNITY COLLEGES TO REVISE

13 ENROLLMENT INCREASE RESERVE
14 SECTION 1.(a) The following session laws are repealed:
15 (1) Subsection (b) of Section 8.3 of S.L. 2011-145.
16 (2) Subsection (a) of Section 10.4 of S.L. 2013-360.
17 SECTION 1.(b) The State Board of Community Colleges shall revise its funding
18 formula for community colleges and allocate funds under that revised formula, beginning with
19 the 2025-2026 fiscal year, according to the following minimum criteria:
20 (1) Each community college shall continue to receive a base allocation of funds.
21 (2) In addition to the base allocation of funds, funds shall be provided to
22 community colleges based on the number of full-time equivalent (FTE)
23 students enrolled in the following:
24 a. Curriculum, workforce continuing education, and Basic Skills courses.
25 b. Courses and programming conducted under the Customized Training
26 Program and the Small Business Center Network.
27 (3) Community colleges shall calculate FTE enrollment as the higher of the
28 current year's total enrollment or the average enrollment of the last two
29 academic years.
30 (4) Funds allocated pursuant to subdivision (2) of this subsection shall be
31 weighted based on the workforce sector of each course, as determined by the
32 State Board. In making its determinations, the State Board shall consider
33 salary data and labor market demand for the applicable workforce sector.

17 adding the following new section to read:
18 "§ 115D-31.4. Enrollment Increase Reserve.
19 (a) There is established the Enrollment Increase Reserve (Reserve) to be administered by
20 the State Board of Community Colleges. The purpose of the Reserve is to allow the State Board
21 to provide funds to community colleges to account for enrollment increases beyond budgeted
22 enrollment levels.
23 (b) Monies in the Reserve shall consist of funds appropriated by the General Assembly
24 in the Current Operations Appropriations Act for a fiscal year. The State Board shall include in
25 its annual enrollment request the appropriation to the Reserve that is needed to fund enrollment
26 increases in the next fiscal year.
27 (c) The State Board may allocate monies from the Reserve to a community college with
28 an eligible increase in full-time equivalent (FTE) enrollment according to a formula adopted by
29 the State Board. An eligible increase in FTE enrollment is either of the following:
30 (1) An increase in FTE enrollment of more than five percent (5%) of the budgeted
31 enrollment level in any of the following course categories:
32 a. Curriculum.
33 b. Workforce continuing education.
34 c. Basic Skills.
35 (2) An increase in total FTE enrollment of more than 325 students.
36 (d) Monies in the Reserve shall not revert at the end of each fiscal year but shall remain
37 available until expended for the purposes of this section."
38 SECTION 1.(g) There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Community
39 Colleges System Office the sum of ninety-four million dollars ($94,000,000) in recurring funds
40 for the 2025-2026 fiscal year to implement subsections (a) through (d) of this section. There is

43 fiscal year for the purposes described in G.S. 115D-31.4, as enacted by this section.

7 PART III. DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC INSTRUCTION GRANT PROGRAMS
8
9 GRANTS FOR STEM EDUCATION
10 SECTION 3.1. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
11 Public Instruction the sum of thirty million dollars ($30,000,000) in recurring funds for the
12 2025-2026 fiscal year to create a grant program in collaboration with the Community Colleges
13 System Office for public school units to promote science, technology, engineering, and math
14 (STEM) education. Grants shall be used for professional development, classroom technology,
15 lab upgrades, or coding programs for teachers teaching STEM courses. The Department shall
16 make an application for grants available to public school units prior to August 1 of each school
17 year. Public school units applying for grants shall submit their application to the Department by
18 October 1 of each school year. The Department shall make determinations on grant recipients by
19 November 1 of each school year and disburse grants funds within 60 days of making the
20 determination.
21
22 GRANTS TO EXPAND CTE OPPORTUNITIES
23 SECTION 3.2. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
24 Public Instruction the sum of twenty million dollars ($20,000,000) in recurring funds for the
25 2025-2026 fiscal year to create a grant program in collaboration with the Community Colleges
26 System Office for public school units to expand career and technical education course offerings,
27 dual enrollment opportunities, and certification pathways for students in grades nine through 12.
28 The Department shall make an application for grants available to public school units prior to
29 August 1 of each school year. Public school units applying for grants shall submit their
30 application to the Department by October 1 of each school year. The Department shall make
31 determinations on grant recipients by November 1 of each school year and disburse grants funds
32 within 60 days of making the determination.
33
34 INDUSTRY-SCHOOL PARTNERSHIP GRANTS
35 SECTION 3.3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Department of
36 Public Instruction the sum of twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000) in recurring funds for the
37 2025-2026 fiscal year to create a grant program in collaboration with the Community Colleges
38 System Office for public school units to create industry-school partnerships to facilitate
39 internships, apprenticeships, and project-based learning opportunities. The Department shall
40 make an application for grants available to public school units prior to August 1 of each school
41 year. The application shall include at least one industry partner that the public school unit intends
42 to collaborate with to achieve the goals of the program enumerated in this section. Public school
43 units applying for grants shall submit their application to the Department by October 1 of each
44 school year. The Department shall make determinations on grant recipients by November 1 of
45 each school year and disburse grants funds within 60 days of making the determination.
