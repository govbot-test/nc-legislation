5 SECTION 1.1.(a) The General Statutes are amended by adding a new Chapter to
6 read:
7 "Chapter 125A.
8 "Free to Learn – Library Bill of Rights.
9 "Article 1.
10 "General Provisions.
11 "§ 125A-1. Short title.
12 This Chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Free to Learn – Library Bill of Rights
13 Act."
14 "§ 125A-2. Purpose.
15 The purpose of this Chapter is to establish and protect the rights of North Carolina residents
16 to access information, preserve intellectual freedom, maintain privacy, and participate in library
17 services without discrimination or undue restriction.
18 "§ 125A-3. Definitions.
19 The following definitions apply in this Chapter:
20 (1) City. – As defined in G.S. 160A-1.
21 (2) Interlocal agreement. – An agreement between two or more governmental
22 units to jointly provide library services pursuant to G.S. 160A-461.
23 (3) Library. – As defined in G.S. 125-18.
24 (4) Library materials. – Books, periodicals, newspapers, audio and video
25 recordings, online resources, databases, and other information resources made
26 available by a library, and as defined in G.S. 153A-263.
27 (5) Library programs. – Educational, cultural, recreational, or informational
28 events, activities, or presentations sponsored or cosponsored by a library.
29 (6) Library records. – As defined in G.S. 125-18.
30 (7) Library services. – Programs, activities, and functions provided by a library
31 to its users, consistent with the "free library services" provisions in
32 G.S. 125-14 and G.S. 153A-264.
33 (8) Regional library system. – A library system established pursuant to G.S. 125-8
34 that serves multiple counties or cities through a cooperative arrangement.
35 "§ 125A-4. Scope; application.

10 (a) When libraries operate under interlocal agreements pursuant to G.S. 160A-461 or as
11 part of regional library systems established under G.S. 125-8, all participating governmental units
12 shall share responsibility for ensuring compliance with this Chapter.
13 (b) Regional library systems shall document how each participating library is

16 (c) The State Library shall provide model language for interlocal agreements and regional
17 library system agreements to ensure compliance with this Chapter.
18 "Article 2.
19 "Rights of Library Users.
20 "§ 125A-6. Right to access information.
21 (a) Every person has the right to access information and ideas through library materials
22 regardless of content, approach, format, or viewpoint of the material.
23 (b) No government entity or public official shall prohibit or restrict access to library
24 materials based solely on their content or viewpoint.
25 (c) This section does not limit the authority of libraries and library boards to develop
26 collections in accordance with professional standards and community needs, as provided in
27 G.S. 153A-266.
28 (d) Libraries may implement reasonable policies concerning access to age-appropriate
29 materials for minors, provided such policies do not unduly restrict access to information and are
30 consistent with professional library standards.
31 "§ 125A-7. Right to access.
32 Every person has the right to fair access to library services, materials, and programs without
33 discrimination based on age, race, national origin, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability,
34 socioeconomic status, or viewpoint. This right shall be construed in a manner consistent with the
35 "free library service" provisions established in G.S. 125-14 and G.S. 153A-264.
36 "§ 125A-8. Right to intellectual freedom.
37 (a) Every person has the right to pursue intellectual inquiry through libraries without
38 surveillance, interference, or intimidation.
39 (b) No person shall interfere with or intimidate any person in the exercise of their
40 intellectual pursuits within a library.
41 (c) Libraries shall uphold principles of intellectual freedom in accordance with national
42 professional library standards.
43 "§ 125A-9. Right to privacy.
44 (a) Every person has the right to privacy regarding their use of library materials and
45 services, consistent with G.S. 125-19.
46 (b) Library records that identify a person as having requested or obtained specific library
47 materials or services shall be confidential.
48 (c) A library shall not disclose library records except when necessary for the reasonable
49 operation of the library, upon written consent of the user, or pursuant to subpoena, court order,
50 or if otherwise required by law.

4 "§ 125A-10. Protection from retaliation.
5 (a) No library employee, volunteer, board member, or contractor shall be discharged,
6 threatened, or otherwise discriminated against regarding compensation, terms, conditions,
7 location, or privileges of employment or service because the person took any of the following
8 actions:
9 (1) Reported or was about to report a violation of this Chapter.
10 (2) Testified or was about to testify in a proceeding under this Chapter.
11 (3) Refused to participate in a violation of this Chapter.
12 (4) Provided information to the State Library, Attorney General, or any law
13 enforcement agency regarding a violation of this Chapter.
14 (b) In addition to any other remedies provided by law, a person who alleges a violation
15 of this section may bring a civil action and may be entitled to one or more of the following:
16 (1) Reinstatement to the same position with the same seniority status, benefits,
17 and conditions of employment.
18 (2) Compensation for lost wages, benefits, and other remuneration.
19 (3) Payment of reasonable costs and attorneys' fees.
20 (4) An injunction against continued violations.
21 (5) Other appropriate relief necessary to make the person whole.
22 (c) Libraries shall post notice of the protections provided by this section in a conspicuous
23 location accessible to all employees and volunteers.
24 "§ 125A-11. Online privacy protections.
25 (a) Library users have the right to privacy when accessing digital resources, online
26 services, and internet connections provided by libraries.
27 (b) Libraries shall collect only the minimum amount of personally identifiable
28 information necessary to provide services to users and shall do all of the following:
29 (1) Limit retention of personally identifiable information to the time needed for
30 operational purposes.
31 (2) Anonymize usage data retained for statistical purposes.
32 (3) Delete browser history, search records, and other online activity data when no
33 longer needed for operational purposes and in no case later than 60 days after
34 collection unless retention is required by law.
35 (4) Secure all personally identifiable information against unauthorized access,
36 disclosure, modification, or destruction.
37 (c) Libraries shall develop and prominently display a privacy policy that discloses all of
38 the following information:
39 (1) What personally identifiable information is collected.
40 (2) How the information is used.
41 (3) How long the information is retained.
42 (4) With whom the information may be shared.
43 (5) How users can access, correct, or delete their information.
44 (d) When contracting with third-party service providers, libraries shall:
45 (1) Include provisions requiring protection of user privacy.
46 (2) Prohibit commercial use of user data except as necessary to provide the
47 contracted service.
48 (3) Require appropriate security measures.
49 (4) Ensure compliance with this Chapter.
50 (e) Libraries shall implement reasonable measures to protect the privacy of users of
51 public access computers, including regular clearing of browser history, cookies, and cached

3 "§ 125A-12. Library programs and events; conduct policies.
4 (a) Every person has the right to access library programs, events, and activities offered
5 by libraries consistent with the library's mission and resources.
6 (b) No governing board, government entity, or public official may prohibit or cancel
7 library programs based solely on the content or viewpoint of the program.
8 (c) When libraries provide meeting spaces for public use, they shall establish
9 viewpoint-neutral policies governing the use of these spaces in accordance with constitutional
10 principles. Library staff shall receive training about identifying the constitutional boundaries
11 between protected expression and unprotected conduct.
12 (d) Libraries shall implement conduct policies for program attendees and presenters that,
13 at the minimum, do all of the following:
14 (1) Prohibit any person from engaging in conduct that disrupts a program or
15 interferes with the ability of others to participate.
16 (2) Prevent any person from engaging in harassment, intimidation, or threats
17 against program presenters, attendees, or library staff because of race, color,
18 religion, nationality, country of origin, sex, disability, age, or other status
19 protected by law.
20 (3) Prohibit any person from threatening violence against individuals or groups,
21 using abusive language that would provoke immediate retaliation in a
22 face-to-face confrontation, or inciting imminent unlawful action.
23 (4) Establish reasonable procedures for audience participation that require orderly
24 conduct while ensuring that the procedures are not used to suppress lawful
25 expression of viewpoints.
26 (5) Ensure that restrictions on conduct are enforced uniformly without regard to
27 the content of the speech or the viewpoint expressed.
28 (6) Require written documentation of the specific conduct warranting any
29 intervention, enforce consequences for violations, and offer an informal fair
30 process for appeals.
31 Libraries shall provide public notice of these conduct policies.
32 "Article 3.
33 "Administration and Enforcement.
34 "§ 125A-13. Policies; notice; training.
35 (a) Libraries shall develop policies that implement and protect the rights established in
36 this Chapter and make information about those rights readily available to library users.
37 (b) Libraries shall provide adequate training to staff regarding the rights established in
38 this Chapter and procedures for protecting these rights.
39 "§ 125A-14. State Library; operational coordination; Attorney General resources.
40 (a) The State Library, established under G.S. 125-1, shall develop guidelines to assist
41 libraries in implementing this Chapter in a manner consistent with existing library laws.
42 (b) For libraries operated by a city, as defined in G.S. 160A-1, the governing body of the
43 city shall adopt policies that protect and implement the rights established in this Chapter.
44 (c) The Attorney General shall establish a Library Rights Legal Resource Center to
45 provide information, training, and consultation to libraries, library boards, and the State Library
46 regarding the implementation and enforcement of this Chapter.
47 "§ 125A-15. Enforcement of protections.
48 (a) Any person who believes their rights under this Chapter have been violated may file
49 a complaint with the governing body of the library where the alleged violation occurred. If a
50 complaint is not resolved satisfactorily at the local level, the person may appeal to the State
51 Library, which shall investigate and make recommendations for resolution. If the matter remains

1 unresolved after the State Library's review, the person may submit the complaint to the Attorney
2 General for further review and possible enforcement action.
3 (b) Any person aggrieved by a violation of this Chapter may bring a civil action in an
4 appropriate North Carolina court for declaratory and injunctive relief. The court may allow a
5 prevailing plaintiff reasonable attorneys' fees and costs in any action brought under this section.
6 (c) The Attorney General shall develop legal guidelines to assist libraries in
7 implementing this Chapter in compliance with federal and State constitutional requirements. At
8 a minimum, these guidelines shall address (i) First Amendment considerations in materials
9 selection and access, (ii) privacy and confidentiality requirements, (iii) due process in materials
10 challenges, and (iv) antidiscrimination provisions in library services.
11 (d) In an action brought by the Attorney General, the court may assess a civil penalty
12 against any government entity, public official, or other person or entity that willfully violates this
13 Chapter as follows:
14 (1) For a first violation, a penalty not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000);
15 (2) For a second violation, a penalty not to exceed two thousand five hundred
16 dollars ($2,500); and
17 (3) For a third or subsequent violation, a penalty not to exceed five thousand
18 dollars ($5,000).
19 In determining the amount of the penalty, the court shall consider the seriousness of the
20 violation, the duration of the violation, any good-faith efforts to comply with this Chapter, and
21 the appropriateness of the penalty to the size and resources of the violator. The clear proceeds of
22 civil penalties provided for in this section shall be remitted to the Civil Penalty and Forfeiture
23 Fund in accordance with G.S. 115C-457.2.
24 "§ 125A-16. Civil actions.
25 (a) The Attorney General may bring a civil action to enforce compliance with this
26 Chapter. In any such action, the Attorney General may request any relief authorized by this
27 section.
28 (b) The Attorney General shall represent the State Library in any litigation arising under
29 this Chapter.
30 (c) The Attorney General may intervene in any civil action brought by an individual
31 under this Chapter if the Attorney General certifies that the case is of general public importance.
32 (d) Libraries shall report to the Attorney General any formal challenge to library
33 materials within 30 days of receiving such challenge. The report shall include:
34 (1) The title and description of the challenged material.
35 (2) The basis of the challenge.
36 (3) The policy followed to address the challenge.
37 (4) The resolution or status of the challenge.
38 (5) Any other information required by the Attorney General.
39 (e) The Attorney General shall publish an annual report on the implementation of this
40 Chapter, including patterns of challenges to library materials, enforcement actions taken,
41 complaints resolved, and recommendations for legislative or policy changes.
42 "§ 125A-17. Public education campaign.
43 The State Library shall develop and implement a statewide public education campaign to
44 inform North Carolina residents about their rights under this Chapter. The State Library shall
45 annually evaluate the effectiveness of the campaign and adjust strategies as needed to maximize
46 public awareness. The State Library shall collaborate with the Department of Justice, the
47 Department of Public Instruction, and other relevant agencies to develop and distribute
48 educational materials.
