Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
S 1
Short Title: Study Automation and the Workforce. (Public)
Sponsors: Senators Salvador, Murdock, and Theodros (Primary Sponsors).
Referred to: Rules and Operations of the Senate
March 26, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ESTABLISH THE STUDY COMMITTEE ON AUTOMATION AND THE
3 WORKFORCE.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. The General Assembly finds that:
6 (1) Automation during the twentieth century resulted in new job opportunities for
7 some, but it also rendered other jobs obsolete, affecting many low-income
8 workers.
9 (2) Continued automation into the twenty-first century has resulted in the further
10 diminishment of employment opportunities for some workers, especially
11 workers who lack computer skills or are disadvantaged by social inequities or
12 the digital divide.
13 (3) A comprehensive review of the current and future effects of automation on
14 the State's workforce will provide government officials, and education and
15 business leaders, with the information and insight necessary to guide the State
16 toward mitigating negative effects of automation on workers. This is
17 especially true in the case of low-income and minority workers.
18 SECTION 2.(a) Committee Established. – There is established the Study Committee
19 on Automation and the Workforce (Committee). The Committee shall include 11 voting
20 members, as follows:
21 (1) Two persons representing labor organizations or nonprofit organizations
22 engaged in workforce development efforts in this State, appointed by the
23 Governor.
24 (2) Two persons representing employers or trade organizations focused on the
25 automated workplace, appointed by the Governor.
26 (3) Two persons who are members of the Senate at the time of appointment, at
27 least one of whom represents the minority party, appointed by the President
28 Pro Tempore of the Senate.
29 (4) Two persons who are members of the House of Representatives at the time of
30 appointment, at least one of whom represents the minority party, appointed by
31 the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
32 (5) Three persons elected to membership by the members of the Committee
33 designated in subdivisions (1) through (4) of this subsection.
34 SECTION 2.(b) Nonvoting Members. – The following individuals or the
35 individual's designee shall serve as a nonvoting ex officio member of the Committee:
36 (1) The Commissioner of Agriculture.

1 (2) The Commissioner of Labor.
2 (3) The Superintendent of Public Instruction.
3 (4) The Secretary of Commerce.
4 (5) The State Chief Information Officer.
5 (6) The President of the North Carolina Community College System.
6 (7) The President of The University of North Carolina.
7 SECTION 2.(c) Duties. – The Committee shall serve as a study and advisory
8 committee on the effects of automation on the State's workforce, with a special emphasis on
9 low-income and minority workers. The Committee shall review all of the following issues:
10 (1) The current and potential future impact of the following automation
11 technologies on the State's low-income workforce:
12 a. Artificial intelligence.
13 b. Industrial robotics.
14 c. Technologies that help customers serve themselves without the
15 assistance of a human worker.
16 d. Word processing software, spreadsheet software, and other software
17 or cloud computing services that reduce or eliminate the need for a
18 human worker.
19 (2) How factors such as educational opportunity gaps, geographical location,
20 urban and rural disparities, social inequities, and income levels or any other
21 relevant factors affect the displacement of low-income workers by automation
22 particularly as they affect workers facing systemic barriers to economic
23 opportunity, including but not limited to low-income, rural, and historically
24 marginalized populations.
25 (3) The education, training, and retraining requirements, and other skills
26 development, that will be necessary to help low-income workers adapt to
27 automation through the early, middle, and later years of employment to avoid
28 or defer job displacement, in collaboration with institutions of higher
29 education, community colleges, vocational training centers, and private-sector
30 partners.
31 (4) Recommend strategies for mitigating economic disruption and identifying
32 new economic opportunities created by automation and artificial intelligence.
33 The committee's recommendations shall include consideration of workforce
34 impacts and potential growth within emerging industries projected to expand
35 alongside these technologies, including clean energy, advanced
36 manufacturing, technology services, and healthcare, where appropriate.
37 The Committee may review any other pertinent matter relevant to the effects of
38 automation on the State's workforce.
39 SECTION 2.(d) Terms. – Except for the persons elected by the Committee, all
40 appointing authorities shall make appointments to the Committee by October 1, 2025, and the
41 terms of all members shall commence on October 1, 2025. The election of members shall be
42 conducted during the initial organizational meeting of the Committee, with the terms of the
43 elected members deemed to begin on October 1, 2025.
44 SECTION 2.(e) Organization/Administration. – The President Pro Tempore of the
45 Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall each designate a cochair from
46 among the voting members of the Committee. The cochair appointed by the President Pro
47 Tempore of the Senate shall preside over the Committee during the odd-numbered year, and the
48 cochair appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall preside in the
49 even-numbered year. A cochair may preside at any time during the absence of the presiding
50 cochair or upon the presiding cochair's designation. The Committee shall meet upon the call of
51 its cochairs.

1 All voting members shall be appointed for a term of two years. Voting members may
2 be reappointed to successive terms. A vacancy on the Committee shall be filled by the original
3 appointing authority using the criteria for the prior appointment. Any appointment to fill a
4 vacancy on the Committee created by the resignation, dismissal, death, disability, or
5 disqualification of a member shall be for the balance of the unexpired term.
6 The Governor may remove any member of the Committee for misfeasance,
7 malfeasance, or nonfeasance, pursuant to the provisions of G.S. 143B-13.
8 Members of the Committee shall receive per diem and necessary travel and
9 subsistence expenses in accordance with G.S. 120-3.1, 138-5, and 138-6, as applicable. The
10 Committee may meet in the Legislative Building or the Legislative Office Building. A majority
11 of the voting members shall constitute a quorum.
12 The Legislative Services Officer shall assign professional and clerical staff and
13 provide other necessary resources to assist the Committee in its work.
14 SECTION 2.(e1) The Committee shall provide opportunities for public input and
15 engagement through public hearings, listening sessions, or written submissions.
16 SECTION 2.(f) Reporting. – The Committee shall submit its initial written report to
17 the Governor and to the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations prior to the
18 convening of the 2027 General Assembly, and thereafter it shall report biennially. The report
19 shall contain a detailed summary of conclusions and recommendations for each issue reviewed
20 under this act. Any advice recommending a change to a policy, rule, or law shall be accompanied
21 by a specific policy or legislative proposal and the estimated cost of implementing the change.
22 SECTION 3. Effective Date. – This act is effective when it becomes law.
