14 "§ 75-70. Title; definitions.
15 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Social Media Control in
16 Information Technology Act."
17 (b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this Article:
18 (1) Accessible mechanism. – A user-friendly, clear, easy-to-use, readily
19 available, and technologically feasible method that allows individuals to
20 exercise their data privacy rights without undue burden. The mechanism must
21 be designed to accommodate diverse user needs, including those with
22 disabilities, and should be available across commonly used platforms. The
23 mechanism should provide clear instructions, function without excessive
24 complexity, and be free of unreasonable barriers such as length procedures,
25 hidden settings, or excessive delays.
26 (2) Algorithmic recommendation system. – A computational process that uses
27 machine learning, natural language processing, artificial intelligence
28 techniques, generative artificial intelligence, or other computational
29 processing techniques that makes a decision or facilitates human decision
30 making with respect to user-related data to rank, order, promote, recommend,
31 suggest, amplify, or similarly determine the delivery or display of information
32 to an individual.
33 (3) Collects, collected, or collection. – Buying, renting, gathering, obtaining,
34 receiving, or accessing any personal information pertaining to a user by any

11 "§ 75-71. User data privacy; targeting minors prohibited; registry.
12 (a) Privacy Requirements. – The General Assembly finds that unhealthy social media use
13 has been linked to depression, anxiety, eating disorders, and suicidal ideation, especially among
14 young people. Exploitation of user data can result in users being targeted in ways that increase
15 unhealthy social media use. It is the policy of this State that user data shall be respected by
16 platforms. Special protections are warranted for users who are minors. Therefore, the operator of
17 a social media platform shall comply with all of the following requirements for platform users:
18 (1) The platform must specifically and clearly inform users in the following ways:
19 a. A disclosure in a clear, easy-to-read, and accessible format when a user
20 first initializes their use of a platform for the first time, or after a period
21 of inactivity greater than or equal to six months, about how the
22 platform collects personal information, what personal information the
23 platform collects, how the personal information is used by the platform
24 for every use case, and how the user can exercise their rights and
25 choices on the platform. This disclosure must be provided in no more
26 than 500 words, and the platform must obtain a user's consent before
27 the platform collects any user-related data on the user.
28 b. A disclosure in a clear, easy-to-read, and accessible format that details
29 (i) the categories of information the platform has collected about the
30 user, (ii) the categories of sources from which the information is
31 collected, (iii) the business or commercial purpose for collecting,
32 selling, or sharing personal information, (iv) the categories of third
33 parties to whom the business discloses personal information, and (v)
34 the specific pieces of personal information it has collected about that
35 user. Such information must be available upon receipt of a verifiable
36 consumer request made through an accessible mechanism on the
37 platform.
38 (2) Personal information may be used in algorithmic recommendations only when
39 both of the following requirements are met:
40 a. The platform reasonably determines the user is not a minor from
41 personal information collected by and available to the covered
42 platform in its ordinary course of business.
43 b. The user has been notified and expressly consents to the use of their
44 own data in this manner by consenting in an opt-in mechanism.
45 (3) Through an accessible mechanism, users must be given the capacity to alter,
46 change, and delete what categories of personal information are used in a
47 platform's algorithmic recommendation system or systems. This selection
48 shall be modifiable at any time. If a user indicates that they intend a certain
49 category of personal information not to be used in an algorithmic
50 recommendation system, then the platform must not include said category or
51 categories within an algorithmic recommendation system. A covered platform

30 "§ 75-72. Design features and digital rights of users.
31 (a) Protective Default Settings for Minors. – A covered platform shall configure all
32 privacy settings provided to any user by the online service, product, or feature be both available
33 to minors and, by default, set to preferences that offer the highest level of privacy, unless the
34 business can demonstrate a compelling reason that a different setting is in the best interest of
35 minors. These settings must include all of the following:
36 (1) Notifications must be turned off by default.
37 (2) The visibility of reaction or interaction counts on all content, including content
38 generated by a minor and content seen by a minor generated from others, must
39 be turned off by default.
40 (3) The ability of other users, not added by the user to a list of approved contacts,
41 to communicate with the minor must be turned off by default.
42 (4) The ability of other users, whether registered or not, and not added by the user
43 to a list of approved contacts, to view the minor's user-related data collected
44 by or shared on the platform must be disabled by default.
45 (5) The ability of other users to see the geolocation of a minor must be disabled
46 by default.
47 (6) Features that increase, sustain, or extend the use of the covered platform by a
48 minor, such as automatic playing of media and rewards for time spent on the
49 platform, must be disabled by default.
50 (b) Rights to Change and Delete Data. – A covered platform shall provide users with both
51 of the following:

20 (c) Digital Rights of the User. – All of the following rights belong to every minor utilizing
21 covered platforms:
22 (1) Right to protection from manipulative design. – Every minor has the right to
23 be protected from manipulative design techniques which exploit
24 psychological vulnerability or have been shown by the preponderance of the
25 evidence to create addiction or dependency.
26 (2) Right to transparency. – Every minor has the right to understand the nature of

35 "§ 75-73. Investigation; enforcement; private right of action.
36 (a) Violations. – Effective January 1, 2026, a platform's violation of this Article is an
37 unfair or deceptive act or practice under G.S. 75-1.1.
38 (b) Investigations. – The Attorney General shall monitor social media platforms for
39 compliance with this Article.
40 (c) Complaints. – A platform user may make a complaint to the Attorney General
41 alleging that a social media platform has failed to comply with the requirements of this Article.
42 The Attorney General may bring a civil action in any case in which the Attorney General has
43 reason to believe that the interest of the residents of this State has been or is threatened due to
44 noncompliance with this Article.
45 (d) Private Right of Action. – Minors can file suit if they are affected by any covered
46 platform found to be in violation of this Article through mechanisms involved in parens patriae
47 jurisdiction by the following:
48 (1) Civil suit brought through private action attorneys.
49 (2) Relief. – In a civil action brought under subsection (c) of this section or this
50 subsection in which a plaintiff prevails, the court may award the plaintiff any
51 one or more of the following:

6 "§ 75-74. North Carolina Data Privacy Task Force.
7 (a) There is created the North Carolina Data Privacy Task Force (Task Force) within the
8 Department of Justice for budgetary purposes only.

10 subdivisions (1) through (6) of this subsection may designate representatives from their particular
11 departments, divisions, or offices to represent them on the Task Force. In making appointments
12 or designating representatives, appointing authorities and ex officio members shall use best
13 efforts to select members or representatives with sufficient knowledge and experience to
14 effectively contribute to the issues examined by the Task Force and, to the extent possible, to
15 reflect the geographical, political, gender, and racial diversity of this State. The members shall
16 be as follows:
17 (1) The Attorney General.
18 (2) The State Chief Information Officer.
19 (3) The Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services.
20 (4) The Director of the State Bureau of Investigation.

22 Health and Human Services.

26 recommendation of the President of the organization.
