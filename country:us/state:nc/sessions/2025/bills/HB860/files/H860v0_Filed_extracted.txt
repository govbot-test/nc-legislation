14 "§ 75-70. Title; definitions.
15 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Social Media Control in
16 Information Technology Act."
17 (b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this Article:
18 (1) Accessible mechanism. – A user-friendly, clear, easy-to-use, readily
19 available, and technologically feasible method that allows individuals to
20 exercise their data privacy rights without undue burden. The mechanism must
21 be designed to accommodate diverse user needs, including those with
22 disabilities, and should be available across commonly used platforms. The
23 mechanism should provide clear instructions, function without excessive
24 complexity, and be free of unreasonable barriers such as length procedures,
25 hidden settings, or excessive delays.
26 (2) Algorithmic recommendation system. – A computational process that uses
27 machine learning, natural language processing, artificial intelligence
28 techniques, generative artificial intelligence, or other computational
29 processing techniques that makes a decision or facilitates human decision
30 making with respect to user-related data to rank, order, promote, recommend,
31 suggest, amplify, or similarly determine the delivery or display of information
32 to an individual.
33 (3) Collects, collected, or collection. – Buying, renting, gathering, obtaining,
34 receiving, or accessing any personal information pertaining to a user by any
35 means. This includes receiving information from the consumer, either actively
36 or passively, or by observing the consumer's behavior.

8 "§ 75-71. User data privacy; targeting minors prohibited; registry.
9 (a) Privacy Requirements. – The General Assembly finds that unhealthy social media use
10 has been linked to depression, anxiety, eating disorders, and suicidal ideation, especially among
11 young people. Exploitation of user data can result in users being targeted in ways that increase
12 unhealthy social media use. It is the policy of this State that user data shall be respected by
13 platforms. Special protections are warranted for users who are minors. Therefore, the operator of
14 a social media platform shall comply with all of the following requirements for platform users:
15 (1) The platform must specifically and clearly inform users in the following ways:
16 a. A disclosure in a clear, easy-to-read, and accessible format when a user
17 first initializes their use of a platform for the first time, or after a period
18 of inactivity greater than or equal to six months, about how the
19 platform collects personal information, what personal information the
20 platform collects, how the personal information is used by the platform
21 for every use case, and how the user can exercise their rights and
22 choices on the platform. This disclosure must be provided in no more
23 than 500 words, and the platform must obtain a user's consent before
24 the platform collects any user-related data on the user.
25 b. A disclosure in a clear, easy-to-read, and accessible format that details
26 (i) the categories of information the platform has collected about the
27 user, (ii) the categories of sources from which the information is
28 collected, (iii) the business or commercial purpose for collecting,
29 selling, or sharing personal information, (iv) the categories of third
30 parties to whom the business discloses personal information, and (v)
31 the specific pieces of personal information it has collected about that
32 user. Such information must be available upon receipt of a verifiable
33 consumer request made through an accessible mechanism on the
34 platform.
35 (2) Personal information may be used in algorithmic recommendations only when
36 both of the following requirements are met:
37 a. The platform reasonably determines the user is not a minor from
38 personal information collected by and available to the covered
39 platform in its ordinary course of business.
40 b. The user has been notified and expressly consents to the use of their
41 own data in this manner by consenting in an opt-in mechanism.
42 (3) Through an accessible mechanism, users must be given the capacity to alter,
43 change, and delete what categories of personal information are used in a
44 platform's algorithmic recommendation system or systems. This selection
45 shall be modifiable at any time. If a user indicates that they intend a certain
46 category of personal information not to be used in an algorithmic
47 recommendation system, then the platform must not include said category or
48 categories within an algorithmic recommendation system. A covered platform
49 shall not discriminate against a user because the user exercised any of the
50 rights under this Article in the provision of functionality or features of the
51 covered platform, unless the use of user-related data in an algorithmic

3 (b) Targeting Minors Prohibited. – A covered platform must establish comprehensive and
4 effective controls to ensure that a minor's personal information is not used in any algorithmic
5 recommendation system.
6 (c) Exceptions. – Subsection (b) of this section does not apply to any of the following:
7 (1) Recommending or presenting content from accounts that a user follows in
8 reverse chronological order or a similar method of recommending or
9 presenting content.
10 (2) A user's explicit search for content or request for information for the sole
11 purpose of providing immediate results to the search, and without retention or
12 use of the user-related data from the search or request for purposes other than
13 providing results to the search or request.
14 (3) A covered platform's action, voluntarily taken in good faith to restrict access
15 to or availability of material as described in section 230(c)(2)(A) of the
16 Communications Act of 1934 (47 U.S.C. § 230(c)(2)(A)), is not subject to this
17 subsection, and nothing in this section otherwise limits or otherwise affects
18 the provisions of section 230 of the Communications Act of 1934, except as
19 otherwise provided in this Article.
20 (d) The operator of a social media platform may be held liable for violating subsection
21 (a) of this section if the user was given algorithmic content recommendations without a proper
22 opt-in mechanism or affirmation from the user from the opt-in process. The operator of a social
23 media platform may be held liable for violating subsection (b) of this section if the operator of
24 the social media platform knew or had reason to know that the user was a minor. The operator of
25 a social media platform that has made an estimation of a user's age based upon the user's
26 self-attestation is not liable if the user was a minor who falsely attested to not being a minor.
27 "§ 75-72. Design features and digital rights of users.
28 (a) Protective Default Settings for Minors. – A covered platform shall configure all
29 privacy settings provided to any user by the online service, product, or feature be both available
30 to minors and, by default, set to preferences that offer the highest level of privacy, unless the
31 business can demonstrate a compelling reason that a different setting is in the best interest of
32 minors. These settings must include all of the following:
33 (1) Notifications must be turned off by default.
34 (2) The visibility of reaction or interaction counts on all content, including content
35 generated by a minor and content seen by a minor generated from others, must
36 be turned off by default.
37 (3) The ability of other users, not added by the user to a list of approved contacts,
38 to communicate with the minor must be turned off by default.
39 (4) The ability of other users, whether registered or not, and not added by the user
40 to a list of approved contacts, to view the minor's user-related data collected
41 by or shared on the platform must be disabled by default.
42 (5) The ability of other users to see the geolocation of a minor must be disabled
43 by default.
44 (6) Features that increase, sustain, or extend the use of the covered platform by a
45 minor, such as automatic playing of media and rewards for time spent on the
46 platform, must be disabled by default.
47 (b) Rights to Change and Delete Data. – A covered platform shall provide users with both
48 of the following:
49 (1) An accessible mechanism to request the correction of any inaccurate personal
50 information about the user, taking into account the nature of the personal
51 information and the purposes of the personal information. A platform that

1 receives a verifiable request to correct inaccurate personal information shall
2 use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the inaccurate personal
3 information as directed by the user. A covered platform shall maintain a record
4 of all requests.
5 (2) An accessible mechanism to request the deletion of personal information
6 about the user, taking into account the nature of the personal information and

15 Otherwise, the covered platform is required to complete the request. A
16 covered platform shall maintain a confidential record of all requests.
17 (c) Digital Rights of the User. – All of the following rights belong to every minor utilizing
18 covered platforms:
19 (1) Right to protection from manipulative design. – Every minor has the right to
20 be protected from manipulative design techniques which exploit
21 psychological vulnerability or have been shown by the preponderance of the
22 evidence to create addiction or dependency.
23 (2) Right to transparency. – Every minor has the right to understand the nature of
24 their digital experiences. Platforms and services should provide clear and
25 accessible explanations of the platform features as well as how covered
26 platforms can negatively affect their well-being.
27 (3) Right to protection from personalized recommendation systems. – Every
28 minor has the right to be protected from algorithmic recommendation systems.
29 (d) The operator of a covered platform may be subject to violations of subsection (a), (b),
30 or (c) of this section if any of the requirements and rights established herein have been determined
31 to be violated.
32 "§ 75-73. Investigation; enforcement; private right of action.
33 (a) Violations. – Effective January 1, 2026, a platform's violation of this Article is an
34 unfair or deceptive act or practice under G.S. 75-1.1.
35 (b) Investigations. – The Attorney General shall monitor social media platforms for
36 compliance with this Article.
37 (c) Complaints. – A platform user may make a complaint to the Attorney General
38 alleging that a social media platform has failed to comply with the requirements of this Article.
39 The Attorney General may bring a civil action in any case in which the Attorney General has
40 reason to believe that the interest of the residents of this State has been or is threatened due to
41 noncompliance with this Article.
42 (d) Private Right of Action. – Minors can file suit if they are affected by any covered
43 platform found to be in violation of this Article through mechanisms involved in parens patriae
44 jurisdiction by the following:
45 (1) Civil suit brought through private action attorneys.
46 (2) Relief. – In a civil action brought under subsection (c) of this section or this
47 subsection in which a plaintiff prevails, the court may award the plaintiff any
48 one or more of the following:
49 a. An amount equal to the sum of any compensatory damages.
50 b. Punitive damages.
51 c. Injunctive relief.

3 "§ 75-74. North Carolina Data Privacy Task Force.
4 (a) There is created the North Carolina Data Privacy Task Force (Task Force) within the
5 Department of Justice for budgetary purposes only.
6 (b) The Task Force shall be composed of 21 members. The ex officio members listed in
7 subdivisions (1) through (6) of this subsection may designate representatives from their particular
8 departments, divisions, or offices to represent them on the Task Force. In making appointments
9 or designating representatives, appointing authorities and ex officio members shall use best
10 efforts to select members or representatives with sufficient knowledge and experience to
11 effectively contribute to the issues examined by the Task Force and, to the extent possible, to
12 reflect the geographical, political, gender, and racial diversity of this State. The members shall
13 be as follows:
14 (1) The Attorney General.
15 (2) The State Chief Information Officer.
16 (3) The Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services.
17 (4) The Director of the State Bureau of Investigation.
18 (5) The Director of the Maternal and Child Health Section of the Department of
19 Health and Human Services.
20 (6) The Director of the Division of Mental Health, Developmental Disabilities,
21 and Substance Use Services.
22 (7) A representative from NC Child, appointed by the Governor upon
23 recommendation of the President of the organization.
24 (8) A representative from a private group, other than NC Child, that advocates for
25 children, appointed by the Governor upon recommendation of private child
26 advocacy organizations.
27 (9) A pediatrician, licensed to practice medicine in North Carolina, appointed by
28 the President Pro Tempore of the Senate.
29 (10) A psychiatrist, licensed to practice medicine in North Carolina, appointed by
30 the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
31 (11) Two public members, one of whom is an educator, appointed by the Speaker
32 of the House of Representatives.
33 (12) Two public members, one of whom is a social worker, appointed by the
34 President Pro Tempore of the Senate.
35 (13) Two members of the Senate, appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the
36 Senate, and two members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the
37 Speaker of the House of Representatives.
38 (14) A representative from the North Carolina Young People's Alliance, appointed
39 by the Governor upon recommendation of the head of the organization.
40 (15) Two youth representatives under the age of 21 appointed by the Secretary of
41 the Department of Health and Human Services after conducting an
42 application-based selection process.
43 (c) All members of the Task Force are voting members. Vacancies in the appointed
44 membership shall be filled by the appointing officer who made the initial appointment. Terms
45 shall be two years. The members shall elect a chair who shall preside for the duration of the
46 chair's term as a member. In the event a vacancy occurs in the chair before the expiration of the
47 chair's term, the members shall elect an acting chair to serve for the remainder of the unexpired
48 term.
49 (d) Beginning March 15, 2026, and then annually thereafter, the Task Force shall report
50 to the General Assembly on its work, with a special focus on mental health issues related to social
51 media, along with findings, recommendations, and any legislative proposals."
