14 "§ 75-70. Title; definitions.
15 (a) This Article shall be known and may be cited as the "Social Media Control in
16 Information Technology Act."
17 (b) Definitions. – The following definitions apply in this Article:
18 (1) Accessible mechanism. – A user-friendly, clear, easy-to-use, readily
19 available, and technologically feasible method that allows individuals to
20 exercise their data privacy rights without undue burden. The mechanism must
21 be designed to accommodate diverse user needs, including those with
22 disabilities, and should be available across commonly used platforms. The
23 mechanism should provide clear instructions, function without excessive
24 complexity, and be free of unreasonable barriers such as length procedures,
25 hidden settings, or excessive delays.
26 (2) Algorithmic recommendation system. – A computational process that uses
27 machine learning, natural language processing, artificial intelligence
28 techniques, generative artificial intelligence, or other computational
29 processing techniques that makes a decision or facilitates human decision
30 making with respect to user-related data to rank, order, promote, recommend,
31 suggest, amplify, or similarly determine the delivery or display of information
32 to an individual.
33 (3) Collects, collected, or collection. – Buying, renting, gathering, obtaining,
34 receiving, or accessing any personal information pertaining to a user by any
35 means. This includes receiving information from the consumer, either actively
36 or passively, or by observing the consumer's behavior.

13 "§ 75-71. User data privacy; targeting minors prohibited; registry.
14 (a) Privacy Requirements. – The General Assembly finds that unhealthy social media use
15 has been linked to depression, anxiety, eating disorders, and suicidal ideation, especially among
16 young people. Exploitation of user data can result in users being targeted in ways that increase
17 unhealthy social media use. It is the policy of this State that user data privacy shall be respected
18 by platforms. Special protections are warranted for users who are minors. Therefore, the operator
19 of a social media platform shall comply with all of the following requirements for platform users:
20 (1) The platform must specifically and clearly inform users in the following ways:
21 a. A disclosure in a clear, easy-to-read, and accessible format when a user
22 first initializes their use of a platform for the first time, or after a period
23 of inactivity greater than or equal to six months, about how the
24 platform collects personal information, what personal information the
25 platform collects, how the personal information is used by the platform
26 for every use case, and how the user can exercise their rights and
27 choices on the platform. This disclosure must be provided in no more
28 than 500 words, and the platform must obtain a user's consent before
29 the platform collects any user-related data on the user.
30 b. A disclosure in a clear, easy-to-read, and accessible format that details
31 (i) the categories of information the platform has collected about the
32 user, (ii) the categories of sources from which the information is
33 collected, (iii) the business or commercial purpose for collecting,
34 selling, or sharing personal information, (iv) the categories of third
35 parties to whom the business discloses personal information, and (v)
36 the specific pieces of personal information it has collected about that
37 user. Such information must be available upon receipt of a verifiable
38 consumer request made through an accessible mechanism on the
39 platform.
40 (2) Personal information may be used in algorithmic recommendations only when
41 both of the following requirements are met:
42 a. The platform reasonably determines the user is not a minor from
43 personal information collected by and available to the covered
44 platform in its ordinary course of business.
45 b. The user has been notified and expressly consents to the use of their
46 own data in this manner by consenting in an opt-in mechanism.
47 (3) Through an accessible mechanism, users must be given the capacity to alter,
48 change, and delete what categories of personal information are used in a
49 platform's algorithmic recommendation system or systems. This selection
50 shall be modifiable at any time. If a user indicates that they intend a certain
51 category of personal information not to be used in an algorithmic

1 recommendation system, then the platform must not include said category or

8 (b) Targeting Minors Prohibited. – A covered platform must establish comprehensive and

11 (c) Exceptions. – Subsection (b) of this section does not apply to any of the following:
12 (1) Recommending or presenting content from accounts that a user follows in
13 reverse chronological order or a similar method of recommending or
14 presenting content.
15 (2) A user's explicit search for content or request for information for the sole
16 purpose of providing immediate results to the search, and without retention or
17 use of the user-related data from the search or request for purposes other than
18 providing results to the search or request.
19 (3) A covered platform's action, voluntarily taken in good faith to restrict access
20 to or availability of material as described in section 230(c)(2)(A) of the
21 Communications Act of 1934 (47 U.S.C. § 230(c)(2)(A)), is not subject to this
22 subsection, and nothing in this section otherwise limits or otherwise affects
23 the provisions of section 230 of the Communications Act of 1934, except as
24 otherwise provided in this Article.
25 (d) The operator of a social media platform may be held liable for violating subsection
26 (a) of this section if the user was given algorithmic content recommendations without a proper
27 opt-in mechanism or affirmation from the user from the opt-in process. The operator of a social
28 media platform may be held liable for violating subsection (b) of this section if the operator of
29 the social media platform knew or had reason to know that the user was a minor. The operator of
30 a social media platform that has made an estimation of a user's age based upon the user's
31 self-attestation is not liable if the user was a minor who falsely attested to not being a minor.
32 (e) Registry. – Beginning October 1, 2026, and then annually thereafter, a social media
33 platform shall provide the Consumer Protection Division of the North Carolina Department of
34 Justice (Division) with a digital copy of the platform's privacy policy and certification that the
35 platform has complied with the requirements of this section. Platforms shall provide notice of
36 substantive privacy policy changes to the registry. The Division shall maintain a registry of these
37 policies and certifications on its internet website.
38 "§ 75-72. Design features and digital rights of users.
39 (a) Protective Default Settings for Minors. – A covered platform shall configure all
40 privacy settings provided to any user by the online service, product, or feature to be both available
41 to minors and, by default, set to preferences that offer the highest level of privacy, unless the
42 business can demonstrate a compelling reason that a different setting is in the best interest of
43 minors. These settings must include all of the following:
44 (1) Notifications must be turned off by default.
45 (2) The visibility of reaction or interaction counts on all content, including content
46 generated by a minor and content seen by a minor generated from others, must
47 be turned off by default.
48 (3) The ability of other users, not added by the user to a list of approved contacts,
49 to communicate with the minor must be turned off by default.

9 (b) Rights to Change and Delete Data. – A covered platform shall provide users with both
10 of the following:
11 (1) An accessible mechanism to request the correction of any inaccurate personal
12 information about the user, taking into account the nature of the personal
13 information and the purposes of the personal information. A platform that
14 receives a verifiable request to correct inaccurate personal information shall
15 use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the inaccurate personal
16 information as directed by the user. A covered platform shall maintain a record
17 of all requests.
18 (2) An accessible mechanism to request the deletion of personal information
19 about the user, taking into account the nature of the personal information and
20 the purposes of the personal information. If the personal information is
21 reasonably necessary for the platform to complete a transaction, to ensure the
22 security and integrity of the user's personal information, to debug or identify
23 and repair errors in the platform, to exercise free speech and ensure the user's
24 right to exercise free speech, to comply with existing federal and State
25 regulations, to engage in public or peer-reviewed scientific research, or to
26 enable solely internal uses reasonably aligned with a consumer's expectations,
27 then the covered platform is not required to comply with the user's request.
28 Otherwise, the covered platform is required to complete the request. A
29 covered platform shall maintain a confidential record of all requests.
30 (c) Digital Rights of the User. – All of the following rights belong to every minor utilizing
31 covered platforms:
32 (1) Right to protection from manipulative design. – Every minor has the right to
33 be protected from manipulative design techniques which exploit
34 psychological vulnerability or have been shown by the preponderance of the
35 evidence to create addiction or dependency.
36 (2) Right to transparency. – Every minor has the right to understand the nature of
37 their digital experiences. Platforms and services should provide clear and
38 accessible explanations of the platform features as well as how covered
39 platforms can negatively affect their well-being.
40 (3) Right to protection from personalized recommendation systems. – Every
41 minor has the right to be protected from algorithmic recommendation systems.
42 (d) The operator of a covered platform may be subject to violations of subsection (a), (b),
43 or (c) of this section if any of the requirements and rights established herein have been determined
44 to be violated.
45 "§ 75-73. Investigation; enforcement; private right of action.
46 (a) Violations. – Effective January 1, 2027, a platform's violation of this Article is an
47 unfair or deceptive act or practice under G.S. 75-1.1.
48 (b) Investigations. – The Attorney General shall monitor social media platforms for
49 compliance with this Article.
50 (c) Complaints. – A platform user may make a complaint to the Attorney General
51 alleging that a social media platform has failed to comply with the requirements of this Article.
