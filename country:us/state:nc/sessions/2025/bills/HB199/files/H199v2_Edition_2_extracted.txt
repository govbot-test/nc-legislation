8 "Nonconsensual Booting and Towing.
9 "§ 20-219.31. Definitions.
10 The following definitions apply in this Article:
11 (1) Reserved for future codification purposes.
12 (2) Reserved for future codification purposes.
13 (3) Boot. – To attach any device or instrument to a motor vehicle, without the
14 prior consent or authorization of the owner or operator of the motor vehicle,
15 for the purpose of preventing that motor vehicle from exiting a parking lot or
16 area on private property.
17 (4) Reserved for future codification purposes.
18 (5) Reserved for future codification purposes.
19 (6) Commission. – The Towing and Recovery Commission.
20 (7) Reserved for future codification purposes.
21 (8) Reserved for future codification purposes.
22 (9) Reserved for future codification purposes.
23 (10) Nonconsensual tow. – Towing without the prior consent or authorization of
24 the owner or operator of the motor vehicle being towed.
25 (11) Nonconsensual towing business. – Any person or entity that engages in the
26 booting or towing of improperly parked motor vehicles from private property
27 without the consent of the owner or operator of the motor vehicle.
28 (12) Private property. – Any parcel or space of private real property.
29 (13) Reserved for future codification purposes.
30 (14) Reserved for future codification purposes.
31 (15) Tow. – To use any motor vehicle to pull, load and carry, or otherwise to
32 transport another motor vehicle over a public highway or road, except that
33 transportation by a motor vehicle with a capacity of three or more vehicles
34 shall not be included in the definition of tow, no matter how many vehicles
35 that vehicle is transporting at any given time.

3 "§ 20-219.40. Towing and Recovery Commission.
4 (a) Commission Established. – There is established the Towing and Recovery
5 Commission within the Department of Public Safety.
6 (b) Responsibilities. – The Commission shall have the following responsibilities:
7 (1) To issue permits to nonconsensual towing businesses in accordance with
8 G.S. 20-219.50.
9 (2) To annually establish the maximum fees that a nonconsensual towing business
10 may charge for booting, towing, storage, and the handling of commercial
11 cargo. In establishing these fees, the Commission shall have the authority to
12 do the following:
13 a. The Commission may, in its discretion, create different maximum fees
14 for towing based on the type of vehicle towed.
15 b. The Commission shall consider regional variations in the cost of a
16 nonconsensual towing business.
17 c. The Commission may, in its discretion, divide the State into different
18 regions based on the cost of a nonconsensual towing business.
19 d. The Commission may, in its discretion, establish different maximum
20 fees for the different regions of the State.
21 (3) To create a standardized form for use by nonconsensual towing businesses to
22 itemize charges billed for booting, towing, storage, and the handling of
23 personal property or commercial cargo.
24 (4) To maintain a nonconsensual towing business database in accordance with
25 G.S. 20-219.55.
26 (5) To record reports from the public of suspected noncompliance with this
27 Article and any resolutions of those complaints in accordance with
28 G.S. 20-219.55.
29 (6) To otherwise administer the provisions of this Article.
30 (c) Annual Report. – Beginning with the 2027 calendar year, the Commission shall, by
31 February 15 of each year, prepare and submit an annual report to the chairs of the Joint
32 Legislative Oversight Committee on Justice and Public Safety containing the following
33 information:
34 (1) Reports from the public of suspected noncompliance with this Article.
35 (2) Any resolutions of reported noncompliance with this Article, including the
36 total number of permits revoked or not renewed in the preceding calendar year
37 based on noncompliance.
38 (3) The implementation of the nonconsensual towing business database.
39 (4) The number of permits issued pursuant to G.S. 20-219.50 in the preceding
40 calendar year and the total number of active permits overall.

46 Speaker of the House of Representatives:
47 a. One member of the Towing and Recovery Professionals Association
48 of North Carolina.
49 b. One member of the North Carolina Trucking Association.
50 c. One representative of local law enforcement.

35 designee shall serve as chair. The Commission members shall elect a vice-chair from the
36 membership of the Commission at its first meeting. The chair shall call the first meeting of the
37 Commission no later than April 1, 2026. The Commission shall meet at least twice annually at
38 times and places deemed necessary by the chair or, in the absence of the chair, by the vice-chair.
39 (d) Compensation. – Commission members shall not receive compensation but are
40 entitled to be paid necessary subsistence and travel expenses in accordance with G.S. 138-5 and
41 G.S. 138-6 as applicable.
42 (e) Vacancies. – If a vacancy occurs in the membership of the Commission, the
43 appointing authority shall appoint another person meeting the same qualifications to serve for the
44 balance of the unexpired term.
45 "§ 20-219.50. Permits.
46 (a) Every nonconsensual towing business shall obtain a permit from the Commission
47 before operating in the State and shall renew the permit annually.
48 (b) The Commission shall collect a nonrefundable fee for a permit application or renewal
49 of a permit. The fee shall not exceed the total direct and indirect costs of administering the
50 permitting system and the database required by G.S. 20-219.55.

1 (c) The Commission shall prescribe the form of the application for a permit and renewal
2 of a permit. The initial application and renewal application shall require information sufficient
3 to confirm compliance with this Article.
4 (d) The Commission shall issue or renew a permit if (i) the application is complete and
5 (ii) the applicant demonstrates the willingness and ability to comply with the requirements of this
6 Article and other applicable laws. Appeals from the denial of an initial or renewed permit shall
7 be governed by the provisions of Chapter 150B of the General Statutes.
8 (e) Engaging in the practice of nonconsensual booting or towing without a valid permit
9 is an unfair trade practice under G.S. 75-1.1.
10 "§ 20-219.55. Nonconsensual towing business database.
11 (a) The Commission shall develop and maintain, or contract with a qualified vendor to
12 develop and maintain, a statewide database on its website that does all of the following:
13 (1) Allows any member of the public to report suspected noncompliance with this
14 Article. The Commission shall provide the Attorney General with these
15 reports.
16 (2) Allows the owner or operator of a nonconsensually towed motor vehicle to
17 search the database by entering that vehicle's make, model, and license plate
18 number or vehicle identification number and access the information required
19 to be provided to the owner or operator pursuant to subsection (b) of this
20 section with respect to that motor vehicle.
21 (3) Allows nonconsensual towing businesses to securely log in to the database
22 and input the information required by subsection (c) of this section.
23 (b) The information contained in the database described in subsection (a) of this section
24 shall be publicly accessible only upon a person entering (i) the make of the vehicle, (ii) the model
25 of the vehicle, and (iii) either the vehicle's license plate number or vehicle identification number.
26 Upon entering the information specified in this subsection, the person shall be shown the location
27 to which the vehicle was towed, the hours of operation of the location to which the vehicle was
28 towed, the phone number of the nonconsensual towing business, and the amount the person must
29 pay to retrieve the vehicle. All other information contained in the database shall be confidential
30 and accessible only upon a proper request pursuant to Chapter 132 of the General Statutes.
31 (c) A nonconsensual towing business shall, within one hour of completing a
32 nonconsensual towing of a vehicle, input all of the following information into the database
33 created pursuant to this section:
34 (1) The name of the nonconsensual towing business employee who towed the
35 vehicle, and the nonconsensual towing business's permit number.
36 (2) The name of the nonconsensual towing business employee or owner who
37 authorized the towing, if different from the person in subdivision (1) of this
38 subsection.
39 (3) The color, make, model, license plate number, and vehicle identification
40 number of the vehicle towed, and any trailer connected to the vehicle at any
41 point during or after the tow.
42 (4) The reason for towing the vehicle.
43 (5) The address of the location from which the vehicle was towed, a certification
44 that the location complied with the signage requirements of G.S. 20-219.60,
45 and the name of the property owner or property owner's designated
46 representative with whom the nonconsensual towing business has a
47 contractual relationship.
48 (6) The address of the location where a towed vehicle is currently stored.
49 (7) The fees the nonconsensual towing business will charge the vehicle owner for
50 towing, storage, and personal property handling.
51 "§ 20-219.60. Required signage for nonconsensual towing.

1 (a) Private property owners shall ensure that signs are prominently displayed at each
2 designated entrance to a parking lot or area where parking prohibitions apply. The posted signs
3 shall be a minimum of 24 inches by 24 inches and shall legibly display the following information:
4 (1) The words "Private Property" printed in bold.
5 (2) A warning that unauthorized vehicles will be booted or towed.
6 (3) The name, address, telephone number, and hours of operation of the
7 nonconsensual towing business the owner has contracted with to boot or tow
8 unauthorized vehicles.
9 (4) The booting, towing, storage, and personal property handling fees imposed by
10 the nonconsensual towing business the owner has contracted with to boot or
11 tow unauthorized vehicles.
12 (5) A notification that nonconsensual towing businesses must accept payment by
13 debit card, credit card, and cash.
14 (6) A notification that damage caused to any booting or towing equipment as a
15 result of removing or attempting to remove the equipment is punishable
16 pursuant to G.S. 14-160.
17 (b) A vehicle shall not be booted or nonconsensually towed on private property that does
18 not, at the time of the booting or towing and for at least 24 hours prior, have signs posted in
19 compliance with this section.
20 (c) A private property owner shall ensure that any sign displaying incorrect information
21 is removed or corrected within 15 days of discovering the information is incorrect.
22 (d) A violation of this section is punishable as follows:
23 (1) A private property owner that violates this section shall be guilty of an
24 infraction. A court may order a private property owner to make restitution to
25 the owner or operator of the improperly booted or towed motor vehicle in an
26 amount equal to the fees incurred by the owner or operator for the booting,
27 towing, and storage of the motor vehicle.
28 (2) A nonconsensual tow truck driver that violates this section shall be guilty of
29 an infraction and subject to a penalty of not more than one hundred dollars
30 ($100.00). A second violation under this subdivision within 12 months of the
31 first violation shall be punished as an infraction with a penalty of not more
32 than two hundred dollars ($200.00). A third or subsequent violation under this
33 subdivision within 12 months of the first violation shall be punished as a Class
34 3 misdemeanor.
35 (e) This section does not apply to the owners of private residential property that consists
36 of four or fewer residential units.
37 "§ 20-219.65. Nonconsensual towing and booting practices.
38 (a) No nonconsensual towing business shall boot, tow, or attempt to boot or tow any
39 vehicle from private property without a written contract with the property owner or the property
40 owner's designee to perform nonconsensual booting or towing on that property.
41 (b) Any motor vehicle removed from a parking lot or area on private property pursuant
42 to this Article shall not be transported for storage more than 25 miles from the place of removal.
43 This subsection shall not apply if there is no storage area within 25 miles from the place of
44 removal that is suitably sized to store the motor vehicle.
45 (c) No nonconsensual towing business shall boot, tow, or attempt to boot or tow a vehicle
46 if the nonconsensual towing business employee performing the boot or tow knows or has reason
47 to know that the vehicle is occupied.
48 (d) A commercial motor vehicle shall not be booted.
49 (e) A nonconsensual towing business shall not attempt to impede or block an occupied
50 vehicle that has not yet been booted from being removed from a parking lot by its owner or
51 operator.

1 (f) After booting a vehicle, a nonconsensual towing business shall affix a notice on the
2 driver's side windshield of the car in a manner that will not damage the vehicle. The notice shall
3 be brightly colored, a minimum of 8.5 inches by 11 inches, and legibly state all of the following:
4 (1) That the vehicle to which the notice is affixed has been booted and that driving
5 the vehicle may damage it.
6 (2) The name, address, telephone number, and hours of operation of the
7 nonconsensual towing business that booted the vehicle.
8 (3) Any fees the nonconsensual towing business will charge before releasing the
9 boot from the vehicle.
10 (g) Booting, towing, or storing a motor vehicle in violation of this section is an unfair and
11 deceptive trade practice under Chapter 75 of the General Statutes.
12 "§ 20-219.70. Nonconsensual towing fees.
13 (a) All fees charged by a nonconsensual towing business shall be reasonable and not
14 excessive and shall not exceed the maximums established by the Commission. A nonconsensual
15 towing business shall prepare and distribute bills using the standardized form described in
16 G.S. 20-219.40(b).
17 (b) A nonconsensual towing business shall not charge a fee for the storage or handling of
18 cargo contained in a trailer or semitrailer. Property subject to this subsection shall be promptly
19 returned to the owner of the property upon request. In cases of a dispute, if the cargo is attached
20 to the trailer and cannot be removed from the trailer, the parties shall execute a trailer swap. The
21 swapped trailer must be of equal or better condition than the original towed trailer and owned,
22 leased, or operated by the same company.
23 (c) A nonconsensual towing business shall not charge a storage fee for days when the
24 business is not open from at least 9:00 A.M. to 4:00 P.M. Unless a nonconsensual towing business
25 is open for the recovery of a towed vehicle 24 hours per day, a nonconsensual towing business
26 shall not charge a storage fee for a towed vehicle until at least one business day has elapsed since
27 the towed vehicle could first be recovered.
28 (d) A nonconsensual towing business shall accept payment with a debit card, credit card,
29 and cash at any time during its operating hours, including at the time of booting or towing, and
30 may choose to accept any other form of commercially acceptable payment. Any payment
31 processing fees shall not exceed three percent (3%) of the owed amount.
32 (e) If a truck tractor, trailer, or semitrailer remains in storage more than 60 days after it
33 is towed, a nonconsensual towing business may sell the truck tractor, trailer, or semitrailer in a
34 nonjudicial auction or private sale pursuant to this subsection. The nonconsensual towing
35 business shall mail written notice to the property owner of the intent to auction or sell the truck
36 tractor, trailer, or semitrailer by registered or certified mail, return receipt requested, addressed
37 to the property owner at the address on the vehicle registration records maintained by the
38 Division. If the property owner does not contact the nonconsensual towing business within 30
39 days of receipt of the written notice, the nonconsensual towing business may sell the truck tractor,
40 trailer, or semitrailer at auction or private sale. Proceeds from the sale of the property shall go
41 toward paying outstanding towing, recovery, or storage fees and the remainder shall be returned
42 to the property owner. This relief is only available to the nonconsensual towing business from
43 which the tow originated.
44 (f) Charging a fee in violation of this section is an unfair and deceptive trade practice
45 under Chapter 75 of the General Statutes.

37 "§ 20-219.65. Nonconsensual towing and booting practices.
38 (a) No nonconsensual towing business shall boot, tow, or attempt to boot or tow any
39 vehicle from private property without a written contract with the property owner or the property
40 owner's designee to perform nonconsensual booting or towing on that property.
41 (b) Any motor vehicle removed from a parking lot or area on private property pursuant
42 to this Article shall not be transported for storage more than 25 miles from the place of removal.
43 This subsection shall not apply if there is no storage area within 25 miles from the place of
44 removal that is suitably sized to store the motor vehicle.
45 (c) No nonconsensual towing business shall boot, tow, or attempt to boot or tow a vehicle
46 if the nonconsensual towing business employee performing the boot or tow knows or has reason
47 to know that the vehicle is occupied.
48 (d) A commercial motor vehicle shall not be booted.
49 (e) A nonconsensual towing business shall not attempt to impede or block an occupied
50 vehicle that has not yet been booted from being removed from a parking lot by its owner or
51 operator.

12 "§ 20-219.70. Nonconsensual towing fees.
13 (a) All fees charged by a nonconsensual towing business shall be reasonable and not
14 excessive and shall not exceed the maximums established by the Commission. A nonconsensual
15 towing business shall prepare and distribute bills using the standardized form described in
16 G.S. 20-219.40(b).
17 (b) A nonconsensual towing business shall not charge a fee for the storage or handling of
18 cargo contained in a trailer or semitrailer. Property subject to this subsection shall be promptly
19 returned to the owner of the property upon request. In cases of a dispute, if the cargo is attached
20 to the trailer and cannot be removed from the trailer, the parties shall execute a trailer swap. The
21 swapped trailer must be of equal or better condition than the original towed trailer and owned,
22 leased, or operated by the same company.
23 (c) A nonconsensual towing business shall not charge a storage fee for days when the
24 business is not open from at least 9:00 A.M. to 4:00 P.M. Unless a nonconsensual towing business
25 is open for the recovery of a towed vehicle 24 hours per day, a nonconsensual towing business
26 shall not charge a storage fee for a towed vehicle until at least one business day has elapsed since
27 the towed vehicle could first be recovered.
28 (d) A nonconsensual towing business shall accept payment with a debit card, credit card,
29 and cash at any time during its operating hours, including at the time of booting or towing, and
30 may choose to accept any other form of commercially acceptable payment. Any payment
31 processing fees shall not exceed three percent (3%) of the owed amount.
32 (e) If a truck tractor, trailer, or semitrailer remains in storage more than 60 days after it
33 is towed, a nonconsensual towing business may sell the truck tractor, trailer, or semitrailer in a
34 nonjudicial auction or private sale pursuant to this subsection. The nonconsensual towing
35 business shall mail written notice to the property owner of the intent to auction or sell the truck
36 tractor, trailer, or semitrailer by registered or certified mail, return receipt requested, addressed
37 to the property owner at the address on the vehicle registration records maintained by the
38 Division. If the property owner does not contact the nonconsensual towing business within 30
39 days of receipt of the written notice, the nonconsensual towing business may sell the truck tractor,
40 trailer, or semitrailer at auction or private sale. Proceeds from the sale of the property shall go
41 toward paying outstanding towing, recovery, or storage fees and the remainder shall be returned
42 to the property owner. This relief is only available to the nonconsensual towing business from
43 which the tow originated.
44 (f) Charging a fee in violation of this section is an unfair and deceptive trade practice
45 under Chapter 75 of the General Statutes.
