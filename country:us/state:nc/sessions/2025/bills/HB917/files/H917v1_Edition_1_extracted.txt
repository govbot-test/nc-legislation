Section 1:
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: Establish DATA Division. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Cervania, Howard, and Logan (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 14, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO ESTABLISH THE DATA ANALYTICS, TRANSPARENCY, AND
3 ACCOUNTABILITY DIVISION OF THE NORTH CAROLINA GENERAL ASSEMBLY.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. Chapter 143E of the General Statutes, Article 7C of Chapter 120 of the
6 General Statutes, and G.S. 120-32(1)j., 120-79(c), 120-131.1(a1), and 143-49(16), as those
7 statutes existed immediately before repeal, are reenacted.
8 SECTION 2. G.S. 63A-23 reads as rewritten:
9 "§ 63A-23. Annual reports.
10 The Authority shall, promptly following the close of each fiscal year, submit an annual
11 written report of its activities for the preceding year to the Governor, the General Assembly, and
12 the Local Government Commission. Each report shall be accompanied by an audit of its books
13 and accounts, as well as quarterly and annual financial statements. The audit shall be conducted
14 by the State Auditor. The costs of all audits shall be paid from funds of the Authority.
15 As part of the report, the Authority shall include the following performance measures of the
16 private sector jobs within the Global TransPark:
17 (1) The number, type, and wage level of jobs created or retained.
18 (2) The actual full-time equivalent jobs employed, as well as the median and
19 average salaries for those jobs.
20 The Authority shall submit its annual report to the General Assembly to both the Joint
21 Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations. Operations and the Data Analytics,
22 Transparency, and Accountability Division. The Authority shall also submit any information
23 about the Authority's activities that is requested by the Commission.
24 The Authority shall also provide a copy of its annual report on its public Web site."
25 SECTION 3. G.S. 90-414.5(b) reads as rewritten:
26 "(b) At the written request of the Director of the Fiscal Research, Legislative Drafting, or
27 Legislative Analysis Analysis, or Data Analytics, Transparency, and Accountability Division of
28 the General Assembly for an aggregate analysis of the data and information disclosed through
29 the HIE Network, the Authority shall provide the professional staff of these Divisions with the
30 aggregated analysis responsive to the Director's request. Prior to providing the Director or
31 General Assembly's staff with any aggregate data or information submitted through the HIE
32 Network or with any analysis of this aggregate data or information, the Authority shall redact
33 any personal identifying information in a manner consistent with the standards specified for
34 de-identification of health information under the HIPAA Privacy Rule, 45 C.F.R. § 164.514, as
35 amended."

1 SECTION 4. G.S. 120-32.01 reads as rewritten:
2 "§ 120-32.01. Information to be supplied.
3 (a) Every State department, State agency, or State institution shall furnish the Legislative
4 Services Office and the Legislative Analysis, Fiscal Research, Data Analytics, Transparency, and
5 Accountability, and Legislative Drafting Divisions any information or records requested by them
6 and access to any facilities and personnel requested by them. Except when accessibility is
7 prohibited by a federal statute, federal regulation, or State statute, every State department, State
8 agency, or State institution shall give the Legislative Services Office and these divisions access
9 to any database or stored information maintained by computer, telecommunications, or other
10 electronic data processing equipment, whether stored on tape, disk, or otherwise, and regardless
11 of the medium for storage or transmission.
12 (b) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section, access to the BEACON/HR payroll
13 system by the Legislative Analysis and Legislative Drafting Divisions shall only be through the
14 Fiscal Research Division.Division and access to the system by the Data Analytics, Transparency,
15 and Accountability Division shall only be through the Division Director and two employees of
16 the Division designated by the Division Director.
17 …."
18 SECTION 5. G.S. 126-85(c) reads as rewritten:
19 "(c) The protections of this Article shall include State employees who report any activity
20 described in G.S. 126-84 (i) to the State Auditor as authorized by G.S. 147-64.6B, (ii) to the Joint
21 Legislative Commission on Governmental Operations as authorized by G.S. 120-76, or
22 G.S. 120-76, (iii) to the Data Analytics, Transparency, and Accountability Division as authorized
23 by G.S. 120-36.12(10), or (iv) to a legislative committee as required by G.S. 120-19."
24 SECTION 6. G.S. 143-341.2(a) reads as rewritten:
25 "(a) Duties of the Department of Administration. – The Department of Administration
26 shall have the following powers and duties:
27 …
28 (7) Reporting. – The Department of Administration shall make the following
29 reports:
30 a. No later than April 1, 2023, and every five years thereafter, the
31 Department shall report the following to the Joint Legislative
32 Commission on Governmental Operations and Joint Legislative
33 Oversight Committee on Capital Improvements, Joint Legislative
34 Oversight Committee on General Government, Data Analytics,
35 Transparency, and Accountability Division, and Fiscal Research
36 Division:
37 1. The plan developed pursuant to subdivision (1) of this
38 subsection.
39 2. A summary of the performance measurement procedures
40 developed pursuant to subdivision (2) of this subsection.
41 b. If any State agency fails to submit the information required by
42 subdivision (b)(1) of this section, the Department shall report the
43 failure to the chairs of the Joint Legislative Commission on
44 Governmental Operations, the chairs of the Joint Legislative Oversight
45 Committee on Capital Improvements, the chairs of the Joint
46 Legislative Data Analytics, Transparency, and Accountability
47 Oversight Committee, and the chairs of the Joint Legislative Oversight
48 Committee on General Government within 30 days.
49 c. No later than April 1, 2024, and each year thereafter, the Department
50 shall report to the Joint Legislative Commission on Governmental
51 Operations, Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Capital

2 Government, Data Analytics, Transparency, and Accountability
3 Division, and Fiscal Research Division on the State's portfolio of real
4 property. This report shall include at least the following information:
5 …."
6 SECTION 7. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the General Assembly
7 the sum of two million dollars ($2,000,000) for the 2025-2026 fiscal year in recurring funds for
8 the purpose of reenacting and operating the Data Analytics, Transparency, and Accountability
9 Division, as reestablished in this act.
10 SECTION 8. The Revisor of Statutes shall replace the phrase "Program Evaluation
11 Division" with the phrase "Data Analytics, Transparency, and Accountability Division" in the
12 General Statutes wherever it appears.
13 SECTION 9. This act becomes effective July 1, 2025, and Section 6 of this act
14 applies to reports due on or after that date.
