SESSION 2025
H 1
Short Title: LEO Special Separation Allowance Options. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Pyrtle, Miller, Chesser, and Cotham (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.

February 5, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT PROVIDING AN ADDITIONAL SPECIAL SEPARATION ALLOWANCE OPTION
3 FOR STATE AND LOCAL LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICERS WITH AT LEAST
4 THIRTY YEARS OF CREDITABLE SERVICE.
5 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
6 SECTION 1. G.S. 143-166.41 reads as rewritten:
7 "§ 143-166.41. Special separation allowance.allowance options for State law enforcement
8 officers.
9 (a) Annual Special Separation Allowance. – Notwithstanding any other provision of law,
10 every sworn law-enforcement officer as defined by G.S. 135-1(11c) or G.S. 143-166.30(a)(4)
11 employed by a State department, agency, or institution who qualifies under this section shall
12 receive, receive an annual special separation allowance beginning in the month in which he the
13 officer retires on a basic service retirement under the provisions of G.S. 135-5(a), an annual
14 separation allowance equal to eighty-five hundredths percent (0.85%) of the annual equivalent
15 of the base rate of compensation most recently applicable to him for each year of creditable
16 service. G.S. 135-5(a). The allowance shall be paid in equal installments on the payroll frequency
17 used by the employer. To qualify for the allowance the officer shall:
18 (1) Have (i) completed 30 or more years of creditable service or, (ii) have attained
19 55 years of age and completed five or more years of creditable service; and
20 (2) Not have attained 62 years of age; and
21 (3) Have completed at least five years of continuous service as a law enforcement
22 officer as herein defined immediately preceding a service retirement. Any
23 break in the continuous service required by this subsection because of
24 disability retirement or disability salary continuation benefits shall not
25 adversely affect an officer's qualification to receive the allowance, provided
26 the officer returns to service within 45 days after the disability benefits cease
27 and is otherwise qualified to receive the allowance.employer from which the
28 officer retired.
29 (a1) Repealed by Session Laws 2014-88, s. 3(j), effective July 30, 2014.
30 (b) As used in this section, "creditable service" means the Definitions. – The following
31 definitions apply in this section:
32 (1) Allowance. – The annual special separation allowance for State law
33 enforcement officers provided for under this section.

1 (2) Creditable service. – The service for which credit is allowed under the
2 retirement system of which the officer is a member, provided that at least fifty
3 percent (50%) of the service is as a law enforcement officer as herein defined
4 or as a probation/parole officer as defined in G.S. 135-1(17a).officer.
5 (3) Law enforcement officer. – As defined in either G.S. 135-1 or
6 G.S. 143-166.30(a).
7 (4) Officer. – A law enforcement officer.
8 (5) Probation/parole officer. – As defined in G.S. 135-1.
9 (b1) Eligibility for Allowance and Calculation of Allowance Amount. – To be eligible for
10 an allowance under this section, an officer is required to meet one of the following sets of criteria
11 that shall also determine the allowance amount:
12 (1) For officers meeting all of the following criteria, the annual special separation
13 allowance to be paid is equal to eighty-five hundredths percent (0.85%) of the
14 annual base rate of compensation most recently applicable to the officer for
15 each year of that officer's creditable service:
16 a. The officer (i) has completed 30 or more years of creditable service or
17 (ii) is 55 years of age or older and completed five or more years of
18 creditable service.
19 b. The officer is less than 62 years of age.
20 c. The officer has completed at least five years of continuous service as
21 a law enforcement officer immediately preceding the officer's service
22 retirement. Any break in this required continuous service that is a
23 result of disability retirement or disability salary continuation benefits
24 shall not adversely affect an officer's qualification to receive an
25 allowance under this subdivision so long as the officer returned to
26 service within 45 days after the disability benefits had ceased and is
27 otherwise qualified to receive the allowance.
28 (2) For officers meeting all of the following criteria, the annual special separation
29 allowance to be paid is equal to eighty-five hundredths percent (0.85%) of the
30 annual equivalent of the base rate of compensation at the time the officer
31 attained 30 years of service multiplied by 30:
32 a. The officer has completed 30 or more years of creditable service prior
33 to attaining 62 years of age.
34 b. The officer has completed at least five years of continuous service as
35 a law enforcement officer immediately preceding the officer's service
36 retirement. Any break in this required continuous service that is a
37 result of disability retirement or disability salary continuation benefits
38 shall not adversely affect an officer's qualification to receive an
39 allowance under this subdivision so long as the officer returned to
40 service within 45 days after the disability benefits had ceased and is
41 otherwise qualified to receive the allowance.
42 If an officer meets all of the criteria under each subdivision of this subsection, then the
43 employer making the allowance payments shall allow the officer to choose which of the two
44 calculation formulas to use for that officer's allowance. This election by the officer is a one-time,
45 irrevocable election and shall be made prior to the first allowance payment. If no election is made
46 by the officer, then the calculation amount under subdivision (2) of this subsection shall be used.
47 (c) Cessation of Payment. – Payment of the allowance to a retired officer under the
48 provisions of this section shall cease at the first of:occurrence of one of the following:
49 (1) The death of the officer;officer.
50 (2) The last day of the month in which either of the following applies:

13 which an individual may be entitled from State, federal, or private retirement systems. The
14 benefits payable under this section shall not be subject to any increases in salary or retirement
15 allowances that may be authorized by the General Assembly for employees of the State or retired
16 employees of the State.

24 institution shall make the payments set forth in subsection (a) this section to those persons

26 section."
