GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 15
Short Title: Support Private Property Rights. (Public)
Sponsors: Representatives Hastings, Paré, Zenger, and Scott (Primary Sponsors).
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
January 30, 2025
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CLARIFY THAT THE INCLUSION OF REAL PROPERTY ON A
3 COMPREHENSIVE TRANSPORTATION PLAN IS NOT A REQUIRED DISCLOSURE
4 OR A MATERIAL FACT FOR THE PURPOSES OF DISCLOSURE FOR REAL ESTATE
5 TRANSACTIONS.
6 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
7 SECTION 1. Article 9 of Chapter 39 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
8 a new section to read:
9 "§ 39-51. Inclusion of real property in a comprehensive transportation plan not a material
10 fact.
11 (a) For purposes of this section, the term "financially constrained" is as defined in 23
12 C.F.R. § 450.104.
13 (b) The mere fact that real property, or any portion thereof, is included in a
14 comprehensive transportation plan that is not financially constrained adopted pursuant to
15 G.S. 136-66.2 or G.S. 136-212, or in accordance with 23 U.S.C. § 134 or 135, shall not, standing
16 alone, be deemed material. This subsection shall apply to all of the following:
17 (1) Offering real property for conveyance, lease, or exchange.
18 (2) Any other real estate transaction, including those listed in G.S. 47E-1(1)
19 through (4).
20 A party to the conveyance, lease, exchange, or transaction, or an agent of any said party, shall
21 not knowingly make a false statement regarding the property's inclusion on any transportation
22 plan."
23 SECTION 2. G.S. 47E-4 reads as rewritten:
24 "§ 47E-4. Required disclosures.
25 …
26 (b3) The inclusion of real property in a comprehensive transportation plan that is not
27 financially constrained adopted pursuant to G.S. 136-66.2 or G.S. 136-212, or in accordance with
28 23 U.S.C. § 134 or 135, shall not be considered a required disclosure as provided in this section,
29 provided, however, that no person subject to this Chapter, or an agent of a person subject to this
30 Chapter, shall knowingly make a false statement regarding any such fact. For purposes of this
31 subsection, the term "financially constrained" is as defined in 23 C.F.R. § 450.104.
32 …."
33 SECTION 3. This act becomes effective October 1, 2025, and applies to claims for
34 relief filed on or after that date.
