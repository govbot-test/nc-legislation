GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF NORTH CAROLINA
SESSION 2025
H 1
HOUSE BILL 1084
Short Title: E-Bike Definitions/Local Safety Regulation. (Public)
Sponsors: Representative Helfrich.
For a complete list of sponsors, refer to the North Carolina General Assembly web site.
Referred to: Appropriations, if favorable, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House
April 30, 2026
1 A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
2 AN ACT TO CLARIFY THE DEFINITION OF ELECTRIC ASSISTED BICYCLE AND TO
3 AUTHORIZE LOCAL GOVERNMENT REGULATION.
4 The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
5 SECTION 1. G.S. 20-4.01(7a) reads as rewritten:
6 "(7a) Electric Assisted Bicycle. – A bicycle with two or three wheels that is
7 equipped with a seat or saddle for use by the rider, fully operable pedals for
8 human propulsion, and an electric motor of no more than 750 watts, whose
9 maximum speed on a level surface when powered solely by such a motor is
10 no greater than 20 miles per hour.watts that meets the requirements of one of
11 the following three classes:
12 a. Class 1 electric assisted bicycle. – A bicycle equipped with a motor
13 that provides assistance only when the rider is pedaling and ceases to
14 assist once the bicycle reaches a speed of 20 miles per hour.
15 b. Class 2 electric assisted bicycle. – A bicycle equipped with a motor
16 that may propel the bicycle without pedaling but ceases to assist once
17 the bicycle reaches a speed of 20 miles per hour.
18 c. Class 3 electric assisted bicycle. – A bicycle equipped with a motor
19 that provides assistance only when the rider is pedaling and ceases to
20 assist once the bicycle reaches a speed of 28 miles per hour."
21 SECTION 2. Article 3 of Chapter 20 of the General Statutes is amended by adding
22 a new section to read:
23 "§ 20-171.3. Electric bicycles.
24 (a) Except as otherwise provided in G.S. 160A-300.2 and G.S. 153A-245.1, the operation
25 of an electric assisted bicycle as defined in G.S. 20-4.01 shall be permitted on all roadways,
26 bicycle lanes, and shared-use paths.
27 (b) A person under the age of 18 operating or riding as a passenger on a Class 3 electric
28 assisted bicycle shall wear a helmet that meets federal safety standards."
29 SECTION 3.(a) Article 15 of Chapter 160A of the General Statutes is amended by
30 adding a new section to read:
31 "§ 160A-300.2. Electric bicycles.
32 (a) A city may regulate the use of electric assisted bicycles on any street, road, or highway
33 within municipal limits. This authority shall include the following:
34 (1) Restricting the use of a class or classes of electric assisted bicycles on multiuse
35 paths, sidewalks, or trails.

1 (2) Establishing speed limits on greenways or shared-use paths.
2 (b) A city may require the use of a helmet by a person under the age of 18 operating or
3 riding as a passenger on a Class 1 or Class 2 electric assisted bicycle.
4 (c) A city may impose penalties for violations of this section."
5 SECTION 3.(b) Article 12 of Chapter 153A of the General Statutes is amended by
6 adding a new section to read:
7 "§ 153A-245.1. Regulation of electric bicycles.
8 A county may regulate the use of an electric assisted bicycle in accordance with
9 G.S. 160A-300.2. Nothing in this section shall be deemed to restrict or repeal the authority of a
10 city to regulate the use of an electric assisted bicycle."
11 SECTION 4. There is appropriated from the Highway Fund to the Department of
12 Transportation, Division of Integrated Mobility, the sum of one hundred thousand dollars
13 ($100,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2026-2027 fiscal year to be used to develop educational
14 materials and conduct a public awareness campaign on the proper use and safety considerations
15 of electric assisted bicycles.
16 SECTION 5. This act is effective when it becomes law and applies to all electric
17 assisted bicycle riders and passengers on or after that date.
